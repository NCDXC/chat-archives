On Feb 21, 2022, at 12:38 AM, Rick NK7I via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

﻿Note also that if one sends the signal report as the opening call (bypassing grid exchange), it is NOT with the letter R (meaning Roger, acknowledged), but the signal report value only.

The R<report> is the correct response if the called responds to signal report.

When working non-F/H DX, watch to see how the DX prefers it; omitting the grid square raises the Q/hour rate and on short openings (e.g. 6M), it can make all the difference.

Rick NK7I

On Feb 20, 2022, at 11:30 PM, Paul J via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Some stations choose to omit the gridsquare portion of the exchange >in times that it's likely irrelevant - i.e. the assumption that the DX station doesn't particularly care what grid you are in.  It saves 30 seconds off the entirety of the exchange.  There are times I will call that way if a station is in a pileup hoping that it gives them the incentive to call me as my QSO will be 2 cycles shorter than one where the calling station starts with the grid square.  I personally don't think either is correct or incorrect, but I guess its up to the DX station and if they consider the grid square to be important or not.  Something to pay attention to while studying the pileup and who they respond to, for sure.

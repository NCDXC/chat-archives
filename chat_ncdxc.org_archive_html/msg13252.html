A multi-national team makes sense for several reasons, but even this “12 man, all Echo India team” with their “wealth of experience” and “well known contesters and world recognized DXpeditioners” should have a much better grip on geographic coverage.  A DXpedition team should have a comprehensive understanding of worldwide propagation and a detailed operating plan to cover all areas as a critical part of their project preparation.  It is equally important as personnel selection, equipment procurement, antenna design, site selection, safety, cost management, transportation, QSLing, licensing/permitting, etc., etc.  Leaving geographical coverage entirely up to team members representing different regions or regional pilots feeding back complaints is very short-sighted. 

 

Their approach to 160 meters suggests they don’t understand how that band might only have one opening to certain parts of the world during their entire trip.  To a lesser degree this holds for all bands.  A thoughtful plan should be in place to maximize the probability of covering the world during their trip.

 

The team sent an update to the DX bulletins yesterday bragging about all their QSOs in spite of zero sunspots.  They congratulated themselves on how they are looking out so diligently for NA.  Out of 10K QSOs, they had 103 with NA and only 7 with Zone 3, all on 20m.  At the same time, they’ve activated over 20 band-mode slots for the easy paths.  That’s not very strong evidence of a thoughtful operating plan.

 

I’m sure this team has sincerely great intentions and I appreciate whatever they can do to meet some of the 9N need.  It just appears that they have fallen way short on at least this one aspect of their venture.

 

Ed W0YK

I've long felt that DXpedition teams are best when they are comprised of members from all over the Globe.

 

I remember back when I still needed my first XT2 Burkina Faso contact. I was excited about a planned operation by a JA group.

Sadly, they went there, worked a lot of JA's and went home. I had to wait a few years longer for my first XT2.

 

When a team has members from all regions it fosters their desire to make everyone happy and not leave any area underserved. I can tell you that the three Americans on our

E30FB team strived very hard to satisfy the NA need a few years ago. We would all operate during the day working EU, then try to catch a nap before the overnight opening to NA which was from about 2AM to 5AM our time. Then we would catch another nap and work EU for the day.

 

It was brutal and conditions were very mixed. 

 

Yes, something seems amiss at 9N7EI. I have yet to hear them on any band. I'm not sure why.

 

Paul N6PSE

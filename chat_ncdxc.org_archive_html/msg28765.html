Will,

I look at the prop wheels, but mostly as hopeful info.  Generally, they're incorrect lately (dismally wrong).  They aren't real time, noting the (frequent) CME events now that the sun is getting more active.  They are based on tables, not reality.  And it disallows that radio, can be magic.

Pay attention to the spotting networks; listen around.  And learn FT8 since all of the DX teams, use that but the hidden bonus is that you get a real time "What is the band doing NOW?" view, you SEE the stations and can learn what band is open where.

And call CQ now and then, you might be happily surprised.

And when in doubt, work on lowering your noise floor as much as you can; you hear more.

73,
Rick nk7i

Will, never in my whole life have I ever made use of propagation predictions.  To me it was more of an issue as to whether I could be in front of the radio.  If you could hear the DX, then it's open.  And I use DX Summit to spot DX I'm interested in.  But most of the time, you can hear when the band is open and then you can work.  Yes, higher bands generally during daylight, lower bands at greyline and darkness, but I've heard quite a number of exceptions to that.  Sometimes 40m is open most of the day.  SP or LP generally follows the sun (at least that's how I look at it).

On 4/25/2014 11:11 AM, Paul Ewing wrote:
The question remains, were these club calls obtained properly before the restrictions were put in place, 

The details page of qrz.com offers a pretty good look at that.
As Ted pointed out, you can easily copy and edit the sample material from the ARRL and within 15 minutes you can meet the requirements for your own club. How convenient is that?
About as convenient as cramming on the question pool for the Extra exam without bothering to learn anything about how radio and electronics work.
Jim-K9YC-you asked me why I don’t have a 1X2 or 2X1 call. When I upgraded to Extra Class, I found no available calls then just as I find now. I didn’t have the time or patience to stick with getting an Extra call and gave up on it.
Gee, it wasn't too much work for all those guys who hold the 1x2 and 2x1 calls that you're raving about. When I looked for a 6-area call around 2005 for my club at Berryessa Peak, it took several tries before I got W6BX (and it was a second choice, with K6YC being my first choice).
You suspect I don’t understand contesting because I am not a contester. You don’t have to be a true blue contester to understand it.
I've seen MANY comments from you over a period of a year or so indicating that you do NOT understand contesting.
I have always known that some contesters would stretch the boundaries for their own needs. I’ve visited many mega contest stations and I know you don’t need Amps with twin 8877’s in them to run at legal power limits.
You and I obviously run in different circles. I'm not at all surprised by your observations, based on the tales you've told of your travels and your expedition partners. But it is unfair to tar all contesters with that brush. I don't know of any respected US contesters who would stoop to that, although I don't doubt that there are some. I understand that it is standard practice in EU. I know from direct experience that K6XX and the teams at N6RO and W6YX play by the rules, and I don't know of any "tubes with handles" shacks within NCCC.
If you really want to help your friend with a 1x2, take Chris up on his offer of assistance. The FCC's rules for the application process are rigid, but clear and easy to follow, and the application fee is minimal. Even at that, it's common for half or more of the applications for a call to be disallowed because of a mis-step, so in the end, there may be only a few good applicants in the running for each lottery, and one of them is going to win. The rules are well delineated on several websites dedicated to vanity calls. 

73, Jim K9YC

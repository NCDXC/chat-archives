My favorite Don Miller story...

When he was on Spratley Islands, 1S9WNV (maybe its first activation) in the 1960's, I heard the following exchange (with made up call signs!):

"Kilo 4 Alpha, again"

"The Kilo 4 Alpha only, again"

"I'm calling Kilo 4 Alpha, everyone else please standby"

Whiskey 6 Radio Radio, standby so I can work the Kilo 4 Alpha"

"Whiskey 6 Radio Radio, you will NOT be in the log tonight.  Now does anyone else want to keep QRMing?"

You could've heard a pin drop.  Don completed his exchange with Kilo 4 Alpha Bravo in about 8 seconds.

Whatever else you make think of Don Miller, he maintained total control of the pileups.

Jim, I don't think publicly outing bad behavior is the right approach. What is egregious to me might not be egregious for the next guy.

For example, the other day, I spotted a DX station on Chat. A few minutes later, a prominent NCDXC member worked the DX. This club member then hung around as part of the Up Police, purposely transmitting SSB on top of the DX station. Now in my view, it's never okay to transmit over the DX for any reason, however this club member obviously thought that what he was doing was acceptable. His belief system was different than mine. Should I shame him to try to make his belief system align with mine?

I don't think that would be wise or realize the intended result.

Paul N6PSE

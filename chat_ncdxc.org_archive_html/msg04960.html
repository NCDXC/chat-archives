Interesting discussion.

It has never occurred to me to use NoAlox or Penetrox on coax connectors for the same reason mentioned by others.

My solution has been to not use UHF connectors. The DO have an impedance bump (actually several) as can be measured with a good TDR. But, these bumps are only a problem when they are closed to a 0.25 wavelength (a PL259-barrel-PL259 is really ugly at 432 MHz), so no problem at HF. I wouldn't want to use them an a 70cm EME array though.

I have chosen to use Type N connectors as they are easier to assemble than UHF connectors. I have over 1500' of coax at my station and not a single barrel connection. I have many N female connectors in use. Cable mount female UHF connectors exist. This removes one more junction that can allow for moisture to get into a connection.

I also use silver plated connectors. I recently took apart a connection that was over 10 years old. The silver showed no signs of oxidation. The trick - I wrap one layer of Scotch 33 around the connection; then coat that with ScotchKote; let that get tacky and cover it with another layer of Scotch 33. I've never had one of these connections fail. They seem to be very water tight (and fog tight for those living on the coast).

Steve, K6UM

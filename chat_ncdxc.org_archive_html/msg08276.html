On Nov 7, 2014, at 5:49 PM, Rick Bates via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Without slamming other teams, I'd also submit that a small tight efficient team with a few compact shipping packages is cheaper than a huge team and monster antennas.  Is this the way of the future?

I see it as tent vs luxury hotel thinking.  Both provide shelter, one is cheaper.  Tiny teams do the same as monster teams, just not in the same style or with the same results.  

Almost two thirds of the contacts are EU, 24% US, leaving a lot of others (~10-15%) around the world less happy; so while not pleasing the entire ham world with contacts, this cohesive team has done VERY well.   Given another couple weeks, the Tromelin itch would be scratched, but the strain on the team would be an issue.  It can't be sustainable for terribly long. 

I may try for more contacts with them, but one Q is in the log, I'm happy. 

Key point: good pilots that are actually being effective (listened to by the team, effecting change) while keeping er, local interests/demands at a minimum.  Good effort all around. 

Now I'm hunting the VU4 team...  Propagation has not been good enough for my low dipole, but that's part of the challenge. ;-)

Paul,

I am a little bit spoiled :-) I always had radios with two receivers for the obvious reason - it's easier to DX that way.

As with you my first big-box radio was a FT-1000 MKV Field with 500/250Hz filters in both IF's and the Inrad Roofing filter mod. I used it for almost 10 years and I only sold it  last year after it started collecting dust when I switched over to my loaded Elecraft K3. 

The K3 is a very well thought through radio that never gets old. New features seem to appear every month. I also picked up a IC-7800 along the way and it sure if a fine radio with very good ergonomics. It's everything the K3 is not - Big, buttons and knobs for everything (times 2 since there are two receivers) . It's like the plush and sporty Maserati compared to a squirrely and purpose built formula one K3. Even though the IC-7800 is software upgradable, Icom has never really bothered to seriously upgrade the feature set. I am sure they could have included a waterfall display or enable VOIP and remote control over the built in Ethernet port. What a shame. However, I find myself drawn to the IC-7800 more often than the K3. 

Enter the TS-990 :-) It has the right size like the IC-7800 with a modern "knobbed" SDR receiver with all bells and whistles like a waterfall display. I think my K3 and/or IC-7800 will have to find new homes soon. I think Kenwood hit this one out of the park.

73,

Andreas, N6NU

Here is your NCDXC Question of the Week:
 
What is your all time favorite rig?
 
 
 
 
 
My response: My all time favorite rig is my Yeasu 1000MP. I bought it new circa 1991 and I still have it. This was my first "big box" rig replacing a Kenwood TS-690. I never really liked or enjoyed the TS-690. The 1000MP is a joy to operate and a very fine radio. My primary pile up busters are a pair of K3's that I use on CW/SSB/RTTY but for casual listening I still enjoy flipping the antenna switch over and listening from the 1000MP.

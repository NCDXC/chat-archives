Dave,

Regarding a club station - in a survey the club conducted recently, that
item failed miserably.  The membership did not support that idea.
Additionally no one would step up to own the project and drive it.  So a
club station once again went down in defeat.  From the reading I have of
the board members, a club station is dead.

Personally I'm against the idea.  The initial cost is simple to cover
but there are all sorts of ongoing costs and obligations e.g. who owns
it, liability, security and maintenance.

If DX'ing needs to be done from a station other than one's home, then
that operator needs to build his own remote of find perhaps a networked
remote.  I often hear Brad K6WR who lives about a mile from me on the
Las Cruces, NM remote.  There are a few of them around.

73,

Bob - W6OPO

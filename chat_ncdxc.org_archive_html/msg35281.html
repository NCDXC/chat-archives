On May 25, 2024, at 11:13 AM, Lucas Ford <lucas@w6aer.com> wrote:

He is MSHV I just got him calling at 258, got a 73!
 

He’s MSHV! No, he’s F/H! No, he’s MSHV . . .

Reminds me of the old Miller Lite commercial: “Tastes Great!” “No, it’s less filling!”. “No, it tastes great!"

I will ask, but he appears to be running software that does BOTH MSHV and F/H. There is no question that many of you have worked him MSHV, calling below 1000. There is also no question that I (and others) worked him F/H, calling above 1000 AND getting my TX frequency dragged down to his run frequency (and getting the RR 73). 

As often happens, the logs (especially in the early days of a DXpedition) always seem to be spotty. It usually is a result of having multiple logs from different radios, and/or different software. I don’t have access to any logs beyond what everyone else sees on Clublog. Marek’s Internet connection is NOT great. Even satphone is spotty. (I would have thought a military base would have reliable Internet, but perhaps that system is reserved for military use, and is not available for civilian or personal use. Manu (the Chief pilot) also thought there would be solid Internet, and even planned for a livestream from the island, at least for logging info, but that is not possible.)

Marek is working like crazy. Already logged over 5000 Qs, and only got 3 hours sleep last night because he didn’t want to shut down when conditions were great. He is up on 6m (fat chance from here!), and will have all antennas and radios going within a day or so. He’s already getting requests for 160m from JA (also unlikely from here).

It’s just too bad he isn’t a CW op. ;^)

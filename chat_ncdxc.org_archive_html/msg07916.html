Ross et al,

That is great news and I regret missing the meeting due to chronic exhaustion.

In 1999, W6OSP and I were part of a DXpedition to Rodrigues using the callsign 3B9R. Our team also included, among others, Kimo, KH7U and Jacky 3B8CF. We knew that Robert, then as now the only resident ham on Rodrigues, had been silent for some years. When we invited Robert to join us, we learned why he had been QRT. A cyclone had  destroyed his station and much of his house. He also had a child with physical challenges, which required all of Robert's time and energy.

What happened next provided one of the most memorable events in my ham life. Robert sat at ah operating position working the pileup--with tears of joy streaming down his face. I can never forget that mment. What a great hobby this is!

We left him with gear to replace what he had lost and when we got home he was already QRV, handing out Q's.

I am very proud of all of you for renewing his station and welcoming him once again into the brotherhood/'sisterhood of DXers. It isn't just the callers in the pileup who are The Deserving.

Garry, NI6T
On 10/16/2014 9:26 PM, Ross Forbes via Chat wrote:

Recently, Rusty Epps, W6OAT, heard that Robert Felicite, 3B9FR, was asking for help getting his current transceiver repaired. Rusty brought this up at the September meeting of the Northern California DX Club where the members were anxious to help.  Ross Forbes, K6GFJ, offered to donate an entire station he had that was not being used to help Robert stay on the air. Kip Edwards, W6SZN, and Rich Stempien, W6RS, made sure the transceiver was in good operating condition before shipping everything off to Robert on Rodriguez Island. Bob Lanning, W6OPO, and Don Ferguson, AI6RE, handled the NCDXC treasury covering all shipping charges, while Don Greenbaum, N1DG, from the Northern California DX Foundation, supplied additional advice and support. As of October 16th, the following items are either in transit to Robert, or have already arrived at Robert’s QTH:

 

Icom 756 ProIII transceiver

Icom SP-23 Speaker

Icom SM-20 Desk mic and hand mic

MicroHAM microKEYER interface

Heil Pro Plus Headset

All documentation and cables are also included, so all Robert has to do is use his current power supply and antenna.  Robert will now be QRV on 6 through 160 meters in SSB, CW, FM and digital modes.

 

73,

Ross

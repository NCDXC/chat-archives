On May 13, 2025, at 08:58, Greg De Hoogh via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

﻿ I'd be interested in hearing from folks with more experience than me, as well as from Yuris, about the possibility that asymmetrical propagation played a part in the apparent slighting of West Coast stations by ZS8W. The night I worked him on 40m FT8 with my 43' vertical, a number of other folks here in SoCal who have 40m beams failed to get him. Is it possible that the primary propagation path from here to Marion Island was in a different direction than the path from there to here, thus resulting in his not hearing them even though they heard him just fine?

I worked him on 20m FT8 about 2-1/2 hours after he first went QRV, and later got him on 40 and 80m FT8. Considering that my only HF antenna is the 43' vertical, I think I did just fine considering it was a one-man DXpedition. I failed to get him on 40m CW, but that was because of QRM on his frequency, not because of anything he did/didn't do. (I'm sure I heard the "PM", but couldn't tell if he got the prefix right because of the QRM - apparently he didn't.)

On May 13, 2025, at 6:33 AM, Gene K5GS via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Hi Rich,

Thanks for the reply.

Since 2012 I’ve been on 7 DXpeditions (6 boat charters) that received a grant from NCDXF (Thank you), and 1 fly-in we funded ourselves.

I was the treasurer for 6 of the 7 DXpeditions, most recently TX5S Clipperton 2024. Except for the self-funded project, grant requests were submitted to the major DX organizations and many smaller DX clubs. In total,  just over 100 DX organizations are listed on my spreadsheet. I don’t recall having a discussion regarding preferential treatment. It may have come up in passing, but as you indicated, it’s silent in the NCDXF grant application.

Additionally, our teams have always been international in scope. I think it would be a difficult (if not an inappropriate) conversation to ask non-US operators to favor NA and/or the west coast. Some of the ops might wonder why their country / region isn’t receiving the same preferential treatment.
 

I think you misunderstood. We don’t ask teams to FAVOR the West Coast or NA, but to make sure they INCLUDE them in their operating plans. For example, often there are very narrow openings where the West Coast is workable, while at the same time there is propagation to EU or other places. We do ask that they be aware of this phenomenon and try to work us when the bands are open.

As others have noted, ZS8W was workable from here during some narrow times: just after our sunrise, and then again just after our sunset. There were MANY instances during these windows when Yuris would continue to work EU/JA, which was available to him almost 24/7. That’s not asking for preferential treatment, just asking to take advantage of propagation when it exists, especially if the windows are small. 

This is where a pilot (or pilots) can help. Yuris didn’t employ any, so he got no feedback that, for example, he was being heard on the West Coast while running a pileup of EU stations.

Rich KE1B

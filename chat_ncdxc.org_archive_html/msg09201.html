On Thu,2/5/2015 6:26 PM, K6XN via Chat wrote:
> After 54 years of being actively engaged in ham radio and chasing DX  
> (so far) I have only recently (the last few years) become finally 
> interested in  RTTY and 160M and engaged in learning and enjoying this 
> mode and the "top" band

RTTY in general was not my point. Rather, it was timing so early in the
DXpedition when only two stations were on the air. As I see it, one good
reason to run high rate CW early on is to reduce the demand, both for ATNO,
and for band slots. All we need is ONE QSO for ATNO, and for all modes, one
RTTY contact on any band, one SSB contact on any band, one CW contact on any
band.

If that early CW operation can work through the band fills, it gives more
opportunity for ATNO and a CW contact on that band for those for whom the
QSO is most difficult. Like Asia, those with limited antennas, etc.

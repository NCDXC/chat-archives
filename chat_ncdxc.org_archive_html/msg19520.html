NCDXC friends, 

After I posted about the latest Microsoft Updates hosing systems, I received dozens of questions which I have not had time to respond to. This message will have to suffice. 

I am Head of IT Operations for a large multinational company based in San Jose. I am responsible for over 10,000 Windows/Mac/Linux desktops/laptops and Servers. 

While it is god practice to keep current on the various patches and Security "Hot fixes" issues by Microsoft and others, it is also good practice as Amateurs to keep backup copies of our log files, our system configurations etc. 

That is what I was urging you to do. 

To state that recent Microsoft updates have wreaked havoc across the IT landscape would be an understatement. On the Flex-Radio forum users there also experienced significant problems after receiving Microsoft updates. 

You should only delay updates for the time needed to backup or document your configurations. Oftentimes, these updates, patches and hot fixes are issued to solve critical issues/security vulnerabilities that have not been publicized but are being shared on the "Dark Web".

In the corporate world, this is our approach to IT risk management:

We "containerize" our User's data in Cloud servers and storage. You can do that easily with Microsoft One Drive or Apple ICloud for example. Box and Dropbox.Com are also other good alternatives. 

With the containerized approach, your home system might be damaged or infected but your data is safe with your Cloud Provider. 

We use Microsoft SCCM (Systems Management Center) to maintain working configurations/Images that we can quickly restore to a Users computer should they become infected or "hosed". We spend very little time troubleshooting laptop/desktop images and instead restore the working image of the Operating System, applications and configurations within 15 minutes. 

Antivirus and Malware protection is critical on each laptop or "host computer". We use Malewarebytes Professional on each system accompanied by Cisco AMP for virus protection. Symantec and Trend Micro also have good solutions for the Home. 

A home router affords no protection to malware or Ransomware. A router is like the front door of your house propped open to let the fresh air in. It also lets the flies in. While Windows Defender is a decent free Firewall, a Hardware Firewall is the better way to protect your home network. 

Running an old operating system such as Windows 95 or Windows XP is not good practice. There are many Security Vulnerabilities with those older operating systems that are well known. Neither of those operating systems are supported or patched by Microsoft. 

I hope that I have answered your many questions with this message to the group. 

Thank you, 

Paul N6PSE

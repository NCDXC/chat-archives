And 100 miles east, an entirely different story, it faded quickly here. I should be able to put in some chair time tomorrow, I wasn't able to do much during the day today, chores and all got in the way.
The lidfest was in full attendance tonight on 80. But after a short break (sudden QRT for five, unannounced), a few sudden QSY's, those folks lost interest and then it was just slogging through the pileup. There were still idiot action in the RX section, sending crap or tones etc, in synch with when K1N would listen...
But band 3 is in the log! I now have 80, 40 and 30 (all CW). But the online log is STILL not uploaded... which would lighten their load from insurance Q's. Plus I could start to breathe again. :) I tried RTTY on 30, until they faded to ESP.
I listened a bit on 160, but my dipole is too reactive on the bottom end so I'm limited to only a couple hundred watts of cloud heating. Maybe when the pressure slacks I can give it a try.
The operators have been amazing. Whoever took over on 80 CW tonight moved things right along, plus added 10 WPM to the code speed (hooray decoder! I don't copy 35 WPM, take it easy on the Red Bull). His Q rate was quite a bit higher and after an hour, I was finally able to watch where he was working, saw the pattern and got in front to catch it.
I saw some um, (snarky) cluster commentary about ops being in the Extra portion of the bands... valid complaints, but I presume there will be some effort after the initial crush for Generals. (No need to dissect that in commentary, they deserve a shot too; this can be a lifetime event.)
On the subject of clusters... some folks are NOT spotting them because as soon as it's posted, miscreants move in. Please continue to post here.
During the (above) sudden QSY's tonight, the op would call CQ, which triggers the RBN spotting, posts that to clusters and ... Perhaps just send the call with up/dn as needed instead of a CQ? It's the "CQ" that triggers the RBN. 

They got the SteppIRs up today, expect things to get better.
BTW, Jim, I heard you LOUD and clear here in the foothills but I'm tired enough to not remember which band it was... :o) I swapped bands numerous times tonight.
73 es good hunting (they're getting loud on 40 now if someone needs it plus it's almost sunrise in EU), 
Rick wa6nhc

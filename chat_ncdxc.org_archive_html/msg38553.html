Paul,

Congrats for 'having them all' (as the list stands now).  We both have 'deleted' in our logs; they still hold some value (memories at least).

I would submit the following (for the sake of discussion):

  • Moving any entity to inactive or deleted, does not diminish what has been already attained.
  • There are some entities that have zero hint/hope of ever being available again; or at least this generation.
  • Deleted are still counted in the grand totals; but not for top award honors of active entities.
  • Once Honor Roll or similar is attained, that place is held not taken away and can be augmented only (with a new entity).  No one that reached that goal with an entity that has since been deleted; ever lost the standing.
  • The goal should be that the current list is attainable.  Over the past decades far fewer on the list are (or will be again).  It is no longer fair or valid to anyone wanting 'them all' as there is no hope of ever working many entities on the list; not in any foreseeable future.  So all top awards really only show those who have been hams the longest and survived the changes (which is NOT the point of the standing).
  • The only present 'lifetime' award is the DXCC Hall of Fame or those still able to stand up at DX conventions when asked 'How many have worked ...", so yes, something should be added to recognize what has been attained; WHILE the ham is still alive.
  • The entity list should be treated as a living document; able to change over time.  The last changes were at least a generation ago and it's long overdue for an update.

But it's managed by those afraid of losing status, challenged by those seeking the entirety of the current list.

73,
Rick nk7i

 

 

On 1/27/2025 4:17 PM, wa6ibu@earthlink.net wrote:

Understand all that is said on the topic here and at other times.  Here's my perspective and keep in mind I'm coming from the position of an being an "Old Timer".....never thought I'd say that.  I've worked them all over the years and have had it done for some time with the only changes being new entities that are added.  I started in 1968.  I didn't have a big yagi, tower (just a roof top), or dual receiver transceiver.  Even though I have them all confirmed I have never submitted for an award.  I was just competing with myself (as others have said here) and the idea that with some developing skill I would work through even the “big guns” and California Kilowatts.  For example I worked Monk Apollo in 1998 with 90 watts.  Having said that, I’m reticent to just drop entities even if they seem unreachable at this time:

-I think there should still be some recognition for those who over the years did work them all, including deleted ones

-Is our goal here to make it easier for more hams to reach honor roll?

-If we change the goal posts depending on the ever changing entities, will some reach honor roll and then lose it?  (I guess that’s true now if an entity is deleted or new one added)

-I think there’s a way to provide recognition for both the younger hams to achieve goals and to recognize what might be a lifetime of achievement for an old timer who has been in the game a long time

-Is the goal to be able to dilute the honor roll class by lowering the threshold?  Sorry to say, we kind of did that by removing the CW requirements (yep, CW is my favorite….old timer) to keep the interest alive?

 

So with all those questions here’s a possible solution to address all the above:

 

-Establish a new honor roll (maybe even change the name), that removes the impossible ones that many have mentioned.  So maybe it’s 330 or something

-Provide a “stamp” that signifies all entities confirmed above that, maybe in values of 1,2, 5 or 10 entities.  This includes ALL deleted entities from today to the distance past.  (don’t tell me this is already being done as like I said I’ve never submitted for anything)

-That way, from new to old, we’ve established an obtainable “honor roll” achievement but also recognize the efforts which for some may be MANY years of effort, to have a stamp signifying that on the award.

 

Just my thoughts and reactions.

 

Paul, W6IBU

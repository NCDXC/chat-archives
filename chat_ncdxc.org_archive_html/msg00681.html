On Dec 11, 2012, at 10:43 AM, Rick Bates wrote:

> 
> The only answer is more servers, in parallel or each new DXpedition or contest will swamp the existing system.  I’m assuming that it is only XML manipulations and additions; it should be simple and fast.
>  

The answer is probably NOT "more servers", or faster servers. The fact that what "should be simple and fast" takes so much time, indicates that there is a systemic/architectural problem in the organization of the database, or in the software operating on that database. Yes, faster hardware will improve things in the short term, but without fixing the design flaw, it only delays the inevitable.

Clearly, Clublog and eQSL are evidence that it is possible to process logs *much faster* than LoTW. In the case of Clublog, we even know that the processing is being performed by a fairly modest, single-server platform.

And kudos to YASME for funding an even faster server, together with real-time fault-tolerance capability for Clublog. Clearly, that money will go to someone who knows how to use it properly. I couldn't say that for ARRL/LoTW.

Rich KE1B

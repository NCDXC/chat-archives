I worked them on 15 and 30M with v2.7.0.rc6, but the QU1RKS callsign was in the decodes.

 

A bit of research told me to update to WSJT-X v2.7.0.rc7.   Since then, clean decodes without any QU1RKS decodes.

 

“By now you know that these decodes are a result of receiving SuperFox RC7 transmissions with the <=RC6 decoder. Here are a few more details.

Effective with WSJT-X 2.7.0-RC7, unused callsign slots in SuperFox messages are filled with the dummy callsign QU1RKS rather than with zeros. This change has two beneficial consequences: (1) the codeword for the encoded message is more random, so transmitted energy is more widely spread over the signal's occupied bandwidth, and (2) in case they had not been paying attention, users are effectively notified that they must upgrade to a later software release.”  - Joe K1JT

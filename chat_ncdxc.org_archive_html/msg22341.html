I haven't tried them with my K3 (yet), but I've been stunned at how my Nuraphone headphones have improved my audio listening experience.

https://www.nuraphone.com/

I think they're not comfortable enough to wear for many many hours at a time, but I regularly wear them for an hour or two, and the in-ear "nuraloop" version is comfortable for as long as the battery lasts.

Not at all cheap, but amazing tech. They both have analog cables as well at Bluetooth, but I'm not sure how the mic component works on the analog cable.

I found this useful and my results generally reflect my RX EQ settings in my K3.  I may tweak them a bit more with this data.

 

https://hearingtest.online/

 

I took the test once with my PC speakers, and once on my iPhone with a set of wired earbuds.  The results were very similar by audio frequency range and, as I expected, up a level or two with the earbuds as they blocked most ambient noise.

 

At the end of the test, you are able to print out the audiogram from your test, which is useful

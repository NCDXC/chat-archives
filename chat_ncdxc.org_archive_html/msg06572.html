I am not a lawyer nor do I have any intent to sue anyone.  But the rules were recently modified, mostly in line with Pauls concerns.. so for the sake of the group.. ill share.

 

Paul, just to save you some bandwith in writing the ARRL and   Doing a little research to refresh my memory, here is the summary of the 2010 FCC report and order that limits the amount of club calls that can be assigned.  Take note that it says clubs can now only have one call trustee, and can obtain only ONE vanity call 1x2… 2 X1 …1 X3.. whatever.  Just 1 vanity call but can have as many sequential calls as they need.  Also take note that call trustees that have already obtained additional vanity calls can retain additional calls obtained prior to the report and order

 

http://www.arrl.org/news/fcc-issues-em-report-and-order-em-on-vanity-and-club-station-call-signs

 

 

The ARRL and Dave Sumner was not in complete agreement with the FCC on this and submitted a PETITION FOR RECONSIDERATION to the FCC.  The FCC apparently denied this petition with impunity.. (e.g. clearly stating they see no purpose in revisiting this and dismissed the petition with no plans to reconsider)

 

http://www.arrl.org/news/fcc-denies-arrl-petition-on-vanity-club-call-signs

 

and the FCC final word on the subject is here.

 

http://transition.fcc.gov/Daily_Releases/Daily_Business/2012/db0111/FCC-12-1A1.pdf

 

This was on 1/2012

 

So thar be the rules as best as the great google finds them ;-)

 

Seems all of this was hashed out and brought before the commission and they ruled. current holders of calls can keep them.. future clubs can compete for 1 vanity call of whatever type if available and that’s the ways the FCC see’s it for now.  They make the rules.  We follow them.

Team

 

I think this is a very interesting topic for both DXers and Contesters. Many of us are both. Frankly I love working both DXers and Contesters and most of my DXing in the early days was primarily working DX in contests…primarily because they were usually the strongest , most proficient operators and located at great DX locations where they went to at their own expense and they were better QSLers  IMHO than most “casual” DXers. Many of my DX contacts over the years have been with contesters using “Club” call signs. I also love being the DX for the other operators now that we have some pretty good stations of our own on the air!

 

I think it may be helpful for some of us to consider being very careful in accusing any Club or any operator using a “Club” call as being “fake” or “phony” (and I am neither saying nor accusing that anyone on this blog would do or has done such a thing). Notwithstanding the above I think it might be helpful for some of us (we know who we are) to consider that the FCC requirements for issuing a “Club” call sign are:

 

Q: What are the requirements for a club station license grant?

A. Section 97.5(b)(2) of the FCC Rules states that a club must have:

1.    A name,

2.    A document of organization,

3.    Management,

4.    A primary purpose devoted to amateur service activities consistent with the FCC Rules,

5.    At least four members,

6.    A license trustee designated by an officer of the club, and

7.    The trustee must hold an amateur service operator license grant.

It may be useful to note that the ARRL has representative Club Constitutions and Bylaws that can be downloaded and tailored by anyone in probably 15 minutes for any particular Club that can be viewed and downloaded from   :  http://www.arrl.org/files/file/Clubs/Club%20Constitution.pdf  .

 

If a Club meets the above FCC requirements and has spent 15 minutes downloading the sample Constitution and Bylaws from the ARRL website and prepared the necessary “document of organization” and has four or more members it is possible that some cranky Clubs may consider slander and libel litigation against those who may carelessly and thoughtlessly call their “Clubs” or their Club operators  “fake” or “phony”. At the minimum they may consider it an undeserved, unjustified, gratuitous, unlawful, slander and or libel, and insult (or not). Call me radical. IMHO there are a *lot* of contesters (and DXers) who are also licensed attorneys at law….. but most would rather be chasing DX or contesting and having fun than dealing with possible gratuitous slander and libel issues. It’s a hobby.

 

FWIW, I suggest those who are concerned about these issues consider very carefully moderating their verbal and written public characterizations of “Clubs” or of “individual contesters” using Club Calls as being either “fake” or “phony” unless they have a pretty good faith belief and a solid concrete basis for concluding that a “Club” call being used by a “Contester” or anybody else is either “fake” or “phony” in that it does *not* meet for example both  the FCC requirements for a Club call license and the ARRL recommendations for a Club document of organization or function even minimally as a club.  

 

OTOH if there are those among us who in good faith believe in their personal opinion a higher “moral” or “ethical” or whatever standard is needed than what is required by the FCC and by the ARRL then a more productive result IMHO is probably just meeting with individual contesters and “Contest Clubs” and discussing with them your own personal opinions and your own personal issues. In addition, those who believe Clubs should be stripped of desirable call signs to provide the Club call signs of active clubs to envious new extra class operators may need IMHO to provide better compelling arguments to the FCC and to the contesting community of radio amateurs to be persuasive.

 

This is an interesting topic and I look forward to reviewing Paul’s  blog at  http://n6pse.wordpress.com .

 

FWIW I think we all need to be a lot nicer to each other. I love this hobby. Back to chasing DX.  Have fun and 73, Ted, K6XN.

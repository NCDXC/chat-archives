I agree with this approach and also attempting contact with the offending station then if that fails, go public.  Nothing teaches like embarrassment (with an attempt to both learn and teach).  And none of us hasn't made a public mistake.  FT8 is still new, not everyone understands how it works.
One can also use the approach of "If it's rare or new, work THEN post".  Not quite as sporting perhaps.

I will repeat my suggestion: FT8 spots should only include the call and the band.   Add the dial frequency for F/H or for bands where there are multiple dial frequencies (e.g. 6m).

This is the same as CW or PHONE, except for the use of a wetware decoder.   Don't call until you can decode the DX.

Bob:  where did you copy the stuff about SF +10dB vs FH from?

I could be wrong, but it makes no sense to me because the BANDWIDTH is also much greater for SF.

I suspect the energy per bit vs noise density (Eb/No) is a wash, and that is what counts.

Mere better signal strength alone is not the whole story.

If we assume FT-8 was already at the Shannon limit, it can't be improved on by multiplexing.


IMHO

On 1/9/2025 10:05, Chortek, Robert L. via Chat wrote:
I think super fox provides better signal strength than F/H.
 
See below:
 
SuperFox mode in WSJT-X provides better signal strength than Fox-and-Hounds mode: 
 
  • Signal strength
    SuperFox mode provides a system gain of about +10 dB compared to Fox-and-Hounds mode. This is because SuperFox transmits a full power constant-envelope waveform, while Fox-and-Hounds transmits up to five streams, each 50 Hz wide."

    Given the improved signal strength of SuperFox, and it's greater throughput nine stations (4 signal reports and 5 RR73) versus five on F/H  I'd vote for SupetFox initially unless than until it proves insufficient.

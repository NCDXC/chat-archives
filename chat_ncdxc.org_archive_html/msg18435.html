Rick,

The FT8 attributes you mention as desirable to you, “I can turn the audio OFF and not have to sift through the confusion and noise.  Click, work, repeat...  FAR less fatiguing.” are the opposite factors from which I gain satisfaction in the making of a contact.  I do not seek an easy contact when working DX; I seek to feel like I really accomplished something, requiring patience and skill.  If a QSO is too easy, I can check a box, sure,  but it is a forgettable QSO.  I love the ones that I can look back at and recall how exciting it was to get the QSO under suboptimal conditions.

Each of us finds our own reasons for DXing.

 

CUL and HNY!

The signal strength at the DX end can be limited (filtered out) by the team, there are FT8 settings to manage that.  dB > x, ignore type of thing (the last time I poked around in that section).  Ditto with zone (AS, EU, NA etc) or call types (EU ONLY mode).

But basically FT8 can be considered an equalizer; if you hear it, work it.... regardless of qrp vs QRO++, it's all in the hearing, signal strength is just the report.

To exclude any mode without providing options (in this case for a digital mode), no matter how rare the entity is... not in the spirit of ham radio (to be inclusive of new ideas, technology and modes) and is not helpful to the community at large.

As mentioned before, it eliminates (rather than includes) those of low antenna ability and those with limited hearing ability, I'm sure there are other limiting factors.  It essentially says "If you're not QRO enough to work CW or phone (much more challenging to have enough signal to be 'heard'), we don't want you." 

I'll take an FT8 pileup any day over phone or CW; I can turn the audio OFF and not have to sift through the confusion and noise.  Click, work, repeat...  FAR less fatiguing.

For a group to display such prejudicial behavior is frankly appalling to me.  Some of us (because the bulk of us are older) may not be around the next time a group goes out, so if digital is needed for some award point; it may never happen for them.

If one doesn't like a particular mode, don't use it.  However a DXpedition group is not such a time and place to express mode prejudice; it should present all three modes (CW, Phone, Digital) to the ham community with some equality.  (One recent team had ONE operator on THREE bands of FT8 at the same time; while running phone or CW with single operators on the same bands!  Just food for thought.)

HNY es 73,
Rick NK7I

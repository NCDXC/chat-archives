Being near the US Canada line, I live under polar conditions, though on the fringe of it.  There are times of the year that some bands are useless for a polar path, while others (down south of me) have no issues.

This morning, 30M I was decoding a52 with -7 signals and then without loss of signal strength, the distortion made them unable to be decoded (not even sure if I was ever answered).

If you watch the panadapter (larger, external used on my K4), the streams get 'fuzzy' which is a key indicator of distortion and almost always, it's a polar path (but not the sole cause).

When you see a late decode (set for deep), the FEC coding in the streams earns it's value, the software is making it's best 'informed' guess at what it almost heard.

There are MANY times that I can actually HEAR the stream audio, but it won't decode (no change in settings ever improves this).  FRUSTRATING.

Note that there are TWO calls you want to track; a52ci and a52p (or a52*).  I presume they are in two locations requiring two calls or each op is required to have a call...  a small complication for alerts and spotting.

LOG THE CALL OF THE STATION YOU WORK.  Which means YOU have to LISTEN, no rhyme or reason here in which call is used.  Great ops but no information posted (band plan, rigs, antennas) on their web page either (we're in the dark).

It appears that their antenna is essentially omni, working both EU and AS at the same time (180 deg apart, with meager reports so they're not hearing anything loud).

Here is what I've been able to (BARELY) accomplish (in North Idaho), so far since they clearly are not (can't be?) showing a lot of effort outside of EU/AS so far...

<image001.png>

Both were short path at full legal output, into 4 elements (~11 dBi gain) at 60' with the receiver narrowed down to boost their audio with less noise.  Both took over an hour each.

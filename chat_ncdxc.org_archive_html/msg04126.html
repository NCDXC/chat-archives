On 11/12/2013 8:54 AM, KE1B wrote:
I can't speak about the issue of measuring 40m gain on a low-height Optibeam (which seems to be the only technical issue the poster had). I have the OB11-5, which does not include 40m coverage. It's also not clear how ANYONE can *accurately* measure far-field antenna gain, particular to tenths-of-a-db (which is what appears in everyone's data sheets).

The published Optibeam numbers are believable. It IS possible to get good data, but it's a non-trivial exercise. :)  N0AX and K7LXC did that around 1999-2000 with a representative group of large and small tri-banders, and N6BT has published some measurements. 

The OB11-5 is a lot heavier and presents a lot more wind load  than the 3-el 20-6M Steppir, so probably wants to be on a bigger rotator and beefier tower. Performance-wise I'd say they're quite comparable if the specs are real, and they're not far apart on cost for the antenna (incl the controller for the SteppIR and the control cable). That difference in weight probably means two people on the tower, while the 3-el Steppir can be handled by one strong guy (K6XX in my case).

My old friend K9IKZ, whose ham ticket is about as old as mine,  has always preached that good antennas are expensive.  Doing a tower safely is expensive. Coax and control cable costs money. Tower climbers cost money. If you do it yourself, safety equipment is expensive. Big antennas sometimes need a bucket truck. Tree climbers cost money.

73, Jim K9YC

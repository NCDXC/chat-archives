For those of you who may be interested in local HF NVIS net, check out the following.  I check into the net whenever I get a chance but 0730 hrs is a bit early for me on most Saturday mornings.  This net has been going on for last couples years and I am amazed as to the radio of a NVIS antenna and how well it is able to get into the back hills and various valleys.

You don't need to be associated with Red Cross but have any interest in NVIS.  This net has helped some of our Red Cross offices to improve their basic HF setup.  If you check into the net and wish to get email reports of captured results, ask the net to add you to the email list, or contact me

73 de N6NKT, George w.

This Net is operated with the cooperation of the San Mateo County Red Cross / ARES organizations, and is held each Saturday morning at these times and frequencies:
0730 hrs    3890.0 KHz LSB 
0745 hrs    5403.5 KHz USB Ch5 
0800 hrs    7181.0 KHz LSB   (7204, 7209, 7224KHz are secondary)
While the American Red Cross utilizes many different communication resources and functions, this particular net is specifically focused on the use of the Amateur Radio High Frequency, or HF Bands.  The explicit purpose of this net is to explore, practice, and improve on various propagation methods and techniques, including NVIS propagation, dealing with various band conditions, and the effective use of relay and volunteer stations to meet specific communication needs based on Red Cross or ARES appropriate application.  It is also the purpose of this net to encourage individual member growth in communications skills and activities, and to encourage station and resource improvements were appropriate.  In total, it is our objective to be able to provide quality and reliable communications as needed, and to do so within the guidelines and objectives of the Amateur Radio Service.  If anyone has any questions about this net, please address those to...

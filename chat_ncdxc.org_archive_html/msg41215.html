+ AA6YQ comments below

As I have hinted, the DX Keeper is PICKY and has found every flaw in the ADIF of other apps.  It is EXTREMELY thorough (well done, exactly what we hope for).

It is function before form (what I do too); make it WORK, then worry about pretty.  Being 'pretty' isn't high on the list; it's FUNCTIONING, the important part.

MOST of the panels can be minimized to save valuable display space (I'm adding a second display).  I use DX View a LOT so I can quickly see what band mode slots I don't have for a given entity (something HRD doesn't do easily or well).

I would LOVE to see SteppIR control added (into rotor? a logical spot) but it does play well with PSTRotatorAZ so it's not critical.

+ Send me a pointer to the SteppIR controller, and I'll extend DXView to support it. I made 22 public releases of DXLab applications last year; this sort of request is typical of what drives them.

I don't know if Dave has planned (has a team to take over) in the event of his expiration date.

+ Continuously updated DXLab source code is in the hands of a fellow ham with instructions to continue DXLab development.

.. but in the meantime; it's worth a look for those not in love with other suites.

At least DX Commander groks the K4 well (HRD, not at all, use the K3 selection for some control, lacking many features).

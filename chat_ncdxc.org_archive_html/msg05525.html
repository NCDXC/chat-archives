Rich Holoch wrote:

I picked up a really fantastic old home brewed amplifier that is labeled “10K” on the front panel. It must have been a joke – because it uses a single 3CX1200A7. It was built with two of the largest vacuum variable capacitors that I have ever seen. It’s a work of art – built by a master.

 

The paperwork that I received includes the 1971 QST article that describes the circuitry and there were two QSL cards in the folder:

 

Dick, W6BZE

Andy, W6FF

 

They were members of the San Diego DX Club, and Chuck Patterson, K6RK owned this amplifier up until 2 years ago when he went SK. I was told that Chuck wanted someone who would take good care of this amplifier, so I have started by writing this blog – that includes a picture of the amp and scans of the QSL cards.

 

http://ky6r.wordpress.com/2014/02/09/10k/

 

It has been stored in a very cool old “Wheat Warehouse” in Port Costa for the last 2 years, and wow – Port Costa is a very nice little hamlet. I thought I was in Sonoma, and the town has great history.

 

73,

 

Rich

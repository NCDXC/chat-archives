It might be an interesting number, Craig, but one you have to be careful with.  For instance, the C82DX DXpedition set as its goal to work stations on the low bands where Mozambique was most needed.  That meant low rates night after night trying to work stations through all the QRN found in that part of the world.  Sometimes, at the end of a four hour shift, the operators had added only 10 to 25 QSOs to the log -- but they all were on 80 and/or 160 and were new band/countries for the guys they worked.  They could have easily made a few hundred QSOs during that same time period on 30m or 20m, but they mostily would have been working stations who already had Mozambique confirmed on those bands.  So, in my opinion, while sheer number of QSOs might be one measure of a DXpedition's success, it certainly is not the only measure.  More important, I think, is what were the DXpedition's stated goals and how well did it achieve those goals.

73 de Rusty, W6OAT

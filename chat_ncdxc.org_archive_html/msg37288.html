Why?  Unless your goal is wimpy QRP and multiple retries.  You would only work the loud stations and less DX.  QRP is a choice, just as QRO (100 watts, is not QRO).

The only other reason is SOME rigs, is that they may be cleaner at lower output; not all.

The JT modes are low signal level on RECEIVE (meaning weak signals have a good chance of decoding); you use what power you need on transmit (to the legal limit) to complete the contact.

As your antennas improve, you’ll see that low (100 watts) power is adequate for everyday DX; but to work the weak more rare DX; nothing beats power.

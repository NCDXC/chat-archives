Welcome to DXing and keep chipping away at the list; it’s still fun even if the “awards” are unattainable. I started (I think) about 2013 and have 324 confirmed; ATNO is darn rare now.

 

There is a list of Most Wanted on Clublog, but no list of unlikely to be heard (for MANY years at least); which would include Afghanistan (once we left, all ham radio was ended) and a large handful of places uninhabited, some downright deadly and nasty.  

 

MOST of those places are deemed ‘preserves’ and all it takes is one (of many) involved parties that manage them, to say no and the hope ends.  (Kure, Johnston, which are US but the problem spans many countries.). 

 

Politics is another concern (Scarborough is ‘claimed’ by at least 6 countries and going there risks actual war between them.)

 

Rather than taking the time to actually SEE the request, ‘No’ is a simpler response.  Many of those empowered to say no, do so because they can (it’s not about a critter or environment; SOMEONE attends the place occasionally, why NOT include ham radio?).

 

Even unstaffed remote operations (except set up, tear down and refueling) have been told no.

 

Sheer expense is another limitation.  The present budget for another Bouvet attempt, is now set at $1.7 MILLION dollars (the last barely helpful attempt spent $700k).  That is a LOT of money, per QSO (I got some).

 

The costs have blown sky high, COVID didn’t help.  Nothing has returned to previous costs.

 

A few have been more creative for access (hitch a ride with the local service crew) and single ops are simpler, slower (harder to work) but also effective.  (Crozet was an AMAZING single person event, everyone got something.)  Sometimes the military is involved (two edged sword but they have great logistics).

 

But we keep swinging away looking for the next ATNO.  That’s called hope.

 

As for an expensive piece of paper you have to buy or a cheap plastic plaque to hang on the wall to gather dust; just keep your own ‘score’ and keep trying.  You know the time, effort and costs you put into it and the plaque didn’t give you pride; knowing what you accomplished does that.  You don’t need anyone to ‘bless it’.

 

Batter up!  Swing away!

 

73,

Rick nk7i

I believe that if the ARRL offers and promotes an award that the Award should be attainable. Currently it is not possible (in a lifetime) to attain Honor Roll for anyone starting the journey now or in the recent past. Change is required when a program becomes stale.

Gene K5GS proposal to have an inactive list makes the most sense. It only requires a rule change and does not delete any entities. However, the outcry from the old timers of “NO CHANGE” is very emotional.

Compromise proposal: #1 Honor Roll rules remain unchanged and in effect becomes the legacy Award. You need to “Work them All” to receive the #1 Honor Roll status. No changes to the current rules for #1 Honor Roll. The rule to count entities for Honor Roll be changed to “you must have a total confirmed entity count that places you among the numerical top ten DXCC entities currently active in the last twenty years on the DXCC List” The Honor Roll has never been a “Worked them All” award. Honor Roll has always been an award for diligently “working the greatest amount of them but not all”. Additionally, a new award “High Honor Roll” could be created for working all currently active entities in the last twenty years.

In summary you would have three award levels (#1 Honor Roll, High Honor Roll, and Honor Roll).

Matt K6EME

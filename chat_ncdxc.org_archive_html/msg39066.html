Vijay,

When you drive your amp with a cw signal, what does the drain current meter do.
Drive the amp with a cw signal of 10 watts with the ability to key the exciter on/off.
Does it increase when you key your exciter? Is it zero all the time or is is some
steady drain current (more than the <2 amp idling current) all the time? Seeing
the current increase when the amp is keyed is a sign the the output FET or FETs
are OK. No current indicates that the FETs have failed open circuit. A heavy standing
current indicates the FETs have shorted. Neither open or shorted devices are a good
sign. When you do this test be sure to have a matched load (50 ohms) on your amplifier.

What do you see?

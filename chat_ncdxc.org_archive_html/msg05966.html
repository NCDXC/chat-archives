Hmm... I just sent off my $5 for a QSL card from the 9J2T folks.  That $5 is exclusive of my cash donation to the DX-pedition. Their site states in pretty clear terms that if you want the card, you must donate or your information will be "deleted" by their system.  Perhaps it was an issue of translation, or my mis-reading of it, but that is my interpretation of what was posted on their OQRS page.  I want the card, so I sent my $5, plus donation.  I have paid similar amounts for cards from other DX-peditions and OQRS hams.

I am about to send out a small mountain of QSL cards plus green stamps / foreign SASE.  My cost on each of these mailings is - including printing of my cards, air mail envelope "kits", mailing labels, foreign postage -is AT LEAST $5 per unit, sometimes more.  

If I want a rare QSL card from a ham in a totalitarian country where official bribery is rampant, I will pay the freight.  If the price is too rich for my blood, I won't pay for it.  No sense flaming the DX op with a radioactive email (pun somewhat intended).  If I could have broken the pile-up and made contact, I would now be sending off my $10 for a card from EP3HF.  I suppose "outside the bounds" is relative. 

As my teenage kids would say ... "yo, peace out" (whatever that means...)

It must be somewhat humbling for an organization that takes so much pride in proclaiming being prepared to the masses; although the insurance is part of that.

That is not snark (well, maybe a hint of a snicker, though it could happen to anyone).

The actual infestation could have been at any time, even months prior.  But the attack was coordinated, once the pieces were in place.   If so, backups could be compromised as well.

What the insurance covers, (ransom, recovery, payment to third parties, loss of income) was also not mentioned (nor the cost of coverage, will that go up now?).  I don’t expect to ever get all of the information (so we could make our own choices on coverage).

Renew now to avoid the potential (added) price boost; if you wish to (still) be a member.

It was very carefully crafted words (no surprise there and it was correct to do so under the circumstances).

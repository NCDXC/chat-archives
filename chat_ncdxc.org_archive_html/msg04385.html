Dear Members of the NCDXC Board,
As a former NCDXC Board member, Vice President, and Treasurer, I was disappointed in the club process I witnessed Thursday evening.  I?m sharing my thoughts with all of you, not expecting you to reverse yourselves, but hoping you might realize how this process could have been handled better.
While I like Jim personally, I think it was wrong that the club membership was not involved in the selection of a new president.  I realize you followed the letter of the Bylaws in making your selections and then presenting them to us members for ratification.  But that process simply presented us with a fait accompli.  We had no alternative available.  This is not how I have experienced any previous election of a club president.
For me, the club president is the most important role in our organization.  The president can lead the members toward his or her vision of where the club should go.  In past regular officer elections, I have always been interested in what the candidates for president said they wanted to do for the club.  You might call this stating a platform.  Right now I don?t feel like I voted for any vision for the club at all.  This is why I am disappointed.
For more supportive roles such as VP, Treasurer or Secretary, I can see having the Board simply pick a replacement for an officer who resigns in the middle of a term.  But for President, the membership should be able to participate in a special election.  Candidates should be nominated by the membership, and then be given the opportunity to present their platforms.  The members should have time to deliberate and then vote.  In most previous elections, we have typically announced the upcoming June elections at our March meeting, closed nominations and finalized candidates in May, and held the vote in June.  So members had a while to mull over who they thought might be best, and maybe even observe candidates in action at Visalia.  A number of times, a good prospective candidate had to be arm-twisted behind the scenes by his friends to accept a nomination.  These things all take time to develop.
There was no compelling urgency to ?get this behind us?.  But there should always be an urgency to give our members the best possible club leader going forward.  Jim may actually be that leader, and I hope he is, but right now I have no way of knowing.  The Thursday Nov. 21st meeting would have been better used to discuss the Bylaws with the members and amend them to better fit our needs.  If the Bylaws had been changed to call for a special election, that election could have waited until the January meeting, giving members time to vet candidates.
The Board should focus on best serving the members of the club, not on slavishly following Bylaws which are by no means sacrosanct.
73,
Steve Jones
N6SJ

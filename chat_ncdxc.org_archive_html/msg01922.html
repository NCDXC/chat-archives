Good question – my favorite rig.

 

As I move through time that favorite rig seems to change.  Then I will drop back to one of the older ones.

 

Of all the rigs I have owned, many top-of-the-line it has been the Yaesu FT-1000MP Mark-V.  Except for it not having a panadapter and its IF is too high for easy conversion to frequencies available for most pan adapters.  I added the QS1R in the RX Ant loop taking the place of a panadapter.  Great work-around.

 

In terms of function and pure engineering design the K3/P3 has been the top of the line but a little difficult to use.  I’m sometimes set back because I hit the wrong button before hitting the keypad and I have just gone somewhere and don’t know how to get back – in the heat of the battle – not good.  (Four functions assigned to a key!)  The P3 makes the K3 station outstanding.  Both rigs (K3 & Mark-V) have set at the main operating position for some time.  Between the two I usually use the Mark-V.

 

BUT – time and rigs change.

 

For about a month now I have been using Kenwood’s new TS-990S.  I have been using a loaner for about 2 weeks and at about the end of the second week I ordered my own TS-990S.  It has replaced the K3/P3 and the Mark-V.  Too many details to mention why.  Just know it has all the features of the others (and they work) and better yet, its ergonomics even beats ICOM’s 7800.  A number of nice convenience features not found in other radios are there too.  This radio has to have been under development for a long time – and by hams.  The new Kenwood flagship radio you have to use to know.

 

Oh – I put the QS1R on the TS-990S’ RX Ant loop but seldom use it.  The RBW of the 990s panadapter is very narrow.  I don’t know what it is but from the display it is very close to the P3’s 8Hz.

 

So on the side-line in the shack are:

 

IC-781

IC-7800

FT-1000D

FT- 1000MP Mark-V

K3/PW

I have got to get started on eBay!

 

73,

 

Bob – W6OPO

What is your all time favorite rig?

 

 

 

 

 

My response: My all time favorite rig is my Yeasu 1000MP. I bought it new circa 1991 and I still have it. This was my first "big box" rig replacing a Kenwood TS-690. I never really liked or enjoyed the TS-690. The 1000MP is a joy to operate and a very fine radio. My primary pile up busters are a pair of K3's that I use on CW/SSB/RTTY but for casual listening I still enjoy flipping the antenna switch over and listening from the 1000MP.

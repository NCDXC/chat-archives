After I do some more research on the tubes and availability, I'm pretty well
set on the ACOM 1500. I watched some Youtube videos on it and the tuning is
pretty cool. It's as close to "automatic" as a manual tuner can get. My
antennas are all well below the 3:1 SWR threshold that they require to
operate - where I would be able to avoid having to add an outboard antenna
tuner. 

I wouldn't have considered this if the prices hadn't dropped like they have.
I almost think the skyrocketing tube prices might have scared a lot of
others away - since I generally like to have a spare on hand - and at
current prices, it was making a decent tube amp a bad investment. In fact -
that's why I went with the KPA-500 in the first place. I said "to heck with
tubes" . . .

On 8/15/2014 7:30 AM, Bob Lanning via Chat wrote:
> So when considering an amplifier and its tube compliment - what is the 
> truth?

K6XX gave a great presentation on the "clean signals" aspects of this to
NCCC this past winter. It's on my website. k9yc.com/publish/htm

Two things that Bob observed are that tube amps are cleaner than solid state
for the same power/budget, and that an amp is cleaner at less than its rated
output level. By "cleaner" he means less IMD, which means less clicks and
splatter.

I was discussing this at the picnic with a couple of guys who had designed
power amps on their job, and they went farther, to say that some devices
have a "sweet spot" where it will be cleaner.

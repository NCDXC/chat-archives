> I’ve noticed a trend recently in FT8 protocol where stations will call out of the blue skipping the TX1 and just hit immediately you with a TX2 signal report. I suppose this is an attempt to shorten the exchange even more for faster QSO rates. Maybe this is what happened to you? 

If you double-click on the TX1 button, it will change the sequence and respond to a CQ with TX2 instead of TX1, shortening the QSO time. 

Buuuut:

When you are running, there is a pull-down menu that allows you to select which station to call if multiple stations respond to your CQ. One choice is “First Decoded”, which will take the first in the list. The other choice is “Farthest Distance”. I usually select that one, so I maximize my DX, rather than working locals or others who happen to decode first. “Farthest Distance” is computed by the distance between your grid square and the caller’s grid square (similar to Makrothen RTTY, Stew Perry or other distance-based contests). But if someone sends TX2 instead of TX1, you don’t get their grid square, and the algorithm will NEVER select that station to call, unless he is the only one, or everyone calling is sending TX2 first. 

I have seen this when S+P’ing. I’ll notice that only the “TX1 callers” are being called, so I will change to sending TX1 instead of TX2. (Again, double-click on TX1 to toggle the function.)

I wouldn’t mind an option to call “weakest first” when running, in case some station is only decodable for a short time, and might later fade out in the QSB. It gives the “little pistols” a better shot.

The ops on this expedition aren't bad at all, particularly on the low bands: in fact, they're fantastic. But, there are other issues.
PT&T is fairly rare: 16 or 32 depending on your criteria. The primary issue is probably that this one is really a "specialty" DXpedition. It is well known that George and Tamas (AA7JV and HA7RY) are low-band specialists, and they are good. Fred (PY2XB) is a 50 mHz specialist; he may be the best in the world. That leaves just one op to "specialize" in all of the other bands and modes, more or less.
If you look at the comments on the spotting networks, you'll see the gang questioning about their being on 6M, or QSYing to 160M just before the PT0S sunset -- looking for JA during the Greyline lift. The purpose was announced, but maybe it wasn't what people expected.
We have all become accustomed to 20-30 operators activating all bands at all times, all the while spreading out the audience throughout the spectrum. This just isn't happening on this one.
In the end, SP&P might be a bit too rare for this type of expedition. More frustrated people crammed onto just a few bands plus some operating faux pax leads to what we're hearing.

I've only been in the pile ups for 2.5 days, but from what I have seen, the pile up behavior is off the charts bad!
I think this stems from several reasons, first this entity is sufficiently rare that there is some desperation among some to get them. Two, there have been logging issues an errors, which adds to the sense of desperation.
While I am loathe to criticize any DXpedition, there are some operating techniques in play that tend to add to that desperation. Many times, I have seen the PT0S Operators have a massive pile up only to QSY to another band or mode just leaving the pile up hanging.
Some of the Ops also seem to clearly give preference to South America/Africa rather than the furthest regions. I noticed that DQRM really picked up the other day when the Op left a large pile up to QSY to six meters where upon he began to work South America.
These are just my thoughts,

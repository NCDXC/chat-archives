GPS satellites as far as I know, don't stop ever sending data streams and are not fixed in location (low earth orbit).
He is at a science research station, internet is available (he uploads logs, no?).
I made my own Pi/GPS based NTP server, it was neither expensive or difficult and serves my LAN.  (IF you can get a Pi these days.)
Maybe it is as simple as his NTP client isn't on the local network, except upload times.  Windows NTP client is badly broken (on purpose for MS needs), which he's likely using too.  [Allows the OS to be MINUTES off accurate.]
In any case, it's something for us to be aware of; too far off and decodes won't happen.  "Time Fudge" is a simple solution.
Hopefully, the Bouvet team have plans for this issue, perhaps even using the new (EX$PEN$IVE) Starlink dish designed for low angle, high snow cold weather.  It's rarely the big things that happen, but the collection of small failures.

Not much different than HF pathing, except 6M has occasional ducting.  The path is actually rarely straight.

This morning, I listened to px0ff on 20 CW.  Direct short path, they were wimpy (and they were clearly pointed at EU as usual).  When I turned to EU to see the spread of the callers, px0ff came WAY up; easy copy; path was now 45 degrees off short path.

It's the size, location and angles of the 'clouds' we use.  They are almost never a flat surface (why I refer to them as clouds instead of layers) and once into that layer, any direction for that path, can happen.

6M DX is a rare beast this far north, but I do have the Falklands and some EU/Asia, one Africa confirmed on 6M.  Openings are typically 30 minutes average, often much less; you have to BE at the station when it happens.  SoPac/Oceana has been elusive so far.

I'm going to chime in here even though it's not really on subject.  Please can the NCDXC consider a "Rookie of the Year" award?  I think it's an excellent motivation for new members, and it's also a means to encourage communication between new members and existing members.  "Elmer of the Year" is another good one.  (But for another 2 cents I agree with some of the others that these awards should be presented at a local meeting at not in front of 800+ convention-goers.)
 
Congratulations to this year's award winners!  And thank you to all the volunteers who worked hard to organize an incredible convention.

On Mon, Apr 22, 2013 at 6:03 PM, <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:
Andreas and Rusty, I respectfully disagree with you that we should not be giving this award.
 
This is one area that I wish the NCDXC was more like the NCCC which many of us are also members. The NCCC gives out awards like crazy!  Some of them are based on contest scores and some of them are subjective. They seem to do it well and they seem to get it right.
 
Eliminating what could be a prestigious and sought after award would be a step backwards, to how the NCDXC was not too long ago, where it seemed like there was little passion or interest in the NCDXC and the club was possibly dying.
 
I think part of our mistake was that the definition of our award was not well defined. Some of us thought of it as a "best" award, while others thought of it as more of a "people's choice" award. 
 
I'm going to propose to the new board of directors that we keep the award and clearly define it. We can add other awards such as the best small/solo DXpedition, and others that fit the deeds of the DXers out there.
 
I am going to propose that each year, during our February meeting, that the topic of the meeting is a review of DXpeditions of the previous year. We will review the statistics as they are available and hear each others views on the various DXpeditions. We will review the criteria for each class of award and then we will vote.
 
As the NCCC knows, it is fun to give awards and it does not cost much. It is a way of saying thank you and we should all do that more often to those that bring us fun.
 
 
Respectfully,
 
Paul N6PSE

Hi All,

I’m in Fiji right now with the advance team for the KH1 2018 trip.  We loaded the boat yesterday and the boat set sail for Pago-Pago today about 2PM (Saturday June 16th).  I will now fly to Pago-Pago, where I will catch up with the boat and the rest of the KH1 team.  We plan to depart Pago-Pago on June 20th, arriving on June 26th or June 27th.

We shipped 3 tons (US) of equipment to Nadi. This included tents, antennas, coax, radios, IT equipment, and all the things needed to build a village from nothing, and survive for ten days, handing out KH1 QSOs.  Many items were purchased in Fiji and stored in the NAI’A (our boat) warehouse till we arrived.  This included generators, lights, gas cans, shovels, table and chairs.  Stuff it was easier to buy in Fiji and save the shipping cost.  The NAI’A also constructed a latrine for us.  Interestingly, Fiji isn’t an island that pursues “camping” as a leisure activity. This resulted in many items that couldn’t be bought in Fiji - tents, camping stoves, coffee makers, outdoor tent lights, cots, coolers.  So, we had to buy camping items and ship to Nadi.

We’ve had a lot of worry over the boat - the NAI’A out of Lautoka, especially after the problems the 3Y0Z had with their boat.  The NAI’A is a live-aboard dive boat, and hasn’t been on a DXpedition before.  We had visited it in February, and found it acceptable.  Our confidence in the NAI’A was complete this week.  All the equipment fit, just a we planned it.  All the planning and arrangements with the crew were confirmed this week.  No surprises.  They did everything they said they would.  We got a chance to ride the boat for a quick trip in and out of the harbor, talk extensively with the captain and the owner.  We also got to meet all the crew, including the cook, and talk with them about this trip.  We also watched the crew install two new outboard motors on the Skiffs, and inspect the third new skiff the owner had bought for this trip.  Again, no surprises.

What really completed confidence for me was listening to Rob (the NAI’A owner), and George, AA7JV talk “shop”, or boating.  Both have extensive experience sailing and boating.  Both Rob and George came away feeling this crew and boat can do the job for us. 

Our biggest concern is possible rough surf at Baker.  If it’s too rough, getting on and off the island may be difficult, possibly denying access for up to three days at a time.  Rob has talked with the NOAA boat captain that visited Baker about three years ago, to transport USF&WS biologists.  Based on what Rob learned, he bought the new skiff to better ride thru the surf at the coral reef.

We have a blog active, written by Marty, KC1CWF - www.kh7z.net.  On that site, you can see photos, news, and a link to the tracker - it’s active now - you can watch the progress of the NAI’A as it sails to Pago-Pago. Full details are on our main web page www.baker2018.net.

In about 12-14 days, you’ll hear us.  W6 should have a front row seat to this show.  Don’t think we won’t work you, if we ask you to stand by for EU.  You can always find us on 20m.  We will also have the best 160m system we can put in place, along with experienced 160m operators. Get your station setup for FT8, as well will make extensive use of the FOX/Hound mode of operation.

Lastly, please donate. 

CU in the log, GL, and 73

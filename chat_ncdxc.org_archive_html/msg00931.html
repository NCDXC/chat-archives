I am using the free version of Ham Radio Deluxe (5.22) otherwise known as HRD. It works fine for me. The free version of HRD was sold to a small group of US based Hams who have started a company and sell HRD version 6 with support for $60.

 

I'm doubtful that I will ever upgrade to HRD version 6, in-fact I am considering moving to DX Lab's DX-keeper. HRD version 6 is in Beta and on the reflector I can see that it is quite buggy. I can also see that some of the exchanges between the paying user base and the company are quite testy. This has caused me enough doubts that I won't consider upgrading to HRD version 6 at this point.

 

For DXpeditions, we use the free version of Win-test, however I don't recommend that for day to day logging.

Steve,

There are many logging programs, so take your time and really think about
what you are trying to accomplish. I used DXBase for many years and liked it
because it made transferring log date to LoTW almost seamless. However,
since 2009, I felt the program was a little old and didn't have a number of
options that appeared in other logging programs. Since I also subscribed to
the DXBase reflector, I knew I wasn't alone. So, I started looking once
again. I tried DX4WIN and found it did an OK job, but still didn't have all
the options I was looking for.

After a number of recommendations, mainly K6MM, I took a look at DXLab. Like
many software packages, the documentation at first appears to be
overwhelming. However, once I understood how the program was set up, I
learned the help files were very easy to use and normally answered my
question. Best of all, the author, AA6YQ, does a fabulous job of responding
to questions (both from users and potential users). By the way, AA6YQ is a
former NCDXC member. When I had a problem setting something up, I don't
think I waited more than 30 minutes for a reply. Otherwise, usual
turnaround is around a couple of hours.

I now use the entire DXLab suite so it pulls the frequency from the radio
when I work someone, plus it tells me what bands and modes a country (sorry
entity) is worked, confirmed or needed on. You can run all of the programs
for DXLab, or just a few. It's your choice and won't hurt anything if you
decide to add them later. I have not experienced any bugs, and the program
is kept up to date as suggestions are made.

DXLab handles LOTW perfectly. Once I decide to update my LOTW account at the
ARRL, I simply make one or two mouse clicks. DXLab handles all the ADIF
conversions, logs onto my LOTW account uploads the data, and then logs off.
Same thing happens when I want to sync up DXLab with any new QSL's I've
received through LOTW. As easy as DXBase was, DXLab is much easier.

For contests, I switched from WriteLog over to N1MM. In either case, after a
contest is completed, data exported from either contest programs can be
imported into DXLab without any trouble.

Good luck on your quest. If you decide to use DXLab, I'm sure many of us
here on CHAT can give you some help; but none will be as good as AA6YQ. :-)

73,
Ross
K6GFJ

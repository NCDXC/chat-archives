> On May 13, 2025, at 8:57 AM, Greg De Hoogh <greg.n6pm@gmail.com> wrote:
> 
> I'd be interested in hearing from folks with more experience than me, as well as from Yuris, about the possibility that asymmetrical propagation played a part in the apparent slighting of West Coast stations by ZS8W. The night I worked him on 40m FT8 with my 43' vertical, a number of other folks here in SoCal who have 40m beams failed to get him. Is it possible that the primary propagation path from here to Marion Island was in a different direction than the path from there to here, thus resulting in his not hearing them even though they heard him just fine?
> 

You may be correct. I worked him on 40m FT8, but my antenna is a full-size Extended Double Zepp. It has quite a bit of gain (about the same as a 2-element Yagi), but the main lobes are broadside to the antenna (bi-diretional), and I have it oriented for best propagation to US/EU and ZL/VK (Northeast/Southwest). That is exactly opposite to what one would expect to use for ZS8, who was Southeast (short path) or Northwest (long path). It didn’t seem to matter.

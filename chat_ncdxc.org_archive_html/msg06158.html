I think it's weird that the NCDXC Board has recently eliminated the Club's own DXpedition of the Year Award (without membership input) but is now sending Club Funds to a Website in EU to sponsor their awards that were announced months ago.

It's like the Board wants the PR but does not trust the membership to make an informed choice. Their is incredible knowledge, depth and experience in the NCDXC and the members can improve and refine the process that was started several years ago rather than throwing "our" award in the trash or sending a sack of money to Europe to put your name on their award.

Paul N6PSE

Fellow members...A few things from last Thursdays club meeting in Redwood City and, by the way, the site worked well.  Andreas reports the Echo Link for our repeater is in final "smoke test" so expect that soon.  The Members of the Year Award is here announced--Mike and Carolyn Gavin...if any two members deserve it for tireless service to the club, they do.  Please congratulate them when next you see them...they are retiring from the meeting raffle as soon as a volunteer steps forward. The job is easy, the internet awaits.   The DXer of the Year is John, K6MM. The committee received many votes for a variety of folks but John is it.  Should be fun watching him present himself the award in Visalia.
 
 John, K6MM asks that you contact him if you'd like to sit at our Visalia exhibit  table---no duties, just sit for a bit, be wise...and go back to the show.

I've commented before how this club sometimes funds expeditions--the NCDXF, for example or our recent Tromelin grant. Other times we provide technical support--Stu, K6TU or  Rich, K6YR as a pilot,  for example. We are also the DX in many instances with Paul, N6PSE and his many ventures or George, N6NKT or K6MM John.   Part of further broadening the clubs image, globally, is the recent board decision to fund the awards given to the top 3 DXpeditions as voted by the annual DX WORLD survey.   Last time over 4000 votes from 123 entities were cast...the board was impressed with the scope and magnitude of that turnout. We are finalizing the details---our logo and name on the awards with the appropriate "Award Donated By" type wording---and our logo and grant visible and prominent, etc. on the site page.  We will be the only donor and as such, given the number of users of DX WORLD, gain some positive global publicity.  Please note---we are sponsoring for PR purposes only and we are NOT  including this club or it's members in the vote or the outcome in any way---we reserve the right to vote individually or collectively as we see fit. It is PR, nothing more.

We are finally getting some candidates for the upcoming election---if you have any interest in making a difference, contact me, our Secretary Mike or Andreas---now is the time.   JIM

A reminder to anyone having interest that I'm repeating the talk I did at Pacificon for the REDXA meeting tonight. It's at Cattlemans just north of Petaluma. The talk focuses on how to get on 160M without a lot of real estate, how 160M antennas work and which work best, how soil conductivity affects 160M, how radial and counterpoise systems contribute to the antenna performance, how to make those work without a lot of space, and so on.
Nothing in my talk is new stuff of my own, it's all summarizing the excellent work of others, including W8JI, K3LC, NC0B (Rob Sherwood), and most notably N6LF's excellent and extensive work on radial systems, and K2AV's folded counterpoise.
Most important, it's all very practical stuff, no heavy theory, but emphasis on basic concepts that can be used to think about your QTH in terms of what is possible that helps your signal, what matters, and what doesn't. 

73, Jim K9YC

Team

 

FWIW, I don’t care what others have or don’t have to work DX…..if I may paraphrase an old Marine Corps Gunny Sergeant whom I admire and respect:

 

“We don’t focus on what we don’t have…we focus on what we do have and what we are going to do with it”.

 

From the time I was a Novice operator as a teenager in 1960 with a wimpy pitiful station using a WWII ARC 5 receiver from a B-17 and a home made transmitter using 6L6 tubes suspended upside down in a large coffee can filled with  water (long story) feeding  a long wire antenna out the back window of our home in Sacramento to a small tree in our back yard I have enjoyed working DX. Over the years I have continuously improved my station equipment, operating skills and QTH locations. I continue to do so as an old fart (over 70).

 

I am still having fun chasing DX and I frankly don’t care (and never have cared) what  other people are using or not using (other than to learn from them and improve from their knowledge and experience). I care about what I am using and whether I am increasing my operating skills and my station to my own satisfaction and whether I am still having fun. I am still having fun.

 

The DX awards and contest awards I have earned over the years have meaning to me in the context that I played by the rules and for me that is enough.

 

This is probably the last solar cycle for me and as a prospective old fart SK I am very thankful for our wonderful hobby, the many friends I have met through ham radio and all the enjoyment I and others have enjoyed because of it so far.

 

I will *never* regret not spending any time at all getting worked up over whether somebody has or has not a better station or QTH than I have or how the “rules” may unfairly provide an advantage to someone else or some other location. I simply want to work DX and really enjoy it…and I do.

 

Someone once said….don’t get into a seething rage over the inequity and moral outrage of there being four balls but only three strikes in playing base ball…if you want to enjoy the game and play the game …learn the rules….play by the rules…or find another game…..

 

So IMHO have fun out there and just enjoy it……while you can.  J

 

73, Ted, K6XN

 

PS If you don’t like your pitiful station or QTH or whatever….improve it…like everybody else….or go on DXpeditions…or be a guest operator at better stations and QTHs…there are a lot of *really* nice ham radio brothers and sisters who will welcome you and help you and let you operate from their station or the outstanding club stations they have built up and have access to.

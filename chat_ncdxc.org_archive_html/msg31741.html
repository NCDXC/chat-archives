John,

You're quite welcome and I share your pain...  SteppIR is great, until it presents a problem; which it WILL at some (inopportune) point.  (Yeah yeah, tell me of any antenna that works for years without SOME maintenance required.  You can't!)

If you have (access to) a VNA, sweep the line and antenna before/after the fault condition.  That tells you where, a key data point.

Did the coax get pulled on and barely connects, inadequate at QRO?

If you have a polyphaser or morgan or similar protector in the coax line, I would look there TODAY in case they're in a semi-faulted condition after 300 watts...  Yes, that would be high on the 'weird crap-o-meter' but not impossible.  Replace or bypass them for testing.  

Take out the *#^^@!! wasp nests too in that box.  Is EACH connector TIGHT?  Dry too?  (Weather exposed here are wrapped in 'slightly stretched' so it binds and shrinks into place, overlapped Scotch 88 tape, then butyl [coax seal], belt and suspender approach but leaves the connectors, dry and pristine.  Tape alone is not adequate, what leaks in, stays in.)

Then bring it down if no success.  Test the feedline with a dummy load.  Sweep it again too.

My suspicion is that it's more than one issue that you'll find, including the one causing the SWR fault.  Mine was not built correctly (estate sale), I was ignorant of that; plus I found several things needing correction plus updates.  AND cleaning up the bowl of spaghetti style wiring (estate sale too and 'good enough') and other housekeeping.  I now have a fully functioning 1:1 from the bottom of 80M through 6M antenna.  WORTH the time and pains and in better shape for next repair time.  (It's still a single point of failure for the station).

That it takes an hour without retuning to recover, strongly hints heat is the cause or marginal component (not movement); the why of the heat is the concern (if you can follow THAT rabbit trail).  Failing brush contacts would be high on that short list of things to fail.  (9/10 times, it's something simple and stoopid so it's easily missed.)

I would think that it's a failed/failing part that you want replaced before the second half of the DXpedition season arrives (and winter, which varies by location; snow is a deal breaker here).  Git 'er dun no later than Dec (DXped takes that time off normally too).

Which part, where is the problem.  Weather now drives the urgency.  Working while wearing snow shoes, tiresome; muddy shoes get heavy.

GL John,

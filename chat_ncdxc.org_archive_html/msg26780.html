On Fri, Sep 23, 2022 at 5:34 PM WB6POT - Peter <wb6pot@gmail.com> wrote:
  1. W/VE QTH: A multiplier of one (1) for each continental US state (48), The District of Columbia and each Canadian call area (14) contacted on each band. Please use only official U.S. Postal Service abbreviations to identify states (e.g., Michigan = MI, Massachusetts = MA, Ohio = OH, The District of Columbia = DC). Note:  Alaska (KL7) and Hawaii (KH6) are counted as country multipliers only and not as state multipliers. Canadian call areas (14 total) are as follows: NB (VE9), NS (VE1), QC (VE2), ON (VE3), MB (VE4), SK (VE5), AB (VE6), BC (VE7), NWT (VE8), NF (VO1), LB (VO2), NU (VY0), YT (VY1), PEI (VY2).

From my cheat sheets…

 

CQWW DX RTTY

For this contest everyone works everyone.  The exchange is 599 plus CQ zone number of the station location (e.g., 599 14). Stations in the continental USA and Canada also send QTH (e.g., 599 05 MA). QTH for USA is 48 States + DC with other US areas counting as DX Countries (e.g., AK, HI, AS, GU, PR, VI, rest of PAC Division).  QTH for Canada is 14 Provs (Not RAC Sections).  Remember the JAs have weird band limits for this contest and you need to work a lot of them.

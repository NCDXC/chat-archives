Very sad news about my friend and neighbor, Jim Abraham W6EB.

I affectionately referred to the hill he lived on as "Mt. Abraham".

Yes, he did fly big birds for PanAm, as did his father before him.

He was also an electronics technician at one time for SLAC.

He handled the raffle at the IDXC for many years, and was a mainstay on the Los Altos Hills Emergency Communications Committee.

He was instrumental in bringing a two meter repeater to Los Altos Hills.

Jim leaves behind his wife Siren and three fine children.  Jim was a caring family man.

We worked on antenna projects together, and he was very helpful in getting my amp to work when it balked.

A big loss to the amateur radio community.

RIP Jim.

73,  Ed  AJ6V

I received the following today regarding Jim Abraham W6EB.

From: Dru Anderson KG6LAD, Los Altos Hills Emergency Communications Committee, Co-chair
 
It is with deep sadness that I bring the information that our ham colleague, Jim Abraham W6EB, passed away Thursday afternoon with his family at his home after a long illness. Jim was among those of us who were original charter members in 2001 of Los Altos Hills Emergency Communications Committee. He was among the early officers of the committee. Jim was very active, as well, in other community service, and served as a Los Altos Hills planning commissioner for several years. 
 
We will all miss his active involvement and many contributions of time, service, and excellent technical support to the group and to the area of emergency ham communications capabilities for the town. 
 
Our thoughts and sincere condolences are with Jim's wife, Siren, and her family. 
 
Services will be private with family.
 
In lieu of flowers, the family prefers that remembrances be to one's favorite charity in Jim's behalf. 
 
Our respect from the ECC community to our colleague, Silent Key W6EB.

Rest in Peace, Jim.
 
Dru KG6LAD, on behalf of the team

On a personal note Jim was one of my best friends. He a was a member of NCCC and NCDXC. Jim was a real fighter. Amazing 5+ year ordeal, lived longer than anyone would have predicted. At the end, a recently diagnosed brain cancer ended his life. He's no longer suffering after a gallant and painful long ordeal.

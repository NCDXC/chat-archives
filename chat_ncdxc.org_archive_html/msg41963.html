On 2/9/2026 2:12 PM, Rick Bates, NK7I via Chat wrote:

From a RECEIVING standpoint:

  • SuperFox requires NINE dB more signal to allow decoding (stops at < -16 report when a -25 dB single stream is decoded); width has a cost and it's a big one.
  • Users cannot narrow the bandwidth to decode a weaker signal (as they can with F/H or single) because of the extended width of the SF signal
  • Spacing has ZERO effect on improvements (except in a lab, perhaps).
  • With a couple out of sequence callers on the right tones (or WSPR), the SF packet is JUST as fragile to decode (it can be interfered with just as easily).
  • It causes the SAME number of repeats because the degraded signal (the 9 dB hit) makes copy more challenging.
  • The smaller stations or those with high noise, can't work the station using SF but can with < 3 streams; consistently (real life).
  • Polar distortion IS affected by either mode in FT8 of any mode (I've HEARD the tones but the path distortion denies decoding); where watery CW still works out.
  • SF has NEVER reached the full potential for max stations at once, because of these flaws (with RARE exceptions for moments).

SF doesn't work all that well in real life conditions.

If it were as you said, SF would be used more often but EVERY team that has implemented it, quickly returned to single or f/h streams because they suddenly lost the pileups (the rates tanked).  It's ONLY effective for the bigger stations where the signal IS LOUD.

SF requires the DX team TO BE LOUD and most are not, much of the time (lower, lesser antennas and limited output).  A big gun running a special event from a large station, it could work; normal DX team, not so much.

That being said, running more than 3 streams causes a similar signal strength hit which again makes it more challenging for the average station.  Running 5+ streams, is so ignorant it just boggles the mind; it's a bold statement that the little guys are unworthy.  (BRAGGING online that only 25 watts was used WHILE running 5 streams; draw your own conclusions on any wisdom there; the little guys had zero hope.)

Since DX pileups rarely get smaller when there is adequate signal, use of SF has been equally rare to none so the greater wisdom is to NOT use SF mode (or accept you only work the big guns, which also never thin out and the little guys just lose out).

Multistream at 3 < streams is optimal for most stations while almost no team (US or not) has used F/H in over a year (because they prefer MSHV which comes with it's own significant issues).

On 2/9/2026 12:14 PM, Gabriel AJ6X via Chat wrote:
A wise DX operator would switch between single stream and SuperFox to maximize the QSO rate:

  • Single stream FT8 works best on difficult paths.

  • SuperFox has about 3dB to 4dB penalty compared to single stream FT8, but allows multiple QSOs in parallel (up to 9) and is often more robust (twice the frequency separation between adjacent tones).

  • Using 2, 3, 4, or 5 parallel streams will drop the signal by 6, 9.5, 12 or 14 dB; just throwing away link budget and potential QSOs with no gain.

Why?

  1. From a strictly technical point of view, SuperFox is combining multiple messages in one package, which allows much more efficient FEC (forward error correction);

  2. The extended frequency (and presumably some sort of M-ary / Q-ary modulation with 127 tones instead of FT8’s 8 tone CPFSK) also allows improved performance at low SNR;

  3. Tone spacing is doubled in SuperFox, improving robustness over difficult paths (e.g., polar paths)

  4. SuperFox can work pileups off faster, allowing switching to single streams for weaker stations. Yes it’s annoying to not be heard with Superfox, but it will get the big station out of the way much faster. People often confuse who causes a problem with who is trying to solve it. And it’s also a nice example of delayed gratification paying off.

  5. The actual signal reports for FT8 and Superfox are irrelevant here, we are comparing apples to pears.

Just my 2 cents.

I worked them on one FT8 slot on 20m so I would know how to use the dxpedition mode. It is amazing how FT8 helped people get Baker in their log in pretty mediocre conditions.

For me it isn’t quite as fun or feels the same as phone or CW modes but for the folks that have very small apartment/condo/rental stations it really is helpful if you are interested in DX.  Before I moved to a good qth only a few months back my QSO portfolio was like 60% FT8 in a rental home.  Now it is much less than that. The type of qth / very restricted antenna capabilities seem to me to have some correlation to whether people use or even like the mode or not. 

For many of my 6m big gun east coast friends who sunk a ton of cash into large 6m arrays or big boom 6m antennas on 100 foot tall towers they are pretty aggravated that smaller stations are racking up the 6m entities like crazy this season with FT8. I am not sure this is justified but that is how they feel and I am not here to invalidate people’s feelings. I am more neutral about it to be honest, to each their own, but those guys certainly have strong feelings and can be pretty nasty about it. To me at the end of the day this is a hobby and one of a few that I have and my focus and worries are elsewhere. I don’t think I could get that upset about it to be honest. 

On 6m, I worked five new European band entities using FT8 in like two days earlier in the Es season from EL09 and then decided that was enough for this season for that mode. I hope as conditions improve in SC25 we get some F2 E/W openings on 6m and then we can work Europe, Africa and Middle East on phone with a wet noodle like everyone talks about in previous good solar cycles.

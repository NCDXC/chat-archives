Hi Hank,

I didn't see this question answered, so... in FT4 or FT8 mode, almost all of the spots you're going to see on this list (not the spotting lists, YMMV*) will be the VFO frequency.  (I think a simple typo was within the answer given.)

The ONE exception will be IF the DX unwisely chooses a tone BELOW 300 Hz. (a really bad idea, self-defeating).  In that case because most radios have a rolloff (filter) at 300 Hz which that lowers their signal/audio so they won't decode as readily unless the STATION IS REALLY LOUD.  So you would have to compensate (move the VFO down 100 or 200 cycles) to bring their tone/s up into the passband.  Then if they are in Fox/Hound, you have to remember the offset and add that back in so your tone is 1000 Hz or higher [1000 Hz PLUS your downward offset].

* SOME station spot reports will add the DX tone to the VFO setting; or THEIR tone to the VFO setting (same thing on CW/Phone spots).  Neither is helpful other than to get you in the right ballpark.

Ever since digital VFO became the norm, hams tend to migrate to the .000 values on their VFO; but not all.  It keeps things interesting.

e.g. A few DX will choose, perhaps 14.090.500 on their VFO (up 500 from the 'default' .000) so one gets to figure that out too (or your 1000 Hz tone, may not be high enough)...  Those who catch on quicker, tend to be logged sooner too.

Rick nk7i

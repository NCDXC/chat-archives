As many of you have read and a couple have seen first hand I have a small antenna farm here at the rental in foothills east San Jose.  Yesterday I decided to move my G7FEK homebrew 1/4 wave marconi that I use on 80m primarily.  I have the antenna on a MFJ push up fiberglass pole now attached to the chimney above the playroom.  Last night and this morning I was making a lot of great new and not so new CW contacts on 80m.  The change in position of the antenna has so far been pretty major.  I have only run barefoot to this point and will see how it plays with QRO.  This weekend I will also be installing a mini yagi up on the chimney.  I am keeping the 20m monoband hex beam but I am going to try this other small yagi for awhile.  Before too long I will convince my landlord to let me put up a SteppIR I am sure.  The only reason I haven’t gone that direction yet is because I don’t know where we are going to end up in Silicon Valley and don’t want to make that much of an investment.  My wife seems enamored with Willow Glen (because of a public Montessori school) and I have been there and it even looks worse as a ham radio location.  At least in this QTH there is no overhead powerlines so it is relatively quiet.

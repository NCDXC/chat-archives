I am sure when SC25 comes and we finally see SFI breach 100 heading towards 200 then we can get back on non digi modes again in earnest. I worked a bunch of new ones on 6m and now up to 53 DXCC worked and 48 confirmed. I can’t say it is as much fun working them but it is definitely a lot easier than working them standard modes. 

I am really looking forward to the sun showing us that we have a solar cycle actually moving like what was in the latest DXer.  I am sure when we can hear Abdallah on 20m at S9+40 again in the evenings we will be back having fun using SSB and CW again.

When I was at Belton hamfest the expert amp guys say FT8 has had a big impact in keeping their sales going. If it keeps these cottage industry boys going during the solar minimum I am cool with it.  For me I don’t get as jazzed about making a new 6m DX band entity on FT8 versus CW or SSB but in these super lame conditions it is what I have seen in the past month plus. I have not made one new 6m band entity during the normal season in standard modes because EU DX here has gotten no better than -11 for me in STX.  

Then again I haven’t been on much as I would like with so much crazy work. I am super slammed!!!  Look forward to my next trip out to San Jose to see my clients and many of you in person in 2021.

I enjoy chasing DX on FT8. Many of the CW stalwarts (W6OAT, W0YK) are also very active on FT8. 

FT8 is a very visual mode. The more you pay attention and focus on what is being worked and the more you learn how the application works (either WSJT-X or JTDX) you will have more success and more fun. Skill and finesse work much better than brute force on FT8. 

FT8 has shown me that there is propagation when you would not otherwise expect it. 

It is all about having fun with radio,

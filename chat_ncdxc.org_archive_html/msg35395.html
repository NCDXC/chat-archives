Aren't unattended robo contacts against DXCC rules?

 

Yet another reason why I have little use for ARRL. And how would they verify whether he was sleeping or not? Even if he IS sleeping, his quarters are the same as his shack, so he is technically there. If his amplifier kicked out, it would probably wake him up. 

 

If I doze off for a few minutes during a digital contest, do I have to invalidate the Qs made while my eyes were closed? I have been told by Andy AE6Y that his brain sometimes goes awry towards the end of a 48-hour contest. If he makes a bunch of Qs while spaced out, such that he can’t remember hitting the buttons, do those Qs count? Do I get a ticket if I drive through a red light at 3 a.m. with no one around? 

 

I saw this weekend that George AA7JV was operating CQ WPX from his boat, docked on Wallis Island. Since his boat was docked, according to ARRL, he can’t claim he is Maritime Mobile (/MM). Since the transmitter and antenna were on the boat and not on land, he can’t claim he is on Wallis Island. He ended up using the callsign FW/AA&JV/M (mobile, not maritime mobile), which is a pretty difficult call to use in a contest. All because of ARRL rules.

 

Rules that can’t be verified are meaningless.

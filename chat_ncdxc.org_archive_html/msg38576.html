That was almost ten years ago now... it's hard to keep track these days (it was a couple years before I escaped CA, almost 8 years ago now).  

I was part of the pileup, about ten minutes too late (on a puny station).  I tried, but little hope (and it didn't last long at all).

A buddy over the hill in Washoe valley got the card; I arranged for a card checker (Corvette road trip AND a free lunch in Tahoe!).  That short window was pushed by all parties as a 'trial event' but those that followed with permission and arrived were suddenly expelled AND lost hundreds of thousands in gear and shipping expenses (still sitting in a Chinese warehouse)... N Korea changed their mind at the last moment.  True to their (bad) form.

One area that I would like to see closed off, is any that limits to a specific geographic area (like the satellite over the Middle East).  Fun, yep; but it should not count for DX awards since it only covers some specific areas.

Which pretty much explains one reason 60M doesn't count; it's not available world wide; it's not equitable (a key point).

But to answer your question; some cases are self-evident.  The wobbly leadership of N Korea does not offer any hope of anything in the civil arena, ham radio included.  Any stability is not evident (although a Chinese/Russian team might be able to pull it off depending on what week the event landed on).

Likewise, any place in the S China sea would be too deadly to attempt and could have worldwide ramifications (trigger a war) when the reactions happened.  A place with multiple nations of warships present; not the best place to be the DX.

Set them on the sidelines (inactive) until the politics or ownership changes.  That allows for (N Korea style) sudden events which are documented.  Then if they go silent for another generation; back on the inactive again.

Others deserve 'inactive' as well; they (Bouvet, dangerous and expensive; but I'd work them if someone was there) just aren't worth the risk OR the stupid high costs.  Just as every flea speck island CAN be worked; most never will be; some should not be attempted; basic ROI equations.  (What was the per QSO cost, of Bouvet?  Was it ultimately worth the losses and risks?)  "Because it's there" is a rather empty statement.

Mostly, worldwide, those in control (politics, special interest groups) need to learn that saying no to something that is mostly harmless, does not push their particular cause.  Rigid things break more often than those things that have some give.

Not that it'll happen soon; it's not the world we grew up in anymore.  It's gotten colder and darker; more lacking in hope every week and not improving for a long time now.

But we do our part and keep on trying via radio, to better things.  And that is one place to put some hope; ground level nudges can turn the tide.

73,
Rick nk7i

Regarding DXCC rules such as proposed by my good friend W1SRD relating to entities that are QRT for 20 years or whatever:

Unfortunately, its not that simple.  For example, 5 years ago or so, I came home and l went to my shack just in case some juicy DX appeared on the cluster.  And to my great shock, there it was:  North Korea spotted by only Asian stations  I quickly fired up the rig and again to my great shock there was NO PILEUP.  Here goes, WFWL and I was the first station in the Western Hemisphere to work North Korea in over 20 years.  Just dumb luck, or, as W1GKK would say "The secret to the Honor Roll is being there".  I posted a spot to the club reflector, and a few dozen of the chosen were also able to work the DPRK.  AFAIK, only a relative handful of stateside stations worked him.  By the way, I have Dom's QSL card.

Now, does that mean that the 20 year drought has ended, therefore if there was a 20 year rule in effect, that this entity would be reactivated for DXCC.  That doesn't seem fair just because I and a few buddies got lucky.  But exactly how to you draw that line? Does the fact that he worked 100's or maybe a few thousand of stations in Asia satisfy the rule?

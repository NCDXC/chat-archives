On May 2, 2018, at 2:42 PM, Bill Richter via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Hi, folks.

Here's a (maybe not so) strange request: Has anyone had any experience in dealing with PG&E and Comcast with regards to easements on property here in Santa Clara County?

Not sure about Santa Clara County (I’m in Santa Cruz County), but a general easement for utility companies to place necessary equipment on private property, and to come onto that property to inspect and maintain it is pretty universal. Here in rural, unincorporated Santa Cruz County, I have a few utility poles on my land, carrying both PG+E electric lines and Frontier telephone. The easement is recorded in my deed. From time to time, utility workers come out to install equipment, clear overhanging branches, and the like.

Up here, they KNOW to always knock on the door and notify the owner when they come on the property. Otherwise, they are likely to be looking at the business end of a 12-ga shotgun.

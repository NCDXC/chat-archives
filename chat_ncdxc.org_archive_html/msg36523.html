Hi Will,
If you are seeing lines every X KHz, then there is a SMPS, (Switching Mode Power Supply), active someplace. Locate that SMPS, and your problem will go away, if you can kill it. See: 

https://www.nk7z.net/i-have-rfi-now-what-locating-it/

for more on how to locate the RFI locally...
With regards to adding chokes, if you have the choke at the feedpoint of your antenna, it might help in reducing the RFI, it depends on how the RFI is being introduced. If the RFI is entering your antenna system by the outside conductor on the shield, (three paths in a coax cable, center, inside of the shield, and outside of the shield), then yes it will help, if it is being picked up by the antenna, (not the outside path on the coax shield), then it probably won't help.
Adding a good quality choke, is almost always a good idea. I run my cables underground, so I don't really need one for RFI suppression, but I run one anyway, (to decouple my feedline from my antenna), at the feedpoint of every antenna, and another at the entry point of the cables to the home in the lightening protection entry panel.

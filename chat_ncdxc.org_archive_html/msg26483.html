Hello Will KN6TZK, 

 

I am delighted that you are enjoying amateur radio and making contacts. I felt your same level of excitement when I worked my first Russian about 40 years ago. 

 

Here, many decades later and I still feel that excitement as I chase DX throughout the bands. As some say, it is a marathon, not a sprint.   I would say, enjoy the journey, savor those moments when you "bag a new one"   Sometimes life gets in the way and we have to set radio aside, but it will always be here.  During Covid, I learned so much about propagation that I had not known about some of these amazing openings.  As you know, FT8/FT4 are visual modes and sometimes you see DX that you would not otherwise expect to see.  I never knew of these amazing mid-day openings to the Middle East, but FT8 showed them to me. 

 

If I have a regret or two in amateur radio it is that I did not start earlier and that I did not hone my CW skills when I was younger. 

 

I'm looking forward to meeting you at a face to face NCDXC gathering, perhaps at the picnic on August 27th. 

 

Have fun with radio, 73

 

Paul N6PSE

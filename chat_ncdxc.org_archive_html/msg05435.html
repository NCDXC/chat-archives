Reports the board have gotten from Pete W6OOL and Russ K6KLY is that the
noise level is high at the repeater site.  Splitting the feed loosing 3db
will certainly threaten those repeater users that had to that point make it
into the receiver just fine may not be able to.

The original idea of a splitter, cavities and yagi was not going to work.

Next report was the use of voting receiver(s) with some kind of link back to
the repeater site.  This is becoming complicated and more difficult to
maintain but remains an option. 

But with a repeater that is rarely used is this a viable investment?  Keep
in mind of the 177 members only two are reporting a problem.  Jim K9YC and
Jerry K6MD, both valuable club members but they are two out of 177.

At this point with no satisfactory solution the problem was voted to do
nothing.  That always means "for now".  

Again the noise floor at the W6TI site is high causing signals from Jim and
Jerry to be unable to capture the repeater's receiver.

Solutions?  If voting remote receiver(s) is the answer the W6TI repeater
club amenity just got a whole lot more complicated.  Maintenance, remote
site and network fees need to be identified.

Answers?

73,

Bob - W6OPO

Andreas, I just read this in the BoD meeting minutes. I think most people
reading this would come to the conclusion that Jim did,

The W6TI repeater was again discussed in light of the proposed additional
equipment needed and potential reduction of signal strength for existing
users. Russ, K6KLY, reported an estimated $923 needed for additional
repeater coverage. A number of Board members reported on the very low usage
of the W6TI repeater. The Board decided to continue to explore ways to
increase repeater coverage but decided to drop the splitter idea because of
the high noise floor and issues around the loss of capture.
Russ, K6KLY, made a motion to drop consideration of upgrades to the W6TI
repeater at this time. Tony, K6BV, seconded the motion and the Board passed
the motion with a unanimous vote.

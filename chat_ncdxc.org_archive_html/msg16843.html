Thanks. I highly recommend that everyone try the VOACAP online website because it's so interactive. There are also many more visual representations of propagation, that one representation might work better than another. When you hover over those squares, you get the percentage.

I can answer for why there are such rapid changes in the LP, and that is because it's a classic "fleeting moment" opening. When I worked Crozet - where a French scientist had 100 watts and a vertical, it was mid morning on 20M and the opening was 20 minutes. No one else was on frequency, so I got the QSO with no competition. This was in the early 2000's - the last time Crozet was activated. I also remember working a VU4 or maybe a VU7 the same way.

So sometimes a 20M opening behaves like a gray line opening, and I suspect it's high angle, because high angle openings seem to be limited to less than 30 minutes.

This is also why I have asked the team to carve out some "West Coast Only" time.

I also remembered that because the Earth is NOT flat, just because I need to point my beam 130 degrees (Southeast), that doesn't mean they receive on a straight line to their Northwest (which would include the hills or upslope mentioned the other day).

The curvature of the Earth really helps us.

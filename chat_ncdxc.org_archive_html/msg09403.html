I recently built a ZS6BKW style G5RV. I modeled different feedline lengths to achieve very sharp resonant points on several bands (where SWR was lower than 2:1). The antenna did perform as expected and as modeled in EZNec. I was doing an A - B test between my phased verticals and such an antenna up only 40' - and the results were quite interesting. The phased array - more or less was equal to a 40M doublet up 40' - and I did quite a few tests - LP, SP and in the broad sided directions and also looking for how the band opened, closed and worked during "just after sunset" to "late evening". The secondary reason was to see if it would be a better antenna on 40M than the phased verticals to work K1N on 40 and 30M. As it turns out - I worked K1N on the phased array, not that doublet. Late into the evening - the phased array worked better than the doublet. Th doublet was better during the early hours just after sunset. Sue seems to point at the changing arrival angles as the F1 and F2 were combining, etc, etc. (But I digress).

I think common mode issues will be a problem at _some_ frequencies but not an issue at others. Is this true? It seems like the ladder line fed length governs this, and that in combination with each dipole element as well. 

In almost all cases, I would use this antenna as a Tee vertical - fed against the 140 buried radials, because its main purpose is a low band antenna. On occasion - I will switch to the doublet mainly as an RX check. 

I will model it in EZNec, but I'm not sure if common mode issues can be determined in an EZNec model. Maybe common mode issues show up as RFI? Maybe an antenna that doesn't perform well on TX? Not sure what the symptom would be. But I sure do remember your presentations on the subject.

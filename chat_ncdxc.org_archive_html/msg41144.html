On Tue, Nov 4, 2025 at 10:03 AM Ryan Huggins via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
This is a good document, thanks.  One tool I recommend (and have since Win10) is "O&O Shutup10" https://www.oo-software.com/en/shutup10   Which is a tool for Win 10 and Win 11 that enables you to disable various telemetry and data gathering features of Windows.

For those a little more technologically inclined, I also recommend installing a "Pi Hole" https://pi-hole.net/   This can be done via a docker instance or through dedicated hardware like a Raspberry Pi.  It's a DNS server that lives on your network and it filters out various trackers and advertisements ACROSS THE NETWORK.  This means that browsing on your phone will now be ad-free (assuming you're on your local network of VPN'd in) and devices like your Roku/FireTV/etc. will no longer be pinging Netflix on a minute-by-minute basis.  There are further blacklists you can add to block known bot control networks, among other things.  Once installed, you'll need to give it a static IP and then point your DNS servers on your router (or whatever issues your DHCP leases) to the PiHole's IP.

This increases your privacy and, if you're on a limited or metered connection, will reduce the traffic going off of your network and (in general) speed up your internet (mostly noticable on tethered or other slower connections) and reduce it's cost (on metered networks).

Of course, no recommendation email would be complete without recommending "Hiding From the Internet" https://www.amazon.com/Hiding-Internet-Eliminating-Personal-Information/dp/1500397814  by Michael Bazzell as well as his entire library.  Great actionable steps you can take, depending on your situation and risk matrix.

Hi Steve,

 

I read your report in the DXer about RF tripping the SWR detector on your amp.  I have a similar problem with my amp (Ameritron ALS-1300 solid state) at my remote station.  If I run full power on the lower bands (30, 40 and 80), the amp trips off line.  I am using a MFJ 998 auto tuner and it will tune to a nice low SWR (1.3 or better) with low power drive.  But yet when I increase the drive to get full power, the amp trips.  This rarely happens on 20m and almost never on 15m and up.  The antenna is a standard size G5RV (102 ft.) and it has a ferrite choke at the transition point from the 32 ft. balanced line to the coax.

 

Does this sound like the problem you had and if so, how did you determine that it was RF on the feedline causing the problem?  I have been thinking it was something breaking down with high voltage but I have tried 2 different tuners and replace the feedline to the antenna.

 

73,

-          Craig, AE6RR

 

From the June DXer:

 

Steve, N6SJ, reports:

I recently cured an RF problem I had using info from K9YC’s Common-mode Choke Cookbook. My new

SteppIR DB42 has an 80M dipole option which wouldn’t work when I ran high power. RF on the outside

of the feedline got back into the amp and tripped the SWR detector, shutting down the amp. With a few

common mode chokes placed at strategic locations, the problem is now cured. Anyone with similar

problems should read Jim Brown/K9YC’s online tutorial.

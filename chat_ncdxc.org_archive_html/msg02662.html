Rich,

 

Congratulations on finding a copy of “DX IS.”  I know that Dick, W6KM, and myself both have copies and really enjoyed Cass’ writing style. I have a copy or two in my files of the West Coast DX Bulletin, but it would take a while for me to locate them. If you use Google and search for the “West Coast DX Bulletin,” you should find a few locations that has some of Cass’ material on line.  In particular, I believe VE1DX was given the entire West Coast DX Bulletin collection direct from Cass when he decided to close the bulletin down. I wish VE1DX would have them scanned the bulletins and put them on the web for everyone to enjoy.

 

As far as who knew Cass, you should check with W6SZN, W6OAT, NI6T, K6ANP, W6RGG and a few others who have been in the DX Club for a long time. I knew Cass and remember the first time he became President of the NCDXC.  If you remember the movie, “Walking Tall” it had tones from that, but in a fun way. You can also find some of Cass’ writings in back issues of “The DX’er” on the NCDXC web site. When I think of some of the characters Cass wrote about (Red Eye Louie, the QRPer, The Palos Verdes Sun dancers (?), and others) it still brings me good thoughts about a wonderful person.  Cass was a great person!.

 

There is also a copy of the book in the Jim Maxwell library at the California Historical Radio Society

 

73,

Ross

K6GFJ

Thankfully, my past struggles with Reunion freed me from having to deal with yet another Eurocentric French expedition. I have 8 bands  confirmed and worked FR5DX 20 years ago on 75m--he was CQing with no answers and a big signal. It's good that many of our brethren got through, but any African expedition that works 80% Europe on any band requires a great deal of rationalization to justify. Positive: rapid upload to LOTW. Negative: everything else. JMO.

I would have been interested in 160, but I knew better than to waste time with this group. I am developing a real antipathy to French expeditions. Maybe that is not fair, but personal experience tends to rule opinion.

OTOH, it's not fair to compare with FT5ZM. Reunion is a polar path, through EU on the SP, making it worse. Amsterdam was low-lattitude and antipodal--a piece of cake in comparison.

Garry, NI6T

Thankfully, I already had Reunion confirmed on 20 and 40 CW from previous operations. TO7CC gave me CW and SSB on 17 and CW on 80, already confirmed on LOTW. Considering the relatively lackluster treatment by this operation of hams outside EU, I think I did as well as could be expected.

Some interesting observations. Strangely, the leaderboard shows NO CW operation on 20 or 40, which should have been money bands for NA. Their webpage said that they planned a low band focus, yet they had only 550 Qs on 160M, 486 of which were to EU. Only 37 to NA, 11 to AS. 80M was slightly less lopsided -- 1057 to EU, 181 to NA, 114 to AS. So this expedition with an alleged low band focus made only 4% of their contacts on 80 and 160, and 79% of those to EU. Indeed, their highest QSO totals were on 15, 12, and 10.

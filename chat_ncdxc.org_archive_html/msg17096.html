On 5/3/19 at 7:22 AM, Don.McKee@gmail.com (Don McKee) wrote:
What I've done is to set the "P1" key to display the voltage (I don't remember the settings option, off hand -- check the manual). To see the voltage, press "F/W" followed by "P1" (above the "7" key). Not as convenient as glancing at the display, but better than power-cycling the rig.
I learn something new about a radio I've owned for several years. Thanks!
A quick experiment shows that I can leave the voltage display up through transmit and receive cycles, so I can monitor voltage during transmit which is a good measure of battery state. With a recently charged battery I get 7.8V during RX and 6.1V during 5W TX. (I do have to go out of the monitoring state to change frequencies or access other menus.)

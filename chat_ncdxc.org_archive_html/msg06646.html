I'm not sure what the solar system goals are ----but here's some input.
I put in a 4.50-KW system using a Sunny Boy 4000TL-us.  I get no RFI at all--any band. I chose this particular unit so I would have emergency power when the grid is down [without storage batteries]. Its limited to 120V 17Amps when the grid is down but I have emergency power--and of course it requires the sun to shine.
This gives me enough power to run my radios, microwave, recharge battries, run the Ref and furnace for a few hours
etc.

Alan

I have three "off grid" solar systems I have put together myself. One powers
our club house/summer cabin in a wilderness area in Nevada County at 5000
feet and a second powers our NN7NN/R 2 meter repeater which is also located
in a wilderness area e.g. no paved roads, no power lines, no neighbors
except for rattle snakes, small bears and deer. The third system is a work
in progress for an off grid backup system here in Saratoga. 

FWIW I use top of the line XANTREX 4 KW pure sine wave inverters and I have
never had a problem with them. I currently have three of them. The cost for
these new is approximately $4000 each but they are available occasionally
used on E-Bay for around $2000 each. My battery backup at the cabin is 24
large monster ( each battery weighs over 100 pounds) commercial 2 volt
batteries wired in series plus solar panels and I use a XANTREX inverter
that runs on 48 V. It has performed flawlessly for over 7 *years* up there.

For our 2m repeater system we have another 4KW XANTREX pure sine wave
inverter that is powered by a 12V power supply, multiple golf cart batteries
and solar panels. 

My off grid project at home includes another 4KW XANTREX pure sine wave
inverter but this time configured for 24V.

Why the different voltages? The first one I bought new and my preferred
choice was 48V. The next two I bought on E-Bay because the prices were right
and luckily the used ones have also performed well.

I recommend you talk to N6RO as he has a serious professionally designed and
installed solar power system for his contest station and not only does it
work great for his contesting needs but it is also saving him money for all
of his other electrical power requirements.

Good luck and have fun!!!  73, Ted, K6XN

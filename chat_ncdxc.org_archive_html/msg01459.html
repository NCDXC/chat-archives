My response: I only needed XT on 160. I worked them on 80 and 20CW for the helluvit, but I still need 160. I have not been able to be QRV during best (sic) 160 time every day--due to having a life---but condx have not been good; I have heard them weakly on topband on one evening for about five minutes. Generally, it has been a Europe/East Coast excursion, notwithstanding your first-day observation, as is usual for African expeditions put on by Europeans, who have about as much trouble working into the African bulge as we have to work Clipperton. The loud east coast and midwest stations sneak in between Europeans but we are weaker and have no place to do the same. Walt AJ6T and I discussed it and neither of us is optimistic about topband given band conditions and the short time left. SoCal has a lower-lattitude shot and is closer--if there is an opening, it should be stronger and longer for them.

My experience with the West Africa bulge on the low bands from here with simple wires over many years has been that 40 is a slam dunk, 80 is occasionally doable, and 160 is a crap shoot and your point is three. OTOH, I knew the job was dangerous when I took it.

Garry, NI6T


On 3/4/2013 8:02 AM, paul@n6pse.com wrote:

It's Monday and time for your NCDXC Question of the Week:
 
The XT2TT DXpedition is in their final days. How's your experience been working them?
 
 
 
 
My response:  I needed XT2 for an all-time new one. I missed the large DXpedition that was there about five years ago. Last year, there was a group of JA's that was active for about a week. They worked JA's and the East Coast and then went home. Never got them.
 
I was surprised and shocked to hear XT2TT calling for the West Coast during their first day of activity!
 
I've worked them on:
 
20 SSB
20 RTTY
20 CW
30 CW
15 CW
17 SSB
15 SSB
 
These seven contacts greatly exceed my expectations. Solar conditions have been quite poor, yet they continue to work hard. I'm really looking forward to meeting the Team Leader, Silvano-I2YSB at Visalia and congratulating them on an outstanding effort!
 
Paul N6PSE

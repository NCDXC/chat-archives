Having lived in the midwest tornado/winter blizzard belt and "enjoyed" the view of a beam so loaded with ice the tips darn near point at the ground I have learned three hard lessons: 1)  2" high tensile, 1/4 wall aluminum---3/8 wall for a stacked pair of beams, is minimum; 2) the farther down the tower the rotor is, the less the stress on the top  bearing and rotor ( keep more mast in the tower than above it) and 3) as the boom is a heck of a lot stronger than the individual elements I point the element tips into the wind at anything over 25-30 mph. I have a Davis weather station a few feet under the bottom beam---gives me both wind speed and direction--wireless--at my shack...have used one for years and many is the night I hear the windows rattle and stumble to the shack to check JIM

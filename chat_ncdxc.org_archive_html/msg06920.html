I had a similar problem and I had thought it was RFI.  It wasn't.  It was due to the fact that my SS amp. likes really low SWR at high power.  The amp. is a THP HL-2.5kfx and tuner is MFJ-998.  The MFJ-998 would provide a SWR of 1.3:1.  The amp. runs fine at 1kw.  When I go to 1.5kw the amp's protection circuit trips.  I experimented with ferrite beads with no luck.  This is despite the amp. showing very low reflected power on its meter.  The amp. runs fine at 1.5kw to a dummy load.  So I borrowed a manual tuner for experimentation.  Using the manual tuner and with a near-perfect SWR the amp. is happy at all power levels.  Not satisfied with using a manual tuner I just purchased a Palstar HF-Auto.  I just got it yesterday.  The Palstar was able to tune to a perfect SWR (less than 1.05 to 1) and my amp. is once again happy.  Due to the discret nature of the MFJ-988 it's not possible to provide a perfect tune.  On the positive side the MFJ-998 uses a modified L circuit which is nearly 100% efficient even on the lower bands.  If your tuning needs are solely on 80m and lower then the MFJ-998 is fine if you use lower powers.  With the Palstar you might get a better SWR and higher power, however much of that high power is lost in the tuner on 80m and lower (see the most recent test results in QST).  I use the Palstar on 17m and 12m as well as on the low bands so it serves my needs better than the MFJ-998.  I have a friend with the exact same setup as yours (MFJ-998 and ALS-1300) and he has the same tripping problem at high powers.  The problem is intermittent so it doesn't bother him that much.  For me it's bothersome as I would be calling a dxpedition with only 50w unless I catch the tripped amp.  GL.

On Friday, June 20, 2014 11:31 AM, Craig-AE6RR via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:


Hi Steve,
 
I read your report in the DXer about RF tripping the SWR detector on your amp.  I have a similar problem with my amp (Ameritron ALS-1300 solid state) at my remote station.  If I run full power on the lower bands (30, 40 and 80), the amp trips off line.  I am using a MFJ 998 auto tuner and it will tune to a nice low SWR (1.3 or better) with low power drive.  But yet when I increase the drive to get full power, the amp trips.  This rarely happens on 20m and almost never on 15m and up.  The antenna is a standard size G5RV (102 ft.) and it has a ferrite choke at the transition point from the 32 ft. balanced line to the coax.
 
Does this sound like the problem you had and if so, how did you determine that it was RF on the feedline causing the problem?  I have been thinking it was something breaking down with high voltage but I have tried 2 different tuners and replace the feedline to the antenna.

From the June DXer:
 
Steve, N6SJ, reports:
I recently cured an RF problem I had using info from K9YC’s Common-mode Choke Cookbook. My new
SteppIR DB42 has an 80M dipole option which wouldn’t work when I ran high power. RF on the outside
of the feedline got back into the amp and tripped the SWR detector, shutting down the amp. With a few
common mode chokes placed at strategic locations, the problem is now cured. Anyone with similar
problems should read Jim Brown/K9YC’s online tutorial.

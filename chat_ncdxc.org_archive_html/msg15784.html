Good Morning from Rome,

     Well HV0A is over and I look back at a very exciting experience. Did it go the way I had hoped? Absolutely not. First band conditions were dismal at best. I intended to start on 40 meters until Francesco advised against it. So it was to be 20 meters all the way. For those of you concerned about a split frequency operation, that is what I set up for, not realizing that the 756 pro 3 had malfunctioned on me until Paul N6PSE advised me that I was simplex. With Francesco out of the shack I decided to continue as is until he returned. By that time I was too far into the operation. I did make deliberate attempts to single out W6s and, as usual not everyone listened to my requests. Surprise, surprise. I was happy to log the few I did hear and believe me, if I heard you, I worked you. I am sorry that I could not work you all.

     The bottom line though is that Karen and I had a wonderful time: a unique experience. Francesco is such a terrific and caring host. My wife KM6IND got her first ever on-air qsos while operating HV0A. What a way to break into radio! 

     I look forward to returning and sharing our adventures with you.

Best 73 de

Tony Dowler K6BV

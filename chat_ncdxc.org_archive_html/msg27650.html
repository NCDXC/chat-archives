I do both LOTW and cards.

I guess I'm old school, but both gives the immediate (relatively anyway) satisfaction with LOTW and some of the cards (at least used to be) are true works of art; something worth looking at. 

And to me, cards are a better wall covering display than chest puffing certificates (that you have to buy once you've accomplished something).  I have DXCC certificates with endorsements; I think I put them in one of the file cabinets here (I have the 'entry ticket' but don't really care beyond that, I compete against me).

The cards that I've collected are in two piles, in boxes; one confirms LOTW and the other box awaits a card checker for band mode slots that are not in LOTW; the nearest checker is 8 hours of driving (400 miles) from me.  "Someday" I'll put them on display, maybe.  I look through them occasionally and recall time and place; the operator, the stations used, the equipment... a check box or certificate can't provide the same satisfaction.

It's just a matter of preference.

I got 3 slots on ft8ww, am happy with the LOTW confirms and await a card.

Several cards (primarily Russian teams) have been pending for months (though LOTW came through).  Which implies the mail is blocked (likely) or they pocketed the funds and don't intend to send cards; or if they're waiting to return home, have little intention of doing so in real time.

For a few dollars, ya rolls the dice.

This is an “interesting story”.

 

Not long after I got back into ham radio and then DX-ing (for the first time ever) in 2001, I met Chuck, K6RK. He told me how he used coax cables for radials for the low bands. He is one of the main reasons I decided to try the low bands. He was a really great guy. Classic amateur radio operator – reminded me of another “elmer”, Bob – W2LV.

 

Tomorrow morning I am picking up an old linear amplifier of his. From what I hear – a gorgeous piece of home brewed engineering, using an 8877 (3CX1200A7). The person giving this to me wanted it bequeathed to someone who knew Chuck . . . .

 

He and I both lived in Lafayette, and I would hear him every morning on CW on the low bands.

 

73,

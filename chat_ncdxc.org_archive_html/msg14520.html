Hi Rich, this is Dave KA6BIM.   You guys don’t hear from me much.  I was active with NCDXC in the 80’s & 90’s. Family and work kept me away from a lot of the meetings and interests changed.  I became active in NCCC around Y2K.  I have now migrated north, live in Oregon and am active with the Willamette Valley DX Club as well as still participating with NCCC.  I still follow happenings with NCDXC on this reflector.  My NCDXC pin has a 15 year bar on it and is pinned on the bulletin board in my shack.  I even still have a stack of NCDXC qsl cards that Rubin (SK) WA6AHF printed for us.

That being said,  peoples interests in our hobby can and do change.  My first interests was chasing DX.  I worked hard in those early years to get my first 100.  It did not come easy with a peanut whistle station sitting over in the Central Valley.  Once I finally got the 100 confirmations required for membership, Jay( W6GO) & Jan O’brien sponsored me to join.  I became active and attended many meetings there at Harry’s Hoffbrow at the airport.  Before we had the DX packet network I would help with the DX nets we would run during the DX contests actually collecting and relaying “spots” by voice over W6TI repeater.  Then came the Packet network and the hobby changed again.  It seemed at the time like there was a lot more DX to work during contests, and the pileups were not as bad.  I started working more contests to get the DX stations.  Eventually my interests changed and I became more focused on contesting than chasing DX, and sort of migrated over to NCCC.  Back then dual members sent their scores to NCDXC for the ARRL & CQ DX Contests and to NCCC for other contests.  At some point along there NCDXC contest activity dropped off. Work & volunteer responsibilities kept me from the meetings.   I continued contesting and working DX, until about 2005 when my contest partner moved and I ended up losing interest for about 5 years.  I started getting active again about 2010.  Shortly after that we moved up here to Oregon where I found a great location and have been able to build up a decent station.  I am now very active in contesting, participating in 25-30 a year. and still chasing DX.  Not on Honor roll yet, but close.  I missed some of the hard ones that were active during my break and am waiting for them to come back around.  New ones are hard to come by.  Getting ZC4 and the new Z6 in the same week was quite a treat.  I still look forward to getting a packet of cards from the bureau, or even better a direct card with an exotic stamp on it.  I am also active with my local ARES/RACES group.

When some people finish up their DXCC, they move to other challenges.  Do it again using QRP, chase IOTA Chase counties,  try for Fred Fish award on 6 mtrs.  The DX marathon offers a fresh chase every year.  I hope you can find another aspect that interests you.  

Although I already have my 5BDXCC I am still working on filling in band slots, and working towards what I call "Blackout bingo”.  Each country, all bands all modes(ssb, cw, rtty, dig).  I will probably never get there, but it keeps the interests going. I have never paid much attention to the “WARC” bands, but am starting to play there a bit.  I have a 12/17 mtr anten na I need  to put up. 6 meters is fun, I hope we have a repeat of some of the great openings that I missed decades ago. I still have not tried EME.    In the ARRL DX CW a couple of weeks ago I worked 3 new 160 countries.  That is one band I have never had great antennas for, either a 18ht vertical or currently a dipole.  Even though ARRL has lumped RTTY and the digital modes together, I keep track of the separate modes.  Being new to digital, FT8 has opened up a whole new aspect of the hobby for me.  And this is something my kids (all 3 are licensed) can do as well, living in antenna restricted locations.  One of my sons lives in San Jose and is now able to chase DX using FT8 where he had no opportunity with ssb.cw or rtty.  

This is something I want to address. 
 I still chase DX because I want to finish what I started.  Rich, you have done that, Congratulations!.  I still have about 14 entities to track down.  I will keep plugging away at it.  That said.   The hobby has changed,  With all the “policemen”, jammers, foul mouthed malcontents, and people who won’t listen to the DX stations directions,  I am disgusted by the current conditions that come out around expeditions.  I am embarrassed by the behavior and I am glad that my kids, and in particular, my daughter, have not been bitten by the DX bug.   I have found that I can avoid most of that by contesting.  I can work DXCC or WAS  in a weekend and do not have to listen to all that crap.  Currently the FT8 mode also allows a way to chase DX without all the BS.  It doesn’t have the thrill of really having a personal contact and a rag chew with someone halfway around the world, but at least you can test your station and say you have contacted the country…..

I am concerned for our hobby.  I talk with the the few local hams near me that work HF.  I have tried to encourage my local club members to get active on HF.  They say  "have you hear those guys down on 38xx  to 71xx?”  They are turned off by it. They check into their 80 mtr nets and call it a day.   If the behavior continues and expands farther, then I worry that we may lose the privileges altogether.   11 mtrs used to be a ham band.  We lost it.  Will we lose them all?  

Long winded answer, but hey ,I don’t usually take up a lot of bandwidth here.  I’ll go back to the sidelines….

73’s & Good DX

Dave KA6BIM

For me, it is like running endurance races like I used to a few years back.  I ran races (5k, 10k, 20k, Oahu perimeter runs, etc for the fun of it and to improve my PR (Personal record). I kept track of how fast I would run these not to beat anyone else because I was always middle of the pack or so but for my own personal enjoyment. It was my hobby.

I was no super athlete or really competitive on any level. Personally I think of ham radio in the same way as I did running races. I hardly consider something like it a competition. It is similar to me as running races but is a lot more expensive and if you want to get good at it and get contacts it is even way more time consuming.

If you read the entire article.. there is a big section on ethics.. and what your DXCC means to YOU.  The bottom line is the ARRL took the high road.. enabling more options. 

At the most recent NCDXC meeting, Wayne N7NG mentioned that ARRL’s view (not withstanding challenge, honor role etc), is that DXCC is a PERSONAL achievement and not intended to be a COMPETITIVE measure.

I heard Wayne make that statement.  If it didn’t matter, and wasn’t a competition, then why do we keep score, and invent new ways to keep score, or use DXCC as a badge of honor.

It took K6YP, and N6SJ to kill the entrance requirement of NCDXC that you have already earned DXCC before you could be a member.

It IS a competition - the ARRL is the official score keeper, but, they don’t make the rules of the competition.  Those that do compete LIKE to compete.  AND, when we think someone has changed the rules of the game in their favor, we DON’T like it.

The point is - let have the debate about what the competition is.  The ARRL really doesn’t care.  They will keep score.

Every competition has classes, except Ham Radio.  DXing has only one class - how many countries/bands/modes you’ve worked.  We can and should consider other classes. From one and one location only, no remote; free for all; same continent; same callsign district; QRP; etc.  Contesting does a good job here, why doesn’t DXing?

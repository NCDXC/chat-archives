On Fri, Nov 17, 2023 at 3:35 PM Rick NK7I via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
Another consideration:

You do not have to program it for a duplex channel, which means no
duplexer, filters, amp, pre-amp and other ($$$!) expenses.  Use it on
simplex.

I have a Kenwood TKR-750 repeater for my IRLP node (programmed for
duplex but unlisted, no one really cares up here in N Idaho). The MAIN
reason I got it, was that it's rated for full power out, for a very long
time.

Most ham gear would melt in an hour key down at full output.  The
Kenwood simply turns on the fan and keeps chugging away (40 watts out)
and does that for much of the day.  I will add a pre-amp and commercial
amp, the 25 mile range is a little shy of my goals (low level, at my house).

You WILL have to have an RX and TX antenna this way, but they're a LOT
cheaper than a duplexer.

The ONLY issue I had in simplex, was that someone thinks the simplex
channels (which are different here) are reserved for EMCOMM (some are
and they have drills); so it was simpler to (listen for a while, make
sure it's unused) take over a repeater channel here (nearest registered
station is 100 miles away, not an issue)... but this is Idaho, FAR less
dense than CA.

I run it in half duplex, because I haven't felt like spending ~$3000 for
the other 'stuff' that must be attached for a full duplex repeater.

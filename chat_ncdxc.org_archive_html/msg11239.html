I studied this issue last night by going though 8 months of Woody Leonhard's AskWoody blog. He's considered an expert on issues related to Windoze, and has published a book on Win 10. Don't have it, but 10+ years ago, found his books on Word quite useful.
His current advice is to upgrade from Win8 and 8.1, but to NOT upgrade from Win7.
A local IT guy, AE6KS, says that he's seen Win10 uninstall stuff it doesn't like. Woody notes that Win10 forces installation of updates.
My own advice is to never upgrade a computer that's working and doing everything you need it to do. Many years ago, an EE colleague, who was at the time working for Microsoft, blew a week cleaning up the mess after trying to upgrade one of his machines. I still have a critical machine running Win2K Pro. No, it doesn't go on the internet.

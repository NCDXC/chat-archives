Chris, I completely agree with you that contest operating, rapid rate operating is great preparation for a DXpedition, however I do not feel that it is essential. I will also say that while I am not a contester, I see that contesters often make the best DXpedition operators.
While I don't want to change the subject being discussed, I will inject that not all DXpeditions are expensive. Since 2010 I have participated in six DXpeditions. Here is what each of them cost:
1) YI9PSE: Each Op paid $2500 which included 12 days hotel and all meals. Flights to/from Iraq were about $2000. 2) ST0R: Each Op paid about $150 a night for hotel and meals. Flights to/from South Sudan were about $1800 3) 3D2R Rotuma. Each Op paid about $5400 for their share of the DXpedition including boat passage, meals. Flights to/from Fiji are about $1300 4) ET3AA: Each operator contributed about $400 to goods provided to the station. Flights to/from Addis Ababa were about $1600 5) 7O6T: Each US Operator contributed $3000 to the cost of the license, paid about $150 a night for their hotel. Flights to/from Socotra were about $2500 6) 3D2C: Each Operator paid about $5300 for their share of the DXpedition including boat passage, meals etc. Flights to/from Fiji were about $1300. 

Most non-Polar DXpeditions are more in the $5000-$8000 range.

Paul N6PSE

Martti Laine-OH2BH has written several recent articles about DXPP’s or “DX Paying Passengers”. Martti describes a DXPP as an operator with only a minimal degree of operating expertise, but who contributes to the Dxpedition financing to help the Dxpedition. Martti writes that DXPPs can seriously damage the credibility of an operation and create chaos on the bands. He is critical of the K5D Desecheo Dxpedition where a strong operator was relieved by a lesser skilled operator and problems resulted. Martti also criticizes the TX5K Clipperton Dxpedition, writing that “a seat was available for anyone who wanted to pay”. Martti suggests that the number of TX5K DXPPs added to the stress level of the Dxpedition team members and the Dxpedition audience. Martti suggests that DX Foundations consider the number of DXPPs included in a Dxpedition when funding them. He writes that a Dxpedition may not qualify for support if the percentage of DXPPs is too high.
Here is your Question of the week: As members of the DX Community, are you concerned about DXPPs? Do you feel that a Dxpedition suffers if there are a few lesser experienced operators on the team? Should the DX Foundations offer less support for a Dxpedition to a rare place even if they have some lesser skilled operators on the team? 
Give us your thoughts-

Paul N6PSE

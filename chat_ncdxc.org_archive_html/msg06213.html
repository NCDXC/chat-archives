On 3/25/2014 10:15 PM, Aaron McClure wrote:

My MFJ Antenna analyzer just took a dive.  Can anyone recommend a decent
antenna analyzer.  What do you think is the best?
Depends on what you want to do with it. I have four (five actually, but the fifth is loaned out), and which one I grab depends on what I want to accomplish. My MFJ259B is the one I don't cry about when I drop it from on high, but need to know approximately where my antenna is resonant, approximately (but not exactly) where the resonance is for a stub, etc. I have an ancient AEA with a switch inside that makes it a 75 ohm analyzer, and that's where I have it set. Then there's another ancient AEA that goes to 440 MHz.
But the one I really love, and use the most, is the VNWA Vector Network Analyzer designed by DG8SAQ, a prof at a German university, and built by a ham in the UK. Very powerful, I can export data to design programs like SimSmith and design matching networks, find the length (and loss) of transmission lines, even translate the measurement I've made in the shack to the impedance at the feedpoint (up in the air). Works to 1.5 GHz, runs off the USB port of your computer (and gets power from the USB port). And it's a real bargain -- about $750 delivered in the US.
The one out on loan is just fine too -- a ten year old predecessor to the VNWA designed by the TAPR design group and built by Ten Tec. It goes to 100 MHz, also generates the files for exchange. It should be fairly cheap used, because of its age.
There are at least a half dozen others. The VNWA blows all of them away. google VNWA to find it. 

73, Jim K9YC

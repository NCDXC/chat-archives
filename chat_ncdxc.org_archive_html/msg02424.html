For sake of argument, let's say you can put up 4 stations on Heard Island.

The best way to "gate" uniques is to limit the band's and modes to say 20M CW and SSB, and 17 CW and SSB.

Two stations stay on whatever band is open to the world, and let propagation determine which areas you work.

The other two stations are the flexible ones that can venture out and take some load off the first two and break the ties when propagation is good to several parts of the world.  

More bands are added in when the team feels its the right time.

Ahead of time - advertise this "mission", and NO leader boards, and DXA will be used to show uniques and a red mark if there is dupe.

The ops just have to work everyone, and DXA can be used to red flag (quiet scarlet letter) that dupes are not appreciated.

No flow is broken and no one is black listed per se.

The ops make sure they ID often and repeat the callers call so the caller has no doubt, and DXA seals the deal.

I think ZL9HR did a great job in this regard, and imagine if they also had DXA. That's what this dx-ped should look like IMHO.

Paul N6PSE, who is on travel, has kindly consented to allow me to pose the Question of the Week. Since I am engaged in finding the right structure for the 2015 Heard Island DXpedition, I would like to ask the following:
 
Heard Island is shaping up to be a very difficult challenge, far more difficult than when we did it as VK0IR in 1997. The main problem is the cost of the vessel. After a year of trying to negotiate a favorable arrangement with the preferred vessel, I reluctantly concluded that the projected cost ($1.5M) was simply too much. So the challenge is how to restructure the operation to enable it to happen at all. One way is to use a much smaller team and therefore a smaller vessel. A small team (4-6 ops) might imply that we would have to change from a goal of logging the most QSOs to logging the most All-Time-New-Ones (ATNOs) or the most Uniques. Most likely this would mean a return to an average of 2-3 QSOs per DXer, which was the norm a decade ago, instead of the 4-5 QSOs/DXer, which has been experienced by recent DXpeditions (e.g., TX5K), stimulated in part by the ARRL Challenge.
 
So here is the question (in several parts): Considering the limitations of the operation, would you favor an initial period of ATNOs-only, say a week or two weeks? If you already have VK0/H confirmed would you willingly stand by and allow others to get their ATNOs? Would you still financially support the DXpedition, even though you may not be able to log 10+ band-modes?
 
The questions above are therefore somewhat equivalent to the following:
 
What should be the goal of the next Heard Island DXpedition?

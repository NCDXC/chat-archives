FWIW I checked my logs and I worked Dave Leeson W6NL from Monaco (as did many others here on the West Coast) when Dave’s call was then 3A/W6QHS back in March 21, 1981, using CW.

 

I think Dave has demonstrated that it is possible to work West Coast stations from Monaco even with a modest “mini-DXpedition” …comprising one operator, one  mobile rig and a mobile antenna. J

Risto and Team

 

If my memory is correct I believe many of us worked Dave Leeson, W6NL, from 3A (Monaco)  years ago and Dave was operating with a mobile rig and a mobile whip from the end of a pier in Monaco. As I recall he was putting in a pretty good signal here on the West Coast. I have only been to Monaco a few times over the years but my experience was that it was expensive to stay in Monaco but it was pretty inexpensive to stay in nearby France at a “bed and breakfast” with great food and great rooms and it was a short drive to Monaco by either car or motorcycle. 73, Ted, K6XN

On Mar 25, 2013, at 6:12 AM, paul@n6pse.com wrote:

 

Here is your NCDXC Question of the Week:

 

What entity has been most elusive for you?   Which one has been active but you just have not been able to get it in your logs, or you have done so with great devotion and patience?

 

 

For me, a number of them come to mind. SU was very elusive until Vlad-SU9VB went there and I was able to make multiple band contacts with him on CW. Mt. Athos was very elusive for a long time. I must have spent ten years trying to work Monk Apollo and had concluded it may never happen. Well, New Years Eve 2012 I found him on 40 SSB about 11PM our time and I now have that elusive QSL card.

 

I've never heard an EP-Iran station-Ever!  I am hoping that I can nail down EP in the coming months or years. I know that there are several that are active but between their timing, propagation and their operating methods it just hasn't happened yet.

Hi Daniel,

This is the kind of ham radio project I really enjoy working on.
In addition to the site survey, a good place to start would be to formulate objectives and a budget. By objectives, I mean what bands, how much activity, how good? You'll also need to do an RF noise survey. That can easily be the most limiting factor in any location, even more so in a campus with lots of electronics. 

73, Jim K9YC

On Tue,5/26/2015 10:19 AM, Daniel Brock via Chat wrote:

Hey gang,
I'm working to set up a club station at Facebook in Menlo Park (W6FBK). We're in the process of selecting a site on campus and deciding where to put the antenna, etc. I am looking for a volunteer adviser to help guide us through the process of selecting a good roof location and installing a mast, rotator, and hex beam antenna.
None of us in the club have done something at this scale before (I know it must sound like a simple matter to you long time DXers!), and I want to make sure we do it right. Would anyone be willing to elmer us through the process? 

Here are the things we'd like some help with:

- confirming our selected site for the antenna is workable
- putting together a bill of materials to purchase for the club station
- installing the antenna, rotator, and feed lines
In return, W6FBK offers all the free food you can eat while you're on site, and long standing goodwill from our small club.
Let me know if you're interested! We're looking to kick this off sometime in June or July.

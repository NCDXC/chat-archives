Guys,

LDMOS devices used in many amateur SSA's are VERY rugged. They
will withstand an 85:1 output vswr which is much more vswr any any real
antenna feed line combination can deliver to you amplifier

The two things that rugged LDMOS FETs can NOT withstand are overdrive
and temperature. Overdrive heats the submicron gates of LDMOS parts
and causes them to fuse or melt. When this occurs the device has failed as
it can no longer deliver either specified gain or power output. Turning up the
drive to compensate only exasperates the problem. Overdrive also causes
increased drain current and often reduced efficiency. This causes the LDMOS 
part's power dissipation to increase as less of the device's dc input is delivered 
to the antenna as RF and instead heats the device. Gain decreases with temperature 
so again we have another self fulfilling prophecy leading to device failure .

Rule: 1. Run your solid state amplifiers at maximum efficiency. Keep antenna
vswr less than 1.2:1. Use an antenna tuner either internal or external to maintain
low vswr. Remember the transformer in your amp's output circuit transforms 50 ohms
to the correct load impedance for optimum power output and efficiency. If the device 
does not see the correct load impedance, it can NOT perform efficiently and begins to 
overheat starting the vicious cycle described above. 

Rule: 2. Do Not overdrive your amp. You don't really need that last watt of power or
the signal degradation (distortion) that comes with overdrive.

My homebrew LDMOS amplifiers have been running well for over 5 years with no failures. 
Your SSPAs can too if you keep the load vswr very low and do not overdrive the amplifier.

On 2/28/2022 13:12, Paul N6PSE via Chat wrote:
I recently suffered a failed PA  in my Expert 1.3K-FA. I was not aware of anything on my end to cause this failure. 

I had been running it in the high power setting and driving it to 800-900 watts in FT8. 

Bob Hardie has given me this guidance which I was not aware of and am passing along to my NCDXC friends that use this amplifier. See below.

Paul N6PSE

1. If power output needed is 550W or below, use LOW POWER level.   The voltages on the final transistor are lowered to maintain linearity on LOW and MID and are maximum on MAX. 
Otherwise, distortion, oscillations, garbage, etc can occur. 

2. Same for MID power.  Only run between 600W and 1kW.   And on MAX power, use between 1100 to 1300 or so watts. 

3. FT8 is NOT supposed to be used in MAX power level at all.    Use only in LOW and MID power ranges.  Observe the different power ranges to maintain linearity.

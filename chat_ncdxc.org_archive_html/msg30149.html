+ AA6YQ comments below

My wife, Medea, and I take a couple of tropical dive trips every year to interesting, if not rare entities, such as ZF, 3D2, and the Eastern Caribbean. 
I've decided to try my hand at holiday-style DXpeditions. I own an IC-7300 with a flexible input DC supply. Weight is usually a consideration, so I'd look forward to suggestions for compact and suitcase-compatible antenna systems that won't frustrate me with poor performance. 
Any ideas are appreciated. 

+ In my experience with holiday operations, an IC-706 or an IC-7200, a multi-band doublet, and an inexpensive lightweight MFJ-901B tuner with a balun worked well. If running barefoot, you can feed this antenna with 300 ohm TV twin lead. You can suspend the doublet from either end, or from the center inverted-V style; if you choose the latter, keep the ends at least 10' of the ground and away from human  reach.

https://www.dxlabsuite.com/Doublet.htm

+ You can be QRV on 20m and above with only 33 feet of wire; if you have more room (and wire), you can add 30m, 40m, and even 80m to your portfolio.

+ A slingshot and a couple of fishing sinkers will help with getting a line over a convenient palm tree or two, but check to make sure that the slingshot won't cause trouble on entry.

+ In ~8 weeks of operating as 8P9RY spread over several holiday-style visits, this antenna got me 8,237 QSOs, with 166 DXCC entities worked and 130 confirmed; I was especially popular on 80m and the WARC bands. It's fun being on the pointy end of a pileup, but it takes some practice to be effective!

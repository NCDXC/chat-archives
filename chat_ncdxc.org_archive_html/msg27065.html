Eric,
It looks like you have some help available.  Bring out of the area, I can no longer throw my hat into the 'helpers pool'!
While you are having the repair done, you should look closely at the potential for any water intrusion into the coaxial cable.  It can lead to trouble and it can migrate farther than one might think.  Think about stripping back on both ends to ensure you are into 'good cable' before terminating.  A wrap of good vinyl tape followed by some self fusing rubber tape and a final wrap of the vinyl should keep out water.
If you want to add a layer of resistance to the critters, consider use of flexible drip irrigation tubing that is about 1/2" diameter.  Coax may slide right in before you get the connector installed.  Or, you could go with schedule 40 or Class 200 pipe but it is usually white.  Or, find and eliminate the critters!

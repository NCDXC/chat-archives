Hi Al,

 

I'd create a one-of-a-kind QSL with the correct callsign which I worked and the correct QSO information.  It might just be an index card on which you've written the QSO data with a ballpoint pen or a Sharpie.  It doesn't need to be anything special, just something with all the CORRECT information. I'd also make sure it contained a signature line something like "Confirmed by: _______", and I'd print his callsign and "QSL Manager" under the place where you want him to sign. I'd then send that card, along with an SASE, to the DXpedition's QSL manager.  I'd probably enclose a photocopy of the QSL he already sent you (the one with the wrong callsign) so he can easily see that the QSO data on your card is correct.  I'd enclose a short note saying how much you appreciated the card he just sent you, but you'd like to get one bearing the callsign you actually worked.  Since he told you he is out of the original, printed DXpedition QSL cards, you've taken the liberty of making up a card of your own, and that if he agrees that the QSO data shown on your homemade card is correct, would he please just sign on the blank line and return it to you in the enclosed SASE.  This procedure requires almost no effort on his part other than to just sign the card. I think there will be a high probability you'll get your card back quickly.  It won't be pretty, but it will count for DXCC and you can submit it in good conscience.

 

73 de Rusty, W6OAT

I recently requested a confirmation for a DXpedition QSO that is not particularly rare, but for which I have only one QSO on 40 SSB.  It was from 1999.   I contacted the manager who told me he had no more cards for the DXpedition but had cards from a participant in the operation whose home call is from the same entity.  I asked that he mark up the card with the DXpedition callsign and sent off my SAE and USD.

 

When the card came a few days ago, all the QSO info was accurate but the card was not the DXpedition, but for that operator.  The call was not crossed out and filled in with the correct call.

I am not inclined to use this card as evidence of confirmation.  What would you do?

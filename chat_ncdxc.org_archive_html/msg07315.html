Yes - but - it costs a lot to lobby - the ARRL has the people, has spent the money (our money), has the contacts.  If we have particular desires regarding certain US Govt owned islands - ARRL is the appropriate arm.  The problem is:

Certain people believe that, in effect, they "own" the right to activate such islands.  They have contacts in the government and can essentially derail any effort to activate those islands that do not involve them - they have done this at least twice in the past.  Unfortunately their contacts are not good enough to get the islands activated - only to establish a veto power.  As a result the government administrators, having been burned a couple of times by this approach in the past have decided that it is best to allow no one on the islands except Haitian fisherman.  To overcome this is going to take the efforts of an organization like the ARRL which can sponsor the entire effort if it wants to and overcome all the petty crap.  To do it any other way just invites the vetoers to call their senator and do it again.

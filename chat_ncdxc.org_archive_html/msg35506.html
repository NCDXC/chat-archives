But is it a credible statement?
Are the secure files in hand, accessible, and unmolested?
And if so, why isn't the system up?
I understand Paul's point that if this is the result of a cybercrime that the ARRL may feel it needs to be less than forthcoming - but if that is the intent, then say nothing at all; and if NOT, then why fail to explain the situation completely?
The statement "This is not an LoTW server issue, and LoTW data is secure." appears carefully crafted to NOT say why the LoTW data and server are not and have not for over two weeks been available.
ARRL is a vendor and this is a service that we pay for.  It is not "free" and it is not a "gift" from the ARRL.  Presumably some of the money that we pay is allocated to cybersecurity efforts.
If your bank or insurance company pulled a stunt like this, what would you do?  More importantly, what would the FBI and FDIC do?
It is time that the ARRL (specifically the BOARD CHAIR and CEO) came forward with a real explanation of what is going on, how they intend to deal with it, and retract or modify any prior statements that have proved to be inaccurate.  If they do not do that, how can we trust them going forward?

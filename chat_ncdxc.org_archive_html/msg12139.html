Whereas I on the other hand, prefer HRD. I find it to be FAR easier to use, better integrated (with JT programs and more). I find DXLab Suite to be a kludge, more difficult to set up with frequent conflicts between elements.
I've been a beta tester for HRD as well, paid for it and think it's evolving faster than the other programs. I will agree that it's bloated and tries to be the Swiss army knife thanks to Simon.
On the RARE occasion that I contest, I use N1MM as contest logging IS a failing of HRD; export that log to the contest folks, then into HRD.
There was a time that it wasn't well received, but with the recent new hires, the programmers are spending more time programming instead of hand holding.
Once purchased, forever free. The update $50 is for obtaining 1 year of phone in/email assistance (and auto-download of certain update-able data files). In other words, you can use it for years after the initial purchase. 

Red or green apples?  A matter of preference.

I used HRD ten years ago for digital modes when it was free and written by Simon Brown. It went downhill after he got tired of keeping up with it and sold it to the current owners. I never liked what little I saw of the logger.
The DXLab Suite is quite nice, well supported, and FREE. I consider myself lucky that I found it soon after getting back on the air in 2003. Initially, I used only the logger, DXKeeper, then DXView. Over the years, I've expanded my use to include Spot Collector and WinWarbler, which is a really nice front end for MMTTY and 2Tone for RTTY. I do contest logging with N1MM Plus; after each contest, I export the log as an ADIF and import it to DXKeeper. (Before switching to N1MM around 2008, I used WriteLog and did the same thing.) Periodically, I upload to LOTW from DXKeeper and download LOTW QSLs from DXKeeper. It's nearly automatic.
N1MM Logger and the DXLab Suite are excellent examples of FREE ham software being greatly superior to the programs that ask for money. I use both because they are BETTER, not because they are free. :)

Years ago I tried the “classic” coaxial shielded loops in the ARRL Antenna Handbook. I had one loop for 160M and one for 80M. It was a bust – they never worked as well as my short MA160V top loaded vertical on RX. With my K9AY Loops now being swamped with noise I decided to see if anyone had improved on the “coaxial loops”. Sure enough, N6RK, Rick – has:

 

http://www.n6rk.com/

 

I built mine with a 300 pf air variable capacitor in parallel with a 500 pf doorknob capacitor – and I think the 50:5 toroidal isolation transformer is the “star” in this improved circuit. Anyway, the noise floor is so low – and these loops have such a sharp nulling effect, that I was able to point them at K9W and hear them very loud and clear – S8 – 9 this morning. When I need it, I do switch in the MFJ-959C – that has a tuner and amplifier built in. It also is used as a switch between my K9AY Loops and this Magnetic Loop. The MFJ-959C is one of the better MFJ products – the amplifier works great with the loops and it seems to be a quiet amp.

 

I also found the third source of noise in my shack. It’s the Astron SS-30M switching power supply that feeds my KAT-500. I think I need to start replacing these switching supplies with linear supplies one by one – as they go bad. Not sure why they create this “howling sound” – but this one does, and it only happens after the power supply has been on a while. I’ll have to read up more on RFI. . . .

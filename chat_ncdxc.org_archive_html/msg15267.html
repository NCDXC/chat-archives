The only times I have heard KH1 well were on 40 meters CW just after our sunrise
and on 20 meters before and after our sunset.
My 17 meter and 15 meter contacts were "vapor"

I got up today at 3AM to try and get them on 80/160. They were on 80 mostly working JA's.
Their signals were very weak and often times they faded into the noise only to come up out of it
moments later. 

No luck yet on 80/160 here. 

Paul N6PSE

On Thu, Jun 28, 2018 at 8:54 PM, AL_N6TA via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

I made a 17 CW QSO at about 0120Z today and had to struggle more than I expected to get good copy. The QSO was so marginal, I spent another 20 minutes to make another that was tough but 90%.

I then went to 15 M where there were NA spots for 21.285 and 21.023.  Nothing to copy at 285 but I could copy about 20% of the time on 023.  Midwest and SW were calling and I gave up due to chances were minimal that I could QSO.

 

I hear QRN but the S-meter is only at 0-1.  It may be that it is enough to bury the desired signal, I guess.

 

So, am I the only one having a tough time copying on these bands?  I am not hopeful for 12 and 10.  Perhaps my clear shot in this direction and 4 elements at 60’ are just not enough?

 

GL to all with your efforts with the KH1.  My next effort will be 80, assuming they get around to it.  Nothing the past two mornings…

On Aug 22, 2014, at 8:49 AM, Wayne Mills <n7ng@bresnan.net> wrote:

Rich,

 

FYI: “which it is, to the extent that LotW is an independent arbiter of QSL validity” LoTW is not an independent arbiter of QSL validity. The issuance of a LoTW certificate defines an accredited operation, callsign, etc. just the same as any accreditation is made in the DXCC system. The requirements are identical. Once a certificate is approved, it is automatically accredited in the parallel DXCC system.

 


I disagree (somewhat). LotW *IS* an independent arbiter of QSL validity, i.e., it is an unbiased third-party who is saying “these two stations worked each other at the time/frequency/mode indicated." This is quite different from ME saying “I have so-and-so confirmed”. That is why ARRL accepts LotW confirmations for awards without any further validation, whereas if it’s just me saying I have it confirmed, they require a QSL checker to verify that statement.

I’m not saying LotW is perfect, just that it IS an independent arbiter of QSL validity.

Rich KE1B

HamALERT  (hamalert.org) is not the same.  You download/install the app (phone/tablet) then set up 'triggers' (for DXped I use ONE call, ONE mode and ALL the band slots I need for that mode).  The app then alerts you when the spot appears (I also filter down by Spotter location).

You can also use it as part of your spotting network (spotting app, like most logging programs have).

Then as I work each band mode slot, I delete that BAND from each MODE trigger.

That allows me to get stuff (chores, ugh) done and be aware of what is being shown.

There are lots of tools out there to help (and as many 'tools' operating too ... well, you get it).

Once you get used to the format, setting up for a DXPed is pretty quick and painless.

I also have triggers set (per mode) for any ATNO that may pop up (because they're RARE to me now).  That's a little more complex but not difficult.

73,
Rick nk7i

PS You'll notice I can also filter out spotters that are not reliable; this one has been spotting when it's just not happening (15 FT8 at 10 PM from Seattle).

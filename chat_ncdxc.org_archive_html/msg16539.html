I listened for XX9D at sunrise +/- 15 minutes,
and only copied their call once.  An hour before
SR, they were not even a waterfall trail.  There
were a half dozen or so stations calling, and it
didn't appear they were working him.  I heard
Glen W6JGB on the Monterrey coast calling him.
The other callers were out of our area, AFAIK.

My plan is to be in the shack every morning
around 30 min before SR and hope for better
propagation.  We need that for both ends of
the QSO.  Looking at the web site for XX9D, it
appears that they are confined to an urban
area because XX9 is so small, so they will
not have low receiving noise.  They also
don't have K1ZM.  How many extra dB is he
worth?  Hi.

Rick N6RK

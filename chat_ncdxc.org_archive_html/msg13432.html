My take, from reading the ARRL press release, supplemented by the monument website …

 

1.  The monument is administered by 4 co-trustees, two of which are part of the State of Hawaii.  However, prior to 26 August 2016 Midway (only) was administered by the US Fish & Wildlife.  For DXCC purposes, this qualified it for entity status separate from Hawaii.  Furthermore, since Midway is between Kure and the rest of Hawaii, Kure previously qualified for separate entity status under another DXCC rule.

 

2.  On 26 August 2016:

a. The monument boundary was expanded, and

b. Midway’s administration changed from US Fish & Wildlife to the monument’s administration.

 

3.  Kure and Midway are within both the old and new monument boundary.  The expanded boundary itself is irrelevant to the DXCC rules.

 

4.  Because Midway’s administration changed to that of the rest of the monument’s islands, it no longer qualifies as a separate DXCC entity.  Therefore, Kure is no longer separated from the rest of the monument by a separate DXCC entity, so it also loses DXCC entity status.

 

Ed W0YK

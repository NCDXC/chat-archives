> 
> Rich and NCDXC friends,

> I am finding LoTW adoption rates among FT4/FT8 users to be very high and I am receiving fewer Bureau cards, even from the Japanese. 

Interesting. I am seeing it differently. Yes, many (most?) FT4/FT8 users use LoTW. That makes sense, since FT4/FT8 *REQUIRE* a computer connected to the radio, and the software automatically creates machine-readable logs which can be easily uploaded to the various QSL services. (Unlike old-fart CW ops, using paper logs.)

However, I *STILL* get bureau cards from JA stations who already have received confirmation via LoTW. I don’t see any tendency for JAs to stop sending bureau cards, and use LoTW for confirmations. I have *thought* about a QSL policy where I would not send a bureau card for any QSO that was already confirmed via LoTW, but I haven’t implemented it. Some people *really like* paper QSLs, even with LoTW (not me, except like yourself, for that really rare DX).

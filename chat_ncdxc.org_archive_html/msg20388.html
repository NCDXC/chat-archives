On Sunday, July 12, 2020, Steve Jones via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
I guess I wonder how much pressure there really is for our HF spectrum these
days.  The same trends that lead potential new hams toward the internet are
the same that have led to a big drop-off in international SW broadcasting.
The only SW stations I hear now are religious broadcasters, and occasionally
Radio Peking (!).  No more Radio Moscow, BBC, etc.  Totalitarian regimes
love that they can control the information their population receives via the
internet, as they have few other sources of uncensored information.

Personally, I'll never get a thrill from using a large network of commercial
infrastructure to contact someone.  It's the challenge of working with
Mother Nature that makes HF DXing so fascinating.  Very much liking fishing:
Yes, you could go to Safeway and buy a frozen trout, but you'd miss the
thrill of casting that dry fly (that you tied) to just the right spot on the
quiet eddy in the stream and getting a bite!

Internet and Safeway = Infrastructure.  HF propagation and the trout stream
= Mother Nature.

We need to inspire these kids to stop staring at their little screens and
start experiencing Mother Nature.  What the heck, the young techies could
even use the internet to share data on HF propagation studies that might
reflect on how the sun's variability affects global warming...

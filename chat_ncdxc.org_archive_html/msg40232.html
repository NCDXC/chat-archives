As someone in their mid 40s who is new to DXing, I agree that any and all awards offered should realistically be attainable.  I chose to not pursue HR for the fact that so many countries are not realistically accessible and I don't see that changing in my lifetime.

 

As to "If there was a "Currently Deleted" list, what impetus would there be for anyone to try to convince governments to permit ham radio operations in their countries?" the people in those countries could still want to have access to the technology and maybe the ARRL could pull a page from the SOTA and POTA programs and have an award for the person to first activate one of these countries or give special recognition for their efforts, beyond giving them headaches like they did for the ZS8W DXPedition.  You know, grow with the time, modernize, be supportive and benefit their members.

 

They are not the only award-issuing entity in town and if they don't wise up, stop their internal scheming, and start better serving their members, they'll learn the hard way that they won't be the only amateur radio organization out there, just like the NRA and very recently the National Association of Realtors learned.  All it takes is a group of people tired of the BS who decide to do something about it.

 

 

-73

I believe that if the ARRL offers and promotes an award that the Award should be attainable. Currently it is not possible (in a lifetime) to attain Honor Roll for anyone starting the journey now or in the recent past. Change is required when a program becomes stale.

Gene K5GS proposal to have an inactive list makes the most sense. It only requires a rule change and does not delete any entities. However, the outcry from the old timers of “NO CHANGE” is very emotional.

Compromise proposal: #1 Honor Roll rules remain unchanged and in effect becomes the legacy Award. You need to “Work them All” to receive the #1 Honor Roll status. No changes to the current rules for #1 Honor Roll. The rule to count entities for Honor Roll be changed to “you must have a total confirmed entity count that places you among the numerical top ten DXCC entities currently active in the last twenty years on the DXCC List” The Honor Roll has never been a “Worked them All” award. Honor Roll has always been an award for diligently “working the greatest amount of them but not all”. Additionally, a new award “High Honor Roll” could be created for working all currently active entities in the last twenty years.

In summary you would have three award levels (#1 Honor Roll, High Honor Roll, and Honor Roll).

Matt K6EME

Hi Jack-K6ZQ:.

Bob K6UDA has a nice video presentation comparing three of the leading Mag Loop antennas. 

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BwN900UVNBs

He compares the Alex Loop vs the CHA-P Loop vs the Alpha Loop. The DX Engineering RF Pro -1B Loop antenna is not among his antennas that are compared. 

Loop antenna prices range from $350 to $500.  Typically, they are used to "null out" a source of noise. 

Something that I think would be effective for you in your limited space situation is to take a five gallon paint bucket and mount a 2' aluminum pipe in it with a sack of fence post concrete. Then attach a Hustler 4BTV vertical antenna or a Hustler 6BTV vertical antenna. With three elevated ground radials, this would be a very effective confined space antenna. When not in use, you can simply lay it over on its side. 

I recently used a Hustler 4BTV from the Falkland Islands  with amazingly good results.

I also welcome NCDXC members to come and use my home station in San Jose if they need to work some DX. The door is open and the coffee is on. 

73 and good luck, 

Paul N6PSE

On Fri, Nov 4, 2016 at 10:05 PM, John Quense via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

I've got a first order DX problem:  I want a DX antenna but I live in a condo without a yard.  I've been using a temporary end-fed 20-meter dipole strung up in a nearby tree with some limited success but that marginal performance coupled with the hassle of stringing it up only to take it down in a few hours has me wondering if there isn't a viable, efficient, HF RX/TX, permanent, small space antenna solution.  

My understanding (admittedly basic) of how antennas work has me thinking that such an antenna doesn't exist.  Then a friend suggested that I look at a loop antenna.  He didn't claim to understand or to have any experience with loops but said that he had heard good reports on the performance of the Chameleon CHA F-LOOP Plus.  I checked out the antenna in the eHam.net reviews and found that this 36" diameter aluminum loop had almost thirty perfect 5.0 user reviews!  Impressive.  Is this antenna for real?

Does anyone know if the Chameleon F-LOOP Plus is actually a viable, HF TX/RX antenna?  Can the loop actually work and work well in both RX and TX on HF (at least 20-meters)?  Your comments and thoughts are appreciated.

73, Jack Quense K6ZQ

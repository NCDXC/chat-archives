If one does not subscribe to VHF Chat lists, can operating frequencies be posted to THIS chat  list?

On 5/19/2015 2:41 PM, Jim Brown via Chat wrote:
Two of our members, Scott, AF6RT, and Frank, W6JTI, are planning to activate CM79 this coming weekend (Memorial Day).

CM79 is quite rare, because 1) a very small corner of it is on land; 2) it's difficult to access -- 1.5 miles that climbs 800 ft; 3) it's in a wilderness area, so no overnight stays, no horses, no motorized or vehicular access; it's a challenge to put an antenna where it isn't pointing into dirt. CM79 is about 4 miles of Shelter Cove, CA.

The current plan is to be active Saturday and Sunday from about 9 am to about 4 pm. W6JTI will assist, both as a CW Op (Scott is a CW beginner) and in dragging stuff up the trail. They are prepared for CW, SSB, and JT65. I've loaned him my KX3 with 100W amp, the backpacker paddle, and a 20Ah LiFePO4 battery. W6GJB loaned him another battery.

I've encouraged Scott to adopt the operating strategy that W7GJ used very successfully for his last moonbounce trip to the S Pacific. He doesn't call CQ, he posts his instructions to the VHF chat rooms and just listens, then responds to stations he hears calling him. Saves a lot of battery.

I'm going to make a serious effort to work him. It will likely take a rover setup somewhere. The locations I'm studying are at K6XX and on Mt. Tam. Careful terrain study suggests that the best location would be something very high and very close to the coast. To count the QSO for the Fred Fish or VUCC awards, we must operate from a QTH within 200 km of our home QTH.

I will use my K3 and P3 with the 3-el Yagi that AD6E gave me, and will drag a Honda 2000i so that can fire up the KPA500 if I get desperate enough.

Anyone interested in making it a team either or both days?

73, Jim K9YC

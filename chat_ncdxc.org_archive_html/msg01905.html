On Fri, Apr 12, 2013 at 7:54 AM, KE1B <ke1b@richseifert.com> wrote:
>
> Was running Russians and Eastern Europe last night on 20 CW, when I got a call from TT8/US3EZ. It's not often that a Chad station has to bust a pileup to work *me*!  A few minutes later, I noticed he had settled in a few kHz away with his own entourage.

This is one of the great benefits of the last couple hours of a big
contest on 20M...calling CQ on 20 from about 2200z on the last day has
always netted some great African mults that come on in the last few
hours to work a few guys. So in those hours, I usually try to find a
clean freq (if I'm DX, sometimes outside the US phone band if its an
SSB contest) and call CQ pointed to East AF. Most times it ends up
with a few new mults and sometimes a stray zone 34 or 37 :) I'd
imagine after a while those guys get tired of fighting the pileups...

On 3/19/2021 1:02 PM, Rick NK7I wrote:
They are on 80 CW now, with the expectation of them staying in that mode tonight.  IF (big if) conditions are as good as last night, a CW exchange is possible on the West Coast (and maybe here in the west Rockies).  Hopefully they will manage the pileup by confining to a continent (they haven't yet, at all).
It's my take they've used FT8 to give the little guy a chance, it's simpler (one person operating) while other station chores (antenna assembly) can happen, plus it takes the ATNO pressure off for many (I had one already prior).  If one mode is not to your liking; be patient, they DO use other modes (some SSB noted today).  'Helpful commentary' has been noted on the cluster spots; we're better than that behavior (and they won't see them likely as not).
I saw two 80M condition peaks here last night (your mileage WILL vary according to conditions elsewhere); at OUR grey line (actually 20+ minutes prior to the sun passing over the mountain to my west) and THEIR grey line an hour or so later (after a tasty corned beef and Swiss sandwich, yum!).  OUR grey line was better.
Give 'er all she's got Cap'n, their noise floor is a contact killer!  Maybe even worse than the condo I owned in CA.
(No, I'm not THAT bored, it's a damp cloudy day and this beats cleaning the house or gardening.  It's also SO NICE to have a working station after struggling for so long with stealth antennas, when I lived in Mordor, hi.  With some confirmed slots now, other work gets done here now too.)
ALL of the prop apps AND K6TU say 80M won't work; but I was confirmed in Clublog for FT8 (at HIGH power out, ground plane 80M antenna*, not directional) so it's possible at your location too (if you can hear them).  Alas, they quit 160M long before darkness (or grey line) here in the west... so we'll never know. 

73,
Rick NK7I
* One 1/4 wave wire over the top of a (~65' tall) larch tree; ONE raised resonant radial (average height above dirt, only 5'); 1:1 common mode choke at the feed.  It's not ideal, works as well as the SteppIR on 80 but MAY give less NVIS (lower angle) in some cases (the SteppIR works well on 80M, neither are directional). It, like the 160M inverted L are temporary (for 3 years now, hi) until I put up a dual band (80/160M with a tuned circuit, no switching) L later this year.

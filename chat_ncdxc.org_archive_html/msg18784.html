I've got a kx3 connected to my laptop via a cheap usb-c - to - mic &
headphone port adapter. Those are going to the headphone and mic ports
of my kx3, respectively. The USB serial adapter is plugged into the
expected port on the kx3 as well, after passing through a usbc->usb
adapter.

This all works fine for wsjt-x under high sierra on my macbook pro,
logs to MacLoggerDX just fine, etc. - my primary gremlin for ft8 is
simply intermittent broadband noise that puts the local beams down to
-10 and washes the rest out.

RTTY, on the other hand.....

I've not yet found the magic to get it working correctly with fldigi
to run RTTY - I will sometimes get a partial decode, usually get what
looks like alphabet salad (kinda like my kx3's default rendering of cw
decode). If I get anything at all. (Operator skills for RTTY qsos
using fldigi are also a clear weak point).

I have reviewed the fldigi docs.

Does anyone have a particular reference they would suggest (likely a
youtube vid?) that would demonstrate correct/best practices for
operating using fldigi (not cocoamodem) and the usbc soundcard (not a
signalink/rigblaster) ?

I figure once I can get basic manual QSOs working, then I can take the
next step to try and deal with logging automation to MacLoggerDX on
modern macOS.

Failing that, is there anyone around San Jose that'd be game to
shoulder-surf? Since this is inherently portable, I can probably bring
it to you.

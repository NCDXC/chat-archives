Scotch 88 (precisely THAT number, it is thicker and UV resistant, less stretch, easier to use) then a layer of butyl tape (once connected, over the terminals too to keep them from sun/water/air contamination).  It is moldable, doesn’t harden or crack but can be a little sticky over time (why you add the Scotch 88 in case you need to disassemble later keep the contents clean).

As you apply the tape, a little back pressure to slightly stretch the tape on contact, overlapping by half the width, each wrap.  That means you end up with two actual layers and the stretching creates a better bond.  Be tidy; no peaks, no holidays.

You can tape around the Y the wire ends (you’ll have to figure out how, telling you won’t make it clear enough) make the connections, then butyl it all.  THOROUGHLY.  Don’t cheap out.

Over each outside connector as well, tape beyond the connector back onto the wire and butyl.  If you take it off later, (to swap wires) there will be a clean connector.

I would suggest Rescue Tape.  I use it on all my antenna connections these days, and it is very good at protecting connections from the elements as long as you apply it properly.   If you use the black tape, it holds up well against UV too.

You can buy it on their website, but it's also available on Amazon, and probably other places.  It may not be the cheapest solution, but it works well, and is easy to remove when the time comes.

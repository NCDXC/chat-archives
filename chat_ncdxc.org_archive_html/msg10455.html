On Thu,8/13/2015 9:57 AM, Steve Jones via Chat wrote:


Tony-
I’ll leave the roof work to the younger set, but I do have a comment on the antenna mount: It doesn’t appear your non-penetrating base plate is the problem. Rather you need good solid guy anchors in the right locations at the eaves of the roof. If you can do that, you probably don’t need to penetrate the roof anywhere, although there may be notches required where the guys bend around the edge of the shingles. A stronger base bolted through the shingles won’t prevent the wind blowing over your GAP antenna. It will simply tear out the base from its mounting holes instead of dragging it sideways.
That makes sense to me, Steve. Great idea -- IF both the base and the antenna is guyed part way up.
Good guys and hell-for-stout guy anchor points should solve your problem. And I agree with Jim K9YC that you need insulators at the ends of the radials before they transition to the poly guy rope.
Misunderstanding. The Gap antennas are not end fed, they are loaded dipoles. As such, they do not need radials. What I'm talking about is guying the antenna part way up for mechanical strength. Those guys need to be non-conductive, AND they must be insulated from the antenna so that RF doesn't cause heating and melt them when they get wet. I have had this happen to the supports for my 80/40 dipole when I failed to use insulators.
Yes, when radials are used, there should also be an insulator between them and a support rope, for the same reasons. The voltage at the end of a radial is high.

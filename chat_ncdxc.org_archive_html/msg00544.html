Good question!

 

I am a casual DXer and often use S&P around a contest for new band/modes.  I am not interested in wallpaper, contests or similar; I am only interested in making the contacts where I am my only competition.  I have over 165 worked and most of that is in the last year since I now have more time to pursue DX but I’m still in casual mode.

 

Having said that, I agree with many of the comments made so far to this question.  The SP&SP crew met the goals they had in mind.  Those goals were simplistic but obtainable for the limited group.  They obviously were not what the DX community would have preferred, but those goals are not obtainable.

 

Was it the best for everyone?  Not even close.

 

Was it satisfactory?  Only for the big guns, small 6 land HOA limited stations like mine didn’t have a chance.  I have a 340’ EDZ at 30’ (hidden in trees) and 100 watts (need a high power tuner to run the amp, its coming).   I never really stood a chance with this event.

 

Was the pileup, jamming, name calling, cops and generally poor operating the worst I’ve ever seen?  ABSOLUTELY!  Appalling.  Even with the K3, it was often hard to hear them.  It was shameful.

 

Could the operation have been run better?  I don’t know as I wasn’t there.  I do believe that some pileup management efforts could be improved; in particular to control the width and to demonstrate a consistent pattern (working slowly up or down the QSX band sliver) and sudden band changes (ignoring the pileup).  There was some obvious favoritism, operating styles not within the DX standards norm, but again I wasn’t there.  It does appear to be some of the worst operating conditions seen in recent history and generally under planned but the conditions are so harsh there that it wouldn’t be fair to point any fingers.  I am satisfied they did the best they could manage, period.

 

No, other than ‘the chase’ it was not fun for me.  I never managed to break through their local or lid induced noise on any band I managed to hear them (sometimes they were LOUD too, so it was possible for me).  I am zero for many tries.  It’s not sour grapes, I tried and lost; I’ll try again at some future date; I move on.

 

One thing they DID do was gather much of the crowds, so I could work other DX that was left to starve.

 

73,

Rick wa6nhc

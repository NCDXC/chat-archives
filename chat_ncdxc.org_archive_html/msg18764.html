My three SteppIR's that don't use trombones and
they have just worked for 10+ years.  Your
experience reinforces what I have heard about
trombones.  An extremely clever idea, but it
is too clever by half as the saying goes.
The successful implementation of it is evidentially
above the pay grade of the current staff at
SteppIR.  I don't know if some other vendor
could produce it successfully, but SteppIR has
the patent for another 8 years or so.

My "obsolete" MonstIR is looking better and better
in its old age.  It is basically the precursor
to the DB-36, w/o the trombones.  It is unfortunate
that it is no longer for sale.  The 70 foot tubes
seem somewhat unwieldy, but have proven to be very
reliable after 15 years.  The price ($4000) now
seems extremely reasonable.

The original SteppIR patents for the straight elements
will be expiring soon.  One could hope that Ultrabeam
might export straight element models at that time.

I have heard from a number of hams that their trombones
get stuck every time it rains, and SteppIR has no
solution.  Until this is resolved, it might not be
wise to purchase trombone models.

Rick N6RK

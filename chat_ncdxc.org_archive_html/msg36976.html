Some highlights from my notes (corrections welcome)
  • Ransomware hackers demanded $5M.  Was negotiated down to $1M.
  • Hackers destroyed backup files so things could not be restored from backups.
  • Decryption tool hackers provided mostly worked, but didn't work on the ARRL Finance System.
  • Not sure when Finance System will be back up.  Manual process in place.  Money spent on specialists ($40K/month) to attempt to recover it, but so far no reports of successful recovery.
  • LoTW recovery required rehiring the guy who wrote it, John Bloom.
  • LoTW was running on CentOS 6.3 (out of support) and using MaxDB 7 (out of support for over 5 years).
  • ARRL I/T Advistory Committee created, ARRL President K5UR appointed K6WX to lead it, but "She has no I/T operations experience."
  • K6WX wants to start from scratch and recreate the I/T environment
  • DXCC System is now being being rewritten from scratch in Python by John Bloom.  Original system was written in FoxPro (gone since Windows 98).  Data recovered, but FoxPro won't run on on modern Windows OS (and they couldn't find a PC with Windows 98?)
  • Membership records were not affected
  • N4MB not happy with the way things are going with the recovery
  • Credit Card info. was NOT stolen
  • MFJ was ARRL's largest advertiser, paying ARRL about $10K / month, but no more.

This video captured during the September 19th meeting of the SEDXC is worthy of ~40 minutes of your time to preview and possibly forward to others in your local ARRL club circles. 

Guest speaker SE Division ARRL Director N4MB candidly discusses, from the viewpoint of an ARRL insider and IT professional, the May ARRL ransom attack:  what happened, the recovery effort, costs, and the factors that have made it more difficult than expected to recover.  He provides meeting attendees an exceptional level of detail about the event and the recovery process.

Mickey also discusses the ARRL Board's decision on the Dues Increase and QST distribution decision, and what it has cost the ARRL in members since implemented.

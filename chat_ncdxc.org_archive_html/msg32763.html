> On Dec 12, 2023, at 9:33 AM, Paul N6PSE <pauln6pse@gmail.com> wrote:
> 
> Rich, I have been studying their plans listed on their website. Based on what I am seeing, I am not sure that they are going to correct prior errors or restore a reputation. 
> 
> Here is what I see:
> Three man team with four non ham helpers. (Why not a large team?)
> 

Because: (1) It’s hard to find people that want to go to Bouvet. Last time, quite a few “team members” refused to leave the ship once the landing conditions were determined. And (2) At least 4 of the stations will be remotely operated, either from the ship or the other side of the world, which reduces the number of people that have to be there.

> Camp 1 (Main camp)
> Radio 1 + Amplifier CW/SSB
> Radio 2 + Amplifier CW/SSB
> Radio 4 + Amplifier  FT8
> Radio 5 (No amplifier) FT8
> RIB 1  CW (No Amplifier)
> RIB 2 CW (No Amplifier)
> RIB 3 FT8 (No Amplifier) 
> RIB 4 FT8 (No Amplifier)
> 

Be careful NOT to conflate what Ken is proposing (essentially, a remotely operated station) with the “RiB”, a specific implementation of a full DXpeditiion station (including amplifier), developed and demonstrated by George Wallner, AA7JV. Ken (at least at this point) is not planning to use George’s RiBs, although my understanding is that George is consulting with Ken on best practices for remote DXpeditioning, having a fair amount of experience now.

> Camp 2-North America Camp  (Cape Fie)
> Radio #3 + Amplifier CW/SSB
> Radio #5 FT8 (No amplifier) 
> 
> So of their TEN radios, only four will have amplifiers and of that, only one amplifier at the Camp 2 North America Camp. 
> 
> For those small US stations that did not eek out a contact with 3Y0J, this cannot be very encouraging. 
> 
> Paul N6PSE
> 

Well, 10 radios with SOME amplifiers is a whole lot better than last time. (Which was two radios, no amps, and very modest wire antennas.) It remains to be seen if he will find funding, even at the claimed $150K level.

And for the record, *so far*, I have no personal association with this mission (last time I was a pilot station), and NCDXF has not even begun a formal assessment for funding.

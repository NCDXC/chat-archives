N5J has been using standard F/H mode frequently in the last few days. As everyone else noted, under noisy conditions, decoding of SF seemed compromised. Most had also commented that the threshold for SF decoding was around -15 dB, rather than the -23 dB seen in usual FT8. -15 dB is the accepted threshold for decoding on the WSJT developers chat. There has also been mention that disabling AGC may improve the decoding threshold slightly.

Despite that, I was impressed by the throughput seen with N5J with multiple streams. I’m not sure if the -15 DB number is directly comparable with routine FT8 SNRs, but if it is, that would make SF roughly equivalent to 2-stream F/H, and still far better than >2 stream F/H. I’m disappointed with the relegation of this promising new mode to the shelf during recent N5J operation. Any thoughts or observations out there?

Thanks, Bruce K3NQ

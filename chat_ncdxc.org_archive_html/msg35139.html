Nine streams at once, but how many stations per stream?  A potential of 18 stations being worked in one transmission?

That should lower the pileup more quickly either way.  And if done using the same encoding, the present WSJT-X should simply see it as 9 streams to decode (and not care); unless the format is entirely new.  I'm hoping it is more robust, more resistant to multipath and polar distortion (a dream hi hi).  It should help those DX operations limited to 100 watts and a wire, in theory.

MSHV also divides the transmit audio tones, poorly.  You can look at the signals (panadapter) and tell what app is playing.

Does it have high 'shoulders' just outside the stream (looks like a stream on either end but no decode)?  MSHV.  That's wasted energy better used in the wanted streams.

Do the streams vary significantly in signal strength (often by as much as 5 dB)?  MSHV.  It's not as efficient, wasteful of energy transmitted.

MSHV tends to be a EU team preference, but if they looked a little deeper, might change their minds.

I've used MSHV (when expecting a pileup after working DX) but limit to one stream only, so these two flaws are not displayed.

Right tool for the job, WSJT-X.  The coders of other apps, just haven't done as much polish for their product.

73,
Rick nk7i

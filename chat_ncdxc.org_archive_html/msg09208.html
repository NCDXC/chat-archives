> On Feb 6, 2015, at 15:54, Rick WA6NHC via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
> 
> Today after dealing with house chores, I decided to (staying out of the rain) attempt 15 M RTTY with K1N.  After some hours in the chair, I was successful (I saw you get one too Jim, well done).  I'm sure this was solid because I saw my call 4 times during the Q.  The op had excused himself to go get a hot meal on the Electra, which I fully understand would be a treat (even in the tropical 90%/100F+ heat).  I waited then jumped in the fray before it grew too large, as soon as he ate and returned (fast eater).
> 
> I now have all three modes in the log, so I can relax.  I'm sure the weekend will be a mess with more folks home, so just in time for this tiny station.  Apparently I was just in time, the band is fading here and gaining in Asia.  What is left on my personal goal is 10, 12 and 20, any mode, for a Q on each band.  160 is just too thin, so I won't be able to sweep the board.  I've heard nothing about 6 meters too.
> 
> A few annoying things have become blatantly obvious by watching today, likely already beaten to dust here before.
> 
> 1)  What part of DN (or up) is so freekin' hard to understand?  We all make the occasional lapse in settings (I thought I set that offset button) but come on; if you're doing your job and LISTENING, you won't be part of the problem.  You won't work the DX without paying attention.
> 
> 2)  What part of LISTENING is so difficult?  Related to the above, if the DX sends out a (partial) call [and more importantly is NOT allowing tailgating, a vile habit] and it is not even close to YOUR call, then SHUT it.  Sending blind is not useful; to anyone.
> 
> 3)  The RTTY operator is also shooting himself in the foot.  By repeatedly sending AGN AGN without any hint means the entire wall goes up again.  It isn't helpful for TX spotting on a scope, it doesn't reduce his near frequency interference and it slows things WAY down because he is too generic.  If one must send agn agn, then give a partial callsign to help lower the noise floor.  Of course, the above still applies, folks have to LISTEN to get the point.
> 
> 4)  Working a DX pileup is NOT the place to learn your software. Several times today, someone ON the DX frequency left their transmitter running diddles AFTER calling in the wrong place... doesn't help.  If you aren't familiar with the software, go play somewhere else until you're proficient.
> 
> 5)  Then lastly there are the channel cops... I know that has been covered thoroughly...  If someone catches one of these miscreants, we'll put them in the stack of other refuse bound for the wood chipper.  And their language is not what you'd use in church either...
> 
> I'm venting, I know; preaching to the choir.  But if we're to be Elmers and mentors for the new kids, remembering that we each have 2 ears and one mouth is an excellent point to consider.  One can generally presume there is a brain between the ears, but it's not always obvious on first view.
> 
> 73 and good hunting, while I take it easy, my main goals are met.
> 
> Rick wa6nhc

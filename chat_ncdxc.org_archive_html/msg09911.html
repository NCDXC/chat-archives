On Apr 3, 2015, at 8:44 PM, SPWoo via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Here in Denver they have a decent signal on 20m CW, peaking between S5-S7.  Even with the decent signal it took me two hours to break the pile running 1,500 watts into a three-element at 80'.  Needless to say I'm at a propagation disadvantage to those East of me.  I can imagine it would be an order of magnitude harder for you guys in CA.

One thing they could improve on is to call certain geographic area with the best propagation.  For example, in the morning they could call EU only.  That way the rate will be higher without all those East Coast callers who would have very little chance of getting through anyway.  Then in the afternoon work NA only, etc.  Just a thought.

Today I heard many JAs getting through on 20m SSB.  Why would JAs have an advantage over CA is beyond me.

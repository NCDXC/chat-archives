On Aug 5, 2025, at 2:37 PM, Paul Booth via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Matt & Co.

 

Most athletes for example are never able to attain Olympic status.  Why water down the top spot that many took their whole lifetime to achieve. 

I think that’s a bad analogy. Yes, most athletes will never win an Olympic medal. But every four years, quite a few folks do. So the AWARD is attainable during a person’s lifetime, even if difficult.

In contrast, #1 Honor Roll may not be attainable by ANYONE who starts now or in the recent past, regardless of dedication, skill, etc.

There seems to be plenty of awards available for all and do we really want the top award to be obtainable by all?  Is there value in that?  All this discussion is making me think I should apply before I die.

Only if that’s something you want engraved on your tombstone. ;^) If you have achieved that level, as you say, then you have achieved it. Bravo, and congratulations. (And you could probably put it on your tombstone even if you didn’t achieve it; I don’t think ARRL could successfully sue for copyright infringement for the use of the term “#1 Honor Roll”.)

Countries like North Korea will surely be available in the next 20-30 years along with other changes if we wait.  In any case, I would leave #1 Honor roll where it is and if needed add an award that provides incentive, still hard to obtain, that would satisfy more of the masses.

I don’t consider North Korea “unavailable”. As I said, there have been valid, confirmed QSOs logged within the past decade. I’m not sure it will require 20-30 years, unless you are talking about DPRK (or some replacement regime) making amateur radio available to its own citizens. I believe THAT might happen in your timeframe. (Heck, you can now vacation in Cuba, a country that used to be completely off-limits to Americans.)

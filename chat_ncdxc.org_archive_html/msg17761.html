Thank you for those kind words, Bob.  Here are some clarifications:

 

  1. William W. C. “Bill” Ball was the ‘Third Man’ who was with Tuska and Maxim during that critical meeting in 1911.  Bill Ball’s important role has been forgotten in ARRL history and lore, and I’m glad I am able to get the facts out there.
  2. No kidding – Earle G. Ham was issued callsign 1AW in 1916 – a couple of years before it was assigned to Hiram Percy Maxim.   I found it in the 1916 online callbook, and found it a remarkable coincidence, too.  And he was likely the first person with the last name of ‘Ham’ who was a licensed Amateur.  I found no earlier example.
  3. If Mrs. Lillian E. Ball had not been such an excellent pie baker and a generous ‘angel venture capitalist’ to Bill Ball and Clarence Tuska, who knows if we’d even have Amateur Radio as a service and a hobby today.

 

For me, the highlight of all this was the opportunity I had to speak with members of the Ball and Maxim families during the development of this presentation.  The families provided me information, materials and insights that made the presentation possible.

 

I was a fun, wild ride!

 

- 73 and good DX de Mike, K6MKF, NCDXC Secretary

On 12/31/2012 11:03 AM, Wayne Mills wrote:
So, what’s fair? Looking at the dilemma from a slightly different direction, one might say that those who have lived in only one place – but a very advantageous place, like the East Coast – where 160M to a lot of countries sounds like 40M – have an unfair advantage over those in less advantageous locations. Why should the folks on the East Coast be allowed to keep that advantage over everyone else? What’s fair?

Exactly the point I've been making for years about scoring rules for 90% of contests. After moving to CT from IL, my old SMC buddy KX9X bragged in QST a few years ago about how easy it was to work DXCC QRP in a weekend DX contest.  Heck -- I've only done that once or twice from here, with legal limit, SO2R, and an antenna farm most guys out east would die for.  I don't see any reasonable way to change DXCC to adjust for it, but with the ease of scoring complex contests in a computer, there's no excuse for not doing so with contest rules. 

73, Jim K9YC

I wanted to chime in on the S-line.  When I was a teenager, I lived a few blocks from an older ham with an S-line.  I used to visit him and watch him operate it.  Once I saw that, it was the ONLY rig I wanted to get; of course I never actually bought an S-line ($-line, hi).  But then I graduated from college and got a job working for Boeing.  They had a company ham club with a club station ... equipped with an S-line including the kW desk connected to a tower with a tribander.  It was indeed like a dream come true.  I spent many weekends at that station.  By that time, the S-line was discontinued.  A buddy of mine I went to college with got a job at Rockwell Collins and worked on the 651-S-1.  It was not oriented for hamming.

At the recent Visalia convention, Don Miller (yes THE Don Miller) at age 90 something gave a fascinating talk about his adventures.  I was very impressed that he did all his operating using an S-line carried in his sailboat.

I keep reading about how much dxpedition costs have
gone up.  I have no specific knowledge about this,
but I do read the Wall Street Journal, and
I don't understand how this "inflation" can be
explained, in light of general economic conditions.
The commodity bubble of the past decade has run
its course and most commodities are back to
2002 levels, most importantly oil.  (The only
exception is gold, which is not just another
commodity, and in any event is only 30% above
the 1980 price).  Wages aren't going up much;
I know my last significant raise was around
2003.  Central banks are worried about deflation,
not inflation.  The US dollar is at a 10+ year
high, so prices denominated in dollars are
especially constrained.  Airline tickets don't cost more,
and there is a glut of world shipping capacity.
Yet somehow, we are being told that charter boats
and helicopter services can command premium
prices?  What is special about them?  Not
arguing, just trying to understand.

Rick N6RK

I picked them up on 80M this morning and 15 and 12M tonight – they were bobbing up and down in the noise on 10M. So now I have them on 80 – 12M. Better than I had expected, and my next goal is 160M  - and 10M  last. What I thought was a slim on 15M CW last night was the real McCoy. That signal wasn’t half as good as 15M SSB tonight – just booming S9++

Same 12M – also booming. The 160M peak was excellent – really want that band – glad I can hear them so well. My current “farthest” on 160M is Namibia . . . so this would trump that by a wide margin.

 

They were very easy on all 3 bands today, and here is why the high bands were so good tonight:

 

SFI = 234! WOW!

SFU = 194!

A = 4

K = 2

 

Pretty darn “classic” good luck for this cycle and this time of the year (high bands open 1.5 hours or more past sunset).

Years ago as an Asst. Scoutmaster after a particularly long camping and hiking and search and rescue exercise with our Boy Scout Troop and several other Troops I was pulling our huge “Troop Trailer” behind my Jeep with all of our camping equipment, back packs, radio gear etc for 34 scouts along Highway 80 on our way back to the Bay Area from a fun but challenging weekend. Everybody was tired. I wasn’t paying as much attention to which lane I was in as I should have been and I was suddenly alarmed by a police siren and red and blue lights in my side view mirrors. A Highway Patrol Car then pulled around to the side of me and through his PA system he said “You are driving in the wrong lane pulling a trailer…you need to stay in the right two lanes, your trailer license needs to be renewed…and you were driving three miles over your speed limit. Clean up your driving. Be safe!  Thanks for the good work and 73!”. The highway patrol officer gave us a boy scout salute and then drove off…..as he drove off he sent us a HI in Morse code with his horn. You can’t make this stuff up. The Scout in the passenger seat next to me asked “How long have you known that Eagle Scout? I have no idea who he was.

 

No ticket, just sighs of relief…and we carefully stayed in just the right two lanes and under the speed limit all the rest of the  way back. How did the CHP officer know who we were? Our trailer had a big Scout Emblem on both sides with our Boy Scout Troop Number under the Scout Emblem, one could see that our monster “Troop Trailer”  was filled with back packs and camping gear…..and I had call letter plates on our Jeep.

 

Life is good!  73, Ted, K6XN, old Eagle Scout, Class of 1957

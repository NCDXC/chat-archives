Paul makes some good points, some other considerations are:

Most all large clubs/foundations require:

- Free LoTW upload in 6 months
- An article within 6 months
- Their logo on QSL card and website
- Team photo with flag and/or tee shirt

A donation condition (aka a "string") becomes a concern when it reaches too deeply in the DX team’s decision-making process, i.e., might accepting the condition cost more than the donation, or is it a wash? Donation conditions should be reasonable and measurable.

We're more than willing to submit spreadsheets that reveal our financials to major donors, and we provide clubs / foundations with a financial summary after the project. Transparency is important and clubs have a right to know how we spent their money.

Some DX-peditions request $5.00 for a direct mailed QSL, and timely LoTW. I think we all know that the total cost of producing and mailing the card(s) is less than $5.00, and the excess goes to offset the overall project cost. As you probably also know, most DX teams have a target of 50% of the project cost paid by the team and 50% from donations / grants. Without the excess QSL money the team payment would probably increase.

Over the past few years, some DX operations upped the ante, $10 for a confirmation, $20 for an expedited confirmation, and some don't ever provide free LoTW or free Buro cards. Personally, I don't see anything wrong with these policies, they put you, the buyer, in charge. If you don't like the price then wait for the next operation to that entity. There are no "ham spirit" discounts for the major expense line items: ship owners, fuel suppliers, customs brokers, shipping firms, port fees, airline excess baggage fees, etc.

When a DX-pedition team funds the project themselves, and accepts no external donations with conditions, that's their prerogative. On the other hand, DX organizations have a fiduciary responsibility to their members. Everyone needs to have confidence their money is spent responsibly and within the organization's charter. If donor conditions don't align with team policies, and the parties can’t find an acceptable solution, it's time to move on.

While this is a hobby, DX-peditions must be run like a business. We're spending hundreds of thousands of dollars (50% of it someone else’s money), in the US we file income tax returns, there must be accountability and transparency.

Over the years our team has had these types of conversations with several DX organizations, we found middle ground with all but one organization.

Like Paul’s Intrepid DX Group, we appreciate the help from those who support our projects.

On Tue Jan 5 13:59:31 CST 2021, Paul Wrote:

As someone who has applied multiple times to all of the major foundations/clubs, I cannot understand this logic.

Most US based Clubs/Foundations are very generous depending on the rarity and most needed standing. Few strings are attached. All of them are quite reasonable. These Foundations don't want to tell you how to run your DXpedition but they will verify your costs and budgetary numbers which is reasonable.

INDEXA requires a DXpedition to QSL the same way they received the QSL, which includes Buro cards. This rubs some DXpedition leaders wrong as Buro cards do nothing to support our costs. Some DXpeditions are now bypassing INDEXA for this reason.

Many of the European clubs "pledge" a donation to be funded after the DXpedition "if" the DXpedition takes place and satisfies the needs of their members. This factor does little to help a DXpedition with their upfront costs. One Major EU Foundation requests a copy of the DXpedition Log in return for their donation and they will then provide QSLs to their membership. This results in a very small donation and has some ethical considerations.

My two cents.....

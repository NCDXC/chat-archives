>> As you can see, we have voted to honor the Dxpedition with the
>> fewest operators, fewest total QSOs, fewest number of unique

>> There are some that believe two items of Dxpedition performance
>> measurement are the percent of unique contacts and the percent of
>> Zone 3 contacts.
>> I don't feel that these two items are valid measurement points.
>> For example with percent of unique contacts this figure increases
>> with the fewer total contacts that a Dxpedition makes. There are
>> only so many unique contacts available to be worked. If a Major
>> Dxpedition such as HK0NA, 7O6T or ZL9HR makes a lot of contacts on
>> many bands and modes, their percentage of unique contacts will
>> diminish as their number of overall contacts increases.
>> Conversely, a small Dxpedition that makes fewer contacts will have
>> a higher percentage of unique contacts. In my view, this is not a
>> valid measuring device for Dxpedition performance.
>> The percent of Zone 3 contacts as part of the total contacts is
>> also not valid as proximity and propagation are factors unique to
>> each Dxpedition. A South Pacific Dxpedition such as NH8S will have
>> a high number of Zone 3 contacts just based on their proximity to
>> Zone 3. A remote Dxpedition such as 7O6T will have a
>> correspondingly smaller percentage of Zone 3 contacts because Zone
>> 3 is further away from Yemen. For a Zone 27 Dxpedition such as
>> 7O6T to make many contacts on Zone 3, they must apply an extra
>> amount of effort and focus on doing so. For 7O6T to make
>> considerable more Zone 3 contacts than PT0S, indicates to me that
>> the extra effort was made by the 7O6T team. PT0S was much closer
>> to Zone 3 than 7O6T was. In my view, PT0S did not try to satisfy
>> Zone 3 and fell short of the goals of this award.
>> We all know that the costs to carry out these major DXpeditions
>> were enormous. As a Dxpeditioner, in my many presentations, we try
>> to convey to you just how costly these major DXpeditions are to
>> carry out. We try to help you understand all of the complexities
>> involved in conducting a major Dxpedition.
>> In examining the tallies of your votes and given the number of

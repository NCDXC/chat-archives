I have three EU-2000i generators that I use up at our Sierra radio site and they have been terrific performers for me. I have used  Honda EU-2000i generators now for over 7 +/- years and they have frankly paid for themselves in fuel economy over the years and all are still running after many many hours of use. We use auxiliary 6 gallon fuel tanks for each Honda EU-2000i and we typically start them up on a Friday up at the cabin and they run continuously until Sunday. I change their oil every 100 hours, their spark plugs and air filters once a year and their pull starter cords every year or two as needed J.

 

We also have three Generac 4000 XL 4KW+ generators up there and if maintained they are reliable but they do not have the fuel economy of the Honda EU-2000i generators and they are much noisier and heavier but they do have both 220V and 120V AC output as the Honda EU-200i generators only have 120V output (and 12v output).

 

I have not used the Generac generators advertised by DX Engineering but FWIW I *love* my little Honda EU-2000i generators….and as an old fart I can easily still lift and carry them.

 

Also, I have the Honda kits to couple two Honda EU-2000i generators together for twice the power as needed for “contest” use and the kit is easy to install and  functions flawlessly.

 

Good luck!  73, Ted, K6XN

Yes…I have both units.  One I take to Field Day, CQP county expeditions, and other Ham outings.

 

The other runs my house when the power is out.

 

The Generac isn’t an inverter type, and hence doesn’t have nearly the load regulation that the Honda does.  It’s ambient noise is a lot higher too.

 

With the Honda set to eco mode, it slows down a lot, making it very quite and gas conservative.

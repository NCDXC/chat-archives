Hi Steve,

I will throw another one in the mix.  I like XMLog (http://xmlog.com).  It
is also free.  I have tried a few of the others including the DXlabs suite
which I found to be overkill, it does everything but it takes too long to
load and uses most of your computer desktop.  As I am usually running other
applications such as sound card apps, windows to control my remote station,
etc., I like a minimal logging program.  XMLog launches separate small
(resizable) windows (as do many of others) and you can minimize or turn off
the ones you are not using.  It does have a packet window and a spots map
window that I really like.  The spot map shows each band that you have
selected like an old style tuning dial with all of the call signs of the
spots at their spotted frequency.  The ones that you need for an all time
new one are in red, the ones that you need for a band or mode are in blue
and all the ones that you already have are in black.  You can just click on
the call and it will tune your radio to that frequency, it can also turn
your beam if you have a computer interfaced rotor.  I keep the spots map
window open 24/7 so I can just look for ones I need whenever I walk in the
shack.  XMLog also interfaces with LoTW.

Don't worry about choosing the wrong program; they can all export a standard
ADIF file format so you can move your log data base to another program very
easily.  Just pick one and install it, put in a few QSOs and see how easy it
is to use.  In fact you may want to try several programs as most of them are
free and it is easy to move your log file between them to compare reports,
etc.

Jump in and have fun.  Don't try to master all of the features at once.

73,
- Craig, AE6RR

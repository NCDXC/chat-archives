Yes, really!

That was using my 80M ground plane (elevated base and elements).  "Use the power required to make the contact."  At legal limit for an hour, I got a -17 report (pretty lousy, proving they aren't hearing well since I gave them a +02, my noise floor is very low).  I was lucky, it only took the minimal cycles (no repeats) to complete the contact once they heard me.  Others, not as lucky (I saw many repeats or restarts from incomplete QSO's).

What is also often misunderstood is that FT modes are low signal reception modes; not low power.  Use what is required for output to make the contact.  Noise floors matter, a LOT.  At EACH end of the QSO.

Full legal limit is often the norm on the lower bands, in particular 160M and on 6M (and above) EME, meteor scatter and similar modes.  Gain only comes in two flavors; huge antenna arrays or raw power (sometimes both are needed).

I did manage to make DXCC on 160M with only 500 watts (inverted L), because that was all I had then.  It took all winter with many nights in the shack and very early mornings (even with low noise, a better RX antenna would have sped that along).

On 40-6M, I can afford much less output (generally, there are exceptions) because I use a gain antenna (beam)... for the lower bands, it's not the case.  I try barefoot (life is FAR too short for QRP if you DX) and if needed, double the output until the contact is made; up to the limits of the band.  Watching others, this is pretty much standard DX practice; add until the contact is made; the rest is technique.

I'm at over 300 entities confirmed and (slowly) climbing; without an amp it simply isn't possible with the recent conditions, even with FT modes.

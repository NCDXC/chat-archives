Hi Al,
On the 2nd at 22:39 (UTC) FT8 for HU1DL and 22:21 for TI9A on CW.  One call each, logged.  Probably at 500 watts into 4 elements, less would have worked as well, the band was 'sparse'.
I also have HU1DL on 17M, same day, CW and FT8 a few hours earlier, phone after the above....  equally easy.  TI9A on 17M FT8 on the 3rd (NOT easy, almost nothing is with them).
Yah, I tend to get 'focused' when teams are out and I'm still a slot slut; who knows when someone goes back there, so collect the whole set now!  ;-)
Do NOT discount the upper bands, even 6M FT8 was spotted a few days ago (in the SE, I checked heard nothing here).  I run DXwatch pages filtered to the group(s) and watch them often, then attack.
When teams are active, I print up a grid sheet (can provide it here or on request in .ODT or .doc) to display as many as 5 teams on one page.  It shows what has been confirmed before (an X on that grid square, leaving what else is wanted unmarked), a check mark for each band slot worked with that team, then I 'cross' the check mark when it shows up in Clublog (confirmation, generally, just not officially blessed).  That makes comparing new spots to log SO much simpler than digging through the log and it minimizes dupes, so others have a more fair shot at the DX.
If the original contact was 'thin', I wait the required 15 minutes (ARRL DXCC rule time filter) and work them again.  Repeat as needed until I'm sure it's solid, the shotgun approach (but most times they're all good, the teams are generally VERY good operators.) 

73,
Rick NK7I

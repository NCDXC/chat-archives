I think the question revolves around what are the criteria for valid. And if they are flagging it what information and how credible is the information they are flagging it for.

Thus my concern on a little transparency about what process they are using.

If for instance I go to.. say KP4 (well technically thats a US entity.. how about PJ4 or something) and operate they will probably take that right into LOTW without question.

Then things like reciprocal licensing and cept and all that stuff..

There is a history of others operating with a non-P5 call that have been determined valid.  

The communications from an entity with essentially no diplomatic ties to the rest of the world would infer their process of approval may not be in line with the  ARRL with a definitive written authorization.

in other words is there a burden of proof?  what are there selection criteria.  are they consistent between all entities, or is there a burden of assuming fake until proven.  or can it be definitively disproved.

All valid things and represent the complexity of the need for some official word from the dxcc desk and some process transparency.

This is the #1 wanted DXCC in the world.  its page one news in any ham publication.  

Interesting stuff here.

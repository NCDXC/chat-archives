It took a while to sink it, but I guess I really did receive an award for helping my fellow DXers at the Saturday banquet in Visalia!  All I can say is ‘Thanks’ for thinking of me.  Being recognized is surely appreciated and I’ll do what I can to earn the award again!

 

Without being fully aware of why I got nominated, etc., it may relate to my efforts to spot DX to those that may need a ‘new one’, whether it is a ATNO, not band or band mode

I send the spots, typos and all, to notify others’ nearby’ of what they may be able to work.  During the IDXC I was asked twice why I sometimes mention the power I was running when I made the QSO.  I believe if a spot is made without the context of the conditions, it only says that someone made a QSO in the local area.  You would know it was N. Cal and may know I have a 4 element Yagi at 60’ for 20-10 M.  If I were always running legal limit with my antenna and I mention the DX was weak, those with lower beams or more simple antennas or with lower power, would likely have a harder time being heard.  If I mention 50 W, someone with a lower gain antenna and 500W would have a similar shot at the QSO.  Anyway, I will try to remember to state the condx on my end so you can better judge the potential.  As an avid DXer, I would be trying anyway since I have heard stations that others 30 miles away did not and vice versa.  Seems like there are ‘micro-zones’ for propagation and if you need it, go listen and see what develops!

 

Again, my heartfelt thanks for the recognition for whatever reasons!

 

Vy 73 and best DX,

Al

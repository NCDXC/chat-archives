The answer is different depending on if you are the DX or the DXer, i.e., the DX chaser.  The DX should maximize time working stations, not writing emails or qrz.com diatribes about dupes.  There are lots of excellent reasons why dupes occur and it is not worth slowing down the DXpedition QSO rate to sort those reasons.  The DX should simply work all callers and not worry about dupes.

 

If the DX station wants to do something useful about dupes, he should increase his QSO rate so that dupes don’t matter!  That is something he can work on.  Trying to get the world to stop duping you is not a very productive, or gratifying, endeavor.

 

The DXer should not work unnecessary duplicate contacts and I believe most DXers are pretty good about this.  Those that do actually duplicate a prior contact do so for good reasons.  They did not think the first QSO was good.  Nothing wrong with that and nothing wrong with trying a second time.  Life, and DXing, are not perfect.  Stuff happens that are out of the DX station’s and DXer’s control.  Both should chill out and put more effort into operating instead of second-guessing the operating!

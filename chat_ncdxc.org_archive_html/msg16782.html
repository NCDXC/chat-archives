A bit off topic but thought I’d throw it out there.

I am going to order a custom positioning lanyard for use when I climb my Rohn 45 tower.  I have been using a Klein Lineman’s lanyard which is made of KleinKord material with snap hooks on the ends.  That Klein is buckle-adjustable but it made too long for simple towers.  It is also stiff as heck and tough to adjust.  But safe.

There are times when I want to tie off to a mast above the tower and a shorter lanyard is needed.

So, I want a continuously adjustable design.  I have only found standards that are 4’-6’, too long.  I want one that will do the 48” long I need for the 45 tower and will be adjustable down to 24” for smaller work.  Options include ropes with Prusik knots and I am uncomfortable with that solution though they seem popular for some uses.  So, a 1” wide web type is my choice and the link here is that type:

https://engrope.com/shop/industries/arborist/1-adjustable-polyester-lanyard/

 

This company will custom make it for no extra charge.  If anyone wants one of these, I will order it and deliver it to a meeting or Visalia.  I’m in no rush but would like feedback sooner than later.  And, if you have another idea, I am interested to hear it if it is a safe solution!  I am done taking the shortcuts and have ‘the good stuff’ now.

 

Thanks for the BW.

"Hey QRP-er - If you haven't already achieved DXCC HR #1, sorry bub, you never will" . . . . 

***************
The unfortunate message to new young DXers had been that you could never work them all. This inequity was finally settled some time ago after many lobbied for what is now the "CURRENT" vs "ALL" (including deletes) differentiation for DXCC and the Honor Roll. Perhaps N7NG can shed more light on the history.

In fact, you can look at the "ALL" totals:

‎www.arrl.org/system/dxcc/view/DXCC-MIXED-20140214-USLetter.pdf

and see quite a number of OT's who's calls are noted with asterisks with very high "ALL" numbers who aren't even on the Honor Roll. An indication of lack of activity. Just a tribute to being old.

‎www.arrl.org/system/dxcc/view/DXCC-HR-20140214-USLetter.pdf

"CURRENT" totals reflect just that - current activity as defined by the number of entities that count toward DXCC. The top of the HR is achievable. It theoretically will always be using the "CURRENT" definition; the definition of what constitutes a country not withstanding. Hopefully the DXAC's current review of the rules will address the latter.

The DXCC HR isn't easy and it shouldn't be. No one wants to diminish the challenge and satisfaction that comes from its achievement. But it is an enigma when a wet rock or a entity with no indigenous population counts for DXCC and Kosovo doesn't.

I know. My mistake is attempting to look at the situation logically.

Steve WB6RSE

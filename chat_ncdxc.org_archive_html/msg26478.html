I got mine for $3,500 (used) but haven't seen any others for less than $4,000. The TS-990 doesn't have the best Sherwood stats (though the IK8TNG custom filter helps), but if you prefer knobs and buttons to menus, it's a great radio. In fact, if they graded radios on number of front-panel controls, it would likely be the greatest radio ever made hihi

 

The 200w barefoot power is nice too, as are the dual color LCD displays, and the feature set is amazing, especially if you like to dabble on digital modes other than FT4/FT8. You can easily do PSK, FSK and RTTY with no additional hardware or software. It even has a CW decoder (may require a firmware update to access), and multiple customizable equalizer settings for both TX and RX. Operation is mostly intuitive after the initial learning curve. Most features can be customized by pressing and holding the button. I learn something new every time I look at the 300-page manual (reading the entire manual is pretty much mandatory to use the rig effectively). The waterfall is adequate, but you cannot display the waterfall for both main and subreceivers simultaneously. 

 

Some users have encountered problems with the finals (the dreaded "Error 005"); using lower power outputs may reduce the risk. Getting service can be arduous and expensive.  I have not encountered this error personally. My main complaint is that the noise level could be lower; some of this is phase noise for which there is no fix (other than buying a TS-890, K3/4, Flex, etc.), and some of it is that the NR1 and NR2 noise reduction circuits are less than spectacularly effective, though they do help some. And this is a big, heavy radio. But mostly I love it!

 

73 and good DX,

Andy K6ZP

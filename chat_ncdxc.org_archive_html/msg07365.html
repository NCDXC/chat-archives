On Fri, Aug 22, 2014 at 10:31 AM, Bob Lanning via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

V or not V….

 

I use DXLabs suite of applications.  Here is how it works.  I have tested this backwards and forwards.

 

When I log a QSO, DXKeeper (the logger application) immediately uploads that QSO to Club Log.  I don’t see it happen but checking Club Log I see a W in the entity row and band column.

 

When I get a QSL card in the mail I manually change the record in DXKeeper to show a Y in the Rcvd field for QSL cards.  I right click and select “Upload to Club Log” and Club Log gets the update.  Checking Club Log I see the W is now a C.

 

When I get an LoTW update via a DXKeeper query I make (button push) the LoTW information is received by DXKeeper which puts a Y in the Rcvd field for LoTW for that QSO.  I then right click and select Upload to Club Log.  Checking Club Log I see the W is now a C just like if a card was received.

 

Now for Verification – a different process.

 

Then I apply LoTW confirmations (confirmations in the ARRL’s LoTW file) to my DXCC account.  Once I receive notice from the DXCC that the submissions have been completed, I go to each QSO record in DXKeeper and manually change the Y to a V.  There is a button “VFY” for that.  Then I right click that QSO record and select Upload to Club Log.  Checking Club Log the C is now a V.

 

The same process is used for QSL card acceptance at DXCC.  The same steps are performed.

 

That is how it works here.

 

I’m interested to learn how some DXKeeper users have V’s in all confirmed locations in Club Log without doing anything.  Maybe I have missed an automatic feature somewhere.

 

Each - LoTW, DXKeeper and Club Log is a separate standalone entity.  DXKeeper (logbook) has a user interface feature for both Club Log and LoTW.  Depending upon how the user has set up DXKeeper some things are automatic like the entry of a new QSO is uploaded to Club Log behind the scene.

I think we are dealing with a problem of semantics and ‘special language’ here.

 

The Club Log’s explanation of the V is:

 

QSL_RCVD = V

This means you've presented the card to ARRL and received credit for the QSO. This is signified by a blue 'V'.

 

However, as far as I know, in LoTW a QSL is a QSL is a QSL – there is not distinction within LoTW as to whether a QSL has been ‘Awarded’ or not - that information is only part of your DXCC account.   ‘Awarded’ has a special meaning within your DXCC Account and has been taken out of context and used incorrectly by Club Log in this instance.

 

When you download your LoTW QSLs into your logger, your log and resulting ADIF file then contains information indicating that QSOs have been confirmed by LoTW.

 

When uploaded to Club Log, this results in the V shown for that QSO.   It works just this way for me when I update my HRD Log from LoTW, then update Club Log from my HRD Log.

 

Club Log’s label for V is:

 

V Verified (Awarded)  is not correct.   To my knowledge, Club Log has no visibility into my DXCC Account and cannot tell whether an LoTW QSL has been Awarded or not.

 

I think Club Log’s label for V should be:

 

V Confirmed in LoTW

 

And their explanation of the V should be”

 

QSL_RCVD = V

This means you've presented the card to ARRL and received confirmation for the QSO, or that the QSO has been confirmed electronically by LoTW. This is signified by a blue 'V'.

 

Just my view of the elephant …

On Aug 22, 2014, at 2:00 AM, John Miller via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

 

There were two questions asked last night that needed further follow-up.

 

Q1 (KE1B + others):  For a particular QSO, what does the blue "V" mean?

 

Answer:   As discussed, the blue 'V' means you have a QSO verified after presenting the QSL to ARRL for an award.

 

ClubLog Reference:    http://clublog.freshdesk.com/support/articles/53201-what-does-the-blue-v-

 

 

That is simply incorrect. I just checked my log on Clublog and there are hundreds of “V” entries. I checked my log in MacloggerDX (the program that exported the ADIF that was uploaded to Clublog) and there is not a single QSO with a “V” anywhere in the QSL-Received field. 

 

I (manually) cross-checked a few entries and determined that Clublog indicates a “V” if the QSL-Received field contains “LotW”; it indicates “C” if the QSL-Received field has an entry other than “N” or {blank} (e.g., “Y”, “Bureau”, etc.) but not “LotW”. That is, Clublog is considering a LotW confirmation as a “verified” QSL (which it is, to the extent that LotW is an independent arbiter of QSL validity), and simply notes “C” when it is the owner of the log that is saying he has it confirmed (e.g., by a physical card). It does not appear to have anything to do with submitting credits (LotW or otherwise) to ARRL for awards purposes. Indeed, MacloggerDX has no facility for even recording such a case. (Perhaps Clublog considers a QSL to be “verified” if it is EITHER LotW or marked with a “V”, but I don’t have any entries marked with a “V” to check this.)

 

And yes, I went and also cross-checked to see if somehow the ARRL awards submission information got into my MLDX log, and it has not. For example, I have worked Ireland on 10 meters, and have it confirmed via LotW, but have NOT submitted that particular QSO for any award with ARRL. (I keep an independent record of this stuff in a big Excel spreadsheet—John will confirm that I create some of the most complex Excel spreadsheets you have ever seen!) However, Clublog shows that Ireland on 10m is “verified”. That is, it considers the LotW confirmation as “verification.”

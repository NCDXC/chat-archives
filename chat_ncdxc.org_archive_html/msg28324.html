   And to further confuse things, a recent DXpedition ran with some kind of FT8 filter software that would automatically filter out duplicate QSOs. I had one of these "busted" QSOs that (to them) was complete, but (to me) looked like it failed. So I ended up spending the rest of the afternoon trying to make a complete contact, but (as it turned out) they ignored all of my subsequent calls because they thought I was a Dup! Of course there was no way for me to know that's why I wasn't getting a callback, so I just ended up inadvertently filling the air with QRM (and getting very frustrated!). 

   The DXpedition web site said they would be filtering out dups, but it was only after they had posted their logs that I discovered they did claim to have a QSO with me. I had to go back through my ALL.txt file to find the one "busted" contact (out of all of the other calls!) to reconstruct the QSO.

On Feb 16, 2023, at 10:30 AM, Gene K5GS via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

FT8 is a terrific mode, it has allowed people to make Qs they otherwise would have not been able to make.

However, there are some anomalies with the mode and/or software that cause the confusion that we witnessed over the last week. 

What probably happened in Paul's situation:

- You call the DX station
- The DX responds with a signal report
- You respond with a signal report
- The DX sends a RR73
- Because of congestion, jamming, propagation, or some other unseen factor you don't receive the RR73
- The DX logs the Q because they sent you the RR73
- Your software doesn't log the Q because the RR73 was not received

This is not an unusual or one time FT8 occurrence, it happens more than you might think, even during the same calling session.

It's easy to miss this sequence and you might never know it happened until the DX log is posted or you pick apart your ALL.txt file. Happened to me with 3Y0J, I noticed the RR73 wasn't received. With no on-line log what's one to do?

On two recent DXpeditions that did not have on-line logs during the DXpedition:
I made 8 FT8 Qs on one DXpedition but their log showed 16, on another DXpedition I made 3 Qs but their log showed 5.

FT8 software isn't as foolproof, its a work in progress.

Of course, there's always the possibility that someone is bootlegging call signs.

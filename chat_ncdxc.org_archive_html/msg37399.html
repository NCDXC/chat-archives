Long answer coming.  But the 3B9 on 15 M, 9N7AA on 10 M and ZD7W on 40 M.
The rest of the story:
Around 2016 ZL7G was a DXPed to Chatham Island.  I needed 40 M SSB and finally found him just before I had to leave the house for work.  I heard him say QRZ and in one call,he came back.  Gave me a report and said he was working EU but I was not listening long enough to hear that he wanted EU.  It was over in 30 seconds from when I found him and when I went QRT.  I attempted to QSL but they said I was not in the log.  Bummer.
I found ZL7IO this morning and heard his call once and then he was gone.  I scanned the band for a half hour and he popped back on and I got an easy solid contact.  FInally will nail down this spot. Whew.
The Rest of the rest of the story:
In 1976, I worked ZL3NR/C on 75 M SSB for a new slot for Chatham Is.  I managed to NOT QSL due to being off for college and when I tried to confirm later, he was SK and nobody else on the operation could help.  I even tried his last known address with a letter to anyone that would read it.  Nothing.  I have wanted this slot ever since and never made it with any other ZL7 operations.  This morning when I worked ZL7IO, I asked if he had any plans for 75 M SSB and he said things were bad but he managed only 25 contacts.  He then asked if I wanted to try now and I thought that it was too late, 1434Z.  But I said OK and he asked me to call on 3750 kHz.  I went there fast and had to call him down about 1 kc.  With the receiving loop improving the SNR, I heard him Q4 and we made a QSO, solid.  I was pretty shocked but mostly very happy to finally get this slot I wanted for years.

Overall, my contest goal was to work 100 countries.  Have not counted yet but must 'have gotten close.  All of this with my beam pointing to 290 degrees.
It was fun!
Al  N6TA

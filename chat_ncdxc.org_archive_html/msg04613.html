I have no problem with remote operation for those who cannot build a station at their home, or for putting good operators at the controls of a remote station for a contest, but these promotional statements from RemoteHamRadio Newsletter SCREAM abuse of the DXCC program. Here's an easy path to all the DX awards that money can buy. Finding it hard to work EU and AF on 160M from the west coast? Simply rent a station in W1 that's 2,500 miles closer and the difficult is made easy. Having trouble with OC and AS from W1? Same solution.
"The W7xx site played very well in the month of November for those seeking DXCC into the Pacific Rim."
"CQ DX from Summit, NY. In the month of November the 160M four square was on the air with a bang! The users on the system reported working many new 160M DXCC entities that they could only dream about in the past."
While we probably don't have a shot at changing the dumb DXCC rule that permits this, I think we as a club should voice our disgust with this sort of thing. 

73, Jim K9YC

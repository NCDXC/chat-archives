On Thu, Jan 22, 2015 at 4:28 AM, Rich Holoch via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

The French had replaced some of their previously manned meteorological stations with remote controlled stations. Places like Glorioso, Tromelin Europa, etc – which all monitor weather in and around Madagascar and the straights therein. They used to be common – with hams stationed there – but now are quite rare and sought after.

 

Would it be “legal” under the new DXCC Remote rule to say, set up a remote controlled – solar powered ham station with a vertical on say 20M – and run QSO’s on that rare entity 24x7x365?

 

It seems like the rule is that the station has to be land based – but the control op can be anywhere.

 

I wonder if the US F&W would allow such “boxes” – in places where they do not allow humans to tread? Maybe save some huge money in the process? (Yes some of the “romance” and adventure would go away too). Romance is expensive these days – hi hi.

 

Maybe this concept would _expand_ what we could chase? Maybe new awards would even be created? HF Grid Squares that have never been activated before?

 

Maybe HF fox hunts of sorts with foxes all over the world – and  . . .

 

OK – I am getting carried away. And I might be wrong with my assumptions.

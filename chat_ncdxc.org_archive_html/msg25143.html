I have both the 1.3K-FA Series 1 and 1.5K-FA Series 2 and enjoyed trouble free operation normally in MAX setting.  The 1.3 since 2015.  Then in June 2019 I received the 1.5.  The 1.3 is on the FT-1000D and IC-781 while the 1.5 is on the TS-990S and IC-7851.

 

The 1.5K Series 2 is CCAS when run at MID (1KW).

 

The Alpha 8410 and Quadra are on the bench being considered for sale.  The 30S-1 remains on the S-Line.  Still being occasionally used.

 

The automatic features and quiet running of the two SPE amplifiers really makes it simple and makes a 1.3 or 1.5 KW transceiver.

On 2/28/2022 13:12, Paul N6PSE via Chat wrote:

I recently suffered a failed PA  in my Expert 1.3K-FA. I was not aware of anything on my end to cause this failure. 

 

I had been running it in the high power setting and driving it to 800-900 watts in FT8. 

 

Bob Hardie has given me this guidance which I was not aware of and am passing along to my NCDXC friends that use this amplifier. See below.

 

Paul N6PSE

 

1. If power output needed is 550W or below, use LOW POWER level.   The voltages on the final transistor are lowered to maintain linearity on LOW and MID and are maximum on MAX. 
Otherwise, distortion, oscillations, garbage, etc can occur. 

2. Same for MID power.  Only run between 600W and 1kW.   And on MAX power, use between 1100 to 1300 or so watts. 

3. FT8 is NOT supposed to be used in MAX power level at all.    Use only in LOW and MID power ranges.  Observe the different power ranges to maintain linearity.

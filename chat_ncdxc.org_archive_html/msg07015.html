AH - Memories! (Sorry – bad pun).

 

My first computer was an IBM 360 at University of Pennsylvania at Lock Haven. We were still using punched cards for COBOL, Hexadecimal Assembler and Fortran, and for Basic – we still had the big yellow rolls of paper and clattery RTTY like terminals.

 

My summer job was as an RJE /RJO “operator” at Worthington Standard Pump in East Orange (14th Avenue I think – that was a long time ago).

 

My first job was coding Octal Assembler at Kodak in Rochester, NY – making Instant Print Film on PDP-11’s and where the OS wasn’t even RT-11 or RSX-11M – but “Kodak Park Operating System”.

 

When I moved to California – I did about “3 years time” at Lockheed as a COBOL programmer on MVS and VM/CMS but with “LOSS” – Lockheed Online Storage System. No kidding.

 

Then in 1984 – joined Oracle as employee #127 on VAX/VMS.

 

At home – had Apple II, Commodore 64 before “real” PC’s hit the market.

 

Was sooooo happy to finally get on UNIX (Solaris, HP, DEC Alpha, DG Aviion, IBM AIX, Sequent, etc) and then Linux. OSX is fine since it’s a BSD variant . . .

 

AH – Memories!

I'm another one who has been tinkering / working with computers for awhile.  My "first" was a PDP-8, followed by a PDP-11/75.  The first machine I ever owned was a Colombia "VP" - a dual disk drive 8088 "portable" computer that folded into a package the size of a large suitcase.  I think it weighed in at about 50# plus or minus.  I can still remember feeling pretty cool hauling it around my client's offices.  Nothing impresses like cutting-edge high tech!  I've used just about every type of platform, but once I discovered Mac, I never went back.

 

As far as a decent desktop that will handle Windows XP & 7, you might look at the Dell Optiplex 760.  I found mine used on eBay for a couple of hundred bucks.  It has about everything you would need to run ham software (2.3GHz quad core processor, 8GB RAM, 500GB HDD.)  It is a decent performer, and there are many now on the surplus market.

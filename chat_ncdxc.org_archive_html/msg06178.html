Club members as a whole,

 

Chris - good message - thanks.

 

Rich - You were never told you were "not wanted" when nominating yourself.  Only the membership can do that by not voting for you.

 

Bylaws are not a "framework".  They are the rules or limits the board has to guide its conduct.  The Procedure Manual is a set of guidelines - yes but not the Bylaws.  The membership is the only entity that can approve changes to the bylaws.  The membership expects the board to obey the rules they adopted.

 

Elections - fix it time:

Any club member can be on the board as long as they have been a club member for a year or more.

 

NO ONE CAN SAY ONE IS NOT WANTED.  ONLY THE MEMBERS DECIDE THAT by their vote!

 

Again - REPLACE THE BOARD.  All seats are available each year.  All it takes is to nominate yourself and then the MEMBERS will have their say if you are wanted or not.  Tell either John K6MM or Mike K6MKF that you want to run for the board.  That puts your hat in the ring.

 

If there is such strong feelings as Paul and Rich describe - why isn't anyone stepping up to run??  Most of the time board members are there because no one else would run.  That is how I became a board member.  Who wanted to be Treasurer?  Silence....  then I said "I'll do it."  I couldn't get out of the restaurant fast enough.

 

This goes to everyone.  Nominate yourself by contacting the nominating chairman or doing so at the club meeting.  You will have a chance to tell the members why you want to be on the board.  Then the membership will decide.

 

This club is run by the membership.  You elect the board knowing those people will carry out your wishes for the club.  If you get betrayed - replace the person the following June - election time.

 

Everyone - go for it nominate yourself.  Now is the time to fix the problem Paul tells us isn't right.  Maybe there are other grievances too.  But FIX it and let’s get back to DX’ing.

 

73,

 

Bob - W6OPO

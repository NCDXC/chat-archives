Absolutely. About 6 months ago, I contacted a governmental bureaucracy regarding gaining access to a rare entity, and the woman in charge said:

The only chance of getting a permit is if some environmental study work is performed.

This wasn't Heard, and that agency was the US F&W.

My point is basically your point. Both access and fund raising, combined with goals above and beyond "Just a bunch of hams playing radio" is the New Norm for "sensitive" rare entities.

DX-pedition leaders will be faced with this more and more.

The impossible will be only possible by thinking out of the box and doing some of the non-ham work like what Bob has done on a handful of dx-peditions, including VK0IR, way back in 1997.

I still think there must be some institution like Stanford with money to finance a scientific expedition to Heard. 

 

If it’s so remote, there must be lots of data yet to be gathered by researchers interested in flora and fauna in those latitudes, effects of global warming, changes in ocean currents, fish populations, etc.  Bob has lots of experience linking biological and earth sciences with radio propagation studies.  He just needs to right hook! There is the recent discovery that the sun is cooler than normal and global warming is proceeding at a much slower rate than expected.  Could lower ionization levels than expected at a solar maximum be related to this warming slowdown?  Could Heard Island’s remote location make it a useful laboratory for such studies? 

 

Millions have been spent to study why people fall in love.  Why not research something a little more down to earth?

There is a heap of good advice on managing and supervising volunteers on the web.  Google "supervising volunteers" for example.  Here is one useful resource:  http://www.worldvolunteerweb.org/resources/how-to-guides/manage-volunteers/doc/supervising-volunteers.html

Who does what?  Board members are volunteers that should be supervised and advised by the membership, and members are volunteers who should be supervised and advised by the the Board.  It is really all about good communications, right?

On 3/25/2014 10:21 AM, Stuart Phillips wrote:
I’m saddened to see the recent email trails slamming NCDXC board members.

For me it brings back bad memories of the events at NCCC which resulted in my resignation as President.

Radio clubs rely on volunteer effort – whether its running a raffle, staffing a table at a convention, or participating in the operations of the club – ITS ALL ABOUT VOLUNTEER EFFORT.

It’s worth doing a google search on “care and feeding of volunteers” - lots of good stuff out there including this one from the open source community – but still relevant.

http://www.slideshare.net/dreamwidth/careandfeeding

I particularly like slide 7 :-)

What bothered me most about my personal experience with NCCC was that no one spoke up or challenged the issues – so much so that a majority of the then board seriously considered mass resignation.

And then everyone wonders why its so difficult to get folks to run for officer and director positions on the board?

Who would want to spend their personal time and energy (and believe me, it requires tons and tons of time) only to result in abuse and attacks?

Please, support the board and thank them (as I do!) for their efforts.  If you believe things should change, get involved and put your shoulder to the wheel.

The best piece of management advice I ever received was “praise in public, criticize in private” - just because no one knows you are a dog on the Internet 

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/On_the_Internet,_nobody_knows_you're_a_dog

By the time I worked him at 1841Z he was most definitely LP. 
Using a “slightly” older (as both I and my rig are) legacy transceiver, as Paul mentioned earlier, I’ve found it useful, especially in F/H mode or MSHV when the DX is transmitting below 500 Hz on the waterfall, to set my transceiver exactly 500 Hz below the base frequency. 
Many older receivers, no matter their selectivity settings, have a basic bandpass that tails off below 400 Hz, undesirably attenuating stations at a low transmit tone on the waterfall. With my rig set 500Hz low, FT8WW was right in the sweet fat spot of my receiver. It seems to make a difference of a few, sometimes critical, dB. 
FT8WW was triple streaming this morning at 347Hz and two higher tones. Triple streaming, his actual ERP at each tone was close to -14dB or thereabouts below what his single stream would have been: that’s why he’s been hard to decode. 
He’s using MSHV, which means he’s decoding the full extent of the waterfall, not just above the 1KHz of the F/H software. He still, however, probably has a slight receive handicap at lower tones on his 590. Best bet is to call above your guess as to his lower bandpass, but not in the center of the waterfall where everyone else is concentrated.
On 30, my impression was that he was favoring callers who were at the lower or upper edge of the pack, and those who were calling with signal reports rather than grid squares. I worked him on 30 transmitting well above the pack, and on 20 well below and only slightly above his upper stream. 
There are still many callers who are unclear that he is using MSHV, and they are locked into the futile off-cycle because they’re set to F/H. 
Thierry’s dipole is making reception the challenging part of this DXpedition, but I am so stoked at finally working Crozet!
Merry Christmas and Happy Hanukkah to all!

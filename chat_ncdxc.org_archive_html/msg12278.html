As a 100W-and-wire operator, I've had a lot of time to think about this while trying to work rare DX. IMHO, Hank's idea is a good one.
I agree that a Skimmer tool at the DXpedition site could be useful -- especially if coupled with specific operating tactics for the dxpedition op: 

<DX> ... TU DE <RareDX> QRZ?
... usual mayhem for 10 seconds or so...
... perhaps skimmer picks up 10 calls out of the fray...
<DX> CALLER1
<CALLER1> 599 TU
<DX> CALLER1 TU NW CALLER2
<CALLER2> 599 TU
<DX> CALLER2 TU NW CALLER3
...etc...
<DX> CALLER10 TU DE <RareDX> QRZ?
Both operators in a QSO know that the other guy heard them without relying just on the skimmer. But by reducing the amount of "usual mayhem" to every N contacts instead of every contact, I'd expect it would be possible to work a lot more stations in a given length of time. 

Maybe I'd even get through...

73,
/Jack de K3FIV




On 06/28/2016 07:24 AM, Hank Garretson via Chat wrote:

I'm not a big DXer. I'll have a go at nabbing a DXpedition for the
thrill of the chase, not for a new-one. There are clearly many things I
don't get. I've never been on the other end.

But ...

It seems that many times DXpeditions have a hard time pulling a call out
of the pileups. Sometimes it will be many, many seconds before they come
back to someone.

A Wild Idea?

How about a local skimmer at the DXpeditions site. The skimmer would
have to be configured to respond to just calls. The skimmer would put
spots on the bandmap. The DXpedition operator would click on a skimmer
spot and work the guy (still split).

Would it be solving a problem that's not a problem? Would it work? Would
it help?

DX Exuberantly,

Hank, W6SX

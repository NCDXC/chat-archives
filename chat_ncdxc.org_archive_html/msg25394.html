On Fri, Mar 25, 2022 at 3:21 PM Bruce H. Bern via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
I've never repaired a fiberglass antenna, but I've repaired gelcoat cracks
and structural damage in our 50 year old fiberglass sail boat. Superficial
cracking is caused by structural flexing and could probably be repaired
using the usual boat repair kits and techniques easily available at West
Marine, which has stores all over the Bay Area. West Marine has a complete
guide to the theory and practical aspects of fiberglass repair, available
here:
https://www.westsystem.com/wp-content/uploads/Fiberglass-Manual-2015.pdf
IMHO, I would think that even if you can get the usual paints to stick, the
bond would be very poor to the gelcoat and/or epoxy, that it would flake off
fairly quickly.
Bruce, K3NQ,  whose boast almost sank yesterday after someone "borrowed" our
shore power cord

I suggest you join the SteppIR google group and search the archives for
"paint".  A lot of info should come up, since every steppir antenna has this
problem.  There is no single type of paint that is "optimum".  There are
tradeoffs with cost, difficulty of application, toxicity, etc.

It is not entirely clear if you can repair exposed fiberglass with paint, as
opposed to resin.

Discussion groups dealing with fiberglass boats may also be a source of
info.

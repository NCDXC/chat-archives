My understanding of the deafening silence from Marion over the past several years (my  log shows one 2011  QSO since 1997, when ZS8IR was QRV) is that after ZS8IR, the existing towers were taken down to prevent bird deaths. At the time, there were an estimated 2 Million + birds on Marion, of which about 15 died at the towers per year. My figures may or may not be accurate, but that is a mortality rate of only 7.5 deaths/million birds/year--hardly a carnage. Were I interested in wintering over on Marion, I would want to know what kind of permanent communications links, including ham radio, were planned. It would be no fun to be limited to a low dipole or mobile whip.

Marion's "salad days" were when Chris deBeer, ZS5IR spent a year as both medical and radio officer on Marion from mid-1996 to mid-1997. Chris was and is a fine op and a fine person, whom I hosted here. On Marion, he was active on all bands and he had those towers. My last QSO with him, in March 1997 was on 160m. At the end of his assignment, the ship that was to take him back to RSA brought the crew that took down the towers. It has been pretty sparse for Marion on the bands since then.

Garry, NI6T

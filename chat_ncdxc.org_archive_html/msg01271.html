I agree the KX3 sounds like a great travel/suite case expedition rig but
would you opt for that approach for your base station rig?  You would end up
with the 100W amp to drive a legal limit amp...
 
*** Maybe a conventional amp Craig but tremendous strides have been made in Solid State Technology over the last 15 years. I'm specifically referring to N-Channel LDMOS Power FETs. Devices being produced by Freescale and NXP are rated from 1.8 to 600 Mhz with a minimum gain figure of 22 db, and a typical gain of 27 db.
 
 
 
 
I think that you could drive a KPA500 with the KX3 but you wouldn't get full
power out.
 
*** At 24 db gain 5 watts, or half of the KXP's available power, would be required to drive Freescale's 1250 Watt MRFE6VP61K25H PowerFet Module to full output. 22 db of gain would require 7.7 watts of drive

*** Elecraft was able to obtain 1500 Watts with a basic K3. Remember the KPA 1500 ? Unfortunately the amp was never Type Certified by the FCC because it violated Parts 97.315 and 97.317. Specifically it violated the 15 db Gain limit for commercial amplifiers offered for sale. But that restriction doesn't prevent a ham from designing and building his own amp.

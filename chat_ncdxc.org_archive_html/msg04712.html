On Saturday, December 14, 2013 9:31 PM, Jim Brown <k9yc@audiosystemsgroup.com> wrote:
On 12/13/2013 11:25 PM, Risto Kotalampi wrote:
> I have never understood what is so exciting in QRP

Successful QRP places emphasis on antennas (on both ends), knowing and
taking advantage of propagation, low RX noise on the other end, and
operator skills. Making a QRP QSO over a long path, and in a pileup,
gives me a LOT more satisfaction than "brute force" with 1.5kW. Most of
my 125 countries worked QRP were during contests or in the pileup for a
DXpedition.

When I make a difficult QSO QRP, I'm thrilled because I've built good
antennas -- they have to be good, because 5W is 25 dB below 1500 watts!

I fully appreciate George's satisfaction.  I often do what George did --
work the rare ones I need with the amp on, then call them again with my
WPX club call with 5W.

73, Jim K9YC

40M - (working "spliff" . . . )

I was already to call and when I opened my logbook - the USB port went wacky and turned off my split and set me into digital mode. 
Sorry if you heard that bonehead move - guess I was trying to work "spliff", not "split".
I did get through - so now have 40, 30, 20 CW and 40M SSB.
Heard them on 17 and 15M last night - but with S6 noise - it was a bit rough. 

QRM

I've seen tons of comments on QRM, lids and jammers (watching the cluster at work - heh heh). The odd thing is - I have not experienced any of that when I was in the pileups. Maybe its because - working in SF during the day - I have to really gauge my times that I can try to work these guys. I have gone into work a little late - or hone in earlier and come home earlier to give it a go. Or maybe I've developed thicker skin - and tune that stuff out? I have not been QRM-ed once and heard FT5ZM come back in the loud and clear every time (except the first QSO - 40M CW - where he first called me KY5R - a more ell known DX-er than I). The log shows he did get that Q logged correctly, but because of that I did work him a second time. That Q was last Sunday - and honestly - I did not expect it to be as easy as it has been. I have not called more than 15 minutes for the Q's I have made. The higher bands have been rougher, and I have made it a point to hear them above a certain level where I think I can be competitive with my 500 W.

I have only experienced the "usual" stuff - and none of what most people seem to be angry about. It seems that EU and the East Coast really have it bad. 

I have simple home brewed antennas with a little gain and decent directivity. My terrain almost totally blocks out any QRM from the East - maybe that's why I have not heard much QRM?

73,

Rich

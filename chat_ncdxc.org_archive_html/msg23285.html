I worked VK9XX on 20m FT8 F/H at 14.090 at about 2 am (0900 UTC) this morning - I didn't send a spot, because I thought it might result in a lot of angry hams and XYL's!

There was a lot of QSB, and a solid wall of JA stations coming in at about +10 or more at Christmas Island. I went to an audio frequency above 2500 Hz and he heard me, gave me a -10. He did seem to be trying to give the non-JA stations a chance now and then. I was the last FT8 QSO before he went to 20m SSB, which he does at the top of each hour.

One really nice thing is he has a near-instantaneous online log on his QRZ.com page, so I was able to see that I was in the log. He matched on Clublog a few minutes later, and the OQRS functionality is already enabled.

Steve VK6SJ will be operating VK9XX on a different band each night. Although the propagation to Christmas Island (the one near Indonesia, not the Christmas Island in the South Pacific) is not bad from our QTH, his scheduled hours of operation may mean a lot of late nights and very early mornings for US West Coast hams.

73 and good DX,
Andy K6ZP
Montara, CA

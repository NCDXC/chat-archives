On Jan 22, 2015, at 11:11 AM, Don McKee via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

> I received my first IRC in the mail the other day and am curious about how they're redeemed. Do I have to go to an official post office, or will a contact postal outlet do? Are IRCs redeemed for cash, or just an international "forever" stamp? Are there typically any hassles trying to redeem them? Could I hold on to the IRC (this one issued from Japan) and include it with some future QSL request of my own?
> 
> Thanks in advance for educating this newbie. 73!
> 

If you take the IRC to the Post Office (any real PO), they should redeem it for an International Forever stamp. However, IRCs are much more useful as “ham currency”, for those hams who want IRCs in exchange for return QSLs. The Post Offices in some places in the world routinely scour ham mail looking for “green stamps” (US dollars). IRCs are less fungible, and so are a better currency for QSL’ing.

I take IRCs, PayPal, green stamps, and/or properly stamped return envelopes for any requested *direct* QSL card. Bureau cards I send for free, on request.

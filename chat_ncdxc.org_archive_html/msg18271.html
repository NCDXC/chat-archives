On Thursday, November 28, 2019, 11:04:55 AM PST, David Harding (WB7WBB) via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Happy Thanksgiving.

I want to increase my chances of being heard in EU. On 20m, I Tx 14.073 and Rx 14.074.

I use FT8; I don’t see that I am often heard in EU on pskreporter.info

I do see the S=Split Mode in WSJT-X, My thought is that I am off frequency…

Thoughts? Thanks in advance. -David Harding (WB7WBB)


On the radio, you should NOT set it into “Split” Mode, and therefore both your TX and RX frequencies should be 14.074 (the std for FT-8 on 20m).

As another poster said, set up the way you were, no one will hear you with any tone setting below 1 kHz, and very few will even hear you below 1.5 kHz.

The “Split” mode in WSJT-X is to make sure you are transmitting within the range of tones that do not have significant roll-off from your audio filter, and also that any audio harmonics are outside the transmitted passband. WSJT-X takes care of this ITSELF; you do NOT have to engage “Split” on your radio.

From my experience, most radios work better with the “Fake It” split option than using actual split. Let the software do the work.

Rich KE1B

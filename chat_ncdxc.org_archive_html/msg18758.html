Hi Tony,

I want to share with you my experience with Steppir and I hope it doesn’t come across as harsh. I have owned four steppir antenna at different times and recently put up a standard two element which works great at the moment. 

My first steppir experience started in 2012 when I was a newly minted ham. I bought the three element with single 40/30 trombone. It was a phenomenal upgrade from the hex beam I had and I was amazed at how well it worked at first. For six months it was worked flawlessly.  Then I started  to have problems. The driven EHU with the trombone was the fault. The copper beryllium tape was all bent on one side of the EHU. It needed replacement. Working with steppir I found out that they had a rev 2 for the trombone and I purchased mine months prior which had some design flaws. That was pretty aggravating to know that after all the assembly it had known issues. They never told me they were working on a rev 2 for the trombone when I was on the phone with them six months prior and that the trombones were having issues in the field. After that experience, I was pretty mad. I decided that I was uncomfortable with the design no matter the fact that they came up with a new rev for the trombone. I sold the steppir and ended up buying a Mosley S33 antenna. 

Fast forward, eight years and I guess I forgot about the challenges I had with my first steppir.  I decided again to take a risk for antenna that would have 40 to 6m at less than 5 sq ft of windload. So I purchased the urban beam at a pretty steep price more than $2500. I received it and did the inventory and everything was there. For this antenna I was surprised about the precision cuts that were required of the Elements and the hokey assembly of the sweeps. I got about 80% through the assembly and said to myself there is no way this is going to last more than six months based on the design of the sweeps. So I decided to go a different path and purchased used NOS fiberglass elements and stick to the non-bending elements. It is up on my tower as a two element and I am having a blast with it. 

Most folks will tell you follow the assembly manual to the letter and you won’t have troubles. I have had a different experience and I probably won’t go with steppir again after this  though it is always tempting because there is nothing else like it in the market. When you consider one antenna that can do 80 to 6 wow that is just absolutely incredible. Just going in with the knowledge with all those trombones and moving parts you could have issues it would be important to have a retractable tiltover tower with tilt plate for it. You might be one of the folks that have zero issues and I hope that is the case but if I ever decided on the DB36 with 80m option I know I would want to be able to get at it at the first sign that there is an issue.

Good luck!!

KG4's in Gitmo are issued by the Commander, Naval Base, Guantanamo Bay, Cuba.   I was KG4TR from 1985 to 1986, my one year tour at the "armpit of the world!"   You're restricted to whatever class of license your stateside call is, and iffen I recall correctly, you had some additional voice privileges on 40M phone - but I'd have to go back and check that as it's been over 40yrs ago now since I had the displeasure of being stuck  down there  😉    It really was the armpit of the world back then - right up there with being stuck out on Adak!  NavSecGru and Marine Cryptologic Support BN had what we called the Shxxhole rotation - depending on who you might have pissed off in the command - that would be rotation in/out of Adak, Guam, and Gitmo.  As a licensed ham though, these were actually great sites to play radio from  😉  

 

I did Team Spirit in 83 but wasn't in county long enough to get an HL9 call.  I recall reading not too long ago that HL9's are no longer available/issued, and that foreigners are only issued a 3 month temp operating permit (I'd have to go back and find info I received from KARL quite a few years back while I was active duty out on Guam).

 

73, Todd KH2TJ (KG4TR)

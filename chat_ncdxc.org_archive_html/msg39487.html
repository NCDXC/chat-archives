Ryan,

Please create and print your cards for your activations!  As the 6th District QSL Bureau Manager, I love to see unique cards coming from all over the world to our QSL Bureau patrons. www.qslbureau.org 

We discovered last year (John NZ6Q and I) running RT66 On The Air as W6V that we got lots of eQSLs - some as early as the next morning.  We answered every one with our printed W6V Special Event QSL in the mail. We had over 200 QSL cards in the mailbox by the time we got home and more poured in thru the next weeks. We were a young hams first HF contact after passing his General and of course he got a QSL card, but receiving his QSL card in return was even more special. Some hams even included a dollar or two or five to help cover their card being answered and others - even though we didn't ask for any money, it was still a nice touch that demonstrated the kindness that is in us radio amateurs as well as the value that a paper QSL card still holds today. 

We discovered truly "the final courtesy of a QSO is a QSL card".  

You mentioned LOTW and that's a great tool provided by the ARRL.  I have over 160 countries confirmed in my short ham radio career. 95 % of those were through LOTW.  I appreciate it for it's efficiency when it comes to awards.  QSL cards, I believe go much deeper than being checked off for an award. While that is important, QSL cards say something, they mean something - from the simple white card stock QSLs from JA (and others) FT8 contacts to the beautifully designed 4 panel cards from DXpeditions and others (like our W6V) - they are a smile and a wave, they tell a story, they say thank you for connecting us together via radio even if it was only 15 seconds on FT8. Looking at them paneled together on the walls in my husband's office and around our home invokes fond memories of those QSOs.

The final courtesy of a QSO is a QSL card. Thank you to all of you who share this intimate part of the story of ham radio with us.

73 and Good DX (or POTA/SOTA, or Special Event)

Annie N6ACL
ARRL 6th District Incoming QSL Bureau Manager 


On 2025-05-02 11:00 pm, Ryan Huggins via Chat wrote:

Thank you all for this conversation.  As someone who is still toying with the concept of QSL cards, I've thought about creating and printing my own as this would allow for greater efficiency and for batches of cards for particular POTA/SOTA activations to show a photo of the park/summit and the operation as I recently received one like that and it was a nice touch.  I've been looking at these electronic services as well.
 
In reading the eQSL rules, I was particularly dismayed to read the following:
"Also, we expect to be able to identify cards as authentic. Many cards are printed on home printers, and, in many cases, the data is printed on card stock at the same time. Although this is technically acceptable, the process often makes verification difficult. In certain cases we may reject these cards. Cards should be personalized or otherwise made unique through the use of a stamp or other personal mark (signature or initials) across a label boundary."
 
While I understand their need for authenticity, this still seems like ARRL needs to catch up with the times and technology.  Maybe an ARRL-provided security code you can print on the cards to prove it is really you.
 
I hope that the ARRL gets with the times soon.  In my brief time operating, I have received over seven times more digital cards than physical ones, but at least the digital cards I've checked have all been confirmed in LotW and QRZ.
 
 
-73
Ryan, KI6BTY

I have S-9 noise level at my QTH now. i have called PG&E two times already when this first started during the CQP. to date no response. now its gotten worse!! I went exploring with an AM radio yesterday and found some problems with poles in certain areas. Loud hums coming from the block area by the poles. This is where the antenna is pointing over the freeway so must be the area the interference is coming from and causing the interference. 

My question to the group is does anyone have a phone number to call for help. I call the 800 number and they took the information down and said someone would get back to me within 7 days. To date no one does!!


The best way to get PG+E’s attention is to file a complaint with the Public Utilities Commission. You can do this online. By law, they have to respond within (I think) 30 days, and I have found that they DO ultimately find and fix problems. FYI: They will send out an interference tech who will check out poles using this really neat highly-directive ultrasonic detector. I worked with the guy, and we were able to pinpoint particular insulators that were either dirty or cracked.

The HARD PART is getting the guys who work on the poles to actually come out and do the work. This can take months, sorry. I had a few poles in my area “completely rebuilt” (they replaced just about everything on them, and required shutting down power to the neighborhood to do it), but I now have had S-0 noise on every band (except 80/160) for years. I can regularly work 20m with BOTH preamplifiers in my IC-7600 turned on, and still S-0, even with SSB bandwidths.

Now, the noise source might not be PG+E. Perhaps a neighbor just installed a large LED lighting system with a cheap, noisy power supply. (For political correctness, I won’t mention the notorious Asian source of those devices.) It’s also possible the noise is coming from a new solar power system with a cheap inverter. We will be seeing more and more of these as folks “go solar” (especially with San Jose about to ban natural gas installations in new construction).

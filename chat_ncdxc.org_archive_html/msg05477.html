I also use to receive WR6ACZ, now W6TI much better here in the 70's and 80's at my location here in Pioneer Ca. Not sure what changed with the system. Now some days I can't even hear it. On Feb 6, 2014, at 4:03 PM, Bob Lanning wrote:

Keep in mind that the W6TI repeater’s coordination is for a fixed set of data describing the repeater. 
Any change to the repeater that impacts its coverage footprint suspends the coordination until NARCC and the user community weighs in regarding negative impact on other machines and their users.  Each repeater needs to stay within its coordination parameters setting its footprint.
Northern California is saturated with repeaters whether active (in use) or not.  Increasing the repeater’s signal strength in any direction jeopardizes the coordination. 
Changing its receiving footprint of course is not a problem and that is where W6TI is struggling.

On 2/6/2014 4:03 PM, Bob Lanning wrote:
Any change to the repeater that impacts its coverage footprint suspends the coordination until NARCC and the user community weighs in regarding negative impact on other machines and their users. Each repeater needs to stay within its coordination parameters setting its footprint. 

Important point, Bob. Clearly understood.
An important point that club leadership needs to consider is that DX with marginal propagation can vary quite a bit over our membership area, and those of us in outlying areas can provide members an early alert. I keep my email computer in the house, so don't have access to this reflector in the shack.
As to upgrade strategy -- I've never seen a power splitter as a viable solution to what is essentially a local noise problem. Voted receivers are hardly a new or complex solution. Back in the '70s, I was part of the tech team maintaining a 2M system with TX on a 57 story office building in the shadow of the Sears Tower, and two voted RX sites at opposite ends of the metro area. There was no RX at the TX site because the Chicago Loop was an intermod sewer. That system had been working for at least a decade by the time I got involved with it, and was quite stable.
I did suggest that PL decode level might be a problem, and since cranked up the PL deviation in my shack FM rig by 3 dB or so. I may be slightly more able to key the machine, but I'm still noisy into it. 

73, Jim K9YC

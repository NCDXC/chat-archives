Friends:

We have purchased a new QTH (in Kentfield) and plan to move around May 1 or soon thereafter.

K7LXC will be doing the antenna system erections at the new QTH and will be taking down my existing antenna system in Corte Madera (sigh!).

If anyone would like my Hy-Gain HyTower which is currently still up, it's available NOW for FREE to the first person who contacts me via email and is willing to come up here, take it down and haul it away.

As for the rest of my existing antenna system:

I have two sections of Rohn 45 (one section with flat top) with associated rotor plates, base plate and thrust bearings and a M2 7,10-30 LP8 (log periodic).

The Rohn and the log periodic will be available on the ground probably in early May after K7LXC has taken it all down.

If anyone would like the Rohn and/or the log periodic, they will be also be available for FREE in early May to the first person who contacts me via email and is willing to come up here and haul away.

Also coming down in early May is my M2 Orion 2800 rotor, which I will be offering for sale on eHam and QTH.com. It's only a few years old and in excellent condition. (At the new QTH, will be putting up a Hex Beam, which doesn't require such a beefy rotor.} If you are interested in the Orion rotor before I offer it publicly, please let me know.

Note that the M2 log periodic is BIG and heavy: 30 ft. boom, eight elements, ~100 pounds. It covers 10 - 30 Mhz with gain and has a linear-loaded element for 40 meters. It has served me well on the ridgetop for 16 years and still is in good condition. But it's for someone who has sufficient space. (QTH in Kentfield is much smaller; hence the Hex Beam).

Hy-Tower Note: it has the optional trap for 160M (inverted L)

Please don't commit to any of the free items unless you are positive that you want them and are willing to come get them.

If there are no takers, all (except the rotor) will be dumped. I'm serious about wanting to make this stuff go away.

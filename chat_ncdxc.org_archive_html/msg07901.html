On Oct 16, 2014, at 21:26, Ross Forbes via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Recently, Rusty Epps, W6OAT, heard that Robert Felicite, 3B9FR, was asking for help getting his current transceiver repaired. Rusty brought this up at the September meeting of the Northern California DX Club where the members were anxious to help.  Ross Forbes, K6GFJ, offered to donate an entire station he had that was not being used to help Robert stay on the air. Kip Edwards, W6SZN, and Rich Stempien, W6RS, made sure the transceiver was in good operating condition before shipping everything off to Robert on Rodriguez Island. Bob Lanning, W6OPO, and Don Ferguson, AI6RE, handled the NCDXC treasury covering all shipping charges, while Don Greenbaum, N1DG, from the Northern California DX Foundation, supplied additional advice and support. As of October 16th, the following items are either in transit to Robert, or have already arrived at Robert’s QTH:
 
Icom 756 ProIII transceiver
Icom SP-23 Speaker
Icom SM-20 Desk mic and hand mic
MicroHAM microKEYER interface
Heil Pro Plus Headset
All documentation and cables are also included, so all Robert has to do is use his current power supply and antenna.  Robert will now be QRV on 6 through 160 meters in SSB, CW, FM and digital modes.
 
73,
Ross

I made a FT8 contact a couple days ago for a new entity on 80 M.  I looked up the call to see about confirming the contact.  He has a policy I have not seen before:

 "On FT8 / FT4 HF bands, 160m to 10m, I call CQ DX or CQ AS or CQ JA and I do not work any Europeans. If you are in EU, you cannot get a QSO so only waste your time. On 160m through 6m, DX is not any station in continental EU.  In general, on HF, "DX" means outside of Europe for EU based stations calling "CQ DX".  

Glad I am not in EU!
Al  N6TA

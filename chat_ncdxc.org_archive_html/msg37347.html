BuddiHex may improve your signal... and hearing.  (Put a common mode current choke at the feed to help lower noise and RFI.)  You're making progress, moving forward (never stop!).

Direction, depends on the band AND time of day.  The low bands follow the sun shadow, the higher bands follow the sun light; except 20 and 17 can work backwards much of the time, so pay attention to long path (except when the long path is greater than 3/4 of the planet).  All bands extend into the shadow/light for a time (varies by band).

Note that gray line (the shadow/light line) creates magical propagation, for a VERY short window of time. USE that window.

When in doubt, swing the antenna around, see what else you hear.

There MAY be a good primer on "prop for dummies", but mostly the forecasts are SWAG (dark magic?) so exact accuracy isn't expected (real life throws a lot of curve balls and sliders).  The actual conditions (once the solar energy arrives) vary a LOT, it's a general forecast and imprecise.  [SWAG = Scientific Wild Arsed Guessing]

Mostly you get on, pay attention and take notes as you operate.  Later you read the notes, what the conditions actually were and you'll build a general understanding of the bands (prop), the timing and how the sun plays into things.

THAT takes a lifetime; part of the fun.  Right now we're at solar maxima, starting to go into the lower solar activity; which is ALWAYS changing, so we adapt to meet our goals.  No two solar cycles are exactly the same; variations are constants.

Eventually you get on more spotting networks to alert you about present activity, learning the prop for your location (to filter down the spots) and it'll sorta, kinda, sometimes, make a little sense.

In the meantime, you enjoy the magical moments (like Madagascar, Ethiopia on 10 meters yesterday) and grab the fun available.

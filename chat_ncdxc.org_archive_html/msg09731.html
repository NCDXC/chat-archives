I just felt compelled to share this with the group.  Many of you know we have been doing lots of remote work for the last several years...   Last year I presented to this club a summary of how the K6LRG remote capabilities were implemented.  This new flex radio capability is definitely breakthrough technology that when fully implemented will make child's play out of some major problems with remote operations, in particular when it comes to real time pan display, extra equipment need and general remote radio connectivity. 

Now having installed this new update on my Flex 6500 and having my radio up and running on a 4 year old laptop...(!! pretty smooth I may add also)   I am sold.  This is good stuff and has now been well optimized.  There are a few features still needed and are in the development queue.  This is very exciting technology for an IT nerd turned contester/dxer like myself. 

Folks picture this.. instead of having to rent a shack or large footprint to get your hilltop remote.. this technology will allow for you to rack the radio in a small corner.. or a tower site rack.. friends closet.. whatever..  and eventually connect and operate the radio from a laptop or pc from anywhere(providing the site has suitable internet connectivity).  It's totally conceivable that an entire contest station could be racked up in a 20U half rack with all multi-op participants comfortable in their own homes.

  While they work out the WAN remote piece, my first objective will be to take this box into battle at N6RO and see how she plays in full contest condx.   Once the WAN piece is completed, my eventual objective is to deploy this 6500 to a closet at my XYL's aunts house in Santiago CE3 and operate as DX over the wire and start over with DXCC #1 from there.  The implications of this Technology combined with the new DXCC rules are staggering.  They have been progressively moving forward now achieving lan based remote.. so I can wander around the house now untethered from my rig with my laptop on Wifi  chasing dx or calling cq contest...pan display and all.  Now that they have the audio streaming and compression in place.. the WAN based capabilities are on the radar next.  This will be the "clincher"  There are some things to work out.. for instance When remote I need to send cw with my keyboard (or a contest logger).. but these details can be corrected, in fact many of the solutions have been worked out for K6LRG already and may be able to get a jump on the game.  SSB audio goes right through the computer mic input.   And the receiver is top notch.  Awesome performance on par with Elecraft and other top of class tranceivers.  (Allthough it does top it, the Sherwood chart isn't everything.. but take it from me this thing is a nice receiver).

Bottom line.. Really fantastic rig now and adding crazy cool features as the rig continues to mature. Still in early adopter stage.. yes.  A few challenges for the platform ahead.. yes. Exciting new tech.  yes.  Two thumbs up from N6WM.  I can't wait to see what comes next.  Its been very interesting to watch this technology develop.

~C.

All,

Flex radio has released a major update to the Flex signature series(6000) system greatly improving stability, adding additional capabilities, adding native lan based remote control of the rig along with many many more improvements.  I have been testing one of their 6500's in the shack for a few months and it sits next to the K3 and the KX3 in my shack.  Its a pretty impressive box.   This is a huge step forward for this platform, should be interesting to see how it plays.

Interesting stuff to say the least.  The receiver performance is already quite spectacular and as these features are being rolled out its becoming prime time ready(a few core features are needed that I am hopeful will be integrated very soon)...  I will start running the updates through their paces and see how she plays now.

~Chris

As long as I've lived in Santa Cruz, I've had trouble working the W6TI
repeater.  I'm running 170 watts to a 5-el vertically oriented Yagi
pointed at the repeater, and while I can nearly always copy it Q5, I'm
so noisy into the system that I'm hard to copy.

A very useful discussion at the picnic shed light on likely reasons --
W6OOL, who I understand is largely responsible for maintaining the site,
notes that while it's a great TX site, it's gotten increasingly noisy
there.  It's also possible that cavities and/or the RX system might need
some tweaking.

I was part of the team maintaining repeaters on 2M, 220, and 440 MHz in
Chicago, where we had similar issues with RX noise on 2M. Long before my
involvement, the issues were resolved by using a single TX on a tall
building downtown, with two or more RX sites linked into a voter.

The multiple RX sites  with voting also helped with talkies and mobiles
in areas where coverage was marginal, and should prove equally useful in
filling in shadows caused by our terrain.

Our club is not poor, and I'd like to see some funds directed toward
improving the W6TI system coverage. Perhaps that means a different (or
second) RX site. Several of our members are blessed with high locations.
Perhaps one of them might host a receiver, and we could add a voter to
the system to make use of it.

73, Jim K9YC

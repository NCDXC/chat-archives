I used this approach on one DXpedition, during one SSB shift.
The EU pileup was unruly, and the DQRMers were very active.

The rate was terrible.
Afterwards I wondered if the rate would have been any worse even with the DQRM and unruly ops.

The big guns will dominate.
The little pistols will be covered up, and probably get frustrated.
There may be significant confusion because "many" of the callers don't read DX bulletins, websites, etc. They won't know what's happening. They are not the people that regularly chase DX, they're not DX club members.

If you've ever been on the DX end of a wall to wall pileup you know there will be times when even pulling out a partial will be challenging.

Split creep will QRM non DXers, they sometimes react negatively.

I only tried this technique one time, and never again.

Personally, I think we worry about DQRM too much.
As a caller I always considered DQRM as part of the challenge to getting a Q.

It will be an interesting few weeks.

Cheers,
GS K5GS

  1. DQRM strategy

This strategy may only be implemented if the DQRM is very bad and slows down our QSO rate significantly.

Normal CW/SSB pileup handling is listening UP 1 kHZ on CW and 5 kHz on SSB.

DQRM strategy is to work simplex on all bands within a 20 kHz bandwidth for CW and 30 kHz for SSB.

We will work simplex or “contest mode” within the 20 kHz window in CW. For example, operate SIMPLEX between 14010 and 14030 kHz.

In DQRM mode - we do S&P within a specified window like in any contest.

All stations are calling SIMPLEX. 3YØK tunes across the simplex window and works stations SIMPLEX.

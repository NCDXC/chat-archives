I woke up about 3am, checked the cluster and saw TX5T listed as on 160 FT8  I went down to the shack and they were gone.  went to 80- FT8 and they were there.  Called twice then he sent qsy 3825 cw  three times and left.  I checked 40 and the ssb station was on.  I got him first call, barefoot & we chatted a minute or so.  He wasn’t too busy that time of our morning.  

Woke up at 5 and checked TF8 again.  I heard him sign off again on 80 and switch to cw.  But the 160 station was up on 1844 and I managed to get thru. 

 

It seems like the 80 meter op was switching modes fairly often.  he seemed to be on FT8 at 0300 (Pacific times) & 0500 but by 0315 & 0515 he would go to cw

 

Good luck.

On Aug 20, 2018, at 11:08 AM, AL_N6TA via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

 

I got back from SFO at about 1 AM this morning and tuned 75 and 40 for TX5T on phone.  They were on 40 CW but on for 2 minutes and off for a couple minutes.  This went on for half an hour.  I needed the SSB QSO and sent SSB? A few times when it seemed nobody was calling.

At 0846, the op popped up on 7122 kHz.  I called him 5 up and got his first and my first QSO.  He was fumbling around a bit mentioning an intermod problem with another station but finally got going a bit.  He worked a AZ station, a VK and a JA.  The signal was S9 on the meter, very strong.

Who knows about other nights, but the conditions were excellent.

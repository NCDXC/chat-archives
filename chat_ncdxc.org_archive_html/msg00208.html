Since the club was a sponsor of the 3B9SP DXpedition and Paul had
arranged a sked for all of us to work them like he had arranged with
his friend (sk) in Afganistan, I was sure they would have worked me
and some of the other low power guys.  Almost immediately everyone in
the country jumped in and I saw that our private sked had come up on
the spotting network.  Maybe in the future a sked could be on a
different frequency so more of us could have worked them.

> Good morning, here is your NCDXC Question of the week?
>
>
> How did you do with the TT8TT and 3B9SP DXpeditions?
>
>
>
> My response: I was pleasantly surprised by the TT8TT operation. I had not
> fared well with any of their 60 operations and my expectations were low for
> TT, even though I needed it for a new one!   I was very pleased to hear the
> TT8TT Ops asking for West Coast starting about mid-way through their
> operation. Indeed, they made a good effort to work the West Coast over a
> period of days. Good band conditions prevailed and I was able to get them on
> 10, 15, and 20 meters!
>
> The 3B9SP operation has been more challenging due to the further distance
> (further than Chad) and the fluctuating band conditions. The NCDXC donated
> to support this DXpedition and I am glad that we did as the DXpedition
> Leader has been in communication with me and has made a good effort to log
> as many NCDXC members as they could. They will QRT tomorrow. T
>
> Thus far, I have them on 20 CW, 20 SSB and 17 CW.  I thought I had them on
> 40 CW but it appears that was a slim.
>

We had one of the guys that is called on by PG&E now (they no longer do this themselves) to find these noise issues, and he did a presentation at a local Ham Radio Club Meeting.
He said he almost never found bad insulators.  It was almost always loose hardware on straps and bracing that has come loose and is arcing.  They assemble all of this differently now with Bolt & Nut Hardware versus wood screw type ends and this helps, but ultimately the wood can shrink and the hardware will get loose. 

Yes, there are many possible noise sources.  At our previous QTH we had Grow Houses (for food!) in the Central Valley - around 800 of them - and they installed new (cheap and fault) controllers for the light/heating/venting systems and the new ones were horrible noise sources.  

Good luck as our FCC doesn't do much (if any) policing of the imported junk (some of which doesn't meet any FCC standards). They are too busy auctioning off our spectrum.

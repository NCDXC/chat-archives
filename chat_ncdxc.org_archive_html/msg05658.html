Paul and Team

 

The only time I worked Reunion Island was FR0FLO back in 1981 and then only on 20M and 40M. Sadly, over the years I  lost my FR0FLO QSL cards so I decided to give TO7CC a reasonable effort. I have a ridge line blocking  the short path direction to Reunion Island from my Saratoga QTH and I was not optimistic but thanks to them every band I could hear them well enough to call they were able to hear me…my QSOs were mainly thanks to them and their operating abilities. How did I do?  Eight confirmed contacts:  15M CW SSB, 17M SSB RTTY, 20M CW SSB RTTY and 30M CW. I could never hear them well enough to call on 10M, 12M and 160M or I would have tried those bands as well. J  The QSLs were credited in my ARRL LOTW account for each QSO within a day of the QSO. Wow!

 

My impression is that they did make a special effort to work many of us on the West Coast and I appreciate those efforts.

 

I am probably just like most other DXers in that if they worked me and I receive their QSLs it is for me a good operation!  J

 

73, Ted, K6XN

TO7CC wrapped up this weekend. They made a total of 49,367 contacts. Initially they were tough to work. Not because they were hard to hear, but they were employing techniques such as “French only” and “UK only”. Towards the end they had pretty much worked EU and they were much easier. I made four contacts with them but could have made more, but found that there was a certain level of aggravation in working them vs. the pure excitement and enjoyment of working FT5ZM. These were drastically different kinds of operations using much different techniques.

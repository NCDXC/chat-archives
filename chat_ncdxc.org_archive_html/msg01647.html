This is sad news indeed.

 

Smitty was always the consumate gentleman. When I first came to the Bay Area in 1973, Smitty opened his station to me for use during Sweepstakes and the All Asia Contest. (Old-time NCCCers will remember the great club efforts of the three teams set up to foster intense competition in the AA.) Smitty was an enthusiastic participant by way of offering us "young guns" a great place to operate. I found out over the years about his great enthusiasm for public service outside of ham radio.

 

I last operated at Smitty's place two years ago in the SS CW. Smitty couldn't get around very well by that time and couldn't get down the stairs to watch me operate in his first-floor hamshack. But he was still as enthusiastic and encouraging as he was 40 years before when I first met him. Yes, Smitty's enthusiasm, kindness and drive permeated everything he did.

 

Rest in peace, Old Friend.

 

73, Dean, N6BV

It is with great sadness that I have to report that my very good friend Smitty W6CS passed away this morning.  He was an active DX chaser, contester, and participated in many other ham radio activities for decades.  He also was very active in humanitarian efforts in Africa and the Soviet Union/East Europe area dealing with the aftermath of Chernobyl.

Smitty was the president of the first Foothill College board starting the very successful school that has had more than two million students pass through.

I will post information on when a memorial service will be held when it becomes available.

Good DX and KB Smitty

de  Jim Abraham W6EB

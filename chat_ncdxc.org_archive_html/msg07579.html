All,

 

At about 6:30pm local time yesterday the next balloon from CNSP was launched from Almaden and 85 in San Jose. It is a Mylar Super Pressure balloon about 6’ in diameter. It is carrying a payload weighting about 15 grams and is at about 39K’ as I write this email. It can be tracked at the following url. The battery failed at -60*C and we are hoping it will restart when it gets enough sun today.

http://aprs.fi/#!mt=hybrid&z=11&call=a%2FK6RPT-11&timerange=86400&tail=86400

 

We are not sure how far this might go and believe it should level off about 40K’ to 50K’ when the balloon becomes fully inflated due to reduced outside pressure of that altitude. Since the balloon can’t expand past this point it will just reach buoyancy and remain at that altitude. The problem may be when the sun comes up the pressure will rise and we will find out if the seams of this homemade balloon can stand the pressure.

 

If there are no leaks it should continue to fly and send packets for some time. It does have solar panels to recharge the battery so we will see how far it can fly before something goes wrong.

 

Wish us luck and good flying.

Don

Who cares about DXCC “credit”? (I don’t.) *YOU* know you worked Mt. Athos. With or without the QSL, *I* know (or believe) you worked Mt. Athos. The whole club now knows you worked (and confirmed) Mt. Athos. Congratulations, by the way. That’s a tough one from the Left Coast.

Personally, I don’t care if someone in Newington, CT knows or acknowledges that you (or I) worked Mt. Athos. FYI: I have it confirmed from back when Monk Apollo was at the microphone. I think I have 323 entities confirmed. I have no idea what anyone in Newington thinks I have confirmed.

DXCC is a “game.” Being a DX club, we all play “the game” to some extent. I chase (and become) DX because it’s a challenge and it’s fun. (Even more fun on the other side of the pileup, I can tell you.) I don’t do it for the benefit of some desk jockey in CT, I do it for self-gratification, and maybe a little bit of bragging rights. About the only reason I remain a member of ARRL is so that I can use their outgoing QSL Bureau for all the cards and requests I get from my Holiday DXpeditions. (And that this comes at no extra charge with NCDXC membership is a great service provided by the club; well worth my dues there too.)

Just my two cents (and probably not even worth that).

Rich KE1B

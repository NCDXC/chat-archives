For Monk Apollo I think the most important aid is some type of cluster alarm.  Not just on the home PC but other means as well (smartphone, email, etc.).  I have a smartphone app that would send an audio alert whenever the Monk is on.  On the day I worked the Monk I wasn't at home initially.  But after hearing the alarm I hurried home in time to work him on 40m SSB.

Propagation wise I could hear him very well here in Denver.  Usually S5-S7.  Breaking the pileup is another matter.  Having a legal limit amplifier definitely helps.  One time I got close to working him on 17m SSB in 2012.  Propagation was in my favor (he was S7) and one of my local buddy worked him but I didn't get through.  When FT4TA was active the Monk was on 10m SSB.  I gave it my best shot however the East Coast wall was unbreakable.  When his German friend was operating CW I was on vacation.  To this day I only have one QSO with the Monk.  GL on your endeavor.  It's darn difficult but not impossible.  One should never give up.

My experience :
I think you have to do a search everyday to see if hes been reported as up and when.  Worry about your actual propagation chances after you see a time/band pattern. At least half the time you will not have propagation. He tends to operate several days in a row, at around the same time, and then nothing for maybe weeks. He does not favor a band/frequency IME. Few people report him until after they have made a contact--and then its likely to late for you.  I still look for him ever so often] even tho I don't need him.
So hang around in those times and hope you run into him or he gets posted early.
He does not like pileups---And he will not/doesn't hang around long for them to build.
Often will make a only few dozen contacts and then he's just gone ---with no "good by".

He does not favor CW [very rare] but he does like RITTY---for the last 2 years.  I think it might be your best bet -- but you have to be ready to pounce.
He used to work DX nets [8-15 yrs ago]-- so he could avoid handling pileups himself. But I am not sure they operate often or exist anymore. And it might not be your favorite venue for making a contact anyway.
Over the last 20 years I have heard him maybe 12 times. Of the 12 abt 6 were in time to just hear him make one contact and then go QRT [frustrating].
Of the other 6--- I had maybe 5 shots Max at getting thru before he signed.
I think he might make a brief schedule with a "donating club"---Paul could probably answer that question.
So persevere !  All contacts come to the deserving---so they say

On 5/10/2013 11:15 PM, Bob Lanning wrote:

What about those local Bay Area residents/members who won't go to the
meetings in out-lying locations?
It's clear from Risto's map, and from a number of posts on the email reflector, that many members don't attend meetings because they are too far away. If our meeting location rotates between several locations, some of which are a bit closer to them, they get to attend a meeting at least once in a while, and those of us on the sound end of the Bay area drive a bit farther for THAT meeting. 


We pick up a few members and we lose a few members...
I think it's a win-win, because we would be giving more of our members a chance to participate, and because we might even pick up members because we are being more inclusive. 

On 5/10/2013 11:46 PM, Jim Abraham W6EB wrote:
I think that the reality is pick up very few and lose quite a few. How many of us living on the SF peninsula will be willing to spend several hours in rush hour traffic to attend a meeting in Livermore.
That's not realistic, Jim. We can get to Livermore in 90 minutes from Santa Cruz, with rush hour traffic, and you are a LOT closer. And in a car pool, with two or more guys rag chewing, the time flies by. We find that we enjoy that time together as much as we enjoy the meetings.

On 4/22/2013 8:33 AM, wayne mills wrote:
Should the IDXC return to the Holiday Inn next year? Apparently, no decision has been made at this point, but the rumor is that because of a substantial fee that the convention has to pay to the city of Visalia, the SCDXC is strongly leaning toward moving back. The consensus seems to be that the fee is and reasonable. Sure, that money would be nice to have in a club's coffers...
I think we should defer judgement as to the reasonableness of fees charged until we've heard from K6MM and K6TD, the two very bright guys who orchestrated the move and negotiated the terms. What I DO know on the basis of my own observations are the following:
1) I heard the registration total of 850, which I think is a significant increase over prior years, and which COULD NOT HAVE BEEN ACCOMMODATED at the Holiday Inn. At the Convention Center, we used about half of the available space for the Saturday banquet.
2) I was QUITE pleased by every single interaction with staff at the Marriott and at the Convention Center. The only negative I heard was that service in the Marriott restaurant was very slow at times. This negative was completely offset by the fact that we could walk 2 blocks to Starbucks, and another block to more than a dozen restaurants and a very decent brew-pub. Not only that, but the hotel gave me maps, marked up with the walking routes to restaurants with specific types of food.
3) I saw a LOT of smiles on the faces of XYLs, who were not stuck "in the boonies" at the Holiday Inn.
4) Meeting rooms were large enough to hold our attendees, and there were enough available to hold multiple tracks of programs at the same time. Projection was fairly good, although it suffered from the spill of light from overhead that apparently could not be turned off, so that many slides with photographs were hard to see. Sound was only fair -- it was certainly LOUD enough, but speech intelligibility was marginal at times because the systems were poorly designed. (I made my living doing GOOD systems like this.)
5) What SHOULD be changed next time around is the choice of emcee, who I find boring in the extreme. If I want comedy, I'll watch guys who are good at it, like Billy Crystal and Bill Cosby. Our very interesting guest speaker, Joe Taylor, had to cut his presentation short because of all the time wasted by the emcee. 

Add me to the list of those who will NOT return to the Holiday Inn.

73, Jim K9YC

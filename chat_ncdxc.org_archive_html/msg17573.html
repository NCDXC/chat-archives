On 7/22/19 at 11:17 PM, chat@ncdxc.org (Bruce M. Croskey via Chat) wrote:
Nice fotos but very sad too... anyone doubt our hobby is in trouble, just review the picnic fotos and try to find the future of ham radio..... the young people..
I try to work with as many young people, mostly in their 20s and 30s, as I can and I see a number of them pushing into elite levels of operation, so I'm not so worried about the future of ham radio. The number of licensees is up. When I help teach ham crams, we always get 20-30 people and we get a better than 90% pass rate.
The other thing that gives me hope is that ham radio is the ideal hobby for the older person. Are your ears going? Try digital. Are your eyes going? Well, CW and phone are still there. Can't run marathons anymore? Try contesting. etc. If we just pick up a reasonable number of people when they retire, ham radio will do just fine.
What worries me most about our recent licensees is that they are primarily coming from the CERT/community service community. Now if they start using their radios for hobby like activities, that's fine. But if they decide that checking into their local ARES/RACES net once a week is enough, they won't have what it takes to do a really good job of emergency communications when "The bad thing happens". We older hams really need to go out and Elmer them into getting active in the hobby part of ham radio so they will be good at supporting their communities when the time comes.

On Wed,5/27/2015 6:26 AM, Daniel Brock wrote:
You raise a great point about doing the RFI survey. We have a ridiculously smart PhD physics guy in the club, and he has volunteered to use his spectrum analyzer and other equipment to do a noise survey.
The equipment needed for an RF noise survey must be hand-carried portable, so that it can be carried up on the roofs of buildings where antennas are contemplated. A compact RX with a calibrated signal strength meter is more practical. I own one -- it's an Elecraft KX3, and with an accompanying PX3 spectrum display, we can even study the nature of the noise.
My experience so far operating portable on campus is not encouraging, at least on the 18-28MHz bands.

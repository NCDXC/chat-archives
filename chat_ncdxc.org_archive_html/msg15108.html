I, too, was surprised at how good the condx were and what I could work with low power.  I tried to work as many as I could with 10 W.  If it was taking to long, I kept bumping the power up till I got the QSO.  Patience would have yielded more at 10 W but who has patience when the band is open?!

 

The results of my 66 QSOs were:

48 at 10 W

10 at 20-50 W

6 at 85-100 W

Very enjoyable experience for dabbling around.

4 hr 15 min at rig in 12 sessions.  Two were ‘long’ at at 1.25 and 1.5 hrs.

I’m not a big contester but I did work the contest for about an hour and a half total, mostly looking for DX that I don’t hear often.  I was amazed at how alive 20m was at night and morning into Europe, and even mid-day.  Some of the areas I heard and worked were:  LX, EU, YT, JW, 9M, ER, OH0, YL, YR, YO, E7, JT, EZ, UN, CR3, 4Z.  Nothing rare and many other countries I heard that I didn’t bother.  Most of these stations were fairly strong.  I didn’t hear any stations from Africa, India, few from South America though I probably heard every entity in Europe and old Russia.  No middle east except for 4X.  So are the bands getting better or is this just a contest phenomenon?  The band seemed completely full.  Where were all these stations before?  Seemed like the good ole days again.

 

73s, Paul, W6IBU

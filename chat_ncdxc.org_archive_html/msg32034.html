I have 3 phone band slots and 2 FT8 so far (skunked on CW so far), so no obvious focus.

There are some curious gaps in the band plan but tomorrow is the last day, don't over promise.

They've also gone to 20 way too late for NA to have any real impact.  I was there (again) as the band shortened and shifted east, no joy for me on 20M.  40 CW was... wow... just wait for 20M if they get there tomorrow.

Paul is correct, the place REALLY needs a proper team and a larger event, but... not very likely with the limitations in place.  At least there are some good ATNO to be happy about... I'd really like a CW too and least get all modes checked off.  Hopefully their Navy will allow more of this in the future (there or other islands); there is hope of good will.

The FT8 site was supposed to be remotely operated, but a 7300 on a DXpedition??? (it crumps badly in the presence of LOUD RF, which may explain a few things).  It's going to make for an interesting story to hear what they did (for antennas too).

For the moment, HamPass is back online and was showing the log in (actual) real time.

Dxpedition lite?

73,
Rick nk7i

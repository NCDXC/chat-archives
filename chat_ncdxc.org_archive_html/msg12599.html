She said that she could not publically discuss specific
cases, and in particular the one you seem to be referring
to.  She said that she would talk privately.  In general,
the advice was that if you want to complain, the only
acceptable way is to submit on line complaints on the FCC
website.  You can attach files, such as recordings.
She blamed "lawyers" for making it much more difficult
to revoke licenses.  Don't expect anything to happen
quickly.  The FCC is well aware of the situation and
it has their attention, but the wheels of justice turn
VERY slowly.  Additional "venting" towards Laura is
probably not going to help.

I don't personally know anything about the case that
can't be discussed at the meeting, except that one guy
stood up and literally yelled at Laura for several minutes
saying jammers at playing recordings of him and this
has been going on since 1977.  Her response was basically
no comment.

She mentioned Riley's "spin the dial" advice, but the
yeller said that the jammers just follow them to the
new frequency.

One thing I liked was that she said that rules against
deliberate QRM'ing are not enforced on contest weekends.

With the sad loss of K7NV (a common back yard fence neighbor of the SK estate where I got my small prop pitch, tower and SteppIR); who is repairing prop pitch (and/or selling them) these days?

Someone commented here a couple weeks ago that someone else was taking over Kurt’s legacy; but there is nothing readily visible online.

Mine died this morning; it appears to be electrical at the rotor (at least) but I won’t know what caused that, until it comes down; which is a problem because the tilt plate wants 0 deg, but the failure happened at 75 deg.

On another tack (plan B), what information is available for the new DX Eng DXE-RT4500HD-2DX (package) rotor (with a different Green Heron); can it successfully work long term with a DB36/80 and tilt plate (~225 lbs) on an HDX-555 tower?  The mast is thick wall chromoly (another 40 lbs at least).

Good, bad, so far so good, too new to know?  I only know of one failure, so far but not the cause.

What is found today:  The controller is putting out the correct voltage on the correct output wire; it’s not at fault.   The prop pitch is a two direction DC motor; the end of the winding with voltage, determines direction; common wire in the middle (in the simplified diagram; a Y with the downstroke being common).

End to end resistance is near 1 ohm (the winding is intact).  Ends to common shows (equal) high resistance and the rotor is not turning (controller faults from no motion detected).  If the common burned, I’d expect an open, not ~7 K ohms.

I had noted that it was turning slower lately; so something binding (bearing failing) could have stressed the common wiring causing it to fail but not open?

If the internal parts (commutator) are damaged, who, where for parts?

I’m bummed.  This is NOT how I planned to spend winter.

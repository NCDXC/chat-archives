You may want to look at propane.  It has more energy per unit (more efficient than natural gas) so you use less.  Unlike gas, it doesn’t go bad, storage time is not a concern.  It requires space that nat gas doesn’t. 

Either way, a generator requires conversion from gasoline, a pricing factor to be considered. 

Buy more generator than you need (20%+), to not overload it ever and to allow multiple high starting loads at the same time (freezer and frig).  Pay high attention to max load AND sine wave format. 

Some form of electrician installed switching may be code required if hardwired or manually switched, which is only slightly more than you’d spend on numerous heavy (low loss) extension cords to power stuff (meaning you can’t close doors or windows).  

I recently laid in the wires, saving a few hundred dollars in labor, to power the new tower.   The electrician only connected the wires at each end (box and panel) to ‘bless’ my work.  cha-ching!

I have a 15 kw generator powering a sub-panel, running all (including the furnace and microwave) but the garage, dryer and the bedrooms, auto-transfer switch.   And 500 gallons of propane for it, the furnace and water heater.  Generator consumption was about 7 gallons a day (no furnace, used a wood stove) on an extended outage, I’m pretty low need for energy. 

Shifting to all LED lighting matters too. 

I even had internet (DSL) during the outage; unlike most in CA recently.  Life simply went on.

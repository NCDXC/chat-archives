The 160m yagi in OH land that fell had some type of active wind load management system.  I recall they had mentioned the tower's failure was due to a failure in the wind load management system.  

The tower system should be engineered to handle the worst case scenario.  In my case the worst case is so bad (122mph) that it was not practical to engineer the system for the 'worst case'.  Instead I convinced my county engineer that I will engineer the tower for the 'worst case' with the tower fully nested.  If my tower is left fully extended and sees 100mph wind it will likely fall over.  But my ham neighbor convinced me that I'm safe.  The elements on my yagi will act as a 'fuse'.  They will come off the boom when the wind hits 100mph, hence reducing the wind load before the tower falls over :-)

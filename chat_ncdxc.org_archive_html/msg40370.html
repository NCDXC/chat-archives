I managed to figure this out as well.  The 'tracker' can be found by a link on the RI42SP QRZ page.  It takes some time to load but does have the position and time of that reading.  The QRZ gridtracker worked for me as well but it fumbles when you want to see it on a map.  Need a different projection to see the north pole region.

On Sat, Aug 30, 2025 at 12:39 PM Charles K. Epps via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
Hi guys.  Several of you have asked me how I'm finding what grid square RI42SP is in since he doesn't usually include it in his FT8 messages.  

A few weeks ago I stumbled onto a couple of web sites that let me track the ship's movement.  First, I start with https://www.dx-world.net/ri42sp-arctic-travels/ .  This opens a window with general info about RI42SP, but at the end of the first paragraph is the sentence "Position of boat using this tracker".  Click on the word "tracker" and you go to another website (written in Russian) where you can see a posting of his lat/long along with the UTC date and time he was at that location.  For example, the last time I checked, he had posted on August 30 at 17:29Z that his coordinates were 87.0107 and -139.245.  Next, I go to https://www.qrz.com/gridmapper which allows me to plug the coordinates into a grid square converter which tells me that position is in grid square CR07ja.  

I go through this exercise at least once daily so I can see whenever the ship drifts into a new grid square.

I hope this helps.

73,
Rusty, W6OAT

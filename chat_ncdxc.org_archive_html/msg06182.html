On Mar 24, 2014, at 6:26 PM, Paul@n6pse.com <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:

I believe the By-Laws need to be updated to require that anytime there is a vacancy on the Board, and there are two or more candidates for such vacancy, a Special Election should occur.

That would be the fundamentally fair way to resolve vacancies and astute Leaders would recognize that. I agree with Rich on most of his views and I also feel that nothing in the current By-Laws/procedure manual would have prevented a Special Election, had the board agreed that this was the fundamentally fair thing to do. Unfortunately the board never accepted that recommendation nor came to that conclusion.

Rich and I have the unique perspectives that when openings recently occurred on the Board, we indicated a desire to serve, yet were ultimately blocked or rejected. The process of replacing board members needs to be removed from the sole discretion of the board. Fundamental fairness should prevail.

Paul N6PSE

On Mar 24, 2014, at 17:51, KE1B <ke1b@richseifert.com> wrote:


On Mar 24, 2014, at 5:22 PM, Bob Lanning wrote:


Rich - You were never told you were "not wanted" when nominating yourself.  Only the membership can do that by not voting for you.

Nonsense. The membership had absolutely no say in the matter; it wasn't even brought up for discussion. This was a unilateral decision by the Board, rejecting volunteers and recruiting alternative members that fit their operating style.


Bylaws are not a "framework".  They are the rules or limits the board has to guide its conduct.

Again, nonsense. The bylaws define the *minimum* set of criteria by which the club operates. They do not prohibit the Board or the membership from taking any additional actions not specifically prohibited.

Analogy: "The Law" says that nobody is *required* to act to help another person, even if they are at risk of injury or death. (Yes, I know there are exceptions for medical/rescue personnel, etc.) So if someone is about to walk into danger, and you can easily stop them but don't, that's perfectly OK and your justification is "I was just following the law." Most people would disagree. Same goes for bylaws. "Just following them" is sometimes not enough. There is "what is legal," and there is "what is right." 

The Procedure Manual is a set of guidelines - yes but not the Bylaws.  The membership is the only entity that can approve changes to the bylaws.  The membership expects the board to obey the rules they adopted.

The membership expects the Board to be more than robots following a set of written bylaws. (At least *I* do.) No one is saying that the Board acted in violation of the Bylaws, and I am not even asking for any change to the bylaws. The problem is that the Board failed to discuss important matters with the membership before making their decisions, even if the Bylaws don't require that they do so. And the current flap is a direct result of those actions.


NO ONE CAN SAY ONE IS NOT WANTED.  ONLY THE MEMBERS DECIDE THAT by their vote!

Again, nonsense. The Board said exactly that to a volunteer candidate. You forget that the latest round of "elections" was held exclusively by the Board, with no membership discussion. I know that the Bylaws *permit* this; I am saying that the Bylaws don't *prohibit* a discussion, which would have helped.


If there is such strong feelings as Paul and Rich describe - why isn't anyone stepping up to run??  

I think I explained that in my earlier post. Who in their right mind would want to step up into this minefield? 

Most of the time board members are there because no one else would run.

Again, nonsense. (See above.)

That is how I became a board member.  Who wanted to be Treasurer?  Silence....  then I said "I'll do it."

The Treasurer position is different from Board members at large, or even the President's position. The Treasurer has to perform a lot of tedious work, and has responsibility for properly managing very large sums of money. This takes a lot more time than other Board positions. I thank you for your tireless work in this regard over the past years. I never volunteered for that job.


This goes to everyone.  Nominate yourself by contacting the nominating chairman or doing so at the club meeting.

I did that at the last Board opening.

You will have a chance to tell the members why you want to be on the board.

I had no such chance, either with the membership or the Board.


This club is run by the membership.

This club is run by the Board, as you yourself note above. Now, if the Board brought significant issues to the membership for discussion, I might agree with you.

Rich KE1B

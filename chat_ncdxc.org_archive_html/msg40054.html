The other issue with kh7al/kh9 in multistream FT8 is that some will call (and work) kh7al but LOTW expects the FULL call "kh7al/kh9" or the exchange won't be blessed.  Some of his packets, show EITHER call; it's a known 'quirk' of FT8.

The time MUST be WITHIN a 15 minute window (combined error); if either/both exceed that time window, no blessing issued.

It's all the more reason to KNOW that your computer is PRECISELY on time.

73,
Rick nk7i

 

On 7/16/2025 12:44 PM, Bruce Bern - K3NQ via Chat wrote:

I worked Allen, KH7AL/KH9 for a new data slot on 20 a few weeks ago, just prior to the LoTW update. Allen usually uploads to LoTW promptly, and I was surprised my contact had not been verified in LoTW as of this week. I wrote Allen, and he confirmed that our log data matched. More out of curiosity than anything else, and in light of the update, I wrote the LoTW Help Desk. They promptly forwarded the log data submitted by both of us, which looked good, but did reflect the split transmit frequencies on FT8 and the expected logging time gap, but did not provide an explanation for the failure to verify the contact. A second request resulted in this:

 

Although it is not required that a frequency be included is a QSO (only call sign worked, date, time, mode, and band), sometimes it may interfere with receiving a QSL.

Upload your QSO again with the EXACT frequency and the Exact time and it should correct the original QSO and give you a QSL for the contact.

 

I replied:

 

Jon,

As you are aware, I've corresponding with you over a puzzling failure of LoTW to verify a contact with KH7AL/KH9 in which, to my eyes, our QSO data matched. You suggested matching my time and frequency to Allen's exactly. Although my reported frequency differed from Allen's by 640 cycles, and my reported time by 45 seconds, you were right! When I adjusted my QSO data to exactly match Allen's, LoTW verified the contact. I am concerned that this may represent a glitch that could impact the LoTW system . ClubLog uses a relatively large time and frequency tolerance to realistically serve users with more primitive equipment, and less precise timekeeping.

I would appreciate if you could bring this to the attention of the LoTW IT team. I imagine a large DXpedition, with idiosyncratic time or frequency reporting could break the system.

Thanks again for your help.

73, Bruce, K3NQ (Life Member)

 

It’s reasonable to assume that any major update may introduce glitches. I wanted to be sure that everyone is aware, in case other vanishing QSOs are encountered. In the words of the immortal rock anthem, “Blame it on my OCD”.

 

73, Bruce, K3NQ

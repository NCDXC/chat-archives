Chris, congratulations on this achievement. Having also just achieved 160 meter DXCC and nine band DXCC, I know how it takes a sustained effort over years, staying up late for the 160 DX and getting up early in the morning for the 160 DX. From the West Coast it is very difficult and takes a big shift in one's priorities to make the sacrifices of sleep to achieve this goal. 160 can be a fickle band with weeks of little to no propagation and then those infrequent "magic" nights where you can work Western EU without too much difficulty. 

Anyway, having done it just now myself, I know what it takes. 

Well done!

Paul N6PSE

On Wed, Dec 16, 2020 at 10:07 PM Chris Tate - N6WM via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

It is with great pleasure that I can comfortably say that after working DG1CMZ,HA0NAR and OH0Z in the clear,  on 160 meters that I have crossed the reliable LOTW threshold to complete my 9th band DXCC, all traditional modes CW, SSB and RTTY.   I hope I am not one of the last to do it this way..  it was quite a journey.

 

I will now fire up and rinse and repeat to do it again on FT8. And work toward 2000 challenges slots.. I have been slacking a bit on that.

 

Thanks to the NCDXC and all my friends in the club for your support.. Time from DXCC # 1 to #100 on band 9 = 12 years.  5bdxcc in 2015.   With FT8 it will go lots quicker.. but I am quite proud of this accomplishment from North W6.

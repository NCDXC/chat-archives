On Tue,3/29/2016 8:48 AM, Steve Lund via Chat wrote:
They were good copy on 20m ssb last night via long path from 0230-0400. The path then switched to short path until 0500z, when they suddenly disappeared. They were working W8/9 for at least an hour after that.
I was listening on 20 SSB last night when you were, sticking with it until about 0400. I heard N6SJ work them and you calling, but I never heard more from them than a few 1-2 second bursts 10-15 minutes apart.
I've worked them on 80, 40, 30, and 20, all CW. The loudest they were on any band was perhaps S6, and most of the time more like S4. 40 around our dinner time was the most difficult because of the DQRM. With obvious coordination with their pilots, the 40M op would slide up and down to try to avoid the DQRMers, and if you had a P3 or other good spectrum display, they were easy to follow, but within 20-30 seconds someone would call zero beat with their split button off, and the DQRMers would be there in a flash.
I probably would not have had half of these Qs in the log without my P3. Those of you who worked EP when N6PSE arranged it may recall that on one of those skeds, the EP station was calling us a few kHz off frequency, and we didn't hear him. But he showed up on my P3, I worked him quickly, and spotted him. A good spectrum display can be of great help with both DX and contesting. 

73, Jim K9YC

Coax experts warn that a solid center conductor will fail prematurely if part of a rotor loop. Davis Bury Flex's stranded center conductor works well as part of a rotor loop. You can run it from the rig all the way to the antenna without worry.
Why loose DB's by running LRM400 up your tower and then using a more flexible cable and couplers for your rotor loop. I've had no problems putting on Amphenol PL259s on Bury Flex. If you don't want to bother doing connectors yourself, you can buy pre-connected Bury Flex from Davis RF.

On Sep 18, 2013, at 5:22 AM, Paul@n6pse.com wrote:



I would use Davis Bury Flex Coax.
I would recommend AGAINST the Bury-Flex cable. I used it for my initial installation here, and it is a BEAR to work with. While it is suitable for direct burial, it is VERY stiff (despite the "flex" name), and the stranded center conductor makes it difficult to fit into standard UHF connectors. (One or two strands always seem to bind at the edge and bend away from the rest of the pack. A solid center conductor, such as in LMR-400, naturally guides itself into the connector hole.)
Better to install plastic conduit and run LMR-400. You'll also be able to easily add/remove cables over time; just leave a pull rope in the conduit.

I have the MFJ version that was given to me (still sells for ~$500).  It can handle about 200 watts max.  I have not spent a lot of time using it but played with it one FD for a few hours.  It is the 40-15M version.  Here is my quick overview.

it's not as good as a tuned dipole in either transmit or receive.

You don't want to be close to it for RF exposure (use it outside)

it needs to be at least 10' above dirt, higher doesn't seem to make much of a difference

it constantly requires retuning with frequency changes, which is tedious even with fast/slow control (no hunt and pounce is likely)

I now use a Jackite pole and tuned dipoles in the RV.  Others have used small loops and are getting good results but frankly, I'm not impressed.

My cure for the HOA limitations is simple, I'm moving to N Idaho once I sell this place.

Rick wa6nhc

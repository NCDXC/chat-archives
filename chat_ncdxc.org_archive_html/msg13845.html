Plans appear to be melding together for a Maritime Mobile Expedition to the shores of Antarctica in February 2018.
I found a co-signer so I will be able to start making the dollar down and dollar a week payments for the fare on the M/V Enderby.

The Operations Manager of the ship is a very cordial person and I think getting the radio setup should not be a problem.

The Yaesu 897 and its back up - an Icom 7100 - are both functional and will get a trial run in October on my cruise from Sydney, Australia to Auckland, New Zealand. I'll be a 100 watts into a Little Tarheel screwdriver, mag mounted on the balcony framework of the Holland America 'Noordam'. I'll get a chance to meet up with a bunch of folks when the ship makes a call at Akaroa which is right over the hill from Christchurch.

Bob Hardie and his wife, Melva, were at the Huntsville Hamfest last week and I picked up the SPE 1.3KW amplifier from them. These are really fine folks and its great to do business with them.

Also present in Huntsville was Richard Stubbs and the group from MFJ. I am going to get the MFJ 998RT tuner from them. Likewise, MFJ is the easiest place in the world to go ham radio shopping. A new addition to the MFJ inventory is Messi and Paoloni Coaxial Cable. I am going to use the Airborne 10 coax because it has a very rugged polyethylene covering and it is so lightweight I can carry gobs of it in the suitcase instead of a couple pairs of socks.

The antenna is going to be two-fold proposition. A New Zealand based company, Pacific Aerials, manufactures a full line of marine antennas including a 23 foot HF Marine Vertical. They were extremely generous to me and it made strictly economic sense to buy the antenna there instead of trying to get a Shakespeare Marine Antenna sent from here down to Christchurch.

I just finished speaking with Rowan Homes who owns Nautic Electronics in Christchurch. He is going to do the install of the vertical antenna. He is also going to run a random wire antenna to an available mast for redundancy purposes.

By the way, if you haven't tried Google Talk, you are missing a real deal. It costs 2 cents a minute to call New Zealand on the Google Chrome phone app. It is fantastic VOIP without delays or static. Its free for USA calls but the Caller ID says you're from Las Vegas. However, I can never get my friend Shifty Moran to answer - he has a bunch of IOU's that are outstanding from the Desert Inn.

Next, is a cruising wardrobe - lots of sweat suits and fleece and long johns. No Ship n' Shore stuff on this cruise.

I saw three sunspots during the eclipse last Monday. I hope that number triples or quadruples this coming February.

73,
John Landrigan KA4RXP

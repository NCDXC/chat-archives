[snip]

In addition, NCDXF (which historically has been a MAJOR supporter of important DXpeditions, worldwide) wants to make sure that the DXpedition team is “invested” in the operation, i.e., they are putting their own money on the table and not trying to completely fund the trip through grants. As such, they (we, i.e., I am a Director of NCDXF) want to see the detailed budget, showing all major expenses and funding sources, to ensure that any grant made is in a reasonable proportion. Historically, this “string” has been a deal killer for Dom and the Rebel DX team. They seem to want to hold their cards very close to their chest, so to speak.

NCDXF also requires things like: 

-The team must provide write-up (article) for the NCDXF Newsletter following the trip,

-Logs must be uploaded to LoTW within a year of the trip (This can also be a deal-breaker for some DXpeditions, as QSL revenue can be affected by such “free” LoTW confirmations.)

Debated getting into this discussion, being the new guy and not a great "joiner," with ham radio the sole exception. My background includes times as a club president, vice president, director, DX column provider, treasurer and parliamentarian....and I also set up a hell of a lot of swap meet tables...so, I figured walk the talk. ( Indoor swap anyone? Ham gear only?)
It seems to me that hams as a group prefer the solitude of a key or mike in their shack over face to face ---and yes, there are exceptions. We barely say hello to each other beyond an immediate circle yet we see each other every month--and as a new guy that can be off-putting.  Efforts to close any such gap and become a more inclusive club might include 1) a DX Elmer paired to a guy just getting the hang of the first few hundred, (throw all the names in one of two hats and pair randomly---you volunteer for either hat);  2) a bit less "program" and more personal presentations however brief (as a has been suggested but with questions expected ); 3) the great idea someone offered up of expedition training via field day or just any time; 4) a little less tone of "I am an expert and have been here since fire so listen up" from all of us old dudes, 5) an  effort to find  potential new hams through schools, organizations of any kind---they are always looking for speakers to introduce a new idea for 10 or 15 minutes--retirement home presentations etc and maybe also convert a few 440 guys to our world, hi. 6) recognition of new guys beyond reading their names--I exaggerate, of course but inclusion begins at that moment or it becomes increasingly difficult or akward. I'd almost suggest we start over and devote  20 minutes every month to just 3 or 4 guys getting up and introducing themselves as more than just a ham until we exhaust the roster.  In any case there are many hams in other clubs that are less focused than we are and I suspect a few of their members might be induced to join us if we pitched our end of the hobby to them.  To that end pair up guys who currently don't know each other well and send them out as speakers. THAT will cure shyness...   JIM K8JRK

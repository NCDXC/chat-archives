Visual inspection is usually the first step.

Second step, check the supply rails.   The drain supply is probably a  bit spicy so take care.

It is typically easy to find a blown FET as they almost always fail short and in circuit tests will often catch it.   A simple continuity tester in diode mode will be sufficient.   

You should see the body diode between the drain and the source in one direction but not the other.  The gate  should be open to drain and source.   If you see any dead shorts, the device has most likely let out the magic smoke that makes it work.

I will reiterate what works for me without having to be as loud, for dxpeditions that are outside normal FT8 frequencies:

-for MSHV, I call under 1KHz, under the DX if I can, but close so as not to interfere with others thinking he's F/H.  It's especially helpful when spotters say F/H, so most of the low frequencies stay clear.

-finding an open slot can be difficult if you can't hear who else is calling, particularly at the higher frequencies and in this case EU is pointing away for us, so you can't hear everything they hear.  So I "listen", capture everyone I can see who is calling.  If he answers someone I can see, I occupy that frequency after the RR73, know that most likely this frequency is open.  This only works on non-std FT8 frequencies cause you know the caller will be gone after RR73.

 

For CW, here's what I've seen on E44OM:  though it probably depends on the op, he moves up from +2 to +10KHz split, never more than 1-2 QSOs before he moves.  Once he gets up to +10, he starts to move back down.  The trick is to not follow the crowd which gradually moves up too, but just stay high hoping he'll see you first before the crowd gets there.  It's still 99% EU/JA though.

Yes. Why do you care? What does it mean to be "DXpedition of the Year"? What are the criteria? 

Knowing you, I seriously doubt that you put in the enormous effort that you do in order to receive an award, a pat on the back, or a global "Atta Boy". I sure don't see you cutting back on future DXpedition plans as a result of some arbitrary vote.

You know better than anyone in this club the level of effort expended by the various DXpeditions, the personal discomfort and expense borne by the participants, and whether the outcome (QSOs, ATNOs, targeted bands and regions, etc.) met or exceeded expectations. The rest of us have an *idea* what it takes, and a few have gone out to join a team, but other than being able to say "I needed this one and got it", we are not generally qualified to vote for "the best" on anything other than subjective grounds.

I say, do what you enjoy doing, as long as you can do it. Achievement is its own reward. If you would do it again, it was a successful DXpedition...for YOU, the best judge.

> Folks,
> 
> I received a call from PG&E today saying they will be installing a SmartMeter in my home.  I asked about RFI problems (as in FROM the meter to me) and was given a number to call.
> 
> The CSR I spoke with said that interference is rare but possible so I asked to opt-out… only to be told I have to pay for the privilege!  They wanted a one time fee of $75 plus $10/month…
> 
> Needless to say, I'm not paying for their inconvenience…  
> 

I was one of the last holdouts around here, until the PUC adopted this pay-for-opt-out plan. Unlike the Sannacrooze loony-tunes community, I was also concerned with RFI, but have seen absolutely nothing. (Other posters have noted that transmissions are spread-spectrum in the 915 MHz ISM band.)

The Santa Cruz folks (and, I think, some Marinites) were complaining about health hazards from the radiation. (Presumably, they did this with their 1-watt cellphone pressed to their head.) I was briefly consulted on the issue by a member of the California PUC who was a former law professor of mine (and who is now on the short list to be named the new Chair of the FCC.)

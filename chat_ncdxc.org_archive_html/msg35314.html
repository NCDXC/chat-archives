The prop apps, lied again.  When they agreed it was a good time, crickets.  When they said band closed; wrong again.  Watch and wait, wins every time.

There was literally a 4 minute time window for 12M SP here tonight.  I called and got a quick +19 report as he faded away... no RR73, he's gone, but I logged it (at that signal level, he HAD to hear my report).  Gray line was over.  <blink blink>

It came through on the uploaded log, whew!  I'd sat in the shack most of the day; 10M never happened at all, just a handful of callers heard so I focused on 12M.  And waited.  And waited.

He apparently loves to run 6 streams, which is 17 dB down, per stream.  At 100 watts rig output, that's 2 watts effective per stream (plus antenna gain, a hexbeam, call it a generous  +5 dB, or about 5 watts ERP per stream, with the gain).  It appears to be not more than about 10M above the dirt, not ideal but typical of DXpeditions.

Which explains the +19 report (1400 watts, 12 dB gain) to his QRPp signal.  And why most of his signals are, uh, meager in much of NA.  The prop has been fully meh... not a lot of help there.  Spotlighting has saved the day in many places.

For a new-ish ham, he is performing very well (a quick study perhaps); but I hope he stops running 4+ streams soon so his rate (outside of EU) can climb simply by being heard better and fewer repeat reports.  

Being there for 3 weeks will prove quite the education for him; leaving time for making improvements and forming his own style.  He tried limiting the phone pileup "Outside Europe only please" and was soundly abused and smothered.  Perhaps 20M wasn't the best place to start phone.  Where other bands, the DQRM would have less effect; some could have heard him to complete the exchange.

The point is; he tried and let's hope he grows a thick(er) skin and continues to adapt and gain some pileup control when he ventures to phone again (without lack of sleep burnout).  He wasn't loud here, but he was readily copied (until smothered); but based on the pileup 'manners', I didn't make any calls on phone.  (Head meet Wall)  [Short path here, is directly into my 'town noise' azimuth, which adds 7 dB to the noise floor, harder to copy well.]

40M at 5-6 streams did not allow him to be heard above the midwest lightning event heard here in N Idaho; not one decode.  But I will endeavor to succeed, hi hi.  I saw his multistream tones ripple on the audio line, 2 streams would have allowed him to be heard here.  It was right on the ragged edge tonight (Kp wasn't bad either).

ATNO to now 3 slots confirmed, I won't complain, he's a few thousand miles from antipodal from here and that's a rough path on most any day.

Based on my observations, 17M should be the money band for the west coast; we will see how that plays out when he gets there.  (LP our morning, SP our evening)

This evening, after ARRL DX SSB was over I thought I would check into where the Clipperton Group was operating. I began checking the Reverse Beacons and found, a spot for the Clipperton group on 12M. I tuned up the rig on 12 and went to the spot and found nothing, so I figured that either I couldn't hear them or they had moved. The next spot I saw was on 15M, down in the Extra band, so I just skipped that one. I found another spot on 20CW, on 14033. When I went there the pile-up was HUGE, so I didn't stay. The next spot I saw was on 30M. This was also along with a spot for XT2TT. XT2TT was on 10120.0. I tuned up near there, and worked them on the 2nd call. I had also worked them as I was checking by 17M. I worked them on the 1st call there, with 100W and my "invisible" antennas. Then I found TX5K on 10103. They were 20db over S9. However, their operator was only working Europe. I sat for the next 3 hours and listened to them working only EU, until their signal finally faded away. I guess that is a demonstration of the focus of their mission. Bert, K6CSL

On 3/2/2013 5:59 PM, K6XN wrote:

FWIW, a lot of us already have Clipperton confirmed on many band modes from previous DXpeditions  and I for one plan to not call them on any band/mode for which I already have a QSL so those who still need Clipperton will experience a little less QRM.  I only need them for RTTY on various bands and 17M PH so I will still be actively engaged in just chasing them on RTTY and 17M phone. Perhaps near the end I may work them on other band modes but only if they are calling CQ with few takers by then. I suspect many other DXers are doing the same as I am and not calling TX5K for any band modes for which we already have a QSL from prior Clipperton operations.

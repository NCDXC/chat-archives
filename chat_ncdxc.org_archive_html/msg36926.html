Sorry for the last minute notice, but we will be running a first semi-public test of the new SuperFox system TONITE. Here’s the info if you want to join the party:

As we get ready for rc7, the development team would like your help in trying out a "between" version -- a pre-rc7 release, that has both fox and hound improvements -- in an On-the-air session on Thursday, September 19, at 1900 Pacific time, aka 0200Z Friday, September 20. The session will last for at least 45 minutes, on 14.094. Robin, W7YED, will be the initial Fox, starting in the SuperFox mode; At approximately 1915 Pacific, Jay Lopes, AC7DC, will take over in REGULAR F/H from Eastern Washington. Then, at about 1930 Pacific, Rob, N7QT, will be the Fox using REGULAR F/H from Western Washington. After 0745, tentatively watch for another Fox on 14.094, REGULAR or SF (you'll have to figure out which!).

Things we'll be testing: 
- general functionality (we didn't introduce any NEW bugs)
- a new method for verification of Fox messages for both regular FH and Superfox
- fixes for bug(s) that made it difficult for the fox to work a station more than once
- a potential aid to reduce congestion by limiting the ability of hounds to "blind call" a superfox
- ability for Foxes to make general announcements on frequency more easily

What we'd like YOU to do:
Install the 64-bit Windows version of WSJT-X pre-rc7 available at https://risse.flammenkamp.de/wsjtx-2.7.0-rc7-win64_463f65_Sep30.exe
  - please install it in a DIFFERENT location than your normal WSJT-X application. For example, I would install it to c:\WSJT\wsjt-2.7.0-pre-rc7-1. We will ONLY have a 64 bit Windows version available at this time. There are new options in the Advanced tab. For these tests, hounds shouldn't need to change any settings at this time. 
- On Thursday evening, tune to 14.094 at or before 7pm Pacific, set up in SuperHound mode. 
- Watch for and work W7YED, AC7DC, and N7QT as many times as you can. They should be able to work you more than once during the allotted time. 
- Watch for "W7YED verified" messages after Robin transmits. Same for AC7DC and N7QT.
- Watch for messages from the foxes (messages can be sent simultaneously with working stations)
- Watch for and work other Foxes on 14.094 after approximately 1945 Pacific. They may be using FH or Superfox. You can tell the difference by the way the signal sounds -- Superfox is multiple tones in the range of 750-2250Hz, while regular FH is in the range of 300-1000 Hz
- When in regular FH mode, remember to please call the Fox above 1000 Hz. 
- Turn on Save -> Save All in the WSJT-X program menu to save audio files from the session.
Send any reports (problems, suggestions, etc.) to brianmoran@gmail.com

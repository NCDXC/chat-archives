I don’t get it…

 

The FCC denies the request to remove from the rules the 15dB gain in RF amplifiers.  Read the ARRL article below.

 

http://www.arrl.org/news/fcc-denies-expert-linears-request-for-waiver-of-15-db-rule-petition-pending

 

It seems to me the comments against removing the rule (Flex Radio cited) are inappropriate.  They claim it removes the level playing field allowing manufactures of amplifiers providing more gain to enter the market.  Protectionism!

 

One point made was concern that removal of the limit would result in amateurs running more power – above the 1500 watt limit.  We already have FCC type accepted amplifiers with over 2000 watt output being sold in the US. 

 

The gain limit removal would allow the rated output of an amplifier to be achieved with less drive.  Whether we users stay within our power limit is our choice – a matter of our conscience.  So how is the 15dB gain limit going to affect our output power?  Most of us play by the rules and run our high power amplifiers at legal limit.  How is that going to change with the 15dB gain limit being removed?

 

It seems to me being able to run 1500 watts with 10 watts of drive is a good and practical capability.

 

I don’t get it.

I believe in giving credit when/where credit is due:

I have been a long time NCDXC member. There have been times when I greatly enjoyed my membership and other times when I resigned in disgust from the board for our inability to come together and get things done. 

I've been watching from the sidelines and hoping for this day to come. 

I am writing today, because I feel that the NCDXC Leadership has really turned around a "rudderless ship". Under the leadership of Mike-K6MKF, the Club became more inclusive and decisions were made to welcome and include new members. I loved how Mike welcomed each member/guest as they arrived at the monthly meeting. The club became more warm and friendly under Mike's Leadership. 

I am very pleased at the Mentoring/Leadership program now in place. This is a very positive activity. I recently asked the board of directors to make a nomination for a respected group to the CQ DX Hall of Fame and they reviewed my request and agreed to do it. 

Just today, the NCDXC is using their position as one of the most respected amateur radio organizations to add their voice to concern about the ARRL activities and positions on several topics. 

I am glad to say from my perspective that the NCDXC is now standing head and shoulders with other respected amateur organizations and momentum is building to take the club to the next levels. 

I want to say "Well done" to Mike-K6MKF, Tony-K6BV and the NCDXC board of directors for stepping up instead of stepping back. 

Best wishes to all in 2018 and good DX!

Paul N6PSE

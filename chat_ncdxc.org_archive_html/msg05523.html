Steve-

 

I worked VK0IR on 40M CW at 1600Z with a dipole and 100 watts.  That was an easy morning short path opening on Jan 18th 1997.  I worked them on 20M CW at 0559Z, on the 19th ie. well into darkness here on the evening of the 18th.  Then my log shows I worked them again on 40CW at 1411Z on the 20th .  So there were at least several days of good propagation in 1997.

 

I should mention that my station is located at 2200 feet on a ridge facing at the Pacific Ocean, and my terrain slopes away toward 210 degrees with a clear view to the horizon about 50 miles out.  That probably helped my peanut whistle station!

 

73,

 

Steve N6SJ

There was exactly ONE day of propagation. (Actually there were 3 but no contacts were made on the first two).

 

It was a Saturday morning via long path on 20m. The ops were alert enough that when they worked a few 6s in the pileup to ask everyone else to standby. Around 100 guys made it during the hour or so of propagation.

 

Their 17m cw signal was loud, too, but no one was able to break the pileup.

 

Steve, K6UM

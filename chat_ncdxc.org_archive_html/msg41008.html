For what its worth, I am also a Stanford (MSEE) RF Engineer. Who ever gave you advice doesn't know a thing about the Hi-Z preamp. This preamp is what overloads. Filters following this preamp do not solve the problem. AFAIK, it is impractical to attempt to insert any kind of filter between the whip antenna and the Hi-Z preamp The impedance level for that connection is 1000's of ohms. It would undoubtedly introduce 
phase shifts that would screw up the phasing.  If you friend knows how
to do this, I would be interested in hearing about it.

73
Rick NRK

I am intrigued by this topic and a non physics major… has to ask a friend (Stanford RF Engineer) and this is his reply for a solution to the BC RFI (RK):
Use a high pass filter of multiple steps, (only 1% caps and inductors), Tune for about 1.6 MHz. Forget the bandpass filter. Then null the output before the receiver to null the BC station, or what's left of it. Or....build series resonant filters at 1160 KHz and shunt them between the verticals end and ground.....a really good ground. 

Or do all of the above.

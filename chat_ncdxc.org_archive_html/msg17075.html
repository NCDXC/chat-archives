All I have been doing some heavy testing of FT4, as you can imagine as a contester and someone involved in Dxpeditioning, this mode has gotten the attention of a number of folks myself included.

I can share that this mode is quite fun to operate, the pace is such that I have been able to interleave qso's from multiple callers, sort of a natural call stacking capability, and the quick turnaround turns this into something that is quite appealing..   rate.  I am also trying to implement it on multiple bands simultaneously, first using my flex that has only 1 transmitter but multiple radio/antennas, then I will move on to attempting some kind of interlock.

The net net here is this is much more fast paced than any JT mode in prior implementations, along the speed of RTTY but with much less bandwidth and better decode capability and the ability to be productive with less than superstation/hilltop antennas.

Its all so new, but I think this will inevitably allow expeditioneers to increase the # of weak sig ATNO stations in the log, as well as providing a relatively engaging contesting capability.

Im pretty excited... I hope others are also.

Well, it is time to try the new Digital Mode, FT4.  FT4 is the contest version of FT8.  If you know how to use FT8, you are ready to try FT4.

Each transmission is 6 seconds instead of 30

Download Candidate Release WSJT-X 2.1.0-rc5  from http://physics.princeton.edu/pulsar/K1JT/wsjtx.html

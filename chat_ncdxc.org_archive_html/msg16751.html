As an experiment I doubt there is much of a big deal about it, assuming they were licensed to use the Ham Bands and it was just an experiment.

It does bring up an interesting point though about regulations - from government regulation of both the Banking industry and by the FCC.

Virtually all banking is already heavily invested in Block Chain.   It's a better cheaper way to do it (they make even more money) and they appear to want governments to regulate it.
The originators of Block Chain had the idea it would bypass banks and it wouldn't be regulated by anyone.  So much for that...

Now what is interesting about the FCC and regulations is that I remember at one time back in the late 1950's or early 1960's (maybe it's been amended) you could operate an unlicensed transmitter with under 100 mW and an antenna of maximum size and height (this might have been only on 27 MCs. These were all over Popular Electronics back in the day.  Enter FT8 and other digital modes and there is a potential for some (even DX) transmissions on appropriate frequencies with equipment that could meet such requirements.  Obviously this would require a radio network but Hams have already created these as well and they are relatively cheap.   The FCC seems relatively lost to their original purpose, but an opportunity to sell off more spectrum seems right up their ally.

They (whoever 'they' may be) just need a place (spectrum) to do it.  Apparently fixed orbit satellites are already being built to do just this with Block Chain.  A search of the ITU Web Site should shed some light on the subject.  That's where the international requests for licenses and spectrum go.

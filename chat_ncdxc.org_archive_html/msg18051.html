I was a bit concerned to hear there was a pretty serious injury..  Having a problem like that is no trivial matter.  They may have had a supply ship, but its a long way from civilization out there..  in fact Mangareva is a long way from civilization..  with only 1 or 2 flights out per week to modern medical on Tahiti.  As Arnies assistant on VP6D, we were very fortunate to have resources to treat issues on site.. and avoid evac.

Agree on all accounts.  First class operation.  I’ve been on DXpeditions with almost all of the VP6R operators….great on-air skills.  Working a CW pileup at 40 wpm isn’t easy but they do it routinely — and force you to really improve your own listening and sending skills in the process. 

This is the first “real” DXpedition sponsored by FlexRadio — so far, so good.  The first attempt was the ill-fated trip to Bouvet (3Y0Z), which never made it on the air.

On 2025-08-04 12:14 pm, Bruce H. Bern wrote:

It does seem redundant. The ARRL should be developing awards that are meaningful and, more importantly, attainable. The recent cancellation of the St Peter and Paul rocks illustrates the problem. 
I became active again about 5 1/2 years ago and was enthusiastically adding entities to my barebones DXCC from 1998. I hit 307 and it felt like I ran into a wall of jello. Between autocratic, paranoid dictatorships, and misguided environmentalists (mea culpa... I'm one too) who refuse to acknowledge the reality of factory fishing boats anchored off rare Pacific entities while hams are barred from even landing a remote raft rig, I'm not sure #1HR will ever become achievable again. 
It's been proposed over and over. Remove entities from the DXCC list if they haven't been activated in ten or maybe fifteen years. Inactivate them, and give us something to shoot for. 
Rather than meaningless, and cleverly named awards that are nothing different than three single mode DXCCs, develop a program that can communicate the financial and PR advantages of opening an entity to a small-footprint DXp. 
I've got a #1 trophy from a drag strip on rural Pennsylvania that doesn't exist anymore, using a bike that's been out of production for 50 years, in a class that's been eliminated. It's meaningless, but still a fond memory. Let's make ARRL modernize DXCC before us DXers become obsolete as well. 340 entities is a meaningful achievement, but it should become a fond memory, not an impediment to our hobby. 
73, Bruce K3NQ 
 

On Aug 4, 2025, at 11:41, John Litz, NZ6Q via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Paul, 

Thank you for your insight on the matter.  To your point, if it doesn't interest someone in the group then they should do nothing - but please don't kill the mojo for the rest of us.   

Maybe the new 10-Band DXCC which the Board asked staff to create at the last board meeting is more of a challenge for you - then chase that when it launches!

For many of us in the group, like me, life probably has gotten in the way too much to ever achieve #1 DXCC Honor Roll status.  My wife Annie N6ACL also has only been active on HF for about 8-years and is closing in on 200 countries confirmed, also may never get #1 DXCC Honor Roll. But here is what the Trident award can do for a lot of us:

1) Motivate some of us hams to get on digital modes seriously enough to get to 100 + countries confirmed (try something new)
2) Motivate some of us hams (like N6ACL) to work on her CW skill to work 100 + countries on CW (learn something new)
3) Up the volume on LOTW and even QSL Cards exchanged in pursuit of the new awards (yes, shameless plug for our 6th District QSL Manager N6ACL and our Pacific Division Card Checkers) 

4) (10-Band DXCC) Motivate some of us to figure out how to get 100 + countries confirmed on 160 Meters and on 6 Meters (experiment with something new)

Not meant to be an all-inclusive list, just a few ideas. For a long time, I heard people say the ARRL Board doesn't do enough for its members or Amateur Radio. Well, this is something. So, how about a nod and smile and then maybe help that fellow ham (NCDXC or other club members) who would like to earn one of these awards, or some other award, get on the air and do it. That's good for this club, that's good for every local club, the ARRL and Amateur Radio as a community! As my friend WA6UXA (SK) would say, "get on the air, you might even make a new friend"

Thank you for listening in print my friends, Stay Radio Active!

73 and Good DX!

John NZ6Q

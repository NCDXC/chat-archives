This was the first convention I was able to attend in a number of year and it was a wonderful time. I went Friday and Saturday. The programs were very good, the vendor room was good and I had a chance to ask a couple of question of various vendors that I had been interested in. Having been active with the League in the past, I enjoyed seeing all the people show up from Newington. I also felt the location (the Marriott) was good. I didn’t have any problems parking, nor did I experience any delays when trying to get lunch. It was great being able to see people I haven’t seen for years (decades?), too. It was fun talking to people at the DX Club table.

 

If there is a Pacificon 2013, I will be sure to attend.

 

Ross

Here is your NCDXC question of the week?

 

 

Did you attend Pacificon/ARRL National Convention?    If so, what parts did you enjoy?

 

 

___________________________________________________________________

 

I attended Pacificon on Saturday and Sunday. On Saturday, I had my 14 year old son Ryan-N6RYN and his friend in tow. As soon as we arrived, we were approached by an ARRL official who asked if the boys would like to have lunch with Dr. Lee Morin, the NASA Astronaut who was there. The boys were so excited and they enjoyed their lunch and asked Dr. Morin many questions. While they did that, I enjoyed the Flea Market. I just love to see that vendor that brings the old restored Microphones and old radios.

 

It was fun to watch W1AW/6 in action. John K6MM was there helping to make sure that everyone got a turn. The vendor forum was super with a good mix of vendors. Elecraft was really busy with a lot of interest in their products. I came away with a new KPA-500.

 

Most of the forum presentations were well done. Most of the rooms were standing room only. On Sunday, the crowd was smaller.

 

I thought that the ARRL did a great job with the Youth Activities. The kids got a real kick out of seeing a live QSO with the International Space Station. What a great event!

 

Paul N6PSE

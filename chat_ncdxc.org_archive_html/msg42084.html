﻿

Agreed.  Their performance is much like a larger team.  The QSO rate is roughly 900/hr over the duration.  They'll bust 100k this weekend.

It was disappointing yesterday to hear them work only EU while they were loud here; but they came back later in the day to focus on NA (with some SA).

As always, signals could be better on the lower band (mine too for that matter) but I have 80-10 FT8 logged and I'm waiting for more phone (they quit on 10 phone, JUST as the west coast was opening).  6 streams on 60M; questionable choice on a QRP band; but logged once they went to 2 streams (yes, QRP with the new restrictions).

They're making a reasonable effort to be fair; though the stats clearly favor EU (and it's not an EU path from NA).  They're noting where the band mode slot gaps are falling and reacting, that's rare and critical (for slot collectors).

The CW ops are first class and the phone ops are really working to get through (with a LOT of unruly callers).  The pileups, are brutal, testing their patience, there have been some outbursts (WELL deserved) from the ops.

A++ grade.

But don't they ever sleep?  Can they maintain this for another couple weeks?  It's not just FT8 all night like many, an op is actually at the controls every hour (phone or CW).

Yesterday they had 5 bands active at once (everyone in the pool!).

They note that their generator will be down for service later today, expecting an hour of QRT; so they'll actually get to walk on the beach to enjoy that.  AND they're moving the 160 antenna and BOG to avoid the local 50 Hz noise better.  No mention of which direction the BOG was looking.

They had enough signal on 160 CW last night that even the PNW could hear them!  But with a 160 contest this weekend; it maybe next week for another attempt.

On 80M CW, our gray line added a measured 15 dB of signal (from mud to almost loud); it was magical.  Their gray line, didn't help.

73,
Rick nk7i

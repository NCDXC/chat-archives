Rich, I suspect the simplex operation from PT0S might have been accidental. My reasoning is two fold:
 
1) Simplex operation makes no sense and would be very ineffective.
2) I know that they are using Elecraft K3 rigs. These are my favorite DXpedition/Home rig. The first time I used them on a DXpedition was last year at ST0R. They take some getting used to. We found that quite often when you are operating Split and you either lose power to the rig or QSY to another band, that the Split set up reverts to simplex. The display can still say split, however the TX and Rec frequency reverts to the same on both VFOs. I suspect this is what happened to the PT0S operator.
 
Aside from that little issue, the Elecraft K3 rigs are outstanding in every way and really fantastic for DXpeditions!
 
Paul N6PSE

Lots of folks (including me) have been complaining about the HUGE spreads that PT0S has been using; as much as 15 kHz on CW, 30-40 kHz on SSB, and at one point they were working up 65 kHz on RTTY.

So yesterday, for some strange reason, they were running on 17m CW, *SIMPLEX*! What a madhouse! Hundreds of folks calling within a few tens of Hz of each other, and loud domestic stations drowning out the PT0. (At least there was no need for the frequency cops! He was NOT listening "Up! Up! Up!".)

And I got through with just one call. (It showed up in his uploaded log today.) Easy, if you are on a mountaintop with a Yagi and legal limit, and you know where he is listening. (That last part is the most important.)

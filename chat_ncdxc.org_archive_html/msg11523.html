Have you ever wondered if you were getting out?  Now there is a collection of website listed at http://websdr.org/ where you may be able to hear yourself.  All you need is your computer and internet access.  I found that few of the SDR website will not come up possible due to internet access limitation.  I am listening to 40M LSB from a station located in Russia.  EA2DV is on 40M LSB asking for US Qs on 7.16491Mhz.

One of my mini-project is to add a Panadapter from http://mypanadapter.com/ for my IC-756 PRO III.  I expect the equipment should arrive in the next few days and costed me about $70 plus S/H.  The package includes a SDR dongle, cable to connect to radio IF output, very short antenna and software along with instruction.  It uses a RTL-SDR tuner as the SDR receiver/tuner and tap the output of the 1st IF circuit of my radio via a plug-in cable.  I am looking forward be able to view about 2Mhz and more of whatever band I am tuned to, hopefully for the weekend.  I am sure that once I understand how everything works, I should be able to reduce the total cost for my next radio project.

I have played with SDR dongle already to receive VHF/UHF signals including Ham band 1.2Ghz FM.  You can download various SDR software include SDR-Sharp (SDR#) from http://www.rtl-sdr.com/.  There are many add-ons to enable various decoding from CW to text messages from pager transmitter and be able to use your SDR dongle as a scanner.  The great thing is that the SDR dongles are from $15 to $30.  

May you find the signal you are looking for...

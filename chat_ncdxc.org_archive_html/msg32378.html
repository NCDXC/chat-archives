On Nov 26, 2023, at 5:08 PM, Bruce Bern <eagleyemd@comcast.net> wrote:

﻿Hi Richard,
I reentered ham radio only about 3 years ago, and QSLing had changed considerably. 
FWIW, when you work a rare entity, the first thing to do is check QRZ.com. At the upper left there’s a line “QSL via”. That usually tells you everything you need to know. Rarely, you may have to Google or go to DX World for further info. 
Most expeditions use OQRS, usually either through ClubLog (hit Log Search, and follow the prompts) or through two Brits, M0OXO and M0URX (your guy for T88WA). 
They all prompt you to enter date and times of QSOs to be sure they were real 2-ways, and then redirect you to PayPal. 
If you set your logging program (I use HRD) to upload all your Qs to ClubLog (as well as LoTW), ClubLog automatically verified your contacts when you request the card. 
For rare, non expedition contacts, you may need to mail a request directly to the op. That’s a whole ‘nother discipline and I’ve got a set of slides from a convention talk I could send you if you’re interested.

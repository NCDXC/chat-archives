I am not sure why people get their shorts in a big knot over "lists" and "arranged contacts"--particularly when they have no control over their existance anyway.  The Dx'er knows whether or not he has a good contact [as does the station being contacted]
So its all up to your own ethics and principals.  Everybody knows whats "right" on contacts and conformations-----thats not even an issue.   Examples of not-right are using far remote stations, 3KW + Amps, forging DX cards, whatever etc.
In the end you are not in true competition with other Dx'ers --only yourself.     And if you cut corners you know it.
So everyone to their own.

On 10/18/2013 1:14 PM, Paul Ewing wrote:
NCDXC Club member Wayne Mills-N7NG has written an article on his DX University blog about our skeds with EP3PK. Wayne often writes about issues that we discuss on NCDXC “Chat”.
 
You can read his latest entry at: http://www.dxuniversity.com/dxing/weeklydx.php?id=63
 
I too remember the DX nets of the 1970’s and into the 1990’s. I participated in a few as a new “DXer” and found them tedious and non productive. It seemed to me that the Net leaders were more concerned with pounding on their own chests rather than moving traffic along.
 
Fast forward to today, I hope you notice that I have tried to play a rather limited role in the skeds with EP3PK. Unlike the skeds of yesteryear that Wayne is reminding us of, I have limited my involvement to informing you of the sked date and time, telling you what frequency and mode and then letting you make your calls. I advise you via chat and the repeater if he changes modes.
 
In a few instances, I had to remind callers that these skeds were arranged for those seeking an all time new one. I have not said “good contact” or worked as a relay in any fashion on these skeds. Everyone that made a contact did it on their own.
 
Wayne writes that “today we may again need to discuss ethics and even morals”. He is concerned that some may be claiming QSO’s that were incomplete.
 
You’ve likely heard the skeds and now you can read what Wayne has written that he feels new DXers need to know.
 
Paul N6PSE

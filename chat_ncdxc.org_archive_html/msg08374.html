A key success factor is often timing relative to propagation.  Sometimes the
window of copy is very short and, of course, at a very specific time.  Copy
can be great one day and non-existant the next.

For me, FT4TA was quite loud on 3523 at 1430z on two days of the Dxpedition.
I began hearing them at 1415z and they had pretty much faded away by 1500z.
On other days, I had little or no copy during the same window.  Outside of
this window I heard others calling but could not hear FT4TA.  I was using a
horizontal dipole at 85', broadside at 70 degrees, on a ridge top.

Those people reporting good copy and actually working them vs. those who
struggled could simply be a matter of which day each person was listening.
And, the differences reported could also be due to differences in QTH,
antenna, direction, time of day, etc.  A lot of important variables to
consider to make an informed comparison.

Regarding the voltage fluctuation.  I know the utility has taps on local substations.  As I recall from my PG&E experience which was a pretty close relationship some or many of the substations have automatic tap switches to compensate for increase or decrease in load.  I had heard at times an automatic tap changer may not home in on a tap and will switch back and forth.  A remedy was for the utility to dispatch someone to at least bolt the tap down until the auto-function got fixed.

 

If it is a big deal and you can show a log of some sort when this is happening, PG&E will be able to check their records or at least have someone check out you local substation.  May work and then may not.

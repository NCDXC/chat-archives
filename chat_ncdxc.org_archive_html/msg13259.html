On Mar 13, 2017, at 11:41 PM, Bob Lanning via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

 

Thanks Paul for the SPE information.

 

I need more info on the new SPE 1.5K-FA amplifier.  Their home website in Italy doesn’t mention the new amplifier.

 

I really like the LDMOS roughness and the switching power supply in the 1.3K-FA.  And it is small and light weight.  

 

The 1.3K-FA idles at 1300 watts and peaks at times 1600 watts.  I have to say though my linear testing shows it is linear up through 1200-1300 watts then starts to roll-off up to 1500 watts.   The LDMOS data sheet confirms it is a 1300 watt device but will operate at 1500 watts with a 3dB overdrive but I doubt the specs hold up in that region.  As a comparison my Alpha 8410 doesn’t start to roll-off until around 2200 watts!  That is when the two 4CX1000s start to draw grid current.

 

I have less than a year left on the SPE 2-year warranty. So far SPE has not offered a schematic for the 1.3K-FA.  Kind of wondering if they ever will.

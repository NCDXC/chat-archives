Why stop at deleting North Korea?
 
Why not just delete any country that practices gross human rights violations? 
 
According to Freedomhouse.org the following countries are the current “worst of the worst” in human rights violations;
 
North Korea, Somalia, Burma, Tibet, Libya, Cuba, Sudan, Turkmenistan, Equatorial Guinea, Syria, China, Saudi Arabia, Eritrea, Uzbekistan, Laos, Chad, Belarus, South Ossetia and Western Sahara.
 
Oh, there is one more country that is on a list of known countries that “water board” imprisoned and unconvicted persons. Want to guess who that is?
 
Jim’s views are typical of the myopic American who gets his news from CNN, ABC, CBS, NBC, PBS and Fox News. Do any of you think these news sources really tell you what is going on in the world?  Yes bad things happen in North Korea. But bad things happen in many other places too.
 
As one of few Americans who has been to North Korea, Yemen, Myanmar, Vietnam, Iran, South Sudan, Egypt, Turkey and Iraq, I can tell you that things are simply much different than our mainstream western media present. You have to see it, feel it and be there to get even a small glimpse of how things might really be.
 
Unless you go there, talk to the people, “break bread” with them, play volleyball and drink beer, you will never really begin to understand the complexities of places beyond our border.
 
If you remain close minded and shut off parts of the world that you fear and don’t understand, well you will just remain misinformed.
 
Just saying,

Perhaps many do not know that in the Past the NCDXC petitioned the ARRL to add North Korea to the DX list.  It was done with the suggestion that it count once it was put on the air.  The ARRL responded that it would do so.  It was done in good faith and the intentions were that it would be really a challenge to find it on the air. It was believed that it only was added by the suggestion of our club.  Now is the time for change for the betterment of the Hobby. 
As Jim has clearly pointed out,  and I agree, it should be removed from the list until such time as the country might have a more forgiving ruler.
I for one would totally agree that the country count should drop down to 339 and with removal of North Korea just make it a deleted country.  This would allow many to look forward to at least operating towards #1 on the Honor Roll.  I do have it confirmed but would have no objection to removing it from the list.

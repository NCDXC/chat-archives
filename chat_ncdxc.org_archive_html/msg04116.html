Russ Bentson, K6KLY, will give an informal talk at this Wednesday's
meeting about Meteor Scatter Propagation.  The talk will include
information about equipment, software, and procedures including
antennas, frequencies, times, reflectors, procedures, and WSJT FSK441.

Russ was first licensed in 1957 and has been in every June VHF contest
since 1959.  He holds the Pacific Division Single Operator high power
record for the June VHF contest and is currently the only operator in
the Pacific Division with 10 band DXCC, with over 2400 band countries.
 He holds WAS #212 on 6 meters, and VUCC on 6 meters and 10 GHZ.  Russ
is a member of the DXCC #1 club.  During the 1980s Russ operated the
CQWW phone contest from several countries, and placed 14th in the
world in 1982.  He also holds the call P40LY in Aruba.  Currently Russ
is working on a VUCC on 2meters and getting very close.

When:  7pm this Wednesday, November 13
Meeting Location: Silicon Valley Chapter of the American Red Cross,
2731 N. First Street at Plumeria Drive (southwest corner) in San Jose.
 Visitors are always welcome!

If you haven't been to the Red Cross, "talk-in" is usually available
on the Association's repeaters. Best choice would be 2m/220.  And for
those who are hungry, several of us get together for dinner prior to
the meeting at Carl's Jr on First Street, south of Trimble Rd (between
Trimble Rd and Component Dr) at approximately 6:00pm.  Club Web Page:
www.wvara.org

Jim Peterson K6EI

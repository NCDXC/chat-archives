On Jun 4, 2023, at 2:18 PM, Roberto Sadkowski via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Sorry, I’m a new Ham. I feel for those who waited 60 years to get China (at least China has a few people) but do we want to punish the future generations of Hams with these ridiculous arbitrary and historical rules?  The future does not bode well for the Hobby based on demographics. Lets attract more attention not dissuade new generations from participating in an activity for which they have almost no chance to achieve success.

I have a different take on ALL of this. I do not define “success” as “winning a game defined by the ARRL.” Therefore, I ignore the ARRL and its DXCC rules entirely, and define DX success as my ability to contact as many places as I can, however achieved. I also consider ANY form of confirmation to be acceptable (to me), even if not acceptable to ARRL (about whom I don’t care about a whit).

So, I’m perfectly fine with contacting a station that may be on a boat moored to a dock, or even a few meters away from land, neither of which qualify under ARRL rules. So be it.

I’m perfectly fine with a confirmation by email from the other station, which is not acceptable by ARRL. (Heck, it hasn’t happened, but I would take confirmation by a phone call from the operator, or their QSL manager.) Yet the ARRL will accept unauthenticated hand-written slips of paper, i.e. QSL cards, as long as they are “verified” by one of their approved “card checkers”. Sheesh! (No insult intended for any card checkers on this reflector. I just don’t care about ARRL approval.)

So I save a fortune on purchasing LoTW credits (although I use LoTW), silly certificates and plaques from ARRL (to them, LoTW and DXCC are *profit centers*, not a service to its membership). In the end, each ham knows what stations they worked, regardless of whether that station was “approved” by ARRL. I don’t need anyone else’s approval. For example, if someone is crazy enough to “sneak” into a forbidden area, whether Tibet or DPRK or whatever, they are taking a great personal risk; but if I contact them there, I consider it a real contact, while ARRL would not consider that an “approved” QSO. Again, I don’t need their approval to validate my life.

DXing is fun, so enjoy it however you want. Don’t let someone else tell you how to play the game, IMHO.

Rich KE1B

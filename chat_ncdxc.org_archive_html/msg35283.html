FTx modes, rightly or wrongly give a DXpedition team (or solo op) a LOT of contacts quickly and without much on site effort (one op can manage more than one band at a time).  The total logged station count, soars.  It also helps small stations to get logged more easily (debatable).  All these factors make is a very popular mode.  (And it's BORING, requires minimal attention in the shack.)

EU prefers MSHV for JT modes; the US prefers WSJT-X (the primary code source for the other variants; the trunk of the tree).

MSHV is broken, it sometimes FAILS to send RR73, but logs the entry (and the calling station continues to report or starts again).

MSHV can send RR73 more than once (minutes later), even if not prompted by the caller report, to ensure that the calling station sees the confirmation (and stops calling).   (Probably faulty buffering)  But...

It appears that certain teams count rise faster than other similar teams and that EACH time RR73 is sent (or not), MSHV logs that as a new entry (on the DXpedition side).  Therefore sending RR73 more than once and logging each time increases the total 'logged stations' count.  We don't know which setting/flaw is in play by any team or if the stats they present, are then truthful.

I do not believe that this 'feature' is completely a mistake, but is intentional to (falsely) raise the total stations worked count and increase the apparent pileup (because some choose to seek RR73, again); that one completed contact (RR73 sent or not), adds 2 or more tics on the total worked counter.  That log, lies, bringing discredit to the team while being praised 'for such a high logged count so quickly'.

It is partly (opinion) for bragging rights and personal justification of validating worthiness (ego stroking).  It falsely boosts the stats, making them a lie too; a disservice to those wanting to track efforts; 'which team did best'.  100,000 in the log; GREAT, how many are unique, from how many zones, bands and modes?

If a station calls again because the RR73 wasn't received (or seeks a dupe); yet another log entry (or 2 or 3...).  What is truth?  I submit that it is ONE valid completed contact, for that band and that mode only.  How do we get there?  Should dupes for any reason, be counted?

A more accurate system of stat counts should be considered; for team rating and effort validation based on data less flawed.

To make this MSHV feature moot  (AND insurance or oops dupes log entries); instead of total stations worked being a goal, a system of incorporating band, mode, zones with the uniques worked; a 'scoring' system could be presented.  

EXAMPLE:
[(Unique calls per band X bands used X modes X zones) / zones not worked] /1000 = score (or similar equation) would present clearly, how well the team managed in it's effort (and reduce or ignore counts that lie).  Did that team really give an effort or where they simply there?

It also factors in the propagation at the time window the team/op was active (poor prop, lower zone score).

[e.g. In the recent Liberia effort, I logged 38 mode slots over 10 bands from one zone; adding 380 points to the team score.  (I plead boredom hi hi.)]

One more consideration in this scoring could also include lowering the 'needed' count by zone but that is an abstract that is difficult to honestly quantify.  Other factors could be actual hours on the air (per band) and the size of the on site team (to better measure solo vs team vs TEAM).  Bonus points could include frequency of logging; livestreaming or other niceties.  Deductions might include money spent by location attended (difficult to rate).

If that score is high; the team/op did very well WHERE IT really MATTERS (global equality and mode used balance and diversity) and those are the areas we should measure if total count is called into question.  It ignores dupes, no matter what the cause (software or insurance or boredom etc).

Scoring in this fashion would benefit (awards or bragging rights) those teams/ops that made a conscious effort to work the world and negatively affect those who tend to phone home most of the time (wannabe home town heroes) and thus only work beyond that when forced to do so (because they don't manage the pileup at all and often react only at the end of their stay for other places).  

Propagation is the only variable that cannot be managed, but using it wisely would pay off in a higher score; ignoring it would greatly lower the score.

The logs are filed, so retroactive evaluations (once the equation is created) would just take time.  And it would help answer "Which team REALLY did the best job for the DX community?" (the final area is subjective, personal worth and gains).

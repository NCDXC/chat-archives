An ideal dipole has 2.14 dBi of gain. The field intensity from 15 watts into an isotropic radiator is the same as 9.15 watts into a dipole. I believe that is how the ERP was derived. It is most likely specified because an isotropic radiator is unrealizable. The Poynting vector is zero (i.e. no power radiates from an isotropic radiator even if one could be constructed.). Furthermore, the specification matches the current one (100 watts into a dipole). As far as feedline losses, they should be very low at 5 MHz. Maybe some hams are still using coax they got from a WWII surplus store, that would be the exception. Connectors should be less than .05 dB each so the power out of your transmitter should be close to the power delivered to the antenna. I mentioned this before, the difference in ERP between the new allocations and the current one is over 10 dB. That would mean most stations you hear below -14 in the current band allocation you will not hear in the new one. That is significant.

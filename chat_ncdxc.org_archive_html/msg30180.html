Hello Hams,

 

Please pardon the broadcast nature of this message, but this threat to ham radio deserves your prompt attention.

 

In a nutshell:

·         A well-resourced ($$$) consortium of investors in international currency exchange has petitioned the FCC to grant permission for very high power transmissions.

·         It is very likely these transmissions will render many amateur radio bands unusable.

·         The window for public input to the FCC ends July 31st. (VERY SOON)

·         You can make a quick and easy online comment to the FCC expressing your views

·         How to make a comment to the FCC below

 

The Details:

https://www.arrl.org/news/commercial-interests-petition-fcc-for-high-power-allocation-on-shortwave-spectrum

More if you are interested:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=glmmYOXghiI

https://www.fcc.gov/document/reference-and-information-center-petition-rulemakings-filed

 

Make a comment to the FCC:

https://www.fcc.gov/ecfs/filings/express

Here are key form fields and what I put in, others are self-explanatory:

Proceeding(s): Specify the FCC proceeding(s) to which your filing refers: RM-11953 (stands for Rulemaking 11953)

Name(s) of Filer(s): [You]

Address: [This will be in the public record so I used my PO box for privacy]

Zip Code: [ZIP+4 is required]

These may be helpful: https://ca.postcodebase.com/zipcode5-list OR https://www.smarty.com/products/single-address#demo

Brief Comments:

My message included the following points, but think of your own and use your own words

·         I am in opposition to the petition from the Shortwave Modernization Coalition’s petition …. RM-11953

·         Time is needed for a thorough and objective impact assessment.

·         Encourage the applicant to develop non RF alternatives

·         If spectrum use is granted, it should be with power and spectral requirements not exceeding those under which amateur radio operates.

 

The commission staff will summarize comments for the five FCC commissioners, so keep comments brief and factual.

Express appreciation, ask for support, avoid negative tone or complaints.

 

Time is of the essence - Please forward this message to Hams you know

 

-Jeff AJ6LG

On 6/19/2013 7:43 AM, rholoch@comcast.net wrote:
What say you?

A 2-element vertical array will beat a LOW dipole by several dB at the low angles needed for DX, but a HIGH dipole will beat the vertical.by several dB. "Low" is less than about 3/8 wavelength, "high" is a half-wavelength or better. So it depends on what you can do. 

These are the sorts of questions that are easily answered by modeling with NEC, and by studying the ARRL Antenna Book and the ON4UN book. 

Also, take a look at AA7JV's "Garden Beam" in a recent QST, which is a rotatable vertical Yagi that sits only a few meters off the ground.  His dimensions are for 20M, but could easily be scaled to 30M.

On Jul 4, 2019, at 4:40 PM, George Williams via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

 

Back in March, I worked 9G2HO.  Today, I would like to send a QSL and donation because of this station is an All Time New One for me.

 

I found a website that indicated that they only do LoTW.  Does anyone know of a direct mailing address or the QSL Manager for 9G2HO so I can check if I am in the log?

 

 

Why not just follow the instructions that the station operator gave you? He did that for a reason. He probably doesn’t want to deal with bureaus, tons of paper cards, labels, stamps, log checking, cost, etc. LoTW can be automatic (most logging software can upload to LoTW, even in real-time on a QSO-by-QSO basis) and hassle-free.

 

I see that 9G2HO regularly uploads to LoTW, and did an upload as recently as TODAY at 0608z. So upload your log (or just that QSO if you must), and you will get an LoTW confirmation if you are “in the log.” For DXCC purposes, it’s just as valid as a QSL card. And for USA stations, getting an LoTW certificate is fairly easy (it can be a pain for foreigners whose country does not have a published, reliable listing of all amateur licensees, like the FCC.)

 

I wish EVERYONE would upload their logs to LoTW; it is so much easier, cheaper, and time-saving that shipping physical paper around the world.

 

(FYI: Between myself and Anna, we have about 35-40 LoTW certificates, for each of our many DXpedition callsigns, special event operations, The Monkeys, etc.)

 

Rich KE1B

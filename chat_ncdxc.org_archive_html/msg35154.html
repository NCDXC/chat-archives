The clock synchronization requirement is derived from the WSJT-X algorithm processing that looks for signals within -2 to +3 seconds of the algorithm time reference by doing correlation processing of the repeated Costas synchronization tone sequence that is repeated three times in the FT8 transmission.

Thus while there are many convenient methods to keep your computer synchronized to UTC, by design the algorithm is very tolerant of signals received out of synch with UTC.

WSJT-X processing is state of the art and uses every current method to optimize receive sensitivity in the presence of multiple interfering signals.

So while we all spend time to synchronize our time and find a “clear” spot in the waterfall, the WSJT-X algorithm does everything possible to decode signals with time errors and small signals under larger signals.

Bottom line is a "computer clock properly synchronized to UTC within ±1s" is adequate.

If you want to see an explanatory discussion of FT8, check out my talk on the NCDXC YouTube channel.

Further info on what this can do:
According to the WSJT-X documentation, it is required to have a "computer clock properly synchronized to UTC within ±1s".
JTSync highlights those stations whose signal's time offset relative to your computer clock (DT) is more than 1 second. When trying to contact such a station, it can be helpful to advance your system clock temporarily to reduce the discrepancy.
To simplify the QSO process with such stations:
  1. Right click the mouse on the required station and choose the "Sync with this Station" menu item.
  2. Click the Update button to adjust the local clock.

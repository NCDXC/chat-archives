As I said in my earlier post, we’re OK, just outside the fire and evacuation zone (for now). We lost power 2 nights ago, but amazingly the generator was able to handle the load from one of my air conditioners (the one cooling the living quarters), so we got through two days of 111 deg F temperatures. All time highs for the 25 years I’ve been here. Power loss was due to winds blowing a branch into the lines, and actually breaking the power lines. The live line fell on some brush and started a (small) fire, which our neighborhood was able to put out before the FD arrived. (Living up here, everyone keeps to themselves EXCEPT when there is a fire, a downed tree, a mudslide, etc. Then everybody is there with chain saws, fire extinguishers, whatever they have to help.)

Power restored after about 21 hours. Never lost Internet, but I wouldn’t be surprised if I do, since my relay tower is right in the fire zone, so if that goes up in flames, no one will be doing any repairs for a long time. (I get my Interet via terrestrial microwave, using the 5.8 GHz ISM band, with a data rate of about 8-9 Mb/s, bi-directional. No cable service here, too long a distance for decent DSL.)

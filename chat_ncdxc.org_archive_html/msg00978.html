On Jan 14, 2013, at 7:52 AM, <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:

Here is your NCDXC Question of the week (QoTW)
 
 
What is your most memorable DX Contact?   What contact sticks in your mind as most memorable to you?


Working the vatican a few years ago.  2004 on 20M SSB.  Tuning around in an afternoon, I heard the pileup, listened, listened some more, and realize who it was.

He was working east coast.  I figured I could put my call in and see if I could be heard over the East Coast.  "Who's the K6?".  Of course, it was me!

He called me back, and given the size of the pileup, I did a quick name, qth ("south of San Francisco"), signal report, and turned it back to him, as that seemed to be his script.

He came back, gave me his info and then asked for more detailed QTH info.  As I started to transmit, I started to say, I live in a suburb of San Jose.  Before I got two syllables out, stopped and said, I live in Cupertino, which is a suburb of San Jose, and named after a small northern Italian Village, Copertino, by De Anza, when he first came up from Monterrey Bay and saw SF Bay.  "Back to you".

The monk said he knew exactly where I lived, as up until a couple of years ago, he'd been a monk at the Monastery in Cupertino, on the hill overlooking SF Bay.  As it turns out, he also lived down the street from me.  We had a wonderful QSO discussing the american city of Cupertino - restaurants, churches, landmarks, etc.  I could hear the pileup going - enough with the old home week, sign so we can make a contact too.

Wonderful fraternity we have.

73, K6TD - KR-

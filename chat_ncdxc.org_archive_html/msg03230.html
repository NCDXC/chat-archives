On 9/18/2013 2:55 PM, Rick Bates wrote:

Zorro wasn't over loud but he was right in the computer hash band
That stuff around 14030, 21052, and a bunch of other frequencies is wired Ethernet. Some is radiated directly from the boxes (Ethernet switches, WiFi routers, cable/DSL modems), but a lot of it is radiated as a common mode signal on the cables. That common mode component can be knocked down a lot by winding a suitable number of turns around a #31 or #43 ferrite core. 5 turns is a good starting point. If the cables are longer than about 0.2 wavelength at any frequency you care about, you need a choke at each end. It also helps to keep cables as short as practical. Trash also can couple to power cables and radiate.
If you have neighbors, you'll hear their systems too. Each is running on its own stable clock, so each is on a slightly different frequency. Kill power to your own router or switch to identify your signal.

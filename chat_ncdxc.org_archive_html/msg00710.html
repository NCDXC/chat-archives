On Dec 13, 2012, at 9:37 AM, Wayne Mills wrote:

You have to be careful in drawing conclusions from the numbers supplied by the online table. The number of logs, QSOs and bytes is pure data. The QSOs/minute shown below is simply calculated from the net change in QSOs – in an hour – showing the net change average per minute. This number is not the QSO processing rate. No such number is given in the table.
 
The processing rate in QSOs per second is the unknown (to me, anyway) in all of this.. That is, the number of QSOs processed per second or minute. My latest log, uploaded right after the CQWWCW contest was processed in the last day or so. There were 1003 QSOs processed in 251.701057 seconds. (This number shows up on your processing report.) That’s about 4 QSOs per second.  If LoTW has 9 million QSOs in the queue, and no more logs are uploaded, just over 24 days would be required to clear the queue at 4 QSOs per second. I don’t know where the delay estimates shown in the chart come from.
 
When LoTW was first turned on, it could process QSOs at the rate of at least 100 QSOs per second. Last year, when the slowdown occurred following CQWWSSB, the rate reported to me on the processing reports was down to about 1 QSO per second. Even prior to CQWW this year, processing rates were something no greater than 10 per second, and in many cases less than that.
 
LoTW verifies the digital certificate of each QSO record that is uploaded. That takes quite a bit of time, and that’s part of the problem. Processing delays can only get worse. As others have noted, as the database grows, processing necessarily slows because the number of comparisons grows.

I note that the queue is ever increasing, while being processed which tells me that the processing server is not adequate to the task.  There is no chance that even if the queue were zero that an incoming log would be processed before the next log came in.  In short, at this rate, it will never catch up to real time.
 
The only answer is more servers, in parallel or each new DXpedition or contest will swamp the existing system.  I’m assuming that it is only XML manipulations and additions; it should be simple and fast.
 
I’m wondering if it would be more efficient to upload an entire log or as you work each contact, but thinking that the time (cpu cycles) consumed for processing would be about the same.  It would depend on how each log was entered into the queue (separate processor/server?).
 
Not impressive.

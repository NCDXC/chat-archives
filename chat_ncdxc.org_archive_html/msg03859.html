On Oct 28, 2013, at 3:10 PM, Rich Holoch <rholoch@comcast.net> wrote:

There is a bit of overlap in the Rohn telescoping mast, so its ~ 27' + 36' for a total of 63 feet . . . . my goal was in fact - to add some capture area, and to make it a little less inefficient (sorry for the double or triple negatives . . . ). I do feel that it has achieved this - and that it has been worth it. You should see how well this antenna performs on 17M - very very nice surprise. My phased vertical array on 40M do show about 3 dB gain over this single tall vertical - which is within spec and as modeled and expected. I did do a summers long A-B test of this very tall vertical over a Cushcraft R8, and this vertical was about 3 dB better on 40 and 30M than the R8. I am sure its the 76 buried radials and additional capture area. Its actually been a lot of fun doing serious A-B testing of verticals here. They can be tuned with some effort! For a 63' antenna, the profile is very low -  lot more "unobtrusive" tun I would have expected. 

There was an article in the ARRL Vertical Classics book called "A Big Signal from a Small Lot" which was my inspiration. Also the fact that my neighbor cut down the 70' tree this spring that used to hold up my high 40M dipole - hi hi.

On 160M I use a series 1:1 current choke followed by a variable capacitor (40 - 200 pf - which is what Ten Tec uses on their antenna tuner)  to tune it to a resonant frequency of 1.821 mhz, and with the KAT-500 in the shack I still have a good 30 kHz bandwidth that its useful in - and can go 500 watts no problem. I have a picture of what the antenna "curve" is posted on QRZ.COM. Its steep - as you would expect for such a short antenna. 

I think the ON4UN Lowband Antenna book says its maybe 25 - 30% efficient. Not sure how much better I made it though - I just don't have any serious test equipment . . . 

Life on a small suburban lot . . . . 

My only goal is 160M DXCC - for my 9th DXCC band. I am half way there is about 3 years with such short and inefficient antennas - hi hi.

On Oct 28, 2013, at 2:37 PM, Rich Holoch <rholoch@comcast.net> wrote:

Its funny - last year I had just the MA160V - which is 34 - 36' tall, and top loaded and hatted. When it was up by itself, the K9AY loops always seemed to win. 

Now that it is on top of the 30' Rohn telescoping mast - it seems that the 60 (+) feet of capture area makes a noticeable difference. 

I have had no way to do any serious A - B tests, but capture area seems to be key. 

On my pan adapter, the noise characteristic looked the same on both antennas, but it seems to me that the extra capture area - and the fact that there is at least 30' high and above everything in the clear - that it provides that much more signal to pop above the noise. 

The noise this Fall is stronger than ever - higher than past years. Its frying pan noise, constant, and as soon as I tune past 1.830 kHz, it drops way, way down - almost to nothing. Very bizarre.

Luckily, the K3's NB and even NR have saved my 160M DX-ing this Fall.

On Oct 28, 2013, at 2:00 PM, Kevin Rowett <kevin@rowett.org> wrote:

On Oct 28, 2013, at 1:30 PM, Rich Holoch <rholoch@comcast.net> wrote:

This morning I worked E51NOU - South Cooks for a new one on 160M. At first I couldn't hear him at all on my K9AY Loops, but when I switched to my transmitting antenna - which is a Cushcraft MA-160V mounted on top of a 30' Rohn pushup mast - (meaning its about 60' tall) and with 76 buried radials (mostly 50' long), I heard him loud and clear. Not sure why - the K9AY loops are almost always better . . .

Receive loops provide less signal performance. They win by reducing the amount of noise.  In effect, they improve the S/N ratio, by reducing the noise received injected into your receiver.  They don't hear better, per capture, or apecture area of the antenna.

Different designs reduce the noise in different ways. It's usually by making the antenna sharply directional, without having to have a full yagi implementation.

Your vertical is a much better antenna than your receive antenna, from the description you gave.  If the noise is low enough, you'll be bale to hear him well enough to work him.

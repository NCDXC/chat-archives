On Friday, April 25, 2014, Paul Ewing <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:
Chris, thank you for sharing the fruits of your research with us.
 
It is interesting to read that the FCC did not seem to quite share the concerns of the ARRL:
 
The FCC noted that the ARRL supports its efforts to prevent club stations from obtaining an unfair share of desirable call signs, but expresses concern that the specific rule language adopted by the Commission “does not preclude the abuses that the Report and Order intended to preclude.” In its Petition, the ARRL stated that if a club has multiple station trustees, each of these trustees could obtain a vanity call sign for the club, thereby allowing the club to obtain multiple vanity call signs. The ARRL also argued that a club could “‘gam[e]’ the club station vanity call sign system” by obtaining multiple FCC Registration Numbers (FRNs) and applying for a vanity call sign under each FRN. “We do not believe that the vanity call sign system is subject to the abuses identified by the ARRL,” the FCC said, “or that its suggested rule changes are necessary.”
 
Given my own findings and the number of vanity club calls that are being controlled by Trustees who are the trustees for multiple clubs and control multiple club calls, the concerns of the ARRL appear to be completely valid.
 
The question remains, were these club calls obtained properly before the restrictions were put in place, or have some figured out how to “game” the system and have obtained multiple club calls as trustees for multiple clubs which is now prohibited. I think the questions also begs to be answered, are some of these clubs on paper only or are they valid clubs.
 
As Ted pointed out, you can easily copy and edit the sample material from the ARRL and within 15 minutes you can meet the requirements for your own club. How convenient is that?
 
Jim-K9YC-you asked me why I don’t have a 1X2 or 2X1 call. When I upgraded to Extra Class, I found no available calls then just as I find now. I didn’t have the time or patience to stick with getting an Extra call and gave up on it. You suspect I don’t understand contesting because I am not a contester. You don’t have to be a true blue contester to understand it. I have always known that some contesters would stretch the boundaries for their own needs. I’ve visited many mega contest stations and I know you don’t need Amps with twin 8877’s in them to run at legal power limits. Some guys will stretch or skirt the rules. Its human nature for some of us.
 
The bottom line is that a bunch of Contesters have created a bunch of clubs and have sucked up a bunch of desirable calls for their infrequent use. Have they followed the rules?   I'm sure some have and some, well, lets just say that they don’t pass the smell test.
 
The net result is that its fundamentally unfair to new Extra Class licensees that they cannot obtain a 1X2 or 2X1 call because others have worked within or around the existing system to hoard them for themselves. This hoarding of 1X2 and 2X1 call signs stinks!

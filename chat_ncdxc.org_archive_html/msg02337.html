Exactly. YOUR opinion, clearly in opposition to the majority of the club.

> [snip]

> I've given many points in contests but I have yet to submit a contest log.
>

That's not something to be proud of. I know that you meant to say that you don't care about winning contests, but submitting a log allows the log-checking process to be more thorough and valid. Submit the log as a check-log, if you don't care about your personal result.

> I would have been so proud to see our award go home with K4UEE the Leader of HK0NA or K3LP the Leader of 7O6T who both sat there and watched us award PT0S. Even ZL9HR and NH8S were more deserving. We got it wrong in 2013 and I feel embarrassed for the club.
>

Again, YOUR opinion. If you don't think the club members are qualified to vote for the award, then change it from a majority decision to a selection by a qualified panel of DXers/DXpeditioners. To say "we got it wrong" is simply saying that YOU don't agree with the rest of the club. I respect that, but it doesn't make the decision *wrong*; the decision is what the majority voted for. If that's not good enough for you, then change the selection process.

I, for one, don't give a rat's patootie which was the "best" DXpedition. I'm perfectly happy letting YOU make the decision for the club.

> This discussion is not about what I want.

Yes, it is, or you wouldn't be arguing against the club's choice.

> It is about the NCDXC and our image as seen externally to the club. I think we look goofy when we give an award to a DXpedition that had some poor operating by part of the team.

Again, in YOUR opinion. The club members apparently didn't agree with you. That's why this discussion is about YOU, not the club. The only reason the decision looks "goofy" to you is because YOU disagree with it. It didn't seem goofy to the people who voted for it.

> I'm not afraid to say that. We can learn from this and be better.

Where "better" is defined as "an outcome that I agree with"?

> Only if we cannot get consensus and improve our process should we eliminate this award.

How do you know if the process has improved? Is it if YOU agree with the outcome?

If we are going to present this award, we either leave it to a club/majority decision, or we decide that the general membership lacks the ability to make an informed choice and we assign the job to an elite panel, or a panel of one. I personally don't care which path is chosen, including the path of eliminating the award.

>
> As for DXpeditions, they will happen whether you award them or not. Our award should be prestigious and well earned. SWODXA usually gets it right in their annual Hamvention presentation.

How do you know that they "got it right"? Is it because you agree with their decision?

The fact is, there are no objective criteria for making the decision, so other than by comparison to one's personal choice, there is no way to say which outcomes are "right" and which are "goofy".


> [snip]

>
> Lets revise and improve how we give this annual reward. Lets make it more meaningful and sought after and the result will be an annual award that will be prestigious and that we can all be proud to bestow on the best DXpedition of each year.
>

I'd still like to know what would constitute an "improvement". How do you know if you have gotten better, if you have no metrics or criteria?

Anna and Rich

My XYL, Laine, K6XXN, and I will listen for you both! I know you are going
to love it. FWIW, the first time I operated from Antigua I was anxious to
learn and if possible adopt the local dialect and I was greeted with big
smiles and people coming up to me and saying "wan bot saw". I smiled and
waved back and said "wan bot saw" back and people laughed and waved. After
three days of this and many of the locals going out of their way to greet me
with "won bot saw" followed with friendly laughter and smiles a local ham
came up to me and said "Ted are you really trying to rent a boat to local
Antiguans?" I was surprised and asked him why he thought I wanted to rent a
boat to somebody in Antigua and he responded "Because for three days you
have been saying "Want a boat sir?" to everyone and it was pretty funny for
the first two days....people would go out of their way to hear you say "won
bot saw" and waive and smile to them!  <smile>. I also learned that back
then all the locals I met pronounced "Antigua" as "on tee ga".

So "won bot saw" and enjoy "on tee ga!!!!!  73, Ted, ex V2A/K6XN

PS I love Antigua...great location for DXing and contesting......and all the
people I met were friendly and had both a great sense of humor and were
blessed with the gift of laughter...watch out for the "rum punch".........

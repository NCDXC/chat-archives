Paul:

You've asked the NCDXC membership to help understand why 7O6T was overlooked by other clubs -- such as the Clipperton DX Club, DX World and SWODXA -- for DXpedition of the Year.  That would be impossible to answer, because we, as a club, have no idea why those other clubs voted the way they did.  Your question is better directed to members of those other clubs rather than the NCDXC chat reflector.

Having said so, my strong feeling -- and I've mentioned this before -- is that people vote for their "favorite" for many different reasons:

Could be any of the following:

- the DXpedition was the only one on the list which resulted in a "new entity" for them
- the DXpedition was the only one on the list where they scored one or more new band-mode QSOs (e.g., 160M CW).
- they were able to crack the pileup:  the DXpedition was the only DXpedition they could work with their modest 100 watt station and wire antenna
- they liked the team's on-air operating technique best (e.g, handling pileups, split operation, looking for QRP stations, etc.)
- they knew one or more DXpedition team members personally
- they were themselves a member of the DXpedition they voted for
- they admired previous DXpeditions -- and became early Fans of --  the Team Leaders (e.g, K4UEE, K9CT, K0IR, AA7JV, etc.)
- the DXpedition team operators themselves took on a big Personal risk (e.g. battling Mother Nature on a slippery rock, extreme heat conditions, etc.)
- the DXpedition operators themselves took on a big Financial risk (e.g, spending $5,000 to $20,000 of their own money + travel expenses)
- the DXpedition used ClubLog efficiently to update their logs every day, minimizing dupes
- the DXpedition uploaded their logs to LOTW immediately after the DXpedition ended resulting in quick, satisfying confirmations
- the DXpedition had a good Band Plan including watching out for General Class licensees
- the DXpedition looked for the West Coast frequently
- the DXpedition operated all modes:  CW, SSB, RTTY
- they voted for the DXpedition that seemed to get the most publicity, and therefore assumed was probably the most "deserving".
- no specific reason -- totally random choice

It is for all of these reasons, that selecting a "DXpedition of the Year" is difficult, controversial, and potentially demoralizing.  

I was on the NH8S Swain's Island team where we received the "DXpedition of the Year" award you mentioned this past Friday night from SWODXA in Dayton.  Did I feel special?  Honestly no.  You and other members of the 7O6T team could just as easily been standing up on the stage with me.

That's why veterans like N7NG, W0YK, NI6T, and W6OAT -- among others -- recommend not having a DXpedition of the Year award at all.  Personally, I'm in that same camp.  

NCDXC should vote with their dollars to help support DXpeditions and not worry about giving out a plaque based on subjective data.  

DXpeditions should be about QSOs and Confirmations, not about bragging rights.  

73,
John, K6MM

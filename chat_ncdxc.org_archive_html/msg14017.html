Here’s what I sent to Bernie for the Daily DX, since updated re. FT8.  I’ll just announce FT8 on CW, not go to the hotel lobby for the internet:

 

A52SJ  “Gus Browning Memorial DXpedition”, October 12-19 & 26, 2017  

The split schedule is due to an initial week in Paro, then a second week on a trek, then back in Paro for one night before departure.

 

In 1965 as a teenager in Sacramento I worked Gus Browning at AC2H.  I was fascinated with the exotic remoteness of Bhutan and dreamed of going there one day.  Now I’m fortunate enough to have the chance.  My goal is to work as many North America West Coast stations as possible on 160 Meters.  Many DXpeditions to Bhutan have been relatively easy to work from West Coast on HF bands like 20/17/15 Meters.  But few recent expeditions have made any effort to work West Coast on 160M.  Bhutan is #3 on ClubLog’s most wanted for 160M in NA West Coast.

 

I’ll be operating in Paro running 500 watts with a K3 and KPA 500.  Antennas will be an Inverted L with two elevated radials for TX, a 1-wavelength Beverage for RX.  The antennas will be on a north facing open slope looking toward NA West Coast.

The best times will be around 1300Z to 1400Z, the hour preceding local sunrise in San Francisco.  I have been working Asia and the Pacific on 160M with these antennas here in California and the best time has been before local sunrise.

I will start operating at 1230Z, an hour after local sunset in Paro, and listen for only NA West Coast until 1400Z.  

After 1400Z I will listen below 1825KHz for JA, VK, ZL, VU, UA, etc.

 

I plan to upload to ClubLog daily if the hotel wifi is working.  Unfortunately I’ll need to carry the laptop from the cottage to the hotel lobby for wifi so I won’t be online “live” during my operating times.

 

CW frequency: 1826.5 KHz TX, Up 3-5 RX.  This might vary depending on other local activity.

 

FT8 frequency:  1840.0 KHz.   I’ll go to FT8 only if 160M conditions are too marginal for CW.  In that case, I’ll go to the hotel lobby and spot myself on DX Summit as going to FT8.

 

Vy 73,

Steve

N6SJ

On 1/24/2013 8:47 PM, paul@n6pse.com wrote:
My question is: What is the best way to capture Youtube audio (not video) and capture it as a audio file on my PC?
 
Should I connect a digital hand-held digital stereo recorder to speaker out on my sound card?  Or should I use some form of capture/mixing software?    I would prefer to make an MP3 file of each videos audio.

As the resident audio guy, I should have an easy, inside the computer answer, but I don't, because I'm not that much of a computer guy. :) I'd bet that if you search long enough, you'll find software to drag audio (and video) from youtube to a computer file.

An outboard recorder, or even the sound input of another computer should work just fine if you set levels right -- that is, not too low, not too high, just Goldilocks. :)  One thing I do NOT recommend is doing the intermediate step as an MP3. MP3 is LOSSY compression, and every time we play and record MP3 we throw away a bit of stuff and the MP3 player guesses at what the MP3 recorder threw away. The more complex the audio, and the greater the level of compression, the more is thrown away. 

You'll probably make the final audio recording an MP3 (although it wouldn't hurt to leave it as a .WAV except for disk space), and you don't lose anything when you COPY an MP3 without converting it to audio so you can listen to it, edit it, or combine it with other stuff.

On Fri, Sep 12, 2025 at 11:19 AM Ryan Huggins via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
There are always risks when upgrading.  Depending on the age of your system it may run Win11, but it may not be as fast as 10 or on a computer designed for 11.

Win11 updates have caused issues with the drivers used for various radios.  The fix has been to uninstall the USB Audio device and to reinstall it.  This has happened twice that I can recall.

Personally, I have no intention of upgrading to 11.  My daily driver and my radio machine are Win 10.  I'm careful about where I go online, I have updated virus protection, I have a variety of network level protections and a stronger than average firewall.  Sensitive data is encrypted.

My radio machine only pulls from a handful of logging sites and is even more isolated from the internet (also running Win 10).  It's old enough that it is not capable of upgrading.  I have no intention of upgrading it anytime soon, so long as the fans keep working.  It runs EVERYTHING I need very efficiently.

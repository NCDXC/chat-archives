Hi Paul,

I'm running an ADSB tracker on a Raspberry Pi 3b+.  I'm using the ADSB Exchange image and SD card that comes with the ADSB exchange "blue stick."  I'm using the ADSB Exchange 1090 blue stick and the ADSB Exchange 978mhz orange stick.  The 978 tracks UAT which is in use by your regional commuter/private airports, of which I have several in my area as well as ground based units (if memory serves).  Both of these feed into a Nooelec SAWbird amp which is fed by a Signalplus 12dbi antenna which is mounted on a PVC pipe up above my roofline.  The computer lives in my attic and has a 30ft run of KMR400 connecting it to the antenna.  From north of Malibu, I can see planes in flight as they get ready to cross into the US from Mexico.  Sometimes I get brief reception of the planes on the ground and people/luggage movers at LAX and aircraft low in Long Beach and surrounding areas.  I haven't figured that one out yet.  It's infrequent, random, and only lasts a minute or two.

I have another Pi that I have the beta version of ADSB's software on that supports GPS receivers and will automatically update your feeder's location based on the GPS signal.  I use this sometimes when I travel for fun.  I run both the blue and the orange stick on this setup as well, and both are fed by a Stratux 1090/978 splitter the size of a dime and are connected to either the basic 1090mhz whip antenna that cames with blue stick, or a general purpose "rabbit ear" style dipole antenna that came with one of those general purpose SDR kits... which works surprisingly well.

I'm happy to answer any questions about my setup or your setup/potential setup.  I've done a fair amount of testing with various antennas in the house, in the attic, and at various heights outside.

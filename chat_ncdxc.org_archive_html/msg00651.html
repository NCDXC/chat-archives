On Dec 10, 2012, at 8:07 AM, <paul@n6pse.com> <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:

> Here is your NCDXC question of the week:
>  
> How was your experience working the ZL9HR Campbell Island DXpedition?
>  

Unfortunately, their schedule was exactly the inverse of ours here at the Monkey Palace. When they went QRV, we were without power from the first storm system that came through. Right after that storm, we went down to our vacation place in the California Desert. Although I had my DXpedition station with me, an IC-7000 and a Buddipole in a small backyard simply did not cut it. I could not even hear them on any band, except once, when they were ESP-level on 17m CW.

We returned to the Bay Area on Friday afternoon, and I hoped that there would be enough time to work them before they went QRT sometime Saturday. We got here around 5 pm, and I immediately turned on the radio, as the grayline was quickly turning to black here. Nothing heard, and a couple of cluster spots indicated that they were now QRT and packing up. Oh, well.

Just for kicks, I left the radio and amp on and checked back after dinner. Around 8 pm local Saturday, they were back, whisper-quiet on 17m SSB, listening up 5 and not getting a lot of calls. Both Anna and I managed to get through with one call apiece (both solid QSOs, no ESP), so they are in the log as ATNOs for both of us. Whew!

Rich KE1B

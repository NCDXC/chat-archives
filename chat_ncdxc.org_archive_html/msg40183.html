It's not a DXCC list problem, it's the HR rules that need adjustment.
Of the two (Inactive) proposals that I've seen:

No one is suggesting that entities be removed or deleted from the DXCC list.

The proposals suggest making the entity "inactive" for HR purposes.
In other words, recognize that a number of entities are unavailable for ham radio purposes.
We may disagree with the owning country's decisions, but it is what it is.

Therefore, for HR measurement purposes, remove that entity from the HR list and recalculate HR.
Leave it on the DXCC list.

If (when) the entity once again  becomes available for ham radio purposes, it goes back on the HR list and those that don't have it confirmed need to work it. There are some underlying considerations to work out, but the concept is intended to address situations that are beyond the ARRL's ability to resolve.

On 8/4/2025 1:53 PM, Dave AA6YQ via Chat wrote:
Regarding the DXCC list, since the inception of the DXCC Awards Program, there has been no guarantee that anyone pursuing this award would be able to achieve Honor Roll, no matter how strong their DXing knowledge, radio skills, and station capabilities. Those who pursued DXCC Honor Roll did so with that understanding. In his West Coast DX Bulletin editorials, Cass WA6AUD referred to them as "true blue DXers" - the distinguishing mark of dedication among serious DXers. His red-faced QRPer steamed up the hill more than once to berate the old-timer on the topic of unworkable entities.

There's an alternative to removing DXCC entities only because they aren't currently available - as 7O, BY, YK, XY, and ZA once were: extend the DXCC Award family with a new "Worked All Available DXCC Entities"  award. I have suggested this to the DXAC.

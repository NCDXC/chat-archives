On Dec 16, 2014, at 11:20 AM, Paul N6PSE via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

I just got done completing the DX-World, NCDXC sponsored DXpedition of the Year-online survey.
 

Odd. I just went to the site and it says that the survey will be up later today. It was not available (at least not to us peons).

I am not an expert in survey methodology. The method in use this year struck me as odd.
 
There is a list of about 15 DXpeditions (I didn’t count them) and you chose five of your favorites.
 

I assume that our fine-dining trip to Anguilla wasn’t on the list. Darn. 

Included in the list were the big grand DXpeditions such as FT5ZM and FT4TA. Also included were a number
of one man shows such as C21CG, UA4WHX and E30FB.
 
Seems like this is much like “ranked choice voting” which gave the City of Oakland their second choice as mayor.
 

It has been proven that, when there are three or more choices in an election, NO VOTING SYSTEM will produce a ranking that is consistent across the community’s values. (See “Arrow’s Impossibility Theorem”, http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arrow's_impossibility_theorem). 

Ultimately, it comes down to what your criteria are for determining the “best” result. Is it just the number of people who ranked it as Number One? That would eliminate a candidate that EVERYONE thought was second-best, while a slim plurality chose another candidate as number one. There are a zillion other boundary conditions; you get the idea.

Any survey experts in the club that think this is a good selection process?  I guess I am “old school” but I
like the approach of pick the best from a set of given criteria. I am impressed by skill, stats, complexity and rarity.
 

You can chose your candidate based on whatever criteria you like, just like in a political election.

I chose to vote for only one instead of the five votes that I was allotted.
 

Then you greatly reduce the significance of your vote, under the rules of this particular game. That’s your choice.

Great points! There’s a huge difference between a legacy rig (such as my ten year old Kenwood) and a modern SDR radio like the K4 or Flex. On a legacy rig the passband may still be constrained by the roofing filters, but the K4 will listen beautifully all the way down to zero Hz. 
We oldsters can fake it by tuning 500 KHz below the lower limit of the FT8 sub-band, and with multi streams starting down at 200 Hz, that is almost a necessity. 
Looking at the email chain today, I’m struck by the beauty of the original FT8 design. The program brings you down into a less-QRMed part of the passband, and if you’re still not decoded because another station is on their second try to be heard by DX, you’re shifted to yet another potentially free slot. All thanks to your own WSJT software, and not that of the DX. 
MSHV does circumvent many of F/H’s thoughtful advantages, in return for more spectrum for calling. It also gives us Hounds a chance to call endlessly without being leashed in (hi) by a limited number of unreturned calls. As far as ZD9W goes, F/H worked far better with dramatically improved throughput. 
Good luck to all as we enjoy an unprecedented number of great DXpeditions.

Most rigs have a better response in the middle of the passband, so that’s the default place to call, although that’s where everyone else is too! Many rigs roll off below 2-300Hz and above 2700, so those are potentially risky places to call unless the DX has a modern rig like a Flex. 

Bruce,

This is exactly where the 'shift' (centering control of the audio bandwidth) comes into play.  Center that ON the (weak or on the roll off edge) tones you WANT to hear for a slight boost (which is fed to the app or ear depending on mode). 

[Note to DXped: PLEASE stay above 400 Hz to avoid the roll off zone.]

You can also narrow the audio bandwidth at times (BW on the K4) for (MAYBE) better reception BUT that 'adjusts' your app perceived s/n report upward when you send it.  You hear (decode) better (maybe) but then tell the other party you hear much better than reality.  If you're scratching at the noise floor and report +23, it doesn't matter.

Exactly like NOBODY cares if '59' is not real (after 18 repeats), it's the exchange and confirmation of the exchange that counts.  So let the app lie.  It still counts.

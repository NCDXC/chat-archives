By far, the most important thing I did was add a Rohn 30' telescoping mast at the base of the MA160V. This means I ended up with a 1/4 wl 80M vert without having to add a coil for 80M.

With it telescoped down - and with a hinged base that I made, I can telescope the 63' vert down by myself and then lean it against a 6' step ladder and work on it at ground level.

My 40M antenna is a seperate phased array using two 24' hatted verts and Christman feed. Worked V51B on LP last week - 13K miles with 500 watts.

The 63' vert has a parasitic reflector on 30M - its my yagi tower!

So, things are quite good for me here.

The tall vertical is resonant on 160, 60, 20 and 15. It works very well on 30 and 17M using the KAT-500 in the shack.

The 40M array and 20M 3el full sized yagi are monobanders of course, and the tall vert was designed for 160/80, with benefits elsewhere.

I do all my antenna work, and easily by myself.

Drill press and pop riveter are always busy here as is EZNec and HFTA.

I'm glad to see you run these experiments.  Nice job.  Please keep the results coming.

In the quest of adding 80m and 160m capabilities I also experimented with a Cushcraft MA160V and MA8040V.  I don't need 40m on the vertical since I already have a 40m beam.  I added a 80m coil and cap. hats to the stock MA160V.  The end result is an antenna that is both resonant on 80m and 160m without using any remote matching device.  On 160m it works just like a stock MA160.  On 80m the performance would be on par with the stock MA8040, which is ok with me.  On 80m the RF stops at just 28'.  On 160m the RF stops at 37'.  On-the-air tests have shown my 80m signal is about two S units down from a HyGain Hy-Tower.  I still have some extra tubing left and I'd love to extend the antenna between the base and the coils.  It seems like your antenna is working very well due to the extra height.  However at 37' my current antenna is already too big to handle easily.  So I'm curios to know what you are using to lower/raise the antenna with.  I'm thinking of getting one of those heavy duty DXE vertical raising fixture, the one with a winch.  This will allow me to experiment without the risk of damaging it.

I read your writeup on using the vertical on 30m.  I have never tried it and to my delight my tuner did tune the antenna on 30m.  I tried running some power and everything seemed fine.  I will play around on 30m tonight.

From my experiments I also learned about corona discharge at high power levels.  When running high powers I got a high SWR due to corona discharge coming off the ends of the capacity hats.  I never saw the discharge in person however it's the only explanation as SWR doesn't change based on power unless due to arcing or corona discharge.  I purchased some brass balls and I made them fit on the ends of the hat rods.  Problem solved!

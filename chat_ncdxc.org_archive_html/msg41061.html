As a retired physician, I became aware of a new cancer early detection study enrolling at Sutter/PAMF in Palo Alto. The REACH study is now enlisting 50,000 Medicare beneficiaries in an effort to demonstrate the clinical benefits of the Galleri blood-based multi-cancer early detection test. Using a single blood-draw, the test screens for DNA fragments associated with more than 50 common cancers. The test has been in use by prescription now since 2021, is not covered by insurance, and costs $950 per test. The test is 99.5% specific, meaning it is accurate in identifying people without cancer signals, but only a bit above 50% sensitive, meaning it may not identify all of those with early cancers.  It is 90% accurate at identifying the organ system affected by the cancer. Think of it as a CW skimmer that misses about half of the important DX but only rarely directs you to a spot that doesn’t exist.

 

The company that developed the test, GRAIL, is using the Sutter clinical research organization to launch this study to prove the clinical advantages of their test beyond the usual cancer screening (such as colonoscopy and PSA screening). Medicare beneficiaries over the age of 50, with no cancer diagnosis within the last three years are eligible. Enrollees will receive up to three free tests and participate in the study for up to three years.

 

I am passing this on not in my former professional capacity, but because this test saved a friend’s life, who had a potentially fatal but asymptomatic neck tumor. After treatment, he’s doing well. My wife and I volunteered as study subjects today. Please look over the attached PDF and discuss this with your personal physician to decide if you should consider participation. If you are interested, please contact Mr. Sudeep Adithem, the study coordinator, at (650) 853-6854.

I would like to share my humble experience as a new Ham (two year ticket).

Chose to become a Ham in the worst propagation down cycle. If it weren’t for FT8 I think I would be fishing in an empty lake right now. It would be more fun.

FT8 brought me excitement. I could work WAS in a month. The excitement of seeing your QSO complete (even though you didn’t diddle anything or copy a faint voice that you guessed is your call sign) makes it worth it. It is a technological marvel.

There are tricks that you learn on how to attack (in the good sense) a target, look for an empty (on your side) spot or slide around looking for an empty (on their side) spot so QRM won’t stop you. Propagation, what times you might find what. Work 6m when there’s no CW anywhere.

 

I spent a year learning CW from scratch (yes, not even an “e” I knew how to send) at CWA and got my Membership in February CWOP#2450.  I like it a lot, it’s very challenging and makes for a “cult” as we all have a code only us understand.

But believe me, when a DXPedition came along and I found out that all I had to do is look for a empty spot up around 1kHz, program my keyer on the rig with my call sign and punch the screen 78 times in an hour (because who in their right mind would paddle that for an hour, right?), until miraculously you faintly hear you call sign back, then punch the other button that sends 599 TU (even though it wasn’t even close to 599, which by the way, FT8 won’t lie to you). That was awesome!!! Yeah, right.

 

I don’t regret learning CW but it was a steep curve for me (notice I graduated at 30wpm) and I believe I am a quick learner. Imagine for most of the youngster today. My Son David (15) got intrigued and he’s going on the second level of CWA. However he has no interest whatsoever on getting a call sign or operate. Videogames is his thing.

 

When propagation comes back (I never experienced it yet) surely I’ll move to SSB and talk to Bangladesh instead of waiting for my CPU to decode it. In the meantime all the excitement is in seeing how my little Pea Shooter Station can communicate with a modest station in India in the lowest propagation cycle in a long time.

 

Sorry,,,, I really feel FT8 is great, but I cannot share it publicly because of obvious reasons.

 

Regards,

Roberto: K6KM

I am sure when SC25 comes and we finally see SFI breach 100 heading towards 200 then we can get back on non digi modes again in earnest. I worked a bunch of new ones on 6m and now up to 53 DXCC worked and 48 confirmed. I can’t say it is as much fun working them but it is definitely a lot easier than working them standard modes. 

 

I am really looking forward to the sun showing us that we have a solar cycle actually moving like what was in the latest DXer.  I am sure when we can hear Abdallah on 20m at S9+40 again in the evenings we will be back having fun using SSB and CW again.

 

When I was at Belton hamfest the expert amp guys say FT8 has had a big impact in keeping their sales going. If it keeps these cottage industry boys going during the solar minimum I am cool with it.  For me I don’t get as jazzed about making a new 6m DX band entity on FT8 versus CW or SSB but in these super lame conditions it is what I have seen in the past month plus. I have not made one new 6m band entity during the normal season in standard modes because EU DX here has gotten no better than -11 for me in STX.  

 

Then again I haven’t been on much as I would like with so much crazy work. I am super slammed!!!  Look forward to my next trip out to San Jose to see my clients and many of you in person in 2021.

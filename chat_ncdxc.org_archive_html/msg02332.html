I agree with Bob and the comments of others.  In selecting a “best DXpedition” of the year, all of the others are relegated to “not as good”.  In addition everyone seems to have a different idea of what is best.  It is hard to vote for an expedition that you could hear on your antenna (I never heard 7O6T which was not their fault).

 

If there were written requirements for the award, a committee could probably do a better job than a general vote in picking the “best” expedition.  The Academy Awards are not given based on a vote of all movie goers.

 

I think that it would be better to give donations to the good expeditions and it doesn’t all have to all go to one.  Donations help to get more DXpeditions, plaques do not.

 

I also think that there were too many awards at the Saturday night banquet which resulted in the main speaker having to cut his presentation short.  The total program is way too big considering that the prize drawings also take a long time.

 

Maybe the NCDXC should give some of the awards such as the Club member of the year and the DXer of the year at the Holiday Luncheon?

 

The bottom line is that there probably is not a “best DXpedition of the year” as many are deserving, so I also say, drop the award as it appears to only generate controversy.

 

73,

- Craig, AE6RR

Paul et.al.,

 

I’m beginning to think the DXpedition of The Year award by whatever club or entity, is a bad idea.  Why?

 

First:

We NEED these DXpeditions!  The DXpeditions may become an endangered species consider the cost and local regulation problems.  We do not want to aggravate the situation by not appreciating those teams and their members.  It is tough enough to organize a team, get funding let alone the time away from work, home etc.  They ALL need our support, money AND appreciation.  To single one out over all others is counter-productive.

 

Second:

The DXpedition of the Year is counter-productive because it can’t support all the teams.  Clearly it discourages those teams not wining.  This award is going to select one forsaking all others.  The others are going to feel not-appreciated.  We DON’T need that.  They DON’T deserve that.  We need to support them.

 

I believe the DXpedition of the Year award is counter-productive and should be discontinued.

 

73,

 

Bob – W6OPO

Steve, I haven't said until now, who I thought the best DXpedition was.

 

While I hoped 7O6T would win something, I think HK0NA was the best DXpedition of 2012 for a number of reasons. I would have been proud to lose to this DXpedition.  

 

Our members voted for PT0S, a DXpedition where several operators went out of their way to have our members QRX so they could work their PY friends at home. This was after taking our donation money. This was shameful.

 

It was even reported to me this weekend at Dayton that several PT0S Operators were telling their friends at home in Portuguese where they were listening. That kind of operating is not worthy of our award.

 

Many aspects of this hobby involve respect. Why do we do the annual last man standing exercise at various DX gatherings?  Because we heap respect on those that have worked them all and have attained the respect of their peers. Why do guys seek CQ Marathon rankings or DXCC challenge awards?  It's because of respect that is garnered when one achieves something after a lot of effort.

 

Those in the know, the guys who really get it, tell me that it is just inconceivable that a major DXpedition to a #5 most wanted entity, making 162,000 contacts did not win a single award anywhere.

 

The 7O6T team had hoped to earn the respect of the DX Community but failed. Or did the DX Community fail 7O6T?

 

I've always had passion for working the DXpedition shifts so that I could work the West Coast. It is usually at 3 or 4AM while in Africa or the Middle East. It takes real drive and determination to operate all day and then be in the chair to give the West Coast their contacts.

 

I hope I can sustain that passion, but right now the 7O6T team feels that our efforts weren't appreciated and we want to understand why. I'm not speaking for the team but writing on my own to figure this out.

 

Paul N6PSE

I for one voted for HK0NA with 7O6T a close second.

Why?

I wasn't present during the political negotiations to activate either entity. Both took a lot of work in this area, so I gave it a tie.

There were other well run DXpeditions, but these were the best of them.

I purchased a FT1000 no suffix and also purchased the optional
Bandpass filter and a bunch of other options.  I don't believe
the Bandpass filter was standard equipment on the FT1000D, but
in any event, my FT1000 (after my upgrades) was identical to
an FT1000D except for the digital voice keyer.  I could have
installed that and made it a full FT1000D.  It is also possible
for someone to have purchased an FT1000D and removed some of
these options.

Rick N6RK

On 8/20/2017 1:05 PM, Bob Lanning via Chat wrote:
They all say FT-1000.  Yaesu never put a “D” designator on the radio. The only way I know you can tell is to see if the External Bandpass module with its PL-259 and rotary switch is plugged into the rear panel on the right side of the radio.  The Sub receiver is right next to it inside the cabinet.
Internally I believe the “D” model came with 7 of the 8 possible selectivity filters.  The only options were for a 2^nd 250Hz filter and the more stable TCXO-1.

Satellites are under 250 miles from the transmitting station, the moon is a bit further. 

The test is where the transmitter is located, not the means of propagation employed.  IRLP, repeaters (except satellites) or similar are disallowed.

I feel there should be a mileage constraint from 'home' to transmitter.  I'm one that never approved of the rule change that unlimited contacts made from greater than 150 miles from the 'home' station (the address listed on the license).

A mileage constraint means that someone not allowed (by local codes, space or other) to build a station at the place they reside can still operate and gain from it.

Being able to operate from different remote sites greater than 150 miles from 'home' to take advantage of propagation is fine for everyday contacts (who would care?), but should not be allowed for any contests or awards.  To allow it means that the playing field is more uneven (while it can never be completely level) and that those with dollars to pay for remote sites have a defined advantage.  It weakens that meaning of the award when that happens.

Consider that the current entity rule (contact must be made from within the same entity) allows contact from ANYWHERE in the US (6 time zones across, arctic and tropical areas), Russia (NINE time zones across, with arctic zones) while limits many Europeans to a much smaller geographic area (some of those countries are well, tiny).  It's not fair to Europeans.

The alternative is to create a specific 'class' of operations, or an asterisk on the award denoting that it wasn't done from home.

While it also means that the applicant could lie about how the contact was made, that is a morality issue best managed by that ham.

Some will argue that moving the residence may mean starting over again.  Yes, it does.  That is one of the costs of moving, for a ham and the way it used to be.  We still managed. In general terms, isn't the 'next' station usually better than the previous station?  ;o)

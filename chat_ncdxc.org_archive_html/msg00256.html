Steve and all,
You are exactly correct that the 1998 rules were intended to simplify the various processes, including those involved in creating new countrie or removing old ones. Clear, "bright line" criteria was our goal during committee discussions. However:
Are you all aware that the current rules are NOT the same that we presented in 1998? Are you aware of the rules change that was made by the Board in the summer of 2004?
Also, are you all aware of the addition of a new DXCC rule -- related to new countries -- in 2006? Are you aware of why and how it was added? Are you interested in why there is no current interest in making a similar change to accommodate Kosovo? There just might be more to the story that you know. 

I have not been moved enough to publish the "whole story," but...

This is a fine example of why the ARRL adopted its new DXCC rules.

Here is your NCDXC question of the week:

The topic this week concerns the recent Z60K and this weekend?s Z60WW
operations from Kosovo.
Kosovo does not currently meet the ARRL DXCC program?s criteria to be a
unique entity. Do you find it odd that some members of the amateur radio
community are operating from Kosovo with a set of call signs that were
essentially made up and have not been assigned by the ITU for use by Kosovo
or any other country? Do you find it odd that a leading representative of
the IARU is part of this activity?

My response: I am in favor of Kosovo gaining full & complete independence
and I hope that it soon meets the current criteria to join the DXCC program

these calls were essentially ?made up? and have not been formally assigned

appears to me to be flagrant rule breaking of ITU rules and procedures?  Am
I missing something here?
I realize this topic can be controversial and polarizing, let?s remember

On 1/19/2014 8:00 AM, Paul Ewing wrote:
I might add that there are also funding organizations that almost seem to search for reasons not to fund anyone but of their own nationality.
 
When we were fundraising for XZ1J, one major EU based funding organization told us that they could not donate anything because they did not agree with the stipulation that any excess money would go to the charity sponsoring and hosting the DXpedition-The Foundation for Global Children. So here you have a major EU based organization saying they won’t donate anything, because some of their money “might” go to benefit children. So very strange!
 
When we did ST0R, there were three major EU based funding organizations that refused to donate anything because they did not agree with our LoTW upload plan. We stated that we would do an upload immediately for any donors/OQRS users, however we would wait a year to upload to any non donors. They insisted on an immediate LoTW upload for everyone and would not support us. Experience has proven that donations cease when the LoTW upload takes place.
 
So when you are planning a DXpedition, you know that you can count on the West Coast and the major US based clubs and organizations but it’s a roll of the dice outside of that.

Rich, it just depends. Timing is very important.
 
ST0R was a new country and everyone needed it. Fundraising was hampered by the fact that two groups announced plans for activating it, but only one group showed up.
 
Funding organizations generally gave half of their normal amount telling us that they wanted to donate to both groups.
 
Timing impacted 7O6T as their was no time to do any fundraising before the DXpedition. The Team members put up the money required and fundraising was done after the trip.  While the West Coast always gives generously, let's just say that the enthusiasm to do so is not global.
 
The ideal situation is like the upcoming Tromelin DXpedition where it is very remote and very rare and they have ten months of lead time for fundraising.  I read this morning where the NCDXF is granting them $25,000 which is incredible.
 
Hopefully that will stimulate more activations of rare French held entities.
 
Paul N6PSE

Magnetic Receive Only Loop –

I had the Pixel loop you probably saw at IDXC the last several years.  I and a few others bought them ($400+) the first time the vendor showed up at IDXC so some of us have had some time the antenna.

 

The magnetic loop purpose is to reject noise – usually E-field local noise.  The result is the noise goes down but so do the signals.  However the noise goes down farther than the signals so the signal-to-noise ratio is increased – good. 

 

Bottom-line is it is less sensitive than other antennas but because it rejects noise so well it is considered a good noise rejecting antenna.  As long as you have a strong enough signal that can still be good with some loss of strength you will like the magnetic loop since it pushes the noise farther down.

 

Also it works best on the lower bands – 30, 40 and 80 meters – that is where the noise is the strongest.

 

BTW – these do need a rotator as there is a significant null on its broadsides.

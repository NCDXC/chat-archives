I found the thread from another group; the statement of leaving Oakland was NOT confirmed in any way...  So TBD.

However the move to (or adding) Sac is not awful.  Not only will it have easier access than Oakland (not just traffic concerns in that location but ease of routing busy city streets) but it also opens the door to places Oakland can't without an all day trip (like Redding, much of the foothills or overnight for me; it's just not enough reason to go to Portland, 8 hours driving time, the closest lol).

As I recall, the Oakland site also had some building issues (water leaks, structural damage), in addition to the usual 'issues' of being in a low rent district.  It's likely a cost:value choice, aggravated by high local taxing, online shopping under current COVID pressures.

So yes, interesting, for those still living in the area(s) and want a walk in store.

It was also noted that it's not REALLY Sac but 10 miles north (this week, considering the growth 😉) but is called Sac because that is a known reference point.  Boston was actually Salem once upon a time.

That's a benefit of watching FTx mode; it gives you a real time view of prop, at least in the direction that you're looking.  You don't even have to transmit.  What calls are scrolling by and whom are they calling?  I've added more than a few slots by noting who is being called, turning that way and making a contact.
VOACAP adds a singular data point.  Having said that, VOACAP (online or app) is often wrong, or early/late.  It's a SWAG (scientific wild arsed guess) based on variables that science and math don't account for quickly in real time (like CME or flares). So look and listen are still the best tools.  Spotting lists and hamalert alarms help too, over as wide an area as you choose (what's heard in MO on 80M has potential here).
Even with all of that, the magic of radio is true; more so when you least count on it.

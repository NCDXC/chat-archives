I am sure for “certain” DX-peditions – if we can’t rely on _some_ amount – which is essentially QSL + “per seat” + donations, grants, etc, then such places simply won’t be activated.

 

FT5ZM’s “thermometer” didn’t do what I expected it would – which is sort of give a “reality sandwich” to DX-ers and they would pony up in advance – almost like a “game” – would try to get the thermometer to hit the mark – like our town does when it looks for donations for the local fireworks fund drive – or school science and arts $$$ drive, etc. My town hits its mark every single year – its “community spirit” . . .

 

I have several models that CAN make this happen – but it is going to rely on some changes in the way we do these sort of things. Those expecting a free ride won’t be happy. But will they be happier if the DX-pedition is cancelled altogether? That is what it will come down to. (I remember your questions and Blog about “free” / bureau cards). You were right on. My models include a bunch of people bailing because it isn’t free. I exclude them from my financial models.

 

It’s very much like buying something on Amazon. You can have it tomorrow, 2 days, 3 days or longer . . . Overnight always costs the most. Apply some of the online buying patterns to a DX-pedition, and we might be on to something (hopefully).

 

It is very much possible to do the impossible . . .

I would very likely donate $100 upfront and then $20 when I submit my QSL request.

 

One thing I've learned is that hams have a big emotional block if they feel they are being "charged" for something that should in their mind be free.  Case in point, take a look at the Club Log bureau card survey results. 

 

I feel that we have truly crossed the tipping point and I think large mega DXpeditions to ultra rare islands may very well become much less common than they are now.

Hi Truman,

I'm in Mountain View...typical small lot. I have a Cushcraft R9 mounted on the roof... picture on my qrz.com page. no radials. I started with an R6, graduated to an R7 and then bought some parts from Cushcaft and turned it into an R9.

I have 8 band WAS and 5 Band DXCC with it (228 countries confirmed/ 230 worked). It's a better transmitter antenna than receive but it's all I can do here....not tall trees to run wires. The general rule here is if I can hear 'em, I can work 'em... but like any vertical, they have high noise.

Hello all, it's Truman N6TRU, and I had some questions for club members included in this email. There are three clubs that this is being emailed to, just for notice.

Anyways, I would greatly appreciate some guidance on a potential vertical antenna installation if possible. All right, here goes:

I'm considering setting up a vertical antenna. I like the Butternut HF9V or similar models, but I'm open to suggestions. I've attached pictures of the potential areas for an installation with estimated dimensions. The primary consideration with the Butternut unit is whether my yard can accommodate sufficient radials, or perhaps the antenna itself. If I go the Butternut route, my other consideration is whether a roof mount would be optimal or ground mount. Perhaps those knowledgeable in this could advise which would work better. I think a ground mount would be preferable though, provided the conditions.
Overall, I could use some elmering on this.

If you want to suggest different antennas, my preference would be an antenna that can work 80m, 40m, 20m, and 10m, but I could work with the lowest band being 40m rather than 80m. Radials are doable, but would need to fit within the proper space.

On the topic of power handling, it would be optimal to have legal-limit capability for versatility, but if that's not possible I could probably work with 500W limit or slightly above. (An amplifier I purchased is in the process of being repaired.)

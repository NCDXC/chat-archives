On 9/25/16 at 10:01 PM, chat@ncdxc.org (Charles K. Epps via Chat) wrote:
I received a bunch of bureau QSLs today and I'm still chuckling over the one from JA1ERB.  He is asking for a confirmation for a QSO we made back on November 23, 1968!  That's almost 48 years ago! But he IS in my log, I still had a few of my old K4BVD/6 QSLs left (that was my callsign back then), and I just finished filling out his reply QSL.  I sure hope he lives long enough to receive my card through the JA bureau because he's got to be really old by now.  :-) 
73 de Rusty, W6OAT
While this late QSL is a much shorter delay than yours, the West Valley Amateur Radio Association recently received QSL from W7WWL for a Field Day QSO made in 1996. I emailed him asking about it and Don said he had made a vow when he got his first license to QSL every new call. He was looking up a contact for a friend, and noticed there were no QSL notations on about 20 pages of his paper log. He duly started checking the calls and sending out QSLs to all the new ones. Our club field day station was one of the new ones. 

I'm glad to return a QSL in these circumstances.

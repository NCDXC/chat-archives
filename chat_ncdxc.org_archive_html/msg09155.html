Kevin-

 

I see K1N plans to have at least one station on 20M 24x7.  That should really help maximize the ATNOs, as almost all HF stations have at least some kind of antenna on 20M.

 

I’d suggest (depending on current propagation conditions in January 2016) focusing on delivering ATNOs on 15-17-20 and 40M for the first few days.  There are fewer people with effective low band antennas, and the 12M / 10M skip might not be great another year from now.

 

I would also suggest starting any RTTY operations only after several days of maximizing ATNO rates on SSB and CW.  K1N is on 20M with SSB and RTTY right now.  I’m pretty sure the ATNO rate would be higher if they were on 20M SSB and CW today.

 

Of course, these suggestions only apply if your goal is to hand out ATNOs.  I have seen DXp’s that announce they will focus on low band, or VHF or satellite operations instead of handing out ATNOs.

 

And shame is a good thing:  it’s how most mothers raise their kids!

I’ve a question/consideration for the general CHAT list..

 

Why are we anxious to get a lot of band/mode slots after our first one for K1N?

 

The point is - each time we grab an additional Band/Mode slot, we potentially take that slot away from someone that still needs an ATNO.

 

Wouldn’t it be a better overall practice to “hang back” till later in the trip to do band/mode fills?  Isn’t this one of the reasons that many DXPeditions no longer provide the leadership board from club log?

On 11/14/2013 2:19 PM, Craig - AE6RR wrote:
I am looking for a new antenna analyzer for field use (at the antenna) with a graphic display.
I know it's not what you're thinking of, but the VNWA 3e, designed by DG8SAQ, an EE prof, built and sold by a UK ham for $700 shipped to the US with calibration loads, is a real winner. With it, you can make a measurement in the shack, measure the line length with its TDR function, then transform the measurement to the antenna terminals. It's pretty easy to use in the field, because it powers from the same USB port that it uses to feed data to the computer. I reads and writes Touchstone files in S1P and S2P format (a plain text format that is the standard for interfacing).
I own an MFJ259B, which is plenty good enough for finding resonances both at the antenna and on the ground, and is great for tuning stubs. 

http://sdr-kits.net/VNWA3_Description.html
I also have an ancient AEA analyzer that is switchable for 50/75 ohms that I have set for 75 ohms.

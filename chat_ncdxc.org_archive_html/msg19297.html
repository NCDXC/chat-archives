Hi Al,

 

Yes I have tried this headset several years ago. 

 

They are cheap yes. 

They are poorly constructed.

They do a poor job of isolation.

Mic – I don’t remember the reports I got – too long ago.

 

I liked the cord’s flexibility and molded ¼” plug.

 

The biggest problem I had with them was the poor fit on my head.  That and the cushions are too stiff to conform to my head therefor provided very poor sound isolation.

 

I don’t understand how they can advertise in QST all these years.  I suppose some are happy with them or don’t bother with a return.

 

It was a money back deal so I got my money back.

 

Arlen’s Radio Sport are excellent but too heavy for me.  I have a Sennheiser HD 280 PRO I like very much.  I found them in Fry’s a few years ago for $90 or so.  The cord is coiled and the perfect length to cross my desk to the rig.  Also the 1/8th plug is threaded for a screw on ¼” adapter.  I find that important - it all comes out of the radio not leaving the adapter in the jack instead. 

 

It does not have a boom mic.  Maybe there is a model with a mic, I don’t know.  But the are light weight, cushions form fit me well providing nice sound isolation.

 

73,

Bob – W6OPO

Brian,

 

I too found those headsets too heavy.  Also didn’t like the stiff/hard cable it comes with.  These headsets and cords were originally designed for the heavy duty race track conditions.  Very good ambient noise isolation. A nice thing about the RadioSport models is the jack on the headset allowing the user to change cords.  One RS model I had a second jack was provided.  That was handy allowing two inputs.  If you wanted the 2-meter radio audio to be available use that jack.

 

I found after about 6 purchases the Sennheiser HD 290 pro 64Ω.  Light weight, very comfortable around the ear cushions.  Noise isolation not as good as the RadioSport but I don’t have the ambient noise here.

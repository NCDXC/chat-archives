I’m not 100% sure on this, but I suspect that the original info was sent in by SCDXC members K6FG, K6YUI, and N6DHZ, since they are all cited in Bernie’s Credits paragraph.  From his 11/18/24 Daily DX at 8:27 AM:

Southern California DX Club
___________________________________________________________________
After 77 years of existence, the SCDXC has begun its dissolution, with no board member nominees for VP or president.  An email was sent out to members.  The NCDXC offered to merge, to form what will  be called the California DX Club.  This will take about a year as they work out their 501-C-3 application and implement any changes. They note only 12 people show up for meetings and "almost all in [using] walkers,” and three on Zoom.  The law requires a board to maintain the corporate status.  "It has been a long time coming."  Said another way, "Last night (Thursday) the Southern California DX Club was dissolved.  Dissolution resulted from the declining membership and from the lack of members who are willing to serve in the major offices."  NCDXC is encouraging SCDXC members to join the "California DX Club.”

On 11/21/2024 1:25 PM, Jim Sansoterra via Chat wrote:
I promise silence after this screed. It seems to me two issues have become intertwined..."inclusion" and the whole "name change"
Most important first: inclusion. After 68 years as a ham I can point to one unchanging truth: no matter who you are, where you live or how you practice your life, once you have your ham license you are instantly included in the Great Fraternity. Age, gender. nationality? It matters not---you are in the Great Fraternity. Internal discord--of course --think AM vs. SSB.  But we never felt excluded for being different or in a different place. Frankly if I was a member of SOCAL I'd be insulted to hear that fellow hams were working to "include" me.  Many of the SOCAL folks were hams before some of us were even born. Our club should realize we are telegraphing  arrogance by implying SOCAL fellow hams need to feel "included"..they already are .  I know I'd not take it as endearing if I believed an effort was being made to "include" me in  the Great Fraternity, much less a club therein. This, to me, is not the tone to take if we want other hams to join us as their clubs gradually dissolve.` A simple  "please join us" is sufficient. "And second:
There is no reason to change our name or Bylaws.  How does dumping our history prove anything beyond disrespect to all the  fellow hams that preceded us and built the global reputation we have?  How does this "prove" or even establish we are inclusive---? Of what? More Directors why? For "inclusion"?  ...and I still want to know who had the unbridled arrogance to suggest California DX Club to the world...JIM K8JRK

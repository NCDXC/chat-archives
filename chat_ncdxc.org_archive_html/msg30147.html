On Jul 12, 2023, at 11:29 AM, Bruce Bern via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Guys,
My wife, Medea, and I take a couple of tropical dive trips every year to interesting, if not rare entities, such as ZF, 3D2, and the Eastern Caribbean.
I’ve decided to try my hand at holiday-style DXpeditions. I own an IC-7300 with a flexible input DC supply. Weight is usually a consideration, so I’d look forward to suggestions for compact and suitcase-compatible antenna systems that won’t frustrate me with poor performance.
Any ideas are appreciated.
Thanks, Bruce K3NQ


Anna W6NN and I have done DOZENS of Holiday DXpeditions, including much of the Eastern Caribbean, FO, EA8, 9H3, and more. Here is a link to an (old) presentation I have given at Visalia and other places about Holiday DXpeditions. It also shows my “DXpedition-in-a-Box” kit; the presentation shows an older version where I used to use an IC-7000 (very small, lightweight, but 100W output). I have upgraded to using a K3S, primarily because the IC-7000 is inadequate for contesting. The 7300 you have may be a good choice as well.

https://tinyurl.com/yc7eu3w8

We use a Buddipole Deluxe, which weighs less than 20 lbs and fits in our suitcase (it’s 24” long). It is easy to set up and works *very* well on 20-6 meters, somewhat less so on 30 and 40 meters. Being adjacent to salt water is a HUGE advantage; I can hold a run frequency indefinitely during a contest, and have achieved 200+ QSO/hour rates on CW. (I currently hold the Anguillan low-power record for the CQ WW CW contest.) We were also the very first “Headquarters” station for St. Vincent and the Grenadines during the IARU contest in 2016. (They issued us a special callsign for the event, J87HQ. We operated from Bequia Island.)

The Buddipole can be mounted on a tripod (it’s part of the kit), but I have found that it is MUCH easier, and more stable (especially in the constant Caribbean winds) to simply find a vertical railing post and strap the mast using tie-wraps. I have also inserted the mast into one of those heavy bases used to hold patio umbrellas; that works very well.

I have tons of photos available on request; just ask me offline or give me a call to discuss.

Rich KE1B

It was a very good convention, significantly bigger than last year.  I got to spend some time in the vendor area but I didn’t have time to attend any of the presentations (other than my own).  I expected to see the new Kenwood TS-990 but it was a no show.  I did spend some time chatting with the guys at the Flex Radio booth about their new SDR.  Their use of a Vertex 6 FPGA is a major step forward in the development of ham radio SDRs.  We use the V6 in our products at work (government applications) and it was interesting to talk to them about the FPGA code development.  It turns out that they also build SDR radios for the government, that’s where they got the experience.  The big Vertex FPGAs are very expensive and it is great to see them come down enough in price to get included in a ham radio rig even though it is a high end rig.  This is an extremely powerful processing platform.

 

My presentation on “Elements of DXing” went well, was well attending and the audience participation was great.  There were new hams, new generals and experienced DXers in the group.  The section on QSLing seemed to be of particular interest.  If you missed it, the slides will be posted on the NCDXC web site soon.  Also, if you want more info on QSLing, checkout the articles that I wrote for the DXer which were published in the Sept, Oct, Nov and Dec 2007 issues, you can find them on the web site.

 

The DX Forum that followed also had a very good attendance as many of the people stayed over and were joined by others.  The forum went very well with some excellent topics discussed and very good participation from the audience.  Thanks to you Paul, for participating on the panel.  I would also like to thank John Miller, K6MM, Norm Fusaro, W3IZ from ARRL HQ and Wayne Mills, N7NG who traveled out here from Jackson Hole to be on the panel.  There was a great deal of experience on this panel and the discussions were very informative.

 

Again, thanks to all that attended!

 

73,

- Craig, AE6RR

Here is your NCDXC question of the week?

 

 

Did you attend Pacificon/ARRL National Convention?    If so, what parts did you enjoy?

 

 

___________________________________________________________________

 

I attended Pacificon on Saturday and Sunday. On Saturday, I had my 14 year old son Ryan-N6RYN and his friend in tow. As soon as we arrived, we were approached by an ARRL official who asked if the boys would like to have lunch with Dr. Lee Morin, the NASA Astronaut who was there. The boys were so excited and they enjoyed their lunch and asked Dr. Morin many questions. While they did that, I enjoyed the Flea Market. I just love to see that vendor that brings the old restored Microphones and old radios.

 

It was fun to watch W1AW/6 in action. John K6MM was there helping to make sure that everyone got a turn. The vendor forum was super with a good mix of vendors. Elecraft was really busy with a lot of interest in their products. I came away with a new KPA-500.

 

Most of the forum presentations were well done. Most of the rooms were standing room only. On Sunday, the crowd was smaller.

 

I thought that the ARRL did a great job with the Youth Activities. The kids got a real kick out of seeing a live QSO with the International Space Station. What a great event!

 

Paul N6PSE

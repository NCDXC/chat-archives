A number of entities had been added to the DXCC list because of pressure by an individual or a group. This has always made me feel that someone "gamed the system". Which then made me ask "If one person or group can pressure the DXCC program into changing the rules to add an entity, then why can't I do the same". And these rules were always changed to ADD new entities, not DELETE some that probably never should have been added. Now, some rules have been changed over the years due to a need because of changes in world politics and geography, and they seem very fair. We very well might see the day where a reef will become barely above water, or even under water. Will the rules change to have submerged entities deleted, or is this an ADD only rule changing game? Changing rules in DXCC seems like it opens a can of worms. If they add Kosovo, make it retro-active to whatever their rule change criteria would have added it. I think that means September 2012..... But I always wonder why some get to change the rules and others don't have any say, word or leverage whatsoever. 73, Rich KY6R

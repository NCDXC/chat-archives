Here's an interesting quote I ran across yesterday in the Twin City DX
Association's Gray Line Report for March [2014].
http://www.tcdxa.org/Newsletters/March2013Grayline.pdf  It's from Ralph,
K0IR referring to his operating at Amsterdam Island.

"DXers and DXpeditioners perceive things differently. DXers hear what's
happening on the DXpedition's transmit frequency. DXpeditioners hear what's
happening on their receive frequency. My perspective is this: Callers were
generally courteous and orderly. If I struggled with a call, others
generally stood by until I completed the QSO. I experienced no jamming on my
receive frequency. If I called for a specific continent, I generally
experienced cooperation. Of course there was an exception from time to time;
perhaps just a simple mistake. So, in my personal experience, pileups were a
pleasure to work ---- worldwide. -- K0IR

I have been through several logging applications and have settled on DX
Labs Suite.
http://www.dxlabsuite.com/

I have lost track of all the logging programs (not contesting) I have
used but I remember these:
DX4Windows - early days bad radio support and didn't like the layout
N1MM - simply doesn't have enough in terms of functions
HRD - it is designed to run radios - I want logging to slave to the
radio
Logger up through Logger32 - good, did lots of good things
DXLabs - I went kicking and screaming into it being pushed by a friend -
wow!

Interfaces seamlessly with Club Log, LoTW with the glue necessary to
update in both directions.
DX spotting called SpotCollector is superior to others I have used
(except for VE7CC).  Especially how it interrogates your log to
highlight in colors what spots you need depending on it being a new-one,
new-band, new-mode...  Totally customizable.

The radio interface (Commander) is flawless.  I have it interfacing
FT-1000D, Mark-V, K3 and IC-7800.

DXView - with just a prefix tells you what you have worked (band &
mode), confirmed or remains requested.  Enter a call in the log entry
window and you know instantly what your status is for that DXCC entity.

All and more are part of DXLabs.

What fits your style will determine the logging program you should use.
For me DXLabs allows me to use only those applications I want.  For
instance I favor VE7CC over SpotCollector but at times I want
SpotCollector.  The user sets up what he wants.

Oh - and it is free.

73,

Bob - W6OPO

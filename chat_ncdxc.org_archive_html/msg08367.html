On Nov 12, 2014, at 7:56 AM, Paul N6PSE via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

> Bob, I worked FT4TA on 80 meters, it was ESP copy for the most part. It was by far my most difficult contact with them.
> 
> With deep QSB fading, their signals were up and down. I would call them when I could hear them and wait when I could not hear them.
> 

Same here. They were in and out, ESP, at 1430Z on Friday morning. I called him and was surprised when he came back to me; really good ears. I was running power, but the antenna is only an inverted Vee dipole with the center at 55’ (but that 55’ is added to my 2000’ elevation). Good news, it was confirmed on LotW just a few hours later. Even better news, that QSO was my 100th country on 80m, completing EIGHT BAND DXCC for me. I then worked the W1AW/KH8 on American Samoa for #101.  A very good morning.

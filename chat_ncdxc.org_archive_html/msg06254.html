On 3/26/2014 1:58 PM, Rick Tavan N6XI wrote:
There's a very rational overview of your decision in QST this month. It doesn't make it for you and it doesn't recommend makes and models, though. 

Indeed there is. Several factors come to the fore..
First, I could certainly try to upgrade something I have, but that is rife with issues, and I still have a 10 year old box, with a video card that Google Earth won't talk to (even though it did a year ago). Second, ANY OS upgrade has potential danger -- one of my pro audio colleagues who worked as a software tester at Microsoft spent a week upgrading one of his computers, and he's a VERY smart guy. Third, hardware is cheap, even from good companies. I can replace computers I paid $3k for in 2005 for $500 that will run 500x faster, and with more "features."
The REAL investment is in time, loading all the software you need on the new machine, and, in some cases, buying it all over again. Thank you Microsoft. I carry around the estimate in my head of 1 week to get a new computer really "running." as your "main" computer. A lot less for a computer that only runs radios in your shack.
As to "making it work" -- years ago, I made a commitment to OS/2 that caused me to burn more hours getting my specialized applications to run efficiently than in actually using my computers to do billable work. To me, a computer is a tool, not something I want to spend my time making it work. In a way, choosing a computer is not unlike finding a wife or a business partner. They can be life-changing decisions.
That's why I spend so much time with due diligence about the computers I buy -- I don't want to blow that week any more often than I have to. I've got a lot better stuff to do :) 

73, Jim K9YC

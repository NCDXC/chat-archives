I can attest that using an Inmarsat BGAN satellite uplink terminal can be a difficult, costly and frustrating process. 

 

When we were on South Sandwich and South Georgia, we found that we could only connect to the Satellite at the same time and direction once per day. We tried many other times but the connection would fail. I called Inmarsat on the Iridium satellite phone and they told me that the Inmarsat satellite travels in a figure eight pattern over Tunisia and that we could achieve success if we would stick to our same time and direction. Good thing we didn't promise uploads of more than once per day!

 

We also found that if anything slightly obstructed the path between the BGAN terminal and the satellite that the connection would break and would be quite difficult to restore. We zapped many birds in that process. 

 

So the user of the BGAN needs to know exactly where the satellite is that he is aiming for. There are many areas of the earth where there is simply no coverage. But I would expect that where they are would have coverage to some degree. 

 

Lets hope they resolve this issue. I would suggest to work them again to be sure if you are not already sure of your contact. 

 

Paul N6PSE

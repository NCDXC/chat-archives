Paul-

 

I put no “faith” in them whatsoever.  HamCap can provide guidelines, but I find I always have more luck if I listen to slightly higher MUFs than indicated by the program.  My first QSO with Pakistan years ago was around local noon on 12 meters on the Fourth of July, a time we all figure we’re in the Summer Doldrums here.  Nothing takes the place of listening. 

 

I think some of your responders are mixing up reports of current solar conditions and real-time DX spots with long-term prediction data.  I think for you DXpedition leaders, who need to decide where to allocate your limited resources, it’s always worth running the prediction programs to choose antennas, amps, etc.  But please always listen on higher frequencies anyway!

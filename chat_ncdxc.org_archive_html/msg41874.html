Great story Rich!  
I very much remember the Novice CQing and reply techniques.  I still have my 7175 and 7168 Kc crystals.  

And we all learned how to shift a crystal’s (fixed) frequency. With the older crystals in an FT-243 case, you could open the case (a couple of screws), gently separate the metal plates that flanked the crystal, and remove the delicate, thin, quartz crystal itself.

To lower the crystal frequency, take a soft pencil and wipe some graphite on both sides of the crystal. That would move it down, perhaps 2-3 Kc, before it would no longer oscillate. To raise the frequency, make a paste of toothpaste and Bon-ami or Comet dish cleaner. wipe the crystal back and forth on the gritty paste laid on a glass plate (to take a little quartz off), clean it off and put it back. You could move it up more than down, without losing activity (oscillation).

I still have my old Harvey-Wells Bandmaster, which uses crystals. I had to modify a crystal to get it onto the standard frequency for the West Coast AM net on Wednesday nights, and remembered how to do it from the old days.

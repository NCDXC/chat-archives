Hi guys-
 I’m available to help as well. 
Started with RS-12/13 Radio Sputnik (cw/ssb) in 1990 and have wk’d a number of the birds up to SO-51 (leo fm) about 2 yrs ago. 

Wow - “AO-40”.. Launched in 2000.  I think ALL but one or two of the SHF transponders failed after launch and/or during initial orbit if I remember correctly. This was a major disaster..Billed at the time, as the most expensive satellite ever built. It was designed with 21MHz to 24GHz transponders onboard.

~ RS-12/13 had a piggybacked 3 band transponder onboard for 10,15 & 2 mtrs.. Mode K,T, K/T. Yep- 10&15m have a sat subband. We often had terrestrial stns calling us on 15m! These guys didn’t know we were working thru a satellite going up on 29MHz.. This sat also had an “auto qso reply” robot onboard. If you called “CQ RS12/13” in cw, and were lucky enough, the robot would issue a “qso contact number”..After sending a qsl card with the contact number to the infamous PO Box 88 Moscow, you might receive a card back. 

~AO-7: Launched in 1974-Dead in 1981- Alive in 2002. Mode A (10m/2m), modeB V/U.. About 8 yrs ago this sat started alternating between mode A and B every 24hrs.. Only the V/U transponder is currently functioning I believe. The batteries completely failed many years ago, and it operates under Sun illumination only. It’s orbit is a little higher, with passes lasting a little over 15minutes. The ssb/cw signals are very raspy with a heavy buzz sound.

See the Amsat.org sat status grid for AO-7 and about two dozen other sats including the ISS ( ARISS, telem,robot pd120, rptr ops).

I have a few tips on how to aim your portable yagi…..Starting a separate email thread sounds like a good idea..hi

73,Eric n6spp-cm98

I use GoSatWatch (visually fantastic)& SatTrackerPlus(clean&simple,doppler),& ProSat(a little cluttered, also with doppler) occasionally on the iphone.

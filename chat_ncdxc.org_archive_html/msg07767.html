> On Oct 1, 2014, at 10:17, Jim Brown via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
> 
> This past weekend, I helped KA6SQG clean out one of the transmitter buildings on property that he just acquired several miles up the road from me. A Google Earth placemark is attached to this email. It is at 2600 ft, has a 150 ft Rohn 45 tower with a lot of stuff on it that he will remove, and two transmitter buildings.
> 
> Matthew has a very nice linked and voted 440 MHz system, and is continuing to expand it. It has little activity, and he's looking for more, so I asked if NCDXC traffic would be welcome. He responded that the system is designed for mobiles, and felt that fixed stations would light up too many receivers. But he is receptive to helping us fix our broken system by helping us gain access to one or more sites with better RX, including this new one. http://www.wb6ece.org/  He's also working on a new site on San Bruno Mountain.
> 
> I met Matthew when he gave an excellent presentation about his system to the Santa Cruz club a few months ago. Audio and slides of that talk are here.
> 
> http://www.k6rmw.net/sccarc/Wide-area%20repeater%20coverage.pdf
> http://www.k6rmw.net/sccarc/SCCARC_Matthew_Kaufman_8-15-14.mp3
> 
> I was very impressed by the innovative engineering of his system, which includes phase-locked transmitters, with clocks and audio synchronized to GPS. The system is on 441.3 MHz, 100 Hz PL. It does not transmit PL, because the TX PL doesn't do well with the synchronized transmitters. This is not your grandfather's repeater system!
> 
> Just in case the attachment doesn't make it through the reflector, the coordinates of this new site are 37° 5'58.95"N  122° 8'22.28"W.
> 
> Among other things, Matthew was the architect of Flash Player and Skype software, and currently works for Microsoft (since they bought Skype).

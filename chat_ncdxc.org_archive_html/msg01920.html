The Elecraft K-line, hands down. 

For once in my ham life, I have an integrated system with most of the optional goodies installed.  I can use as little or as much of it as I choose for portable ops and it is simple to use (thanks to good engineering).   

The support, if needed, sets the standard high.  No one else comes close.  The support lists are also helpful and are generally well behaved (amazing).

Conveniently enough, computers are actually making things even simpler; most of the time.  Good timing. 

The TS-940Sat is a great CW rig too, but it's getting tired.  The K3 is a pure joy. 

Rick wa6nhc

On Apr 15, 2013, at 7:37 AM, <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:

Here is your NCDXC Question of the Week:
 
What is your all time favorite rig?
 
 
 
 
 
My response: My all time favorite rig is my Yeasu 1000MP. I bought it new circa 1991 and I still have it. This was my first "big box" rig replacing a Kenwood TS-690. I never really liked or enjoyed the TS-690. The 1000MP is a joy to operate and a very fine radio. My primary pile up busters are a pair of K3's that I use on CW/SSB/RTTY but for casual listening I still enjoy flipping the antenna switch over and listening from the 1000MP.

On 2/13/2013 10:39 AM, Craig-AE6RR wrote:

I agree the KX3 sounds like a great travel/suite case expedition rig but
would you opt for that approach for your base station rig?  You would end up
with the 100W amp to drive a legal limit amp...
Sure. Remember the K2? A 10W radio, to which you could add a 100W amp and tuner, and which you could use to drive a legal limit amp. I did that, and it worked very well. Think about it -- most transceivers are essentially the same thing, a 5-10W exciter driving a 100W output stage, all in one box. The beauty of the Elecraft concept is that they can be in two separate boxes, one of which can be out of the way under your desk, or in the trunk of your car .
The only problem with using the K2/100 to drive a legal limit amp is that the off-frequency phase noise is a lot more than your neighbors will like. The K2 started out life as a QRP CW radio, and grew into a full featured radio, but not an ideal one. The KX3 was designed to work both as a full featured QRP radio AND yet be clean enough to run legal limit. 


I think that you could drive a KPA500 with the KX3 but you wouldn't get full
power out.
Right. Roughly 200W, I think. The limitation is a legal one -- the FCC limits the gain of a power amp, and I think that limit is 13 dB.

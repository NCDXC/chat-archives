On 12/30/2012 5:29 PM, paul@n6pse.com wrote:
Question: Should the ARRL consider de-listing any entity that has not been activated for a prescribed number of years, perhaps 15 years of no activity?
 

Perhaps, but that would tend to remove the incentive for many DX trips, and reduce both the fun of organizing and making the trips and the fun of working them.  On the other hand, there clearly needs to be some intermediate but challenging stage of recognition that rewards something other than sheer longevity of the operator. It's depressing to see the annual "ceremony" at Visalia where guys stand when they've achieved a certain counties count, and at some point the last ones standing have to be propped up.

We need more things in our hobby that recognize the contributions and achievements of younger hams. I would favor awards that recognized the achievement of working all entities that have been active within a certain reasonable time, perhaps 10 years.

It also continues to disgust me that QSOs made from a QTH anywhere within a very large country like the US, Canada, and China are aggregated for the DXCC award. Any seasoned operator knows that many entities are really tough from one coast, but easy from the other. Part of the challenge of a high country count is dealing with those hard ones, both technically and as an operator.  But an operator can work a major contest from anywhere on the east coast, another from the west coast, locations that are 3,000 miles apart and greatly reduce the challenge. Do it from a vacation home, or rent a shack. Or simply move with your job and operate for several years at both ends. The fastest and easiest trip to the honor roll that money can buy. And all because the honor roll takes decades to acheive, where the limiting factor is staying alive long enough, and it simply isn't possible for most of us to do it from a single location.  This really dumb ruling does far more to "cheapen" the DX award structure than being more realistic about the Honor Roll, which really ought to be re-named "the old fart survivors" list. 

73, Jim K9YC

Hi Paul,

I've seen you on the bands a lot lately, you're doing very well in working the DX.

Not using LOTW is a choice, but implies that you don't want conformation fairly quickly.  For the US ham, it's (except typical ARRL complicated) not a huge deal to set up.  I don't intend (lack of) LOTW choices to be a judgment; just that much of the world prefers fast and cheap and what we offer is (or should be) more than just a contact (i.e. I live in a state with fewer hams than other states and a county with less yet HF hams [under 10 total], so state and county collectors need my contact proof for their needs).

I no longer send 'green stamps' anywhere by mail (even in the US).  The world being what it is, the actual costs unknown make it risky.  And US funds are not useful in all places (one VP8 ham returned the cash with the card because it would cost more to convert the $10 to local funds, than the cost of a stamp was worth; he took the lesser financial cost).

Online exchanges (i.e. PayPal) is one answer (just include the exchange data in the message WITH the call used).  The QRZ page and others provide more QSL information (DX lists that are emailed or online) and bureaus (for now) are options.

Only a few contacts of the thousands I've made, demand cash up front or make it near impossible to get a card (and they are not the only station in the entity, so there are other options).

Or ask at the time of contact (or via email); what is your preferred means for QSL cards and what needs should be provided to allow that?  (Yes, how 'quaint' to still want a card, hi hi.)

My EP contacts were OQRS/PayPal confirmed.  LOTW didn't take long (long sigh of relief) and the card, eventually arrived.  Other EP since, also used PayPal.

I have taken to collecting cards when LOTW is already processed, because I enjoy looking at them (and the envelope stamps go to a collector friend).  And some are true works of art; displaying the shack, local area and some cultural influences (a key point to me, vicarious traveling).

I use PayPal, pay the exchange rate and overhead costs; because it's accepted and protected by US credit card laws should something go sideways (you can get your money back).

73,
Rick nk7i

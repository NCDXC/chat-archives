This message is intended for the NCDXC membership.

Dear NCDXC membership: I'm writing you today, from Myanmar to express my interest in succeeding Ross Forbes as your club President. 

Currently, there are two candidates for President, however your Board of Directors wants to install one of the members without allowing other consideration of the full membership. 

When I learned of Ross's resignation, I informed the club's board of my interest in filling this role.  Without considering me, other candidates were approached and one is being nominated for a full member vote by the board.

The club's by-laws do not speak directly to this process. Some on the board are making up the rules by the seat of their pants. There is a fundamental lack of fairness and transparency taking place. None of this is a reflection on the other candidate. He is a fine man.

I am strongly urging the board to put the decision to the full membership for a vote. As board members, we need to remind ourselves from time to time who we serve. It's you, the general membership and we should not forget that.

If elected as your President, here are some of the initiatives I will champion:

1) I will immediately bring back public board meetings, publicized in advance so that any member can attend.
2) I will foster a mentoring program where mentors are listed and shacks are available to members without stations capable of working the DX.
3) I will bolster the long legacy of our awards program and ensure that it is timely and contemporary with the desires of the membership.
4) I will foster a "virtual" club station, accessible to club members via the Internet. This concept could be done from a mountain top (a la K6LRG) or a University Club Station. I will strive to make this a reality.

Lastly, ask your board why there are efforts to provide you with only one candidate. Insist on full transparency and accept nothing less. I will accept the results of a vote by the club membership.

I'm sorry to have to address this matter in this manner but because I'm
In Myanmar, I cannot speak out on this at the club meeting on November 21st.

Thank you,

Paul N6PSE

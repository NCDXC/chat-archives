I'd have to say this is one of the worst DXpeditions that I've listened to.

There are several reasons for this, but all come down to a comment OH2BH made - You can maintain control of the pileup as long as everyone thinks they be the next QSO in the log.  We all know that isn't the case for PT0S.

Much of the QRM on there frequency has been from frustrated operators. The other day they were on 24940 listening from 24945 to 24990 nearly the whole band. Ok, they were listening for PYs from 24930-24935 (we weren't supposed to know that). So, that was the whole band. Several of the QRMers were just trying to find a quiet spot on 12m for a QSO.

On 15m RTTY the first day they were on 21081.5 listening 21085-12150. Yes, a 65 kHz split on RTTY.

The splits on the other bands haven't been much better. That creates frustration from folks who don't care about the DXpedition. These wide splits should not be allowed in the DX Code of Conduct!

Obviously, the emphasis has been on the low bands and 6m. That is fine, but 160m opens for them around 1930Z and closes around 0730Z. So, much of the time they are operating there is no propagation to the West Coast (& US).

And clearly they have a preference for the EU ClubLog group. With the limited number of operators, why not just stick to phone on some bands and cw on another. I see many EUs claiming 9 bands of QSOs. I doubt that many US stations are at that level. Clearly, maximizing unique QSOs is not a goal.

The leaving a US pileup to change to another band is also frustrating. But, if you are lucky enough and pick the right band, you might be one of the early callers and get in the log.

Fortunately, I only need this one on 6m (ain't going to happen) and RTTY (I can only hope).

We can all pray that ZL9HR does better.

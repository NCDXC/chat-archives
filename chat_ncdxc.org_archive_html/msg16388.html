On Jan 11, 2019, at 12:25, KE1B <ke1b@richseifert.com> wrote:

This Sunday (Jan 13th, 6 am to midnight, local time) and Tuesday (Jan 15th, also 6 am to midnight, local time) is the “Classic Exchange CW” event. This is a no-pressure “contest” where the intent is to operate using vintage radios, the older the better. Full details at: http://www.classicexchange.org.

The warning is that, for at least a few hours during these two periods, I will activate my 1952 Harvey Wells Bandmaster, mostly to make sure it still works. ;^) I apologize in advance for any drift (although I mostly use crystal frequency control), chirp, whoops, and key clicks. (One year, I got the award for best “whoop”. And even with a big filter in the keying line, my key clicks have key clicks with this radio.)

The organizers always time the event so as not to interfere with any major contest. NAQP CW is on Saturday, but that’s the day before Classic Exchange. And it’s scheduled for the winter, because those big tubes can really heat up the shack!

If you hear me, give me a call, especially if you have a boat anchor. (I’ll be running relatively slowly, with a mechanical bug; no computer interface to a 1952 radio!) And listen for lots of other stations using hand keys, bugs, and rigs with less-than-perfect CW signals. It’s a great opportunity to hear some radios that you may have owned, or forgotten about, from years past. Part of the event exchange is the make/model of transmitter and receiver being used. And in this event, the points-per-QSO is the sum of the AGES of your transmitter and receiver. (My combo is 122 points-per-QSO.)

73 de Rich “Real Radios Glow in the Dark” KE1B

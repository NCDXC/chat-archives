For me, propagation prediction programs offer just one bit of the total information that tells me what time of day might be helpful for whatever is happening. (Don't try to get nine significant figure results out of three figure data...)
W6EL can suggest what time during the day I might expect openings of interest. There are many other clues to add. Geomagnetic info is helpful on Topband. (Those who look for the lowest A and k indices will miss lots of stuff on 160M.) Solar flux, A indices and k indices are helpful, but they don't tell you everything you need to know.
I am thankful for those who depend on real-time information. That's just less competition. On the other hand, people will do what fits their style and modes.

The fact that a member criticizes aspects or characteristics of an organization does not merit a response of "maybe you should go somewhere else" -- or worse. Jim may be blunt --even grumpy--but, given his commitment and many contributions to the DX game we all enjoy, perhaps his comments should be considered on their merits. Loving DXing does not imply a requirement to embrace everything about either DXing or the DX club.

Personally, I agree with some of his observations, particularly about Holder's, whose geriatrically-oriented menu is less than an attractant to me. Yes, I understand the logic of meeting there each month--I just don't dig the food. I can't make every monthly meeting--it often conflicts with a monthly board meeting I am obligated to attend. When I can make it, I try to eat somewhere else beforehand---and so do other members of my acquaintance. Those of you who think Holder's is great should not ruffle your feathers because I do not agree with your assessment.

Hams are a diverse lot. Accept the diversity.

73

Garry, NI6T

Those of us who care contributes depending on our time and talents. I have done at least one presentation at most IDXC meetings, and have offered to do more. It was late when I composed my "shot across the bow," and I failed to acknowledge the considerable contribution of K6KLY to the club and to the ham community.  I'm also certain that I forgot others.

Whenever I have had criticisms of the club, I have also made positive suggestions about specific actions that would make things better. I have, for example, noted several potentiall sites 2M RX for the W6TI system, including one that a colleague owns on Berryessa Peak (3,000 ft, 40 miles west of the Sacramento airport), and another at 3,000+ ft in the mountains above Saratoga that has a great look around the Monterey Bay, as well as to all of Silicon Valley and the Sierra . And one of the resources I've been studying, without discussing it here, is a 900 MHz repeater system that is linked from the Bay Area, to Tahoe, to Reno, to Bakersfield, the the Sierra N of Bakersfield, up the valley to north of Redding, and even down here in the Monterey Bay. There's very little traffic on that system, and radios to access it are cheap (mostly decommissioned Motorola gear).

On 7/24/19 at 4:40 PM, chat@ncdxc.org (Richard (Rick) Karlquist via Chat) wrote: 


Did Joe tell you what bandwidth he was assuming for CW?
Without that, this anecdote doesn't mean much.
OK, lets do the math. Wsjt-x measures signal strength relative to a 2500 SSB bandwidth. When I operate CW, I sometimes narrow my K3 down to 50 Hz, 1/50 of the SSB bandwidth. My calculator gives me a 17 dB improvement due to bandwidth. I occasionally decode signals at the -24 level, so FT8, with its 50 Hz bandwidth is 7 dB better than CW at the same bandwidth. My 1500 watt CW signal is about the same as a 30 watt FT8 signal. If I use my amp with FT8, I am getting communication power well above the legal limit for CW. (But then Clay, N5YJZ who lives just up the hill might have problems with overload.)
If you normally operate in a wider CW bandwidth, the difference will be larger.

> On Jul 15, 2025, at 12:26 PM, Paul N6PSE via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
> 
> ﻿
> Incoming bureau cards have little to no value to me. 

IF you want to display something in the shack to intrigue (or brag to) a newcomer or another ham, you have something (occasionally REAL artwork) to put on the wall besides showing off the rigs (ooh, blinky lights and noise!) to attract.

It is VISIBLE evidence of a completed effort; even if you’re the only one that smiles at the memory or display.  It’s also part of a legacy spanning centuries now.

LOTW provides none of that.  None.

I haven’t used the outgoing BURO in over a decade, but do pay the postage for incoming (still used heavily from Asia and EU).  More often I pay the bounty for a DXpedition card.

> Lastly, as an ARRL life member, I recognize the value of the organization. They are advocating for us with our government and they are protecting our spectrum. They represent us at the IARU. QST is a great monthly publication that I enjoy.  I am saddened by the pettiness that I see taking place within the ARRL board but thankfully, I can sit back and focus on other things in life and not worry about who is doing what to whom.

The EXPENSIVE choice to become a lifer adds zero value (sucker bet at our ages?).  As a youth, it was out of reach if one had a growing family or house to buy.  Now it’s a poor ROI.

QST is now (without MORE cost) digital only and rarely had much of value but is filled with the constant ads, bragging “LOOK what WE did!”, “WE’RE SO GREAT!!!” or articles made so dumbed down as to be useless (or just flat boring to glean the ort).

While every year the paper got thinner, the ink more easily smeared; the entire magazine quality just kept getting worse.

The value in the political realm is HIGHLY debatable since the ONLY source of information is what the ARRL says it is while their track record of transparent honesty is dubious (at best).  They may be useful or they may be a running gag, we have no reliable honest point of reference; no other ‘insider’ sourcing.  But they’ll nag for more money!

The BOD has significant flaws and internal strife negating their real value; but one of the largest is that they decide (any topic) THEN ask the membership (ignoring comments if answered) to tell the members what they already decided.  Most often, if that involves income; they’re totally down with that, even to the detriment of the members.  Then they’ll happily extol what they did; again regardless of what the members want.

We’re NOT members, we are cash producing pawns to be ruled over (since the FCC gave up it’s duty).  Members would have viable input; they would be heard and their thoughts or wishes, actually considered.

The ARRL is a sinking ship, MAN THE LIFEBOATS!

Yes I use LOTW and the DXCC system (but I don’t care the least about buying an award for the efforts I made) because it is the current standard, but we can (and should) do better.  Pay the ARRL when their track record is so poor (using ancient tools and hardware)?  Not me.

Hardware, access and ability to ensure all the bits end up in the right box is not free; a small fee makes sense.  Even if volunteer staffed, there are costs.

I would not mind paying a small annual fee for any system that we can agree on as a standard, IF it was kept easily available (and cheap) for all to access (same for Clublog, but they WISELY avoid tar babies like DXCC).  Not political, not divisive, inclusive to all hams.

Education?  Pah.  Really?
Books?  No idea who actually pays the publishing costs but once put on paper it’s often cheaper to use another source than ARRL.

Almost NOTHING has changed with the ARRL for the 50 years I’ve been in ham radio and the changes were to benefit the ARRL, not hamdom.

73,
Rick nk7i 
(ARRL VEC examiner and member until that times out, then I’m DONE with them, my patience has flat run out)

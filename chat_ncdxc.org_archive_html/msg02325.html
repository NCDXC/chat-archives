On Mon, May 20, 2013 at 9:49 AM, <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:
Steve, I haven't said until now, who I thought the best DXpedition was.
 
While I hoped 7O6T would win something, I think HK0NA was the best DXpedition of 2012 for a number of reasons. I would have been proud to lose to this DXpedition.  
 
Our members voted for PT0S, a DXpedition where several operators went out of their way to have our members QRX so they could work their PY friends at home. This was after taking our donation money. This was shameful.
 
It was even reported to me this weekend at Dayton that several PT0S Operators were telling their friends at home in Portuguese where they were listening. That kind of operating is not worthy of our award.
 
Many aspects of this hobby involve respect. Why do we do the annual last man standing exercise at various DX gatherings?  Because we heap respect on those that have worked them all and have attained the respect of their peers. Why do guys seek CQ Marathon rankings or DXCC challenge awards?  It's because of respect that is garnered when one achieves something after a lot of effort.
 
Those in the know, the guys who really get it, tell me that it is just inconceivable that a major DXpedition to a #5 most wanted entity, making 162,000 contacts did not win a single award anywhere.
 
The 7O6T team had hoped to earn the respect of the DX Community but failed. Or did the DX Community fail 7O6T?
 
I've always had passion for working the DXpedition shifts so that I could work the West Coast. It is usually at 3 or 4AM while in Africa or the Middle East. It takes real drive and determination to operate all day and then be in the chair to give the West Coast their contacts.
 
I hope I can sustain that passion, but right now the 7O6T team feels that our efforts weren't appreciated and we want to understand why. I'm not speaking for the team but writing on my own to figure this out.
 
Paul N6PSE

I for one voted for HK0NA with 7O6T a close second.

Why?

I wasn't present during the political negotiations to activate either entity. Both took a lot of work in this area, so I gave it a tie.

A tougher call was which was more dangerous. I don't think I would consider going to 7O - just too risky. But as a climber, I could really appreciate the effort in putting that West Coast station on the top of the mountain at HK0NA. It worked, as I added a new ones on cw and rtty due to those efforts.

Both were well run and ran up the #1 and #2 QSO totals for DXpeditions that year.

There were other well run DXpeditions, but these were the best of them.

But, I would hope that one planning a DXpedition is not doing it for a hardwood plaque. I would hope the successful conquest would be enough.

Steve, K6UM


On Mon, May 20, 2013 at 8:49 AM, <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:
Dick, this isn't about just NCDXC's voting. We are talking about a global result.
 
You have to understand, these things are our passion and we put our heart and soul into them. We divert time and finances away from our families to carry out these DXpeditions, so we have every right to ask these questions even if the answers aren't forthcoming.
 
The Clipperton DX Club (France) overlooked 706T, NCDXC voted PT0S. the readers of DX-World.net voted for HK0NA (a well deserved honor) and this weekend, the members of SWODXA voted NH8S as their DXpedition of the Year.

The DX Community has no problem making it known what they want and need, but they seem pretty fickle with their thanks.
 
I really have to wonder if the DX Community as a whole "gets it".  I tend to think that they don't.
 
Other comments?

Hi Paul,

At my old QTH just spitting distance from the windmills on the east bay hills (yes you could hear the motors run 24/7 we were that close), i had the same exact slippage with my 3 ele SteppIR on my 40' tower.  When Paul mentioned the "Slipp Nott", I bought two and installed them on the top and bottom of the SteppIR center point.  That stopped the slippage and we had stronger wind up on the pass than most folks.  It did the job for sure.  I did have to get a much beefier mast which I got from the folks at Teledyne as my slippage was creating deep trenches in the pole as the SteppIR had worked its way down to the rotor.  Now I live in Florida and deal with hurricane wind.

See you in Visalia

Bill, N6BM

On Thu, Apr 13, 2017 at 8:00 PM, Paul N6PSE via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
I have a question for the NCDXC "brain trust"

I have a Steppir DB36 with an M2 Orion 2800 rotator on a 72' tower. 

This big Yagi catches a lot of wind. 

I have had a lot of issues with mast slippage in the rotator's mast clamps. 
The bolts are as tight as they can possibly be. The mast clamps are the Kurt-K7NV specially modified clamps to provide for more mast grip. 

I am considering buying a "Slipp Nott" from Teledyne: http://www.tennadyne.com/slipp_nott.htm

Has anyone else had this problem and found a good solution for mast slippage?

I want to avoid pining the clamps through the mast as that voids the rotator warranty.

Ted, I agree.  I started off as a dx'er, chasing stations as I could find
them,  Then I got involved in contesting as a means to pick up more
countries to get my DXCC.  It worked.  I was an active member of NCDXD for
over 15 yrs, but gradually switched over to mainly contesting, and focused
more on NCCC.    The competition was fun, and I kept working more countries.
I wish I had kept up on qsl'ing though, I could use cards from some of the
stations I worked 25 yrs ago. I still need a few more on 80 for my 5band
DXCC.  I know I picked up some new band countries this weekend, and a couple
ATNO's  This is the third contest this season I was able to work 10 mtr dxcc
in a weekend.  Contests ARE a target rich environment!  

Dave KA6BIM


 On Mar 4, 2014, at 7:24 PM, K6XN wrote:

> Team    
> 
> I agree with Jim. This weekend was the ARRL DX Phone contest with 
> *lots* of DX activity and many motivated outstanding DX operators 
> operating contest stations from all over the world.
> 
> As many of you know I am the trustee for several club stations all of 
> which have club members who are both DXers and Contesters. Each of the 
> club stations are attempting to complete obtaining all of the QSLs 
> needed for achieving DXCC this year. Each club has worked 
> substantially over 100 DX countries over the last few years but as we 
> all know working DX is one thing but actually obtaining the QSLs is
something else.
> 
> This weekend I operated at each club station, N7PR, NN7NN and NC6DX 
> for several hours at each station mainly using typical "search and 
> pounce" Dxer techniques to help out working and obtaining QSLs for the 
> last needed DX stations for the three radio clubs. The three club 
> stations this weekend cumulatively made 738 DX QSOs and made 467 
> different DX band mode contacts over four bands resulting in N7PR as of
today earning 14 "new one LOTW QSLs"
> for 96 ARRL LOTW QSLs so far, NN7NN earning 10 "new one LOTW QSLs" for 
> 85 ARRL LOTW QSLs so far and NC6DX earning 15 "new one LOTW QSLs" for 
> 71 ARRL LOTW QSLs so far. More QSLs are showing up in the club ARRL 
> LOTW accounts and achieving DXCC for each of the clubs this year is
finally in sight.
> 
> I had a lot of fun and I was *very* impressed with the band conditions 
> and with the rapid LOTW QSLing by many of the stations we worked.
> 
> Surprises? We were able to work a station from the Ukraine on 10M 
> Sunday morning that was only running ten (10) watts and each club 
> station was able to work Morocco CN2AA on four bands. Wow!
> 
> IMHO the DX contests are excellent "target rich environments" for 
> chasing DX and both the "contesting" DX station and the Dxer are 
> enriched IMHO by working each other.
> 
> In any event, I agree with Jim, and I for one really enjoyed chasing 
> and working DX this weekend.
> 
> 73, Ted, K6XN

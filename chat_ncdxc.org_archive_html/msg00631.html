On 12/6/2012 8:32 AM, paul@n6pse.com wrote:
Not many responses to this weeks question.

I've said several times that I consider AA7JV's trips to be the best by far, by several measures. They are well engineered at every level, George and Tomas are excellent operators, they tackle the most difficult bands and do so quite productively, they make more QSOs per DXpedition member, and I'd bet that their cost per QSO is far lower than other higher visibility trips.  Moreover, their trips exemplify the true spirit of ham radio far more than some of the megatrips I hear about.  Some have groused about aspects of their most recent trip to PY0, saying that operation was "erratic."  I would suggest those doing the complaining read George's blog about the trip, then tell us how they could walk not a mile, but a couple of feet in their shoes. 

FWIW, my first impressions of the ZL9HR trip are pretty good. On the higher bands they've had pretty good signals, some ops have been quite weak, but most have been pretty good. And while they did not expect to be able stay on the island overnight and thus likely did not prepare to operate 160M, they have managed to stay after dark at least some nights, and have been on a bit with what they've been able to improvise. Not loud, but they are there, and from what little I've been able to hear, the op seems to understand 160.  But -- to appreciate AA7JV's engineering, consider that his trips are often loud on my TX antenna, while most trips, including this one, are weak on my Beverage. 

73, Jim K9YC

Well… since the answers were coming in, I have been reading more and understanding more the nuances.

It seems like more clones are being created, MSHV is an example. Who knows what else is out there. Most of these tools are made for DXPeditions but not all. MSHV can be used in a special event station for instance.

 

Now I understand Paul’s indication better. In MSHV you can answer the DX anywhere you want within their RX passband. Which is +-1500Hz. So if you get within a few hundred Hz of him (provided there’s a clear frequency) you are a candidate. Also, don’t move because MSHV won’t capture you as F/H.

More manuals to read.

 

Here is an embarrassing LID anecdote of my first CW DX QSO (3 years ago). I was coming from making SOTA activations and chases. Had no clue about the DX QSO CW protocol, probably established back when.

So I heard the DX answer and I go: “TU UR 539 539, TU FER QSO, 73”.   The operator was probably chewing his mustache thinking “Who’s this moron?”  The pile up was probably thinking “Are you going to tell him your name and QTH?”

 

Anyways, next day I asked a seasoned operator what the protocol was. Oh, you just send 599 TU. That’s it!

 

That’s it? 2 years of CW Academy, 100 CWT’s, and all I have to do is fake an RST, thank and move on?

Yup.

 

Oh, well…. At least no need to read software manuals.

On 4/29/2013 7:35 AM, paul@n6pse.com wrote:

 
 
What would make the NCDXC a better club?

Varying the meeting location so that at least some meetings during the year are easier for members who don't live in Silicon Valley to attend.  I don't attend NCDXC meetings now because NCCC was treated rudely there several years ago, but almost anywhere in the Bay area works for me.

NCCC rotates its meeting locations around during the year, and we're trying to widen the area over which we are doing that.  I also agree that giving each attendee the opportunity to share what he/she has done recently is a very good thing.  Something else we've done a bit with NCCC that I think worked well is a short (5-15 minutes) Power Point highlighting the station of one member at each meeting.  This is nice in a lot of ways -- it gives us ideas for station setup and antenna farms, it gives individual members an opportunity to "shine,"  and it gives the presenter the opportunity to learn from comments from other members.  I've learned a lot by talking about what I think I know, or what I'm doing, and having others give me construction criticism or ask questions that get me thinking about stuff in a different way.

I'd like to hear presentations by LITTLE PISTOLS about how they manage to work the rare ones. Almost anyone can do it with brute force, but I'm more interested in the operating skills and tricks of doing with a smaller station.

Another thing that has worked very well for other clubs is a loose structure for providing help to other members with antennas, RFI, computers, etc.  I've gotten great help from other hams in building my antenna farm, I've been on the ground crew for lots of antenna parties, and I've occasionally visited or hosted other hams to help them think through designing their own antenna farms, and helping them fix RFI issues.

73, Jim K9YC

Thanks Paul – I think I missed you by just a little bit – I left the shack at 10 PM PST. Just before that, I did make my first QSO with Wayne, N7NG, and it was 599+ both ways – which was a relief because 40M just seemed strange yesterday. Maybe because of a CME that wasn’t earth directed, but still – the band had that strange feeling of being wiped out most of the day here.

 

I had used a plastic shim to prop up one side of a moving “slug rack” in the KWS-1 transmitter, and that let me tune the rig up on the bands. Today I will receive a second roll of brass “foil” and also a nice set of computer repair tools – which includes a phillips head attachment and a flexible screwdriver shaft so I can get in and repair the broken drive “brass tape”.

 

My KWS-1 is one of the last 25 made – and there were only 1600 made and supposedly, only 600 on the air world-wide these days – maybe even less. Mine most likely was used in the 1960-ish SAC (Strategic Air Command) SSB tests that Collins did with the Air Force, and it most likely was used at Travis AFB. I know this because while I had the KWS-1 RF Deck completely refurbished by master craftsman Howard, W3HM, I restored the power supply. On the power supply – there is this goofy tan paint covering an asset tag number just like what you would expect the government or military to do when selling off (liquidating) old gear. It reminds me of what they did when I worked at Lockheed back in the early 80’s.

 

I “fell into” this transmitter after also “falling into” the Collins 75A-4 that I purchased from Bruce, AH0U – also expertly restored by Howard, W3HM. Bruce really helped me get into stepping my game up as far as being able to restore and repair old tube gear – and I learned how to use an oscilloscope in the process, and also learned when to use a DVM vs. VTVM vs. oscilloscope. The test points and just the workmanship on the Gold Dust Twins is amazing – by any era standards – and I feel very much honored to run what is truly a rare “museum piece” on the air.

 

I hope to properly repair the mechanism today – then I can “run wild” and wreak havoc on the bands again!

 

73,

 

Rich

KY6R

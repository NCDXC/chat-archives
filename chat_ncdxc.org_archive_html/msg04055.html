I made the first 300 on QRP with a Mosley TA53M @ 60' and a Hustler 6BTV. That was good for 1500 on the DXCC Challenge.

The push to Honor Roll required using 100w. That also got me to the 2000 level on the DXCC Challenge. The Hustler was set aside for the GAP DX-IV, which made DXCC on 80m easy. I also added the Force12 Sigma30. That got me to 8BDXCC.

I need T5 and FR/G for #1 Honor Roll. I'm now chasing DXCC on 160m and 100w is NOT enough. I now use a KPA500 and that has gotten me into the log of anyone I can hear (37 so far). I must admit that breaking the pileups is much easier with 500w than 5w, but maybe I learned a few techniques in those QRP days.

Steve, K6UM


On Sat, Nov 9, 2013 at 12:43 PM, Rich Holoch <rholoch@comcast.net> wrote:

Just curious – does anyone “just” run 100 watts, and if so – how have you done? I’d love to hear anyone who has earned Honor Roll with “only” 100 watts. I promised myself in 2001 that I would only use “100 watts and wire antennas and prove to the world that I could make HR that way in 10 years”.

 

Well, guess what? Aluminum and amplifiers have appeared in the KY6R shack.

 

I’d be more impressed with any of you who had more patience and perseverance than I have with “QRO”. My idea of QRO has been 500 – 1200 watts max. I tried 1500 watts for a very short while until a neighbor complained, so I turned the power back down, and that (very nutty) neighbor completely went away, and it did not affect me getting on HR one bit.

 

Now if you made HR QRP – then like in “Waynes World” I have to get down on my knees and shout “I’m Not Worthy, I’m Not Worthy” . . . .

 

My amp is on the fritz, but with a little more skill and finesse, I’m breaking huge pileups. It’s a great lesson being “amp-less” . . .

 

73,

I have to second Rick's comments about not hearing them.  I am also HOA limited but only in the sense that I can't have a tower and beam.  I am able to have a 2ele triband poking about 5ft over the top of the house. 

 

I never was able to hear them when they were spotted locally.  I only heard them one day when I was out sick (Yes really sick) and worked them CW on 15M.  They were very strong at the time almost a 55 at my QTH in a low spot against the Fremont hills.  This is the same day I got lucky with my one 20M CW contact to 5T0SP. 

 

I also agree that the crowds were much more well behaved.  Less UP in CW or "he is working split you idiot" on phone when I was listening for them.

Hi Paul,

 

For an HOA limited station, it was indeed a challenge.  On the rare occasions that I was able to hear them at all on any band I was not able to get through the crowds.  I had all but given up hope when the KAT500 autotuner arrived so I was able to apply the amp.  (It’s a full K line station.)  It took a day or so of playing to get the tuner to play nicely.

 

Still I couldn’t hear them when others were reporting excellent signals (90-150 miles from me) so I’ve presumed interesting propagation or that the low N/S wire antenna has a null in that direction; yet I had been recently able to work ZL/VK on 75 and 40 (a pleasant surprise for me too).  Even with the amp, I was not able to get through on RTTY (they were S9 too).

 

Finally in the last days, I was able to both hear them well enough to copy AND apply the amp, so I have two contacts on 17 CW (one was really shaky, so I made an insurance contact the next day) and one on 15 CW for an ATNO.

 

For the most part, the crowds were MUCH better behaved and properly managed.  The folks at ZL9 were OUTSTANDING ops.  All the cops, tuner uppers and well, anal sphincters of previous DXpeditions had apparently worn themselves out on PT0S and had crawled back under the rock where they live.  At least there is hope.  ;o)

 

Overall it was a good couple weeks as I’ve added more ATNOs and some band/mode additions to the list.  I’m still in casual mode, but plugging away at building my countries worked list.  I’m only ~165 but most of that is in the last year (and I was gone all summer) so I’m feeling pretty good about it.

 

And if anyone has read this far, I’ll take the time to wish everyone on the list a Merry Christmas.

 

73,

Rick wa6nhc

 

PS If my seasonal religious message offends you, I’ll get over it.  You can too.  ;o)

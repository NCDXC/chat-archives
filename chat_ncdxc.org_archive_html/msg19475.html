On Apr 7, 2020, at 9:40 AM, Bob Van Tuyl via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

A while ago there were two paths:

  • Upgrade to Windows 10 and suffer with an unstable operating system
  • Stay with Windows 7 and risk being targeted by a hacker

Not an easy choice.  I chose the latter choice with a good virus protection and a router between me and the outside world.  I still don't know if I made the right choice.

I use a third option (sort of). Stay with Windows 7 but use it EXCLUSIVELY for ham radio contesting purposes. I use the Macintosh OS for any “real” (personal) information use, and also for everyday DX’ing and such. Windows is running on a virtual machine under VMware, using the Mac hardware.

If anyone hacks my Windows machine, they are welcome to anything they find there. It’s just my contest logs and associated applications. And those logs are already exposed to the world via Clublog. I don’t even have email on Windows, so no contact list, no banking information, not even a browser history. All of that is on the (much safer and well-protected) Mac side.

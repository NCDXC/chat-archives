Some of the time, I'm calling specific DX and start with the report (speeds up the exchange, best in marginal conditions when packet losses are high).  

To me, starting with the report shouts "I'm BUSY trying for THIS station".  While doing this I STILL get continual callers from (Italy, France, Russia, Japan and some US high on that list); which make my ignore list immediately.  That is annoying, bordering on rude (selfish at the least).

I realize that my station is often LOUD in many parts of the planet (planned that way and depends on the band).  I realize that Idaho is not a common state for WAS hunters and that my county has exactly TWO DXers (with others randomly operating less loud HF in the county); making me sorta kinda a little bit rare in those circles (DX seeks WAS too, all bands, all modes).   I'm HAPPY to help you attain your goals, really.

Without intending to sound snooty, it means that I have to 'filter' callers.  To that point, sometimes I'll run MSHV in expectation of a run of callers (one stream only, 2 contacts, higher efficiency).  I'll take the 20 minutes to clear all callers once I'm done with the DX that I was hunting.  Everyone wins, once I've worked the DX I wanted.

Here are the FTx general rules that I follow (hopeful guidelines, wish list):

  • If I HAVE to stop to answer you, I'm not working the wanted DX.  That annoys me.  Don't be annoying with constant calling, or your chances diminish.  Keep pushing and I'll use JT Alert or Grid Tracker filters so I don't have audible alerts that you're calling (ignore list, forever gone).
  • Call once, so I know that you're interested; then stop adding tone clogs to the band, be patient.  Repeat ONCE every 5 minutes or when I've completed the contact with the DX that *I* am seeking.  THEN I will spend time with your needs.
  • Allow me to get what I'm trying to work to be completed; you are NOT rare and if you're not rude, THEN I'll work you eventually.
  • Pay more attention; in particular to the station I'm trying to work so that you NEVER call on the DX tones, if more than X callers, they won't decode that mess, so it's wasted energy and time plus if F/H it will cause others to miss closing the exchange.  Be respectful of (AVOID) the 60 Hz windows above their base tone too should they go multi.
  • FIND A CLEAN space for YOUR tone (even though it's only what YOU hear, there is less chance of collision), NOT the DX tones ever, even off cycle.
  • Reset YOUR tone, once you've worked a F/H station.

Manners; they have become more rare than a P5 calling CQ.  Use some and we'll make contact and everyone is happy.

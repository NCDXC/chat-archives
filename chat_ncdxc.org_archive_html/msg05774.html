Kip, I’m not suggesting that at all. I visited Kosovo in late 2011. I met with Kosovar and Serbian hams in Kosovo and in the region. I was very surprised at the strength of their emotions and feelings on the issue of independence for Kosovo. I saw it and felt it first hand unlike 99% of most Americans.
 
I saw the security situation in Kosovo as I passed through the various Serbian and KFOR checkpoints.
 
http://www.aco.nato.int/kfor.aspx
 
This region is a “tinderbox” of emotions and long standing hatred and disputes. They have been at war or some form of conflict for a thousand years.
 
The UN owns this situation and the ARRL is wise to stay out of it by merely relying on the UN to admit or not admit Kosovo. The ARRL should remain as impartial as possible by not taking sides in this issue. Most Americans simply know that Kosovo is friendly. Indeed, Bill Clinton is their hero. Most DXers want a new entity to work. Most Americans have no idea the history, the level of conflict and the emotions in this region.
 
I’ve written more about this subject on my Blog: http://n6pse.wordpress.com/2013/11/04/my-thoughts-on-kosovo/
 
Paul N6PSE

I think the DXAC was wise to reject the motion to add the US Department of State’s list of independent States to the DXCC program. As Kosovo is on that list, this is how they handled the issue of Kosovo. The DXAC is wise to recognize the strong emotions within EU regarding Kosovo and other potential break-away states. The current criteria of following UN acceptance puts the onus on the UN to qualify and recognize a country/state and leaves the ARRL out of the politically charged business of creating countries.

 

Perhaps our Pacific DXAC representative, Ken-K6TA can give us more insight and an idea of what the ARRL board might do with the report?

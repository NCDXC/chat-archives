On 12/31/2012 9:00 AM, Wayne Mills wrote:
Good question Paul:

Thanks for your detailed and thoughtful responses, Wayne.  With respect to the rule about station location -- it would seem to me that moves of moderate distance ought to be allowed, and that distance tailored to realities of propagation. Before moving to CA nearly seven years ago, I operated from Huntington, WV, Cincinnati, and Chicago, all close enough that they shared similar propagation to DX locations, so I felt quite comfortable in aggregating my DX activities and "counting countries."  When I moved 2,000 miles west to my new QTH five miles from the Pacific, I started over. It's not easy to do that with LOTW, which automatically chooses all the earliest QSOs from any uncredited band/mode/countries from all locations for which you've loaded logs, and there seems to be no way to turn that off.  I don't mind claiming QSOs that are more difficult from Chicago than Santa Cruz, but not vice versa. I just worked #301 from Santa Cruz. 

There are very logical geographic limitations on WAS and VUCC, and there should be on DXCC as well. I would see something on the order of 500-700 miles between most distant locations as reasonable.  Or, as a compromise, the option for a claimant to choose an un-aggregated award. 

Another DXCC rule that makes no sense to me is that I can't count QSOs from a club station that I personally built, or using a club call that I'm using for WPX, that is only a few miles from my home QTH, but I can travel 3,000 miles to W1, use my call at someone else's station there and count the QSO.  And think of the thousands of guys who have no decent station of their own, but operate contests from a friend's station. 

73, Jim K9YC

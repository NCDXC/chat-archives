If asked, I'd say no it shouldn't count since it was not a QSO between two operators.  To allow it would dumb down DXing, grid chasing and lead only to fully automated exchanges while the operator(s) are away from the station, doing something else.

But, the ARRL (who gives out the awards) has other 'ideas' that are often not in the best interests of tradition or progress (regardless of member input), such as allowing rented remote stations in more propagation friendly areas being counted towards DX credit.  This means anyone licensed with enough money can rent a station and acquire awards all without owning ANY RF gear at all.  I hold DXCC with multiple stamps, having the ARRL make decisions like this, reduces the value of the award.  Mostly I track it to keep my personal 'score' of progress (287 worked).

While I operate my stations remotely at times, it is MY station, I built it, I operate it.  When it transmits, it is at MY bidding, not a machine.  

So I have no real idea if it will be allowed or not.  I hope not (no offense intended or implied). 

Rick nhc

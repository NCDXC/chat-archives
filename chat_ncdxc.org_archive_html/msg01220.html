Hi Paul,

 

Sorry for the delay in response, it’s been a busy couple of days (still I added some ATNOs).  Just to reinforce my questions, everyone has a slightly different perspective on what is unique.  More input allows better choices; so I’m curious about the DXpedition point of view.

 

I do try to predict the pattern of the DX by watching the P3 scope, then get ahead of them and be better placed when they move.  But if I can’t hear the stations they are working, I just sit in one place I know they’ve listened (a few don’t tend to move often, part of the pattern).  Lightning strikes many places multiple times.  ;o)

 

As I am HOA limited, I love the concept of pausing for a few moments for QRP (not for me) and limited antenna stations (I have a stealth ~110 meter dipole, 30-50’ above dirt, that’s it).  But I suspect the reality worldwide won’t allow that to happen until the DX community adopts some sort of cross check with a known Big Gun list during those moments.  In short, while everyone operates with a sense of morality; not all have the same sense (i.e. a Russian KW may be 10KW actual or more).  We all want the DX; some are willing to cross the line to get it.  [ALL my DX has been on modest <500 watt stations.]

 

Which leads to one more question:  Should and how can the playing field be leveled to make it the same for all?  Not everyone has the space, money or simple ability to put up phased towers/arrays for each band.  Is there a fair means to compensate?  Yes, that is where technique comes in, which ties back to the first question I asked.

 

73,

Rick wa6nhc

Hi Rick-WA6NHC.  This is a great question and one that is tackled at DX University each year. W9KNI also discusses his approaches in his book.

 

While I intend to submit this question to the DX Forum Panel, I can also say that from both ends of the pileups, if you are not a big gun station, try to stand out from the pack or separate yourself. Don't follow the pack. I rarely try to be where the DX just called. I use my P3 Panadapter to study where the DX is listening and I try to find a clear spot where he might listen next. 

 

When I am operating from a DXpedition such as 7O6T I notice that many now use their panadapters or "fish finders" to call right where you took the last caller. The days of working 4-5 guys from the same spot are pretty much over and you have to move around a lot to hear callers clearly.

 

Thanks everyone for the great questions, feel free to submit more up to IDXC in April.

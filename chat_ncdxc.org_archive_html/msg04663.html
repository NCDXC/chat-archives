Thank you very much for all of your thoughtful replies. I am researching ways to be able to fund (outrageously) expensive DX-peditions – which, as Don, N1DG masterfully outlined in his slide deck presentation a few years back, is tricky business. As you might know, I am a Planner on the Heard Island DX-pedition.

 

I need to answer my own question – “How much would I pay for a QSL”, and here is what I do:

 

I donate $100 per ATNO. Once I got up to about 325 entities – my “OCD” was to get to Honor Roll. The cost of these DX-peditions really became more front and center than ever. I almost always pay this up front – knowing there is no guarantee I will work them. I also donate my time by being a Pilot, writing a Blog and possibly doing some web site work for that same DX-pedition.

 

I add $20 for the QSL after the fact. Before 325 (which is the point where I learned how expensive these DX-peditions were, I donated to several clubs and foundations and just let them do the donating for me. I still donate to some clubs and foundations that give to DX-peditions, BTW).

 

For DX-peditions that are not ATNO’s – but where I am a Pilot, $50, and I usually add either $5 or $10 after the fact for the QSL.

 

All others (band fills) – I pay whatever the OQRS amount is or just $2 for the card. LOTW helps offset the cost – I don’t collect cards for band fills since my DXCC Challenge participation is “casual”. Honor Roll #1 is my new goal, and 9BDXCC is second (meaning 160M DXCC).

 

Like Amazon purchases, higher priority “items” get higher donation amounts (I’m using the shipping cost metaphor here).

 

I am also very sensitive to the fact that many DX-ers cannot afford this level – and that some may even have a hard time adding a few dollars for a QSL. I also understand LOTW saves people money that they otherwise wouldn’t have for a QSL.

 

73,

 

Rich

I’ve been embroiled in a longstanding rotor adventure. I’m using an old Tristao/ US Towers MA-40 rotating mast with an MARB-40 rotor base. The entire mast turns.

The mast is well over 60 years old and has been host to a number of different rotors. I bought it 35 years ago with an HDR-300 rotor. To accommodate all the different rotors, someone had drilled multiple mounting holes in the rotor shelf. They are not perfectly concentric.

I’ve shimmed the MARB shelf where it clamps to the base, I’ve shimmed the thrust-bearing extension where it clamps in the rotor, and I’ve centered the rotor directly below the center of rotation of the mast.

Everything works, but only sorta. Frequently when I try to rotate clockwise, the rotor will not move. It moves a few degrees backward and forward and stops. I’ve checked the limit switches, and they are disengaging as designed. When that happens, if I rotate anticlockwise, which is usually possible, and go beyond 240 degrees (N center), I can then rotate clockwise to whatever heading I choose.

I’ve replaced the motor start capacitor, which did not help. The currently installed capacitor measures out correctly. I have not checked voltage-with-load at the rotor.

It seems to me that my brake wedge is sticking, but I’m not aware of anyone who still works on CDR/Ham-m/Hygain/MFJ products, and part supplies are nonexistent.

Short of buying a new G-2800 and starting from scratch, does anyone have any suggestions?

Thanks to all!

73, Bruce K3NQ

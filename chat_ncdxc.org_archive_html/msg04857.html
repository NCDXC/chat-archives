I know some of you may frown when you hear the phrase 'CQ Marathon'.  Since reading Bob's book 'A Year of DX' I've always been fascinated with this award.  When you consider that some countries were active only for a short time in a year it's truly amazing when a contestant works all or nearly all of the active entities within a year.  I also learned that those who are very close to the top scores not only need good DX'ing skills but also thick skin to beg DX stations for a QSO whom are normally not active at all.

I've been following a ham on his progress towards the 2013 Marathon.  Wayne N0UN is local to me and we are often in the same pile ups.  He keeps a popular blog and his latest blog entry on the CQ Marathon has just been posted.  In a nutshell in order to 'work 'em all' you need to be extremely lucky, speak 100 different languages, and have propagation on 40m in broad daylight, hihi.  Wayne believes that those ones he missed cost him a chance to win the overall.  So he decided to go for a different award category all together.  Do you think he made the right decision?  You could read about it here:  http://www.n0un.net/2014/01/2013-dx-marathon-claimed-scores/

ps I see a few familiar callsigns from this group in the submitted logs.  A big hearty congratulations to those who participated and submitted a scoring sheet.  I hope to see many stellar results from the toughest part of the U.S. :-)

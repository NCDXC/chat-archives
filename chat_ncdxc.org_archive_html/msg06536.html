Jim, I don't know to what degree that foreign nationals play in the shortage of Call Area Six 1X2 and 2X1 call signs. My concern is the those in the contest community hoarding the more desirable calls for their contesting usage under the guise of a legitimate contest/DX club.
If you look up many of the local contesters, you can see that in addition to their own call, they are the trustee for several other 1X2 or 2X1 call signs. Why does anyone need three 1X2 or 2X1 call signs? Don't most of us operate with one call sign at a time?
While it appears that many contesters hold multiple calls for contesting, it is also apparent to me that some of the best, most successful and most respected contesters such as W6OAT and N6RO hold only their single call sign. These guys have achieved the pinnacles of success without holding multiple call signs.
I have no concern with legitimate clubs holding a call sign as allowed by the FCC rules. It’s the sham clubs created as a guise to hold desirable calls signs for infrequent use that should be the focus of our concern. The solution for the shortage of 1X2 and 2X1 call signs in call area six is for the sham clubs to fold and give back their calls back to the assignment pool and for the guys holding multiple calls to do some self examination of what they are doing and why. Your peers are looking at this and holding their nose.
Lastly, the FCC should consider changing the rules to allow only the less desirable 2X2 and 1X3 calls for use by radio clubs. 


Happy Easter!

Paul N6PSE

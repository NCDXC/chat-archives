Thank you Steve Jones & Jim Brown for your comments!
And a special thanks to Tony, K6BV for all the work he did to try to get my enterprise approved to help him out! It didn't work out, so now you all see it!
Let's put this insurance issue into it's real perspective.....insurance is needed when untrained & underexperienced people do things that they are not really qualified to do! That is the formula that creates those things that lawyers call "Accidents"......they just love it when these things happen, because it is job security for them!
Now, myself and the very few partners I will work with have at least 35-40 years of experience each, and we have already seen all the things that can go wrong with any move on any tower anywhere. Here, I mean the most minute things that no one here would ever be able to detect. So, we have decided to just not allow any of those mistakes to happen on any job we do, "It's our way or the highway"....that is completely selfish for prolonging our lives & reputations in the business, and taking care of all the pundits that might show up on any given day on any job ;-)
Now, most Amateur Station owners are working overtime to make all the pennies in their station funding jar turn into dollars! And, we ar trying to help them.......by keeping our rates as low as possible.
Insurance is really expensive and would likely just put us out of business, which I will chose if this becomes too hard to deal with....and most of you would of course not want to pay for what it costs in increased rates.....

And in the case of rangers, their normal shift is not 24 hours a day (means overtime pay, hazard pay and whatever they have contracted for) plus someone has to cover their shift at their home station/office while they're gone; cut that bill at least in half, if not by 2/3. I know that as a Fire Captain on a strike team assignment, I was making $900/day on OT while the billing was higher to cover the costs at home; plus engine expenses, mileage... some jobs just aren't cheap.
And I would presume that the rangers had other chores other than hand holding and watching for pirates; it gives them an opportunity to get other tasks and chores done while there without a line budget expense.

Again, it helps if you've managed a service business. Example: Joe Techie drives a truck around town fixing installed systems. He gets paid $25/hr. He gets billed at $125/hr (or even $175), including half time for travel. Why so high? There's support for whatever he's fixing -- spare parts, doc, training on specific equipment, cost of that truck and its maintenance, parking, dispatching/scheduling, accounting (someone has to send the bills), in office support -- someone has to schedule him, keep parts in stock, etc. And there are taxes on his payroll, business taxes and permits, medical insurance for him and insurance on the business. And there's the real estate cost of the brick and mortar where that support and those spare parts live.
During my last years consulting (roughly 10 years ago), I was billing myself at $150 - $175/hr. I paid to attend trade shows, product training sessions, etc. I could have any computer and software on my desk, and test equipment in the closet that I was willing write the check for. And most of those expenses cited above.

Hi All,
 
I’ve monitored the meeting location discussion from a distance (San Diego), and sympathize with those who would love to attend but can’t stomach the commute.  When I lived in Los Gatos and worked in Palo Alto, getting to NCDXC meetings at the Palo Alto airport and/or locations between Redwood City and San Jose was tolerable, but I was amazed that some folks, like Jay and Jan O’Brien, drove all the way from Sacramento.  Now that I’m 35 miles from the SDDXC meeting site, I too “feel the pain”.  I even joined the SCDXC, but there’s no way I’m going to make it to West LA (100+ miles) for meetings…..there’s got to be a better way !
 
Moving meeting spots around has been suggested, but it’s difficult to reserve restaurants sporadically, and some of the OT’s, like me, may lose track of what-where-when.  How about multiple, simultaneous meetings (MSM).  Isn’t Silicon Valley the Networking Capital of the World (Cisco Systems, et al ) ?  We attend meetings mainly for camaraderie.  If a dozen or so of our friends show up, a good time is had by all…right?  With a webcam, a screen and a black box or two, meetings in Marin, East Bay and South Bay could be synched….everyone jolly; tolerable commutes; a larger, more cohesive club….wunderbar. 
 
This may be too advanced for anywhere outside Silicon Valley, but you guys have the talent to lead the way !
 
Dick,  W6KH

Hi Paul,

 

Yes, passion... the pot, it be stirred good this time.  ;o)  I score that as a good thing as the apparent opinions that needed to be expressed are somewhat varied, yet opposing views.  I enjoy lively discussions, as long as all sides listen to the other viewpoints.  For your QOTW, jackpot!  Well done.

 

Myopic?  Absolutely as this list has been the sole source of my club news input (other than the newsletter which tends to be somewhat filtered and neutral in opinions [yay team, rah], but still interesting).  Apparently I didn’t make that clear; my bad.

 

I didn’t mean to imply that the club was facing imminent failure; just that one of the warning signs is appearing.  The biggest warning sign that I’ve seen before that appears on this list (I’m an outsider peering in and relatively new here too) are: “We’re fine as we are; we don’t need to consider X, Y or Z” (maybe, but discussion clears the air and confirms the position[s] for folks to accept or attempt to change or they move on).  Tunnel view rigidity kills.

 

Maybe I’m wrong, I actually hope so.  I don’t mind that (well not TOO often at least).  Discussion brings knowledge, perhaps a new opinion or outlook.

 

Obviously, some members are (not) happy or the discussion wouldn’t be so lively.

 

I try to be positive in my approach (it sometimes works out that way).  The discussion has degraded into two camps: (Don’t) move the meetings; a binary choice.  Flexibility is something we all lose over time but it’s fatal for a club.  You’ve traveled up here, you grok that.  I’ve tried to offer other meeting options here to allow for more attendance but the membership must decide.  Based on that/those decision(s), attendance may change, including mine.

 

Comment:  I rarely see any discussion about the meeting content, except some attaboys (always intriguing, after the fact) on this list.

 

You mentioned status quo (stability), here’s my take on that: While the licensing numbers are higher than ever, hams are still generally an older group; dying off at an increasing rate; status quo for a club isn’t good enough; it’s grow or fail.  Stable isn’t good enough for survival.

 

I’m hoping that the club actively seeks new and younger members for example; many clubs haven’t and fell by the wayside.  The gift of the younger crowd is that they accept new technology faster while the older crowd can show them HOW to use it appropriately (that’s mostly a moral or ethical issue).  That’s a challenge for any group, not just hams or this DXer sub-set of hams.  Maybe I’m soured by too many years of watching ham clubs drift away; I’d like to see ham clubs prosper (for a change).  It always seems to require new energy, which younger blood has in buckets.

 

On the plus side, the club has been around for as long as I’ve been a ham (um, almost 40 years now already?).

 

*I* want to see this club prosper for selfish reasons, it’s a good resource for current DX information (often faster than the DX cluster for example) and I can still learn how to DX more effectively (up to 181 countries in 18 months now, part time).  I still enjoy learning.

 

Providing a wide variety of meeting topics to attract attendees is a significant challenge too; perhaps the newest toy (or software) from a manufacturer; some folks want to spend 15 hours on the care and feeding of ferrite (no, NOT talking about you Jim); others want to see the latest DXpedition video; while some want to talk antennas.  It’s a TOUGH task to satisfy the needs of the membership and keep it fresh, lively and new (interesting).

 

A challenge:  What does THIS club offer that is so enticing that someone new eagerly DESIRES to be a member?  Elmering (HOW to DX appropriately, radio operation styles etc), local assistance (tower raising, antenna parties) and technical (radio control settings, antenna questions and other similar things) are only part of the package and it will vary from person to person.

 

Basically, the club has to be Visalia, extended and enhanced, with a video game twist.

 

A meeting is the one place that allows personal interaction most effectively.  Making that meeting available to more people (over time) creates more interaction, therefore increased learning and growth.  Rotating sites and combining meetings with other groups are excellent ways of increasing membership (or attendance), although attending is obviously a challenge to some (including me).  I hope that happens; a South Bay (only) evening meeting is simply not viable for me.  I submit that Sacramento has direct major freeways from all members areas listed previously (hint hint).  It merits a review (don’t blame traffic, it’s at least as bad in the Bay Area).

 

Can we better announce the meetings (and the current topic/demonstration) here on the list as well as the newsletter?  At least it might remind me to make more of an effort to attend (more so if the topic tickles the interest spot).

 

Thanks for the podium, yah I talk a lot once I get rolling ;o)

 

Rick wa6nhc

It's great that there is so much passion and dialog about the NCDXC and our direction.

 

As a current, and hopefully future Board member (elections are next month) I will take an action item to work with the Board and I would like to coordinate some possible future meetings in the East Bay and North Bay, possibly in partnership with the NCCC  and/or REDXA. This could be beneficial to every member.   Due to traffic, work hours etc, these special meetings may need to be scheduled on a Saturday to allow for travel.

 

Rick, I cannot agree with you about your perceptions of the NCDXC and your concern about "imminent failure". The NCDXC is doing very well. Our meetings are well attended and there is great interest in what we are doing, even by non-members such as yourself. To make judgements about us as an organization based solely on what you read on NCDXC chat might be myopic.

 

I invite you to join us and to attend what meetings you can, when and where you can. What the club needs going forward are candidates for President and VP. These are vital roles and we need interested candidates. This is the best way to champion change and to work in a collaborative fashion with the NCDXC board to make it happen. The Club's election committee is seeking those interested in officer roles for our June elections.

 

Clearly there is a desire for some changes. Some of them are quite reasonable and attainable and others pose significant changes and a disruption of the status quo.

 

Keep the good ideas flowing!

On Apr 10, 2021, at 9:59 PM, Rick NK7I <rick.nk7i@gmail.com> wrote:

Kevin et al,

What worked tonight (without an interfering signal):

The C92RU tone was 481 (much better than the 281 last week); on the K3
Set the shift as low as it'll go (.40), width set to 700-760 (leave room for more streams, null the second hump) so what you see is a single mound, with the signal(s) on top (I printed 3 streams on 80M tonight).

When they got to +10 (and after I finally logged them), I put the settings back to normal and they were no longer copyable.  Set them low and tight, they were still there.  Night and day.

So even without other signals to compete (which wouldn't be heard this method at all), narrowing the bandwidth made ALL the difference  (yah, yah, I hear the DUH, lol).  Now I wonder what it might do with an RX array, not just the 80M GP.

The notch filter setting by itself made a large difference (brought the C92RU team WAY up), even with the bandwidth at 4K, but the two settings together; magic.  The report, stupid high, who cares?

I was focused on making the contact, didn't look at the S meter, there wasn't much of a trace on the P3.  Downside, is if you want to be polite and not share a tone, you have to open wide, then narrow down.

Step by step, tricks are learned.  73,
Rick NK7I

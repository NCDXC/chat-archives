A few years back there was an effort such as this which nearly resulted in a disastrous bill that fortunately was derailed.

I don't know if the bills you are citing are reruns of the disastrous ones the last time.

I am puzzled that ARRL itself hasn't put out a press release about them.

I would advise everyone to be very cautious about blasting off letters to congress on your own.

On 2025-09-17 14:49, Jeff Miller via Chat wrote:

Hi All, (at the risk of redundancy)

 

Your action is needed to help hams that are in HOAs (home owners associations with deed restrictions) and can't set up antenna.

 

ARRL makes it super easy to tell your elected representatives in Washington that we want them to support H.R. 1094 and S. 459, bills that claw back our lost right to erect amateur antennas outside or on our residences.

 

Even if you are not an ARRL member, just go to https://send-a-letter.org/hoa/, put in your call sign, make any edits you want, and push the SEND button.  ARRL does all the work to deliver your letter directly to your representatives in Washington.

 

-Jeff AJ6LG

 

More clips on the back story:

"Federal law — since 1996 — has guaranteed to every American — except Amateur Radio Operators — the right to erect antennas outside or on their residences for the purpose of TV reception, satellite TV and internet access, wireless internet access, and even wireless internet redistribution. But Amateur Radio operators are denied the equal right to erect comparable antennas."

 

"To eliminate these private land use restrictions, the ARRL has worked over the past several years with our elected ofﬁcials to draft Congressional legislation — H.R. 1094 in the U.S. House of Representatives and S. 459 in the U.S. Senate — that, when passed will extend to all Hams the right to operate from their homes and the right to install antennas on the land they own. We have bipartisan support for this legislation"

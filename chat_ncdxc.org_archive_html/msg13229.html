Ed,

Unfortunately, this is more the normal practice.

DXpeditions really need to take a dedicated 160m operator with them. It takes a special person to listen to noise and only work 10-15 stations (or less) and hour. The other problem is that every night is not the same. I chased ZD8 for a week with ESP signals, then one night he was S9+ for 15 minutes.

XX9D was very similar. They were only on the first night - worked 1 WA, 1 OR and a few CA stations. They had a great signal, but obviously their receiving was not up to par with their transmitting. Don't know if this first night was normal propagation or not.

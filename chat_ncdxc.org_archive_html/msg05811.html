Very good, thanks. I sort of remember Petrus having the signal and path like working VP8ORK or South Shetlands - or Ascension - more so than say, Marion Island - which was much harder. Of course, Petrus had 100 watts and a vertical, and ZS8M had a little more power - but a broad banded inverted fee, so was also not the same as say a big amplifier and big antenna DX-ped. 

Tromelin on the other hand - will probably be best for the West Coast morning grey line via the LP. 

Funny how much different propagation is one side or the other from South Africa.

While dreaming about a scientist / ham / DX-er showing up at Norway's new Bouvet base and activating it for say, a year (heh heh), I started wondering if Bouvet's propagation is more like South Africa - or something more along the lines of Tristan de Cunha / Gough or Ascension. I did hear Petrus when he was on Bouvet, and while he had a vertical and 100 watts, by the time I could call him - he said he was having a terrible wind storm and had to shut down. It seemed to me at the time that propagation was a bit "easier" from the West Coast beaming on the Short Path. Of course the big issue would be along the lines of what happened with PT0S - NA, SA, AF and EU could all hear them and work them equally strong, and so it didn't seem like the West Coast had it too easy. Luckily I already had St. Peter and Paul Rocks on several bands - and chose not to even bother with PT0S. Bouvet would have similar "howling" and nasty pileups.
Your thoughts?

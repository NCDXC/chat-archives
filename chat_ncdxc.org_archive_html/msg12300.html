Thanks for helping! I'll just make a few quick comments, and then we should probably take any further discussion off the reflectors. My thanks to the clubs for the temporary use of their email systems.
As far as I know, the problems I'm having hearing NPOTA stations are entirely due to having a simple vertical antenna that's barely peeking above the roofline of my house--and of course, the houses here have metal frames. As I mentioned, I have an idea for getting the wire into the trees that I'd be happy to talk over in more detail in private email. Someone here may have a better idea!
Rick and Mark, yes, the MFJ tuner I bought will indeed create an end-fed antenna system very similar to that LNR product you mentioned.
I know this is a frustrating compromise in terms of antennas, but it's really all I can do given the circumstances. At worst, it will perform no better than the Hustler but will be available to me all the time instead of just on weekends. But I have every hope that in fact it will be better than that.

If Patty's problem is hearing, either she is limited by her
receiver noise or is limited by noise coming in.  If the
former, a wire having more efficiency might help.  Otherwise,
she is probably wasting her time in terms of receiving.
However, it's always good to get out better.

As far as getting the wire up, I would think that a telescoping
fiberglass fishing rod type support could be hidden within the
foliage of the trees.  This doesn't require anyone to climb
except maybe onto the carport roof.

The proposed plan entirely depends on the carport working as
an untuned counterpoise.  Also, certain wire lengths may
not tune on certain bands, or may not be able to handle
the RF power involved.  An alternate solution is to use
an LMRPrecision end fed half wave wire.  See www.LMRprecision.com.
It doesn't need a counterpoise.

The Hustler on a tripod could be greatly improved by mounting
a decent screwdriver antenna on the roof of a vehicle that is
parked in the driveway (not under the carport).  I don't see how the HOA
could say anything about that.  If you put a 102 inch whip on top
of the screwdriver, it will get out well.  Of course, that gives
the option of driving the vehicle to a quieter area.
KH6DX/W6 used this strategy to make 160 meter DXCC mobile.

Rick N6RK

Hi, guys.

Do you know anyone who might be willing to help me install an end-fed wire
in a row of trees at my house?

I'm encountering a lot of frustration trying to work NPOTA stations.
Most of my contacts have been at the ESP level. Almost daily, I hear some
other ham in the South Bay work an NPOTA station that I can't even hear, so
the current situation with a Hustler on a tripod on my porch is very
discouraging. :-( Plus, I can only put the Hustler up on weekends when the
mobile home park management and maintenance people aren't around to spot it.

I had a really nice talk with Norm Fusaro W3IZ in Newington a few weeks ago,
and he suggested an end-fed wire antenna with an MFJ auto-tuner. So I bought
those and asked someone in a local ham club if he knew anyone who could help
me install everything. About three weeks ago, he said he'd found a couple of
people willing to help, but he's still not heard back from them about when
they might be available. Although NPOTA runs until the end of the year, I
expect activity to drop off dramatically in the next 4-6 weeks as people
wrap up their summer vacations, so I really want to get the antenna up in
the next week or so if at all possible.

I'm hoping that among the three of you, you might know a couple of people
below Medicare age who'd be willing to climb a ladder next to my carport. I
think two other people plus me would do the trick.
Heck, maybe even one other person and I could do it. I have a friend in Napa
who usually visits me quite frequently, but she's been tied up with work for
the past couple of months. And all my neighbors are even older than I am.
:-)

I'm attaching an annotated photo of the antenna site. At the far end of the
carport, the wire would come down from the trees and attach to the tuner
lying atop the carport; I'd attach the counterpoise connector on the tuner
to the carport. Then a coax feed would run from the tuner into the house via
the window at the far end of the carport. I have an idea about how to get
the wire into the trees, but of course I'd be very grateful for any advice
the three of you might have, since you all have experience with such things.

Thanks in advance for any ideas you have on this!

>>>> On 5/11/2024 07:50, Paul N6PSE via Chat wrote:
>>> As your Pacific Div DXAC Representative, I wanted to update you on a recent matter.
>>> 
>>> The DXAC has received a number of requests to consider Jarvis Island as a separate and new DXCC entity.
>>> 
>>> Below is a summary of the DXAC's finding/recommendation.
>>> 
>>> Paul N6PSE
>>> Pacific Division DXAC
>>> 
>>> Date: 28 April, 2024
>>> 
>>> Subject: Additional Jarvis considerations.
>>> 
>>> At his request, Don Greenbaum (N1DG) was allowed to address the DXAC on initial findings regarding his request to consider Jarvis (currently KH5) a new “entity”. Don's request labeled “The case for Jarvis A rebuttal of DXAC determination” was distributed to the committee  prior to his presentation and discussion on several points were held via the reflector.
>>> 
>>> In summary, Don's presentation revolved around several points of contention: 1) Exclusive economic zones (EEZ) surrounding Jarvis, Palmyra and Kingman. 2) Bathymetry of surrounding waters. 3) The 2013 agreement between the U.S. and Kiribati on the sale of Palmyra. 4)  The 2009 establishment proclaiming the separation of Jarvis and Palmyra regarding the National Wildlife Reserves.
>>> 
>>> Concluding arguments referenced bringing “energy and excitement” to the program. The committee overwhelmingly felt that the aforementioned arguments did not specifically address any DXCC rules that are currently in place and ones used to come to our initial conclusion (which was presented to the league and PSC in January of 2024). We also felt that additional entities would indeed be a welcome addition to the program but that the current guidelines should continue to be used in making such determinations. It was also noted that if additional entities are to be considered, maybe the current guidelines should be reviewed allowing such considerations in the future.
>>> 
>>> We agreed at this time, no further action on our behalf is necessary. The entire DXAC committee holds the utmost respect for Don and their continued appreciation for what he has done for the DX community through the years. We wish him the best on the upcoming Jarvis DXpedition.
>>> 
>>> Respectfully submitted, Chris Shalvoy, K2CS Chairman, ARRL DX Advisory Committee 2

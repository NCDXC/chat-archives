Although Smitty's passing was not unexpected, it is a great loss.

I got to know him over the past several years, especially during the time of our great club efforts in SS--when I was privileged to use his station. I also learned much about his long career of service to his community---as a veteran, pediatric dentist, educator, ham mentor. I learned from a few explosive encounters that he and I were not on the same political page(!), but that he had not only served in the Reagan administration, but had met with the Reagans, Jimmy Carter, Margaret Thatcher, and other movers and shakers. He told a great story about Thatcher and their "dinner" at a local fast-food franchise. He told another about twisting arms to get the Foothill Board to approve the purchase of the land Foothill College occupies at a price that, even allowing for inflation was ridiculously low. He was instrumental in the transformation of the State's junior colleges into full-fledged community colleges that have provided the entry to higher education for many thousands of California students. And how, when his 20m roundtable of medical providers complained that their fully-depreciated medical equipment was headed for landfills, he created an organization to ship these still-viable machines to third world venues where they were desperately needed.

Smitty was, above all, a committed public citizen, with strong convictions and the ability to get things done. This trumps personal politics, and I admire him greatly for these achievements. He had a long and useful life---who could ask for more?

Garry, NI6T

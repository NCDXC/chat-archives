I think that the real answer is that there is no “one size fits all” in radios.  There are many applications that require different features.

 

A couple of points that haven’t been mentioned are:

 

The TS-590S has an USB port on it that not only gives you a virtual serial (CAT) control COM port on your computer but it also contains a sound card interface.  You can do PSK or RTTY without an external box.  The K3 only has an RS-232 port.

 

The K3 firmware supports the “twin” mode, one radio can control another.  With RemoteRig and a K3/0 this makes a great remote station rig (that is how I am using mine).  The TS-590 does not support “twinning”.

 

Now to my wish list:  The USB port on the TS-590 is something that every modern radio should have as many of the newer computers do not have serial ports any more.  However I would like to see the USB port also support a K1EL, WinKey keyer style interface and a COM port for FSK keying.  Then it would be a complete package.  Of course for remote operation, the radio should also have an Ethernet port which would support all of the above for remote operation.  All of this is a natural for a high powered SDR rig and I believe that the Flex 6000 will eventually support it all.

 

With CC&Rs, apartment and condo living many hams will be looking towards remote operation in the future.

 

The bottom line for me is that the Flex will probably be my next major purchase but maybe I will get lucky and win one at Visalia! 

 

73,

- Craig, AE6RR

 

Full Disclosure: I have never operated a TS-590S.  I own a K3/100 and a K3/0, a TS-2000, a TS-480 and a SDR-IQ.  I have owned a FT-1000MP.

Craig makes some pretty good points about the Kenwood TS-590 vs the Elecraft K3. The TS-590 in my opinion is the "best bang for the buck" currently within medium sized & priced radios. While I still prefer the K3's (I own two of them) I used the TS-590 at 7O6T. It was a good rig but my audio preference is for the K3. The TS-590 has a harsh digital sound whereas I like the warm analog sound of the K3.

 

For DXpedition purposes, more and more DXpeditions will be giving the TS-590 a serious look because not only is it a performer, but with the cost significantly less than the K3 it is a really attractive option for a DXpedition looking to bring 5-7 identical radios. I'm not in the market for a new radio, but if I was, I would have to give the TS-590 serious consideration.

Besides -- if you want to consider the K3, anyone with even the slightest of assembly skills can buy the K3 as a kit  for a saving of about 15%.  You've got stuff on your bill of materials that are not needed, like the mic and KBPF.  Virtually any decent mic works with a K3 or KX3, and the KBPF makes it a general coverage RX.  And the roofing filters you put their are extra options that are most likely not on the Kenwood. Another "by the way" -- both the K3 and KX3 can decode CW, RTTY, and PSK31 directly on their display and send RTTY and PSK from a paddle or from a memory. Think about the convenience of that when you're trying to quickly work a DXpedition on RTTY!  Program one memory with your call, another with 599TU, and  you're ready to rol!  During WPX RTTY this weekend, I found that my K3 consistently decodes stuff that MMTTY with a very good sound card misses, and W6GJB tells me that the decoders in the KX3 are even better.

 

[Craig] Yes, the decoding in the K3 is nice, I used mine in the WPX RTTY also this last weekend and I found the dual band pass filtering and the RTTY tuning indicator to be particularly useful.  I still want to use MMTTY though as the display scrolls by too fast in the limited space on the K3’s LCD.

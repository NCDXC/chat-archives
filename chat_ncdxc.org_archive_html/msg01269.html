Hi All!

I thought this one through quite a bit and thought I would share my thought process with you all since its all fresh.  The value of the KX3 sort of sold itself... so I pulled the trigger.

Pro's 
- Reciever performance equal or exceeds that of K3 in some respects(wow)
- integrated IQ out (no need for expensive Pan adapter for a bandscope/waterfall/skimmer)and enhanced SDR capability
- excellent local support (not to mention in the bay area UPS ground from Watsonville is as fast as fedex, mine arrived 23 hours after the mouseclick)
- totally useable as a home dxing or contesting rig with soon to be released 100w PA and amp of choice
- inegrated dual watch (not nearly as powerful as a K3 subreciever) but still.. wow.. great stock support to chase the operator up the pile!
- you can throw batteries in it and take it on the road or whatever
- limited K3 remote control ability (more functionality promised)  (read > mobile remote :)  .. you can have HF in your BMW now with an air card and a remote rig and no big ugly antenna.. work that dxpedition on the way to dinner!)
- easy integration with many K3 accesories you may have allready
- with elecraft updates, the radio will be kept up to date for years to come.

Con's
Its a bit small
the menus are a bit more intensive and cramped because its a bit small
cable managment may be a bit cumbersome and visually unapealing since it comes off the side
with the amp it will come in 2 peices...  and you may need a tuner out in front of the new amp

but the clincher

Assembled KX3 with antenna tuner and dual passband roofing filter 
$1300 (mic and paddle would be extra.. but same with the k3)
assuming the amp will be equal(ish) in price to the ten tec 100w, may come in at $700 
grand total
$2000

assembled K3/100 with antenna tuner and sub receiver and some filters.. essentially loaded k3 to do most of what the KX3 does (some better.. some not so much)

$3909 (ouch and mine outfitted for contesting is even more)

All of a sudden that KX3 makes alot of sense for a Powerful DXing station rig..at a great value.. with alot of added benefits you just wont find in the kenwood. especially if you can live(or even be glad to have) the small form factor. (btw I like the Kenwood also..its a sweet little rig and those who have purchased them should be quite happy with them...but for me.....).

Having an elecraft front end sensitivity and noise reduction capabilies from my QTH was a game changer for me in the DX chasing game from the qth. period.

73 C U in the pile ups on the ol' K3 or the new KX3!

Chris
N6WM

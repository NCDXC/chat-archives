Have some observations on recent or ongoing dxpeditions.  Not a criticism, nor am I picking on them, I appreciate all of them and their efforts but observations.  Of course I've never been on a dxpedition to deal with the situations they have either (on my bucket list - I would love to operate from Johnston Isl if I could do so w/o getting blown up or irradiated): 

 

T22T:  Some said on this already, but lack of consistent 73's, and operating in standard FT8 frequencies has overcrowded the bands and even using F/H within the band instead of on one side or another.  I just stopped bothering with this one.  Hardly identifies too.

ZD9W:  Just getting started but again operating within standard FT8 bands, no F/H, and operating Odd.  Band is super crowded and QSO rate is slow.  Also not always decoding despite a decent signal;  could be due to another operator overlapping his frequency.  And many calling on the wrong sequence.  They are there till Oct. 22 so hopefully this will change.

ZL7IO:  A top notch operator dealing with equipment situations he had.  One of the best CW operators I've ever heard.

VK9LAA:  Another class act (wish for more CW though), operating F/H outside the standard band and fairly fast.

6W/IV3FSG: Elvira, a good and experienced op.  Her CW QSO rate is higher than her FT8.  I notice she starts within standard FT8 band, when it gets too crowded she moves out.  On CW, answer up 1.5-2 typically.  Also, she registers the QSO if you just send your report instead of standard calling.  For me, again a lot of decodes I never got, but saw the QSO on her livestream.  She does not use F/H within the standard FT8 band.

 

FT8 in general:  I've noticed during the last year the bands are getting more crowded as more people adopt FT8. Is that my imagination?  Many times I've seen the DX QRM'd with someone on top of them from the states or EU.  I saw this happen to a lot of DX, 5A1AL for example, such that I didn't decode his 73 due to QRM;  he emailed me that he got me, hi hi.  As conditions improve this will happen more often.  It would be nice if US based stations stayed out of <1KHz area and leave that for DX.  The QRM that happens is most likely not deliberate.  You can't always see/hear stations on the frequency you might occupy and visa versa.  I'm not smart/knowledgeable enough to figure out how to avoid that.  The DX station is going to see different stations on the band that we do.  So when operating outside the standard FT8 and using F/H, I pay attention to a QSO he just 73'd and that person's initial frequency and I occupy that instead of looking for a blank spot that may not be blank on their end.  That's my technique at least I use now, to get a clear frequency and not QRM someone accidently.  Am I off base anywhere?

 

73, Paul, W6IBU

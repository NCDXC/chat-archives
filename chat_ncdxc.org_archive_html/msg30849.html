Which would bring us into the clean(er) transmitter arena.  And WAY off topic.
I'm positive that some of the 'shoulders' (sidebands, IMD) seen on some signals make it MUCH more difficult to decode nearby tones on crowded bands (i.e. ZD9W with the lidfest on top of Yuris). Their overlap exceeds the 50 Hz bandwidth of the wanted signal, in addition to simple fundamental overlap.  (For an example, watch someone strong that is running multistream, the edges are not sharp but spotless steep cliffs in single stream.)
Not all transmitters are set correctly, nor are they all 'clean'.  I've seen my K4 on test gear and it's really clean; other brands... not so good.  Poorly set up (using a computer sound card and sending unwanted audio, like the TV show you're watching) and a bad (overdriven) TX takes up a LOT more real estate and helps nothing.
The recent Flex fiasco sending spiked across the band (some clearly haven't updated firmware yet) adding more 'stuff'... the list is endless of causes.
We can do better and should.  We all win that way.  The FEC in JT modes can only do so much and sloppy transmitters make it harder for everyone within range.  We CAN do better.
The original thought of phase shifting over the path comes from the old HF RTTY days, with other 'features' like selective fading where one (of 2) tones, falls out.  Fun times!

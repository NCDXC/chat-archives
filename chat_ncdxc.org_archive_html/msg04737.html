Hey Rusty,
 
I'm part a group of engineers up in San Francisco that have done a few short distance balloon launches to collect video (we helped out with a high altitude Citibank commercial, a BBC documentary called Earth's Wild Ride - here's a link to one video we took with a GoPro: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UAifzh7_-cg ). Presently, we're working on a high altitude balloon system to make at least one revolution around the world. 
 
Previously, our launches have included a SPOT tracker in the payload, as a means of tracking/recovery for our 2-3 hour flights, although we're interested in a receiving slightly higher fidelity telemetry during this around the world flight.  While we're all techies, no one in the group has real COMM experience (although 2 of us just got our amateur licenses and just started getting familiar with the field).  We've also played a bit with the Micro-Trak 300 a bit as well to get familiar with APRS. 
 
Our present payload design (shooting for under ~5 lbs) consists of a microcontroller to collect analog and digital data telemetry, a small power system (solar cells/battery), and a TBD Comm system.  Our general COMM system requirements are such that we want to receive flight telemetry (temps, pressure, battery voltage, GPS, etc) on the ground regularly, and then we want the payload to be able receive at least a single "cut-down" command to the balloon.
 
Our thought was to program the APRS AX.25 protocol onto the microcontroller and pipe that to a ~5W transmitter.  Since we'll be following the jet stream in the Northern Hemisphere, we were thinking that we would transmit at the four international APRS frequencies in that path (either with a transmitter that gets reconfigured when we're over a geographic area associated with that frequency, or iteratively output across all 4 frequencies).  We don't have a good sense of how we should best transmit the cut down command from the ground.
 
We were wondering:
1.                       If there are any major holes in this general design (or large obstacles that seem objectively daunting) 
2.                       If there are easier ways to accomplish what our initial requirements (listed above)
3.                       Any specific hardware recommendations you might have (transmitters/receivers on either end)
4.                       If you would be willing/able to discuss some of these items in the near future with us over the phone or skype - (we assume that would be much easier as opposed to a lengthy email exchange)
Thanks again for any advice you can offer - hope to talk with you soon,
 
Luis

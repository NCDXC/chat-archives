All,

 

NARCC (Northern Amateur Relay Council of California) is the repeater coordinating group for Northern California.   They recently sent the following email to all members:


Every year, half of the NARCC Board comes up for election. This year we have six openings. Five Board Seats will be for the full two-year term and one that will be for about a year. Four of the existing Board Members are willing to re-run for office, leaving us with two open seats. Being a Board Member typically involves attending four Board Meetings and one Membership Meeting every year. We need your thoughts and opinions. Please send an email to election@narcc.net to sign-up.

 

If you are the owner/operator/trustee of a repeater, I hope you’re a member of NARCC.  And I hope you’ll strongly consider running for a board position.

 

If you don’t have a repeater, you can still join NARCC as an associate member and run for a position on the board.  I was originally nominated for and elected to the board before I had my own repeater.  That was based on the work I did with others on proving that the band plan they were proposing in 2009 would not work.

 

Speaking of band plans, there is once again an effort afoot to get NARCC to restructure the band plan to make better (some might say “special”) accommodations for digital repeaters.  One proposal involves using frequencies outside the accepted repeater portion of the band plan.  It’s a topic that stokes many people’s anxiety and raises their blood pressure.  Lots of folks complain on both sides of the issue.  But complaining doesn’t solve anything.  What we need are people who are willing to run for board positions and/or serve on committees and actually do the work properly.  So, I hope you’ll consider stepping up.

 

As the quoted email above explains, to run for election you’ll need to let them know at the email above.  I don’t know what the cut-off date is, but I think it’s very soon.

 

Michael, N6MEF

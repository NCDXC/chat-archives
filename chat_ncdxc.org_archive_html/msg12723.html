I never heard or worked the TA, but there were definitely some strange propagation things going on. At one point when 15 was closing to NA I had tremendous backscatter that made sending by hand impossible with QSK. Despite that I was still working NA stations through it, but getting a lot of JA calls at the same time. I also saw very odd scattering / path skewing at various times although my little 3 element isn't very sharp. Generally, they didn't last long.

73, Alan  AD6E / KH6TU



On Mon, Nov 28, 2016 at 7:17 AM, Jeff Kinzli N6GQ <jeff@n6gq.com> wrote:
There was a lot of skew going on over the weekend and some real
anomalies.  This was a really cool contest from that perspective IMHO.
Not saying the TA was real, but there were some odd things happening.

Notable ones I experienced were JT5DX on 40M at our sunset - S9+20 and
easy in daylight (over a daylight path), but VERY fluttery and coming
direct short path. I worked him at 0049Z, which I believe is 08:49AM
local there, and almost right smack dab on top of our sunset.
Awesome.

Another was VQ9X on 20M in the morning, no copy direct shortpath, but
swinging around to NE he came out of the noise. It was apparent that
the east coast guys were having a real hard time with that one, they
were having trouble working him, sending really slow, etc.

73 de N6GQ

Over the years, I've learned that it's good practice to avoid the use of switching power supplies anywhere around the house and shack. Every piece of electronics we buy nowadays seems to come with a wall wart that's a switcher, and I've learned to dig an old linear supply to replace them. I simply break the cables for both old and new warts in the middle, install Power Poles on the linear supply and the connector going the equipment, and toss the switcher in the trash.
The problem is that each of the six Panasonic walk-around phones that came with the set from Costco has a little switcher to recharge it, and I've run out of linears of suitable voltage rating. In addition to suggesting that members might want to do this themselves, I'd like to buy or swap for a half dozen or so linear warts in the 4.5 - 6VDC range.
Two ways to tell if it's a linear or a switcher. First, linears tend to be a lot heavier and larger for a given current rating. Second, I take my 2M talkie that has HF RX capability and hold it next to the wart. If I hear noise increase, it's a switcher. This would also work with any portable AM RX that has a loopstick antenna, tuned to an empty channel near the top of the AM broadcast band.

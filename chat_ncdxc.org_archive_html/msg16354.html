I heard a DX station on 15 CW Friday morning that I needed for a first ever CW QSO on that band.  He came back N6? but a K3 called over me and got the QSO.  The DX then went QRT or QSY and I did not find him.  Since I had never worked that entity and maybe never heard one on 15 CW, I was disappointed.  So, I did something I never did before: Sent an email and asked if he planned to be back on the band so I could listen for another chance.  He replied Saturday AM and suggested we make a sked.  He asked me what other bands I needed and I said 30 and 12M.  So, he asked me to meet him on 30 M that day, after dark, and I got a QSO.  He was S9 and I expected a weak signal!  I doubted there would be propagation and was wrong.

Next morning, yesterday, he suggested we try 15 and then 12.  At 2030Z, we met on 15, made an easy QSO and then QSY’d to 12M and made another.  He was Q5 for both bands.  I thought 12 was a lost cause and learned that bands may not be dead when you think they are!.  (I called CQ today on 12, 15 and 17 M CW this morning with no success.  But I tried!)

I do not plan to make skeds to fill slots but this kind of dropped in my lap.  I had never heard this entity on these bands.

Am I the only one to make this kind of QSO?

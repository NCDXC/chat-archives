This email is directed at those contesters and DXers who responded to my help request for my K3/Microham RTTY problem.

That the system worked fine and then suddenly did not work on RTTY brought a lot of suggestions and did suggest to me that perhaps something had been unintentionally disconnected, either partially or fully or that a setting had been changed. All the obvious settings candidates were cleared. I had a series of exchanges with TV Bob, who strongly felt that my Router setup was non-viable, despite its having worked flawlessly for a long time.

Today I noted that my 'puter was running sluggishly so I applied Matt's Principle and rebooted Windows. Suddenly the symptoms were gone and RTTY operation was normal--using the same settings I had used for a long time. I strongly suspect that the line to PTT IN on the rear deck of the K3 may have partially pulled out. The rear deck connectors on my K3 are pretty much all employed and it is difficult to visually inspect the RCA and mini-stereo  plugs, but I felt that the PTT IN RCA plug may have been not fully inserted. That at least could explain the loss of functionality. I have had a lot of trouble in the past with the LINE IN and LINE OUT connectors not staying fully inserted.

Bob appears to understand the M/H Router pretty well, so I cannot explain why the existing setup works when Bob says it should not, but the fact that it worked previously had to carry some weight.

Thank you all--and especially TV Bob-- for trying to help out. I need now to go find some RTTY QSO's.

73,

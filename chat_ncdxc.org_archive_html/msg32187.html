With a modern radio and some amp added; what you hear is what you can work.  This is true when I was on simple wire antennas and it's true now with even better antennas (but I hear a LOT more now, mostly because it's directional and I'm heard at legal band limit much better).

Being heard well is simple, if too weak, add gain (antennas and/or output).  With the exception of some pileups, the what I can hear, I work rule, holds true; I'm that confident now.  And I can bust a fair amount of pileups too (being loud, a dash of skill for timing and place with some good fortune).

The problem is always what you can hear, not always in being heard (but that problem is at both ends of the contact, each much be louder than the other noise floor).

On ANY station, lower that noise floor.  You can't work them, if you can't hear them; prop is only a means to make it happen.  Important, but your station needs to be noise clean.

Which takes us off of the main thread; but hearing better is a critical point, THAT is where the time and energy needs to be spent while prop does what prop does (and it's randomized too).

And then, there is always one way prop, where the return path of a signal, is inadequate.  THAT is REAL.

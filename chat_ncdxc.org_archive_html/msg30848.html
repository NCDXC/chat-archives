To the extent that FT8/FT4 signaling is sensitive to phase shift, your thinking is plausible, particularly if the path is passing over or near either magnetic pole. (I’m not familiar enough with the underlying encoding scheme used in FT8/4 to speak knowledgeably about it’s phase sensitivity.) 

So-called “polar flutter” (very noticeable and obvious on CW signals coming via the polar path) causes dramatic phase shifts in the signal. For this reason, PSK is essentially useless on the polar path, while RTTY (FSK) works just fine. CW works because our brains can “filter out” the flutter.

That said, the path to ZD9 is trans-equatorial, and should not be affected by polar magnetic flux.

Hi Lucas,

I agree, now to calculate the coax, switch and lightning arrestor losses to the antenna then turn that into RF Power lost to see what to set the transmitter, ugh.

I am still reading the fine print of the document…

See page 18 paragraph 32 to page 24 paragraph 45 for the FCCs reasoning for the new limits.

The NTIA stated EIRP but the FCC is continuing the use of ERP in its regulations.

So, 9.15 Watts ERP to a dipole is the new limit for the new band of frequencies on 60 meters.

At least they kept the 100 Watt limit for the channelized frequencies.

I presume that the new frequencies are now authorized for use since the document is dated 12/09/2025.

Fun times…

I was referencing the old frequency allocations. I don’t push those to the max as is. ERP depends on the type of antenna (vertical here) and line loss calculations need to be done to do the math, I figure when 15w out from the radio, likely half reaches anyway, 150ft run. Yes agreed, would be nice to be able to do the same power, but I suspect most traffic will remain on the FT8 frequency. However, since some other international allocations do not match the US channelized system, until now, this will make it easier to DX on 60m, even if only with a few Watts.

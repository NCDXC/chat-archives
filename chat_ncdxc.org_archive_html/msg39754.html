On May 21, 2025, at 12:06 PM, Rick nk7i via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

He would lose callers because some couldn't decode him that wide; there is a 9-10 dB hit in signal strength in SF mode.  He's 3 streams now at +20 here; I could afford the dB hit but EU (many calling) perhaps not.

IF the station is heard LOUD, SF is no problem; he would lose callers because he could potentially work at a higher rate.


Yes, this is the standard tradeoff for SuperFox. Generally, stations will run SF first to clear out all of the “big guns” or even little guns with good ears. When the rate drops, then they often switch to ordinary FT8, using 1-3 streams as appropriate.

I just suggested it because Allen called me directly yesterday (email and telephone), saying that he was asked by many ops to get a SuperFox key to increase his rate when the band is open. I was able to give him a key almost immediately, and post it on the authentication server within minutes. 

Of course, he should use it or not as he sees fit. I just saw a large group of callers, and thought that SF would be an easy way to thin the herd. (But I worked him on ordinary FT8 anyway.)

I don't understand the lack of 17m signals, either.

VU3WIJ was amazingly strong today. S9 via short path and S9+10 on long path.

Around 0130Z I called CQ on 17m pointed LP to VU7. In 15 minutes I received no responses to my CQs. Hard to assess whether this was due to lack of listeners or propagation. The only signal I heard on the band was T32RC.

Around the same VU7AG did have a good signal on 30m. They were quickly QRM'd by the pileup from 6V7D. I did hear a local, NE7D, work them on 30m yesterday around the same time.

I've only been able to work them on 20m - ssb, cw and rtty. cw will put me on the cw Honor Roll.

On Fri, Dec 6, 2013 at 2:17 AM, Garry Shapiro <garry@ni6t.com> wrote:
Steve and all,

Here is my 17m post-mortem.

First, the patient died...

The cluster shows VU7AG posted many times from Europe and the east coast on 18145 from at least 1215 to 1418Z---way too early for us---with comments indicating workable signals. Then came a two-hour break, then an appearance on 18075.5 from 1613 to 1907, which was most of the time slot I asked for, but mostly pointing LP, which I did not ask for. At 1635 KI7M in Kiezer, OR posted a comment suggesting he may have heard the team working Europe.

I received an email at about 1907 saying a phone call was being made to the team to turn them SP  The last post on 18075 came from VU7AG, and did say "looking for west coast SP over Japan", but that apparently did not drum up any business. I don't know if anyone was still listening on this end by then. Minutes later, they were back on LP and SSB.

I have no idea if anyone on the west coast succeeded. It is starting to appear that, for most of us, the game is 40/30/20m. I have to say the team is  trying to help us break out of the core bands, but we appear to be on the short end of the stick on this one.

BTW, for VE7CC users: here at NI6T the real-time postings seem not to include what a sh/dx after the fact shows. I think it is because postings are replacing subsequent postings of the same station.

I am at a loss to understand the apparent non-existence of that path.

Garry, NI6T

I built the 3-400Z grounded-grid amp out of the 1965 ARRL Handbook.  Same year I worked Gus in Bhutan cross-mode.  The plate transformer I got from a surplus store, Selectronics in Sacramento.  When I first plugged it in it blew the fuse in my folks’ service panel!  (no circuit breakers then)

I remember watching the filaments glow brighter as I keyed the CW rig.  Worked a couple hundred countries on 20M CW with a ZL-Special pointed at 30 degrees, fixed between an upstairs window and a big deodar cedar in the back yard.  Fun memories!

73,

Wow! That brings back memories! Instead of devoting my life to the mysteries of the opposite sex, I spent the summer of my fifteenth year building a 2x 4-400A amp. All of the issues I faced you guys have brought back in sharp focus. For adequate B+,  I had a giant isolation transformer (5KVA?) that I used for step up/ step down driving another giant plate transformer. I ran the amp in swamped grid AB2. For screen regulation, I had a chain of OA2s (?) IIRC…. It’s been 60+ years, but the regulators glowed blue, or maybe orange, the 866As were blue, and the 4-400s plates glowed cherry red until they gave up the ghost to be replaced with other tubes from the dollar box at the hamfest. 

I loved that amp. 

Thanks for the memories, guys 

73, Bruce K3NQ

I was also planning such a 5-band amp way back and collected some very nice parts for the RF and DC.  Still have a tube on unknown condition.  As Rick said, you have to do the right B+ supply or it will be a bad design and my transformer was sweet, but not the right voltage.  That meant an appropriate Variac or Powerstat which I got to add to the many pounds of goods.

I managed to never get off the ground and sold the parts one by one and made a lot of money on the good ones.  Like the huge edge-wound (226-1?) roller inductor. Most of the important parts are from businesses that closed a long time ago.

On Thu, Jan 8, 2026 at 11:01 AM Richard Karlquist via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

A long time ago when I was young and foolish, I picked up a 4-1000A at a swap meet on the basis of its "mystique".  Turns out the reality was not so great.  I started to build a homebrew amplifier for it but discovered that if I tried to run it in grounded grid (with screen grounded as well) the gain was poor that way unless I ran it with 5,000V on the plate.  It was never designed for that.  Also, standard bandswitched output tanks were not designed for 5,000V  Running it with the filament grounded required a screen voltage supply, and a neutralization circuit.

Anyway, it never got completed.

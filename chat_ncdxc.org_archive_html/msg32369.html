I generally use VE7CC, which works well with just about every logging program, both Mac and Windows. For Windows, he also provides a very powerful application (CCUser) which allows connection to multiple clusters, with EXTENSIVE filtering capabilities, both on the spotter and the spotted stations (and bands/modes/etc.).

For everyday use, I typically list spots from anywhere in the world, but only from spotters in the Western US and Canada (and not for bands I don’t use, such as 60m, 23 cm, etc.) For contesting, I tailor the filters depending on the contest. For example, for WAE CW, I *only* want to see spots of European stations running CW on the contest bands; no need to waste local processing power looking at all the other junk. Similarly, if it’s All Asia, I only want to see spots of Asian stations, in whatever mode that weekend’s contest is in.

VE7CC (and similar cluster software) allows you to store multiple (hundreds) of different filters, so you don’t even have to re-enter the filter data for each contest. Each filter set is stored for a different “suffix” on your call. For example, K6MMM-22 is set up for WAE RTTY; K6MMM-18 (which is in use right now) is for any worldwide contest that is CW-only, such as today;s CQ WW CW. It gives me CW-only spots, of stations anywhere in the world, but only from spotters in the Western US and Canada, and only on the contest bands. I really don’t want to see that a French station is being spotted by a German station; it is unlikely that I will be able to hear the spotted station.

Lots more capability exists than mentioned here, including having my local logging program send me “Alarms” when certain spots show up, e.g., DXCC Entity not worked (either at all, or on a certain band/mode slot), DXpedition spots for band/modes I haven’t worked, etc. My logger can even send me a text message or email for certain spots.

Also, the use of skimmers and the RBN are especially helpful for CW, RTTY, and FT8/4 modes. (Still waiting for a viable SSB skimmer, which is why ARRL is now allowing self-spotting in many contests on SSB.)

In the case of the several DXpeditions of which I have been a member or a leader, what NCDXC might have thought after the fact about what we had accomplished would not have been a big factor either in our own evaluations or in deciding whether to do another.

In any given year, one's effort could have been one of many excellent efforts, or just a few.

As Dylan said, you don't need a weatherman to know which way the wind blows.

IMHO, the commenter here who "got it" was the one who voted for the expedition that got him the most excited. And that depends upon your own objectives and expectations.

Garry, NI6T


On 4/23/2013 8:30 AM, Steve Lund wrote:
PT0S did do what they set out to do. The emphasis was on low bands and 6m. They achieved success in those areas.

Unfortunately, Malpelo is in high demand on all bands. I think that many DXers were expecting a full out all band DXpedition. This would be impossible for a 4 man crew. Their efforts on the other bands left many frustrated.

I'm surprised that there was little support for HK0NA. It took as much work to put that station for West Coast on the top of the mountain as anything done at PT0S. That station was easy to work and loud. It was new for me on CW and RTTY as well as several bands. This was obviously a bigger crew, but it met its objectives and then some. They received my vote.

But, I agree with others that defining the DXpedition of the year is difficult. But, I suspect that receiving such an award is an incentive to do it again.

Steve, K6UM

Dear Truman,

When I was first chasing DX, back in 1961 (yes, the previous millennium!) I used a Gotham V-80 vertical.  No radials, and mounted on the backyard fence in Sacramento.  Like Paul N6PSE, I worked lots of JA, VK, ZL, and South America, but no EU.  Also 40 and 15 meters worked really great into the neighbors’ TV 21.4 MHz IF circuits.  I strung up a 20 meter ZL-Special between the second story window and a tree in the backyard.  It was about 30 feet off the ground.  Got up to over 200 countries with that simple wire beam.  I worked all over EU, the Middle East, and Africa with that antenna, fixed on about 30 degrees east of due north.  All that DX is right over the north pole!  I couldn’t tell by your photos whether those trees could support a 32’ horizontal ZL-Special at 30 feet high. 

It looks like you already have some vertical tubing mounted on the fence in that first picture.  I agree with Paul, that would be a great spot to run radials along the fence each direction.  The roof of the workshop might be better, but you need to get up there to guy it to the corners and then periodically maintain it.

I’m sure any modern commercially made multiband vertical is a whole lot better than my Gotham vertical.

Good luck!

73,

Steve

N6SJ

On Tue, May 14, 2024 at 11:36 PM Larry-KF6NCX via groups.io <ncx4kf6=gmail.com@groups.io> wrote:
Was there a radio blackout affecting us Tuesday morning? Kevin, VE3EN, creator of Solarham.com, reported there was a blackout.  I tried to operate in the Randomgram cw event between 1600 and 1700Z.  I had very little success.  Only heard two or three stations on the HF bands.  I called CQ quite a bit and checked the RBN to see if I was getting out.  I had decent reports from around the country (although not too many).

Throughout the hour, I called CQ on 15 and 20, alternating between tuning around the bands and transmitting.  What seemed strange was often the RBN reported "no new spots."  Between 1604 and 1608 I was spotted 16 times from various parts of the country.  Both on 15 and 20.

Then at 1621, I was spotted five times on 15 meters, from various parts of the U.S.  Then no spots till 1640, when I was spotted twice, by a beacon in AB and another in 4-land.  Then no more spots the rest of the hour.  This seems odd.  Under normal conditions, I would expect to be spotted continually.

Is this what you might expect during a solar "incident"?

Larry

Hi Sheryl,

You didn't mention whether your RX problem is with low bands or higher bands.  However you did mention 'changing conditions' so I assume you are referring to the higher bands.  Although I don't have a mag loop antenna I do have a dedicated RX antenna for the low bands.  The low band RX antenna has directivity to null out atmospheric noise however it also has a very high negative gain.  This is by design and not unusual in a RX antenna (i.e. high directivity at the expense of gain).  It's job is to increase the S/N ratio on low band signals in the presence of high band noise.  While it works wonderfully on the low bands I have tried it on the higher bands to see if it will help with weak DX signals.  The RX antenna is practically deaf on the higher bands.  My bet is that the mag loop RX antenna will not help you on the higher bands.  On the higher bands you are encountering weak DX signals rather than high band noise.  You'd need a gain antenna to hear the weak signals in the absence of band noise.  A RX antenna won't help you in this case.  A mag loop should help somewhat on the low bands or if you have a very specific and strong local RFI that you wish to null out.  In summary I think you should think twice on getting a map loop for RX.  Perhaps you can explore a wire beam that will give you some gain.  Attached is link to some good resources on attic wire beams.  GL.

http://www.ac0c.com/main/page_prior_antennas_gen_5_attic_array_generation_5__overview.html

On Sunday, October 16, 2016 5:29 PM, Rick WA6NHC via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:


I have the MFJ version that was given to me (still sells for ~$500).  It can handle about 200 watts max.  I have not spent a lot of time using it but played with it one FD for a few hours.  It is the 40-15M version.  Here is my quick overview.
it's not as good as a tuned dipole in either transmit or receive.
You don't want to be close to it for RF exposure (use it outside)
it needs to be at least 10' above dirt, higher doesn't seem to make much of a difference
it constantly requires retuning with frequency changes, which is tedious even with fast/slow control (no hunt and pounce is likely)
I now use a Jackite pole and tuned dipoles in the RV.  Others have used small loops and are getting good results but frankly, I'm not impressed.
My cure for the HOA limitations is simple, I'm moving to N Idaho once I sell this place.
Rick wa6nhc

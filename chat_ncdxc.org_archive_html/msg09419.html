Quick question on the K2AV folded counterpoise. Everything I have found on it is for 160m. What are the dimensions for it for 80m? I think this could be the answer for my location.
A good starting point would be to scale it by a factor of one half. Better to ask the developer, Guy, K2AV. See his email as cc: to this one. I know that NI6T is happy with the one he built for 160M. 


Just as an aside, I worked five new entities on 80m the last two nights on CW with the G7FEK antenna. This is a far cry from what I had with my 80m and 160m slopers and my K9AY at my old QTH but still fun.
Congratulations! Contesters sometimes don't realize the value of being active during, before, and after major DX contest weekends. The weekends are obvious -- they're on the contest bands. Before and after, expeditioners are often active for a day or two on the WARC bands. 


I heard you this morning on 80m. Hope you had a good contes
Thanks. I concentrated on 80M, but also worked 160 and 40 a bit. Yesterday morning and this morning, I worked a total of five EU Russians long path on 80. Both days, the contacts came between 1430Z and 1515Z. There were also a lot of JAs, some with very big signals. I worked one guy running 5W and several running 50W. Also 9V1YC. 31 countries on 80M for the weekend, 61 on 40M. And I added PJ7 to the log on 80M.
It's apparently not widely known, but 40M is very good to EU for 30-45 minutes beginning about two hours before our sunset, and on some days 80M can be good an hour later (that is, beginning an hour before sunset). 80 was not good yesterday (Saturday), but 40 was very good. I made almost 40 EU Qs in that hour on 40M to more than 20 countries, mostly eastern EU. Some of them were quite loud, and a few told me I was very loud. By the end of that hour, signals were getting much weaker, and were not workable. I also had good success to EU on 40M in the hour or so after their sunrise. 

73, Jim K9YC

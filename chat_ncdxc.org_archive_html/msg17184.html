In a message dated 05/19/19 10:17:32 Pacific Standard Time, chat@ncdxc.org writes:

I've been shopping for a radio silent solar system and wanted to quickly
share a few recent findings.

All of this is going to be relative to the level of noise you already
have and the proximity of your receive antenna to any RFI generating
part of the system.

And to be sure, even the latest Solar gear generates RFI, some of it a lot.

I was fortunate enough to find a local solar installer that would take
me around to installations of the equipment he was proposing to verify
noise which we did this past Friday. With a mini W6LVP loop and a KX3
running on battery we listened to:

Sonnen Eco 3 battery system - extremely quiet, barely detectable with
the antenna pressed against the chassis. Tested in both charge and
discharge modes. All the noise in that install was coming from an
ethernet over powerline system plugged in next to the Sonnen. Threw me
for a few minutes until I saw it!

Solar Edge inverter with optimizers - extremely noisy from both inverter
and optimizers. I can get the exact model numbers if anyone cares, but
the install is less than a year old. It was so noisy we left immediately
after doing the shutdown to confirm it was the solar system.
Coincidentally this was the installers personal home and the system he'd
originally spec'd for me :-0.

enPhase IQ7+ micro-inverter system (with ferrites) - detectable but not
radio silent. This system was installed on a hams garage and he had the
installer put 1 clamp on ferrite on each micro-inveter as well as put
the AC output into the mains through multiple turns of mix 77. Not sure
how much good the single small ferrites actually did, but with the test
antenna within a few feet of a panel it was just a slight buzz on 80m.
Since the op primarily operates at night, it's not a huge concern and
his antenna is 100' from the panels so it is good enough for his needs.

My solution is none of the above (except the battery). The best answer
is still the old school string inverter with no electronics in the air.
I'll run DC down to an SMA Sunnyboy inverter which is well known for
being quiet and built like a tank. Also made in Germany.

My methods were not particularly scientific, but it quickly confirmed my
suspicion that putting active devices on the roof directly under the
beams is a bad idea...

After the fact I found this fantastic blog
http://ka7oei.blogspot.com/2016/03/the-solar-saga-part-1-avoiding.html

Perhaps I say fantastic because he came to the same conclusion 3 years
ago, but the logic holds.

Hope this helps anyone shopping.

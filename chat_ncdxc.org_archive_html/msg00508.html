Craig,

 

The yellow LED is really indicating that the TX frequency is different than the RX frequency – as in Split mode.  But RIT and XIT will cause that LED to light also.  The LED is a lighted  indicator the TX is not the same as the RX which for me calls my attention to the configuration – Split or Simplex.

 

Then there are the latest ICOM rigs like the 7800 and 7600.  When in Split mode, dead center at the top of the front panel is an LED that is bright white – it is more like a headlight!  Trouble is I need a bright warning light when I’m NOT in split mode and should be.

 

73,

I have often wondered how many of the ‘lids” on the DX frequency are running K3s.  You have to look real close at the display to see where you are transmitting on a K3 and if you are new to the radio, you may still have problems.

 

I wish that Elecraft would do something like the way Kenwood radios work.  I have a lot of experience with Kenwood TS-2000 and TS-480s and I watch the main frequency display.  When you key up the display changes to the transmit frequency (the VFO displays are swapped).  It is very easy to see where you are transmitting.

 

I like the K3 and I am currently working on getting mine going at my remote location where I currently use a TS-480 with RemoteRig but I think that the K3 is very complex and I would not recommend it for an inexperienced operator.  Getting to know the K3 requires a lot of “time in the chair” in my opinion.  Taking them on a DXpedition with operators who man not have ever seen one could be a challenge.

Bob W6OPO noted:  

 

But with a “power cycle” as in power failure common on DXpeditions, the K3 cannot do any housekeeping before shutdown.  It is immediate lights-out.  That is why Elecraft suggest to only turn the radio off with its power button, not the external power supply.  If the power supply is used as the master ON/OFF switch then the last settings are not stored.

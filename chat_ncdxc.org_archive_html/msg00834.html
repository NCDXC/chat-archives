I don't think the ARRL should delist entities simply because they are rarely activated or unlikely to be activated.  

Politics and policies change over time, although DXpedition logistical costs will continue to increase. 

The ultra-difficult/unlikely entities are not an absolute bar to achieving DXCC HR. 

And I think such a move might create two classes of HR membership - when it was 'difficult' and when it was 'easier'.   Just like the Extra 'code' vs 'no code' debate that still rages today. 

If somehow I might achieve HR, I'd like it to be the same achievement with the same degree of difficulty as those who had achieved HR before me. 

-- Mike Flowers, K6MKF, NCDXC - "We do DX!"

Here is your NCDXC Question of the Week (QoTW)
 
 
We all know that there are some DXCC entities that have not been activated for a number of years. Some entities appear to be impossible to activate for various reasons.

Question: Should the ARRL consider de-listing any entity that has not been activated for a prescribed number of years, perhaps 15 years of no activity?
 
Would this enable some to reach the Honor Roll, or would this be a step backwards and make it too easy to reach HR?
 
What are your thoughts?

As a new Ham I found no activity on the bands at home. Later I figured it was too much RFI from neighbors.

Somebody showed me FT8 and I started moving up WAS. Then suddenly DXCC started climbing. Was it as much fun as SSB or CW. Nope. But I guess if it hadn’t been for FT8 I would have sold the rig and moved on.

Then came contesting, learning CW and loving Summits on the Air. Using Remote quiet stations, operating from MM stations and portable.

QTH still stinks RFI. Hardly do FT8 except when there is an ATNO of filling a band slot.

My point is: Most Hams these days suffer from elevated noise floor and FT8 is the only way to play the game.

Read some interesting statistics in the recent QST I believe:

85% QSOs on 6m are now FT8, only just over 4% is CW

60% QSO's on HF are now with FT8 at the expense of the other modes

Not saying for or against but some interesting statistics since FT8 came into play in 2017. What I find interesting too given FT8 is very helpful in working DX weak signal when no other modes work, is the number of USA to USA contacts I see on FT8 at any given time now.

You two guys are invaluable sources of DX info.

Zone 22 on 80 is one of my last 3 slots for 5BWAZ.
I just realized what I am going to do during "Christmas vacation":
Learn to do FT8.  Never made a digital QSO before.  Now
is the time.

I will comment on Abubaker's "end fed antenna".
That probably refers to an end fed dipole.
(Technically, all verticals are "end fed" but
that's probably not what he was talking about).
A long time ago I had an end fed 80 meter dipole
hung between two 55 foot towers.  It was fairly
non-trivial to feed a KW into a 3,000 ohm load,
but I worked out all those details.  It worked
great ... as a cloud warmer.  I was really strong
from San Diego to Seattle.  DX?  I tried but
failed many times to work JA's.  When I got my
Sacramento county QTH, I put up a vertical and
was shocked to find that working JA was a piece of
cake on 80, and eventually I even worked JA
on 160 meter phone.

I am also planning some receive antennas for 80 meters.
Maybe I'll build a dedicated one aimed at 5A.

Another thing I would mention is that from the west
coast, we rarely have short path propagation to
Europe.  5A is essentially in southern EU.  It
is much more reliable to work EU from W6 on LP
in the morning.  I am wondering if we can talk
Abubaker into being QRV for that.  The added
bonus is that the USA East Coast will be in
daylight at the time so no competition from
them.

73
Rick N6RK

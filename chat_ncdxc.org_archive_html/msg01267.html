I would second Jim's observations on the KX3, having an early KX3 myself,
I've had the great opportunity to take it on a good number of trips already,
including one through the Mediterranean, and it does very well as a
full-service radio. In my case, I used it in CQ WPX CW last year from SV5
and even in the deafening QRM of 40M in central Europe, the KX3 held its
own.

The form factor allows you to carry an entire station in a small camera
case, in mine I pack the KX3, a 100w capable PS, fullsize wire antenna for
40-10m with radials, paddle, and miscellaneous hardware necessary for
putting the radio into service in the field (twine to raise antennas, weight
to throw the twine through a tree, BNC stuff, etc., etc. - and all of this
in a very small camera bag that is smaller than a typical SLR bag.

YMMV, but I think the KX3 is a winner, especially at it's price, a true
bargain in today's market.

73 de N6GQ

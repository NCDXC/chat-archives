A few scattered souls amongst you may have noticed that I have posted no reports for two days or so. The reason is simple--I have had little/nothing to report!

In addition to looking for E30FG  on 30 or 40m, I have been scanning the horizon for several IOTA groups that announced themselves and ostensibly are "on expedition" right now. Not only has E3 remained too buried in noise for me to copy, but the IOTA efforts appear to be non-events for the west coast.

  • I have yet to hear E30FG well enough to call, much less hunt them. A few of you have done better, but my noise level on 30m is about -110 dBm. They were on 80 today, but that seems out of the question for most of us.
  • The Spanish op (EA3NT) who activated OC-195 (Flinders) a few days ago then went on to H4x, ostensibly to activate no less than three Oceania groups. That itself is a journey--from Tazzie to northern Oz. I did not need OC-047 (Guadalcanal) but that is the only one I have heard. Due to bad WX, Christian canceled OC-168 and went directly to OC-127, where his sig has been underwhelming and rarely posted. Both of these groups are rare--needed by >80% of island chasers. He also hoped to activate OC-137 during his stopover in Brisbane on the return leg. I am not holding my breath on that one.
  • Jacek, SP5APW was to activate AS-130 as 3W2JK, needed by 83% of IOTA chasers and he is there. However, he found that the only places on Con Son Island with tourist accommodations are on the SE coast, and that enclave is blocked on SP to both EU and NA. He has been posted from Asia--on RTTY, no less--but no copy here. Another washout, so far, except for the JA's. RTTY? On a one-man expedition to a rare island? What was he thinking? I am sure he knows that one must log >1K QSO's for the activation to count!
  • Finally, IOTA Iron Man Mike, K9AJ and 2 Italians are activating a New One, OC-296, Tobi Island, using three call signs, albeit coming and going within less than three days! They were posted a zillion times today--but I rarely heard them. Luckily, I had logged Mike when I got up for a nocturnal bathroom visit in the wee hours on 30m, but could not buy an insurance QSO today. Best bet? 30m before our  sunrise.

What seems strange is that we seem to be isolated prop-wise. I heard plenty of east coast callers chasing the OC-x and AS-x operations, but the prop never showed up here.

So, given the paucityof positive results, keeping a journal seems relatively  unproductive---two new IOTA's but several out-of-play and no luck at all with E30FB on the bands below 20m. I know several of you have pulled off new ATNO's that I don't need; more power to you.

Garry

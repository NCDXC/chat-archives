Twice I tried without F/H and did not get the RR73 until I switched to F/H (just in time).  That’s not proof of anything, just a data point, twice.  Coincidence once, the second time is a hint of a pattern.

Since then, I just used F/H for them.  They are not running WSJT-X (leaving JTDX or MSHV) because at times (20M now) they present 6 streams (audio is a little wonky and some path distortion but great copy).  

Except 12m FT8, (60M no license there) and 6M, I have an FT8 sweep, so I can’t flesh out what I’ve seen any further.

Other than being blatantly obvious about looking for and hand picking EU callers in all modes, ignoring all others until the band demands change (not the only team phoning home in recent history) this has been a pretty good event.  Not fast and simple, but productive.

The stats don’t lie; east coast is just as easy to work as EU (same water path, similar distances) while the rest of the world, gets leftover scraps (surprisingly AS too and I know there is a daily e/w path open on most bands).

While the free (new upload today too) LOTW is gracious, so is maintaining a balance to all places on this rock.  They’re a very young crew (oldest is 26) and show great promise, so that important tidbit (fair and balanced) falls on the pilot/s  (in a mentoring role) who can step back to watch the propagation and the bigger picture.

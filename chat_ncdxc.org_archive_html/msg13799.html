When I lived in San Jose back in the 80s, I tried to go through the tower permitting gauntlet.  The building department said that I would need to get a variance from some commission - about $500 whether I won or lost.  I talked to a commissioner and she said that they were not the tower police and to put it up anyway...  I did have good neighbors including an assistant district attorney next door.

Fortunately, here in Nevada County, our county rules and regs for antennas and towers state "except for federally licensed amateur radio stations".  If your tower height would allow it to fall and hit a structure, then you just need a building permit to make sure that it was installed correctly.  It is a structure and the Building Department wants to make sure that your other permitted structures will be safe.  Maybe that's why we have so many hams up here...

On Aug 8, 2017, at 9:09 AM, Kelly Johnson <kelly@kellyandshari.com> wrote:

I gave up fighting the city of San Jose.  The only way I was going to get a permitted tower was if I took them to court.  I've got better things to do with my money, so I moved to an accommodating city that gave me a tower permit the day I walked into the planner's office.  My issue with PRB-1 is that it leaves too much to the imagination.  I much prefer some of the state regulations that make it clear that 70' is the minimum they must allow.  This regulation appears to leave much more to the imagination than PRB-1.  I don't think it will be very effective at all.  I don't know why ARRL is championing it like a cheerleader.

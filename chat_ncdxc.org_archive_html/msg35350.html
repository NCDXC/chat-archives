MSHV already has a send report limit variable (a setting).  But when it’s broke, it’s broke.  The watchdog broke too.  broke too.  broke too.

I’ve used it some, but only in cases where I expect a pileup i.e. I call Bangladesh and a WALL of JA answers (I work the Bangladesh THEN answer if I wish).

But I limit to one stream (in part because the MSHV audio streams are rarely set up to be clean; a give away trait seen when watching).

My strong suspicion (as a non-programmer) is memory leakage or the buffers max out, scramble or lose data and the program gags.  Translated, sloppy coding on this branch.  (Save the log, reboot, restart.)

[And when the new WSJT-X comes out with the 1.5 KHz bandwidth and 9 hounds at once; will the others hold out or follow?  What flaws and mistakes will be incorporated then?]

To do it correctly, any app AI (probably coming soon) isn’t yet smart enough in real time for a proper analysis of callers, zone and signal strengths.  e.g. only a human can see the wall of callers; compare the calls, note where they come from, compare their signal strengths and KNOW where prop favors; yet pull one or more callers out that have been underserved (Oh, it’s AN continent and that’s uncommon) or recognize the band shifting (Oh, AS is rising, I should turn the antenna that way or away from, as needed).  AI won’t know the goals of the effort either.

An attending human DXer can do that nearly effortlessly, but AI on a laptop; doubtful anytime soon.  And the day that happens; I won’t work that station on FTx mode (or AI produced phone or CW).  The key point of the exchange is human to human contact, where the mode is the medium to get that connection albeit brief.

Knowingly working a NHI robot or automatic station (no human involved) for DX purposes is immoral (opinion) and if it isn’t against DXCC rules, should be.  (Key word, knowingly.)  Such an exchange has zero merit; devoid of any human contact.

We already KNOW our stations can reach any flea spec island on the globe with the right conditions; the fun for us, is proving that.  What would be next, wake up to see what your computer worked to overnight or during the last week?; where’s the fun in that?  You are then the slave of the computer; useful only for maintenance and repair so the computer can succeed.

RIB use is exactly on a similar line of morality; the saving grace is that a human has to BE there to set up power, antennas on site, periodically maintain and refuel and a human sits behind the controls.  And when the humans leave, the RIB goes with (so far).

Or we let our computers talk to each other and we all go find another fun thing to do.  Oh, right, we can do that already; its called the internet and we don’t even need expensive radios, antennas; won’t have to fight HOA limits or “Karen” neighbors… just sterile joyless and devoid of any human activity.  You want DXCC (DX Computer to Computer)?  Done, in 0.0285 seconds.

Because we CAN do something doesn’t mean we SHOULD and robot radio use falls in that should NOT zone.  Humans understand down time; even at the cost of another couple thousand contacts with stellar prop.  We may (surely will) grumble, but we get it.

73,
Rick nk7i

I have grown to really like MSHV. 
 
We first saw it during FT8WW over a year ago.  We quickly realized its benefits as we could call in clear spaces below or above the DX station. We got more spectrum real estate and that was a plus. 
 
We also saw how the station could operate 24X7 with few breaks.  But then the cracks appeared. We soon saw where the station would go wonky and give a single station a signal report over 20 minutes before stopping or moving on.  We saw this a lot, usually in the overnight hours at the DX location. Then there is the "feature" where not everyone gets an RR73 after a signal report. 
 
We thought the Operator was an "Iron Man" operating 24X7 for days on end. We did not know about the "robot mode" within MSHV. I've later learned that some EU groups choose MSHV for the very reason that they can sleep while the station makes QSOs. You can't do that with Fox and Hound. An Operator has to manually load the que. 
 
Just now, we are seeing the FT4GL "robot" calling CQ over and over again without working anyone. Good propagation to us is being wasted.  I would much rather work a competent operator than an automated/unattended station. 
 
Whereas I once liked MSHV, I now greatly dislike it. 
 
Paul N6PSE

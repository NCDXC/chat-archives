I agree with Jim. This weekend was the ARRL DX Phone contest with *lots* of
DX activity and many motivated outstanding DX operators operating contest
stations from all over the world.

As many of you know I am the trustee for several club stations all of which
have club members who are both DXers and Contesters. Each of the club
stations are attempting to complete obtaining all of the QSLs needed for
achieving DXCC this year. Each club has worked substantially over 100 DX
countries over the last few years but as we all know working DX is one thing
but actually obtaining the QSLs is something else.

This weekend I operated at each club station, N7PR, NN7NN and NC6DX for
several hours at each station mainly using typical "search and pounce" Dxer
techniques to help out working and obtaining QSLs for the last needed DX
stations for the three radio clubs. The three club stations this weekend
cumulatively made 738 DX QSOs and made 467 different DX band mode contacts
over four bands resulting in N7PR as of today earning 14 "new one LOTW QSLs"
for 96 ARRL LOTW QSLs so far, NN7NN earning 10 "new one LOTW QSLs" for 85
ARRL LOTW QSLs so far and NC6DX earning 15 "new one LOTW QSLs" for 71 ARRL
LOTW QSLs so far. More QSLs are showing up in the club ARRL LOTW accounts
and achieving DXCC for each of the clubs this year is finally in sight.

I had a lot of fun and I was *very* impressed with the band conditions and
with the rapid LOTW QSLing by many of the stations we worked. 

Surprises? We were able to work a station from the Ukraine on 10M Sunday
morning that was only running ten (10) watts and each club station was able
to work Morocco CN2AA on four bands. Wow!

IMHO the DX contests are excellent "target rich environments" for chasing DX
and both the "contesting" DX station and the Dxer are enriched IMHO by
working each other.

In any event, I agree with Jim, and I for one really enjoyed chasing and
working DX this weekend.

73, Ted, K6XN

As I had done a month ago with the ARRL DX CW contest, I concentrated on 10M
with the goal of adding to my band-country count on that band. In about 12
hours, I made 475 QSOs in 83 countries. That gave me one new band-country
(Romania) and one new SSB country (Tunisia). Back in the CW contest, I
worked 98 countries on 10M, 7 of which were new for 10M.

In addition to picking up new countries, serious contesting can make you a
better operator, and you can also learn a lot about propagation. And for
some of us, it's fun.

73, Jim K9YC

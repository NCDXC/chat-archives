Hi Bob,

 

Really good to hear from you today.  I'll be a little more wordy than usual to bring you up to date since we haven't had a chance to catch up for a long time.

 

I had broken both wrists for a second time.  The first time was during a tennis game where I reached out too far behind me and threw myself off balance.  The second time last year was my being up on a ladder and one leg sunk down into the dirt and the other leg stayed put and over I went....out went the hands and SNAP  The hardest thing in the world is to be face down in the dirt and you can't use your hands to get up.  But I'm all healed and good to go.  I do have one small bone longer in my right hand that prevents some movement and that's a bummer since I'm right handed.

 

I think we're all suffering from horrible band conditions from the CME.  My only hope is that it clears up for Field Day.  This year I'll be up with a group of guys in Gainesville, FL signing with the call W4GJ.  The guys are really excellent CW OPs and I said I'd do a mixture of RTTY, CW and SSB on 20 meters.  I setup N1MM+ to switch between all three modes with three individual macro groups.  It all plays and I hope Murphy doesn't come to visit!  I'ts noon now and I'll start breaking down my station.  Tonight at 6:00 we'll have a table at Sonny's BBQ ... THE best ribs in the South.  http://www.sonnysbbq.com/   If you were a little closer, you're invited to join a great group of hams, eat and play radio.  Tradition has all of the OP's wear Hawaiian shirts.  They'll make a big pot of homemade chili and we won't starve.  It's a lot of fun.

 

This year Alice and I drove up to Dayton for my first time.  Total drive time was only around 10 hours with an overnight in Tennessee.  I have a ham buddy - Dave, K9FN that used to be one of the announcers for NPR radio and he and his wife invited us to stay with them for a few days in West Lafayette, IN.  

 

It was great fun and a big surprise to see John Miller at the NCDXF booth.  I'll drop in two photos of John and Dave.

Alice is pounding away with her next novel which will become a series of adventure fiction.  The first book was done so well that it got the attention of her New York based publicist and she has been doing radio interviews.  She keeps pretty busy.

 

Say hello to the guys for me and y'all are missed very much.  We wanted to come out to Visalia this year, but having never been to Dayton before, we'll probably make it out there next year.

 

I had worked Trindade Island and PayPal'd them a ten dollar donation, but never received a card.  I REALLY need their card for an ATNO.

 

It rains hard every afternoon with thunder and lightning.  The day before yesterday we had about four inches of rain so unlike California, our lakes are full and the grass is green.  Outdoor work is done in the morning and the afternoons are spent indoors with the A/C.

 

Take care and have fun with FD.

 

73,

Bill, N6BM

OK, folks. We have three days before many of us trek down to Visalia for the biggest ham event in California. 

The GOOD NEWS: 771 people have pre-registered for this event, and it will be well over 800 in attendance with walk-ins and last minute arrivals. 

The BAD NEWS: 400 of those people will still be waiting in line to pick up their registration packets when the convention is over on Sunday morning, because VERY FEW PEOPLE HAVE VOLUNTEERED EVEN ONE HOUR OF THEIR TIME TO HELP AT THE REGISTRATION DESK!!

First of all, I want to express thanks to those folks who *have* volunteered, including some who have committed to being there at the opening bell on Saturday (7:30 am). You know who you are, and I will be sending out a schedule if and when I get some more of the slots filled in. I also want to thank the great crew that spent most of Saturday up here, stuffing all sorts of goodies in those 771 packets. That was service above and beyond the call of duty.

But now, I need *YOU*! I need at least one more person in EVERY time slot, from 11 am - 5 pm on Friday, and 7:30 am - noon on Saturday. I can accommodate anyone's schedule. If there's a panel or session that you MUST see, then by all means go to it. But if you're not attending one of the Academies on Friday, or can skip the "Old Timer" Introductions on Saturday, then stop by and hand out packets and shirts. 

Would you believe that the Central Arizona DX Assn. has committed to help, and has offered to provide more bodies if needed? This is OUR event, but we need to import migrant labor from Arizona?  Geez Louise!

Remember: Everyone who helps out at the Registration desk gets to pick up their own packet without waiting in line, and gets a brand-spankin'-new baseball cap with the 2013 convention logo (very stylish, and limited availability).

Please send me an email directly if you can help.

mailto:ke1b@richseifert.com

PLEASE do not reply to the reflector.

Thanks in advance.

Rich KE1B
Registration Chair, International DX Convention

It looks like he may have been working F/H.  When working normal FT8, you basically send the information twice in a single 'frame' or 15 second period.  F/H allows the DX to split that frame into two distinct messages, and WSJT-X displays them as such with the semicolon in between.  They are basically trading redundancy for the ability to cram more into the 15 seconds.  

Regardless, if you were running standard FT8 and they sent you an RR73 despite your software not moving down to the TX freqs (because you weren't in Hound), you should stil;l be in the log.

Hi Mike,

You'll be close to reality if you choose the "Sandy, Dry" or "Poor: Rocky, Mountainous" soil conditions. What matters to the model is the soil in close proximity (a few wavelengths) to the antenna.

You'll find that soil strongly affects the vertical radiation patterns and relative field strength of vertical antennas, but not horizontal antennas. On the other hand, soil has a strong effect on the feedpoint Z of horizontal antennas.

Be sure to carefully study the discussions in the EZNEC manual with respect to which kind of ground model to use with various antenna types, and also to carefully construct the model so that segment lengths are as close as practical to equal.

73, Jim K9YC

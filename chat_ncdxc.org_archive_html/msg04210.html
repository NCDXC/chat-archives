I’ve done a full propagation and terrain analysis for my QTH:

 

http://ky6r.wordpress.com/2013/11/18/ky6r-and-ft5zm-an-hfta-view/

 

Bottom line – since I had no problem working FT5XO back in 2005 – with lesser antennas, I should do OK. If I want to “up the ante”, I might replace the 20 and 15M nested Moxon with a 4 element OWA 20M mono band design that I have already completed – and where I have the boom and 4 elements almost completely built.

 

VOACAP – with the SSN set at 100 (maybe this is wishful thinking?) says 17 or 15M might even have longer openings than 20M, but if I had to bet on the best FT5ZM strategy, I would have a station on 20M handing out ATNO’s 24x7.

 

Run some predictions – or read about mine – and you will see why I say this. The West Coast will have short openings – and when we do – other parts of the world will have even better propagation (see the N6BV propagation table to see the side by side analysis in my blog).

 

Does anyone who has a good connection with the guys at FT5ZM know if they will do this 24x7 ATNO kind of strategy?

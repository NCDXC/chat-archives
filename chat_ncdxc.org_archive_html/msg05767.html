"The requirement for a maximum distance between the control point and the transmitter in the 200km to 200 mi distance has been mentioned so as not to gain a propagation advantage."
This proposed requirement is intended to rule out propagation advantages, and an arbitrary number of miles is selected -- but then the question arises:
? Do you get to count DX obtained at a super station that you travel to specifically in order to get better propagation/access to better antennas/equipment ?
Certainly a super station will have better propagation than one with a low dipole, and the op is travelling to that super station to obtain better propagation. Would that be outlawed too? 

So, no, operating within an entity, remote or not, is OK.

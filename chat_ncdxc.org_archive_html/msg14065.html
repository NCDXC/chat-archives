Hello Michael, 

Here is my antenna evolution over the last 30 years:

Cushcraft R7 verticle w/o radials (not very good)
Multi-band fan dipole (truly awful)
G5RV dipole with tuner (a little better)
four element KLM KT-34 Yagi on a 40 ft mast (Spectacular on 10-15-20 meters)
Force 12 C3SS on 40' mast (spectacular 10-15-20 and okay on 12/17 meters)
Three Element SteppIR Yagi on 55' tower (resonate 6-20 meters superb antenna)
SteppIR DB36 four element Yagi on 36' boom on 72' tower  (incredible 6-40 meters) but really really big.
Alpha-Delta DX-LB (80/160 dipole hung at 70' (works decently on 80/160) Can also work on 40.

On DXpeditions, I have used:
Two element SteppIR Yagi (really good-resonate 6-20 meters)
Hex beam (not very good)
5 band Spiderbeam (very good if up high) terrible if down low. (they really need to be high)
SteppIR vertical 6-40 meters (very good with three elevated radials
SteppIR Big IR 6-80 meters (not very good on 80 meters)
Cushcraft 3 element A3S tri-bander (pretty decent)
Force 12 C3SS (pretty good for a small Yagi)

Michael, a multi-band vertical with two or three elevated radials will work pretty well. If you had a SteppIR 6-40 vertical with your elevated radials running along the top of your fence, you would have a pretty decent vertical antenna. The Hustler multiband verticals are decent and low cost. Work well with elevated radials. The motorized SteppIR vertical is a step up.

If your wife will tolerate a small two element Yagi, the SteppIR 2 element Yagi on a 30' push up mast would do very well. 

Just keep in mind, any decent vertical needs either many ground radials or 2-3 elevated radials. These elevated radials can be temporary. In other works hang them out when you are operating and put them away when you are not. 

Once you make the jump to a small yagi you won't miss the wire antennas and the vertical antennas one bit. 

Paul N6PSE



On Fri, Oct 27, 2017 at 11:13 AM, Michael Wheatley via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
I need some help and advice regarding antennas.  Currently I am operating with a long wire (107') in an inverted V configuration along my fence line.  It is held aloft with a 35' Channel Master telescoping mast at the center and with a 10' mast at each end.  The telescoping mast is not guyed except parallel to the antenna along the fence line.

The antenna doesn't work worth squat (it may be the 9:1 balum I use to attach it to the RG8x feed line).

I need a new and better antenna.  Unfortunately my wife, who acts as my HOA, has decreed, "Thou shat not turn my back yard into an antenna farm".  This rules out most guying options and vertical antennas that require ground radials.

Ideally I'd like one antenna that covers 160-6 meters, but wouldn't we all?  I'd be satisfied with something that covers 80 to 10 meters or 40 to 10 meters plus perhaps a dipole for 80 meters.

Two alternatives I've been considering are:
  • a terminated folded dipole (http://www.b-squareengineering.com/TerminatedFoldedDipole.htm)
  • a vertical dipole without ground radials that can be deployed without guy wires or with minimal guy wires (http://gapantenna.com/shop/antennas/titan-dx/)

I'd love some advice from more experienced hams.

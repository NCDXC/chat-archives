Re RTTY early in the operation:

Rev. Jim asks "why RTTY still early in the trip?" and compares the QSO rates, which strongly favor CW And SSB. Good argument, but not the only one. Here is another...

When I first sailed out into the blue for God, Country and DXCC, RTTY was an afterthought and the province of mavericks--and worse. So, too, was 160 meters. Not so long ago, either--as recently as the mid-1990's. And the orthodoxy ran deeper than that. I will never forget listening to a group of OF's and YF's of the SoCal tribe railing at Visalia against any band beyond 20, 15 and 10 meters. No lie, honest. Even in 2000 at K5K, and at Conway in 1995 and Rodrigues before that, my attempts to switch a station over to RTTY or topband --even at the end---would be met with catcalls and abuse. And maybe this was justified when the team was two or three stations and a half-dozen operators.

Fact is, RTTY's popularity has exploded in the past decade or so--attracting many more newbies beyond Ed Muns, and topband started to draw its current crowd around the same time or a little earlier, when Loran and power restrictions disappeared. Neither offers 200/hour rates but the constituencies are large and getting larger. DXped planners have responded by including serious efforts on the diddle mode and the top band--and fielding larger teams.

But Navassa is also special. As we all know, the last expedition to Navassa was in 1993--and offered no RTTY and--I don't remember--maybe no 160. So you have a pent-up demand wherein almost everyone old and young needs both, whereas many of us grayer types already had one or more conventional Q's in the log and confirmed---and needs both as well.

I was certainly not alone in only needing RTTY and 160, and I went after those single-mindedly. This is borne out by the depth, density and intensity of the resultant pileups. And Rev. Jim was right in the thick of it, I might add. We are not in Kansas any more.

I would be more inclined to observe that JA has a hard time with the Caribbean and that more resources should be dedicated to Asia, as was the case today on 12 meters.

JMO, flame suit on.

Garry, NI6T

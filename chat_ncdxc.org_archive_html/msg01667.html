My experience at chasing DX for only a scant couple of years pales to the experience of the rest of the club but it was back in 2011 I had only one entity left for garnering my first WAZ award with RTTY and I needed FR / Reunion Island.  I got in contact with FR5MV who had never made a contact on RTTY before so we setup a sked...actually multiple skeds.  I've since logged way over 11k Q's on my simple FT-950 and only 100 Watts but back then I thought Ray knew USB from LSB and he could never hear me even during the optimum time for propagation between us.  Months later I got him to sign up for Skype and somehow I got him to turn up the volume during one of the times I was transmitting...listening to his audio during my RTTY transmission.  I wiggled the VFO a few hertz back and forth and BOOM (well it was more of a whimper) , there was my RTTY signal.  Raymond and I just about fell off our chairs in excitement.  Understand he was becoming bored with the persistent American and we were about ready to throw in the towel.  He was on the wrong side band and I wouldn't have known that without the help of hearing my own signal through his speaker via Skype.  Since then I've received multiple WAZ awards and DXCC's but that one was most memorable for me.

Bill, N6BM

Here is your NCDXC Question of the Week:
 
What entity has been most elusive for you?   Which one has been active but you just have not been able to get it in your logs, or you have done so with great devotion and patience?
 
 
For me, a number of them come to mind. SU was very elusive until Vlad-SU9VB went there and I was able to make multiple band contacts with him on CW. Mt. Athos was very elusive for a long time. I must have spent ten years trying to work Monk Apollo and had concluded it may never happen. Well, New Years Eve 2012 I found him on 40 SSB about 11PM our time and I now have that elusive QSL card.
 
I've never heard an EP-Iran station-Ever!  I am hoping that I can nail down EP in the coming months or years. I know that there are several that are active but between their timing, propagation and their operating methods it just hasn't happened yet.

On Fri, Sep 18, 2020 at 12:57 PM David Grybos - KK6US via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

One reference I have says that WSJT-X will process signals within -2 sec -to- +3 sec offset. (or at least looks for signals in that range)

 

I don’t believe that offsetting your local clock will have any benefit.


My personal experience is that WSJT-X can't handle stations that are more than +/- 0.25 seconds different than my clock, maybe a bit more.  But that doesn't happen often, so I suppose the problem could actually have been something other than the difference in our clocks.  That said, a difference of -2 to +3 seconds is much wider than I thought the software could handle.

And FWIW, I haven't used Timefudge myself but I know people who have used it successfully to make FT8 contacts when they're clock wasn't accurate.

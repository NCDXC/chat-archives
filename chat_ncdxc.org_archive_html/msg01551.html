Paul; Thank you for your thoughtfulness in posting this subject as your question of the week. First I realize that the choice of where a DXpedition will go depends on a number of factors; The general value of the entity, is it highly desired by a large portion of the amateur community? Next will obviously be, how expensive will it be to get there? What size of team will be able to do the job? Is it feasible and doable. When it comes to evaluating the performance of the DXpedition, here are a few of my criteria; first consider that though this is my 64th year on the air, I now operate a small, 100 watt station, limited by a severe restriction that the mobile home park I live in allows no visible antennas. I have 4 antennas, a 40M Delta loop that is uniquely, radially mounted on the back wall of my mobile home, a 40M indoor coaxial dipole that runs the length of the center ceiling beam inside the mobile home, a 40M horizontal square loop attached to the peripheral ceiling of my 12 x 13.5ft radio room and a 40M stealth end fed wire that runs from the northwest corner of the house to a 25ft mast, hidden by a tree at the southwest corner of my back yard. I am a General Class licensee, studying for Extra and hoping to be able to take the exam in June of this year. Now, the questions, does the DXpedition establish as a practice, operating at 3525KHz, 7025, 14025, and 21025, accessible to the General Class licensee, or do you only operate hidden down in the exclusive, extra class band, where I can hear you, but cannot work you? (You may think you are promoting "incentive licensing", but you are not, you make me only resent your elite attitude) If you do operate only in the Extra Class segment, you are only available to me on the WARC bands and you will work me in 5 band spots as opposed to a possibility of 16 band spots. 80M SSB is also another question; I have never heard a DXpedition, ever, show up in the General Class Phone band! Not once! An on line log is not essential to me, though where there has been one it has really been nice to have virtually instant verification of a successful contact. It is essential that a DXpedition log to LOTW. i will try, when I am able, to make a donation to your DXpedition. However if you make your  QSL contingent on an "adequate" donation, I have long ago learned the value of patience. I don't mind waiting 6 months for you upload to LOTW. However you will find that EVERY QSO I have made with anyone is uploaded to QRZ.com Log and EQSL at the time of the contact, as it enters my log(contest QSO's at the end of the contest) and to LOTW every 30 days.(except in contests, where my log goes to LOTW at the conclusion of each contest) One recent DXpedition published an established schedule of when you would get any QSL and the route yu would get it. They specified that the route and expediting of your QSL request would be contingent on your donation at the copper, silver, gold or platinum donation level and they had no intention of uploading to LOTW. I worked them 3 times during their operation, 2 of which appeared in their on-line log. I printed out their on-line log record of my QSO's with them. I am unable to donate at their specified levels. I wonder what the ARRL will say when I send them a copy of the on-line log that says I worked them. Bert H. Cook, K6CSL P.S. Amateur radio is my hobby, not the devotion of my life.

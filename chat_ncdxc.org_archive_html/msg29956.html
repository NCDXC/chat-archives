When I worked at Farinon, making microwave radios, Ted Davis W6BJH (SK, RIP!) was our manager of Customer Service.  He was a big DXer and had a 4-element cubical quad on a 40(?) foot boom at around 80 feet in Belmont.  He also loved 160M but knew his wife didn’t want to be woken up in the middle of the night by an alarm clock!  Ted’s trick was to drink a large glass of water at bedtime.  Then, with no alarm clock, he woke up to pee around 3 or 4 am, and he could check 160M, all without disturbing his wife.  That’s exactly what I did last night, and got my 160M Q with VP6A just a few hours before their QRT.  Valerie got to sleep through the whole thing.  My profound thanks to “Whiskey Six Big Juicy Hamburger”!

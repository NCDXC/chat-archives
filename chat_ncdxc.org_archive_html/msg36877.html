While I’m not technically competent to answer in depth; as someone that has used FT8 a LOT since it appeared; my findings were simple.

1)  The lowest signal that I was able to decode on SuperFox, was -16 dB, not the typical -24 dB of normal FT8 (fox or not).  The claimed 10 dB performance improvement, is negated with a 9 dB hit on real world signals from BOTH teams (it’s repeatable).  This presumes that the signal strength portion (app coding) of the decoding has been corrected for the increased bandwidth (50 Hz to 1500 Hz) for noise floor determination or if it presume the new 5 KHz bandwidth.

2)  Narrowing the audio bandwidth does not help with SF (it’s precisely 1500 Hz wide), while it does with normal F8 streams (50 Hz wide).  While this makes the hound report an erroneous value (it doesn’t matter; the exchange DOES matter) narrowing not only increases the sensitivity (below -24 dB) it helps to keep extraneous noise out of the passband, increasing chances of decoding.  [I have many examples of no decode of any FT8 DX signal/s at the normal 3k passband, but when focused (tight bandwidth) on only the stream/s of the DX, decoding was possible, even loggable.]

3)  IF and only if some portion of the beginning of the SF packet is lost (noise, late tuning, whatever); the entire packet is lost.  My presumption is that without the data redundancy of normal FT8; less can be recovered (I’ve not read through the coding; it’s a guess).  But a SF packet is not as fragile as FT4.  Likewise, loss of > 2 of the SF tones from DQRM, noise, tuner uppers, lowers the chances of decode.  It’s not bullet proof.

So IF (and only IF) the SuperFox signal was -16 dB or louder; cool, many streams and higher rates; giving the pop gun stations a ray of hope.  And if > 2 streams of normal fox; it takes away that hope.  Similar ballpark, different rules.

The typical user flaw in Fox mode is that the (robot?) op doesn’t watch the hound signal reports (zombie mode, see and click, see and click; often using multiple bands at once) and the call (location) sending them; to correlate band conditions (prop) or how well overall, the signal/s are being presented.  The correction is simple in classic F/H; if the rate is falling (or it’s the third repeat), use FEWER streams; preferably no more than 2 because the lower signal hits get worse more quickly as more streams are added.  SuperFox is binary; decode or not.

[Which touches on limiting callers to one zone, SF can send a “CQ AS” packet each transmission with little cost; normal F/H that isn’t a great idea, the rate loss is too high.]

With the caveat of not as deep decoding, SF mode should be able to maintain higher rates IF they are loud enough to compensate for the 9 dB s/n hit.  I’m not the Fox, I can’t track the rates easily. (That would be a nice added feature of Livestream; rates per band in play, in realtime.)

Again, these comments were based on MANY DXpeditions that used classic F/H and the n5j/cy9c DXpeditions using SuperFox.  Noise is atypical for my station (lower than my CA station was; often better than others I’ve talked with), polar distortion is frequent; yet there’s plenty of good decoding.  (And using Fox on 60M with 3+ streams on a QRPp band; unwise and not over bright.)

I’d score SuperFox as a C+; Good in concept, improvement needed (and fix the verification concerns).

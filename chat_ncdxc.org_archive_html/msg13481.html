Paul, I think the only way to be sure is to drill and ream a hole thru the clamp and mast that matches the diameter of grade 18-8 bolt shank. Then make sure you get a bolt that is long enough to have the shank part go thru both sides so that you have a very tight fit between the bolt shank and the reamed holes. Then stack washers an put on a nylock bolt. To prevent loosening, maybe even two bolts. An 18-8 grade bolt will not rust. Chrome moly masts wont rust. If you want to avoid putting a hole in the Orion clamp, you need to have a clamp that has a clamp hole that exactly matches the mast size with-in .005+ with engagement of at least 90% around the mast. I am a machinist so I might be able to give you some advice or possible some help with the mechanical part.  Richard wb6ewm NCDXC

> What type of support do you have?
That's always a great starting point.  Also, remember that an horizontal loop is omni-directional in the horizontal plane, while a dipole has gain broadside to it. That gives the dipole an advantage of several dB in it's favored direction. If you have the supports and real estate to do it, a pair of dipoles at right angles are a great option.  The first antenna I put up when I moved here was an 80/40 fan broadside to EU and VK/ZL.  It started out low (I needed help and tools to get it higher), and each increment of height made it play better, especially on 80M.  Eventually I got it up to about 110 ft, and added a second at 90 degrees to it. A big Douglas Fir holds one end of both antennas.

There are lots of ideas about antennas for limited space on my website, including various things I've learned about construction of wire antennas.  .

http://audiosystemsgroup.com/publish.htm

And if you have only a single support, check out the excellent ideas about sloping antennas (and a lot more) by Rudy Severns (N6LF) on his website. Google to find it.

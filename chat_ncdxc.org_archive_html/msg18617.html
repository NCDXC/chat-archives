Bob,

I'm not staying up all night for them either.
I managed to get TI9A on 80M FT8 tonight and it took a while. They have a hearing problem; the numbers show that... they were +06 dB and gave me a -21 (600 watts into my ground plane, I know I do better than that).  They worked some (a lot of) DX and even those reports were 'low' across the board.
So far I have 4 band slots from TI9A, each one was hard fought (except 15M, that was easy and the band was empty).
The HU1DL team on the other hand (similar distance and bearing), has been easy and a pleasure; great operators, polite on phone (typically one call and logged).  They've filled 14 band slots for me.  Their Elecraft KPA500 broke, they're were trying to get switching diode in San Salvador.
I've added a couple slots with VK9NK, when they're available, which is often but not the slots I need.  After the last couple DXpeditions, I was in pretty good shape starting with 9 confirmed.
Top band added 2 new countries, now up to 127 worked, 103 confirmed.  It's a slow climb (296/273 mixed, plus a few deleted so add 3).
Overall, not a bad week, except the SteppIR is acting up again (I think I have moisture in one of the tubes and it's currently 18.5F here).  It worked last night, didn't this morning until it was hit with some sun, then it was flawless and tonight, something froze up again.  I just hope I didn't mung up any of the tapes.  The 40M resonance has been 'variable' for a couple weeks now.
I plan to bring it down in Spring (after the ground thaws, then dries up a bit) for overhaul anyway (replace some wires, check all tapes and tubes, paint the tubes, reseal with shrink) so I'll limp along.  I have other antennas up, but I was really enjoying directional resonance the last few weeks (and the wire antennas are deaf when used on other bands). 

73 es gud hunting,
Rick NK7I

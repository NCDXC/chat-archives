Don’t know about y’all, but EP6T has been a real tough one for me.  The only copy I’ve been able to get has been just above WSPR levels, and has tended to vanish without a trace before I could make contact.  

I have heard that the team has extremely high local QRM (S9+) from a nearby business that refuses to cooperate with the team.  They are only able to copy sigs that are 599 or better.  Also heard that their own power source (don’t know if it is local mains) is very unreliable, and has been dumping on them periodically, which accounts for the sudden QRT, often on all bands at once.  Add all of this to a declining solar flux, an extremely rare DXCC entity, AND lousy paths from Western North America at present, and you get the radio equivalent of the Great Wall of China for those of us in California without a full gallon and stacked mono-banders at 100’.  I knew this would be tough one, but this is making Amsterdam Island look like a cake-walk-in-the-park.  

I will be interested to read the after-action accounts in QST and elsewhere after the dust settles.  Given the flame and carp that I have been seeing on the clusters, I really feel bad for this group.  They have put a great deal of time, money and effort into this venture and it seems many hams just want to throw rocks at them.  What a shame.  I think far too many DX hunters around the globe have no idea what it is like to be on the receiving end of their calls, or their jeers.  Too bad some of these folks can’t do a “ride along” on a DXpedition to get a sense of what it really takes to stage something like this for THEIR benefit.  

- Peter Gambee

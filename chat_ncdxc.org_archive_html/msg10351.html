> I used a dipole and a vertical on 40 meters and found it worked very

Yes, diversity is great. I mostly use it on 40M and below, with the
second antenna being a Beverage. But both diversity and Beverages are
useful to at least 20M, and, as you've noted, almost any combination of
antennas can be useful at any given time and on any band. For example,
N6RO patches several of his TX antennas to his operating position for
use as RX antennas in 160M contests.During the All Asia contest this
past weekend, I was using my JA Beverage as the second RX antenna.

Another point about diversity. Selective fading is the interference
between two arrivals of the same signal that are close to 180 degrees
out of phase with each other and nearly equal, which commonly occurs
with signals that have taken a slightly different path. The
cancellations will be different for antennas located at different points
in space, so antennas that are spatially separated work with diversity
reception -- the signal may be cancelling in the left ear while it's
peaking in the right.

There's also the matter of the vertical pattern of the antenna(s) -- at
different parts of an opening, the DX signal may arrive at different
vertical angles. And, of course, there's the matter of noise. :)

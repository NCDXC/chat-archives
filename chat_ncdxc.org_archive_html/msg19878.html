"W6 W7" is a GEOGRAPHICAL reference to the 6th
and 7th call areas, NOT to the caller's literal
call sign.  For example, no one would say that
this excludes calls with prefixes of A, K, or N.
Also, VE7 and VE6 would also be in spirit of
the greater west coast.  Even KL7 would seem
to be OK, IMHO.

One countermeasure is for the DX to work the W8,
but not QSL the QSO unless his callbook address
is on the west coast.  If this policy is made
known ahead of time, it might discourage this
conduct.

Rick

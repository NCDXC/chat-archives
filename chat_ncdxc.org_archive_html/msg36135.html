   Like many of you, a couple of nights ago I tried my hand at making an FT8 SuperFox QSO with K8R. I succeeded, but with a twist - just thought I'd share.

   I'm a little pistol, so despite K8R's healthy signal here at the time (-03), it wasn't surprising for me to call lots of times without a response. After a while I felt the need for a bio-break, so I let the WSJT-X Fox/Hound watchdog time out, which shut off my Enable Tx, and I left the shack to stretch. My last transmission was at 04:05:45 - note that there's no transmission at 04:06:15. When I returned to the shack several minutes later, I was surprised to discover that after I thought I had "finished" and walked out, K8R called me with a signal report, my Tx Enable automatically came back on again, and (after one unsuccessful reply from me) finished the QSO!

   I've read similar stories from others using the original Fox/Hound mode, and I can certainly understand that the Fox operator(s) are necessarily running "ahead" of the current transmission looking for new callers to add to their Fox processing queue. And, of course, I'm grateful for any QSO whether I was there to see it happen or not! However, the thought of my Tx turning itself on autonomously is a tiny bit unnerving, so I have a couple of questions for the braintrust:

1) Is there a maximum limit to how far ahead a Fox operator could be loading new callsigns into the queue? Or, to ask it another way, if I turn off my Tx Enable, is there a maximum number of decode cycles I should wait "just in case I'm in the queue" before giving up on making a QSO at that time?

2) In my case, I had let the WSJT-X F/H watchdog turn off my Tx Enable by timing out. If I had wanted to really shut off any further transmissions while I was away, short of closing the app or turning off the rig, how would I do that? If I manually click off Tx Enable (as opposed to letting the watchdog do it) would that have disabled further responses? Or turn off F/H mode while I'm away?

   TIA for any advice or additional info - it's always fun to learn new tricks!

   73's,

   - Richard, NC6RJ

Subject: Antenna failures

  Well since the nameless guy in Oregon was sharing his issues about his station I thought I’d make him feel better and tell a story about my catastrophic failures (plural).  Since late 2024/2025 we’ve had one series of wind events/weather after another.  I would lower the 72 foot tower and play that game for the last 6 months.  It first started with my DB36 spinning out of control.  I was wondering why it wasn’t pointed in the direction of my rotor controller.  One of the biggest issues of living alone is it’s hard to get another set of eyes/hands up here.  So, I would turn the controller and try and figure out why it wasn’t turning correctly in the M2 rotor.  At age 70 I feel less inclined to climb up the tower anymore but I did.  I first thought the mast was spinning inside the rotor.  I climbed up and tightened the u-bolts on the rotor and thought it was going to seal the deal.  Well, not so fast.  It still wasn’t pointed in the right direction.  I would set the rotor to turn another 180 degrees and run outside to see what was now going on.  Okay….  I guess the mast was spinning inside the beam/mast connection on the beam.  Once again I climbed the tower and tightened the bolts….  Yay.  That was the issue.  Well, not exactly.  For the third time the beam wasn’t turning in the direction of the rotor.  WTH!  Now I’m having a meltdown.  I grabbed the binoculars, set the rotor to a new heading, ran out the door and watched as the beam tried to turn.  Honestly, what else could it be????  Then the ugliness reared it head.  I could now see the rotor itself was spinning inside the tower cage…….  For the third time I crawled my way back up the tower only to see that two bolts that secure the rotor to the mast plate were sheared off.  Actually they unscrewed themselves and maybe one never got bolted in.  You could see where the tower was rubbing against the rotor this whole time and I thought I was going to have to get a crane up here and lift the beam out of the rotor and replace it with my backup M2 rotor.  Finally a little luck on my side.  I could physically wrench on the rotor and align the bolt pattern up so I could screw two bolts back in.  That took care of the problem and my arms/legs and hands have never really gotten over that much climbing.
Unfortunately that isn’t the end of the story.  The following month I looked up at the rotor only to notice that the tape/zip ties had finally disintegrated and the coax/steppir cable/rotor cable bunch no longer had the necessary loop required to allow the beam to turn the 380 degrees.  Upon closer inspection I noticed that the steppir cable got caught up around the top of the tower and ripped it out of the box.  Geezus!!  Not sure what else I was going to find so I rented the 35 foot JLG portable boom lift for the weekend.  I usually get the weekend rate for $290.00 and drove that bad boy up to the house and set it up below the US Tower.  I took all the cables down, rewired the 16 or more hard to see color coded steppir wires and zipped it all back together.  I cleaned things up, greased the tower, taped and zip tied the living hell out of the bundle of cables and left more than enough room for the beam to manage it’s 380+  degrees without catching on anything for the final time.  
Bingo!  Test the antenna, settings etc and we’re now good to go.  Yay!  Now I’m back in the game I thought..  I would see all you guys posting all the great dx daily and I was going crazy.  
Finally it’s the day to raise the tower to its full height and test it all out.  OMG.   I didn’t pay attention to “detail”…….  Yesterday when I raised the tower I had shorten the length of the cables so that they weren’t able to stretch the whole length of the 72 foot tower.  Do you know what happens when you raise the tower but the coax is attached to the junction box at the house.  I can tell you it wasn’t good.  At that point I hit the bottle hard.  
Incredible to believe but it tore the coax completely apart near the base.  I couldn’t get to the off switch fast enough .  Now my 100 foot cable needs me to put a barrel swivel inline until I want to rent a boom lift and replace it all with a whole new run of 219.
Today I will go outside if the rain permits and put on a new PL259 male end and a lightening protector inline.  I guess I’ll drag out the soldering gun and see how that plays out.  I had my local physician increase the dose of my prozac/lithium and Xanax for the next few weeks.  This might be what gets me through the stupidity and depression I’m dealing with presently.  I’ve attached a few photos to share this blessed event.  If anything else goes wrong I’ll take it all down and run the G5RV for the foreseeable future.   Thanks for your kind and continual support.  Please show pity.  WX6B
I'll try sending pictures if possible.

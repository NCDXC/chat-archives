The XYL is starting to think I am nuts and others may agree.

 

So after 5min of listening for Pooyan from my QTH I headed for what is called Area52.  A friend of mine grew up on Vargas ranch in the Fremont Hills.  He told me I could use the same place he had an inverted V when he use to work some contests.  I had put an inverted V earlier in the day just in case I could not Pooyan him from my QTH.  So I hopped in the car and headed out.  Now this is the place where I need to apologize to everyone.  I had set the mobile rig to Pooyan's frequency and headed out.  The first turn had my water bottle flip over. Next I heard Da-Da-Da-Da-Da. What!  I reached down and fumbling in the dark I tried to move the bottle.  My finger caught a spring and ping the rig was not transmitting but the key was in pieces.  The next turn had Dit-Dit-Dit-Dit, I turned the rig off because parts of the key were flopping around.  Once again sorry everyone.  What saved the evening so far was that I had preprogrammed the FT-857 with my call and RR 599 TU.  At least I could press buttons and try to make a QSO.  

 

Doing my best to drive the car up to Area52 avoiding the spots in the pasture that would take out my exhaust or worse yet oil pan I was almost there when little black critters with white stripes came into view.  I really don’t like skunks.  They have made me miss too much sleep when they go off in the back yard in the middle of the night.  Not fun.   The XYL has finally learned not to close the windows when the smell fills the bedroom.

 

I hooked up the antenna and WOW I could actually hear him.  I gave a few calls but unfortunately no QSO.  Finally I after switching back and forth between the TX frequency and RX Frequency I accidentally hit the VFO and after figured out what I had done he was gone. 

 

The good thing about this evening is I realized that when I need a QSO after someone in the club spots them 599 but I can't hear them from home I can probably work them from Area52.  I need to get set up with a good rig that I can throw in the car at a moment's notice and probably an antenna that I can be easily deployed, I'm thinking about the crank IR. 

 

I guess it is all in fun after all it is only a hobby.

 

73s

-Aaron

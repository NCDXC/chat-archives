Hi Chuck,

 

Normally I monitor 21.025 on my K3. Starting on January 5th, I saw a strong carrier on 21.003 that also appeared to have “grunge” for sidebands. I could clearly see the “stuff” on my P3. The sidebands didn’t have any obvious sound other than some sort of noise. The carrier appeared to go away in the afternoon, but honestly I don’t recall what time I didn’t see it. The carrier is back today (January 6th). I’ve been watching it since 1700z and it’s been solid all morning. It’s currently 1922z.

 

I put out a request on the NCDXC and NCCC reflectors to see if others were also hearing it. I heard from a number of members on both reflectors. We all agree that it appears to be coming from due north (zero degrees) but is very broad as we swing our yagis around. One station on the east shore of Oahu finds it appears to be coming from the Pacific Northwest. A station on Maui, without a yagi, also hears it.

 

Has anyone else reported this?

 

73,

Ross

K6GFJ

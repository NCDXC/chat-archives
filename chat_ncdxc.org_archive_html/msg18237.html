A superficial analysis of the tilt plate would
make you think that the antenna could be
perfectly balanced so that in the absence of
wind, it wouldn't want to lean either way.
Or alternately, it could counterbalanced so
that it always wanted to lean into the stops.
It turns out that this would only be true
if the antenna were a perfectly rigid body.

The reality is that the level position of
the antenna is an unstable equilibrium brought
about because of the flexing of the boom.
Therefore, especially with a really floppy
boom like my MonstIR, the first wind would make
the antenna lean into the Karlock and then it would
never go back to level again, even when the wind
stopped.

The solution to this dilemma per NN4ZZ is that
I was supposed to put side guys on the boom in
one direction only so that the antenna always
wanted to lean into the stops.  The side guys
would constantly put a curve into the boom and
bias the unstable equilibrium away from horizontal.
THAT is the "secret" to making it work with a
MonstIR.  May not be necessary for stiffer antennas.

After learning this, I did nothing since my own
latch design already worked fine without side guys.

Maybe this will be useful to Rick.

Rick N6RK

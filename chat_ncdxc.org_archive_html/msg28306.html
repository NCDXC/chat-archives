Paul, you surely don't owe anyone an apology.  You, along with the rest of the NCDXC Club have brought

us so much in knowledge, contact announcements, helpful hints that has been so beneficial.  All of us

had an opportunity whether big guns or pistols to work 3y0j.  It wasn't always clear on how or where

we might work them.  In retrospect, they did a very good job under extreme conditions and the lessons

learned will help all of us in the future.  I for one, didn't make the grade but I had as much opportunity

as the rest to get in the log.  For me, it was my poor operating skill in the heat of the moment.  Hi hi.

 

So, please don't beat yourself up over the dupes.  It happens to most from time to time.  If logs aren't

uploaded for awhile and we can't tell whether we really worked a station or not, we would most likely

try again.

 

I came away making some new friends in the NCDXC for one, I had a ton of fun trying to work Bouvet for

two, and I did order and receive a nice Hoodie for three as a remembrance to the Dxpedition.

 

Finally, my heartiest congratulations to everyone who were able to work 3y0j as well as those many

that tried.  We may get another chance to work Bouvet in our lifetime, one never knows.  The best is

to continue to enjoy our fabulous hobby and care for each other.

 

73 de Alan, K6IPM

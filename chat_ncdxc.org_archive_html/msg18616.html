Bob,

I agree, if we can find a meeting location large enough, hi.  It IS frustrating because of their obvious bias; not just at the US but anything not EU or JA.  It's wrong.

This morning (and now on 10.145) they were working all callers, still preferring EU if you watch trends.  I was able to bust the FT8 pileup this morning by doing two things in particular:

1)  The K3 in data mode has a bandpass of (up to) 4 kHz; setting the center to 2 KHz.  This chops the bottom end of the audio spectrum (a LOT).  Reset that to a center of 1500 Hz and you hear what you need to hear.  In F/H mode, this is critical as the Fox (DX) tends to be in the
200-400 Hz range.

2)  Randomly STOP transmitting to make sure someone isn't using the same tones as you.  MOST operators don't bother or care and it may not have an effect on the DX ears but it avoids an obvious conflict, a slight edge.

They were running 1 to 5 streams, I copied them all no problem (even with the other signals from non-hams).  The noise floor is -127 dBm on the P3 (some 20 dB better than my CA location).

I don't think I'll bother losing more sleep over this team but I really feel sorry for those wanting the ATNO.  TI9A also won't be getting any donation via OQRS from me unless they change their habits.  I won't reward poor behavior (but WILL take that QSL, hi).

Good hunting!

Rick NK7I

I have one of the smallest 40m beam, the Force 12 D240.  36' long elements and a 18' long boom.  Due to its small size I'm able to stack it below my tribander yagi.  Is it worth it?  Most definitely yes.  It gave me an ATNO with SV2/A :-)

ps these days not much is happening on this band.  But in a few year's time when the SN drops I'm sure I could put the beam to good use.

On Tuesday, February 4, 2014 9:05 AM, Rich Holoch <rholoch@splicemachine.com> wrote:
If you do, what do you have?

I have a pair of phased verticals - 24' hatted - tuned for 40M and fed using the Christman Feed through a Array Solutions Stackmatch II box. Phasing cables cut using an MFJ-259B and LMR-400 all around.

44 buried radials on each, oriented about 10 degrees / 190 degrees for the end fire directions, and broadsided at about 280 and 100 degrees. 

Cardioid pattern. Probably 3 dB-ish gain in the end fire direction, 2 dB-ish in the broadside directions very noticeable F/B, guessing about 18 dB-ish. Don't quote me on these figures, but compared with my 63' vertical for 160M - my A-B tests seem to validate my EZNec model. 

These directions are where I need the last 8 entities to get them all. 

My 40M band totals have been steadily catching up with 20M - the ultimate "money band".

I have noticed that more and more people seem to have 40M antennas with gain and directivity than they did back in 2001.

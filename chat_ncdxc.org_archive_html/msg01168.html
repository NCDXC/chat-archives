Hi Rex, I think I will take a stab at answering your question as a few years ago I was asking the same thing and I got some worthless and bad advice.
 
Four or five years ago, I wanted to participate in a DXpedition-any DXpedition. It looked like fun and I like to travel. I had taken my rig with me on personal/family trips many times.
 
Some of the advice I was given was that I needed to win a contest to get noticed. I was also told to master CW at 40 WPM. I contacted a few organizers and let them know of my interest but nothing resulted.
 
Then I decided to organize my own DXpedition. I chose a destination that was intriguing and interesting. At first, it appeared impossible but once the ground work was laid, some very experienced DXpeditioners took notice and joined my team.
 
I am very grateful for the mentoring and guidance that I received from K3LP, N6OX, K3VN and N2WB. I've become good friends with them as well as YT1AD, K1LZ, JH4RHF who joined the team. The destination was Northern Iraq (Kurdistan) and there just happened to be a war going on.
 
The DXpedition went very well and I was hooked!  Doing YI9PSE was a lot of fun and I could not wait for the next one. Since doing YI9PSE in April 2010, I also co-organized ST0R in South Sudan in 2011. I was a team member of ET3AA in Ethiopia also in 2011. 2011 was a busy year with three DXpeditions also including 3D2R from Rotuma. 2012 started out with a bang with 7O6T in May and ended with 3D2C in October.
 
What I have learned Rex is like the Military, each team member brings their strengths to the table. I am a strong organizer, logistics guy. I check and double check every detail but I am not in the league of the strongest operators on the team. I watch guys like the 7O6T team in amazement at how fast they can operate and how they can endure under really tough conditions for long hours at a time. I acknowledge I am not built like the Russian 7O6T team members who can operate 18 hrs a day with little sleep, rest or food. They are amazing operators indeed!
 
So to answer your question, here is my advice for how to get to join a DXpedition:
 
1) Aptitude is important, but attitude is everything.
2) Be ready, willing and able to go. Your passport must be up to date.
3) You've got to pay attention to what is going on. I was looking for additional Operators for YI9PSE, ST0R and 3D2C and made that known on W3UR's "Daily DX". Everyone had a chance to join those DXpeditions.
4) Know your strengths and weaknesses and be ready to talk about them. Guys that can configure computers, solve logging issues etc and just as important as the fast and sturdy operators.
5) Good health is paramount. Many places we go have little or no health care.
 
I hope the advice that I have given you is good. You can ask the DX forum panel at Visalia. It is a great question and many guys have wondered that as well. In the last few years, I have had a number of guys ask me if they can join us for a future DXpedition, however when I call them and tell them we are forming a team they quickly decline. It's the cost or the wife who gets in the way. All that has to be worked out before you get the call.
 
While participating in DXpeditions is fun and hugely positive, there is a down side to it as well. Not only is it costly and mentally taxing. For every hundred guys that want to see you succeed, there are a few who wish to see you fail and will throw rocks of every variety at your efforts.
 
There are some in the DXpedition community who are not welcoming or friendly. They are jealous of others successes and never have anything positive to say. They criticize any crack or fly in the ointment for their own ego's.
 
Some want DXpeditioning to remain an elitist function. They don't want to see the common ham do DXpeditions, let alone succeed. It can be a tough crowd but well worth it. You need to have thick skin and a good attitude to thrive in these environments.
 
Rex, I anticipate being involved or organizing DXpeditions for the next 20 years. I often reveal our team building via NCDXC chat and NCDXC members have had many chances to join us. You are now in the right place to get involved, so stay tuned and we have DXpeditions under development all the time. Be ready for the call and you never know where you may end up.
 
Sincerely,
 
Paul N6PSE

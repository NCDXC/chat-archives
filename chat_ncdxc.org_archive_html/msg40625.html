It is likely that this is when the bill is coming up for vote in the committees.  I see this ALL THE TIME with various bills related to housing and firearms (the two main groups that send me RED ALERT messages for immediate action).  If you start emailing/calling your legislators too early, they'll forget you supported/opposed a bill long before it makes it to a vote.

This is a nice system the ARRL put into place to fill out your callsign and click send, though it is the least impactful.  Form letters are like making an HF contact two blocks over.  No big deal.  Emailing your officials with a personally crafted message gets more attention, like getting that 12th DX contact in Japan.  What has the most impact is a phone call.  That's like getting the ATNO rare DX with 5 watts and a wire.

That has been my personal experience from talking with the Chief Lobbyist for the California Rifle & Pistol Association, the Chief Lobbyist for the California Association of Realtors, and the President of the Apartment Association of Greater Los Angeles.  Each of whom do a remarkable amount of lobbying.

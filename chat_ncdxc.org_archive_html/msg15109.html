I made a more concerted effort, as a part of my attempt to get better at CW. I contacted a number of SA stations: PY, CX, PJ2, all 15M except for one 40M outlier. Like you, I enjoyed the rich EU pickings on 20M. There even may be an ATNO in that bunch. AF showed up with 5 QSOs: EA8, CT3, A2, and CN. I'll see if any ATNOs materialize, as it is a bit early for the LotW confirmations to come in. (I've gotten about 45 confirmed out of 255 QSOs.)
Note that given my poor CW skills, some of these QSOs may be blown calls and turn out to be much more prosaic DXCCs.
I would say the upper bands tended to be open between two rather small parts of the world, and not open in general. 

73 Bill AE6JV

On 5/28/18 at 10:03 AM, chat@ncdxc.org (Paul Booth via Chat) wrote:


I'm not a big contester but I did work the contest for about an hour and a
half total, mostly looking for DX that I don't hear often.  I was amazed at
how alive 20m was at night and morning into Europe, and even mid-day.  Some
of the areas I heard and worked were:  LX, EU, YT, JW, 9M, ER, OH0, YL, YR,
YO, E7, JT, EZ, UN, CR3, 4Z.  Nothing rare and many other countries I heard
that I didn't bother.  Most of these stations were fairly strong.  I didn't
hear any stations from Africa, India, few from South America though I
probably heard every entity in Europe and old Russia.  No middle east except
for 4X.  So are the bands getting better or is this just a contest
phenomenon?  The band seemed completely full.  Where were all these stations
before?  Seemed like the good ole days again.

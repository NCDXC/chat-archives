Paul,

There was a great article in QEX a year or two back that tested various types and layers of tape over coax connections.  The author soaked the connections in water for a period of time and then checked for water infiltration.  The result was that any method of taping that included dielectric grease worked well - no grease, not so good.  The grease fills the gaps in the interior of the connection so there is no room for the water.  I use "STUF" dielectric filler in all my permanent outdoor connections.  Do not use it for temporary connections, such as portable operations, where the grease may pick up dirt when things are later opened up.  This STUF is also good at lubricating the threads.  

 

https://www.amazon.com/Stuf-STUF-Dielectric-Dielectric-Waterproofing-Grease/dp/B00SS1J4JG/ref=sr_1_32?dchild=1&keywords=dielectric&qid=1603768753&sr=8-32 

 

By the way, it is also important to completely fill the solder holes in the PL-239 connectors when installing.  Otherwise those holes become a path for water to wick into the braid - a good way to ruin coax.  (You probably already knew that.)

 

73,

Glen W6GJB

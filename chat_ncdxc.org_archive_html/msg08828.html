I was given an MFJ loop antenna good for 40-15 which is very tiny and light.  I'll play with it in the trailer soon after I get some masting (sched 40 PVC likely, also light).  Set up is trivial, but it takes a bit to tune it (a few minutes to learn how).  Bandwidth while adjustable over that spectrum, is very narrow in the tuned portion (remote motor adjust a huge cap).  ANY frequency change greater than a Khz should be checked.  That is from my five minute experiment in the dining room.

While it may not play as well as a beam (it can be directive, but lacks capture area), it at least provides a simple  and moderately quick operation.  To buy one is in the $5-600 bracket.  It won't handle full legal limit, but most portable ops aren't QRO.

I also carry a DX engineering dipole (160 on up), 100' of ladder line, a common mode choke and some coax next to the antenna launcher in the RV.  The last component to add is a small air compressor for the launcher.

I ended up using the truck station in AK... the trees were all shorter than the truck!

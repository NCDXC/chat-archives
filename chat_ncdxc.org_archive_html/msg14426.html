It’s all pretty scary conditions. We pray for their safe arrival in Cape Town.

I’ve been at >20,000 feet in a 4 engine aircraft where you often shutdown one engine to conserve fuel. As far from land as one could be over the Pacific Ocean.

Trouble is when the engine next to it catches fire you now have a plane with limited distance before making an unscheduled and very sudden stop in the water.   You start pushing stuff to the side door ready to jettison anything you can in case they can’t extinguish the fire and safely restart the previously idle engine.

Fortunately we could and landed at a much higher speed than normal on 3 engines after about the same distance as these folks. But in hours not days. 

When things go wrong regardless of how careful maintenance is done, you push other limits if not careful. Nothing but experience gets you through those events.

I’m sure the Captain is in communications with other vessels along their route to Cape Town and fortunately they do have Ham Radio along with them.

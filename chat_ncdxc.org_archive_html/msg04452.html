Team

 

Each of the six main operators on the XZ1J Team apparently averaged *883+* contacts per operator *per day* for *ten (10) consecutive days* from Myanmar !!!  Wow!!!

 

These wonderful guys worked large pileups of DXers world wide for many hours each day for many days and they also took the special effort (Paul, N6PSE, and other members of the team) to listen for and work NA and those of us on the west coast of the USA when propagation conditions were optimal for us from Myanmar.

 

In my trying to comprehend and appreciate at a personal level the truly outstanding effort and accomplishment of these wonderful guys I compared my experience in the recent ARRL Sweepstakes Phone contest a few weeks ago from my home QTH without jet lag from travelling to Myanmar and without being fatigued from setting up towers, antennas and equipment before even beginning the contest. Operating from my home as N7PR under ideal conditions without jet lag or fatigue I was able to make 853 contacts over a 20 hour period but when finished I was exhausted mentally and physically *after only the one day* of effort. These guys each did better than that *every day* for *ten* days….. and after first being jet lagged travelling to Myanmar and after first working with the rest of the XZ1J team in setting up the antennas and stations in Burma! Double wow!!

 

The more I think about what these guys each personally accomplished *every day* and as a team gave us for *ten consecutive days* the more I am really impressed and appreciative. Wow!

 

When Paul, N6PSE, returns and gives a presentation I for one intend to be there and to thank him and I look forward to learning more about what the XZ1J team accomplished and how they did it!

 

Thank you to the XZ1J team doesn’t really cover it adequately but….thank you XZ1J Team!

 

73, Ted, K6XN

Bob and the NCDXC fellas,

 

I'm glad to hear that you had fun with this Dxpedition. The XZ1J team really had a great time with this activation. Myanmar is a very unique and special place. The team got along so well and everyone made a huge effort.

 

Despite what the impression the team pictures would give, this was really a big effort by our very small team.  Our JA organizers only made a few contacts and three of them went home after the first three days. That left Franz, Peter and myself doing SSB/RTTY and David, Champ and Rafael doing CW.   Both David and Franz had really not done RTTY before and they got a real kick out of doing it while the CW contest roared on all around us.

 

As with most DXpeditions, we found ourselves calling CQ quite a bit in the last days. I really think that we worked everyone who was workable.

 

We did RTTY to the very end and we could sense that band conditions were really declining.

 

We are very satisfied that we were able to make over 53,000 contacts in ten days, with a small but focused team.

 

All in all, the team had a great adventure!

 

Paul N6PSE

Seoul, South Korea.

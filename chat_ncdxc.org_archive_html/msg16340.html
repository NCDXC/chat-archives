I worked at a Lafayette Radio Electronics when I was a teenager in Newton, NJ and was WA2QHN. I was lucky to see Radio Row in Manhattan – and purchased my first new radio at Barry’s – a Kenwood TS-511s (forerunner to 520 series). Ms. Kitty – heh heh. Finkelstein at the FCC – yowza.

 

When my parents moved the family to the Bay Area in 79’ – I was happy to see the Lafayette in (I think) Santa Clara. A few years later – in 82 I worked at Lockheed in Sunnyvale and at lunch went to Hated and Haltek.

 

I have some 50’s magazines – amazing what both Lafayette and Radio Shack sold back then – Lafayette sold National and others and Radio Shack sold all kinds of crazy stuff (oil filled filter caps and even non electronic hardware – almost “odd lot” stuff). I also remember all of the smaller retailers like Burghardt, etc that advertised in QST.

After 40 years, as many of you may already know, HSC Electronic Supply (formerly Hal-Ted Specialties Company) is finally going to close its doors for good on January 12th.  They currently have almost everything in the shop marked way down in price, so now is a good time to stop by for one last visit, or your very first visit, if you've never been.

 

They are located at 

 

3051 Corvin Dr, Santa Clara, CA 95051

https://goo.gl/maps/CGcaxobyn6z

 

The alternatives now for local electronic parts are Excess Solutions (similar surplus shop, limited customer service), and Anchor Electronics (mostly new stuff, with a very helpful staff).

 

73,

Bob, N6TV

Some folks know that my focus on some DXpeditions, is rather intense.  This is one of them since I wanted the ATNO.  Others, because I also search for the entity sweep, all modes, all bands (feasible but unlikely for some places).

I'll argue (not surprising eh?) that HamPass.org proved itself unworthy to the task (likely greatly under estimating the traffic loads hitting the server/s).  It has been down as much as up and catch up of logs from while it was down was often delayed.  And IF it was up (loading the page many minutes later), it was not live.  Only last night did it show real time, yet this morning, it's log lagged or down again (weekend, callers are even more numerous, meaning both larger pileups and even more server hits).

I had confirmations HOURS sooner on Clublog than HamPass.  Clublog has a very proven track record, including livestream.

On the plus side, besides being deaf or otherwise unable to pull calls from the hordes calling them, to be able to spend an hour (per shift) on phone/cw... more than I'd want to face.  The slow rate on 40 CW (reaching a high of MAYBE 3/min) wasn't for lack of callers (it was BRUTAL).  Yet I'm VERY grateful that they put in the time (for my 3 phone slots, confirmed).

I'm not trying to be critical, (I'm just an observer too) but hope that other teams notice and learn from any mistakes made by any team so that all are more successful (like my gripe of not using a tone ABOVE 350 on FTx mode so the posted frequency is correct without having to tune lower on the band; for more success).

They HAVE Starlink up and running (should be up to the task based on my use, WHILE they watch 4K videos) so even if sneakernet is required from the other logbook/s to upload, it's a simple task.  Again, good internet, Clublog uploads with no issue (except frequency of the upload, the eternal issue); HamPass is... the cork in the bottle (not ready for the big leagues).

73,
Rick nk7i

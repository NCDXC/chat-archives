I would then say that our best hope is higher angle openings at sunrise and sunset on 40 and 30, and maybe having a yagi lower than you usually might could be a strategy. Long Path could work, but with very short openings.

On the higher bands maybe trying horizontal antennas at different heights could be something to try.

Thinking a bit out of the box here since some creativity will be needed.

On Mar 21, 2019 8:20 AM, Paul N6PSE <pauln6pse@gmail.com> wrote:
Good morning Rich and NCDXC fellas.

Rich, not to be argumentative, but having studies Bouvet closely, I cannot agree that if the 3Y0I team beams due West, that it will be a mostly water path to us. 

Dom-3Z9DX recently shared that they intend to land at the South East side of Bouvet and climb up onto the glacier to set up camp. Terrain up to 2,100' feet will be a factor if they are beaming North West (East Coast) and even more so if they beam
due West (West Coast) as they are beaming directly into Olavtoppen just over 2,000 ft of ice covered mountain. 

This short video which starts from a helicopter departing a vessel anchored on the Eastern edge of Bouvet and follows the Southern coast of Bouvet almost all the way to the Western edge gives you an idea of the terrain involved. My guess is that they will land at the Shore seen at 1:26 to 1:30 in the video:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zirAkuCK8Jw#action="">

I do think we are going to be able to work them on 20/30/40 meters but it isn't going to be easy. FT8 might be a life saver for this event.

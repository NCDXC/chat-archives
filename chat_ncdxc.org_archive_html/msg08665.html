Hi folks,

I recently received this e-mail (below) from Donald, K6RV.  Although Donald now lives in Texas, some of you old timers might remember him as one of the four founders of the Northern California DX Foundation and an active member of both NCDXC and NCCC.  Donald is seeking antenna help for his friend N6NG who lives in El Sobrante.  I myself am not the right person to head up an antenna work party although I'm certainly willing to be part of the ground crew.  Are any of you out there willing to lead, or be part of, a team to help N6NG get his antenna fixed?  If yes, please respond directly to me and I'll try to coordinate things.

Thanks!

73 de Rusty, W6OAT
w6oat@sbcglobal.net

Hey Rusty,

 I have an old friend, N6NG (formerly WB6CQY) - who was a member of NCDXC for years.  He is now in his 70's and is in a wheelchair.  He lives in El Sobrante in the East Bay, and has a KT-34 that has high SWR - may just be bad coax.  He has a 60' Rohn tower.  He was, for years, a big time DX'er and Contester.  Believe he has all the countries worked except  P5.

 I correspond with him via email and he was telling me he figures he can never get back on the air with his beam again. All of his friends in the East Bay have either moved or died.   He does have a ground mounted 4BTV vertical that he uses.

 Wonder if you know someone who could help him out?  Would require taking down his antenna, fixing it, and putting it back up.  It would have to be Pro Bono, because I don't believe he has much money.

73, Donald K6RV

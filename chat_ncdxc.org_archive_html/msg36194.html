In 2017 we chose Solar Technologies in San Jose. On the whole they did a good job, but their foreman was on paternity leave, and his second-in-command was not detail oriented. He drilled about 40 pilot holes into the vaulted ceiling of our MBR without being aware it was a vaulted ceiling. I arrived home to many tiny hills of sawdust, and well as a brighter bedroom. Ultimately, they replaced a portion of the roof and were apologetic. Their design was impeccable and the system has outperformed the estimates.

At the time, the higher bands were dead, and it took about 6 months before I realized that it was producing S9+ switching noise on 15 and up. The system they choose used microinverters, so I went through two cycles of ferrite installation. The first when they removed the system to do an inverter upgrade, and the second when we installed a new roof. The cost to remove and reinstall 28 PV panels in 2019 was about $6000. I ameliorated most of the noise on the first cycle, but added additional ferrites on the second cycle because I could. I have three #31 clip-ons and 2 #75 clip-ons on each panel. Best source is DigiKey, although I got my first batch from Palomar who provided great technical advice, and deserved the business. The panels are now silent from an RFI perspective.

We added two Tesla batteries in 2021, which were installed by Semper Solaris. They did a professional installation, but spent a bit less time than Solar Technologies on improving the aesthetics.

I’d recommend comparing several systems, getting price estimates, and then overseeing the installation process closely. Many companies are new to the bandwagon, and many installers have had only a brief course in installation. None of the systems seem to have adequate RFI filtering… the trunk lines between the panels, the panels themselves, and the lines going to and from the inverter(s) can all radiate RF. The fundamental switching frequency is usually agile, and below 100 KHz, so you will need ferrites for both low and the usual RF frequencies. You may be able to work out something with the installer.

If your roof isn’t in perfect shape, think of replacing it before the installation, because your entire system will need to come down for roof replacement in the future, and it is pricy.

My son and his wife just purchased a system for their home off Summit in rural Los Gatos. Noise level was S9+10 on his TS-430 with a SolarEdge Power Optimizer, which is reputedly notorious for RFI… here we go again.

73, Bruce K3NQ

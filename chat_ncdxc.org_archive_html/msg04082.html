Dick's antenna weathered in an expected manner for the SteppIR Yagis of this
age.  Of interest to me was the inconsistent degradation in the tubes: some
of the same size weathered differently with the same orientation.  It
suggests a lot of variability in the construction/finishing of the tubes.
Maybe does not matter...

Since his initial design, many changes have been made based on experiences
in the field and a new one should fare no worse than Dick's. 
Dick needs to troubleshoot or take apart his EHU DE to see what caused his
failure.  I am especially interested to know about the terminal block
connections inside the PVC tube that serves as the connection point for the
control cable.  If it survived, I will rest a little easier with my older
4L.

Greeting.
I am pleased to report that yesterday after nine years my SteppIR came down.
Thanks to the efforts of Al N6TA who did the climbing and Larry W6OD who
handled the work on my roof it was down by noon yesterday.

A couple of comments on the condition of the beam. All the rubber element
connections were broken. Some had pieces missing.  One element had come out
approximately one inch. The new brand connectors should do better.  The
elements had many areas of white with exposed fiberglass on the upper side.
The silicon tape on the joints was gone in some cases and partly off in
others. some were still intact.  The new polyolefin shrink tubing will be a
big improvement.  The control cable insulation was hardened and broke easily
with  movement.  There were a few areas with insulation breaks where the
cable had been bent..  The control cables to the individual units seem to be
in better shape since I mounted it under the boom where it had some
protection from the sun. There is a problem in the EHU for the driven
element which started this operation.  I haven't opened it to find the
problem.  It will be replaced by 30-40 meter driven element. If anyone has
any specific questions e-mail me.

Dick NATD

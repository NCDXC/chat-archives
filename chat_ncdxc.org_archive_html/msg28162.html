There is considerable transparency with respect to the budget and expenses for Bouvet. NCDXF gave them a $100K grant, and we require quite a bit of detail, including a review of the contract with the ship (the biggest expense, generally). Dom (3Z9DX, the Rebel DX Group) made a grant request a year or two back, and because of his TOTAL lack of transparency, NCDXF refused to even work with him, and he withdrew his grant request.

Thierry on Crozet is a different story. Getting to Crozet, landing there, and surviving there is not the problem. The island maintains a fully-staffed scientific research base, with housing, an on-site chef, heat, a nice place to operate (a small room with tables and chairs), and transportation provided by the French Navy. The ONLY problem with Crozet is getting permission to operate; that is, the difficulty is political/bureaucratic, not geographical or environmental.

The landing point at Bouvet turned out to be unusable, which is what caused everything to fall apart. Without the ability to land the Zodiac, they had to “float” everything over, including people and food along with whatever gear they could manage to transfer. So without lots of stations, no amplifiers and no big antennas, they did a minimalist operation. It’s not what anyone expected, planned for, or wanted, but it’s the best they could do at that place and time. Unfortunately, their experience may deter anyone from trying again for a long time. Perhaps a larger craft, with a helicopter to transfer gear, could do much better, but that would cost WAAAY more than $700K.

Rich KE1B

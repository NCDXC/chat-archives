My profuse thanks to everyone who weighed in on my overweight antenna. My wife and I renovated our driveway a few years ago with some nice pavers. It was about that time I stopped lubricating the area between the lower mast section and collar. The grimy runoff looked to me like the sign of a well-maintained antenna--- sadly, other members of the household disagreed. After Paul and Steve's comments I surreptitiously grabbed some high temp grease and slathered it around the coupling mating surfaces and between the lower mast section and the guide collar. The mast and antenna no longer go into oscillation while turning, and move as smoothly as I recall from the F12 days.

Rick, I think you're right about the thrust bearing canting off plumb under load, and I'll order a new one from US Tower tomorrow. I also have a call into MFJ for a rebuild kit for the rotor. If everything still appears overloaded, 40m might have to go. With Cycle 25, it might not be so depressing!

73, Bruce K3NQ

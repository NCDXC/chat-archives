The SteppIR controller applies a holding voltage to the motors even when the controller is turned off. This prevents the tapes from creeping due to the spring force of the wound tape reels. This is why a calibrate is sometimes needed if you leave the tapes out and remove power. The only 100% safe way to remove the connector is to unplug power to the controller (after doing a retract). That said, it is not a common occurrence when just unplugging the connector on the SDAxxx. But it does happen. 
I've had mine blow once, but it was due to a short inside the EHU.
If you are lucky, you only blow the chip which is a $10 field replaceable part. Unlucky means you cook the surface mount resister (aka fuse) above the chip.

On 8/24/2020 3:31 AM, Keith Heimbold via Chat wrote:
Worked yesterday on rewiring shack to take advantage of the shelving unit that came with the house in my office/shack.
It gave me a lot more room on the countertop but still have some cable management to work on. During the process I ended up blowing up the control board of the SDA100 and have to send it in to repair,
I talked to my buddy John, W1JCW, who works at steppir and he said my mistake was to pull the cable while the unit was plugged in and off and that will blow the chip. Just something to keep in mind for those who own steppir antennas. You need to unplug the SDA100 from electricity before you unplug the control cable. Of well, it is $50 for the repair including shipping so not super expensive but still a pain.
Just something to consider if you ever need to pull the control cable on your steppir controller unit.

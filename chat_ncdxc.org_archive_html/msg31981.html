Dave,

I concur with your comments regarding receiving an R# report and an RR73. 

On the subject of not sending your grid (TX2) you maybe missing a point. When conditions are marginal / poor, starting with TX2 saves a 15 second slot to complete the QSO. Can be the difference between completing and not completing in certain situations. Often times HF ops couldn’t care less about your grid. 

My two cents.

On Nov 16, 2023, at 20:00, Dave Tucker KA6BIM via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

﻿If I don’t get a R# or RR73 then I assume the qso is not complete, and if it is a desired DX station I will keep calling him, delaying us both. 

 I also STRONGLY object to the practice of calling a station using TX2.  That does not send the grid square (Tip #3 below)  WSJT uses the grid squares to determine if the station is a dupe.   Same grid square, same call and band and it classifies it as a dupe.  With same callsign and no grid q=square WSJT determines it is a valid station to work.    I have seen  on at least 1 persons QRZ page that they don’t work dupes and say they dont need the grid square.  I find that is counterproductive.  For stations calling me, if they do not have a grid square, they go to the bottom of the list or even ignored.   Also,  If you have WSJT se on answering by farthest distance, it skips those with no grid squares, and will answer any station with a grid square instead

Dave KA6BIM


On Nov 16, 2023, at 2:21 PM, Clay Couger via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

There are some folks that run FTx without sending rr73 so they can work  more people in a pile up. The POTA folks even document this practice at https://docs.pota.app/docs/hunter_guide-english.html#ft8ft4-hints. I do not agree with not sending rr73 as it causes hounds to keep sending signal reports.

Don’t transmit on the same frequency as the activator! WSJT-X was designed to be used in split mode. Select the option “Hold Tx Freq” and select an open portion of your waterfall before calling an activator.

 

Tail-end an existing QSO. Anticipate when the current station being worked will send 73 and send your exchange in the same transmit window.

 

Tip: Skip sending your grid square when hunting an activator when using FT8/FT4.

 

Once you send 73 to an activator, you might not receive a 73 in return if the activator is working a pileup. If you received a signal report, you should log the contact.

 Clay N5YJZ

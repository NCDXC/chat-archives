On Sat,2/21/2015 7:04 AM, AK6ZZ via Chat wrote:
As many of you have read and a couple have seen first hand I have a small antenna farm here at the rental in foothills east San Jose. Yesterday I decided to move my G7FEK homebrew 1/4 wave marconi that I use on 80m primarily. I have the antenna on a MFJ push up fiberglass pole now attached to the chimney above the playroom.
I think you are saying that you have a 1/4 vertical that you feed against some counterpoise. What is your counterpoise? Radials? How long?
A year or two ago, I gave a presentation on a modeling study I had done on the effect of mounting height on HF verticals. The question was, "if I can put my HF vertical on my roof, should I?" I didn't look at 80M, but I did study 40, 20, and 10. I looked at both base-fed antennas (essentially "ground plane" antennas) and center-fed antennas (essentially some form of dipole, usually loaded). In all cases, the answer was an emphatic YES. The primary reason is that getting the antenna higher reduces losses in the earth under the antenna. A secondary reason, which applies at 40M and above for typical roof heights, is the shape of the vertical pattern formed by the first reflection in the far field. 

That presentation is on my website. k9yc.com/publish.htm

I was listening to 7O2A on 20M SSB at the end of the contest. The pileup was the usual – and I hadn’t even bothered jumping in except for maybe a call every once in a while. Tuner uppers, people making commentaries, one guy asking for RTTY, constant callers, etc, etc. I was happy that I already had Yemen – that’s what crossed my mind.

 

Right at 0000z everyone just stopped calling him – thinking the contest was over. I waited just a bit and in the clear called him – and he came back to me. Without all of the “din” of the contest, I could hear him and he could hear me in a way that just wouldn’t have happened in the contest. I’ve got my fingers crossed that it wasn’t some wise guy impersonating him, but then it sounded legit – when he said he had to take down the antennas.

 

WOW. It sure does pay to listen, listen and listen. And be very selective when you transmit.

 

I remember working P5/4L4FN the exact same way on multiple bands – but on a published frequency that he was known to appear on, and before the pileup went nuts. It doesn’t always go this way – most of the time it’s just a slog in the pileups and that I get through once the herd of big guns has had their fun and the pileups thin out a bit. Patience. That’s the key.

FWIW, my sentiments are the same as Bob, K6UJ.  "I am in the camp that
thinks DXCC is a personal achievement and not competitive." "I enjoy doing
the best I can with my own station, such that it is."

Notwithstanding the above, I also participate in contesting....sometimes
seriously....but for me...I do not consider contesting either as an
individual or as part of a contest team the same as personal DXCC
achievement. In chasing DX I consider my competitor as being myself and my
test is what I can do with what I have and whether my skills and station
capabilities and efforts are getting better...or getting worse....working
new DX moving forward.

In contesting I have a completely different attitude....the goal for me in
contesting is not necessarily to work new DX but to make the best score that
I (or my team) can do within the particular contest rules and with our
limited station capabilities....and often focusing on working "rare" DX
during a contest rather than focusing on maximizing QSOs and contest
multipliers is a recipe for not being competitive in the contest. YMMV.

I love this hobby and I love both DXing and Contesting...but to me working
to achieve a new ATNO DXCC QSL and obtaining QSLs from as many entities as
possible over a lifetime is not the same as maximizing a contest score in a
limited timeframe focused contest event particularly when a contest includes
multiple bands and multiple modes over a one or two day timeframe. YMMV

73, Ted, K6XN

Fellow NCDXC'ers,

Here is my two cents on this discussion.

I am in the camp that thinks DXCC is a personal achievement and not
competitive.
I don't see how it could be competitive anyway.  Their are too many
variables to have a level playing field.  One example is our individual
qth's.  One DXer could live in a low area with hills blocking some
directions, another could be on a mountain top with down sloping terrain.
One could be right next to the ocean....  you get my drift ?
I don't care how others achieve their DXCC entities.  I enjoy doing the best
I can with my own station, such that it is.  Its very rewarding to me to
improve my antennas on the low bands,
160 for example, from my little city lot and see my signal improving and
contacts increasing.

DX is fun !

73,
Bob

On 4/20/2014 10:59 AM, Paul Ewing wrote:
I don't know to what degree that foreign nationals play in the shortage of Call Area Six 1X2 and 2X1 call signs.
Some time spent studying a callbook CD would likely bear fruit. Perhaps I can run through the thousand or so JA cards.
My concern is the those in the contest community hoarding the more desirable calls for their contesting usage under the guise of a legitimate contest/DX club.
If you look up many of the local contesters, you can see that in addition to their own call, they are the trustee for several other 1X2 or 2X1 call signs. Why does anyone need three 1X2 or 2X1 call signs?
Why haven't you gone for a 1x2 or 2x1? If you're like me, it's probably because people know you as N6PSE, and you don't want to give that up. I am trustee for W6BX, which I originally acquired for a station I had set up at an AT&T Long Lines site that a colleague owns. I was living in Chicago at the time, and we planned on making it a contest station. On the three occasions when I applied for a vanity call (the first being K9YC), there were a limited number of calls available, or becoming available in the near future. K9YC was the only one for which I did not have to compete with at least one other applicant, and it was the only one that I didn't have to wait for.
I suspect that you don't understand contesting because you're not a contester. Believe it or not, there quite a few REAL local clubs or groups that qualify as clubs Many are members of larger clubs like NCCC, NCDXC, REDXA, and Mother Lode. I still use W6BX in Sweepstakes when I trade stations with other club members, and I use KU6W both in Sweepstakes and WPX.
Just this morning, I suggested to W6GJB, a member of the Ridge Runners Radio Club, W6BX, that he use our club call on one of two transmitters that he's using to test performance of a new antenna. He'll use W6GJB on one antenna and W6BX on the other. He can then run both antennas at the same time, which makes the tests far more accurate. He'll be using either WSPR or QRP with the RBN.
If you poke around, you'll find K6MI as trustee for several clubs in the Fresno area. All look pretty legit to me. 

73, Jim K9YC

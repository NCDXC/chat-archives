﻿ Hi Bruce,

There is no publicly accessible web reference yet. We are still working intensively on various details.

But the constant envelope 1.5 kHz transmission is pretty easy to explain. Surely you know what JT65 or Q65 signals look like. A SuperFox signal also looks something like this, only 1.5 kHz wide (starting at 750 Hz). Take a Q65-15C signal, for example, and you have a pretty good idea of what a SuperFox signal will look like.

The trick is that the SuperFox signal uses a new, cutting-edge technology. It can transmit so many bits that we can transfer the information from all 5 slots of the previous Fox modes in a single signal at the same time. This has a number of advantages. For example, the transmitter can be operated at full power, unlike with 5 individual signals, where this necessarily results in a loss of at least 10 to 12 dB. Furthermore, the transmission is more effective because we can make better use of the bits. By the way, this is the only way to optionally transmit free text messages of up to 26 characters in length. And last not least, we can add digital signatures to finally be able to offer the user a way of distinguishing the many fake stations from the real (Super)Fox.

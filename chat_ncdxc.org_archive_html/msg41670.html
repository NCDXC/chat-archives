I’m grateful for the first Desecheo activation since 2009. I also appreciate that as the first major solar/battery expedition, we’re in uncharted territory. Success could open many difficult entities. 
They’re essentially a 100w and a wire DXp, except when they’re running 10w. Guys with modest antennas in NA are having difficulties even hearing them, and outside of NA there is even more frustration. 
It’s a learning experience for us and the DXp organizers. Most tropical entities experience convective action with cloud build-up every day. At least in my eyes, they need more PV panels and batteries. 
I think their emphasis on CW is based on duty cycle… again, more power and storage would allow more FT8 for the lil’ pistol ATNOs. 
It’s frustrating, but hopefully ham radio will be in a better place after we’ve proven we can have a truly zero-impact expedition.

Ulrich Ann It's a very simple answer to your question. Highest and strongest sun for charging occurs from 1500-1700 which is midday. We don't stop at 1500 so we wait until 1600. Europe is being worked daily on 10M starting right when the band opens around 1200 and continues with a huge EU only pileup until 1600Z. At 1600Z the USA signals start increasing and are normally much louder than Europe signals.. 1600-1800 is NOT the main EU window on 10M. This actually eats into USA pile up time. 1200-1600 is the EU for 10,12,15. Low bands from 2200-0600 and 20m sunrise, sunset and night. We are working 99% Europe on 10M CW every morning from 1200-1600. It's an ATNO for many all over the world including USA and EU and JA. A quick look at ClubLog shows 1300+ EU callsigns worked on 10 meters in 6 days of operating and 24 days remaining to operate.

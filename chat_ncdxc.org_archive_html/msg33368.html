Ted Davis, W6BJH (SK, RIP) years ago told me his trick for working 160M in the middle of the night:  He drank a tall glass of water at bedtime, and when he had to get up and pee he checked the band.  I still needed TX5S on 40M CW, and when I saw they would be on “30-40-17-12-10 meters” for their final night, I used Ted’s old trick.  Woke up at 1:00am, did my business and checked DX Summit on the iPad.  No 40M CW yet, just an HA8 asking for that mode.  Went back to bed, fell asleep and woke up again at 4:00am.  Checked DX Summit and saw he had been on 40M CW from 2am to 3am, but was now back on 40M FT8.  Remember Charlie Brown yelling AAAUUUGGGHHH!! When Lucy pulled the football away?  That was me.

 

73 es tks to Paul N6PSE and all the great operators on this expedition.  Nodir was great on low bands!

Steve

As mentioned days ago, I put all my E30 eggs in one basket and shot for 30 and 40m, not needing 20m and above. The pileups on them were always huge, but not once did I experience a copiable signal worth calling on those two bands. The result is an empty basket.

I managed 3W2JK   on AS-130, barely, on 10m SSB on a dead band, but have not yet heard him anywhere else. He  is blocked by terrain SP to NA and EU on Con Son Island, so it looks like one marginal Q for that new IOTA.

I logged a weak VK7FG for a new IOTA, OC-195. The op then embarked on an ambitious string of IOTA activations in northern Oz, but canceled OC-168 and I totally blew OC-127 due to hosting a small dinner. His last night at the latter island group was last night and he stayed on 21003 all night working EU; I never heard him and wrecked myself gg sleepless. He must have been on that QRG for 10 hours. I question the strategy of only operating on one or two bands. He may make another New One stop on the way back.

I think I got 7QAA on 80; did not need it. Ditto for 9Q0HQ on 12m. Was elsewhere when he was on 80 CW at his sunrise.

Cherry-picking has its ups and downs, especially when condx are not great.

Garry, NI6T

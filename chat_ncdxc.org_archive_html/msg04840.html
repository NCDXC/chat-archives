If you saw my presentation at Pacificon, you saw that 17M two over two yagi stack that I had built – from old aluminum that I purchased from Carl, AI6V. I’m taking those 4 elements, adding a little more aluminum and making the attached 4 element 20M yagi. It will replace the nested 20-15M Moxon. The boom will be an old KLM 3” boom.

 

I’ve been wanting to build this a while, and held off until I felt that the second of the double peak was upon us. I expect that 2014 will actually be a very good year for DX – especially since FT5ZM and FT/T are being activated – and they are the first possible ATNO’s for me since making Honor Roll. I’ve always wanted a Mono Band Yagi.

 

My #1 goal now is to just make the last 9 ATNO’s for HR #1 – even though I think this will take many years to do. Within that goal – I want to break pileups more easily than I have before – without resorting to buying an amplifier that has more power than my KPA-500. I’d much rather improve my antenna than add more power in the shack. Modeling shows that the 4 element aluminum yagi will very seriously out play the wire Moxon. Plus, I just love building my own antennas.

 

So for 2014 – my DX-ing goal is at least one QSO with FT/Z and FT/T. I’m hoping they will both have at least one station dedicated to ATNO’s – which usually is done on 20M, although 17M might work well for FT5ZM to the West Coast.

 

The antenna will most likely go up at about 35’. EZNec confirms that there is no secondary lobe above the main lobe, and HFTA says 35’ is the perfect height for my QTH. My AB-952 can go as high as 55’, but at 35’ it will

(safely) support this bigger, heavier antenna. I use a $7 “closet flange” turned upside down and rotate the bugger using the “Armstrong method”. I feed it with LMR-400.

 

I have attached the plots for this antenna – at 55’ and 35’ and also a picture of my current “Crappie Pole” nested moxon and “tower”.

 

Comments and suggestions are welcome and even encouraged!

 

73,

 

Rich

KY6R

 

P.S – the antenna was originally designed at a height of 55’ – and at that height the “SRC DAT” is right on. I may tweak this model slightly because it will actually go up lower than designed.

Not uncommon anywhere, actually. W6KH, the last man standing at Visalia, reputedly ran 20 kW on 20m SSB when he lived in Saratoga. He never denied it and his signal was filthy. The holder of the 2nd 160m WAZ in zone 3 is widely considered to run at least 5 kW--and he is hardly alone. (The holder of zone 3 160 WAZ #1 reputedly altered at least one card before submitting.) A former (or perhaps current) club member told me of his visit one time to ON4UN--yes, that ON4UN--where he observed a large amplifier and asked what it ran. "Is that important?" John reputedly asked. I cannot verify the absolute truth of these assertions. YMMV. And there are plenty of 3-holer Alpha 77's still arouind.

And in my own experience, over 20 years ago I visited one of the two or three VK's whose signals dwarfed those of their countrymen on 75m. He was frequently asked about his signal. Bear in mind that the Australian  limit is 400W. He always attributed his loudness to a Bobtail Curtain and his ridge-top location, so when I visited I was naturally looking for a Bobtail. I did not see anything on 75m but an inverted vee, which initially puzzled me. The shack had a TS-850 and a small Dentron amp, which deepened the mystery. So I followed the coax outside to a standalone corrugated iron workshop. Inside the workshop was a four-foot rack with meters reading kV and Amperes, from which my host obligingly removed the top cover. I saw Jennings, Jennings, Jennings and something with handles on it. It was a 4CX5000. So I asked my host: "is this the Bobtail Curtain?" It was. "What about the '400W' limit?" He smiled. "Filament power, mate," he said.

Clearly, East Europeans are not the only ones loading down the power network.

BTW, the tube in that "10kW" amp has a 10kW plate dissipation rating, implying that if enough air moves by it and it is driven by a large enough driver, it could put out about 10 /0.6 or over 16kW in Class AB GG..

When you encounter a signal that seems too loud to be "only" 1500W, it probably is. Physics is physics. There are too many guys in this game with weak egos who just have to win, no matter what.

Garry, NI6T

BTW, that 10kW amp for sale is quite beautifully made. Obviously a labor of love by a competent builder.

Paul-

I always enjoy Visalia.  Even though I saw most of you guys at the last club
meeting, I really enjoy sharing with you all our enthusiasm for this great
hobby.  And I always come away with some new ideas for antennas or station
improvements.  And I get to spend a few days with my long time buddy George
Steinert, N6ZS, who I grew up with in Sacramento when he was WA6SLU and I
was WA6SVY.

I enjoyed the forums and the presentations, although was disappointed that
there were fewer talks this year.

My only sour note is that for the first time I bought no raffle tickets, due
to the rather unexciting prize lineup.

I look forward to IDXC 2015, when I hope we of the Northern club will put on
another spectacular show!

73,

Steve

Good morning NCDXC fellas,

How was your IDXC 2014?



I enjoyed catching up with friends and meeting new ones. The DX Forum was
really good, they focused on Q & A with the DXAC members. Topics such as
remotes, Kingman Reef, Kosovo were discussed. You can tell that the DXAC
guys really care about the issues and want to do the right thing. A lot of
thought goes into what is considered.

It was nice to see the NCDXC honor K4UEE with an honorary membership. It's
good to see the club extend honors outside of the club.

There was some confusion as to whether their was going to be a NCDXC award
for DXpedition of the Year, but apparently there was not.

The forum presentations were good but somewhat limited. There were not as
many presentations as last year. I really enjoyed W4VKU's presentation on
VU7AG.

It seemed that the raffle prizes were much less this year. Very few big
ticket items. Last year's prizes were astounding. I could do without the
Sunday morning game show "What's my Call" apparently I'm not the only one.

I learned of two different DXpeditions to very rare and exciting places
being planned. Get your stations tuned up as you will really need these two!

73 for now,

Paul N6PSE

Paul and Team

 

The most elusive  ones for me since 1963 were North Korea and Yemen.  I had never heard a station on from Yemen for 37 years until the German DXpedition 7O1YGF came on the air in April 2000. I was able to work them on SSB and  CW on multiple bands, received the QSL cards and then the ARRL would not accept the documentation for the DXpedition due to a “regime change” in Yemen after the operation even though the Yemeni government had issued  permission and documentation to the German group for the operation prior to the “regime change”. Arrrgggghhh! The ARRL a number of years later (7 or 8 years??) thankfully reconsidered and ultimately accepted the submitted 7O1YGF documentation as an “ARRL approved” operation. I was delighted as Yemen was the only country I then needed for top of the Honor Roll at that time (Thank you ARRL.)  It was an absolute pleasure to recently work Yemen again for needed band slots (and the fun of just doing it) again for the 7O6T operation in May 2012 (Thanks Paul).

 

Another elusive one for me was North Korea P5/4L4FN that I was able to work back in 2001 and in 2002. At the time I found it incredible that North Korea would really authorize such an operation or that the ARRL would ultimately credit the operation but I decided WFWL so I took several days of vacation from work to engage in the pileups and I was able to work P5/4L4FN and receive QSL cards for each operation. To my delight (and surprise) both North Korea and the ARRL credited the operations. Another lesson relearned…always WFWL for the rare ones……  J

 

The most elusive ones for me now are working needed band modes for EK and ZA.

 

I love this hobby…and FWIW  I am *really* fond of LOTW, Club Log and “online” logs for actually confirming relatively quickly whether I really worked my “elusive one”!  J 73, Ted, K6XN

Here is your NCDXC Question of the Week:

 

What entity has been most elusive for you?   Which one has been active but you just have not been able to get it in your logs, or you have done so with great devotion and patience?

 

 

For me, a number of them come to mind. SU was very elusive until Vlad-SU9VB went there and I was able to make multiple band contacts with him on CW. Mt. Athos was very elusive for a long time. I must have spent ten years trying to work Monk Apollo and had concluded it may never happen. Well, New Years Eve 2012 I found him on 40 SSB about 11PM our time and I now have that elusive QSL card.

 

I've never heard an EP-Iran station-Ever!  I am hoping that I can nail down EP in the coming months or years. I know that there are several that are active but between their timing, propagation and their operating methods it just hasn't happened yet.

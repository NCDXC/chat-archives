Sine 1980, when I started doing this, I have seen em come and I have seen em go.  Generally when they come it is because they were always there but inactive and someone went to a lot of trouble to activate them.  You can’t expect someone to live on Bouvet Island.  I think that is part of the fun.  Someday some clown will decide to rent KH3 from the government for a couple weeks and put it back on the air and the fact that it is completely contaminated with nerve gas remnants and unspent Pu239 will not stop him. And we will all gladly work him (again).  Deleting countries because they are hard sort of cuts against the mystique of the whole thing.  There are ways to make it really easy, for example cut DXCC down to 50; or eliminate all the countries with less that 10000 QSOs reported to LOTW in the last five years and only bring them back after someone does a dxpedition and brings the average up above that number.  It doesn’t bother me that I will likely never work DPRK and it doesn’t bother me that the French parcel out some of the best ones according to a completely unfathomable algorithm – they are French, after all.  What shows up I will try to work if I can and am not supposed to be doing something else.

W6DR

I agree IF someone from the ARRL is present (to observe, perhaps answer questions) to add value to the discussion then take that to the DXCC board.  Those odds are high at IDXC, but wear body armor hi hi.

 

The ARRL could just treat some areas as deleted since they are very unlikely to ever appear again within our lifetime (DPRK, Afghanistan, others) which in no way lowers the bar (I have deleted as well).  You can see that with some (few still surviving) hams holding up to 370 or so entity counts which does not diminish their effort in the least.

 

My basic premise is that if the place cannot support reasonable expectations for human living (for more than the DXpedition duration or because of environmental/legal/political concerns); then it should not be on the list at all (some low tide only reefs come to mind).  Make the award match current reality (even more when it costs hundreds of thousands to attempt some places once every decade) as they do with changes after wars and break away countries.

 

The ARRL wants to promote hams and activity on the air; it's time to update where the bar lies to encourage more activity and make DXCC real and obtainable.  And it has been time, for some decades now.

 

73,
Rick nk7i

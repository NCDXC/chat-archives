On Mon, Sep 1, 2025 at 8:31 AM Richard Muller via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
Greetings all,

Last year I purchased a KLM-KT34A from the wife of a SK. He lived on the slopes of Mount Hamilton.
I bought the antenna and tower thinking I would be able to use it at my house. Unfortunately it was a pipe dream.  I don't have room for the tower or antenna.  I'll have to stick with hexbeam.

M2 now has the rights to this antenna and offers an upgrade kit that replaces some older parts with newer, better designed parts. With the upgrade kit installed, the antenna becomes the KT34M2.

This is the link to the KT34M2:
https://www.m2inc.com/FGKT34M2

This is the link to the upgrade kit for the KLM-KT34A to make it into the KT34M2 antenna.
https://www.m2inc.com/FGKLMUP11

This is the link to the KT34M2 assembly manual:
https://www.m2inc.com/content/PDF%20MANUALS/70CMANTS/KT34M2MAN03-W.pdf

Here's the eHam reviews for the upgrade kit in case. One reviewer points out that the upgrade kit didn't come with upgraded phasing tubes but it does now, according to the M2 website.

https://www.eham.net/reviews/view-product/5934

The antenna elements are in my garage, the 18' boom is on the side of my house, and I have all but a few of the nuts and bolts needed to reassemble. When I took it apart I lost a couple of pieces of hardware in the tall grass. But these are easily replaceable from any decent hardware store.

I've been keeping this antenna thinking I'll use it someday but, like I said, unless I buy a house with more property, its a pipe dream.  If you want the antenna, it's yours for $450.  If you buy the upgrade kit from M2 for $406 plus tax and shipping, you'll likely spend about $900 for a $3,400 antenna.

I'm also selling the tubular tower.  It's a U.S.Towers MA550MDP (tilt over base with an electric motor for raising and lowering and a rotor at the base).  The tower needs to be reassembled and the cabling needs to be changed for raising and lowering. Also $450.  

Here's a link to the U.S Towers information:
MA-550 - US Tower

I live in Santa Cruz.  I transported the tower with a borrowed boat trailer and it worked perfectly.  I don't have access to the trailer anymore so you'll have to figure out transport yourself.

Let me know if you are interested.  Feel free to call me 831-254-7025.
Cheers,
Rich WN6A

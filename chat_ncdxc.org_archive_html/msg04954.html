I would never put a conductive compound anywhere close to a connector.  It
may, in fact, "make a better electrical connection" between the center
conductor and shield causing a short.  This can happen either from the
installation process or migration over time and environmental conditions.

The only compound appropriate for connectors is a dielectric compound, just
the opposite (non-conductive) of NoAlox (conductive).  For RF connectors, a
Teflon compound such as "Stuff" can be used to fill the air space in coax
connections as a way eliminate a cavity for collecting moisture and water.
Another type of dielectric compound or grease can be used on DC and small
signal connectors to inhibit oxidation.  But, again, these are dielectric
compounds, not conductive compounds.

Outdoor connections should be 100% sealed with the various types of taping
products designed for this application.  That is the only thing needed if
the connectors are clean and not oxidized to begin with.

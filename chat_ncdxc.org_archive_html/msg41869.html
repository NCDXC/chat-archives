Thankfully, no real regrets here.  My first rig was a TYT 9800D+ VHF/UHF/6M/10M quad bander, which was a dumb interim purchase to get me on the local repeater nets but ended up being a good one for VHF/UHF local ops and contests, so no regrets there in the long run.

After that I bought my first true HF rig, the Icom 7100.  Great entry HF rig, with VHF/UHF.  The all modes ability is coming in handy now with SOTA focusing on SSB and CW on 2m/70cm.

After that I got the FT891 for travel, a G90 that went back (built on a Friday, poor antenna connection), got a tru(SDX) for ultra portable travel which is fantastic albeit limited, and then the FTDX101MP which has been a God send for HF work and a G90 which has made local portable ops much more efficient.

I had a reservation down for the new portable Optima from Yaesu.  Seeing the amount of people I know who are returning or selling theirs, I'm glad I cancelled that.  That would have been a regret, it seems.  Now if only the Xeigu 6x00 series can add in VHF/UHF...  Well, next year at least.  This year I'm trying really hard to not buy any more radios.  :)

I do have some regrets on HTs, but that's another story for another day.


-73

On Sunday, February 1st, 2026 at 6:41 PM, Paul N6PSE via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
Last week, we were discussing antenna regrets. Many of us described antennas that we had bought that did not meet expectations. I described my Cushcraft R7 dummy load.

How about Rig regrets? I have a few. I bought the first version of the Yaesu FT100 mobile VHF/UHF/HF radio. This is the rig that aspired to be the later FT857D or the FT-891 of today.

This rig was awful. It was very deaf and just very poor quality. I used it for about six months mobile and then tried to off load it but the word was out that these were very poor radios and I still have it to this day. I thought of giving it to a kid but what is the point with such a poor radio?

I once bought a used K3 and found it to be absolutely deaf. I sent it to Elecraft and they determined that all of the filters were in the opposite sequence of how they should be. Elecraft fixed the radio and I have taken that trusty K3 on many DXpeditions. No regrets there.

What is your rig of regret?

Paul N6PSE

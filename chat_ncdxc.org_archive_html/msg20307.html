I think the bigger issue, and also what leads to things such as this is that there are some folks call stations they do not hear/decode, just because they see others working them they start to call. I think this is what occurred likely as I see it all the time. Very frustrating!

 

This eludes logic all together for me… Just as one would not call on SSB and CW a station they do not copy, same goes for FT8 or any digital more. I have often looked at PSK reporter for some of these Alligator stations and see they are getting out but are not receiving past a certain point. Can’t have a QSO…I don’t get it.

 

I did NOT see what happened in this instance, but it is aggravating when this causes QRM for those who can copy and possibly need it for an ATNO. Really simple, if you do not copy, do not call. It would not cause this issue. And Frankly we would not even have to announce odd/even. As it is pretty obvious when you can hear. Clearly not helping.

 

Just my 2 cents, 73

Frank, 

 

I don't believe that any of the four instances were malicious. Careless yes but not intentional. 

 

We have all made cockpit errors, myself included. But we all should avoid transmitting at the same waterfall frequency as the DX and especially in the same time synch. Evan transmitting in the same spot in the opposite time synch carries risk if the DX decides to change his own time synch. 

 

My simple rule is to find a clear spot on the waterfall and hold me shift key and click my mouse into that clear spot. 

 

That makes it impossible to transmit on the DX if he should flip flop to Even or Odd as they often do. 

 

Paul N6PSE

Yes, a nice operation amidst tough conditions.  It was fun to add some band slots and learn some tips about how to work DX:

  1. Do not avoid getting up in the middle of the night to try to get a QSO.  For me, I wanted 80 SSB and it took three nights of getting up twice to get that elusive 80 SSB.  Had I not ‘put out’, I would be wishing I had.
  2. It can pay to sit and listen to noise for a long time until the briefest of openings happens on the high bands.  For me, I tried this on 12 M but learned the lesson too late and missed a new band for KH1.  But, with Rick, Paul and others, making the suggestion, I know it can be done and will be in my quiver next chance.

It also pays to know what you need to work before spending a lot of effort.  I ‘needed’ 15 SSB and failed despite looking and looking and being home, etc.  On Wednesday, I noticed that I had it confirmed years ago!  My time could have been better spent getting a 160 M antenna up.  As it was, W6IBU lent me a wire but it took too much effort late in the game to make work and get me my first 160 M QSO.  Next time, I will be ready before the DX gets to their QTH!

 

All in all, a good op with good operators!

Like any party, there comes a time that it's over.  That time has now come for KH1/KH7Z as they went QRT at 1200Z.  They have to be off the island in about 24 hours.  Sooner than expected, but their safety comes first.

First I'd like to thank them for a job well done under physically trying conditions, miserable (yet surprising for moments) band conditions and for making it all possible for us.  NO one likes 100+ temps at the high humidity they are under.  It's fatiguing, raises the body core temp to unhealthy (fever) and hydration just isn't enough.

Second, I wish them all a safe journey home.

I was fortunate this morning.  I awoke WAY early for me, for an unknown reason, decided to check the bands and saw they were active on a few bands slots not in my log.  Rather than risk the time on 60M FT8 (the 100 watt ERP limit would bite me) I chose to attempt them on 40 CW (made it for slot 13), then on 80M FT8; now slot 14 in the log just 19 minutes before QRT.  It can't get much closer than that and I appreciate it.

One nice thing to watch, when they're so loud on FT8 and easy copy, is the calls rolling by as they QSO with others.  It was CLEAR that the op knew EXACTLY what he was doing, a batch of VK, followed by a string of JA (did they miss ANYONE in Japan?) and then a string of US calls.  Seeing that, I understand just how fortunate my last QSO is with them; but it also shows their attempts to balance who gets worked. 

Luck, plenty of chair time and patience were involved for me.  This morning on 80M FT8 while they came up to -0, my report received was -11 when the contact was made.  I've used my 160M inverted L, 80M dipole and R7 (with a manual tuner as the KAT500 died, now headed to the shop).  I'm NOT a big gun station, yet.

I note a series of (whining) comments on the clusters (mostly EU); no worse than usual but the above proves just how hard they tried under the conditions they were under.  Being limited in antenna height, all vertical elements and environmentally challenged yet working over 60k stations; impressive.

Well done is not enough but it'll have to do for now.  Show up around here, I'll buy them a beer too.

73,
Rick WA6NHC (far North Idaho)

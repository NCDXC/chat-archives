Paul, in all seriousness this time, here would be my recommendations:

 

  1. I know that FT8 is very popular now but I also think it is used unnecessarily and is inefficient (aka QSO rates) when propagation is present.  I would like to see CW/SSB used first, RTTY, and FT8 last when propagation for the standard modes is not working.  The reason I say this is that a primary goal for the DXpedition is to make as many contacts as possible.  CW/SSB QSO rates are MUCH higher (I think, tell me if I’m wrong).  Handle the big guns early and get them out of the way for the moderate and weaker stations.  I’m not against FT8 but I see it being used when CW and SSB can be supported.  For example, the C5 and A25 recent DXpeditions mostly used FT8 when propagation existed for CW/SSB.  Perhaps keep an FT8 station on at all times (only one band at a time of course) while the others operated the standard modes.
  2. If FT8 is used, it tends to favor the big guns more than using CW/SSB which can overcome big guns with more skill.  Perhaps in F/H, you can first use SNR thresholds to pick out weak ones earlier.  That to me would be an advantage of using FT8.
  3. It would be great to know quickly if the contact was verified to avoid dupes.  I’ve heard a lot of people making dupes on recent DXpeditions.  But don’t know how expensive or practical this might be from your location.
  4. It would be nice to know who the operator was on your end.  I found this out on my first VK0 qso and was able to have a nice dialogue with him later.
  5. Daily news releases with photos would be nice to see what is happening on your end.  Sometimes those of us waiting, don’t understand or appreciate the challenges that occur with storms, antennas, generators, etc…and I see subsequents posts complaining and indicating impatience.

 

73, Paul, W6IBU

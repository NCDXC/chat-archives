On Sat, Jun 24, 2023 at 10:52 AM Rick NK7I via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
Paul et al,

Like many of you, I use several messaging apps and I've reached my
threshold where they are useful.  They include FB Messenger, Signal,
Telegram, Slack, JTAlert, HamAlert, GridTracker (and you get the idea,
there are more).  As a member of several groups, the app count grows and
it's rather frustrating.  One size does apparently, not fit all needs.

I would love to consolidate to fewer apps, but it won't happen. Here is
my take on them.

First, iOS allows 'quiet time' when all notifications (except as YOU
choose) are muted.  "Favorite" contacts can ring through, else there is
blessed silence.  I would expect that Android users would find a similar
control.

Signal and Telegram works on all platforms, base themselves on security.

Signal has frequent (!) updates, confirmation of who you are demands
that can be annoying.  They also beg for money.

Telegram updates now and then, has been fast, faithful and reliable;
doesn't crump in large file transfers; it just works. You can edit your
message (or delete it), which can be a nice feature in the heat of the
battle.

I haven't used Slack a lot; someone else can comment but it is business
based, appears to have bloated overhead in that direction.

JTAlert and GridTracker messaging; only when active in the shack, useful
there only (no device alerts).

HamAlert is not messaging, available on most platforms but has no web
interface, you must use it on a device or accept email delays.  The
advantage of this is that as life happens, you can respond or ignore.  I
have 'triggers' set for any remaining ATNO and often set triggers for
specific DXPedition needs (VP6A in the slots I don't have).  Trigger
writing can be a little tricky; but it works very well (+1 vote). 
Again, it is NOT a messaging app. The server CAN be delayed, so some
spots may be several minutes (too) late.

FB Messenger, well it's Meta-ware.  Works on everything but not my
favorite company (invasive, limiting. poor privacy and security) but it
works.

So, I focus on Telegram for messaging (several groups and people, easy
to use, fast, reliable, all platforms) and HamAlert for spot filtering
and notifications.  I don't know if a group can be too large, is the
only question mark.  And email chats, like this one.

Email alerting is fine, except I don't have a selective notification (on
devices) when email comes in, except yes or no (all or nothing).  This
is great if one has a life away from the shack; just like a messaging app.

Getting everyone on one app, is not likely but it would be nice.

Spotting networks have chat functions too (mentioned for completeness);
no privacy or security, complete wild wild west (no!).

I know this is wordy, but email (this chat list and others), Telegram
and HamAlert are my go to apps and the order, varies. The others, as needed.

73,
Rick nk7i

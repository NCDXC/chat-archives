The decision on using Power is very simple.
1] If its needed, and its an uncrowded band,  I call with 90 Watts. Calmly and with great patience.
2] If I really want him bad for a new band slot--and hes very busy-   I turn on some power til something good happens. Slightly less patience.
3] If its an all time new country [I only need KP1]  its ALL KNOBS RIGHT.  No patience.
Simple, and easy,  decisions.


On 10/26/2013 7:44 AM, K6XN wrote:

Team

 

We are very fortunate in that here at our home QTH at Saratoga in that we are currently using a FLEX-5000A Software Defined Radio (SDR) system driving an Alpha 77SX amplifier (if and when  needed) as our primary station and our backup second station here is a Yaesu FT-2000 with the optional Yaesu DMU-2000 Data Management Controller coupled to drive a second Alpha 77SX (if and when needed). Our primary antenna system for HF DXing is a four element SteppIR system.

 

Normally I can work anything I can hear without need for using a linear amplifier (and I can hear very well from our Saratoga location). When I do need to QRO I usually don’t initially run full legal limit and  I will start to QRO at about 500W out. If I need more than 500W up to and including full legal limit then I will use it but the last few years I haven’t had to use full legal limit very often. On the 12M band I never run more than 100 Watts. Our beloved old antique Alpha 77SX amplifiers are usually in the low power low voltage settings when in service and even then I have to be careful to keep my driving input power to the amplifiers under 35 watts on most bands.

 

We also have a summer QTH up in the Sierrra in a wilderness area where we prefer to do our serious  contest operating and for contesting with a group of friends as a team we usually run between 500W and full legal limit up there using a collection of Icom IC-775DSP and Icom IC-751A transceivers driving monster home brew amplifiers using 3-1000z or pairs of 3-500z tubes in the finals and an Alpha 76PA. When seriously contesting particularly as a team in a multi-multi operation using multiple stations and multiple beams and antenna arrays and networked logging computers at each station we normally  run more power than in DXing. Even in contest operating we usually don’t need to run more than 500W out but it sure helps to have the full legal limit at each station when needed…particularly on 160M, 80M and 40M.

 

I have been having a lot of fun the last few days chasing DX and picking up new band modes particularly on 10M, 12M and 15M mostly running around 200W but I have gone to full legal limit a couple of times to break a tough pileup. Wow! I love these conditions.

 

Have fun all! See you in the pileups and good DX to you all.  73, Ted, K6XN, and Laine (Ted’s XYL), K6XXN

Rich, I too have the K-Line and I love the KPA500, however I also have the Alpha legal limit which I use about half the time.

 

I had a blast on ten meters these last several days. I‘ve easily worked over 100 countries just on ten meters this week. The bands are spectacular at the moment, I hope it holds for our sked tonight with EP3PK.

 

Prior to the contest I find some pileups are tough slogging. I usually start calling either with 100 watts or with 500 from the KPA500 and turn on the Alpha only if needed. The biggest problem is hearing the DX under the kops, lids and tuners.

 

My new SteppIR DB-36 is working very well although suboptimum at about 40 feet. For eight weeks now, I have been trying to get a working motor and up/down control from US Tower. Their customer service is extremely poor and I have been unable to raise my tower. That would make a big difference on this end if I could do that.

 

The new Steppir gives me three elements on 30/40 meters which is very new and exciting to me. I was able to work C82DX on 30 RTTY and 40 CW which I could not have done with my dipole.

 

Have fun in the bands today!

I run the full Elecraft K-Line and turn the amp on whenever I am trying to work a DXCC Challenge band slot or an ATNO. Most of the time (>85% of the time) I make the Q, and am "happy enough" with my antenna farm (see QRZ.COM) and power level. I run the KX3 just for goofing around – and just because I like the rig more than any other – even at (maybe because) its 12 watts max. So I guess you can say I only use the amount of power required to make a Q.

 

What I have seen lately - especially this past week, with great conditions and obviously a bunch of top notch contest stations on, my rate busting through the pileups seems to have dropped down a bit. I still have racked up about 20 band slots just this week, and I am getting very close to the 1900 mark, which will be a big milestone for me. I’m no longer in a hurry (which I was when working toward HR), and now just having fun.

 

My rate might be about 70% because I think more people in the pileups are running full power, and so its tougher competition. I’m pretty sure it’s the number of additional people in these pileups AND what sounds like higher power levels that I am running. My favorite band is 40M – and my new phased vertical array is performing wonderfully – I am hearing lots of long haul DX very strong and substantially better (3 dB with 25 dB F/B) than my 60’ vertical that has 76 buried radials. But WOW – 40M SSB seems to be one of the most competitive bands these days. The CW segment is much easier for me to bust through pileups.

 

What say you? What is your experience?

 

73,

 

Rich

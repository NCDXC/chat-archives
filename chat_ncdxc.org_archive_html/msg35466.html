Anyone anytime can click “H” and that is the behavior you will see from your transmitter.  The two stations do not negotiate a mode – he picks his mode, you pick your mode – if it works, great, if not, oh well.  Generally if the DX is not in F/H mode, you can work them either way – I have done both with various dxpeditions.  The only time it faults is if he is “true” F/H and he won’t look at your callsign after he instructs you to move to his frequency.

 

I too am looking forward to “new” F/H which appears to be a good solution – but we shall see how it shakes out…

We have had numerous reports of people getting a signal report, but not getting the “RR73” to confirm. Since Marek is NOT running F/H, it has been suggested that callers double-click on the TX1 button in WSJT-X. By doing so, instead of transmitting TX1 (which would send "FT4GL YOURCALL YOURGRID”), the software will send TX2 instead (which would send “FT4GL YOURCALL HIS-SIGNAL”). When Marek comes back to you with your signal report, WSJT-X will then send TX4, which is the “RR73”. That is, you reverse the roles; YOU send the report first, he responds, then YOU send the RR73 and log the QSO.

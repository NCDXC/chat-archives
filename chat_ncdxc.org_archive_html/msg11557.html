Hi Dan,
The signal reports on RBN are usually dB referenced to the noise floor in the receiver that is feeding RBN. I think that most RBN receivers use a vertical antenna. So -- if you're spotted on the RBN, a station with a reasonable antenna near that RBN reporter should be able to hear you with a decent antenna.
Also, in a major contest, an RBN receiver may be running at reduced sensitivity so that it isn't overloaded by strong signals. N4ZR would be a good guy to ask about that. I'm copying him on this reply. 

73, Jim K9YC

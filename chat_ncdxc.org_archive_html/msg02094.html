Bob,

Your statements are open to an alternative interpretation!
On 4/29/2013 5:08 PM, Bob Lanning wrote:

Jim K9YC,

 

I for one am not interested in seeing the club’s meeting location move around the greater bay area.

 

Somewhere around 2007 a demographic evaluation showed the preponderance of members are in this area and therefor the meeting location is located central to that population.

Your observation of centrality is probably true, but locking the meeting location ensures that most outlying members will rarely attend a meeting. Has the leadership counted heads and asked these outliers what they think of that? In NCCC a substantial number of monthly meetings are held away from Silicon Valley, and the result has been increased interaction with our brother and sister members who do not reside here--and, incidentally, a huge spike in membership and participation.

 

From what I hear from those who have been members since before 2007, since meeting at Holders in Cupertino, attendance is up and not just a little.

Having observed the ups and downs of this club for about 23 years, I would opine that increased attendance in recent years reflects the resurrection of the club from its sad decline into coma and  near-death in the 1990's by the efforts of the (younger) leadership, and not the menu at Holders. It may be that the fare at Holders is acceptable to many members---the joint caters to the walker crowd, and many of us are part of that crowd---but I personally grade the fare as barely tolerable but nowhere near exciting. I try to eat elsewhere whenever possible, then attend the meeting.

I do recognize that finding suitable venues re food quality, price and meeting space is an ongoing challenge. I disagree that the status quo is optimal and should be left as it is.

73

Garry, NI6T

40 has been a main-effort band for them. 40, 30 and 20 have been the "money bands" and the primary producer of ATNO's out here. For suffering sixes that want more, especially if 40, 30 and/or 20 are already logged, that has mostly been the story because other band openings to our region have been mostly ignored by the team, day after day, in favor of the east coast and midwest, Europe, SA, etc. It is an old and sad story. 

Pilot Rich, KY6R and I and probably others have been complaining (loudly in my case) to team leaders and  pilots and maybe--just maybe---we will get some attention in the short time remaining. At least I was asked to contribute my view on where and when they should be listening. The best bet would be 17m  from mid-morning to early afternoon, but so far, if they have been QRV before our "time", they have QSyed before the band opens. Often, they return after it is dead for us. Very frustrating and an indication of a lack of understanding how prop works and/or how NA is constructed. Too often, "NA" means eastern NA.

They did appear on 80 CW this morning--and stayed almost to sunrise. That was a first. Alas, they were weak at my place, and the local noise level facing NW soared as neighbors near and far awoke to go about their business. Plenty of switchers and other consumer crap. I got home from an all night sleep test just in time to almost hear well enough and they were mostly working JA's--who were not loud. I heard no locals. No joy and at sunrise it was over. I will be there in the morning but my noise levels are fierce, as they were for Z8.. Is pain, is good.

Garry, NI6T

On 7/31/2019 4:53 PM, eddrad--- via Chat wrote:

Paul:

I have been using a Gap Voyager on 80 and 160 for many years.
The Voyager uses a capacitive "top hat" on 160 to get the antenna to load on that band.  160 meter guru NI6T says that makes 
for less than optimal transmit performance compared with other designs.

73,  Ed  AJ6V




I don't agree.  A capacitive top hat is one of the best things you
can do for a 160 meter vertical.  However, the top hat isn't very big
so although it doesn't do any harm, it may not do much good either.
The wide bandwidth quoted on 80 and 160 meters indicates substantial
loss somewhere.

Folks,

Although a little early, I thought you might be interested in a propagation "widget" that I've developed to help generate customized propagation predictions for Dxpeditions you might want to work.

The first example of this is for Wake Island later this year.  Although not yet posted on the Wake Island web site, you can access the "widget" at:

https://K6tu.net/?q=node/add/dx_prediction_kh9w

This is a link to the site  have developed – some of you may already know about this so I won't belabor it here.

I call it a "widget" although strictly its a simple form you can fill in.
  • Enter your call sign
  • Enter your grid locator
  • Select an antenna configuration that most closely resembles your own antennas
And then click on the Save button.  You will quickly (< 1 second typically) get a graphic that is tailored around the time of the DxPedition, the antennas and power levels that will be used on the expedition and your own station/location.

All bands/all hours are shown in a simple graphic that shows the signal strength you should reasonably expect to hear the K9W signal.  On the grounds of "can't hear them, don't call them", this is a reasonable indication and when and where (band wise) your best chances will likely exist.

As we get closer to the Dxpedition itself, the site will automatically pull the latest equivalent SunSpot Number (SSNe) from NWRA and feed that into the prediction engine.

I thought NCDXC folks would enjoy this heads up and also be able to provide useful feedback.

This is also going to be available for Amsterdam Island (or for that matter, any Dxpedition that would like this and propagation/antenna modeling help as a contribution).

Enjoy!
Stu K6TU

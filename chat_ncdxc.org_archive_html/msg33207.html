On 1/21/2024 6:17 AM, Al N6TA via Chat wrote:
A 'squirrel cage blower' which forces air up the tube socket is best but those blowers can make some serious noise

I have a Henry 2K Export version, heavy duty, with two 3-500Z tubes.  I took out the blower from the cabinet and instead created an air intake on the back of the cabinet going to the underside of the chassis. That accepts a large diameter clothes dryer flexible plastic tube which goes through an appropriate tube through the wall of my room to the outside  of the house.  There I have a built a wooden structure with a huge squirrel cage blower which provides two times the air flow of the original internal one.  So now the outside one is in a screened (to keep animals and bugs out) waterproof enclosure outside of the wall of the room.  So when I turn on the blower which is coupled with turning on the filaments, you can barely hear it at all.

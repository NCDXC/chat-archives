I have also found it very valuable having a simultaneous  “dual watch” feature with simultaneous receive capability for both the DX station transmit frequency and the split frequency he or she is listening on and working DXers. Lately I have also been using dual receivers with dual panadapter displays for  both frequencies and I am finding the combination of using both sound and visual displays for both frequencies very helpful for me. The more I use the dual panadapter displays to spot both the DX station’s listening frequency movement pattern and the “mob” behavior the more I like the dual panadapter dispays and the real time information they provide.  

 

73, Ted, K6XN

 

PS I am not using a K3 but a Flex-5000A with the second receiver option  installed. I highly recommend using both a 2nd receiver and dual panadapter displays if you ever have the opportunity. If I may use some of  Bob’s wording:  “Others will say “why” but until you have it and use you don’t know the benefits.” I sure didn’t. J

 

73, Ted, K6XN

All though the K3 can operate dual-watch with a limited delta-T with the main receiver, I found the second receiver indispensable.

 

The audio is split so DX in one speaker or headphone and pile-up in the other speaker or headphone - simultaneously.  Then with the P3 you can easily position 2nd receiver and TX while listening to both receivers.

 

BTW the SVGA is an incredible but expensive add-on for the P3.  Keyboard usage, send and receive buffers for CW and data modes and more.

 

But alas – I gave up my fully loaded K3/P3 and the IC-7800 after using a TS-990S for 3-weeks.  After three weeks on the loaner 990 with A-B-C switching for  the other rigs, I ordered my own and later sold the K3/P3 and IC-7800.  The features and other good aspects of those two stations Kenwood merged into the 990.

 

I highly recommend the 2nd receiver in any radio.  Others will say “why” but until you have it and use you don’t know the benefits.

 

73,

 

Bob – W6OPO

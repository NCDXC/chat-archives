I can't speak about the issue of measuring 40m gain on a low-height Optibeam (which seems to be the only technical issue the poster had). I have the OB11-5, which does not include 40m coverage. It's also not clear how ANYONE can *accurately* measure far-field antenna gain, particular to tenths-of-a-db (which is what appears in everyone's data sheets). You simulate, determine *theoretical* performance, and then verify that it is roughly correct via fieldstrength measurements. Height, ground variations, nearby structures, etc. all affect gain and performance, and will swamp the tenths-of-a-db part (and perhaps more) of any measurement.

As to the signals being the same at both sides of a Yagi-to-G5RV contact, we all know which side is doing the "heavy lifting." The Yagis gain works on both transmit and receive, and so I would expect similar signal strengths at both ends, for the same power level.

Hihi the other way (besides that big old knob) that I discovered by accident was to just type the frequency (in MHz) into the little box on the left side of WSJT-X where it says "20m" etc.

This works great for remote FT8, and is much easier than entering every possible f/h frequency into WSJT-X in advance. I just leave the standard frequencies programmed in, and any other freq I just enter on the fly using the keyboard.

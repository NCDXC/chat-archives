Like AI6KG, I just look at the timing-error list from other stations in wsjt-x and adjust my PC clock manually but i use the standard clock app in Windows.  I usually get within a half-second that way.   The last couple of versions of wsjt-x can handle a couple of seconds of timing error.   One day i'll pull ethernet to that windows machine and get NTP running again, so i don't have to set the clock every few days, but in the mean time, this trick works well for me and doesn't require a network connection or extra hardware.

When I work WSJT modes remotely and don't bother to bring the GPS puck, I use a small trick: I line myself up with the stations I'm hearing.  

I have a little program [1]  that lets me offset the clock by a specified # of microseconds.  

Within a couple FT8 cycles, I am usually where I need to be.    If the clock frequency isn't too far off, the correction "sticks" for a while.

73 de AI6KG

[1] https://github.com/charlieh0tel/ham_radio/tree/master/jamclock

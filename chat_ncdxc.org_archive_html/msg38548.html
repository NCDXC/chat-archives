The saddest facts/limitations of the DXCC list, remain:

  • One person, board or governmental agency (often operating as one voice) in too many locations; has the ultimate power to say NO to any visits (including some US possessions, like Johnston, Kure and others).  A few can be 'guided' (excessive fees, coerced, bribed) by paying the freight for the needs of the controlling agency (pay the scientists to do what studies they need done).  That's the current reality.
  • Political environments aggravate this to the point of being a threat to life and limb (China heading that list; but there are more including N Korea, Afghanistan and others); Scarborough being just one example (guaranteed loss of life, if not a full blown war for being there).
  • Those managing 'the list' are well entrenched (by the numbers >350 confirmed) almost entirely 'old' and mostly fear losing their status so they cling to it, ignoring any hopeful requests for change.
  • SEVERAL locations have thus become unobtainable, probably forever or at least a few generations; so should be placed on an 'inactive' list, pending deletion.

It's (WAAAAY past) time for a major overhaul of the DXCC list because getting all current entities for someone starting out (or midway through the list), is simply impossible now.  

The only benefit of not adapting, is for those with them all as it stands now.  "Status quo" does NOT promote amateur radio; not maintaining a current list constrains instead; limits (removes) hope for those seeking Top Honors (regardless of age).

Which is also the main reason I only use 'the list' as my own personal score card since I don't give a fig about wallpaper 'awards' that I have to pay to 'earn'.  (I have 323 worked, 320 confirmed plus 3 deleted entities as well; mostly renamed from political changes.)  I compete against me; the list is merely a reference point since I cannot work the entirety of the current list.

My suggestion for entity criteria is simple (just for the sake of discussion), since we KNOW that we can 'work' any remote location on this planet regardless of solar season:

  • Can/does the location fulfill some useful function to mankind (study, harvest of any food or mineral)?  (One op on Crozet became a HERO to many of us, because it's a study site.)
  • Can/does it reasonably support human life without extreme measures (without costs like living on Antarctica, used for study)?
  • Is it ABOVE the mean high tide line, every day (including storms)?
  • Can it be accessed at a reasonable cost  AND safety (Bouvet budget last time was US$.75 MILLION and lives were absolutely at risk)?
  • Could such a location benefit by the visit of a DXpedition team (donations, medical assistance and similar)?

For most countries, the answer is yes.  For most IOTA/POTA, again yes.  By updating the list; we'd lose very few entities but 'the list' becomes a reason for hope that would promote the locations AND benefit ham radio.   Religious sites/groups should be excluded (to be fair to all other beliefs).

Those criteria seem reasonable, unless one is in the stodgy elite group with 370+ confirmed entities (and old enough, frankly, that they won't be here much longer anyway).

It's time for a change; let's push for it.

/soapbox off

The one thing I don't know for sure - but am guessing at - is that
propagation changes over time as far as supporting many different angles -
and that there is some kind of progression. At some point in time, higher
angles seem to be supported, and then I have a very short try at the DX.
Either that - or there is actually some kind of scattering and "knife edge
diffraction" going on - where someone like Oliver - W6NV will hear something
like Glorioso at S9, and they might just barely bob above the noise (not
really worth calling - but sometimes I act like a total alligator and do
anyway - 1000 apologies folks).

The better option would be for me to take Olivers invitation next time and
drive the 2- 3 miles to his house - which faces the SP to Zone 21
unencumbered. In fact - he has the hills going to Grizzly Peak probably more
in the way toward his West - just like I have in the NE. Orinda is very
hilly - and pretty based along several "creases" and steep canyons in and
around the old volcano that makes up the Sibley Preserve area. Great for
hiking and cycling - heh heh.

I missed Glorioso because I was so pig headed about only working them from
my own station and back yard antennas. That could have cost me HR #1 - but
I'm 54 years old and hope Glorioso is activated again. It's become pretty
darned rare these days.

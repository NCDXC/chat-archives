I enjoyed participating as a panel member of the DX Forum.......just wish we had more time to chat with the crowd.

Setting up the booth staff for NCDXC (and also NCCC) via the websites worked well, and there was good coverage at both tables all weekend.  I think both Clubs signed up at least a couple of new members.

Unfortunately, I wasn't able to attend any of the DX-related presentations, since I spent most of my time as the control operator for the W1AW/6 special event station outside in the "Carport" tent.  The station ran for 48 straight hours --- from Friday @ 1PM until yesterday at 1PM.  Almost 900 contacts were logged from all over the US, Canada, and 4 DX (6Y, FM, LW, and PY).

There were many highlights for me, including the three 9-year old Webelos who showed up yesterday at 11:30 with their leader Christina (WO1NDR) -- i.e., wonder woman.  Each boy operated for 15 minutes, making 4-5 contacts on 20M SSB.  Just seeing the smiles on their faces was worth the price of admission.  Several visitors from ARRL HQ also stopped by to say hello.....and a couple even operated for a few QSOs, including:  W1AW/6 working W1AW......cool.

Sitting next to me was the 2M station set up to monitor the Parachute Mobile Skydiving QSO jumps.  We had to stop operating HF, while listening to AF6IM and KF6WRW jumping from 13,000 feet as they made as many QSOs as possible on 146.430 with an energetic crowd standing by.  What a crazy hobby!

All-in-all a very good combined PACIFICON + ARRL National Convention.

73,
John, K6MM

Here is your NCDXC question of the week?
 
 
Did you attend Pacificon/ARRL National Convention?    If so, what parts did you enjoy?
 
 
___________________________________________________________________
 
I attended Pacificon on Saturday and Sunday. On Saturday, I had my 14 year old son Ryan-N6RYN and his friend in tow. As soon as we arrived, we were approached by an ARRL official who asked if the boys would like to have lunch with Dr. Lee Morin, the NASA Astronaut who was there. The boys were so excited and they enjoyed their lunch and asked Dr. Morin many questions. While they did that, I enjoyed the Flea Market. I just love to see that vendor that brings the old restored Microphones and old radios.
 
It was fun to watch W1AW/6 in action. John K6MM was there helping to make sure that everyone got a turn. The vendor forum was super with a good mix of vendors. Elecraft was really busy with a lot of interest in their products. I came away with a new KPA-500.
 
Most of the forum presentations were well done. Most of the rooms were standing room only. On Sunday, the crowd was smaller.
 
I thought that the ARRL did a great job with the Youth Activities. The kids got a real kick out of seeing a live QSO with the International Space Station. What a great event!
 
Paul N6PSE

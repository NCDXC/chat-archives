I wanted to do this quickly since the topic is fresh from yesterday’s luncheon and Bob, KK6EK’s presentation. We had a wonderful discussion on the drive back home – and at Bob’s QTH, and thank you very much for your thoughtful ideas. Bob will announce the winner of the DXA Book.

 

Bob asked me two questions:

 

1)      How much should a Heard Island QSL card cost?

2)      What percentage of all QSO’s made on Heard Island would pay that QSL card cost

 

My initial response was $20 and 80%.

 

But you might know me as a ridiculously over optimistic person (unrealistic dreamer). I also have been toiling over financing this project for many months now.

 

I’ve reset my idea to $10, (maybe $15 if we were to really push it), and 50% (which I think is safely conservative now).

 

What do you think? I also asked the question on the eHam DX Forum, which includes several “hearty nay-sayers” and the “lunatic fringe”, but I wanted to get a non-Bay Area read on this – especially since I know several who would give me the low end of the range – call it a “reality check”.

 

The lowest I heard is “The Cost Of Postage”, and if everyone felt that way – then there is no way the DX-pedition could happen. Luckily – almost surprisingly, most responses were between $10 - $20 in this very un scientific poll, but it included people in many parts of the country, and was very well behaved (especially by eHam DX Forum standards). Don’t worry – that thread will soon be hijacked and turned into complete and utter drivel – probably about right now.

 

I also know that there will be a group that would call “foul”. But after being a Pilot (aka “complaint department”) for ZL9HR, TX5K, T33A and VU7AG, I know that there will always be those who want everything, complain loud and often and don’t do their fair (fare?) share for the common good.

 

But some people simply don’t have the means – and I respect that as well. I want to be very realistic. The budget has to be.

 

73,

 

Rich

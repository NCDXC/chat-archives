I know that FT8 is very popular now but I also think it is used unnecessarily and is inefficient (aka QSO rates) when propagation is present.  I would like to see CW/SSB used first, RTTY, and FT8 last when propagation for the standard modes is not working.  The reason I say this is that a primary goal for the DXpedition is to make as many contacts as possible.  CW/SSB QSO rates are MUCH higher (I think, tell me if I’m wrong).  Handle the big guns early and get them out of the way for the moderate and weaker stations.  I’m not against FT8 but I see it being used when CW and SSB can be supported.  For example, the C5 and A25 recent DXpeditions mostly used FT8 when propagation existed for CW/SSB.  Perhaps keep an FT8 station on at all times (only one band at a time of course) while the others operated the standard modes.

Do you mean total QSO count, by mode?  Another factor to consider is uniques and ATNOs. (counts by mode).

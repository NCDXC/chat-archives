On 12/10/2021 8:00 PM, Al - N6TA via Chat wrote:
Part of my move means disposing of things that I will never use.  I lived in the microwave systems world for a long time and hated to see ‘good stuff’ thrown out due to not being currently useful in a production environment.  Or parts that are parametrically out of spec but still working fine.
This is a link to parts I want to get into hands that might use them: https://photos.app.goo.gl/ckyDn4vYsShQxVbP6 <https://photos.app.goo.gl/ckyDn4vYsShQxVbP6>
There are waveguide isolators with directional couplers and terminations that were to protect expensive TWTs.  They are in various bands but all are in satellite uplink bands.  There are coax parts that are L or C band and one amp that is 6-14 GHz.  Also some upconverters from L-band in to uWave out.  One assembly has three Q-band detectors that may work at lower frequency.  And, if you are a real builder, you can use some GaAs devices to make a power amp at 20-30 GHz, Ka band.  The resistors are a very high value, like 5 M-Ohm and about 7W, used for HV measurements. 

Some of these I have several of the same thing.
Anyway, let me know if you could use anything.  Or, forward to someone that is a real geek that would like to play around /with their SA and VNA to evaluate these guys.

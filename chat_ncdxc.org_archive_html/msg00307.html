I oppose the proposed rule change to eliminate the sale of International Reply Coupons.

 

Due to the high level of corruption in the post offices of many foreign countries I and many other amateur radio operators normally use International Reply Coupons (IRC).  When requesting returned mailings of confirmations of radio contacts it necessary to include appropriate return postage with a request.  Without the use of IRCs it would be necessary to include currency in the envelope and increasing the amount of currency in the mail will only increase the motivation of corrupt postal employees in these countries to open the mail.  In some countries most of the mail is already opened and searched which results in the failure of the addressee to receive the postage funds.  IRCs have little value to these postal employees.  In addition it is illegal in some countries to possess foreign currency.

 

If the USPS eliminates the sale of IRCs it will only increase the level of corruption in the post offices of some foreign countries.  Please continue to offer IRCs for sale by the USPS, this is a valuable service.

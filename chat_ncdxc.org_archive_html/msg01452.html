I needed them for a new country.  Because my QTH is up against the Fremont Hills Africa is hard.  Even long path is a challenge.  The previous weekend on 20M SSB they were in the noise from home but I could hear them pretty well using Paul's remote.   Taking the 0.6-1.8S delay into consideration I was able to transmit from home and receive on Paul's Remote.  After about 1/2 hour I was able to work them and it was confirmed within a couple minutes.  Yesterday I could barely hear them on 18M CW.  I tried for about 10min and could just make out my call.  Once again I was confirmed within a few min.  I am glad to have them on both CW and SSB.  Someday I will have a decent QTH with better antennas.

It's Monday and time for your NCDXC Question of the Week:
 
The XT2TT DXpedition is in their final days. How's your experience been working them?
 
 
 
 
My response:  I needed XT2 for an all-time new one. I missed the large DXpedition that was there about five years ago. Last year, there was a group of JA's that was active for about a week. They worked JA's and the East Coast and then went home. Never got them.
 
I was surprised and shocked to hear XT2TT calling for the West Coast during their first day of activity!
 
I've worked them on:
 
20 SSB
20 RTTY
20 CW
30 CW
15 CW
17 SSB
15 SSB
 
These seven contacts greatly exceed my expectations. Solar conditions have been quite poor, yet they continue to work hard. I'm really looking forward to meeting the Team Leader, Silvano-I2YSB at Visalia and congratulating them on an outstanding effort!
 
Paul N6PSE

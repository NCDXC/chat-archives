On Monday, September 5, 2022 at 02:01:23 PM PDT, Will Shattuck via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:


Hi folks.

I just left a discord server that helped me get started.  They helped encourage me to get my General and a bunch of members helped me get my straight camelback key assembled and working.

On Sunday I noticed I had a hard time hearing anything.  I looked at the solarham site, noticed the "storm" notice and the 3-day forecast was 6, 6, 5 and it was harsh.  The global D-Layer absorption map showed 20m should be good but signals were down.

So I made a screenshot of that page, posted it on the discord and asked if that is why (the storm) I can't hear anything.  No responses.  I asked on another discord and they confirmed it.

But here is the kicker.  The owner of the discord who runs a almost daily youtube ham show private messaged me and asked me to take down the post.  Because he didn't want to "embarrass" me by saying I need a better antenna.  All I have is the G5RV JR and that's all I can afford and it was given to me by an elmer.

Prior to these recent solar and geomagnetic events the G5RV JR has worked extremely well.  Much lower noise than my DIY 20m speaker wire antenna.

All he had to do was post a reply that said "Yes, that storm is causing a bunch of problems" but instead he took down my post and privately messaged me.

I'm a bit bummed out.  I'm not stopping ham radio.  But Man! this sure felt bad.

Did I do anything wrong by asking that question?  I don't get to listen to his show because it's an hour long and I don't always have the time to listen.  

He's also not an "angry ham" or "lid" from what I can see, but I sure feel like he treated me poorly.  I own my stuff.  If I did something wrong tell me,and I'll own it..

Thanks for letting me share. 

Will

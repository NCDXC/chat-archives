I’ve been able to work them on 15M SSB & RTTY in open competition, but have been unsuccessful when they were calling ‘NA’ or ‘West Coast’, which I ascribe to all the West Coast competition.   

 

After getting my 15M QSOs, I noticed they seemed to be calling for ‘NA’ or  ‘West Coast’ more frequently.   One time I listened to them calling for ‘West Coast’ on 20M SSB until their signal faded out for me.   My perception is that they are paying attention to us here on the West Coast.   Of course, they are #8 and nearly everyone wants to work them.   The pileups are huge and often 10, 15 or 25MHz wide.   One op SSB was calling ‘West Coast – up 5-15 and he largely kept the pileup in that 10 KHz spread.   Other CW ops were calling ‘FT4TA UP’ and the pileup would spread out – and keep spreading.

 

I agree with Larry that they could likely make better use of gray line propagation to the West Coast.   

 

With only six operators allowed on the island, all things considered, I think that they’re doing a pretty good job.

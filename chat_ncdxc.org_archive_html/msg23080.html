I thought the decision to close the Sunnyvale store — right in the middle of high-tech Silicon Valley — and keep the Oakland store was odd to begin with.  I know the building rent in Sunnyvale was an issue, but they could have potentially moved to somewhere else in the South Bay.  But if business has waned at the Oakland store, it's no surprise; no one wants to drive to Oakland, if for no other reason than the traffic on Hwy 80 is ugly 24/7.  But it doesn't seem like moving to Sacramento is likely to generate more in-store business than being in Oakland.

Looking at the photo, it appears to be a TRANSMITTING loop, not a receiving loop.

Regarding dimensions:  it is entirely possible to make a receive loop TOO LARGE, which doesn't just waste money, but makes the nulls less effective.

The 20 foot perimeter loop described in my NCJ article is about the largest you should use on 160 and certain the largest for 80.

I did build a 40 foot perimeter rotating loop for 160 but as expected the nulls weren't all that great, and mechanically it was MUCH more difficult.

I also built a 10 foot perimeter loop for 160 that had terrific nulls; I used it for simultaneous TX and RX on 160.  (TX was a 90 foot vertical).

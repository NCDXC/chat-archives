I do not recall that you have ever stepped up to serve the club as an officer or director.


Nor have I been asked.


Nor do I recall your participation in making IDXC happen.


Those of us who care contributes depending on our time and talents. I have done at least one presentation at most IDXC meetings, and have offered to do more. I try to do at least two at Pacificon each year. When I do a presentation, I'm not throwing a few slides together and shooting from the hip -- I typically spend a month preparing for each of them. I've offered to do more, but they can't seem to find room on the schedule.  I'm currently scheduled for a presentation on 160M for an NCDXC meeting this winter. 


An indeed you have not attend many meetings at Holder's.


And I will not. Nor do I believe that any ham club should  support that business. 

It was late when I composed my "shot across the bow," and I failed to acknowledge the considerable contribution of K6KLY to the club and to the ham community.  I'm also certain that I forgot others.

Whenever I have had criticisms of the club, I have also made positive suggestions about specific actions that would make things better. I have, for example, noted several potentiall sites 2M RX for the W6TI system, including one that a colleague owns on Berryessa Peak (3,000 ft, 40 miles west of the Sacramento airport), and another at 3,000+ ft in the mountains above Saratoga that has a great look around the Monterey Bay, as well as to all of Silicon Valley and the Sierra . And one of the resources I've been studying, without discussing it here, is a 900 MHz repeater system that is linked from the Bay Area, to Tahoe, to Reno, to Bakersfield, the the Sierra N of Bakersfield, up the valley to north of Redding, and even down here in the Monterey Bay. There's very little traffic on that system, and radios to access it are cheap (mostly decommissioned Motorola gear).

I also thank W6SX, W6GJB, and N6NG for their "off the list" support for my comments.

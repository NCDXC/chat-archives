Andreas,

For many years I did the unthinkable: used Home Depot water pipe!  TH6DXX was the antenna and the pipe was no more than 4' above the thrust bearing and 50' up.  Rotor 6' below the bearing.  Any more above would be asking for it.

I made a gin pole from a 20' piece of 6061 that was .125 wall. I had to but the full 20' stick.  Had the vendor cut it to 12' and 8'.  Not too expensive and free delivery to a business address.  I think they were in Hayward or thereabouts.  I can find out if you want but you could get a nice piece of aluminum there.  Not sure if I want to part with the 8' piece yet.
There is a shop in Santa Clara right off 101.  They do only aluminum and may also do tubes.  Plate is easy in any size or thickness you could ever want. 
 Lusk REM Center
3433 De La Cruz Blvd
Santa Clara, CA (408) 980-9598
Aluminum remnants. No minimum order.

I have a 20' 4130 chrome moly mast I need to dispose of.  No way you would need that, though.

If you want to do the math, D. Leeson's book on yagi design addresses it fully.  It will tell you what will work based on load, wind and height above the tower.  Very educational and takes the mystery out of knowing if your installation is going to fail in that wind storm that you hope will never happen!

Steve,

As you know, on Sept. 5, 2024 I started an email discussion with David Minster, ARRL CEO regarding the work we did in 2023-2024 on creating an “inactive status” for DXCC entities that are unreachable for: military, political or environmental reasons.

On Sept 16th our written proposal went to David Minster, Rick Roderick, Bart Jahnke, and later to Bill Lippert (representing the PSC). Prior to sending I mentioned the work to Minster and Roderick at various events.

At this writing I have heard nothing from ARRL, which doesn’t surprise me. Disappointed yes, but not surprised. In September Minster wrote I’d hear back from himself or Lippert, crickets.

In August, 2024 I was in Tokyo and met an American DXer. His DX club submitted a similar proposal to ARRL. Last I communicated with him, no reply from ARRL.

It should be obvious to anyone chasing DXCC that ARRL has continually pushed back on any changes to the DXCC rules. The world has changed and the DXCC rules no longer fit this new world order.

Many of the suggestions I’ve read on this reflector discussion are in the two proposals that went to ARRL.

I am an “old timer”, both in age and years in the hobby, #1 Honor Roll. I’m also a realist who sees the DXCC program is out of sync with reality.  While I don’t have the numbers, I’m guessing that Honor Roll members might be dying off faster than people qualifying with 331.

Additionally, I'm surprised that the Program -  Services Committee hasn't tasked the DXAC to study the 2 pending proposals. The DXAC rep for the Southwestern Division is a ghost, we never hear from him, nor does he return emails.

As Matt wrote "Awards should be attainable", he's correct. When there were only 2 out of play entities Honor Roll was still possible. That's yesterday's news, it's not possible today if the candidate doesn't already have the needed out of reach entities.

Cheers,
GS K5GS

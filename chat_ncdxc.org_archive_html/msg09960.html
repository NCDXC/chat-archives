Al,

Congrats on snagging Egypt.  That’s one Ive really struggled to snare so far.  And on phone, no less!  So far, Ive been finding it easier to work DX on CW than on phone (CW skimmer makes all the difference in slicing through pile ups.  Still haven’t figured out how to do that with phone, short of the old-school spin, listen & pounce).  And, yes, it feels incredible!! Its almost a zen experience - a little like fly fishing, learning to “read” a stream and think like the fish you are trying to catch.  Where skill, patience and timing converge into a single sublime moment.  Wait for it, listen to it, spot it,  massage your rig to optimize it, track it, pounce on it, work it, log it, do a quick happy dance & then post it on the spotting clusters.  Then on to the next spot ...

73

I think the paths between T6 land and the Western US are pretty few, far between and weak.  For the past couple of weeks, they would just start coming up to detectable levels here, and they would QRT.  Could see a faint trace on CW skimmer, and copy about 25% until I resorted to the old CW op standard:  AGC off, RF gain to max, tweak the AF limiter on my K3, and ride the audio level to avoid permanent hearing loss.  The audio distortion made me twitch a lot (like nails across a blackboard), but I was able to copy him at about 75% - just enough to work him.  Apparently he had me at roughly the same signal quality, as he needed me to repeat my call and report about five times.  Now, if I could just do something about that loud whistling noise  in my left ear …  ;-)

73

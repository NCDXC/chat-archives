It’s not part of the grant application or even a mandatory requirement, but when vetting a funding proposal, we do discuss with team leaders (particularly on major DXps with lots of funding) their plans to cover NA and the West Coast. (Depending on the location, East and West Coast NA may have VERY different propagation, in some cases with beam heading differences of 90 degrees or more.) 

Example: There was a recent DXp to Tristan de Cunha. However, his total focus was to be 6m EME, which did not justify NCDXF funding. (I did, however, issue him a SuperFox key so he could get better rate during the narrow openings for 6m EME.)

I’m also surprised that Yuris never requested a SF key; I would have surely issued him one. I think that many EU ops prefer MSHV and ordinary FT8.

I enjoy the hunt and the satisfaction when successful (some hunts lasted DAYS for that elusive single contact and ATNO), but (except in rare simplex operations like 7X7X at times) we don't have to hear, filter and sort the pileup.  (I also enjoy antenna experimentation and building the actual station, the technical side.)

As my closing comment, I'll submit that by the time the sunspots reappear with any useful numbers, FT8 will BE the traditional mode, surpassing RTTY, PSK and other digital modes, in part because the current generation is well inundated with point and click mentality.  That's for another discussion as time marches on and change is constant.

On 1/1/2020 5:36 AM, Keith Heimbold wrote:
I think FT8 is a pretty interesting mode but I think a Dxpedition should have the right to decide whether or not to use it. I personally enjoy the fun of the pileup and find spending hours tackling a major DXpedition using CW (mostly these days) way more fun and challenging than sitting there hoping my computer will make the contact for me. I don’t mind putting in the time to make a CW contact in heated pileups. Working 7X7X on 80m CW took me over an hour, 6O7O on 40m CW took me seven hours over a couple nights. This is the fun part for me. Trying to watch / listen to the pileup and figure out where the operators are listening.  Hearing my call sign come back and getting the DX in the log.

I use FT8 as a propagation and antenna test mode primarily. I know that commercially one particular vendor distributor of amps I spoke to at Belton Hamfest in October told me that FT8 is helping keep the commercial aspect of the amateur radio business thriving during a pretty bleak solar minimum. For that particular data point I am glad that Joe Taylor and his colleagues have come up with this set of modes. Do I enjoy them for DxIng, not so much. 

I am sure when conditions come back in a few years we will have more of the traditional modes being the primary modes and FT8 will be less discussed, debated and used. Maybe it is now the de facto digital mode replacing RTTY.  For now, if a DXpedition decides it doesn’t want to use a particular digital mode it is up to them.  I have seen CW only Dxpeditions in the past and they don’t get much discussion.

On Jan 1, 2020, at 12:20 AM, Rick NK7I via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

﻿ HNY Al,

If you’re the DX or a contester, fatigue is an absolute factor, but being the DX making contacts is not the same kind of satisfaction one can get from just working the DX.  As the DX, you’re still looking to maximize total Q rate and uniques while getting the most you can get in sheer bulk but that contest goes on for days or weeks, much like shift work.  Fatigue is apparent and unavoidable. 

It’s hard enough trying to bust the pileup to work the DX (which I clearly enjoy); compared to having to confront it as DX, sort out one call among that pileup mess (any mode); is more than my poor hearing can handle.  The little I’ve done of being DX when traveling tells me that being DX isn’t my thing, I don’t have the talent or the  willing endurance. 

My comments earlier were meant along that line, from the DX point of view.  To do point and click contacts here, is really rather boring, as the DX, it’s a break almost from other modes.  

I mostly use FT8 (the current digital mode of choice, not RTTY like we used before) to add digital band slots, being selective about calling and having occasional runs in response because of my current location (apparently this county is rare).

Last winter, because I have no RX antennas for low bands yet, FT8 helped a LOT in gaining 160M DXCC in one season (my personal self challenge).  CW was the second most common mode for that.   Since I’m relatively new to TB, both modes presented band ATNO for me as well as 160M Q’s with most of the DXpeditions that went out.   For a couple of those, FT8 was the only single contact that was possible (even one or two were any mode ATNO), while running some power (my meager antennas, weren’t adequate).  So I understand urban noise (I moved away) and inefficient antennas (getting better each year) and the ‘need’ for FT8.  

As the current flavor of digital mode, I maintain that any DXpedition should include FT8 to present QSO opportunities; which is the point of going there in the first place.  Let the little guys in on the fun, don’t put up roadblocks.

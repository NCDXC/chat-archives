Early on in my DX career I was told that listening was very important.  Everyone knows this, find out where the DX is listening and don't call when he's calling someone else, etc.  I found in my experience that this is useful on FT8 too;  particularly when trying to get through a wall of JA and EU stations.  When looking for a clear frequency to transmit, you find one, but you can't see/hear all the stations that the DX is seeing, so it may not be clear on the other end.  So I look to see who the DX is working and the frequency, and then occupy that same frequency which I know the DX can hear and is clear (I don't transmit until I see the 73 from the DX to that station though).  Maybe this is obvious but I find it quite successful and wanted to share.

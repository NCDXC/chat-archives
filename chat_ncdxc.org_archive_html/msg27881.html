Guys, given this new information, and my complete lack of knowledge about JTDX and how it works, I am very reluctant to give advice. 

As for me, If I see them transmitting Even with multiple streams, I will assume they are a classic Fox and Hound. I will call as Odd above 1000 Hz. 
If I see them transmitting Odd with multiple streams, I will assume they are JTDX F/H (which I know nothing) and I will call Even above 1000 Hz. I do not know if the calling above 1000 Hz is required when calling a JTDX Fox but I will assume for safety that it is. 

Last night, when I worked them at 10.44, I recognized that while they were multistream, they were not a WSJT-X Fox, so I called opposite of them above 1000 Hz and I was able to get a QSO. My red transmit cursor was never moved by them and I did get an RR73. 


Paul N6PSE

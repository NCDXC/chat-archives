Paul and Team

 

Up in the Sierra at our “summer QTH” we are working on installing  directional  beverage receiving antenna systems  for 160M, 80M and 40M. We have purchased everything we need from DX Engineering and we have just about everything  on site already.  Our site location is in Nevada County on top of a ridgeline/mountain top at an elevation of 5000 feet in a forest located in a wilderness area on several hundred acres of  property that is several miles away from any paved road, commercial power lines, neighbors etc except for deer, rattlesnakes and an occasional bear or two. We have our own antique surveyors transit and equipment and have already laid out the location for the beverage antenna system. Although the antenna location is within the forest a major portion of  the Beverage antenna system will be through an open area covered in dense Manzanita and  our next task is to clear a path through the Manzanita with our antique (but beloved) monster 9000 pound Allis Chalmers Diesel tractor using its hydraulic front blade.  The Beverage antenna system will be located approximately 2000 feet  from our cabin and from our other antenna systems and power sources. You can see a picture of our home made cabin at   http://www.qrz.com/db/K6XN .

 

In the past we have irritated the local rattlesnakes on occasion in putting up our existing ham radio towers and our 160M horizontal loop antenna but the good news is that neither the rattlesnakes nor the Manzanita is a match for our monster tractor and we are not in harms way as long as we stay on the tractor.  Ham radio is not for wimps!!!  Hopefully we will have everything working long prior to the DXpeditions and in use during the upcoming CQP and neither rattlesnakes nor ham radio operators will be harmed during our project. Some Manzanita however is in big trouble. 

 

K6YN, K6ON and KJ6WQQ are helping me install the steerable Beverage antenna system and they will be chasing DX along with K6XN as soon as we get everything operational.  See you in the pileups! 73, Ted, K6XN

My Qs pretty much match this pattern, too.

10/12m not heard.
15m: 0148Z (SP) cw no trace of signal until 0140Z then jumped to 579 for next hour
17m: 0041Z (SP) cw 54
20m: 1652Z (SP) ssb 59++; 1859Z (SP) cw - S3 running EUs (matched in ClubLog)
30m: 1556Z cw 59+
40m: 1543Z cw 59+
160m: heard 1545-1615Z 569

The 6 80m QSOs that show up in ClubLog are questionable - 23Z?? lots of daylight - suspect remote stations used in zone 5.

On Thu, Jan 30, 2014 at 4:57 AM, Paul@n6pse.com <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:
NCDXC fellas, here are my notes on FT5ZM so far

10 meters-have not heard them.
12 meters, very weak on CW before our sunset
15 meters, very weak between 5:30-6:30 PM via short path. (Watch for skewed paths)
17 meters, very weak on CW before our sunset, short path.
20 meters, very loud, S9 short path after sunrise and long path around sunset.
30/40 meters, quite strong at our sunrise and sunset, usually short path is better. These have been the better bands for me,
80/160 meters, I have not heard them

I hope this info helps any club members trying to get their first contacts.

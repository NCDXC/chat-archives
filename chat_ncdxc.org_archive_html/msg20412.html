Thanks for putting this together, Mike.  And to Ross for the history.

What a great idea for generating DX to try for W6s!  We could use that sometimes, maybe even now with poor condx.

I have received many QSL cards in the past that asked for my card for the ‘CA Award’ but I never knew what it was or who sponsored it.  Never really thought about it back then and when it faded out, it was just forgotten unless I was looking at an old card.

My cards were probably in the mid 70s onwards.  I looked at some of the pages Mike linked and find callsigns that I am sure I have worked.  Some were notable DX stations that lots of DXers worked.  It was interesting to see some big DXpeditions getting the award, like KP6KR on Kingman operated by club members.  I will look more to see what else jogs the memory.  Do we know when this was discontinued?

 

Thanks for the fun!

Al

NCDXC Members,

 

President Art Rizzi N6MFT recently received a box of records from Robert Bickel K6FX which contained an archive of records about the NCDXC California Award program that the Club ran for many years.  This award program ran from sometime in the late 1950s or early 1960s until 2002 when the last certificate was awarded.

 

The registers and lists in the archive contain many of the active DXers from that era, and I recognized quite a few callsigns.  I’ve scanned the significant lists and registers that were in the archive as well as other papers of relevance and these are all at this link for you to review:

 

https://www.dropbox.com/sh/fbhotns35ajvsqk/AAAb7a3Xy_AUQb0tTw9G6ewPa?dl=0

 

Our Club Archivist and Historian Ross Forbes K6GFJ recalls this about California Award program:

 

“I’ll have to go back and dig out when the award was first created, but it was created to get more DX stations to make an effort to work W6’s. Afterall, once a DX station received a single card from California, he had enough towards his/her WAS. So the NCDXC decided to create an attractive award that required the DX station to work lots of W6’s. The award was offered ONLY to stations outside of the 48 contiguous states (I believe VE’s were eligible, but I’d have to verify that). Any station within the US was NOT eligible to win the award (after all we are a DX club). In order to obtain the California Award, the DX station needed to have QSL cards from 200 stations who were in California when the contact was made. This was back in the days when a “6” really was in California instead of anywhere in the US. Besides the 200 stations within California, the DX station also had to have received 50 (it might have been 25 members) QSL cards from NCDXC members.

 

Not so hard you think? The problem was there were a number of very active, well known, NCDXC members who were shall we say rather poor sending out their QSL cards. I remember seeing letters from DX stations sent to the club asking for help to obtain QSL cards from members, since they already had the 200 “6’s” but were lacking the 50 (or 25) cards from the NCDXC!  Growing up in the area, I also remember listing to the HF bands and hearing DX stations calling for 6’s and telling everyone they were trying to obtain the California Award. It was not that easy to obtain.

 

The original certificate was in full color and was rather large. I recall seeing the certificate that was sent later on and it was much smaller (maybe 8.5 by 11 inches) and in black and white. When the award was popular, the club also had a distinctive Orange QSL card that included the details about the award on each QSL card. If somebody didn’t want to use the club card, there was a small flyer members could include their own cards that had all of the rules for the award along with a list of all the current NCDXC members (at the time of the printing), so the DX station to search for those cards. Another problem was that some time members didn’t include the letters on their QSL card indicating they were a member of the NCDXC. This was way before we had a club logo from W6KM (then WB6WKM). If there wasn’t an original certificate in the box, I can dig out my own copy of the California Award. The picture of the one awarded to Rubin Hughes (WA6AHF/HK0) is one of the original certificates.

 

Who was responsible for the award? I recall it was managed by one person, but maybe there was one or two helpers. Again, I’d have to go back in my files and look.”

 

This archive of materials about the NCDXC California Award will eventually be put into the NCDXC Archives along with the W6IKQ QSL card archive.

 

- 73 and good DX de Mike, K6MKF, NCDXC Secretary

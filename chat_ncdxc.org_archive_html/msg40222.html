A little DXCC history.

Calls were always listed with absolute totals. 4X4DK is now at 395* mixed. The asterisk indicating Honor Roll. Eventually calls with very high numbers showed no asterisk. Inactivity and deletes eroded HR ranking but the absolute numbers remained. The unfortunate message to new DXers was that those high totals were unachievable. You can never "work them all." Goa anyone? So why bother? 

"Current" finally came into use. More realistic. HR now lists calls under their current total/deletes. Modes are listed with absolute totals and asterisks. Absolutes for band totals, no asterisks.

When the DXCC Challenge was announced it sounded like a worthwhile goal. But the fine print had its start date for contacts retroactive to the resumption of amateur radio at the end of WWII. November 15, 1945. No surprise that W4DR was the very the first recipient of the DeSoto Cup.

CW DXCC started as of Jan 1, 1975, no retroactive Qs. It took some time for activity to ramp up the CW active counters. For a time you could be on the CW HR, even at the Top, with totals well below 300. You could also be on the CW HR with totals more than 9 from the top number because some of those slots had no calls and just weren't listed.

The hunt for new ones on CW caused some to work stations on SSB and ask them - sometimes begging - to flip over on frequency for a CW Q. Some of these were one-day CW. The SSB station copied your call. You card said "UR CW Report 599." Two-way didn't have to be explicit to count. Then a rule change. "Confirmations for cross-mode contacts for this award must be dated September 30, 1981, or earlier." After that Qs had to be two-way despite some ops with totals that included cross-mode Qs with their listed call. No practical way to correct this. If your card said one-way it wouldn't count for dates after the rule change. But it didn't have to explicitly say two-way either.

Some haunted the W7PHO SSB  "family hour" waiting for the emperor to finally allow a CW Q on frequency.

These posts have run the spectrum from the joy and pleasure of DXing to no reason is too small to bash the ARRL while freeloading off of and extolling the virtues of Clublog.

Some posts are just really good for a chuckle.

Back to DXing. See you in the pile ups.

73 - Steve WB6RSE

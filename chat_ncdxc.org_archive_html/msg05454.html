I am a new NCDXC member, and I live well beyond the fringes of any South Bay repeater system in Sacramento.  I am sometimes able to copy the club repeater @ about 40% quieting, but that is heavily dependent on conditions - iffy at best - and I have never been able to get into the machine from my QTH.  What I would love to be able to do is at least monitor the repeater, if not work it, via an Echolink node if one could be set up.

However, at the risk of throwing high-octane on the embers, I have an echolink node box, a GE MASTR II 440 MHz repeater, an ICS Linker 2-Port controller a four cavity PASS-REJECT duplexer and an Angle Linear UHF pre-amp that I would be happy to donate to the club.  The unit was assembled by a retired engineer down in the LA area. I had an approved repeater pair from NARCC, 444.350, for which the repeater is currently crystalled.  All was set to go, then PAVE PAWS took a swipe at me and the unit never went live. It was fully bench-checked and air tested prior to being moth-balled, but has been sitting on my bench ever since.

If the club could make use of it, either as a linked machine or as trading stock for gear necessary to improve the club's existing system, I would be happy to donate it.  All I would ask for is a pledge to get an Echolink or other IRLP node up and running on the repeater system at some point, as it is likely the only way I will be able to get reliable access the system from SAC, though Im even flexible on this.

I am also be willing to go through the process of re-applying to NARCC for a new repeater pair, as the frequency pair it is now set up with is incompatible with machines in the local area (there is a coordinated Palo Alto machine on the same pair).

Please let me know.

Thanks & 73

Peter Gambee
K6TTD

It was great meeting and its a blast to meet people in person. 

I just noticed that Kingman Reef is now firmly in the Top 10 Most Wanted on several Clublog lists, and just off the Top 10 in the DX Publishing List (which is already out of date due to FT5ZM) . . . 

I feel much better to have had the discussion(s) that we did at the IDXC, and the big surprise was talking to Ned Stearns at the Top Band Dinner - where he explained what it was like on Kingman in 200 with K5K, and that he actually measured the reef at that time. 

I think my fascination with Kingman should be obvious - it would be easy to work from the West Coast - so yeah - I am "selfish" in that regard (hi hi), but for a Top 10 - even though it wouldn't be cheap - it sure is a lot less to activate than a Polar Entity . . . 

I was very pleased to hear that they are still working diligently on Navassa - I always have had confidence that the KP1-5 group would persevere. Very surprisingly, K3LP/KP5 was my first Desecheo QSO - so Dave and Kam beat the KP1-5 team to that punch, and K5D was many landfills (when I used to chase those).

Andy,

With all the potential confusion in what should be simple(r), I maintain that these points should happen with future the DXpeditions (no harshness or slurs intended); 

1) announce the precise FT8 mode (MSHV or F/H), tell the caller what to expect; 
2) ensure that the DX only transmits on the EVEN slot AND 
3) that they take some means to ensure their clock is on the correct time… GPS puck, GPS NTP server or at least listen to one of the many time signals around the world that should get them pretty close.
4) Further that callers TO the DXpedition team LEARN how to use the mode well BEFORE the team departs for the DX entity.  (It is THE most used mode blah blah blah.). 

We do the same learning for CW or phone; RTTY or PSK and even how to correctly use the rig; this is but one more extension of knowledge to gain to reach the goal, contact.

Those points should lower the trepidation, questions and make things easier for all, including the pilots and the team.

Yes, you can consider these as points learned from the 3Y0J adventure (and many other teams, doing their very best efforts in going places most of the time I certainly do not wish to attempt).  But this is in NO WAY a slur to that effort or any particular operator or team.

It’s always in the smallest of details where things are often missed.

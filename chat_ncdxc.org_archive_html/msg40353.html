As you probably know, Anna W6NN passed away in June. Historically, K6MMM has done very well in the California QSO Party as a Multi-Single, ALWAYS taking 1st place in Santa Cruz County, and almost always one of the top three statewide. I’m looking for one (or possibly, two) monkeys to help out this year.

Need to be a contest-competent SSB op, CW a plus but lack of contest-class CW not a showstopper. Prefer someone willing to switch off 3-4 hour shifts during the day, and work the night shift (80/40 meters, mostly), but we don’t have to go for the full 30 hour limit (it gets pretty slow in the wee hours). If we get two extra monkeys, workload is reduced for everyone.

Must be contest-competent, N1MM-fluent. Station is Icom 7600 driving Acom 2000a at legal limit. Antennas: 80m inverted vee, 40m Extended Double Zepp (kicks butt!), 3 elements at 70’ on 20/15 meters, 5 elements at 70’ on 10 meters, entire station at 2000’ elevation. Set up for hands-free operation (headset, footswitch, function keys for all normal exchanges, individually recorded CQs, full Telnet/Cluster/RBN assist and more).

Full automation, integration of logger, radio, amps, antenna tuner. Everything tracks band and mode changes with no manual intervention. Whole-house generator backup (26 kW) in event of power failure, capable of supporting legal limit station indefinitely.

Includes all the bananas you can eat (all meals, snacks, whatever you like, actually), and sleeping accommodations. Ability to make monkey sounds a plus (Hooo! Hooo! Hooo!). PL259 membership a BIG PLUS (we usually win the Small Club Category).

Great opportunity for small-pistol operator to try their hand at a big(ger) pistol station, or an older op who can’t do 24 hours Butt-in-Chair anymore.

Contact me directly at: ke1b@richseifert.com.

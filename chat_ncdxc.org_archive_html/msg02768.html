For Top 10 activations, I do understand the need to assemble and send a team of absolutely top notch operators.

 

FT5ZM is certainly doing this, and I would expect that “seat availability” is a driver. One of my favorite teams is the Microlite Penguins, and the FT5XO operation for me was one of the absolute best. They went to a top 10 ultra-rare and very difficult place to get to and just amazed me on CW on the low bands – heck – all bands. I can only hope that all South Seas operations go as well as that one.

 

For example, if you are chartering the Braveheart, which can take 12 operators, you will want to be a lot more selective than say, something like the Marion DuFresne, or even the Shogun. And by this – I’m not being negative – but for the larger and less rare DX-peditions, heck – bring along as many serious and conscientious people at all levels, and bring as many future top notch operators. The future of DX-ing and DX-peditioning counts on this.

 

But in the end, it’s the “pay per seat” that pays the lion’s share of the trip. This means the people available – at whatever skill level – make or break an expedition even happening. If you can only find 8 out of the 12 for the Braveheart who are “world class” for a top 10 activation (because the cost is so high you will be forced to be less “selective”, then you have to find 4 other very serious future world class ops). Act like you are hiring a new employee where everyone on the team can make or break your business. The best businesses I have worked for were very diverse – and the best teams had older, very experienced programmers, and new, very smart programmers right out of school – who gave the experienced a run for their money. They had come from all backgrounds and countries too. Somehow – the team was “magical”. I believe the interview process is critical here.

 

There have been a few DX-peditions where I thought the team was excellent, and I can’t remember this same group of people activating as a team before. I’m thinking about ZL9HR, which I thought radio operation wise was masterful, and am very glad to see that a good part of that team is continuing on as a newly “minted” DX-pedition team.

 

So – the future “top notch operators” already exist – and they are people we aren’t accustomed to seeing them on DX-peditions in the past. Maybe they have only gotten to that time in their life where they can afford the time and money to go on one of these? They might have been contesting for years, but have never been on a DX-pedition before. And they end up looking like a seasoned DX-er even on their first DX-pedition. I think there are a LOT of people out there like this.

 

73,

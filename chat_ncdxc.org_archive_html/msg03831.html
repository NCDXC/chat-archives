On 10/26/2013 10:15 PM, Paul Ewing wrote:

Congratulations to all who made it in his log!


A fine, highly cooperative enterprise, Paul..Many thanks fo all.
This event emphasizes the importance of improvements to to the repeater. I worked EP3PK at 03:03Z, but could not alert the gang to his operating frequency until I went back to my email computer in the house ten minutes later. Tha't ten minutes of prime propagation lost to others who might have made it, and the internet software introduced additional delays that appear to have been about five more minutes.
BTW -- the thing that got me tuning around for him was your important cimment that his primary rig was down and he was using a secondary rig, so I assumed his frequency might be wrong, which it was. And the P3 quickly found him, returning my 16 WPM call.
Another point -- there was endless blather on the repeater about 706, with no break for me, even if I could have managed to hit it, which I could not, even with 160W and 4 elements. We need some discipline about use of the repeater in these circumstances. Some of us are not the great operators we like to think we are.

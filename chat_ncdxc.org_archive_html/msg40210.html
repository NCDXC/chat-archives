+ AA6YQ comments below

I believe that if the ARRL offers and promotes an award that the Award should be attainable.

+ The DXCC Award was first introduced in 1937 - 88 years ago. From the outset, there was no guarantee  that #1 Honor Roll would be
attainable, and yet countless DXers have pursued it, and continue to pursue it.  

 Currently it is not possible (in a lifetime) to attain Honor Roll for anyone starting the journey now or in the recent past. Change
is required when a program becomes stale. 

+ The award is not stale to me, nor to the many other DXers currently pursuing it. It wasn't stale to the DXers whose pursued it
when QSOs with BY were impossible, or when QSOs with ZA were impossible, or when QSOs with 7O were impossible, or ...

+ What is so special about *now* that suddenly demands a change?

+ If you're not interested in pursuing #1 Honor Roll as it has long been defined, then don't pursue it.  Don't advocate it's
elimination simply because you personally don't' like it when there are obviously plenty of DXers who do - more than 46 pages of
them:

http://www.arrl.org/system/dxcc/view/DXCC-HR-20250805-USLetter.pdf

+ I have no objection to the ARRL creating a new award that is "attainable"; it can be done without eliminating #1 Honor Roll has it
has long been defined, and long been pursued.

        73,

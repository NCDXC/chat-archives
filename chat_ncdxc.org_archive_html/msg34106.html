Thanks for the thread about the OE3IDE software. Though I’m a Mac user, it got me curious. As it turns out HamAlert also has several other modes of receiving spots. Look at the “destinations” tab at hamalert.org. In particular there is a telnet interface reachable at hamalert.org:7300. You can just log into it with a telnet client using your HamAlert credentials and see whatever spots your triggers detect (so this should also work on Linux). This apparently complies with some standard folks have used for software integration, so with a bit of finagling I got it working inside MacLoggerDX’s spotting display. So I not only have access to HamAlert spots on MacOS but it’s integrated into my logging software. It can use the text-to-speech alert mechanism of MacLoggerDX so I now get voice announcements when a new spot shows up, with or without the radio being in the loop.

I thought this might be of interest to folks that are not Windows users and particularly MacLoggerDX users.

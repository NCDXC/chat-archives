On 4/3/2013 8:44 AM, Steve Jones wrote:
I didn’t hear you last night, but at twilight I was working a bunch of RW9s along with 4K6C, 4L1MA, EX2F, and A65CA.  As the skip moved west to Europe after dark worked RW7B, UA3KW, and finally heard EA5UB a little before 9:00pm.  With a SFI of 121 and a K index of 1, this was a classic springtime opening. 

Another great reason for getting into contesting is that you learn a lot about propagation from your home QTH to various parts of the world. It is pretty common for there to be 20M openings to northern EU during the mid- to late-evening hours. Last night, I saw some spots for 15M around 9pm for EU and made some Qs. RI1ANP was also there calling CQ, loud.

Figuring that if 15M was open, 17M might be too, and I need more band-countries there than on 15M, I started calling CQ on 18,070 beaming over the pole to EU.  First call yielded RI1ANP, loud enough to work off the back of my SteppIR, and 589 when I reversed the SteppIR to point south.  I then reversed the SteppIR to go north, and made several Qs.

It's also quite common for 30M and 40M to be open to EU for several hours around their sunrise.

During WPX SSB this weekend, I worked a couple of dozen northern EU and UA0 stations during that evening 20M opening. 

73, Jim K9YC

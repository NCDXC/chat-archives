When I first joined the NCDXC after getting my novice ticket in 1990, the old-timers on the club's repeater preached "listen, listen, listen!". Great, but listen where? And listen when?

I saw this an opportunity to convert the burgeoning capabilities of computer technology - ever-faster and ever-cheaper microprocessors, RAM, hard drives combined with ever-denser ever-cheaper color monitors to create tools that would enable DXers to discover exactly when and where to listen for a DX station they aspire to work.

DXLab's SpotCollector component inserts every spot received from up to 7 sources - DX Cluster nodes and local instances of WSJT-X - into a database with one entry per active station. Among other things, SpotCollector can display the information in some or all of this database on a Band vs. Time-of-Day chart.

 

On the left side of this diagram,

 

https://www.dxlabsuite.com/Where%20and%20When%20to%20Listen.pdf

 

the chart shows when and where ZC4GR was spotted in FT8 over the past 3 weeks. This is ZC4GR's "Activity Pattern".

On the right side of the above diagram, the chart shows when and where stations within 500 miles of my QTH spotted stations in ZC4, 5B4, TA, OD, 4X, and SU over the past 3 days. These are actual - not predicted - propagation openings between me and ZC4GR.

As the two charts reveal, there's an intersection between ZC4GR's activity pattern and my propagation openings to ZC4: 20m from 18Z to 22Z.

Since I'm trying to confirm every DXCC entity in FT8, whenever possible I patrolled 20m FT8 starting at 18Z. I worked ZC4GR a few weeks later at 2034Z.

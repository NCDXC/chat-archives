Paul,

 

I’ll put politics aside.

 

I travel so only DX when I’m home.  That limits my exposure.  Last year was a pretty good travel year.  2012 was even better; I was away about half the year (4 months in Alaska being the greater part).

 

The Wake teams (there were two different expeditions) were easy because of proximity; it’s all water from here to there and the bands were wide open so I discount them (though they did very well).  Another venture filled almost ALL the band modes for that entity, without significant effort (should I discount them too?).  For others, I’d have to review the log to remind me of how I did (not) do.  I don’t get them all, I wasn’t home enough, the road beckons.  I don’t make log notes, so I’d have to compare my successes to remind me.

 

All in all, it was a GREAT DX year for me, plenty of ATNO (South Korea on 100 watts at 160M shocked me, it’s a new band for me) but I won’t turn down more, I’m greedy that way.  Amsterdam is #216 since roughly Oct 2010 when I restarted DXing, all ‘holiday’ mode operating.  My favorite is always the current or last one :o).  And for someone that never really enjoyed CW, that’s the greater amount of contacts (most efficient for my station).

 

I *DO* however strongly believe that the current FT5ZM team has greatly raised the bar.  I’ve heard NO complaints only compliments.  They understand and have used ALL the tools and tricks to maximize their efforts to all locations.  They haven’t missed much and even tossed in a few new twists (‘drifting’ CW frequency to foil jammers).  They’ve been amazing, except I still only have the single (40 CW) contact but I keep trying.  My station isn’t competition grade QRO.  While others have offered the use of their stations, I don’t work that way; it’s mine or nothing.

 

The FT5ZM team will leave a very positive lasting impression and will be a point of comparison for decades to come.  But that’s next years discussion.

 

I’ll make that log review and let you know, but it was a REALLY good year for DXing.  Hard choices...

 

73,

Rick wa6nhc

Here is a QOTW I can comment on.

 

1 – Better internal automatic antenna tuners.  With many DX contacts being made barefoot, the internal tuners are handy.  Elecraft and now the Kenwood TS-990S can match up to 10:1 SWR values and do it fast.  I have both radios and found both can match 10:1 SWR.  You may note the TS-990S specification says up to 3:1 but in real life experience it matches up to 10:1 like the K3 and KX3.  All relay switched L and C.  The radios with motor driven C and switched L have never been able to match better than 3:1.

 

2 – Amplifiers – need a full legal limit solid state amplifier with these features: plenty of protection, large capacity internal antenna tuner.  Two excellent examples are Elecraft’s KPA500 and in the case of protection the Yaesu Quadra.  Both rugged, tough and well protected amplifiers.  Have you ever seen an amplifier with an automatic 100 watt input attenuator (in case of 200 watt radios)?  The Quadra has it but its internal tuner basically sucks.

 

3 – Amplifiers using a common “standard” band-data interface.  The KPA500 and Quadra do an excellent job.  It is nice to see Elecraft and Yaesu are using the same interface making it simple to connect both to the Yaesu Quadra – two inputs.  Change a band on either radio causes the amplifier to immediately change band.  No RF required.  Need all that in a 1500 watt solid state amplifier and by more than one or two manufacturers.

 

Note:

Getting our stations to be more nimble is important.  We have seen the manufacturer’s providing features moving us along that vector but not far enough.

 

Having a station that is automated does not mean we don’t need to know how all this works.  This is not to dumb-down our hobby participants.  We should be able to go back to Pi network output stages with little difficulty.

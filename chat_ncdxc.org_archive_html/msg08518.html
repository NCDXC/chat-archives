On page 11-24 of the ARRL Antenna Book, a 2 element 30M yagi example shows a
15 foot boom, with elements described using _some_ taper schedule. 

It's important to know what the KLM taper schedule is to be able to model it
correctly. If you are lucky - all you would then have to do is remove some
aluminum at the tip - and go with a 15' spacing on the boom. My guess is
that the current boom is somewhere in the vicinity of 22' (??). Deciding to
cut such a boom is a big decision - unless it conveniently is pieced
together in sections that can just be removed - as you would the elements.
Once you cut that boom . . . . (ask me how I know!).

If you can get the EZNec file for that antenna from KLM, then I could try to
play with it. 

Luckily, a direct feed 2 element yagi is pretty easy to model - once you
have the correct taper schedule. 

Even easier - YW can be used for such a basic antenna.

Right now I am in the middle of modeling a dual bander - 2 elements on 30M
and 4 on 20M. Just for fun.

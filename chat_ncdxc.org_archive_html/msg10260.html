Interesting.  But the site is silent about whether the different entities which sponsor awards (like ARRL, RSGB, DARC, JARL etc) will accept this form of QSL as valid for awards.

Unfortunately, this is the strangle hold for all forms of electronic QSL including LOTW.

I have thought for a long time that ARRL has a marketing opportunity to turn LOTW into a revenue share opportunity with other national societies and in the process, scale by distributing the certificate origination process used to validate QSO records (I won’t digress into crypto-overkill for validation – it is what it is).

After (I think) 20 years, LoTW (still only) shows 77K registered users with 115K active certificates.

eQSL FWIW claims almost 300K member accounts (but that includes me and I no longer submit eQSL because other than for its own awards, its not a lot of use) - http://eqsl.cc/qslcard/StatsTableCards.cfm

A worldwide, adjudicated database for QSL’s would be great IFF it were accepted by the majority of award issuers.

ARRL has taken a step forward with WPX and QRZ, it would be great it it could embrace being THE logbook of the world rather than A logbook of part of the world!

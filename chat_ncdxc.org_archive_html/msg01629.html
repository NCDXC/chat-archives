Oh my, that is so sad to hear.  I knew Smitty since I was in high school, but fondly remember my days at Foothill College and working very close with Smitty on a number of projects.  Besides his support for Project OSCAR, I remember the days while I was managing the radio station at Foothill. The NCDXC was looking for a new location for its repeater. While Smitty convinced the Board of Directors of Foothill College, and I handled the details at the radio site, we were able to move W6TI up to its present location on Black Mountain.  Smitty was always there to support young people and Amateur Radio in particular.  There are just so many ways that Smitty was involved in Amateur Radio. We have lost one of Amateur Radio’s great individuals.

 

My family and I have so many fond memories of Smitty. Good DX and rest in peace, Smitty.

 

73,

It is with great sadness that I have to report that my very good friend Smitty W6CS passed away this morning.  He was an active DX chaser, contester, and participated in many other ham radio activities for decades.  He also was very active in humanitarian efforts in Africa and the Soviet Union/East Europe area dealing with the aftermath of Chernobyl.

Smitty was the president of the first Foothill College board starting the very successful school that has had more than two million students pass through.

I will post information on when a memorial service will be held when it becomes available.

Good DX and KB Smitty

de  Jim Abraham W6EB

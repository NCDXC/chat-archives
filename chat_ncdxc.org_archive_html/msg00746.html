On Dec 17, 2012, at 8:46 AM, Andreas Junge wrote:

> 
> I have been wondering why it always has to be done in form of Mega Expeditions. There are lots of groups of two and three and they are activating fairly rare places. Why not do 4 smaller expeditions every other year vs one Mega Expedition every 10 years? Pile-ups would be smaller and maybe the expeditions can focus on low bands vs high bands …
> 

The problem with the super-rare locations is that the bulk of the expense is transportation--leasing ships (and in some cases, helicopters), and moving heavy equipment (generators, fuel, etc.) across the globe. That baseline expense is approximately the same whether there are two people or twenty in the DXpedition party. It only makes sense to spend the big transport bucks when you can generate LOTS of QSOs in return. The cost-per-QSO of a two-man trip to Bouvet or South Georgia or any of the Antarctic islands would be prohibitive.

That said, I would welcome a pre-expedition funding drive. I don't think that the current DXpeditions do a very good job of marketing or advertising BEFORE the trip. I would contribute to many of these DXpeditions, except that I don't hear about them until I see the spots (or hear the immediately-pre-trip chatter). I invariably send *something* afterwards, particularly if I work them a few times, but I wish they had contacted the community well before the trip, in the planning stage.

The problem with "We'll go if/when the funding is sufficient" is what to do if the funding doesn't reach the target. People donate in expectation of a future DXpedition; if it doesn't happen, do they get their money back? Do the funds go into a "slush fund" at NCDXC or some other escrow agent? For sure, if there is $300K in a fund towards a $500K DXpedition that didn't make it, someone will show up the next day with a proposal for a $299K DXpedition somewhere else, and beg/plead/cajole for the pile of money that is already there.

My personal conclusion: "Contingency" funding (taking donations and not going unless some minimum is achieved) won't work. A DXpedition team must commit to the journey, but should "go to the people" (i.e., solicit individual contributions) early on, during the planning stages. I haven't seen this happen; for the most part, the big DXpeditions go to the foundations early, but not "the people." 

I wish there was some practical incentive that could be offered for modest pre-trip donations. (For large donations, you can buy a seat at the table, but that has its own issues.) Of course, the best incentive is a private sked, but that is almost impossible to pull off, as everyone can hear what is going on. We have tried things like unpublished frequencies, unusual splits, etc. They *sometimes* work, but only for a few minutes, at best. Once someone figures out what is going on, the pileup magically arrives. And if there are a LOT of folks that are in on the secret (as would be the case for pre-trip donors), then it isn't really a secret anymore.

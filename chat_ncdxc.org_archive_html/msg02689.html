Hello again Ross Forbes --


Here's the latest on those two large yagi antennas in Carmichael. 


Could you let the members of your organization know about two large, yagi antennas that are now being offered at no cost? This is because they were offered on an Ebay auction and didn’t receive any bids. The accompanying towers are also free if needed.


The school in Carmichael, California will be building on the site of the two antennas and will have to destroy the antennas and tower/pole on or about August 15 if there are no “takers.” The school would like to save the antennas and have them used elsewhere. Both antennas appear to be in good shape. The original ham owner is now deceased.


The largest five-element yagi has an estimated boom length of 46 feet and the driven element is approximately 35 feet long. The tower with the antenna is approximately 75 feet high and is secured with 16 guy wires. Unfortunately, the tower is not the crank-down or tilt-over type. There is also a discone-type antenna above the yagi.


The smaller five-element yagi has an estimated boom length of 24 feet and the center element is approximately 22 feet long. Because of coils on three of the elements, this yagi must be a multi-bander. This antenna is supported by a wooden electrical-type pole with climbing inserts on the sides of the pole.


The antennas would best be reached by a “cherry-picker”-type vehicle (or a long fire-truck-type ladder), although the antennas could be reached by experienced climbers. Whatever method might be used, the school will have the person or persons taking the antennas sign a waiver removing the school from any liability in case of an accident.


I am helping the school with this offer. Please contact me initially.

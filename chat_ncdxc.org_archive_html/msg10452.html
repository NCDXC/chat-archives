On Thu,8/13/2015 8:46 AM, Tony Dowler via Chat wrote:

I am still in need of a few people to help get my GAP antenna back up.


Tony,
I think the problem is that we're all getting old, and fewer of us are capable of working safely on a roof. I helped NI6T a few years ago, and I felt pretty shaky up there. I don't go on my own roof.
This is when we need some younger folks to step up and pitch in. I've been quite lucky to find the help I've needed among NCCC members and Santa Cruz locals.
Mounting safely to a roof is a non-trivial matter. Attachment points must be made into structure so that the first good wind doesn't pull them out. That may require reinforcement on the underside of the roof. It's also important to weatherproof around those points so they don't leak. And, of course, the antenna must be guyed. The nice guy rope that HRO sells works well, BUT must be insulated from the antenna so that it doesn't get melted by RF. Ask me how I know that. :) 

73, Jim K9YC

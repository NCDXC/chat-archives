Hi Bob,
I'm using a 160M inverted L that should not work.  I have a DXEngineering 1:1 common mode choke, with the case and the 'shield' side connected to a water pipe (no counterpoise).  Then the radiator goes over the top of a tree at about 65' and is tied to a shed.  It loads beautifully on 160M, I've been using it on other bands while putting in a tower and SteppIR.  It was always meant as temporary.  I also have a 80M L (not inverted) with the radial about 3-6' above ground (that adds at least a couple dB less loss).  Again, it's not ideal but the 160M antenna won't load on 80M.
Verticals are known to be noisy.  These are.  I don't have the trees aligned for a 160M loop.  One hard rule is NOTHING ham radio can be seen from ANY window in the house (I bought the view, it came with a house).  The garage is an exception, that is going to be the new shack.
I figure on the 160M inverted L, at least half the RF is keeping worms warm.  I've been using a typical 500 watts.  But it has allowed 117 entities worked on 160M (95 confirmed so far).  I added H40TT last week (he has GREAT ears, barely heard him; worked a couple times for insurance); I have confirmed South Africa and Saint Helena on 160M  (sure shocked me to work them!).  It involves a LOT of late nights/early mornings.
I am replacing the inverted L with an 80/160M resonant vertical antenna next year (after the ground thaws) along with a pair of RBOG for receiving (NW/SE and NE/SW).  The 80/160 will have a traditional counterpoise (on or slightly above dirt) and a tuned circuit so that no further tuning is required.  As there is no tree where I want it, it will be a compromise (what isn't?) but with a real counterpoise, should produce more signal (that's the easy part, add power, improve the antenna).  That requires only 2 coax feeds into the shack (plus RBOG and the SteppIR and rotor cables).  Simple and clean; my goals.
Receiving is 90% of the effort, you must reduce noise and directivity helps.  RBOG can be put in a smaller space than a Beverage and often outperform the Beverage (you'll want a pre-amp at the RBOG feed).  Some folks bury them (keeps the tools and critters from destroying them).  I have the space here, but not enough for a Beverage (not without losing it every time the hay was harvested).  The K9AY receive system is another option (less gain, less directive)...
Back on the original point, last night EU was working some West Coast stations, it was a fine night, even here under the arctic cap (I'm on that fringe, almost Canada eh?).
It's ALL about the noise, the rest is common sense and there is a LOT of noise around, even here (typical -116 dBm on the P3 scope) although lately it's been -112, on 160M (it was -80 dBm when I lived in CA).
At least that's my take on things.  I'm having a blast on 160M. With the almost negative solar cycle, it's the perfect time to play there. 

GL es gud dx, 73,
Rick nk7i

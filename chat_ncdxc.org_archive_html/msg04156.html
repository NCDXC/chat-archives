Thanks to all for the great comments.  There are quite a few really nice products out there.  With the current technology driven by very powerful low cost microprocessors and the availability of low cost graphic displays, analyzers that were only a dream a few years ago are now affordable.

 

I have been looking these products for a while and it’s good to know that there doesn’t appear to be too many bad choices out there.

 

I have decided on the RigExpert AA-230 Pro.  The RigExpert products have received good reviews both here and on the web.  Part of my choice is personal preference.  I like the ergonomic hand held design that fits nicely in one hand (at least in my large hand), also the graphic display.  The PC software looks good also.  I moved up from the AA-170 for a several reasons:

 

·         Transistor stage with additional protection

·         Sinusoidal output signal vs. rectangular

·         4 impedances (25, 50, 75 & 100) vs. 2 (50 & 75)

·         16-bit ADC vs. 10-bit

·         Rechargeable battery pack with charger vs. AA cells

·         Time Domain Reflectometer (TDR) built-in

 

You can see a comparison chart of the models at: http://www.rigexpert.com/index?f=compaa

 

But the biggest factor was that the AA-230 Pro is now on sale and I could get one for about $15 more than the AA-170 so that was a no-brainer.  Apparently this module is being discontinued, hence the sale prices.

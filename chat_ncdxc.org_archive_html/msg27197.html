I've worked FT8WW on 30 and 20 meters with about five minutes of calling. 

Here is how I did it:

1) I decoded him as ODD so I called Even (critical step) 

2) I saw that he was spotted at 10.131 and was 300-400 Hz on the waterfall. 

3) I purposely put my VFO at 10.130 which put him 1400 Hz +/- on the waterfall. 

4) Recognizing that he is NOT Fox and Hound but is using MSHV, I called him below his QRV frequency on the waterfall. Around 700 Hz. I had no competition and a clear signal to him. 

5) Amid the huge pileup and overlap of signals, he saw my signal in the clear and worked me quickly. 

6) I did the same on 20 meters. He was at 14.085. I put my VFO at 14.084 and saw him around 1300 Hz on the waterfall. I called around 800 Hz, completely in the clear and quickly had a QSO. 

Try this technique, it works marvelous as long as the DX is NOT Fox and Hound. 

73 and good luck!

Ted Davis, W6BJH was an early influence in my DXing. He definitely gets most of the credit. I met him at a ham radio class that he was co-teaching with another fellow named Art at the YMCA building down the hill from Carlmont HS. Art taught the CW - no problem there - and Ted taught the theory - that I needed. I was in the 8th grade, and I had been having trouble several years earlier with the General Class written test - fifth and sixth grade. I was licenses in the 4th grade, but my license expired and I was officially off the air until I got my General Class in 1956.

 I also learned that Ted was a DXer, and to one meeting, he  brought a large stack of DX QSL cards that he had collected from his operation during Cycle 18 (1947ish). That did it! He had a bunch of Russian cards that impressed me, along with a large number of cards from other places, too. At the time, he lived in Redwood City, just around the corner from my aunt and uncle in "south" end (Blenheim and Berkshire). I visited a few times, and was hooked on DXing. Before moving to WY, we did one contest together in the late sixties.

I ran into Ted again after moving to Wyoming. I was in a UHF telephone link building on Snow King mountain adjacent to Jackson. On the wall of the building was written in pencil "Ted Davis" and his San Carlos (Fairnon) telephone number. Since there was a 'phone in the building, I just gave him a call! We talked for some time . . . not about telephones either.

The last time I saw Ted - and for some time - he was and had been living about 1/4 mile from my Mother in Redding, CA. I visited several times, and always enjoyed our time together.

Ted WILL be missed here, for sure.

Wayne, N7NG
Jackson Hole

On Feb 23, 2014, at 12:41 PM, PETER GAMBEE <paramountsa@mac.com> wrote:

> 
> A couple of quick questions for the group:
> 
> I currently have 91 confirmed DXCC contacts on LoTW. I have at least another 60 or so contacts in the log that have yet to be confirmed that indicate that confirmation by LoTW is either  or "ok" (and more than 100 "direct" that I have yet to process and mail out - Ugh) Many of these LoTW contacts I posted to my account a couple of months ago and have yet to get a confirmation.  
> 
> Is there an accepted waiting time on my end before I attempt to politely encourage a response? 
> 
> What is the best way to prompt these 60 DX contacts to post their logs to LoTW, and/or to confirm that I am actually in their logs? 
> 

It’s not that easy to get signed up for LOTW if you are DX. Some just don’t bother. I suggest to ask for paper cards before the QSO get’s too old. Some stations turn around cards within a week or two. You might be surprised.

Once you get the cards I’ll be happy to check them for you.

> Is there any way - other than a manual lookup for each individual contact - to find email addresses for this group of contacts, such as a database of LoTW users that lists email addresses?
> 

One of the best sources is QRZ.COM. Get a free account and get access to email and postal addresses. 

If you are not on www.clublog.org then may want to check it out. There are a lot of DX stations that let you order the QSL cards through this system.  

I also think it would help to sign up for the Daily DX by Bernie, W3UR. Lots of info for QSL routes and current DX information. 

I hope this helps.

73, Andreas, N6NU

Hello to all,

Excuse the multi-list posting here, but I would like to give an update on what's going on with the Collins KWM-2 repair.
I've also included my friend (Garrett) who's repairing the radio in the CC section, so we can both keep updated with this project.

Before I begin, I wish to say thank you for all the superb and helpful responses. Garrett's been repairing most of said Collins KWM-2, and I've forwarded many of the responses received to him. They've been quite helpful in repairing the radio.

According to Garrett, here are the current problems:

1. Reduced sensitivity on the knobs.

2. This one's difficult to describe, but essentially: The RF Gain and AF Gain knobs are much more sensitive when turning the latter 1/4 of the knob (Increases much more dramatically at the last 1/4).

3. Might be normal for tube equipment, but the radio seems to run rather hot.

4. There's still an audible buzz while receiving (sounds the same as a lot of old A/V equipment).

5. Lastly, while not exactly a problem, I've purchased the parts to make a microphone adapter cable so that the KWM-2 can connect with a Astatic D-104 model microphone. I attempted to make an adapter with another microphone, though it ended up malfunctioning. Once I get the parts in the mail, maybe someone here could help me with the wiring; I've looked in the manuals and specification sheets, and I was unable to get the other microphone (a Courier BTM-4) functional. Both Zoom and in-person would work for me.

Okay then, I hope the problems I have listed make sense. If not, maybe Garrett or I could give some clarification.

Thanks and 73 for now,
Truman Lindsey N6TRU

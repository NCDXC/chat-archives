I wanted to evaluate the new Multi-Threaded Decoder (MTD) included in WSJT-X 3.0.0 RC1. This is Uwe's version: WSJT-X Improved Plus 3.0.0 RC1

I devised a test bed to compare MTD 2 Stage Decoder with non-MTD Decoder.

==================================
Initial Conditions: 20M very crowded band.
Decode Menu was set to Deep, Enable AP, Reduce False Decodes was NOT set.
All Filters were By-Passed.
(The wav files were recorded with MTD off, but that doesn't make a difference, because the wav files are unprocessed audio.)

The parms under MTD were all default, except I changed Decoder Start from NORMAL to 2 Stage, back and forth to arrive at the data points.

MTD Parameters:
Number of Threads: Auto
Decoding Cycles: 3
QSO Rx  Freq Sensitivity: High
Decoder Sensitivity: Use Subpass
Decoder Start: 2-Stage
===================================
I recorded 7 *.wav files on a very busy 20 meter band. (MTD vs non-MTD) and compared the number of decodes per sequence. Here are the results:

NUMBER of DECODES per Sequence
MTD     non-MTD
50         44
49         40
44         40
57         53
47         44
47         41
55         45

I played them back several times with and without MTD to be sure of the results.

The MTD decoder does better in a 20 meter crowded band with FT8 signals....and by a significant number of decodes.

If you view Uwe's video, you can see that on FT8, it is more sensitive for weak signals (even than JTDX), but the surprising thing is that the 2-Stage decoder does better in a crowded 20m band than the non-MTD decoder. It seems we have more sensitivity, as well as more resistance to interfering signals.

N.B. I did not get any false decodes during my testing.

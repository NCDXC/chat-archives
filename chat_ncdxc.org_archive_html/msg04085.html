I ordered my 3 el in September 2012 and it was delivered in November. I did not assemble and install it until late this past summer. The new connector box was not included as part of the basic antenna I receive--I ordered it as an add-on. Having previously experienced the original connector and tube in assembling numerous SteppIRs, I purchased the new box despite its ridiculous price. It proved to be very practical, as it simplified connect/disconnect of the control line and easy continuity checking. I have always thought the original connector arrangement was a kludge and that scrunching the wires into the tube was begging for trouble.

Garry, NI6T
On 11/10/2013 10:36 PM, Phil-W6PK wrote:
Regarding the Steppir terminal blocks:  many, if not all of the new
Steppir yagis, including my DB11, now ship with the new terminal block
connector boxes.  In my case Steppir had to be reminded to include it.
 In my opinion the big benefit is reduction of installation problems
because the terminal blocks make it easier to follow the connection
charts, resulting in less confusion, and the wiring can be visually
checked easily.  I assume this results in fewer calls to customer
support caused by miswiring.

However, if connected correctly I don't see why PVC tubes would be
less reliable.  As long as the connections were tight to begin with
and the cables are not yanked, the connectors in the PVC tube should
not come loose.  (Keep in mind that I didn't used the PVC tube in my
Steppir.)

The terminal block connector box mounts to the mast plate, as shown in
the attached photo.

73 de Phil, W6PK

Greeting.
I am pleased to report that yesterday after nine years my SteppIR came down.
Thanks to the efforts of Al N6TA who did the climbing and Larry W6OD who
handled the work on my roof it was down by noon yesterday.

A couple of comments on the condition of the beam. All the rubber element
connections were broken. Some had pieces missing.  One element had come out
approximately one inch. The new brand connectors should do better.  The
elements had many areas of white with exposed fiberglass on the upper side.
The silicon tape on the joints was gone in some cases and partly off in
others. some were still intact.  The new polyolefin shrink tubing will be a
big improvement.  The control cable insulation was hardened and broke easily
with  movement.  There were a few areas with insulation breaks where the
cable had been bent..  The control cables to the individual units seem to be
in better shape since I mounted it under the boom where it had some
protection from the sun. There is a problem in the EHU for the driven
element which started this operation.  I haven't opened it to find the
problem.  It will be replaced by 30-40 meter driven element. If anyone has
any specific questions e-mail me.

Dick NATD

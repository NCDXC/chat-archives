Wow, Jim.  I'm afraid your outcry isn't very motivational nor is it accurate.  There are many adjectives that describe NCDXC -- Selfish is certainly not one of them.

Have you checked the Meetings page lately on the website? http://www.cqp.org/Counties-Plan-2013.html
Next meeting is in Foster City -- trying to reach out more outlying members.  Not selfish.

Yesterday at the Board meeting we discussed the VHF issue you brought up.  Not selfish.

We honored two Members and a DXer of the Year at the IDXC meeting in Visalia.  Not selfish.
http://www.cqp.org/Counties-Plan-2013.html

We offer free QSL support for the ARRL bureau.  Not selfish.

We have a booth at Pacificon where we're trying to reach out to new DXers.  Not selfish.

Some members have opened their shacks to new operators for a DX coaching experience.  Not selfish.

We take an annual membership survey to help determine Club priorities.  Not selfish.

If you have an issue with RSGB not accepting electronic confirmations, then please become part of the solution by drafting a suitable statement for the Board to review and approve.  That would be a non-selfish move on your part.

Perhaps if you attended more Club meetings, and relied less on the chat reflector to form your opinions, you might have a completely different view of what's really happening in the Club. Personally, I hope that happens soon.

On Fri, Sep 13, 2013 at 12:03 AM, Jim Brown <k9yc@audiosystemsgroup.com> wrote:
On 9/12/2013 7:14 PM, Paul@n6pse.com wrote:
The NCDXC is one of a few organizations that is an "influence" organization and shapes the hobby as we know it today.

I have an alternative view.  I see a bunch of old farts with honor roll status who care little or nothing about those new to DXING. How else to explain no interest in LOTW for the rank and file DX stations that someone new to DXing needs QSLs from? I posted about this, and the silence was deafening.  We require all DXpeditions to load to LOTW -- that takes care of those of us with 320 countries worked, but screw everyone else.

A bunch of tired, SELFIISH old farts -- yes, we support DXpeditions that benefit us, but not those other "moving into the 21st century" changes needed to accommodate new DXers. Selfish, not willing to drive a bit farther to a meeting so that outlying members might attend.  Selfish, not caring about improving coverage of the VHF repeater so that outlying members might participate (and contibute).  Selfish, not even interesting using our influence on RSGB to get them to accept electronic confirmations for IOTA.

I could count those who have actually CONTRIBUTED without taking my shoes off -- guys like W6OAT, N6PSE, W6PK, W6OSP, NI6T, K6SRZ, N6ML.  I'm too out of shape physically, and too poor, to contribute by going on trips, but I do my best with techie stuff.

Frankly, I've just about given up on you guys. And this shot across the bow is an attempt to wake you up. And I'm not ducking, I am proud of this post.

73, Jim K9YC

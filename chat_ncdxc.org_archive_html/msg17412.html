Reading the DXer today, I took interest in Mike K6MKF’s complete shack overhaul. I did the same thing a few years back, completely emptying the room, laying down new carpet, new paint, and installing a new operating desk, along with cable trays, grounding bars, etc.

I discovered an INCREDIBLE buy that has worked out quite well. This multifunction desk is designed for studio/music-production, but it works great for a ham setup:

It happens to be on sale right now for $299 (I think I paid $450 in 2010).

https://www.guitarcenter.com/Studio-RTA/Producer-Station-Maple-1274228073432.gc


Besides having lots of room, note that both the left and right side bays are designed as 19” rack mounts, so anything that mounts that way goes in automatically. (You can also buy 19” rack mount shelving and customize, as I did.) There is a slide-out shelf on the upper bay, and one below the desk (for a keyboard). You can’t see it here, but there is a cable tray running along the back side that is handy for keeping all the cables from flopping around. There are also large holes (you can see two of them) in the back of the main desk surface, to allow cable routing between the lower and upper areas.

I mounted a 1” x 0.25” solid copper bar along the back, and drilled it every 6 inches or so, and installed bolts, washers, and wing nuts for easy grounding of equipment. You can mount all sorts of things into the metal structure. For example, I have my 13.8V DC distribution box mounted against the rear wall in the center, below the desk area, and an automatic antenna switch on the left outside of the table. This has served me well for almost ten years, and shows little sign of wear.

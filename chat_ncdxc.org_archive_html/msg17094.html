I'm quite happy with my Yaesu FT-60, which is, of course, no longer in new production. (VHF/UHF radios change as quickly as clothing fashions.) It performed well during the Henry Coe Park Backcountry Weekend public service event. I can even remember/figure out how to program in a new repeater, complete with offset and tone, without reading the manual, which is a minimum UI usability test.
The "feature" that gets new FT-60 owners is Yaesu's "Wires" Internet linking protocol, which comes turned on out of the box. Unfortunately, it causes a 2-3 second delay between pressing PTT and getting RF and modulation output. That is long enough to lose entire transmissions. It is toggled on the FT-60 by pressing the "0" button, so it gets turned on by accident quite frequently.
The only other major lack is some kind of battery status indicator. The radio will show you the battery voltage when you turn it on, but there are no warnings in continuous operation until it refuses to produce full output during transmit. I admit that battery status is a hard problem with the number of different battery packs available for the radio. (BTW, I consider a pack which uses standard AA cells or similar to be an essential accessory for emergency communication use.)

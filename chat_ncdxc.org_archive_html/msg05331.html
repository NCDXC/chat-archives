Good points. I won't try to compare apples and oranges - heh heh. Well - actually - maybe I will:

What I will do is do real world A-B tests, like one I did last night - which is pretty darned interesting:

I was ready to roll right when FT5ZM came up on 17M at about 6:30 PM PST. They were about S9 - but it was pretty noisy - probably S7 noise, and varying - maybe down to S5 or 6. 

I switched over to my 63' hatted 160M vertical (76 buried radials) - that models as having some gain on 17M - and, there was virtually NO noise, and an S6 signal. 

The _sound_ of this signal - without looking at the S meter was clearer than the 4 element yagi, and there was no QSB. There was a lot more QSB on the yagi - but the peaks on the yagi seemed to indicate that the yagi would be the better antenna to use. That yagi is only up 30'. The vertical has surprised the heck out of me - I have used it on 17M and busted many decent pileups. 

Anyway, I sure was tempted to try the vertical - but I had built the 4 element 17M yagi - just for this DX-pedition . . . I needed to validate putting the effort in that "experiment", and it did come through.

On Feb 4, 2014, at 10:28 AM, Jim Brown <k9yc@audiosystemsgroup.com> wrote:

On 2/4/2014 10:07 AM, Rich Holoch wrote:


I've been overlaying "traces" in EZNec to compare all of the possible antennas that I can put up. That has allowed me to compare verticals and horizontal antennas.

Yes, this is really the key to understanding the modeled results.  For vertical antennas, it's also very important to use the ground condition that applies to your QTH.

BUT - you can use HFTA for the horizontal antennas and then use trace overlays to then compare horizontal and vertical antennas - kind of an indirect method of "cheating HFTA" . . . .


Danger, Will Robinson!  The reason that N6BV wrote HFTA only for horizontally polarized antennas is that horizontally and vertically polarized wavefronts interact differently with the earth. As I understand it, the vertical wavefront interaction is more complex, which is why Dean did not write something for it.

On Wed, Feb 8, 2023 at 10:51 PM Rick NK7I via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

He's using MSHV or a variant tonight; not F/H but does have 2 streams, 2 stations per stream.  If you cannot hear him, do not call.  That can't be said any plainer (being in the wrong mode, means you can't hear him!  So STOP).

Tonight you do NOT want to use F/H mode, it will force you into the wrong cycle (last pass, 21 are in f/h mode compared to 60 that are not and I only hear one direction with a beam, it's a VAST pileup!).

Adrian made a mistake; they announced F/H only, he isn't following that but to change modes mid-stream compounds the error.  Fix it next band start up time.

FOr most DX, if you oops calling the DX and do NOT use F/H mode (with MSHV and WSJT-X) they will still hear your acknowledge report without your changing tones (but don't need/want your RR73 once they send it to you).  (The program corrects some of your oops, but best to do it right.)

I suggest one NEVER EVER calls ON the DX tone/s; because too many do that, it's a mess and your success rate will tumble (the decoding algorithm can only manage so much).  Near their tone/s, ok; on, no bueno.  That has been demonstrated tonight, wrong mode, ON the DX tones, blocking streams.

Get in there, this is FUN!

On 2/8/2023 10:31 PM, Paul Booth via Chat wrote:
Jerry, a couple of tips I can offer:
1) only call if you can hear him (odd or even slot)
2) only call on the opposite slot (after you hear him and know which slot he's on)
3) find an open frequency to call
4) He's F/H if the DX transmits on even, then you're on odd.  F/H also if he transmits to more than one station on the same freq/slot. We transmit above 1KHz.
5) If he's transmitting odd, in this case tonight, assume FT8 regular, or MSHV and you can transmit anywhere (but not his freq), even.
Well, that's what I've doing at least.  Let me know if I missed something.

Good luck.

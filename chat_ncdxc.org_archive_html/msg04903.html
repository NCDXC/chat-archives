On 1/14/2014 8:11 PM, Garry Shapiro wrote:
A more cynical view....

Ralph---a good man--- is trying to achieve by anticipatory soft diplomacy what is probably an impossible goal---to quiet the Europeans long enough to let the team work someone else. I wish him the best but I am skeptical that he will succeed.

Rich, you are looking at this from one leg of the elephant---what you expect the path to be from there to here, --akin to trying to light a cigarette with a single match in a howling rainstorm, I think. Match plus ciggy should equal a smoke, but the rain and wind must be considered. Extending what Ralph writes, our best openings will indeed be roughly at the graylines, and the Euros will have better prop ---stronger and longer--during those times and also almost every other time.

Experience tells us they won't be quiet, even if the ops specify W6/W7. Experience tells us they will call at will and, if reprimanded, will QRM. The Euros translate the long openings into 18 to 20 band countries for them and are not thinking about us or anyone else. So it becomes a battle of wills between the operators and the Euros.

I certainly hope Ralph succeeds--so we can succeed. I hope the team will have the fortitude to demand compliance---there are great ops among them. But it ain't gonna be easy. If they can enforce discipline, this DXped will merit a fat contribution.

OTOH, as the Locust is fond of saying "It is what it is." It is the uncertainty and the surprise that make this game what it is. The sun will be a major factor. The island is a mountaintop jutting out of the sea; does anyone know which side of the mountain they will be located?

To end on an upbeat note--at least the operator group is not French military. Patience and hope, my friends...

Flame suit on....

Garry, NI6T

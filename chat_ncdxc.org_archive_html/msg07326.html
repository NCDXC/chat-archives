The answer to Peter’s question:

 

“Has the DX Community thought about agreeing to either fund or conduct environmental mitigation & preservation efforts at these sites . . .  as a trade off . . . “

 

The answer is Yes. There is one entity in particular – that if you ask if they would issue a permit for an amateur radio DX-pedition, they will flat out say NO.

 

However, if you call and ask if they issue a permit to do “environmental research” which involves diving, and where you could also survey a reef which was just cleaned up – then the answer is Yes, they would issue a permit. Since asking I have found that there are still hurdles, but the first one could be cleared.

 

I know from this first-hand experience.

 

I have no idea if the “DX Community at large” is interested in such work – but I also know that such an operation would be very small – it would certainly not be a large DX-pedition. We are talking 2 – 4 people in a sailboat or whatever . . . But they would have to do some survey work – possibly dive and film, etc.

 

So – combine such an activity, and you might just be successful.  The number of “heritage / protected” sites are growing – other countries are following the US lead in creating very strict access rules. How strict? More strict than they are today, but who knows how “off limits” these will become. I think the trend will be for less access as time goes – and as I said – it won’t just be the US.

 

I am looking forward to the KP1-5 projects current work with KP1. I attended the KP1 presentation at Visalia and was encouraged when they said they might have an update this Fall.

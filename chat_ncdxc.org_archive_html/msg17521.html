FWIW, I have 141 DXCCs confirmed on CW, 135 on digital, and only 70 on phone. I consider myself a digital guy, particularly for contesting, but CW is where the DX is. I tend to avoid SSB because it is so hard to those hear stations.
I really try to run all modes, and a good day is one with at least one contact on each of CW, digital, and SSB. FT8 is now the place to find digital stations. It used to be PSK31/63 and RTTY is still good during contests, but now it's FT8. FT4, although "only for contesting", may become the new place to be, but looking at the bands, it hasn't made it yet.
Note: Please upgrade to wsjt-x 2.1.0. This release improves the quality of FT8 signals by making the transition between tones less of a square wave. This means that the skits of the signal are much narrower when you get to 50+ dB down, making your signal a better citizen.
FT8 is the place to be if you're tired and just want to make some QSOs. CW, PSK, RTTY, and SSB are all more work. FT4 happens faster, and so is less relaxing. Yes, you can improve you chances of contacting the hard ones a lot with careful operation of FT8, but you can also lean back and just work the easy ones.
Many's the time I've been just relaxing, contacting FT8 stations, and something interesting has come up forcing me to actually work. That's probably good for me in the long run.

My question for those awaiting a better tide is "how long do you wait?"

The first few days are obviously going to be nuts, and they were. Now, 3 or 4 days in, some of the pileups are still huge, but others can be broken in reasonable time. Some are already at the stage where the ops are in complete control and running their piles smoothly.

I predict that by the time they start taking down antennas, many in the ATNO crowd will be flush with Q's and bragging to their friends. There are only so many callers. In a way it is like a new offering of a hot stock or a hot concert ticket.

JMO,

Garry, NI6T
On 2/5/2015 5:00 PM, Jon Zweig via Chat wrote:
" Moreover, I’ll bet that the majority of folks seeking band/mode slots are well equipped - high power and good antennas - so making it more different for the little pistol."

I agree with Stu on this. As I was about to jump back into the fray, it occurred to me that an entity as rare as K1N will be an ATNO for the large majority of hams. If powerful, well located stations with good antennae are in the pile up seeking to fill out their leaderboards, there is less room for the peanut whistle stations. Frustration.  Maybe the best approach would be wait till things calm down, the little stations will have made at least one contact, and then go after the band fill slots....

Just my 2 cents

I use FT8 more than I prefer, but that's the nature of DX most days.  Therefore I have a fair amount of experience.

Here is what I sent Paul:

"IF the DXPed limits to TWO streams ONLY, the small stations have a better shot at hearing the team, compared to SF which takes an 8 dB hit in lowest possible signal (it cuts off at -16, instead of -24).  Wider (1500 Hz) equals quieter (less signal compared to F/H, 300 Hz max width)

This means that the larger stations can work the DX in SF mode, en masse while the little guys, hear less; little guys lose out.

SF mode is also fragile; my guess is that redundant data (sent twice in normal FT8) is not as robust, IF it's there at all.  It just is not as reliable unless each side is loud (no hits).

I've been running the latest improved version but no one so far has been running SF so latest SF changes have not been vetted yet (my opinion).

I suggest that F/H with 2 streams max, while limiting to the rate, is the optimum plan that is most fair to all.  More people can hear which means more people have a shot.

2 Streams also means that narrowing the bandwidth can be used to pull the most meager signal out; which is not possible using SF."


I would add that the 'security' feature of SF, is a joke, it adds exactly zero (at the cost of adding more data to the stream).  It was hacked in a week which negates any real confirmation of a valid call.

SF only helps those who can hear well; those who can afford the 8 dB signal hit, compared to 2 streams of F/H.

The average ham station is surrounded by noise; they lose when SF mode is used.

73,
Rick nk7i

I think both modes have their proper place. With good propagation conditions and a big pile-up, SF gives better QSO rates. For poor prop, F/H gives modest stations a better chance.

A DXpedition might want to start out on SF, then migrate to F/H after all the big guns have been worked.  Of course, F/H lacks the authentication features of SF.

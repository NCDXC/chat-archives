On Apr 15, 2013, at 9:28 AM, K6XN wrote:

My all time favorite rig from a nostalgia standpoint is my antique Collins S line ensemble comprising a Collins 75S-3B receiver, 32S-3 transmitter, 312B-4 station console and 30S-1 full legal limit linear amplifier. When I was a teenager back in 1960 I dreamed of some day having a complete Collins S line but I was unable to afford one for many years. The S line I have now l have restored over the years and I enjoy every day just seeing it in the radio room and I still  use it on a weekly basis. My antique Collins S line equipment is still a thing of beauty and quality  to me and has been for many years. Call me biased.
 
The ensemble I primarily use for chasing DX however  these days  is a Yaesu FT-2000 with the optional Data Management Unit driving an Alpha 77SX linear amplifier. The backup DX rig is an Icom IC-775 DSP with all the filters driving a second Alpha 77SX.
 
Ted, K6XN

Here is your NCDXC Question of the Week:
 
What is your all time favorite rig?
 
 
 
 
 
My response: My all time favorite rig is my Yeasu 1000MP. I bought it new circa 1991 and I still have it. This was my first "big box" rig replacing a Kenwood TS-690. I never really liked or enjoyed the TS-690. The 1000MP is a joy to operate and a very fine radio. My primary pile up busters are a pair of K3's that I use on CW/SSB/RTTY but for casual listening I still enjoy flipping the antenna switch over and listening from the 1000MP.

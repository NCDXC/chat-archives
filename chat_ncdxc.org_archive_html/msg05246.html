My experience tacks Steve and Mike’s comments.  I put the TS-990S pan-adapter on the Sub receiver and watch to see if I can figure out what is happening.  Sometimes it simply doesn’t work but often it does.  When I see (and hear) the station being worked I either tailgate if the OP is “sucking” contacts there or try to identify a pattern – visually.  Not always easy.

 

If nothing is working, I sit on a frequency and hope he finds me.  An often it works.

 

Bob – W6OPO

I've been thinking about adding a P3 to my K line. I should note that I don't have the subreceiver.

 

But, your experience is why I haven't done it yet. Is it really faster than just tuning through the pileup? With so many guys calling continually, the guy sending 5NN is hard to find, but probably doesn't stick out with the P3 either. It would have helped the other day when I was calling up 2 and they were really listening up 13. Much easier to get in the log if you call where their receiver is listening!

 

Each op seems to have a different method - the 160/40m guys - DO what they say and it helps to be loud. The other guys - some move slightly after each Q and others seem to give the knob a full rotation.

 

Steve, K6UM

 

On Fri, Jan 31, 2014 at 12:19 PM, Rich Holoch <rholoch@splicemachine.com> wrote:

I have the full Elecraft K-Line and have used FT5ZM to try to see how effective using the P3 is in slipping my call in a "hole" in the pileup / split.

 

I have found that it isn't nearly as effective as my old method - which is to simply tune and hear the last station worked and jump in there - ESPECIALLY when the DX station makes an "excursion".

 

HOWEVER, the P3 does tell me where the MONSTER DX-ers (you guys with the full power and massive antennas) are - and to stay away from trying to compete there. Some of you guys have the power to "duke it out". I don't.

 

I have found that the ops I have worked at FT5ZM are jumping around rather than going up and down by small increments. I think every time I worked them - I tracked the "swing" out and away from the "pack".

 

How about you? What's your experience?

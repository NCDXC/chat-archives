On 12/1/2013 2:42 PM, Alan Maenchen wrote:
Any vertical suffers ground absorption well beyond the radial system. A vertically polarized wave induces eddy currents in the ground out several wavelengths until the energy becomes an established sky wave instead of a ground wave. So even if you have the world's best radial system, it still suffers compared to a high dipole.
Yes. I noted this in a private response to Bob. This issue is particularly significant here in NorCal where our soil is pretty lossy. However, these ground losses are much less if the soil is a lot better. This is well illustrated by the study I presented at Pacificon on mounting height of vertical antennas. It's on my website as a pdf. k9yc.com/publish.htm

It just depends. 

It depends on the propagation overhead, your terraine, antenna gain etc. 

I have always worked 3A-Monaco long path on the higher bands. 30/40 meters have been short path (beaming into darkness)

Last night, I was watching for the 3A long path when I saw Richard WB6EWM calling him. I asked Richard if he was seeing him long or short and to my
surprise, he was seeing him short path.   When decoding DX, I very often use my Steppir controller to check signal strength short, bi-directional and long. 

My particular terrain profile makes Lonth Path the usual path for 3A.  What is different with this operation is that they have had strong signals from 3A and my sense is that
they have found a unique operating location that might be better than previous operations. 

73 and good DX. 

Paul N6PSE

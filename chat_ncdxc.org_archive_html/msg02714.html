Jim,

I'm enjoying all that cw activity on 6m, too! I give credit to that to all those new hf radios that include 6m and getting hf folks on the magic band.

One area that I'd like to hear more activity is on meteor scatter on Sunday morning. The hf folks aren't aware of that mode.

When I lived in CM88, I worked many CN8x grids via meteor scatter on Sunday morning. The meteors are still there, but folks in CM8x/9x don't turn their antennas north and don't know the proper sequencing. Maybe, Russ, K6KLY, could put together a talk for one of the club meetings.

On Mon, Jul 22, 2013 at 11:01 AM, Jim Brown <k9yc@audiosystemsgroup.com> wrote:
Living on the west side of the ridge, VHF for me is limited to 6M. I'm chasing grids on 6M, and like to take advantage of the heightened activity of a contest to pick up a few more. While those east of the Mississippi have been working EU, the Caribbean, and South America, this has been a pretty stinko season on 6M for us in the Bay Area, with only a handful of openings all summer. Indeed, the last one was during the June ARRL contest.

This time around, I managed 96 QSOs to 19 states, 2 provinces, and 52 grids. Five of the grids were new ones, bringing my total to 300 worked.  A double-hop Es opening Saturday around my dinner time yielded NY, PA, MD, OH, MI, IA, IN, and IL, all on CW. The opening lasted about 45 minutes.  CW was most productive, both in QSOs and grids, and all but one of the new ones were CW.

Heard K6EU/R making a lot of folks happy rovering from CM86 during that double-hop opening from his favorite high spot on the UCSC campus. Other NCCC and NCDXC members heard included AF6RT, NU6S, WA6ZTY, N6EE, W6OAT, and N6TU.

73, Jim K9YC

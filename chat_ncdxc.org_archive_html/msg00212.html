On Oct 22, 2012, at 12:13 PM, Paul@n6pse.com wrote:

> Hi Bill, yes it is unfortunate that the W3LPL "robot" detected 3B9SP and automatically posted them on DX Summit within one minute of our Sked starting.  Band conditions were excellent but the East coast and Midwest stations crushed many of the W6 callers participating in our Sked.
> 

Yes, it's hard to keep a "private sked" on CW these days. Perhaps in the future, we should ask the DXpedition NOT to call "CQ", so they won't get picked up by the skimmers. (And I assume club members were wise enough not to spot him manually.) Another idea (which was used for the T6 sked) is to pre-arrange a very unusual split offset, perhaps 50-100 kHz or more away. (I know that's normal on 40m SSB, but it's pretty weird on 20m CW.) None of this will absolutely prevent hoi polloi from showing up, but it might keep them away for a few more precious minutes.

FYI: Worked 3B9SP on 20m CW and SSB, and TT8TT on 20m SSB, 17m SSB, and 40m CW. Both ATNOs. FWIW, I just could NOT figure out what the 3B9 operator was doing with respect to receiver tuning on 20m SSB (going up the band? down the band? jumping around like a monkey?), and I ultimately just plunked myself down on a reasonable spot and called until *he* found *me*. Whatever works.

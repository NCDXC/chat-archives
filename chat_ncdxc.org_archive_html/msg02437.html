My fellow DXers.

This discussion sounds like a solution looking for a problem.

Take ATNO South Sudan. Because of great ops and rates, how long did it take for the pile ups to die down and yet still have some complain about missing it?

There are also some implicit assumptions that I question:

Propagation will always be great. (You just worked a new band. A new mode is on the next day. But wait a week. You'll still have prop.)
There will be no unrecoverable equipment failures.
There will be no severe weather or personnel emergencies that will cut the operation short.

In the day it was "work 'em early and often." "Early" because of the unknowns that could cause the operation to end earlier than planned. That's still true. "Often" - just in case there was a malcontent masquerading as the DX - has been greatly mitigated by realtime or close to realtime log updates. 

If you want to emphsize ATNOs, start and sustain ops on the money bands dictated by prop. Anyone who doesn't need it as an ATNO most likely has it on 20m.

You'll have world class ops. Don't waste time culling out dupes. Log it and keep going.

Yes turn off the leaderboard. Please!

State your goals and ask for co-operation. May I suggest:

- We want to give all who need it an ATNO.

- We want everyone in the log wherever they need a new band or mode.

- If you hear us on a band or mode you don't need, please QRX unless we're just not busy.

- Please send complete calls and standby. We will QRX until we get the one we want in the log.

- We just need your call and report.

- Check the online log. If you're not sure of your Q, by all means, please do work us again.

Attempting to restrict Qs and trying to enforce compliance is a recipe to recruit deliberate QRM. They'll be enough of that anyway.

Ask for the best in everyone. Make all who are trying to work you feel that they're at least getting a fair shot no matter what their personal wants might be.

73 - Steve WB6RSE

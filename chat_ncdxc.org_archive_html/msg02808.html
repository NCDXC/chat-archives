Jim, This is a great question, and I feel like throwing in my 2 cents on this..

I Also have high qso volume, at about 50,000 since 2008.  my lotw qsl rate stands at about 47%.

It seems that there are quite a few people that enjoy the hard card aspect of the hobby.  Having something physical that can be hung on the wall. 

 Eqsl has taken steps to try and capture that (via the creation of a picture based qsl card that can be printed), but since eqsl doesnt work for DXCC I find it difficult to focus my attention on qsling there.

Its my opinion that capturing this aspect of qsl is critical in getting ubiquitous acceptance of the program.

LOTW on the other hand, seems to have been code locked by the ARRL without any major feature enhancements since inception. The LOTW program does not include the visual or personal aspect of qsl or the ability to create a visual representation of the qsl.  you get a data bit confirming your qso and are pretty much left to your imagination as far as the cool qsl card this would have produced for your wall.  but you do get dxcc credit.  t.

So development of a program that could be used in tandem with LOTW  or and ENHANCEMENT to LOTW, to produce a personal qsl  would be a great thing.  I am not sure the club could accomplish this in any way but financial, but we do have influence.  a merger between LOTW and EQSL capturing the best of both would be super cool but probably not possible for various reasons. 

bottom line, whatever we can do to have the best of both systems in 1 arrl approved system or perhaps one approved and endorsed by the IARU for international use could just pull it off. 

~C./N6WM

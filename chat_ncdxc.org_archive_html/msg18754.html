Historically, SteppIR goes dark in April and May.  There
may be a sneak preview of Dayton at Visalia.  If you
wait in order to get the convention price, what happens
is that they collect convention orders and then make
a big manufacturing run after they get back.  Nothing
ships for months after Dayton.  I don't know how they
prioritize shipments after that.

40 meters is a crucial band.  The DB-18 is well worth
the extra money to get a unidirectional pattern.

SteppIR's have many desirable features, but they are
not easy to construct and the documentation isn't great.
Don't be in a hurry. Double or triple check everything
ON THE GROUND.  Point the completed antenna straight
up with the reflector on saw horses and check VSWR on each band

BTW, I am the owner of 3 SteppIR antennas.

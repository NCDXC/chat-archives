Dave, right you are.  Funding is important. Being respectful to those providing the funding by requiring good talent is important. New talent is EQUALLY IMPORTANT.  but again, one does not need to be thrown in cold to face the incredible "wall of noise" that Martti described. some time at a quality contest station running full bore, such as N6RO, K6IDX, W7RN,NK7U K3LR, OH8X,  etc. could give the right amount of experience, attitude, teamwork exposure to just make the difference to lay ground work prior to deployment and make a newbie very effective.  there are plenty of these stations worldwide with very willing owners hoping to put warm bodies in chairs and I am sure would be happy to participate in such a program.  Integrating a bit of pre expedition training should not be that big of a deal for someone who really wants it.

A little extra effort could go a long way..

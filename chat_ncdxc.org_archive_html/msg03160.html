I am having a bad computer day. Let's try again without my repeatedly pasting the same paragraphs.

My SteppIR 3 El was working---except for 6m. On 6 the default values for element lengths were totally FUBAR by a factor of 2. I contacted K4LDI this week, he consulted the firmware people and they suggested updating my SDA-100 to a beta version of new firmware called Mustang. Jim sent email with attachments:
  • Flip_Installer_3.4.2.exe (had to convert the file descriptor after reception to .exe)
  • SDA-100 Flip Instructions.txt
  • mustang_1307060026.hex
  • mustang_user_guide.doc

He also included the following arcane instructions:

Here are the files for Mustang.
On the first update use the instructions for starting the upload in the Flip instructions.
After that you start an upload by using it procedure in the Mustang second paragraph.

Rename the Flip program to .exe and you should be ready to go.

I managed to download the Flip S/W and installed to my root directory in XP Pro. I found the next instructions in the SDA-100 Flip Instructions file. In following them I wound up with new firmware--FOR A DB18. I don't know how to reverse this and get on the right path. I suspect the driver I was instructed to use by the Flip instructions was not the write one.

The Flip instructions he pointed me to told me to find and select the following driver: AT90USB647.

I have no idea what Jim is talking about when he then instructs to use a procedure in the "Mustang second paragraph".  the document came here with no illustrations. The "paragraphs" are single sentences that are supposed to have illustrations.

I am deep in the cave with no string trailing from the entrance. Can someone versed in SteppIR firmware handhold me through this?---Jim ain't answering on a Saturday.

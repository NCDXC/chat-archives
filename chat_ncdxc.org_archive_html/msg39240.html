Since we’ll all need rotor repair at some time or another, here’s an update on my progress. When I google Norm’s Rotor Repair a website that was maintained today pops up, but there’s a side note from Google that Norm’s is permanently closed. I tried calling the number listed, but it is out of service. I’ve emailed Norm and I’ll let everyone know if he responds. Steve, N6SJ, recommended M2. I contacted them, but they only work on their own units. At this point John, K6YP has recommended W7KD; Chuck, NA6XX has recommended AD6D, who serviced hid prop pitch motor, and Paul, W6XA has heard good things about KT4KI.

I am grateful to everyone who responded, but I can only thank you on-the-air if you’re due north of my QTH.

73 and thanks, Bruce K3NQ

My Hygain T2X “tail twister” has finally packed it in. It’s been getting stalled from time to time, but would always recover after a spin to the opposite direction. I’ve concluded that the issue is internal to the rotor, such as missing teeth on the ring gear, since the mast/antenna assembly rotates easily by hand. This afternoon I had to uncouple the rotor from the mast and swing the beam manually to 3B9 to get the my needed 10m slot.

Norm’s rotor repair is out-of-business, and I’m not even sure about parts availability. Does anyone know of any surviving rotor repair businesses?

Dave, WI6R has a solid used one waiting for me at Visalia, but I’d like to get my current one up and running as a spare for the future.

Rich Holoch (KY6R), technologist, prolific blogger, noted DXer, DXpedition organizer and ham radio philosopher, became an SK after a short illness.  Born in New Jersey, Rich began his computer science career at Kodak before making the leap to the San Francisco Bay Area, where he enjoyed a 43 year career in the tech sector.  He was employee #127 at Oracle, and went on to work at many other Bay Area firms, big and small.  Rich will be remembered for his wide-ranging and sustained blogging output, focusing on DXing, DX history, techniques and technical aspects.  He presented at numerous gatherings on the progress of his successful attempt to achieve DXCC Honor Roll in one solar cycle, and charted the history of the DXCC entities list in detail, as well as recording his decade-long exploration of antennas for the DXer.  The proudest moment of his ham career came when he co-organized the 2016 VK0EK DXpedition to Heard Island.  In 2023, Rich retired to Bend, Oregon with his wife, Kat.  He is survived by Kat and two adult sons.

Rich had recently retired and moved from Moraga to Bend Oregon.

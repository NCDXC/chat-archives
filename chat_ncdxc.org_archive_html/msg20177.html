Time Machine is great.  Sometimes, I’ve had TM say it can’t verify the backup on the filer, and decide to start from the beginning.

I have a specific filer just for Time Machine.  I back up that filer to backblaze as well.

TM has trouble handling VMs though.  It sees the VM as one file, and consequently in backups to the entire VM (all 60GB) each hour.

TM is good about handling open files thru OSX, but, not so much inside of a VM, as you’d expect.

> For those using a Macintosh, backups happen automatically (in fact, hourly) with Time Machine. (I just checked, and my last backup was at 8:07 am today, less than an hour from when I am writing this.) Time Machine keeps hourly backups, and then archives EVERYTHING, although older backups become daily, weekly, and then monthly to save space. The user doesn’t have to do ANYTHING.
> 
> For extra safety, I clone my Time Machine drive to an external HD every six months or so, and store it in the locker in my tennis club. (Offsite backup, without needing lots of Internet bandwidth.)

So a new (DX side) TX format entirely, not streamS combined (just one).  Instead of 60 Hz spacing per stream, now 166.66 Hz. per station (equivalent), 2.5 times as wide but probably interwoven data?  (At 1.5 KHz, 25 streams potentially but the current system would be so weak that it's unusable.)

Again, hoping that it is more robust against multipath and fade/polar resistant (it IS a frustration this far north to HEAR the tones and have no decode).

  • Does the width vary up to 1.5 KHz by number of active exchanges (like streams) or is that a constant?
  • Is the timing the same per transmission (13.24 seconds)?  
  • Is data sent more than twice per transmission (data rate increase)?  Higher rate means wider, explaining the station:width ratio but so would a mode other than phase shifting multiple tones for one station (or two presently).
  • In marginal conditions (where one of X streams can be decoded now); is the one stream all or nothing?

One tradeoff of using more spectrum, is that more signal strength is required for solid copy (any mode but CW which is fixed width).  So it will be interesting to see how this plays out once released.

And yes, only useful on a discrete spectrum used by DXPed teams, never on the current windows (but someone, will).

NOT my favorite mode, I just work what the DX offers and they almost ALL run days of JT mode (only).  But the techy part is interesting.

Nine streams at once, but how many stations per stream?  A potential of 18 stations being worked in one transmission?

That should lower the pileup more quickly either way.  And if done using the same encoding, the present WSJT-X should simply see it as 9 streams to decode (and not care); unless the format is entirely new.  I'm hoping it is more robust, more resistant to multipath and polar distortion (a dream hi hi).  It should help those DX operations limited to 100 watts and a wire, in theory.

MSHV also divides the transmit audio tones, poorly.  You can look at the signals (panadapter) and tell what app is playing.

Does it have high 'shoulders' just outside the stream (looks like a stream on either end but no decode)?  MSHV.  That's wasted energy better used in the wanted streams.

Do the streams vary significantly in signal strength (often by as much as 5 dB)?  MSHV.  It's not as efficient, wasteful of energy transmitted.

MSHV tends to be a EU team preference, but if they looked a little deeper, might change their minds.

I've used MSHV (when expecting a pileup after working DX) but limit to one stream only, so these two flaws are not displayed.

Right tool for the job, WSJT-X.  The coders of other apps, just haven't done as much polish for their product.

Best convention ever, but could use some tweaks:

The banquet program was extremely boring.

Joe Taylor - the potential star of the show, should have been first - and not rushed.

All of the thank you's should have been scrolled on the big screen at the end of the show - like they do credits in a movie. Going on and on and on was very boring.

I agree with Jim - you need a new emcee. The Top Band Dinner was WAY more entertaining. Steve was a masterful emcee (sans microphone feedback issue).

The last man standing should not have been glossed over so fast. You should have started with a lower number - like 331 - just for impact.

There should have been some questions like "Has anyone made Honor Roll this year?"
"Anyone make HR #1?", "Anyone win a DX Contest?", and have people raise their hands. Just a tad more audience participation would have really helped.

Besides the banquet:

Between presentations, there should have been more time - several seemed to get the bums rush.

Multi-tracking the DX Academy caused serious competition. One track was stacked with known experts, the other with unknowns (like myself).

I had a good (enthusiastic) crowd, but how could I possibly compete with Carl, K9LA at the same time slot? The people who attended (maybe 20?) Seemed to like it - so I feel it was still very successful anyway. Several asked questions for at least 40 minutes after and gave very nice kudos. I still enjoyed it very much.

I'll respond to the (very good) question of the week in another email, but more important I think, is the following question:

Should the IDXC return to the Holiday Inn next year? Apparently, no decision has been made at this point, but the rumor is that because of a substantial fee that the convention has to pay to the city of Visalia, the SCDXC is strongly leaning toward moving back. The consensus seems to be that the fee is and reasonable. Sure, that money would be nice to have in a club's coffers...

IMO, the convention center at the Marriott is FAR, FAR superior to the HI. That place is stuffed, in more ways than one. I really wasn't aware that it existed until the last year. How the HI could even be reconsidered is amazing to me.

Is a fee fair? I imagine it is a pretty expensive facility, and Visalia isn't really the convention center of California. Jackson Hole really needs such a facility, and is debating the concept right now. These facilities are expensive, but in my view worth it. We have been running in to more events opting for larger facilities. It seems clear that the convention can afford it. It certainly made a better event, and the NCDXC is to be commended, applauded for taking the risk. I  believe it paid off.

If the IDXC does go back to the HI, there may be plenty of room at the HI because I know of a number of long time DXers who simply won't attend. I could be among them. As an OT with 35 conventions under my belt, I may need more motivation (or less disincentive) to go again.

So again, MY question of the week is: Should the 2014 IDXC return to the Holiday Inn?

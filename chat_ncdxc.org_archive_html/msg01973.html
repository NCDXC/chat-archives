I disagree.  It is not to “get the best possible deal possible…”.  It is supposed to be to get what works for the attendees and vendors, not a clubs bank account.

 

As Vendor Hall Chairman, I can tell you first hand that the vendors really liked the hall.  They are ready to come back and that included a few 1st time vendors.

 

I heard SCDXC’s president saying they want maximum profit before providing the service to the DX community.  What is this about a proposed tent!?  He wants the maximum profit for “their future projects”.  I did not react kindly to that message.

Like many of you, I, too, loved the Convention Center venue this year and hope we will return there next year.  But let's give the SCDXC folks some slack.  They have not yet signed a contract with anybody for where the convention will be held in 2014.  They're still negotiating prices and what we get for our bucks.  Like Fred, K1VR noted, you don't have much negotiating room to work on getting an even better deal when you've publically locked yourself into one venue prior to signing the contract.  I believe the SCDXC negotiators got plenty of input from this year's attendees about their feelings toward the various possible venues for next year.  Let's support them now in their effort to get the best deal possible for us.

I fully agree with all of the comments so far.  There is no requirement for either club to make a profit on the IDXC.  If everyone has a good time and the convention breaks even financially, it is a success.  Profits are good if they are used to promote DX such as contributions to DXpeditions or the NCDXF.  Profits that sit in the bank are a waste.

 

A visit to http://www.ncdxf.org/pages/contributors-major.html will show that the SCDXC did not contribute last year whereas the NCDXC contributed $4,000.  The SCDXC is listed as having contributed “at least $5,000 over the years”.  So if the reason to go back to the H.I next year is to maximize profits to be donated to the NCDXF, well…

 

After last year at the H.I., I was seriously considering not going this year until the venue was changed.  I am extremely glad that I did go (great job John, Kevin and the crew!).  If it goes back to the H.I. it is not likely that I will go next year.  We need to get this message to the SCDXC group.  Do we need a petition?

 

73,

- Craig, AE6RR

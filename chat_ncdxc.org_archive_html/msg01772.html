Kosovo is a very interesting place. I visited with some Kosovar hams back in November and operated from a Club station there. The situation in Kosovo is very complicated with long standing and deep seated feelings on many issues.
 
The area is a tinderbox of strong racial, political and religious emotions. Interestingly, while Kosovo has declared their independence from Serbia, they depend very much on Serbia and the UN to the degree that they do not intend to seek UN membership any time soon. They are essentially a welfare state with few natural resources or crops to support themselves and a young population with a high birth rate.
 
The ethnic Serbs who are Orthodox feel that they are being pushed out of their country by the ethnic Albanian Kosovars who are Muslim. The Kosovars are coming from places including Albania and they are tired of being pushed around by the Serbs.
 
You can see the tension over there with very high security, and the UN presence is everywhere.
 
As for Amateur Radio, the Kosovars reject having to ask Serbia for a YU8 call sign so they are now using Z60. Unless the ARRL modifies their DXCC criteria Kosovo won't become a new entity unless they change their UN stance.
 
Very interesting place. Very complex problems. Very nice people on both sides of these issues. It is too bad it is so difficult to work out their issues.
 
Paul N6PSE

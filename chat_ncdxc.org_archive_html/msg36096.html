On Jul 2, 2024, at 11:55, Kevin Rowett via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

﻿A key is mandatory to operate in the fox side of superfox mode.   The idea is - the software will validate the key.  If the key doesn’t fit it’s notion of validate, then the TX considered a pirate.

W1SRD - yes, a key is required to get the software to send in super mode, as a fox.  

And, yes, the authors have been over the FCC rules - no hidden message is in the superfox message.

There is some effort mitigate DQRM’ers, as well.  We will see how well that works.

Note - this is all part of the game that pirates (and other cyber criminals) play - whack a mole.

Fox/hound mode came about as a result of KH7Z/KH1 - the desire to operate digital modes without the usual performance issues.  The fox gets to control the frequency, and who calls.

SuperFox does two things:  The superfox TX can use many streams w/o loss of power per stream; fight the pirate issue.

73, K6TD

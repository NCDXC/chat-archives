On Feb 22, 2015, at 3:54 PM, Guy Olinger K2AV via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Hello all,

Two answers:

1) You can half size the wire lengths in the 160 L and FCP (but not the spacing in the FCP). That works on 80 meters. The 160 transforner works on 80, or you can buy one or wind one with fewer bifilar windings (you can get away with 15 bifilar turns on 80m).

2) We are very close to publishing the way to use a 160 L over FCP, essentially as-is, as an 80m end-fed half wave L (EFHWL), using the 160 FCP and a small trick as the 80 counterpoise. 

Requires relays, coil stock and a fixed value TX cap to pull it off. The tuning stuff will need to be in a waterproof project box at or very near the L/FCP feed point. The extra stuff goes between the isolation transformer and the L/FCP. The network does not use a vacuum variable cap or variable cap. Tuning is done with taps on one coil and relays. A vacuum relay is required because of the high Z feed on 80m. 

The 80 EFHWL is probably the best 80m single wire antenna there is, unpopular only because you can't feed it directly with 50 ohm coax. With the tuning method used, a single tuning range (one specific relay-switched-in set of taps) is 50-100 kHz. So enough relays will cover all one's favorite 80/75 operating frequencies.

The hemispheric pattern of an 80 EFHWL will cover the low angles that an inverted V cannot, and will cover the high angles that a vertical cannot. It does not have any of the loss factors of a vertical at ground, because the current max is up at the bend 50 to 70 feet up in the air. There are exceptionally low ground losses for anything with vertical polarization. The up high current max also helps in getting above ground clutter and tree top dielectric losses for low angle radiation, something the models cannot analyze. 

External to the tuning box, one wire L and a single FCP can cover anywhere in or even all of 160 and 75/80 bands. 

I'm sure at some point someone will build the tuning box or offer a kit. 

I have a very typical medium L over FCP at my house now and can monkey with the network inside my tractor shed out of the weather.  It will be my 160/80/75 antenna ongoing. We'll investigate 40 and 30 when we've put 80/75 to rest. 

73, Guy K2AV

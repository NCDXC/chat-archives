I have a good ham friend (callsign not given to protect the innocent) who used to be a commercial flight instructor and a test pilot for Boeing, as well an accident reconstruction expert for airline crashes. (He knows EVERYTHING about commercial aviation.) As a side hustle, he would do maiden flights for aircraft (requires a special license) and deliver aircraft around the world.

 

He was hired to deliver a new long-range 737 ETOPS to Turkmenistan. The aircraft had a custom paint job, with “Air Turkmenistan” on the side, in a bright green color (same as the flag of Turkmenistan). He was accompanied on the trip by a group of “ministers” (i.e., thugs) from the Turkmeny government. He flew the craft from Boeing’s final assembly plant in Washington state to London Heathrow for a refueling layover, where they got some very strange looks from both passengers and other pilots who could see this rather unique-looking aircraft on the tarmac.

 

When they got to Ashgabat the next day, the “ministers” thanked him for his work, and handed him final payment for the aircraft, in the form of USD $30 million IN CASH in a suitcase! They then arranged for private, secure return transport (thugs included at no extra charge) for him to deliver the funds to Boeing. I guess that’s how you do business in a rogue nation.

 

FYI: Ordinarily, a twin-engine aircraft like the 737 is not permitted to make extended flights over water, where there are no nearby airports available for emergency landings. An ETOPS-rated craft is allowed to fly in places where they can be as much as 3 hours from an emergency landing place. While the acronym officially means “Extended range Twin-engine Operations Performance Standards”, pilots call it “Engines Turn, Or Passengers Swim."

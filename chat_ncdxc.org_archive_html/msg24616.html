On Dec 10, 2021, at 2:42 PM, Rich Seifert KE1B via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

﻿Just to let folks know:

My 40 meter Extended Double Zepp is now fully repaired and back up in the trees. When I finally pulled it down, I found a number of problems:

1-One of the series capacitors was charred, I suspect due to moisture infiltration, it arc’ed over under high power. I built and replaced both caps, using ~6” of LMR-400 as a high-voltage, high-current capacitor. They are mounted on a Corian insulator, sealed with RTV, and encased in PVC tubing with end caps.

2-One side of the dipole was *completely disconnected* at the center feedpoint! This *may* have happened when I pulled the antenna down using the feedline, or it may have happened due to metal fatigue in the wind. In any case, it would cause the feedline to radiate and generate tons of RF in the shack. 

3-One of the “drooping ends” of the EDZ had broken off completely, clearly due to metal fatigue. I replaced that section of wire, and connected it to the horizontal run using a short loop of *stranded* wire, to avoid a recurrence.

3-I was able to use the existing rope on one side to pull new, *neon orange* rope over the same high branch. I’m surprised the old rope didn’t break. There was about 20-30’ in the middle where the black outer covering had worn off, and it was down to the white core only. Fortunately, it held up and the new stuff is even thicker (1/4” instead of 3/16”), and quite visible from the color.

A *HUGE STANDING OVATION* to Bob K6XX, who took the time and trouble to come over and lift a rope over a tall madrone using his quadcopter and surveyor’s line. (This definitely beats the old "potato launcher" method.) It took two tries. First time, the wind (up at 200’ or so) dragged the line to a weaker portion of the tree with light branches, so it didn’t stand up to the tension when we tried to lift the real antenna. The second time worked perfectly, although it took a bit of maneuvering to get the antenna lines over various obstacles, including another madrone and my existing inverted Vees.

Everything tested fine, under power. I expect that winds and rain may cause the support lines to shift, but I can now re-lift any resulting slack from whichever side is needed. And I’ll see how it holds up during extended (contest) use, but at least for now the antenna is resonant, easy to match, and stable, even under full power.

Again, a BIG THANKS to Bob K6XX for his help.

Rich KE1B

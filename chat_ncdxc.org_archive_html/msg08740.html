I feel pretty much the same as Bert since he expressed my feelings and opinions nearly spot on:
Paul: I have enjoyed your posts, spots and many, but not all, opinions.  They need to be expressed regardless of ones agreement.  You are a significant contributor in the hobby and our club and your absence from chat will be a downer.  I too follow your blog and hope to 'see you around'.
 
As I read about the clubs contribution to the award of the other guys across the pond, I wondered why we needed to be involved at all?  If we decided to not have our own award, that should be it.  I bought umpteen tickets to the Luncheon drawing knowing I would be unlikely to go home with a prize.  I was really helping to offset the cost to the club.  To send money overseas for the award chewed up any contribution I made for our costs.  I'd vote against it in the future, if given the chance to vote.

Paul; I hope you will reconsider your withdrawal from chat. I for one,
highly respect your knowledge and opinion on DXpeditions and DX matters
in this day and age. I happen to agree with your questions and feel that
the response you got from your question to Col about criteria for awards
we contribute to was very rude and inappropriate. It smarts of a fifedom
attitude and an apparent threat to his ego that you would question his
process or thought. The donations given by NCDXC and NCDXF are a trust.
We should have at least some input to what we help finance. When I
donate to a DXpedition I try to have some knowledge of who the people
are and their planning process, with a hopeful view of whether or not I
might have chance to work the DXpedition. I prefer knowing that the team
has clear intentions of benefiting Amateur Radio in general and
especially those with small, limited stations in Zone 3. I will
certainly miss seeing your contributions to the discussion on Chat, even
though I am following your blog. 73's, Bert, K6CSL

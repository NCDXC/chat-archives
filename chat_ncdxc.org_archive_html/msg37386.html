I’d tend to agree with Paul and his recommendation of an Elecraft KPA500. In considering solid state vs tube, it’s important to consider the shrinking availability of tubes. 

That said, tubes are far more tolerant of abuse, such as a high SWR. LDMOS amps don’t like SWRs>1.5, so a tuner is a necessity. 

I’ve been happy with my KPA1500 which was my first new amp purchase 5 years ago. SS amps may need periodic repair, and the Elecraft guys are right there. The built in tuner will match most antennas up to 3:1 and has been fine. 

If it’s in your budget, the 1500 is a great amp and probably the last you’ll buy, although not a robust as the combat-grade Flex which does require an external tuner and does not have a local repair facility.

On Saturday, October 26, 2024 at 10:53:38 AM PDT, Paul N6PSE via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

 

 

Will,  the best bang for the buck amplifier is the Elecraft KPA500 amplifier, which will give you 600 watts output all day long. With your wire antenna, you may need to protect the amp with a tuner. I recommend the Palstar AT2K which is a manual tune tuner. There are many used tuners on the market. 

 

If you need more than 600 watts, I recommend an ACOM 1010 or the ACOM 1000 tube amplifiers, again protected with an outboard tuner. 

I have found that amps with a tuner built in are not as effective as having an external tuner. 

 

When you go up to the 1,500 watt amplifiers, you are certainly likely to "meet your neighbors" as RFI becomes quite an issue in the shack and adjacent to your station. 

 

Have fun DXing!

 

Paul N6PSE

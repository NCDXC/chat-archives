That brought back unpleasant memories of my being detected a half-mile from my parents’ house on a freshly built Heathkit stereo. The owner refused to allow a thirteen-year-old to mess with his new stereo, and refused to accept that the issue was in his stereo, and not my 2X4-400A amp.. It sounds like you have a great neighbor.

First of all, I’d attempt to isolate the source of the problem by deconstructing his audio system. Be sure that once the turntable, preamp and all interconnects are disconnected, the interference disappears. If it does, reintroduce each component in the chain one by one. Start with just the RCA cables that used to be attached to the output of the preamp, then only the preamp,, then the preamp with the turntable RCA cables, etc. until you have a good idea of what component in the chain is rectifying your RF. Don’t forget that power cables (to the turntable, preamp, or amp) can also act as antennae.

The it’s time for new toroids… the more wraps, the better. It’s a crazy exponential function. You may need to be sure that every lead into or out of each component is choked.

I hope you figure it out! GL de Bruce, K3NQ

Hello all,

 

I've met a couple of NCDXC folks at Visalia this past spring.  Thanks for accepting me as a member but I haven't been to any club meeting yet.  I hope to be able to do that soon!

 

I hope you don't mind me asking, but I sure could use some help with RFI I'm causing on my neighbor's stereo.

 

My station:

1) Operating barefoot (100W max) SSB and CW. I see an amplifier in my future too.

2) Home brew EFHW inverted "V", grounded with 100 ft of RG-8U feedline, homebrew 1:1 choke balun at shack (read Jim Brown's choke cookbook info, understood some of it, but really appreciated the info I got out of it!).

3) E-Antennas 10-6M Moxon, Choke balun at antenna feed point (series of clip-on ferrites, came with the antenna).

4) Mike Traffie design Hexbeam (thanks George, W3AB), use the 1:1 choke balun at the shack.

 

The problem and the solution, phase one.

 

When I first set up my station with the EFHW and Moxon Yagi, my good next-door neighbors, were hearing me through their subwoofer on 10 meters only. I wrapped their RCA type subwoofer connection wire through a type 43 toroid, 12 times, and it solved the problem. Everyone was happy.

The problem, and no solution yet, phase two.


A couple of months ago they got a 70’s era turntable and a modern preamp they use with it. They use the turntable/preamp through the aux channel on their stereo and when they do, they can hear me talking, or code, through their left and right speakers. So far, I've just tested 20 meters. 

I tried type 43 toroids on the speaker wire with no luck.

I tried wrapping the RCA cables both from the stereo to the preamp and from the preamp to the turntable with, first type 43 toroids and then type 31 toroids. The 31 toroids seem to make my speech more garbled than the 43 did, but the interference remains. 

 

Does anyone have any ideas for next steps? 

Stack two toroids on input and output of the preamp?  

Any suggestions would be appreciated.

 

By the way, these guys are great neighbors.  In fact, all my neighbors are great. I want to preserve the good neighborly vibe since I live in a fairly congested neighborhood, Santa Cruz Gardens.

Cheers,
Rich, KN6TRY

I was able to test the KWS-1 that I picked up last weekend a little more, and it seems to check out as much as I am willing to test. I’ll be having the RF Deck restored as nicely as my 75A-4 was. I was able to find the 110v Collins relay for $50 bucks and it is in pristine condition. I also found a D-104 mic – which seems to be “the usual” for this rig for about that much and it’s in great shape. I will use my Begali Intrepid Bug as the key – which will bring me back to my WN2QHN and WA2QHN days back near the Delaware Water Gap in NJ. Finally, I found a pair of NOS 3Cx250B Eimac tubes – plus some other spares, so I’m getting ready to have some big fun with this old set. I’ll be rag chewing and checking into vintage radio nets for sure – really looking forward to that. I’ll also start having longer “conversational” CW QSO’s – I’ve had enough “599 TU” QSO’s to last a lifetime now – hi hi. I want to help keep CW alive . . .

 

A couple of questions – for those of you who have owned these (I know Al – K6RIM and Bruce – AH0U have):

 

  1. What other 50’s microphones would work? Would a ribbon mic work? I looked at the old RCA ribbon mics – but they are ridiculously expensive. But other 50’s vintage mics are reasonable. The Heil PR77 is a retro mic that might be nice – and a few mics sold by Sweetwater and Guitar Center might work – some are even ribbon mics. If the D-104 is the best – then I’m done . . .
  2. The KWS-1 is 1000 watts input – what would be the SSB, AM and CW output be? I’ve seen all kinds of different stories on this – and I doubt I will bother with AM
  3. Any hints or tips? Tuning the KWS-1 looks about the same as any tube linear amplifier – and yes, while I’m practicing I’ll have a 2KW air cooled dummy load

 

These rigs are really something – now I know why Collins is still revered.

 

73 and the best of Christmas, New Years and all other Holidays to you!

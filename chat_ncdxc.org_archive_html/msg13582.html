On 5/6/2017 11:15 AM, Russell Bentson via Chat wrote:
icing our concerns that the

    language of AB1222 does not go far enough, insofar as completely
    clarifying and excluding mobile radio devices with corded
    microphones from enforcement to the existing law.

    *In the current bill as amended, the use of a "handheld device" (a
    microphone for example), can be cause for a citation, while
    operating a mobile radio while driving.*


Maybe someone can explain to me why we need to use handheld microphones.
When I operated mobile, I always used a headset and VOX.  I never
had any problem with the VOX vs PTT.  Besides keeping my hands on the
wheel, the headset earphone allowed me to hear better.

BTW, are any of the people lobbying for handheld microphones also
lobbying to legalize CW paddles?  It seems to me that is a higher
priority since I don't have a hands free option.  Of course, it
is very hard for any passing officer to see my paddle on the
armrest (I am left handed).

Rick N6RK

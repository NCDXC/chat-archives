Great year for DXpeditions! All of these gave me something new, except for T33A.

I'm going for the unconventional one - VU7AG. I had really low expectations for this operation as it was a group of very newbie DXpeditioners. Krish did an outstanding job of training them. They also modified there operating plan based on actual propagation. They were always there for our evening long path opening.

Steve, K6UM

Hello fellas,
 
Well since the NCDXC has eliminated the DXpedition of the Year Award, we will never know who would have won this years award from our perspective.
 
Just for fun, lets take a look at this list of major DXpeditions for 2013 and see if we can figure it out. Feel free to respond with your top pick and if you care to tell us what made it the best of 2013 for you. Lets keep this fun and light-hearted!
 
I’ve taken this list from NG3K.com and reviewed it against the list of DX World.net DXpeditions. Small 2-3 man DXpeditions were not included in this list. Which one should get the imaginary prize?
 

2013 DXpeditions

 

S21ZBC/S21SBB  November 17-26, 2013, 26 Team Members, No stats available (no Clublog)

XT2TT, March 2013, 7 Member, 76,377 contacts by the Italian Dxpedition Team

9U4U, 9 Operators, Feb 14-23, 2013. 70,330 contacts.

TX5K, 24 Operators, Feb 18-March 18, 2013. 113, 601 contacts.

XR0YG, March 20-27, 2013, 4 Operators, 24,000 contacts.

XR0YY, Nov, 2013. 11 Operators, 19,948 contacts.

XR0ZR, November 8-20, 2013, 8 Operators, 59,288 contacts.

VU7AG, November 20-December 10th, 2013, 9 Operators, 55,325 contacts.

TO2TT,  Oct 3-17, 2013, 7 member, 64,000 contacts.

C82DX, October 15-23, 2013, 14 Operators, 26, 496 contacts.

XZ1J, November 15th-26th, 10 Operators, 53,653 contacts.

VK9NT, May 3-13th, 2013, 11 Operators, 24, 665 contacts.

5J0R, November 1-11th, 2013. 12 Operators, 80,508 contacts.

Z81X, 8 Operators, Nov 15-28th, 2013, 41,322 contacts.

9M4SLL, March 10-18th, 2013, 9 Operators, Stats not available.

5X8C, Feb 6-16, 2013, 20 Operators, 81,352 contacts

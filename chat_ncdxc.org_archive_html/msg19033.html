On 2/28/2020 11:41 AM, David Jaffe via Chat wrote:
In my opinion, ARRL should wise up and take the lead of the CQ 160 m contest, with the entirely reasonable rule of allowing a remote receiver within a reasonable distance (100 km, I believe).   Otherwise, city dwellers in noisy environments will eventually give up on DXCC... or they'll just ignore the out-of-touch ARRL rule.
I would like to turn this rule inside out and operate a remote transmitter. I'm not sure if the remote receiver rule covers this. 
(In some sense, it is like a remote station which itself has
remote receiving.).

What I envision is that I drive to a quiet QTH and listen
on a mobile antenna using a radio in the car.  I
then operate the transmitter remotely.  With the car
parked, I could string up a quick beverage or set
up an array of receive verticals using whip antennas, etc.
The bandwidth for the transmitter control link could be
very low.  MUCH lower than what would be needed for a
receiver link.  I would only need temporary access to the
receive site, leaving nothing behind when I leave.
And of course, DC power requirements would be very
modest.

You can even have teams go together and share the RX
array and control their individual transmitter sites
back in civilization.

Ideally you could find a good receiving site that also
had line of site to some access point for the control
link.  Maybe a cell tower than you can connect to with
your mobile hot spot.

I've earned all my Q's (243 countries and counting) *AT MY* station in my spare time and never moved further than 120 miles from my first (meager) station.  I've been offered elegant stations to use but my moral code won't allow me to cheapen my efforts.  I will earn those Q's on my own, with my own gear at my station; period.  Anything less is cheating. 

A return to the 150 mile from 'home' rule (all contacts originate within 150 mile radius of the starting point) for any award is the 'high road'; to tighten or challenge hams to do better instead of pandering or wimping out.  That rule would allow reasonable accommodation for the HOA inflicted or similar for entities that span multiple time zones. 

Shame on you ARRL, shame, shame, shame!  You've opened the door wider to cheapen the certificate.   Shame!

If you read the entire article.. there is a big section on ethics.. and what your DXCC means to YOU.  The bottom line is the ARRL took the high road.. enabling more options. 

At the most recent NCDXC meeting, Wayne N7NG mentioned that ARRL’s view (not withstanding challenge, honor role etc), is that DXCC is a PERSONAL achievement and not intended to be a COMPETITIVE measure.

DXCC is what it means to YOU - not to someone else.

I think ARRL would do themselves a big service to highlight this explicitly and put the debate to bed once and for all.

Individuals can and do hold themselves to higher standards than others - true in many aspects of real life (as opposed to Ham Radio life), contesting and Dxing.

Perhaps this should warrant an end to the “last one standing” at IDXC during the banquet when folks stand based on their challenge numbers?

I know the basis of my own DXCC as well as the other award/contest certificates I have (with even a couple on the wall).  

That is what is important to me.

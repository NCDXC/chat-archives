Dave, to me the DXPP is a positive term and opportunity for further funding for costly mega-DXpeditions. The question is just that DXPPs need to be facilitated the way that the overall DXpedition

would gain and still perform the best it can while those DXPPs would get best ROI for their money. In my discussion I had suggested some that were already covered here, such as allocating their

operating on most suitable bands/modes and giving them a maximum guidance. This is a matter of DXpedition organizers and funding community for most part and the main thing is that the matter

is noticed and discussed for the better of all parties. I do not suggest that the DXPP logo would be printed on the DX-pedition T-shirts. If it would it should be for XXXL size I guess!

 

I was helping DL1MGB of TX5K fame and there we used the matrix to indentify the talent and it was as:

 

A: Those that think they can handle pileups  ---   and they can.

B: Those that think they can handle pileups ---    but they cannot

C: Those that think they cannot handle pileups – but they can

D: Those that think they cannot handle pileups – and they cannot

 

Now talking to Chris after the event he felt obviously that B group was very challenging while C was very rewarding. For management purposes we compared our early evaluation to the outcome

and we were off only by one. This kind of thinking can help the DXpedition management to allocate its resources the best they can.

 

Dave, I was just bringing this valuable issue up for discussion just as we do today. I am an Old Man and I had no intension to bring my name into any comparison as such but if you honestly ask

how can one improve his skills for pileup management and demanding operating events there are many possibilities. Personally 50 years ago my selection was entering the contests where many

similarities exist. When I hear you on 160M SSB Contest I realize that you are at that job!  And you do a good job! But 50 years ago I had no idea that I will be talking this subject with you today.

 

Was Martti DXPP one time….to me DXPP are often older types with fat valets – Martti was not such. 50 years ago I had no valet and I was young with burning desire to do DX. Honorable Merle, K6DC

(SK) once called DXPPs as “Oil-Barrel” DX types” which term I was also thinking. DXPPs being XXXL size is my dry humor! Pardon as they say in Montenegro!

 

Now up to 14.226 KHz to my morning exercise. See you there.

 

Martti, OH2BH

Paul, great topic. I keep up with your topics of the week and its replys, but never have chimed in until now.

 

I dont't like the term "DXPP" and what it stands for. It seems Martti groups these guys in a class that labels them inexperienced and inadequate. Many so called DXPP's didnt just get their license yesterday. Lots of guys have been in the hobby many years unable to go on DXpeditions because of family and career. Now that they have the time and money, the only thing they dont have is the "reputation" of being a world class operator by the dx community. I can tell you there are many world class operators who have never been on a DXpedition. This does not make them a DXPP.

 

Being a New England Patriot fan, what if Tom Brady never got to play. Sitting on the bench he would never have become a world class QB. Same with guys wanting a chance to operate on a DXpedition. They may turn out to be great world class operators if given the chance. It is not all about the money. DXpedition team leaders should always consider new guys with the passion for DXing. We need to mentor new guys to fill the void that is fast approaching.

 

Funding to any DXpedition is important, but having one guy on the team not well known or considered a "newbie" should not hurt the teams ability to aquire funding. Again does this make him a DXPP? I think not. All DXpeditioneers had their first DXpedition sometime. The great ones didnt just begin as "world class" over night.

 

Fifty years ago how good was Marrti on his first DXpedition? Was he a DXPP then?  

 

Dave

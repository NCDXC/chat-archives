On Jan 9, 2015, at 10:54 AM, SPWoo via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Hi Keith,

I could never learn how to solder a PL-259 properly.  My work looks awful, with melted cable jacket on every connector.  After much frustration I'm now using crimp-on connectors.  There are two kinds of crimp-on connectors, one requiring soldering on the center pin only and the other one does not.  I have used both types with great success.  I purchased a crimper kit from DXE.  With it life is good and with much less frustration.  If you look at the pre-cut coax assemblies from HRO and DXE they are also the crimp-on type.  GL.

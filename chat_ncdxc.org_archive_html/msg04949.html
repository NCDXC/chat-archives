K9YC make a critical point about first having a VERY tight mechanical bond.  In my misspent youth we built many drag engines and learned the hard way that the absolute tightest mechanical-only  bond--leaks. Be it water, gas, oil even grease, when hot, the microscopic "hills" and "valleys " on the two metal surface are, on even the best machined pieces, still able to allow wicking-(call it osmosis? )---as my dads driveway testified.  It doesn't take any pressure at all--  it simply wicks through. Worst case is salt air--not a Mi. problem but in the Pacific islands or coastal regions like Fla, well, simple to cure. We all know how aluminum loves salt...hi.   So, long way around, absolutely tight as you can but when the water gets in I prefer the conductive surfaces have no place for it--and they need only the thinnest of conductive grease films to work.

Quick search of the internet, my first entry gave me what is posted below.  Wrapping your coax connectors with good solution is much better idea than to use NoAlox that improves electrical conductivity (between core and shield).

Antioxidant Compound
Noalox is antioxidant compound that penetrates aluminum oxide. It guards against oxidation.  Noalox improves electrical conductivity and produces a cooler connection.  It is excellent for wire connections and aluminum antenna parts.  This is what you should use when assembling an antenna that has parts that will oxidize.  This is great stuff.

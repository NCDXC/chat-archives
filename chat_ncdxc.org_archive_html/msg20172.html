NCDXC friends. I want to remind everyone to backup your logs. 

I am on "pins and needles" waiting to see if my data can be recovered from a Samsung SSD drive that I had installed ten months ago. It suddenly died without any signs of trouble. 

I was religious in backing up my logs to an external drive until 3-4 months ago. Fortunately, I had just done an upload to LoTW.

+ While better than nothing, you can only recover a subset of your log data from LoTW: callsign, date, time, frequency, mode, station callsign, and propagation mode. Information like names, signal reports, comments, QSL card confirmation status would not be recovered.

So avoid my situation and back up your logs to an external drive. 

+ As a complement or an alternative to an external drive, you can backup your log over the internet to a free "cloud" service like DropBox or GoogleDrive or Microsoft OneDrive. The advantage: there is no cost, and your log data will survive a local disaster that might damage an external drive.

+ And don’t' forget to backup your LoTW Callsign Certificates and Station Locations:

<https://lotw.arrl.org/lotw-help/backuprestore>

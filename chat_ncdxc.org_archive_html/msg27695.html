On Jan 30, 2023, at 11:42 AM, Kent N6WT via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Looking at the weather, I don't see a weather window till Friday to get stuff on the island. I think they said they need at least 6 hours to start.


Ideally, they would like a 6 hour initial WX window. But these folks have prepared; there is a “Plan B” which allows them to move equipment, supplies, and people onto the island in a series of 90-minute windows. They would prefer not to do that, but it is in the contingency plan. 

Because anyone on the island can possibly be stranded if they land and the weather prevents a return, they have a protocol for what people, gear, and supplies are to be loaded on each and every Zodiac traversal. Their rule is that they must ALWAYS have sufficient food, water, and shelter on the island for at least three days, for every person on the island at any time. That means that Zodiac trips are like the old puzzle with the farmer who has a single boat, a goat, a cabbage, and a fox to transport across a river. The goat and cabbage can never be left alone, the fox and goat can never be alone, etc., and only two items can be transported. You get the idea.

This is the hardest part of the trip; landing, unloading, and hauling all of that gear to the top of the glacier.

Good luck to the team, and good DX to all!

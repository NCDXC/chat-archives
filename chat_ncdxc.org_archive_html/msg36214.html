I think the point here, Rick, is to find an antenna to get on top band that is not NVIS like the low trap dipole.  The vertical mentioned has been used to get newcomers on the band and to add all of the 'easy ones' to DXCC totals.  It is not suggested as a great antenna like getting a wire up in the trees and then over in inverted L fashion.  PVC that is 2" or larger schedule 40 is pretty stiff and will work for a long time until it breaks down and by that time, this antenna will have done its job and the user is done or on to a better antenna or QTH.
I should mention that I have procrastinated getting up something for 160 m for 2+ years and time is running out for the summer.  I'll have a wire in my trees or a wound shortened vertical before I miss another season.

A helically wound vertical is based on wishful thinking.  It's just
a very long, small diameter coil.  Coils of this form factor are
VERY low Q.  The wishful thinking is the idea that somehow they
are less lossy than a lumped inductance IOW a conventional coil.
They only seem less lossy because they spread out the dissipation
over a long length and don't get hot.  Many builders of helically
wound verticals are tempted to use PVC pipe, because they can't
use aluminum tubing.  PVC pipe is a terrible structural material.
At least use fiberglass tubing.  Finally, it is an immense amount
of work to wind all the wire.  Good luck

Hi,

I fail to copy most signals announced here, and I’m often avoiding 20m because of that. Especially when 20m is busy, if you can see a DX signal with a good antenna, most likely that looks like a free frequency to those with poor antennas (because with an omnidirectional antenna the band looks even more busy and weak signals are drowned in noise)

When someone else (NCDXC member or not) is drowning a DX signal, you could try contacting the offending party (who is probably not aware of this, I doubt anyone would intentionally jam):
  1. Calling on the repeater might work
  2. The qrz email might work (but it could be slow)
  3. Sending a few “JammerCallsign QRM QSY” FT8 message *should* work, we are supposed to pay attention anyway, right?

Thanks,
Gabriel AJ6KX
On Jun 22, 2020, 2:25 PM -0700, Frank via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org>, wrote:
Paul, are you saying they know they’re covering a DX station and don’t care?  If so, I’m not sure how to handle that. Maybe revoke their membership to NCDXC?

But if that’s not the case, then I suspect it’s because the member who is covering the DX station doesn’t simply hear them. It’s not uncommon for me to not be able to decode announced FT8 spots when they come from members with superior antenna systems (I just have a hex at 35’). 

And since the usual practice on FT8 is to NOT call on the DX station’s frequency, someone who doesn’t decode them would have no idea they’re on top of the DX. 

Assuming it isn’t malicious, can we find a way to let a member know in real time that they’re covering up a station people are trying to work?

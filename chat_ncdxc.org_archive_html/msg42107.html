WSJT-X will NOT prevent someone from transmitting even if they haven't decoded the SuperFox if the user is in regular FT8 mode. Those people Paul listed most likely just typed 3Y0K into the DX Call window and started transmitting in the time period their software was already in - possibly without decoding 3Y0K first. If 3Y0K were in SuperFox mode, those people would then be QRMing 3Y0K, since the SuperFox uses a wide bandwidth. At least with regular Fox mode or MSHV, the odds of the  yahoos accidentally QRMing 3Y0K are much smaller. Also, I'm currently seeing N7BUD calling CQ and IW4CIL calling TF3VE during the even cycle, both of which would lead to QRM if 3Y0K were in SuperFox mode.

Which brings up yet ANOTHER advantage of SuperFox:

The software (at least WSJT and it’s “improved” variants; I’ve never used MSHV) will NOT ALLOW a station to transmit until it has decoded the SuperFox. You can’t manually enter the callsign in the call window and hit “Enable TX”, it simply won’t transmit. So only those who are really hearing the Fox can try to work him.

I recently gave a fairly in-depth presentation on the operation of SuperFox to my local club (San Lorenzo Valley), including the underlying encoding, the mathematics involved, and the authentication system. I would be glad to repeat that at a future NCDXC meeting, or perhaps at Santa Maria. I really dug into the guts (source code) to get all the internal details.

On Oct 15, 2013, at 5:46 PM, Garry Shapiro <garry@ni6t.com> wrote:

Hi Kev,

The K index has been bouncing between 0 and 5--it is 3 as I write. I suspect we really need a quiet ionosphere.

Your 80 and 160 operations will obviously draw heavy attention from Europe, which will be audible and probably loud all night on an essentially N/S path. OTOH, W6/W7 will have less than 2 hours or so of mutual darkness from our sunset to your sunrise, depending on how far east the caller is in that swath of territory.

May I suggest that that your topband operators concentrate on Europe and JA early in your evening after your sunset and then rigorously QRX them during your last two (or three) hours of darkness to work Stateside? May I also suggest that as you draw close to sunrise, you ask for W6/W7 frequently?

The late NM7M, an ionospheric physicist at UC  Berkeley, believed that virtually all 160m paths longer than 10000 km involve ducting between the E and F layers, which in turn requires a high entrance angle into chordal hop mode and an exit path near a terminator. The path from here to you is over 17000 km and the opening can be expected to be short. The strategy I suggest offers at least a fighting chance to your homeboys. There is a good chance that in NA propagation will sweep southward along the East Coast and jump to South America and disappear.

The 80m opening should persist for up to an hour or so after your sunrise, but 160 can be expected to die quickly as you pass sunrise.

I hope condx are better than that, but I am drawing on my personal experience from 3B9 in 1999.

73
Garry, NI6T

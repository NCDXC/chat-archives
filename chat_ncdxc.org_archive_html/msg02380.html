On 5/26/2013 6:47 AM, Rich Holoch wrote:
I am putting up phased verticals this weekend for 40M. they will share buried radials, and my plan is to separate them by 35’ and use the Array Solutions Stackmatch II and Phasing Unit – built for 40M. 

Hi Rich,
I bought W6NF's very nice phased 40M vertical package when he moved to HC2 last year. I then did some modeling, and compared the results with my high dipoles (at 110 ft). Thanks to their height, they interact well with the earth to provide strong low angle radiation, so they outperform what a 2-el vertical array can do.
I plan to use it for Field Day and CQP expeditions, where it is typically difficult to rig high dipoles. Optimum dipole height varies a bit with terrain, but in general, my modeling shows that the dipoles are better when you get them above about a half wavelength.
I haven't looked at the DXE box, but W6NF did his phasing with transmission lines. For our CQP and FD setups, we typically use a fixed 70 degree aim, so I plan to simply make a simple Yagi array, with the reflector a bit longer. This sacrifices a half dB or so of gain, with the advantage of simplicity -- I'll simply drive one against radials, and connect the reflector to radials. I expect to see an advantage of about 6dB to the east coast as compared to the low dipoles (20-30 ft) we have been using. 

73, Jim K9YC

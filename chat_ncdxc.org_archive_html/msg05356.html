On 2/4/2014 11:44 AM, Jeff Kinzli N6GQ wrote:

There is a lot at play with yagis on hilltops or stacks that has to do
with the band opening and timing.
It's important to understand that my modeling is for antennas in "flatland" terrain, and with limited options for where antennas can be installed. This applies pretty well to stations in our broad valleys, but not much to those with irregular terrain. No question about it -- we need to use HFTA to find desired mounting heights when the terrain is anything but flat within 5-10 miles in directions of interest.
BTW -- I copied HFTA author N6BV on my comment to Rich about HFTA not being applicable to vertically polarized wavefronts, and that I remembered Dean saying that it wasn't practical. Here is his response. 

=   =   =  =   =   =   =
The main reason HFTA doesn't do vertical polarization is that there is no database for the ground conductivity and dielectric constant for each and every point in a terrain profile. 

73, Dean N6BV

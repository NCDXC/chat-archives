HF AMPLIFIER SELECTION - A distillation of NCDXC braintrust



  1. 110 V vs 240V  : Unless its temporary shack and 500W AMP is the limit one is working towards its best to  make the 240V  investment. 1500W AMPs even at 60% efficiency may need 25 amps so if the target is more than 500W get this done. 

  2. Tube Amps : For 600-800W output Tube is a great way to get started if one can find one. Collins, Alphas etc  It also is kind to operator error compared to Solid-State. Best to get used  tube from known hams as there are a lot of duds on the used market. 

  3. Solid State : Prone to usage error during band and antenna switching. Also sensitive to high SWR antennas. Automate band and antenna switching and use good resonant antennas to reduce error.

  4. Repair: Almost everyone has “repair/service” done to Amps  So understand the cost and impact (loss of use) of getting repairs done. 

  5. Design : Look for good protection for  RF overdrive, overcurrent, overvoltage, device operating temperature and high load SWR.  The SWR protection must be fast (<50 usec) 

  6. Solid - State is the way to go if looking out 10 years as support and supply of tubes are disappearing. 

  7. With the exception of Elecraft that has “great” local support all others [ Mercury | SPE | FLEX | Palstar | ACOM ] needs Amp to be shipped out for repairs. RF2K (RF_KIT) also has a designated repair person now.



Now for the specifics - assume > 1000W


  1. Elecraft and SPE lead the pack in terms of actual recommendation.  

  2. Similar “high” praise for Flex genius  

  3. RF-KIT - Very quiet and well designed but needs to be comfortable working on it. 

  4. 1 recommendation for Mercury KM3KM 

  5. Folks have used ACOM 

  6. No reports of Palstar usage

On Fri, Jan 19, 2024 at 6:32 PM Dave Bottom <ars.kd6az@gmail.com> wrote:
You've gotten some good advice and it is understandable why there are so many questions to answer before deciding what to do.

Back in the 1990's I ordered an Alpha QSK Amp that after more than a year still was not delivered.  I finally gave up, cancelled the order.   I then purchased a nearly new ICOM IC-2KL Solid State Amp.  It was an easy choice for my ICOM transceiver back in the 1990's. It was 500W out and although the RF deck was essentially right out of the 1977 Motorola tech Notes, it had a very well designed set of protection circuits though and that amp served me well for 20 years.  It's still a great amp and I added the missing 10M Band including the unavailable to US amateurs Band Switch so I could use it with any rig on 10M, not just ICOM with auto band switching.

My Alpha 87A that I was running at the timeI started looking again was way too noisy for me and my old Vapor Cooled monster Alpha P40V Amp was a joy to operate with my vintage gear, but not chasing DX in the Heat Of Battle.  They are both gone now.

Ready for a legal limit amp that was quiet I did a lot of homework and decided with a significant amount of input from folks with several years working with LDMOS amplifiers that I would choose the RF-Kit RF-2KS, a fifth generation LDMOS amplifier.  My overriding motivation, besides knowing Reinhard, the designer, was I'm comfortable working on any of my own gear, and I wanted  a Silent Linear.  It IS ultra quiet.  I can't tell if it's on without looking at the power switch.  

If you can spend the time to fully automate Band and Antenna switching so things can't easily be screwed up, it is a solution I'm very happy with.  Drive power is set with my transceiver in a profile (by Band) that means it changes automatically when I change bands.  Automated Antenna switching is getting easier all the time.   As I have no problem working on my own gear, maybe an amplifier built in Germany is not for everyone.  

Then there are the little Mercury LDMOS amplifiers.  I've so far heard almost all good things about them.  Prices seem reasonable enough, but there are some limited features as their product line evolves.

Finally, there is no replacement for having great local tech support.  Especially if you're not comfortable working on your own gear. Here obviously Elecraft shines big-time.  They have both a more conventional 500W FET Amp and the 1500W LDMOS Amp.

That all said, there are many (many!) Collins 30L-1 Linears 600W out typically with 4 811As, (I have three 30L-1s, one I use, two waiting for someone, and then a 30S-1 waiting restoration - not for beginners) that once properly restored a 30L-1 will serve for many years and be reliable, and run $600-$850 (after rework) depending on cosmetics.  A hard to beat option these days to get started.   Learn and then upgrade.

Dave WI6R

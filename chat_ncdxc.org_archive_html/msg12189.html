Hi Paul,
Choice of antennas for 160M all boil down to available space and supports. In general, more is better -- more height, more/longer radials. Verticals are definitely better. Hearing is at least as important as transmitting, often more so. My limitation in working VK0EK was HEARING him. 

Some specific thoughts about your suggested options.
The Spiderbeam poles are NOT mechanically strong enough for use without guys. See our experience with one of their shorter poles here. http://k9yc.com/80M-FDVertical.pdf It broke with wind gusts in the range of 45 mph.
I also don't like like the electrical design of what you suggested using the pole. The lowest part of the antenna carries the greatest current, so it does the most radiating. Helical winding in that region reduces the radiation efficiency. If you're going to use inductive loading, use it as high in the antenna as possible. That's why top loading is so good (and it's why the DXE antenna is a very good design). I would also try to make the top a Tee if that were possible at your QTH. I would also try to use more radials. And I would consider inductive loading of the radials, with helical winding at their far ends.
At frequencies as low as 160M, loss in a short length of coax is negligible, even with relatively high VSWR. Rather than using inductive loading, I would accept the mismatch and either match the antenna in the shack with a good tuner, or design a stub match to be inserted at the feedpoint. In appreciation of your many efforts to activate rare places, I'd be happy to assist you with that. Same offer goes to Rich. 

73, Jim K9YC

On Thu,5/26/2016 9:10 AM, Paul N6PSE via Chat wrote:
Hi Rich, like you, I am planning to improve my station's 160 meter performance.
I am currently using a Buckmaster 270' off center fed dipole with the apex at 55'. I know, its way too low for 160 meters. Before the OCF, I was using an Alpha Delta 80/160 inverted V antenna. That was good enough to work FT5ZM and K1N on topband.
If cost were no object (which it is) I would probably chose the Force 12 Magnum 160VX2 shortened 160m vertical: http://www.force12inc.com/products/magnum-160vx2-shortened-160m-vertical-antenna-62-feet-tall.html
I've also looked at the Titanex V160 but the cost of shipping from Germany is just too high.
If I had the space, I would seriously consider the DX Engineering 160 meter Thunderbolt vertical: http://www.dxengineering.com/search/product-line/dx-engineering-160-meter-thunderbolt-vertical-antennas?autoview=SKU&keyword=160%20meter&sortby=BestKeywordMatch&sortorder=Ascending
I have also considered the Zerofive-Antennas 43 foot 10-160 multi-band vertical with elevated radials. I like the cost at under $300 but the performance is quite compromised.
My choice is likely to be a 60' Spiderbeam pole with an inverted L helically wound around the pole and the short horizontal leg will be directed towards the South Pacific. I will use three 65' elevated radials stapled to the top of my redwood fences going away from the antenna. I do not have room for a field of radials on the ground.
I've talked to Mike-K7IR at Steppir about modifying a BigIR for 160 meters but he says the motors could not push a longer/heavier tape up a taller vertical tube so no plans for a Steppir 160 meter antenna. 

Good luck with your project!

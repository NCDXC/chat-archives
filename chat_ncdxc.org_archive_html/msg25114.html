Paul,

Like you, when I work a CW pileup, and I hear the DX op send my call correctly along with a “5NN”, I reply with a “5NN TU”.  I expect him to reply with “TU”.  Then I know we have completed a QSO. 

I look at “RR73” like the “TU” on CW.  On my recent 40M SV2RSG contact, my WSJT software sent “SV2RSG N6SJ R-08” four times, while he was working 3 other stations.  Then I received “N6SJ RR73” and my FT8 software stopped sending anything further.  It then opened a new screen in the upper left corner that said something like “Log this QSO?”  Seems to me like the software doesn’t recognize a complete QSO until “RR73” has been decoded.

But that does not explain John’s experience of finding him in ClubLog even without ever having received “RR73”.

73,

Steve

N6SJ

Interesting discussion.  I've noticed many times that some DX will not send a 73 of any form (RRR, 73, or RR73).  What I usually do is try again until I get that 73.  To me that is the most assured way to confirm that it actually happened.  And then I find out later the DX may have me down for 6 contacts since I kept trying to get a 73 and then I feel like a pig.  Of course he may have sent that 73 but I never decoded it for whatever reason.  Until now I used to think a valid QSO consisted of call signs and exchanging reports (in a non-contest situation).  I always send QSL, TU or 73 depending on the situation and considered it a courtesy vs. a requirement.  But doing some more research on this it is looking to be more like a requirement.  Here's an interesting read:  https://www.amateurradio.com/what-is-a-valid-qso/#:~:text=The%20three%20steps%20in%20the,QSO%20was%20valid%20or%20not.

Getting a confirmation of receipt for the report given (other than some contests which have their own set of rules) is not being moralistic by any stretch.

It is PART of the contact; signifying an ending, a successful exchange COMPLETED.  Each mode has it's own format.  Without this closure, contact validity is in question.

Accepting less than that, is where integrity enters; and THAT is a personal choice.  The log entry is either confirmed or is a hopeful attempt.  Either LOTW (Clublog, others) or the contest judges sort that out later.

I (like most) draw the line at one 73 reception, requiring more is simply a waste of time better spent working the next contact.  If the other side doesn't get my 73 and sends a report again, it is my option to repeat it for them (I usually do that in this case).  But by then it's logged on my side, so if they didn't get my 73 ever, they'll still be able to confirm later.

Even the JT software accepts that as a standard (except in fox/hound mode, a different set of rules on the fox/DX side).

I had not been to the DX convention since 1976.  A wild trip from Hotel DeMille in San Jose, doing ‘maneuvers’ with 2 M car to car, while driving down CA 99 and meeting this guy that seemed important (OH2BH).

Since it had been a long time and I have more time now than anytime since I went to work after earning a degree, it was time to do it.  I am glad I did and went with little knowledge or expectations.

-The presentations I attended were by guys that can hold the attention of a big group and pass along knowledge along with some laughs.  Fun to hear about the exploits in Africa, for example.  There is something missing from simply reading or watching a video that you get from a live presentation.

- I got a lot out of the DX forum hosted by Wayne and a panel of experts.  A few straw polls were taken about a number of topics that could lead to a change in the future.  Fun to learn if I go with the majority or not.  Some, definitely not!

-I learned some tips from the DXLab Suite forum and got to meet some vendors and ask some questions face to face.  Mastrant was particularly enjoyable as a small manufacturer and vendor.  Very knowledgeable and pleasant.  They took some of my money for a new and very nice positioning lanyard.

-Most of all, I got to meet some guys that I know only from their being in a sought after entity.  In no particular order:

--Dave, WD5COV.  Great CW op that hinted about a potential operation by the Intrepid DX Group

--W0GJ, Glen.  We were lucky to be at the same banquet table with Glen and his lovely wife, Vivien.  I got some first hand details about the Bouvet trip, Navassa and others.  Also was delighted to talk about lots of other topics including his horses.  What a nice guy!

--Ken, LA7GIA.Gave us all some great African activations and listened for West Coast without having to be begged.

KO8SCA, Adrian.  Another very active DXer that has been around a lot.  I missed his 6O Somalia activation but look forward to having 4U1UN get on again.  He dropped some hints about it but it is not this month…  Also found some common ground in that Adrian is from Romania as is my XYL, Rita.  He was amazed that I was visiting there in 1983 that led to a marriage in Romania before the end of the dictator Ceausescu.

--Wayne, N7NG.  I have chatted via emails with Wayne and listened to him work DX as along as I can remember.  We had a nice chat from radio to politics!

 

These eyeball QSOs were really fun.  And, Adrian presented me two V84SAA QSL cards for good measure.

 

So, I plan to attend this event again and I hope you consider attending one.

 

CUL

Al

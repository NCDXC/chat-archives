Several comments. 

First, anyone contemplating a DXpedition ought to be either a serous operator or have other important resources to bring to the table -- physical strength and stamina, ability to deal with physical hardship, technical smarts, the ability to fix/build stuff when things go wrong, and a real TEAM attitude. And, of course, medical support.

I agree with Chris that contesting from a hot station with great operators is a very good way to build the operating skills and attitude needed. You won't necessarily have learned to deal with the massive pileups, but you'll at least have learned how to be FAST and ACCURATE.  So while I agree with your philosophy of bringing along new blood, I would insist that if that new blood wanted to operate, he/she should have done some serious homework first. Like serious contesting time. And I would do that whether the new blood had deep pockets or not. 

But that's operating -- all those other skills are important too, and sometimes the guys with those skills aren't great operators. 

73, Jim K9YC

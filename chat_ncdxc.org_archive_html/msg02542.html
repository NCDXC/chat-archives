On Jun 14, 2013, at 10:59 PM, Paul@n6pse.com wrote:

> Hello Dear Northern California DX Club Friends:
> 
> I've just returned from a five day visit to the DPRK.  We enjoyed a very cordial meeting with representatives of the DPRK Government.
> 
> Our proposals were presented and are receiving serious consideration. There is some interest in a radio sport type of event with International competition. 
> 
> I am very encouraged by the tone of our meeting that there is serious
> Consideration for our proposals.  In fact, the Deputy Minister of Tourism told me that if the decision was solely his, then permission would be granted.  
> 
> We must wait for all levels of both Ministries to review our proposals.
> 
> Tomorrow, David Flack-AH6HY and I will return to the DPRK.  We will visit various potential venues including the Rason Free Enterprise Zone.
> 
> We will also visit the Orphanages & Bakeries of the "Love North Korea Children Mission of the DPRK.
> 
> Best wishes from Beijing.
> 
> Paul N6PSE
> www.Intrepid-DX.com/humanitarian.php
>

>> Hello fellow NCDXC friends.
>> 
>> I've had a very enjoyable time here in Beijing.  My meetings with our Chinese emissary to finalize our strategy and presentation have gone well.  I also enjoyed a nice meeting with the founders of the Love the DPRK Children. These  fine gentlemen operate Orphanages and food production bakeries in the DPRK.
>> 
>> Last night, I was taken to dinner by Chinese hams and we discussed their own efforts to carry out a DXpedition from the DPRK.
>> 
>> We will have further discussions about aligning and combining our efforts as a means to increase our chances of success. We are delighted to enjoy the friendship of the amateur radio community in China.
>> 
>> In just a few hours, we are flying to Pyongyang where we will present our proposal for a large DXpedition, complete with humanitarian components to representatives of the various Ministries.
>>

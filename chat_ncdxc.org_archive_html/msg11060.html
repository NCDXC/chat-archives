According to his QRZ page, he travels a great deal, and so direct QSLs to his home address would not be seen for a long time (and not responded to, either). He recommends OQRS via Clublog.

So I went to Clublog to see if I was there, although I’m sure he won’t be able to upload until he gets out of DPRK. According to Clublog, he DOES has an account, but there haven’t been any uploads yet (not surprising).

However, there was an additional note on the account:

"3Z9DX visited Pyongyang in December in with the intention of acquiring permission for a future operation. During the visit he was able to be QRV briefly as P5/3Z9DX as part of a demonstration to DPRK officials.”

 So apparently, his brief foray (DXWatch shows spots on 15, 20, and 10 meter SSB) was for December 20-21 only, to show officials how ham radio operates.

I’ll be looking forward to him uploading his QSOs to Clublog.

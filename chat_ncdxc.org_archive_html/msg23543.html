On Tue, Jun 22, 2021 at 10:28 AM Paul Booth via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

I've had good experiences with Mosely. I started with just a TA33jrwarc on a rooftop tower and worked them all with less than 500w (which was the limit of the antenna). I could work everything I heard. I currently have a TA53 up on the same roof tower but was planning to replace with a Steppir 3 ele with 30/40. My main reason was to get a better solution for 30/40 as with the Mosleys I had to use a separate dipole. K6RIM has a hexbeam and he still beats me in the pileups! So a hexbeam seems like a good solution too.

73, Paul, W6IBU

I love my hex beam, and it will cover 6 meters where the Mosley won't, but it won't have the gain or F/B ratio of a Mosley Pro57-B.  The hex is just 2 elements, and since the elements are bent, the pattern is much wider than a multi-element yagi.  The Mosley is at least 3 elements on every band (4 on 10m) so if I had the room on my tiny urban lot, something like the Mosley would be my choice.

73,
 Frank N6OI

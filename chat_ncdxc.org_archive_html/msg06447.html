Saw this couple weeks ago and would like to share it with you all.  
http://www.youtube.com/watch?feature=player_embedded&v=5Z9136_Nhh4

Great education for those who may not know what Ham Radio can do for our community.

I once was a volunteer member of California Department of Forestry - Volunteer in Prevention, Incident Response Communication Team, this video enabled some old memories of my experiences with the CDF-VIP program.  During my 12 years of activity, I saw parts of California's back country that has been burnt over, major active fire around me from a short distance, walking the fire lines with fire fighters all the while providing Ham Radio communication (HF, U/VHF and digital) to and from Base Camp.  Knowing that I and other team members were providing communication to support the actual fire fighters, it made the job easier and happier memories.

It was in this time frame that I first learned about HF-NVIS communication and actually used it to support few fire events.  When I am available, I do participatein a Red Cross NVIS net Saturday morning on 40M.

Frank K., remember those trips we made up north?

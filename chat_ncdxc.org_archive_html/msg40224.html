I'm a baby DXer (started in 2018) and I don't understand where the rules of DXCC came from, so I went to the source (ARRL). Here is the link from DeSoto's original write up in QST (circa 1935)
http://www.arrl.org/desoto

The most interesting paragraph (IMHO) is this one:

PROPOSED DX SCORING PLAN
Now we come to a somewhat different, although an allied, subject, broached by N. M. Patterson, W4EG. He is perturbed, and it seems many DX men agree with him, over that fact that under the countries-worked plan one VK QSO from America counts for as much as working all eight VK districts. Similarly, the European ham with one lone W QSO gets as much credit as another who has worked all nine call areas. "That there is a whale of a difference you will readily see," he writes through Director Caveness.
In view of this situation, W4EG proposes that there be created a "rule for counting DX, to be known as the DX Score." This score will be computed by taking the number of districts worked in each country, and adding it all up into a grand total. For example, we'll suppose that W8BKP, who had when last reported worked 123 countries, counts nine W call areas, eight VK districts, six Spanish districts, etc. On the basis of adding the figures for these countries alone, the score would be 143; probably the grand total would be well over two hundred.
This seems to us to be an entirely rational suggestion, far more so than many that have been perpetrated. For ten years or so it has been impossible to work any farther, in terms of terrestrial miles; the only room for expansion is to work as many places as possible. The first recognition of this fact resulted in the inauguration of the WAC certificate. Totaling the number of countries worked followed naturally. This new proposal, expanding and improving the countries-worked idea, seems to be a logical next step. Indeed, following along this line of thought, one foresees the time when DX will be counted in cities worked, or stations per square mile, or something even more fantastic!
But for the present the DX Score idea looks pretty hot. It has the major beauty of simplicity. Just total up the districts worked, and there you are! We expect it won't be long before a lot of QSL cards will bear the legend, "Continents worked: 6; countries worked: 66; DX score: 92."

So, it seems to me that the DX Scoring got lost somewhere in space and time. There seemed to be an attempt of making the Award more meaningful. For instance, working European Russia + Asiatic Russia (11% of the Earth mass land) could be done with just 2 QSOs.  Working Scarborough Reef ( a rock semi-submerged in water) could be done with 1 QSO.
Had they implemented the DX Scoring plan, this problem of unobtanium entities would have been more diluted.

Working all districts (as they call it) in a particular Country is a challenge by itself. I tried to do it recently in LU, PY and it was a logistic nightmare (similar to DX). Get somebody who operates from a very low Ham populated area, make the contact and get a confirmation.  

Having them implemented the DX Score, North Korea would be diluted as it has only one active call sign: P5. Scarborough Reef would not be an issue with a rock semi-submerged in the water with one prefix.

Anybody here has the story of why DX Scoring was not implemented? Mr. De Soto seemed to be totally convinced back in 1935.

Roberto

One of the goals of the ARRL award program is to drive on the air activity. So we need an awards program that will support and drive folks whether they are just starting out or have been very active for 80 years. If we adjust the rules for honor roll how does this affect the overall ladder of endorsements in the DXCC program? If we make a change it should be to drive more on the air activity.

We now have DXCC Honor Roll and DXCC Trident Honor Roll. So we have created a whole new higher level tier in the DXCC program. Due to this change I prefer not to change the Honor Roll rules, but creating a stepping stone to Honor Roll based on an active list of entities. I would also be fine with changing the Honor Roll rules now that we have DXCC Trident.

I also wonder how many folks currently qualify for DXCC Trident Honor Roll. I assume there are a few.

As to "If there was a "Currently Deleted" list, what impetus would there be for anyone to try to convince governments to permit ham radio operations in their countries?" the people in those countries could still want to have access to the technology and maybe the ARRL could pull a page from the SOTA and POTA programs and have an award for the person to first activate one of these countries or give special recognition for their efforts, beyond giving them headaches like they did for the ZS8W DXPedition.  You know, grow with the time, modernize, be supportive and benefit their members.

 

They are not the only award-issuing entity in town and if they don't wise up, stop their internal scheming, and start better serving their members, they'll learn the hard way that they won't be the only amateur radio organization out there, just like the NRA and very recently the National Association of Realtors learned.  All it takes is a group of people tired of the BS who decide to do something about it.

I believe that if the ARRL offers and promotes an award that the Award should be attainable. Currently it is not possible (in a lifetime) to attain Honor Roll for anyone starting the journey now or in the recent past. Change is required when a program becomes stale.

Gene K5GS proposal to have an inactive list makes the most sense. It only requires a rule change and does not delete any entities. However, the outcry from the old timers of “NO CHANGE” is very emotional.

Compromise proposal: #1 Honor Roll rules remain unchanged and in effect becomes the legacy Award. You need to “Work them All” to receive the #1 Honor Roll status. No changes to the current rules for #1 Honor Roll. The rule to count entities for Honor Roll be changed to “you must have a total confirmed entity count that places you among the numerical top ten DXCC entities currently active in the last twenty years on the DXCC List” The Honor Roll has never been a “Worked them All” award. Honor Roll has always been an award for diligently “working the greatest amount of them but not all”. Additionally, a new award “High Honor Roll” could be created for working all currently active entities in the last twenty years.

In summary you would have three award levels (#1 Honor Roll, High Honor Roll, and Honor Roll).

Matt K6EME

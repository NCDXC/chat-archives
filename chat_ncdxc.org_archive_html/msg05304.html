An open letter to the NCDXC Board:

 

Dear NCDXC Board of Directors;

I think it is very unfortunate that at the January board meeting, the board quietly decided to eliminate the recently resurrected “DXPedition of the Year Award”. I don’t believe that the board sought or obtained any input from the membership. Is this transparency?

I believe the board is overreacting to my criticism of our process and result after the 2012 DXPedition of the Award was awarded at IDXC. I felt that the 2012 award was a debacle then as I still do now. The initial indicator of that is that only about 1/3 of our club members bothered to vote. There was no clear guidance or information as to what constitutes a “good” DXPedition. There was no review of the process or relevant information and performance statistics of each of the DXpeditions being considered.

In the following months, I made recommendations to the board on how we could refine and improve the process for subsequent annual awards. I proposed to our current President that the March meeting be set aside to review the statistics and other performance indicators at the March meeting. A well informed vote by the membership could then take place. These suggestions fell on deaf ears.

I find it interesting that we have a lot of board members and club members that are also members of the NCCC as I am. The NCCC gives out many awards each year without drama or heartbreak. As an organization they have refined their award process so that everyone seems to agree with the validity of their results. Participation is enthusiastic and exciting.

The NCDXC board on the other hand has just quietly cancelled the award as if it is now dead and buried.

Why have these awards?   In resurrecting the DXPedition of the Year award, we hoped to bring more visibility to the NCDXC and to create an awareness of the award to the extent that future DXpeditions would make an attempt to satisfy the needs of our members by working Zone 3.

We hoped that the NCDXC annual DXPedition of the Year Award would become coveted by DXpeditions and in time would become a prestigious event just as the SWODXA award has become which is awarded each May at Hamvention in Dayton.

I also feel that the prospect of possibly winning these awards can draw DXPedition Leaders from far and wide to our events such as IDXC in Visalia. With the elimination of this award, I think the NCDXC has reduced the attractions that draw DXPedition Leaders from far and wide to our events.

 

A properly administered DXPedition of the Year award can benefit both the organization doing the giving and the receiver DXPedition. The receiver benefits by the approval and acceptance of their performance upon the culmination of many months of planning and hard work. The organization doing the giving can also benefit by gaining more visibility for their organization and stature within the DX Community. The organization doing the giving also benefits when their award becomes coveted and sought after by the various DXpeditions who will often tune their performance to meet the goals and needs of the award giving organization. Some disagree with this concept but it is part of our current day reality.

I think DXPedition of the Year awards are a positive event if they are administered and given in proper recognition for the best effort of the year. They should not be used as a “People’s Choice” award, given to the most popular DXpeditioners for an effort that was not as significant/worthy as others.

I think it is unfortunate that the NCDXC board has taken the simple and swift action to cancel this award rather than to recognize the potential value of this award and to create a committee to hone and refine this award to make it more valid and important to all. Other clubs do this kind of award well, and I think we could too.

 

Sincerely,

Paul S. Ewing-N6PSE

On Aug 26, 2014, at 1:26 PM, Rich Holoch via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:


Because I will be able to get a rotatable 40M dipole up 60’, I plan on replacing my 2 element 40M phased vertical array (Christman Feed) with this rotatable dipole. I looked at the difference in EZNec, and it seems that I pick up 2 dB gain with the rotatable dipole over the phased vertical array. 

I do know that the phased vertical array was the better choice over a “low dipole” on 40M, but height makes this change worthy - or so it seems. 

I will also be able to use the linear loaded 40M dipole on 30M with my tuner quite nicely, and at 60’ with a pretty nice take off angle.

I would put the ladder line inside a pair of Jackite Kite Poles, and the single wire on the outside of the Kite Pole. It will be a very lightweight antenna that should handle full legal power and be very easy to build and stack above my 3L 20M yagi. 

Any thoughts?

I agree IF someone from the ARRL is present (to observe, perhaps answer questions) to add value to the discussion then take that to the DXCC board.  Those odds are high at IDXC, but wear body armor hi hi.


The ARRL could just treat some areas as deleted since they are very unlikely to ever appear again within our lifetime (DPRK, Afghanistan, others) which in no way lowers the bar (I have deleted as well).  You can see that with some (few still surviving) hams holding up to 370 or so entity counts which does not diminish their effort in the least.


My basic premise is that if the place cannot support reasonable expectations for human living (for more than the DXpedition duration or because of environmental/legal/political concerns); then it should not be on the list at all (some low tide only reefs come to mind).  Make the award match current reality (even more when it costs hundreds of thousands to attempt some places once every decade) as they do with changes after wars and break away countries.


The ARRL wants to promote hams and activity on the air; it's time to update where the bar lies to encourage more activity and make DXCC real and obtainable.  And it has been time, for some decades now.

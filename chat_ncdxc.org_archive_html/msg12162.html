On 5/22/2016 2:13 PM, SPWoo via Chat wrote:



With an analyzer he could home-brew better antennas.  I think it's
probably the most useful tool for him.  I'll see what else I could stuff
in the same box.  I highly doubt one could buy even a coax connector in
Libya.



I'm all in favor of helping him (I might even be willing to
contribute) but I am a little wary of throwing money at the
problem.  Possibly, his problem is more lack of expertise
than lack of test equipment.  He may need some elmering
to go along with the analyzer.  I understand that he is
operating under extremely difficult conditions, but it
should be possible to build a vertical using a tape
measure and then trimming it using the built in SWR
meter on the rig.  (I too own a 706).  Also, I thought
the 706 has a built in antenna tuner.  Thus it is
curious that he is looking for a tuner.

Maybe he wants to feed a random wire, or an existing
gutter, etc.  I am wondering if a telescoping fiberglass
"fishing pole" would be helpful?

The issue of the 706 overheating is simply that it is
not designed for RTTY duty cycles.  I don't know why
he would want to use RTTY anyway, unless his CW skills
are weak.

I am thinking that it might make sense to send him
an amplifier of the 30L1 class (four 811's) as made
by various vendors.  Ideally, it would have a wide
range pi network output.  (I don't think the 30L1
fits the bill).

Also, I recommend a single point of contact for
him to communicate with stateside, if we can "volunteer"
Glen to continue to do this.

I am worried that he was on every day last week but
now he has not only missed our sked but hasn't been on
for 3 days.  I see that he self spots, so we can probably
rule out his being on the air, but no one spotted him.

I'll help out if I can be useful.

Rick N6RK

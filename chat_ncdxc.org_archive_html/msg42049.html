It's presuming they are able to get established on the island; not an insignificant challenge and we all hope they do.  It presumes they're able to put up a reasonable station (iffy with the typical winds) AND keep it on the air.  THEN the concerns are other entities being louder (and less polite).  We're hoping but lets move on.

That DQRM avoidance is being considered, good.  But every change earns a response, the advantage is quickly overcome (like computer security methods, constantly updating).  Smart folks create, smart folks with a character flaw find ways to destroy or bypass that.  Creative folks that want to cause harm despite precautions, will always exist.

This plan is replacing one chaos for another; potentially worse.  Typical DX pileups are one chaos but this plan requires:

  • They have to be LOUD to break through the disaster they're creating to be heard; odds are against that being the case.
  • Hearing the ENTIRE phone calling spectrum (up 20 Khz or more?) to know where they are (to call), not possible.
  • If you don't know where they're transmitting because they always move, how do you know when to call?  It will increase the amount of blind calling that already exists (non-stop) which would easily cover them (most calling stations will be MUCH louder than Bouvet).
  • There is no such thing as a random QSY; everyone has a pattern (and the DX op becomes more fatigued attempting faking random).
  • Given the manners of (FAR too many) hams (ID machines, not listening and worse), finding where they're transmitting will be even more challenging; the QSO rate will tank.  THAT point must be considered, by them; THAT is the GOAL; make MORE contacts!
  • Given the mess of callers, this method implies working only the loud stations (which never stops); little guys lose.
  • Contest stations have an edge, perhaps (used to listening to a spectrum cacophony); the non-contesters won't make it simple.
  • The smaller stations best hope is the digital modes (which demand precision and timing), limiting the slots they could fill.
  • Semi-organized chaos (traditional ops), is still chaos; but replacing one disaster for another (potentially worse), is not an improvement and not everyone will get 'the word' so that's another level to compound it.  The military term Charlie Foxtrot comes to mind quickly.

I see zero advantage and to do something so radical on such a highly prized target at the last moment (no testing, no practice with an unclear style); draw your own conclusions.  I'd bet it has been thought of before and if it worked, would have been the standard for generations.  (Horse:midstream axiom)

I would expect a more traditional operating style will occur (if only to keep the rate up) with attempts at the new plan.

IF they were to stay (drift) within, say a 1 KHz window (CW), it would diminish some of the DQRM (tuning) but answering on the caller frequency over a wide spectrum is the definition of chaos.  Similar drifting for phone could help (causing those wanting to interfere to use digital/wider modes of noise).

Those <impolite descriptive> that want to QRM, will (human nature).  Nothing changes for the better.  Constant QSY only complicates (everything).

I'll try to add slots, like everyone else but it doesn't sound like a good plan.

73,
Rick nk7i

This strategy may only be implemented if the DQRM is very bad and slows down our QSO rate significantly.

Normal CW/SSB pileup handling is listening UP 1 kHZ on CW and 5 kHz on SSB.

In DQRM mode - we do S&P within a specified window like in any contest.

All stations are calling SIMPLEX. 3YØK tunes across the simplex window and works stations SIMPLEX.

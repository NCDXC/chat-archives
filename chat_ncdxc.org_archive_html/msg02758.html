Paul:

Sorry, but I believe your statements about Martti's DXPP position are somewhat misleading.  With 50 years of DXing under his belt, Martti is one of the most accomplished DXers on the planet.

Read his article more carefully:  http://www.dxuniversity.com/dxing/weeklydx.php?id=23

Quotes:

"DXPPs can and should be accommodated. It is reasonable that the DXpedition management can set aside some bands and hours exclusively for DXPPs."

"New times are coming for the business of DXpeditioning. Implementation of the DXPP concept will require far greater attention to operator scheduling and guidance."

"At this point, however, the DXPP could assume that the DXpedition leadership is prepared to assist with the selection of his operating bands and hours as well as assistance with his operating techniques. Unfortunately, this doesn’t always happen."

"DXPPs are likely here to stay because of the rapidly increasing cost of mega-DXpeditions and because the DXing community has failed to contribute adequately to them. I hereby challenge the first mega- DXpedition to present openly their DXPP plan to the general public and to the supporting organizations."

His tone may be a bit sarcastic, but Martti essentially supports the idea of DXPP.  What he is saying, is that having less experienced operators can work, but only if the DXpedition Leaders set expectations properly:  

- make sure that newer operators are properly trained before the DXpedition (i.e., thru contesting, webinars, pileup practice sessions, etc.)
- set up an On-Air operating schedule that balances skills sets on the team (i.e., blend seasoned operators with newer operators)

My Bottom Line:  A DXpedition won't "suffer" with less experienced operators onboard but DXpedition Leaders do have a responsibility to put together a successful operating plan and not just make it a random event for everyone.

I think it's unfortunate that Martti chose "Paying Passenger" as a derogatory term for newer operators, because when you think about it, essentially ALL members of a DXpedition Team are Paying Passengers.

But let's not speculate any further.  Let's see if we can get a first-hand perspective from Martti himself.  I'm copying him on my response to your question.

73,
John, K6MM

On Jul 29, 2013, at 8:22 AM, <paul@n6pse.com> <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:

Martti Laine-OH2BH has written several recent articles about DXPP’s or “DX Paying Passengers”. Martti describes a DXPP as an operator with only a minimal degree of operating expertise, but who contributes to the Dxpedition financing to help the Dxpedition.
Martti writes that DXPPs can seriously damage the credibility of an operation and create chaos on the bands. He is critical of the K5D Desecheo Dxpedition where a strong operator was relieved by a lesser skilled operator and problems resulted. Martti also criticizes the TX5K Clipperton Dxpedition, writing that “a seat was available for anyone who wanted to pay”. Martti suggests that the number of TX5K DXPPs added to the stress level of the Dxpedition team members and the Dxpedition audience.
Martti suggests that DX Foundations consider the number of DXPPs included in a Dxpedition when funding them. He writes that a Dxpedition may not qualify for support if the percentage of DXPPs is too high.
 
Here is your Question of the week: As members of the DX Community, are you concerned about DXPPs?   Do you feel that a Dxpedition suffers if there are a few lesser experienced operators on the team?   Should the DX Foundations offer less support for a Dxpedition to a rare place even if they have some lesser skilled operators on the team?
Give us your thoughts-

Jim,
You have good suggestions from several others. I do not see the need to migrate to a new OS for any of my uses, although for some programs 7 is more stable than XP. I do not need any of the features in 8. I do not see the hazard in continuing to run XP, particularly for programs that don't interface to the web.
Only you can resolve the pointer requirement, if that is a deal breaker then go figure out an acceptable compromise, maybe a wireless mouse. My choice for a new laptop was a General Dynamics GD6000. They are big and heavy and the screen isn't cinema style, but seem bulletproof, have all the ports, including a real UART serial port, and stock with Win 7. The touchscreen is slow but works well with a stylus and is effective if you want to poke a box on the screen. You can find them off-lease for less than $400.
Macbook with Parallels is more solid than it would seem. I have a 10" Macbook Air with Parallels that is my primary travel computer. I use OSX for all the things that require constant updates for web hazards including Windows Office for Mac 2011, and PC-only apps live on Parallels. Of my PC apps only Solidworks isn't happy with it. The VGA adapter dongle works fine for presentations. 

73
David WA6NMF

Crew,

I started looking for a new laptop back in the fall, and the best advice (I think) was to wait for a Thinkpad with one of their new "Haswell" processors. Well, they're due to hit the street in late April, and I see very attractive units at equally attractive prices. Specifically I'm looking at a G710 with 8GB RAM and a 1 TB hard drive, for $600 (this is a special price from Lenovo -- they say the list price is $849). I see two issues, though, that give me pause.

First, they only come with Windoze 8, and I've seen suggestions to wait for 8.1. At least for the shack computer, I need a computer that doesn't fight with my ham software. I'm mostly thinking audio here.

Second, they have only a touchpad, not a red mouse button. I hate touchpads, and have mine turned off.

One "gotta have" is a VGA port so that I can feed a projector to do presentations at meetings.

I don't mind backing up a generations if I can get something that better meets my needs, but I will probably buy more than one (the XYL needs one, and I need something on which to do modeling, etc.), and I really like the idea of being able to use common accessories (power supplies, batteries, etc.)

Comments and suggestions appreciated.

73, Jim K9YC

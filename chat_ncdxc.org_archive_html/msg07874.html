Dick,

I have no love for the DPRK, but you should consider that there are other counters on the current DXCC list that have not been activated in quite some time, are not likely to be activated any time soon--or both. They are where they are for political reasons, natural reasons--or both.

Among these are entities at war or engaged in civil war, such as Syria, entities under water at high tide, such as Kingman Reef, entities under the thumb of hostile administrations, such a Navassa (which may be activated sooner than later), entities that in modern context appear to have no reason to be entities, such as SMOM, and entities of questionable physical qualification and in political contention, such as Scarborough. I don't have the Most Wanted list in front of me, but you get the picture--there are always tough ones, and maybe that is what should be the case. The DPRK, as detestable as its government may be, is without a doubt a real country, unlike some of the coral head collections admitted to the list in recent times.

Consider also that the DPRK was last activated 12 years ago, compared to Kingman (14 years) and Navassa (21 years). History shows that eventually politics changes and what was rare may no longer be rare. Recall that when you and I were puppies, Libya was overrun with oil companies and was daily fare. Then along came Gaddafi and it became rare, and then not rare again. And I recall that Albania went from no way to tough but obtainable.

Finally, consider that, during its brief activations, many worked P5 after waiting many years to do so. I think one needs stronger reasons to deprive those DXers of their prize because it is no longer conveniently available.

This game---and it is a game---was never intended to be easy or quick, and it generally isn't. In the case of HR#1, it may be a lifetime challenge.

73,

Garry, NI6T, who has P5 cfd on phone and RTTY, but needs CW for the Last One on code.

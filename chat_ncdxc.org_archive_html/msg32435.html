After the discussion on Geochron  I played with Hamclock and Simon but also found another product by a Ham from UK. .

https://www.mapability.com/ei8ic/tmu/index.php

I put in a request and got a Trial version. Still evaluating but looks better than Hamclock which I have also setup and configured and is dynamic (unlike Simon's map). 

Here where it's better than Hamclock

1. 4K maps - truly fullscreen map 
2. Easy configuration to get what you want and get the clutter off
3. You can add your logs (from files) & feed it live data (only from N1MM though for now)
4. DXspottting (i don;t know how to configure this yet - still figuring it out  ) 
5. Other stuff - satellite tracking anyone ? , NCDXF beacons, PSK reporting etc. 

Cons 
-You need a PC but you can get one that is competitive with  the PI kits  - search "mini pc" on Amazon and it will likely run all your Ham software (Ryzen 5   or Core I5 class) . These things are 5x5x2 inch or smaller that you can glue to the back of a monitor/tv . I got one for $150 with dual 4K HDMI over the weekend!) to run all the ham software.

-It's not "free" but check out the price

Worth checking it out in my opinion if you want a Geochron in your shack.

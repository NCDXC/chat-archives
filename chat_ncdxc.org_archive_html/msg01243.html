Jim,

 

I’m fascinated by any new high end HF radio and Kenwood’s past performance with high end HF radios has been impressive.

 

As for American made radios – I have never liked any of the Ten-Tec radios right up through the Orion II.  The fit and finish not being of quality I would expect just annoys me to no end even if it is a good performing radio.

 

I have a fully loaded K3 ($5k) with the P3 and SVGA ($1k) plus the fully loaded KX3 ($1.7k).  Awesome radios especially the KX3.  Sitting next to these is the IC-7800 an OK radio, FT-1000D and IC-781 all excellent performers with excellent fit and finish but getting a little out of date.

 

So far the two radios at the main operating position is the Mark-V and K3.  Now with the Mark-V having the QS1R receiver in its RX loop providing P3 or better quality panadapter features – it is challenging the K3 station.

 

The TS-990S is probably not in my future but after waiting so long for a Kenwood high end offering with some great features seen in videos – I wonder how it actually works.  I have to put my hands on that rig.

 

73,

 

Bob – W6OPO

On 2/11/2013 9:32 AM, Bob Lanning wrote:

Kenwood will be there with the TS-990S.  I want lay my hands on that one.


I fail to understand the fascination with radios from JA when truly world class radios like the Ten Tec Orion II and Elecraft K3 and KX3 are made right here in the USA.  If you think Elecraft is out of your price league, take a look at the KX3 with the not yet finished 100W amp. $900 for the KX3, I'm guessing under $500 for the amp, and a few hundred bucks for the 100W tuner if you want it. The plain vanilla KX3 is only slightly larger than the remote heads for most mobile rigs, but has specs that beat the K3, puts out 10W, and comes with the second RX as standard equipment!  When I visited W6GJB last week I saw a really nice desktop stand for the KX3 distributed by the guy who does the Nifty manuals. 

And, BTW, the Elecraft and Orion II rigs do much better with lots of strong signals on the band and put out cleaner signals, so you're being a good neighbor.  Last I looked, you've got to be pushing $10K to match that performance with a JA rig.

73, Jim K9YC

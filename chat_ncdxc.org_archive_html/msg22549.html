With the first quarter of the DXpedition in the logs; it appears that the team will present chances for the hopeful on 40, 80 and 160M tonight (so far, substitutions are always possible).   ALL using FT8 (although they are excellent CW operators, but choose to push along at 39 WPM, above my ability to catch it all; numerous repeats needed for the masses).
FT8 does take away the ATNO itch quickly (gives small stations an equal shot) while they are migrating to CW over the past couple days.  Based on the 20CW pileup today (a SOLID EU wall until the band closed there); phone will be a utter zoo (small pun). Hopefully they will contain that to zones, paying attention to the propagation (still not awesome).  I have some confidence in that as they seem to know what it's all about.  This after story will be interesting.
It's clear that they are still struggling with obscene levels of noise (signal reports prove that).  Comments were made on 20CW today by the op; who also showed some signs of fatigue, in part due to the wide variety of poor operating skills in use (ID machines, not listening, calling when NOT the targeted call; the usual); comments from the op were made, again.  Clearly that combination causes frustration.
I watched on the waterfall; it's appalling how many don't bother to listen, a rather simple behavior to manage and transmit at the wrong times.  And how many choose to load an amp ON the DX frequency; which is currently running on a par with the up cops with their colorful commentary.
Gee I've missed complaining about poor operators (NOT) :-P but it's sure nice to knock the rust off of the DX skill set; to have a major pileup to bust... it's been a long time.
Of the prop apps I'm using; one is excessively hopeful another less so and both proven dead wrong.  The K6TU forecast seems the most accurate; but keep checking around while watching the spots; magic happens.  Openings that 'cannot' exist according to the apps, (60M last night for hours, but unknown what their power is set at) often surprise us.  Conditions just add to the amusement (challenge) value (I was +12 on 60M last night in the Antarctic but unheard anywhere in AF; a strange band). 

73 es GL, good hunting!
Rick NK7I  (having a BLAST, finally some new DX to dance with!)
PS What I've tried to do lately in the group is to inform and encourage others to participate.  I hope it's working.  Git on, git some!

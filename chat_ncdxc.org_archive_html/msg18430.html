While his comments are all valid and I believe them to be true, he misses one HUGE point about operating in an urban or otherwise confined space:  modes like FT8 allow tiny, inefficient stations to dabble in DXing or simple contacts in our beloved hobby.  Or put another way, QRP can play with the big boys, some.  In some cases, it's possibly the only means available to use the radio due to high noise or antenna limitations.

For me it is much more satisfying to complete a CW contact, even though my skills are very meager (I passed the 13 wpm test at the FCC but never really loved CW.)  I cheat and use memory or keyboard keyers which is only to stop the suffering of those having to hear it by key.

But since I changed calls in early Nov, in part because of FT8, I have already achieved (again) DXCC (less than 7 weeks).  Using the more 'traditional' modes would have taken much longer.

So my stance is that eliminating FT8 from a DXpedition is that it is somewhat self-defeating.  Since he wrote this, versions are available to multistream, raising the Q rate to 'acceptable'; fox/hound mode shortens even further the time required.  In the greater sense, it's akin to not operating phone because CW is more ham radio-ish...  we are INclusive, not EXclusive operators; we generally want MORE participation, not limit it.

That particular tech has been updated; the decision should be reevaluated, that choice is exclusive by it's nature (no digital modes at all from a DXpedition?).

It doesn't mean you must use any particular mode; they all matter... (well at least 340 entities times three [phone, CW, digital] modes times 9 essential HF bands...).  One can use the mode of choice, as few or many as there are.

Has anyone else noticed that RTTY hasn't been used much by most groups?  FT8 (et al) satisfies the digital bar that must be met for many awards.  The Q rate is MUCH higher than RTTY too and it's bandwidth efficient by comparison.

I hope they reconsider; give the small stations a shot and provide digital modes for all since the point of a DXpedition is to provide the answer to the need, not extend the need.

HNY es 73,
Rick NK7I

Thanks everyone for your comments. A few more comments from me below.

On Tue, Jun 28, 2016 at 8:26 AM, Paul N6PSE <pauln6pse@gmail.com> wrote:

Hank, a skimmer approach might help a poor/inexperienced operator but it also raises the ethical question, if the skimmer heard the caller and the Op did not, is it a valid contact?  Many would say it was not. 

I wasn't suggesting that. I was suggesting that the DX op use skimmer to help find stations to answer. Just like Assisted category in contests. You still have to hear and interact with the real station. 
 
The skimmer is also going to pick up remnants of the other signals being transmitted from the other DXpedition operators. 

Multi-multi and DXpeditions have to deal with this problem all the time. Certainly not insurmountable.
 
As Kevin said, skimmers only decode the best, most clear signals.

That is not my experience. I am amazed how well skimmers detect even the weakest, crummiest signals.


On Tue, Jun 28, 2016 at 7:30 AM, Kevin Rowett via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote
 
IMO - Good DXp operators need to work the entire audience - Loud, weak, slow, super fast (hi hi), chirpy, nervous, etc. 

Exactly. Skimmers could help find everyone.
 
CW Skimmer tends to pick out the “best” signals.  Yes, gets the rate up, but, excludes too many folks that you should work.

Not my experience. I repeat and emphasize that skimmers are amazing at picking out stations.

It’s just you can’t use it in the mode of “click and call”.

I was not suggesting that. I was suggesting using skimmer to help find a signal, click on it, listen to it, and then work the guy.

Again, thank you everyone for your thoughts and comments. You are the experts. I'm just a guy who is sometimes frustrated when it takes a DXpedition operator many, many seconds to pick out a call. I think skimmer could help with that. Which could improve rate, which could make sure more stations including the little pistols are worked, which would mean more fun for everyone.

73,

Hank, W6SX

The latest ARRL propogation report from K7RA gives hope for our current cycle.  He stated that spotless days continue this week and I was going to delete 
the message after reading this far (yawn)  but then I read his statement :  

Note that 1954 had more spotless days than last year, and it was
just prior to the biggest solar cycle (cycle 19) in recorded
history.

This sparked my interest and he included a link with info on spotless days,  below.  

http://www.sidc.be/silso/spotless


If you look at the graph:   Number of Spotless Days vs Solar Cycle Amplitude     it states below the graph that:
"the graph conveys pretty well that -in general- a LOW amplitude cycle is preceded by a solar cycle transition with a high number of spotless days, or vice versa"

So if 1954 had a low amount of spotless days and had more spotless days than last year, and it was just prior to the biggest solar cycle (cycle 19) could it be that this 
cycle will indeed be a big one ?

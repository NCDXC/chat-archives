It appears that they are done with Zodiacs.  The last
expedition pretty much debunked the Zodiac concept.
I am concerned at to how they plan to address the problem of the helicopters being grounded. For setup, they can always wait 
until they get a weather window, then execute the setup.

But then, there is the problem of getting back off the island.
In that case, they somehow have to be able to predict in advance
a flying day to use for retrieval.

I am wondering if the "proper Antarctica vessel" is somehow
equipped with more robust helicopters and/or better skilled
pilots to be able to fly in less favorable wx conditions.

It will be interesting for find out if they are planning
on employing the Radio In Box paradigm.  That may mitigate
to some extent the helicopter availability problem.
Possibly it would eliminate the problem of people being
stranded on the island.  Only equipment would be.

I am encouraged that the doomsday predictions about the
demise of the Braveheart meaning the end of DXpeditions
south of 50 degrees have been proven wrong.  The budget
of $440K for a boat with 2 helicopters seems almost
"cheap" compared to other numbers I had read about.

Congrats to the whole team!

Rick N6RK

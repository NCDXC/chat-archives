I keep visualizing in my mind trying to sleep in a tent that is beating you
up from the wind.  Then trying to work the pile-up again with the noise of
wind - constant noise.  I'm sure it was fatiguing, days of it...

Sleeping on the island wasn’t any problem at all.  I don’t recall anyone staying awake more than five seconds after hitting the bunk.

Operating was a little more challenging.  The table kept moving as the wind buffeted the tent.


I admire that team.  It must have been miserable yet they made the QSOs for
us.

It was challenging, but, we were never in any danger, nor was it miserable.  

The boat ride back to New Caledonia was miserable.  I’d had tossed my cookies by dinner time, with others soon to follow.  Even the boat captain described the ride as “unpleasant”.  He was a very understated guy!

We enjoyed each and every QSO and thank all of you for your patience and support.

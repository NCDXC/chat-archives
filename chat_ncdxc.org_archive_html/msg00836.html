FWIW I agree with John. Honor Roll is already achievable within 15 years and the Top of the Honor Roll is and IMHO should be a lifetime achievement that is extremely hard to earn.

 

Eliminate the “hard to work” and/or  the “maybe impossible to ever work under a current political environment” countries from the DXCC and this will have a chilling and degrading effect on those wonderful guys (and gals) who would otherwise go to extraordinary effort and expense and years of effort to get the “hard to work” and “impossible to work” “countries” on the air for us.

 

In my 50 years as a Dxer there have been many ARRL “countries/entities” that were “hard to work” and considered “impossible to ever work under the then current political considerations” that became workable because there was an incentive (and an opportunity) among the ham radio community for a few wonderful people (we know who they are) to change the environment in the particular entity and to create an opportunity in that entity to foster ham radio in that entity and to open up that entity for the rest of us to work.

 

IMHO eliminating all of the “hard to work” and/or the “impossible to ever work under existing political environments” ARRL DXCC entities which are not available for a fixed time period (e.g. 15 years) cheapens the top DXCC award and lessens the incentive to foster and create ham radio operation from the tough ones. I also believe IMHO achieving the Honor Roll which requires achieving confirmed QSOs with *less* than all of the ARRL DXCC entities  is a significant achievement and it is a possible/probable achievement in substantially less than a normal lifetime.  

 

I wouldn’t change a thing.  Call me radical.  J  73, Ted, K6XN

Bob,

I disagree. I was licensed in 1957 and my ticket expired in 1975. Work and family
kept me from getting back into ham radio until 1998. I did not start DXing until
about 2000 when I put up a beam antenna. Now in December 2012 I just made
Honor Roll with cw and SSB QSOs with ZL9HR and phone HR with several SSB Q's
with PT0S. When the QSLs arrive next year I will have achieved honor roll in about
12.5 years. Other have done it far faster. I certainly missed some very rare entities during
those years but I kept track of DXpeditions and tried to work most of them.

My point is Honor Roll is possible to achieve. It may be a little tougher as the cost
to get to certain islands becomes astronomical. Certainly there are also political
issues that also prevent certain countries like P5, XZ, E3 and others from being
active for years. Eventually a ham gets to Heard and Peter One and the political
situations dissipate. That to me is what makes the chase fun. The last few are
truly hard to get. That is how it should be.

Best 73,

john k6yp

 It's great that there is so much passion and dialog about the NCDXC and our direction.
 
As a current, and hopefully future Board member (elections are next month) I will take an action item to work with the Board and I would like to coordinate some possible future meetings in the East Bay and North Bay, possibly in partnership with the NCCC  and/or REDXA. This could be beneficial to every member.   Due to traffic, work hours etc, these special meetings may need to be scheduled on a Saturday to allow for travel.
 
Rick, I cannot agree with you about your perceptions of the NCDXC and your concern about "imminent failure". The NCDXC is doing very well. Our meetings are well attended and there is great interest in what we are doing, even by non-members such as yourself. To make judgements about us as an organization based solely on what you read on NCDXC chat might be myopic.
 
I invite you to join us and to attend what meetings you can, when and where you can. What the club needs going forward are candidates for President and VP. These are vital roles and we need interested candidates. This is the best way to champion change and to work in a collaborative fashion with the NCDXC board to make it happen. The Club's election committee is seeking those interested in officer roles for our June elections.
 
Clearly there is a desire for some changes. Some of them are quite reasonable and attainable and others pose significant changes and a disruption of the status quo.
 
Keep the good ideas flowing!

I’ll offer one more opinion as a non-member, then I’ll shut up.
 
According to Paul, the current membership is primarily South Bay and Peninsula.  Further, the repeater (without any linking ability like IRLP) saturates only that area and the members on this list (that have been vocal) fail to take into consideration those of us that don’t live in/near the Bay Area.  It’s clearly inconvenient for them to travel, even to the East Bay, JUST as it is for everyone that doesn’t live in the South Bay (resounding DUH heard).  What a great way to limit your membership, by being stodgy, exclusionary and rigid; just as other ham clubs tend to be and most of them died off. 
 
Which means the club should be renamed the South/West Bay Area DX Club, which is merely being truthful as it doesn’t fully represent Northern California.  Honestly, why would anyone in Eureka (or Redding or Fresno) be interested in a South Bay (only) meeting?   What does the club offer them that they can’t already get from ARRL membership?  [I am a member, but not a fan of the ARRL.]
 
Frankly, I’m appalled (but not surprised, seen it before too many times) by some of the attitudes shown here.  What’s good for the goose should be good for the gander.  You won’t be adding many members from any other areas or providing any Elmering or service with the attitudes expressed here.  It’s also a sign of impending failure which must be heeded.  What is the club really about?
 
I get it; some have jobs, some are retired, some are older, some are tired.  We all (should) have lives beyond DX which means we’re drawn in many different directions.  DX is the one thing that brings this list/group/club together.
 
I submit that if a rotating location quarterly meeting was for a WEEKEND they could also tied around something social other than the meeting, like a Christmas party, BBQ, RV campout or other social event and you’d find that it is more relaxed, there would be more time for real discussions and non-hams (family) could be drawn in creating potential new hams and members.  Heck you could have dozens of mini-seminars over the course of a weekend since it’s only quarterly and it could be located at an RV friendly hotel, KOA (Kabin/RV/tent) or similar.  But the membership (as a group) must figure out what the true goals are for the club.  They’re apparently not clearly understood (a second growth potential or imminent failure warning sign).
 
I don’t mind travel; I’ve driven some 20k miles just in the RV over the last 16 months (includes /6, /7, /VE7, /VY, /KL).  I just don’t like to travel at night (harder to see and there are more drunks on the road and other hazards) so, for me, most evening meetings are out.  A single day, long distance meeting would have to end at a reasonable (to me) hour to interest me (Saturday lunch?).
 
I appreciate and enjoy reading the DX spotting here (which is only one reason that I was considering membership).  Since I live in the Sierra foothills, I’ll pass, for now, on joining so that way, the only one inconvenienced, is me.
 
Sour grapes?  Nah, there is a small DX/contest club up here with rotating Sat. afternoon meeting locations that has good information potential and they actually WANT new members.  Heck, even Paul PSE has been here for his presentations, so has Jim YC.  It may be a month or two later than it is heard in South Bay, but good information is still good to get.
 
I’ll continue to watch this list with interest; the ball is in the club court.  A call for specific improvements has been made; what is the response?
 
73,
Rick WA6NHC

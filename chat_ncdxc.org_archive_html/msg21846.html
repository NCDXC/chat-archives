The KEY POINT that I learned for 160M in particular should be obvious, but is often ignored, so I'll state the obvious.

You're dealing with very tiny signals (most of the time) for DX on low bands.  Reducing your noise floor is mandatory.  You can be an alligator (all mouth, no ears) which only serves to frustrate everyone... or you can do the best you can in the environment you're in by cleaning your own house first.  Having cooperative neighbors is a great option too, hi.

An RX array to provide noise reduction (which provides a small amount of gain in the wanted direction) is pretty much a requirement for 160M, as is running legal limit much of the time.  Although when I lived in CA (noise floor ~-80 dBm!) I was able to work several entities (yes, the easy ones), barely.

DXCC on 160M was my winter project a couple winters ago; it's achievable with some concentrated effort, but it's the move away from most noise sources that make it doable in one winter (now -110 to -120 dBm).

It's the same old adage; "Ya can't work 'em if ya can't hear 'em".  I still have to put up an RX array and am around 120 confirmed on 160M (haven't looked lately).

Great job Chris, I know how much effort you had to put in to make this goal.  Well done!

It is with great pleasure that I can comfortably say that after working DG1CMZ,HA0NAR and OH0Z in the clear,  on 160 meters that I have crossed the reliable LOTW threshold to complete my 9th band DXCC, all traditional modes CW, SSB and RTTY.   I hope I am not one of the last to do it this way..  it was quite a journey.

 

I will now fire up and rinse and repeat to do it again on FT8. And work toward 2000 challenges slots.. I have been slacking a bit on that.

 

Thanks to the NCDXC and all my friends in the club for your support.. Time from DXCC # 1 to #100 on band 9 = 12 years.  5bdxcc in 2015.   With FT8 it will go lots quicker.. but I am quite proud of this accomplishment from North W6.

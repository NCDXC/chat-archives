Good job I actually read the distribution here - since you cc'd me directly, suspect you want my 2 pennies worth on your questions.

Q#1:  I suspect the gray line path will be better than an all darkness path but the propagation models don't handle gray line well (or in most cases, at all).  On one side of the propagation you have collapsing D-layer adsorption offset with collapsing F layer ionization which results in lowering critical frequency - the theory is this leads to ducting between the E and D layers along the terminator...

Q#2: If you have a the option of directivity to select between SP and LP (and the folks at the other end do as well), on the margin, I would think the SP, regardless of the actual propagation mode encountered at gray line time would have a lower loss - every hop regardless of the mode will on-average, incur a loss... few hops, less loss....

There is an interesting presentation at http://www.norfolkamateurradio.org/Talks/G0KYA%20Greyline_Propagation.pdf - courtesy of the RSGB Studies group that would support this premise.

I have used multiple propagation prediction models as well as analyzing the logs from different DXpeditions (kindly provided) and it reinforces:
- Models are useless for grayline analysis
- Grayline effects are very visible in the post analysis but no way to split SP/LP
- Clustering of Qs by hour suggests that grayline is better than total darkness as path length increases

Hope this helps
73 and best of luck with the DX!

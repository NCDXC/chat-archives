I Have an AV680. They are all similar. It’s on a 20ft pole so the radials are those that came with the antenna. You don’t need much when it’s up in the air that high. Yes, I have 205DXCC, mostly in FT8. About 109 on CW and 62 on SSB.

These multi-band verticals are true half wave up to 15m. After that they are shortened by coils and capacitor hats. Don’t expect full bandwidth after 15m but the tuner will take care of that. Except efficiency start going down quickly.

On 80m they are a joke. Mine is 10kHz wide so you QSY and you need to tune again, basically.

Also they are marketed as full legal limit. The small print says: Key down 1 minute. Mine will take 500W in 40m on FT8 (worse case) for about 20 minutes continuously before the transformer at the feedline heats up and your SWR runs away.

On 80m that’s more like a minute or two. Enough sometimes to get you a QSO with EU but if there are repeats you’ll be sweating.

20m and higher no problem. You can be TX 500W all day. Never tried more than that because that’s all I have.

Yes, they are noisier for sure.

The good news are:

  1. It’s multi-band
  2. Low take off angle (good DX)
  3. No need to maintain rotators
  4. That is the only thing I can have here so I’m enjoying it… 😊

HI Truman,

 

I share most of Paul's experiences with the R7 (in my case a R8). I certainly worked my

first 150 countries with that antenna. Like Paul I found that a few elevated radials really

helped but it was still possible to work stuff with the built in counterpoise provided with

the antenna. My only caveat is no more than 500 watts with R7-R9 antennas from MFJ

or Cushcraft. The traps will arc over an d fail almost immediately as I discovered with

my first homebrew 1 kW amp. Don't believe the 1.5 kW ratings in the R8, R9 ads.

Hi Truman, 

 

I started out with verticals in my backyard as a new DXer. The Cushcraft R7 was my first. It got me on the bands and I could work the South Pacific, Asia and South America but I could not work Europe with my vertical. 

 

Cushcraft claimed that the R7 did not need radials, but guess what?  When I read that adding 3-4 elevated radials would improve any vertical, I gave that a try and saw remarkable improvement. I've not used the Butternut but a lot of guys use them.  I like the Hustler 4BTV and other verticals and we use those on DXpeditions with 3-4 elevated radials. 3-4 elevated radials is much better than many more directly on the ground. 

 

If you install your vertical in the corner of your yard, you can run elevated radials along the top of your fences and no one is the wiser. 

 

Verticals can be fun but just keep in mind that they are very much a minimal antenna or a compromised antenna. Verticals also have a higher noise level than a horizontally polarized antenna. 

 

Good luck with your project, 

 

Paul N6PSE

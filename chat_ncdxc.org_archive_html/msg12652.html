I've got a first order DX problem:  I want a DX antenna but I live in a condo without a yard.  I've been using a temporary end-fed 20-meter dipole strung up in a nearby tree with some limited success but that marginal performance coupled with the hassle of stringing it up only to take it down in a few hours has me wondering if there isn't a viable, efficient, HF RX/TX, permanent, small space antenna solution.  

My understanding (admittedly basic) of how antennas work has me thinking that such an antenna doesn't exist.  Then a friend suggested that I look at a loop antenna.  He didn't claim to understand or to have any experience with loops but said that he had heard good reports on the performance of the Chameleon CHA F-LOOP Plus.  I checked out the antenna in the eHam.net reviews and found that this 36" diameter aluminum loop had almost thirty perfect 5.0 user reviews!  Impressive.  Is this antenna for real?

Does anyone know if the Chameleon F-LOOP Plus is actually a viable, HF TX/RX antenna?  Can the loop actually work and work well in both RX and TX on HF (at least 20-meters)?  Your comments and thoughts are appreciated.

73, Jack Quense K6ZQ

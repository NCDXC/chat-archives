Food for thought:    Why is there such a push to delete Kingman Reef today?  During the last operation there, they operated from a debris spit of broken coral and shells.  That sounds like it was debris washed up on top of the reef.   Last years storm could have washed that away.  Next years storm may deposit it back, leaving more above the high tide line than before. Such is the nature of sand bars and small islands.  Wouldn't it be better to have a wait and see attitude? We could end up in a situation where it is deleted and then have to add it again.   Now if there was an underwater landslide and the island completely collapsed that would be a different story.

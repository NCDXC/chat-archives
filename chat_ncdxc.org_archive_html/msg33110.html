In particular, re. "Nothing new here":

K1VR Response: To the extent that elements of Proposed ByLaw 46 existed previously, and are now criticized,  it means that there have been bad parts to ARRL governance in the past that have been carried forward. Bad ideas carried forward remain bad ideas.

Also, in response to the director's request to "Read the entire set of documents," you cannot do that, because the motion refers to other documents for certain details, such as the "Directors Workbook," to which we have no access because it is "confidential."

We need six Director votes to defeat this.  At this point, there seem to be only four.

The next ARRL Board Meeting starts just one week from today, January 18th, 2024.

So what can we do besides writing to the Pacific Division director, whose mind is apparently made up?  If any of you live in the Northwestern Division (Washington, Oregon, Idaho, Montana), or have ham friends who live there, please have them write or call director Mike Ritz, W7VO w7vo@arrl.org (contact information here:  https://www.arrl.org/divisions).  You can do the same for other divisions of course.

Mike once or twice voted on "the right side" of issues according to the Minutes of the July. 2023 meeting, such as the change to the Roll Call Vote Rule.  He might be persuaded to withdraw his support of the Motion 46 proposals if enough of his constituents contact him before the BoD Meeting.

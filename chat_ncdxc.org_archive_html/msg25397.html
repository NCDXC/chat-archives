I let my SteppIr get pretty bad before painting.  The bottoms that face down stayed green but the tops turned white and the fiberglass fibers were showing and some areas were downright hairy.  I realized I had better get it protected before it was too late.
I gently sanded and wiped with simple mineral spirits and let it dry well.  I then put on two coats of this: Interlux YHA183Q Perfection 2-Part Polyurethane Platinum-Quart.  Many colors available and I used a grey/white.
It was expensive, about $64 plus surface shipping.  They recommended a cleaner followed by a primer before the paint.  I did neither and it worked well.  The antenna was up for 6 years and still looked good, doing its job.  It may last much longer but I parted with the antenna so cannot monitor.
This paint needs to be used with extreme caution: skull and crossbones on the labels, etc.  They refer to brain damage and death if you are not careful.

I would use it again in a heartbeat but I would buy a new respirator for the job.

I tried Krylon clear as well and it broke down within a few year. If you really want to protect long term you need to use marine paint designed for fiberlass. This is the general consensus on the SteppIR board.  I used Interlux Brightside Polyurethane. Great stuff, but not cheap. A quart and a half easily covered the 28 SteppIR tubes with 2 coats of paint. Sea Foam green matches the SteppIR green pretty close if you care.

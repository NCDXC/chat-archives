Bob, I worked FT4TA on 80 meters, it was ESP copy for the most part. It was by far my most difficult contact with them.
With deep QSB fading, their signals were up and down. I would call them when I could hear them and wait when I could not hear them.
A few W6 guys told me that their signals were coming in from a skewed path over Japan and once I turned my elevated rotatable dipole (steppIR DB36) in that direction, their signal improved and I worked them with less difficultly than when my dipole was broadside to them at 189 degrees. 

Paul N6PSE

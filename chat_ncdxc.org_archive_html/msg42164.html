On Wed, Mar 4, 2026 at 11:20 PM Rick nk7i via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

The 3y0k ops have FAR more patience than me on my best day.  That shows real commitment and dedication (or a mental illness, a close call to make 😉).  I like winter, but ... it's too much there.

Today the phone op took about 90 seconds to get ONE call right (and of course, lowered the rate AND still had too many ID machine callers chiming in).  But he got it at last, which I'm positive that the other station is MOST grateful for the pause; possibly an ATNO.

It is abundantly clear that more folks need to read, understand and USE the DX code of conduct.  Imagine how easy it would be if more followed the code.  (i.e. a hundred stations calling on FT8, an HOUR after they left is but one example of far too many <fill in the blank> 'operators').

And with the first upload stats, it appears that they're trying to be balanced about whom/where they work (but OC had some requests); a nice change from other groups.  While on FT8, you can SEE the rotation of zones cycle through.  I'd bet money that the prop there is 'unique' as well making that an interesting challenge.

I had doubts they'd even land but so far, Ken has been redeemed from the last effort.  Even the solar storm was brief which helped, but the weather there is the real boss and I hope it holds off surprises until they are safely WELL away.

They're doing well but we can't be fully impressed until they spend their time there, continue to do well AND safely vacate.

I had 4 slots from last time, they've added another 8 new slots (and 3 reruns) and only the polar distortion and prop overall here has been the limiting factor.  (The PNW does not have amazing prop.)

Well done.

On 3/4/2026 10:45 PM, eric norris via Chat wrote:
Last night I was beginning to despair about ever working 3Y0K.  I couldn't hear them on any band in  CW, and rarely even printed them on FT8.

Last night I tried again, and around their local sunrise, they were so loud on 40m CW,  I thought it must be a pirate.

I worked them 30 minutes after their sunrise, on a noisy vertical close to HV lines and leaky Comcast cable.  They got my call wrong, I sent it twice more.  They sent the report, I sent my 599.   They sent NF6S 599?  OH, SHIT.  I sent NF6S 599 599.  I didn't hear a TU, but other stations were calling.  I held my breath.

The next day--IN THE LOG.

Do not give up, my fellow little pistols.

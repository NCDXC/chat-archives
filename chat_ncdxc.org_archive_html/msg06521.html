Ted, I think you are one of the finest, most ethical guys in the hobby, and I certainly wasn’t implying any wrong-doing on your part.
 
I wasn’t pointing any specific fingers, other than I feel that some in the Contesting community abuse the privilege of having a club call. Clearly, some do and some do not. Clubs like K6MMM and K6LRG appear to do it properly.  I frown when I read on a certain contest club reflector how some club calls are loaned or shared for various contests. It seems that some guys just want a club call in their own back pocket for their occasional contesting. This is something that the hams should police and clean up themselves.
 
Part of the problem is that the FCC allows the much coveted 1X2 and 2X1 “Extra Class” calls to be used by Clubs. This makes it very difficult for a Ham upgrading from General to Extra to obtain a desirable call sign for him/herself. This phenomenon would be diminished if club calls could only be a 2X2 or 1X3 call sign, they would be much less desirable by the Contest community.
 
I would like to see other hams call for a “give back” of less than legit club call signs and more concern and policing of hams by hams.
 
Respectively,
 
Paul N6PSE

Paul and Risto

 

I love working DX. I also love being DX for the other operator.

 

You may wish to consider that many operators just use what is already recorded on club station equipment voice keyers . I do this myself as do others in the clubs. It should not be surprising that club station equipment will  often have the same “voice” for calling and for confirming QSOs with  pre-recorded club station voice keyers.

 

Notwithstanding the above I do not feel particularly restrained at any time from  working any DX using one or more of our club calls and our club stations if the band is open and if the club needs the QSL for a “new one”.

 

Please don’t hesitate to let me know if you hear any jamming, profanity or improper operation of any of the Club Stations I am the trustee for and I will take immediate appropriate corrective action.

 

Paul, I look forward to reading about what “this phenomenon” is  and your personal views and concerns on your blog. I will also forward your blog URL to all of our club members as they may also have an interest in your views and opinions. You may wish to take a look at our club posting for NC6DX on QRZ.COM and view one of our club stations and pictures of the cabin, some of the members of the club, operating positions, homemade amplifiers etc. There are similar postings for the other club stations.

 

Risto, I am perfectly happy with the good friends I already have. If I am precluded from working new DX stations that are needed by a club station I am a trustee for (or needed by any other club station if I am operating as  a guest operator) as a *precondition* to friendship then I will probably decide to just work it, celebrate the good friends I already have, and continue working  DX.

 

All of the club stations I am trustee for have participated for over 15 years in DXing, Field Day operations, Museum Ships Weekend, the CQP, emergency operations, various contests, and emergency operations using solar power, battery power and generator power operating from a wilderness area and from other locations. We do this a lot. We not only work DX but we also train new ham radio operators and give them an opportunity to work DX using great antennas from a great location. We  have our own 2M repeater system that we maintain and operate for the benefit of the public and ourselves. Each of our club stations have earned DXCC and we encourage our club members to learn DXing skills and to work and love  DXing ….. and yes…achieve DXCC.

 

I love this hobby. See you in the DX pileups.

 

Thanks and 73, Ted, K6XN

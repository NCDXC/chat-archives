From all of the opinions shared, it is clear that each DXer has his own passions, motivations and hot buttons.  I'd not thought in these terms before now.
 
Not having been on the other side of a DXpedition, I can only imagine the pride one could feel resulting from receiving an award.  But would anyone organize or maybe even participate on a team for that alone?  Doubtful.  For me, the thrill of giving out the Qs, having the world want to put me in their log, and want it really bad, would be a pretty continuous rush!  Getting an award later would be nice, but a rush?  As I said, I can only imagine what I have never experienced.
 
If the club gave an award every few years to the individual or perhaps few individuals that contributed the most to the DX community by conducting one or more operations, I would have an easier time voting a winner. Perhaps that would be a more meaningful award based on its being less frequent and dependent on some form of longevity
 
Paul, you would be on a very short list of those that have done the most to enhance the DXCC position of a very great number of hams.  To me, that is an award to which one would be really, really proud.  The award for best operation of the year might fade.  The QSLs The Deserving put on their shack walls do not fade and will endure in the hearts of those that benefited from your time, efforts and generosity and from those efforts of other individuals on that short list.
 
Anyway, if there is going to be best of the year award, ground rules are needed including enough votes to make the stats meaningful.
 
73,
Al

Al, thank you for your nice message. You are one of the few that responded to the question and shared with us what your vote was and why. I find these kinds of responses very interesting.
 
Based on the responses received to this weeks QoTW, there appear to be several camps:
 
1) The right selections were made so just shut up about it!
 
2) Given all the considerations discussed, I might vote differently today!
 
3) We should just cancel this award process so we don't have to go through this again.
 
 
I definitely think that we (NCDXC) can improve on our process and possibly as a result we can have more informed voters and more meaningful results.
 
A goal should be to have more than 1/3 of the club actually voting. That will increase the validity of the award right there. I think that cancelling the award for what ever reasons is entirely the wrong approach. One of the benefits of this award is that it entices the leaders and often the entire DXpedition team to come to IDXC or Hamvention. Sure they come for other reasons but there is hope to win an award and that is motivation to attend, make presentations and mingle with the many DXers. I think some of that would diminish if the awards were not offered any longer.
 
I'm just sayin,
 
73,
 
Paul N6PSE

This was my first opportunity to vote for this award and did not have a frame of reference for how to vote.  Reading all of the posts sure fixed that, giving me a lot to consider going forward.
I worked all of the main DXpeditions over the year but did not need any for a ATNO which probably would have affected my vote should one have been the ATNO.
 
When I started DXing in the early 70's as a teen, working someone on a rock in the ocean was pretty darned cool to me.  St. Peter and Paul's was my first such, followed by Kingman, Spratly (when it was on rocks), Malpelo, etc.  I guess I had a nostalgic inclination to such operations and voted for the PY0, the first such 'on the rocks' operation I worked.  My vote was partly based on my perception of the multiple hassles endured by the ops.  Yes, Malpelo is a rock but seemed 'different'.  Like I said, not a lot of thought when into it.  I might have voted differently if there were guidelines or a way to rank defined parameters.
 
Working each was a thrill to be sure and I truly appreciate those that went out and made it happen.  The effort from 7O impressed me most in one respect in that it was the signals on the different bands that I could hear and even work.  I never expected to put more than one Q in the log.  Awesome!
Al

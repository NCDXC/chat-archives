Hi Paul,

I also have the K7NV clamp.  I used to have a lot of issues with 'windmilling' but after switching over to the K7NV clamp I haven't had any slipping issues.  The clamp bolts have to be pretty tight, to the point where you could see the steel clamp deform.  I have spoken to Kurt and he told me to tighten the clamp so that there's 1/8" gap between the clamps.

I have no experience with the Slipp Nott clamp.

You should never pin the mast.  Unless the you use a tapered bolt that will match the diameter of the hole, a regular straight bolt will always be smaller than the hole, which over time will ovalize the hole and defeat the purpose of pinning the mast.

In the worst case and as a last resort, you could spot braze the K7NV clamp to your mast.  The spot braze will prevent any movement at all and it can be grinded off in the future to uncouple the clamp from the mast.  But doing so will put more stress on the rotator.  The slipping mast is currently acting as a 'fuse' and therefore your rotator is being protected from damage.

If you have a Green Heron controller, it can be used to realign the mast to true north.  I do this as long as the slippage is less than ten degrees.  This is done by lining up the mast to true north and use the 'calibrate' function to reset the controller to 0 degrees.

On Apr 13, 2017, at 6:00 PM, Paul N6PSE via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

I have a question for the NCDXC "brain trust"

I have a Steppir DB36 with an M2 Orion 2800 rotator on a 72' tower. 

This big Yagi catches a lot of wind. 

I have had a lot of issues with mast slippage in the rotator's mast clamps. 
The bolts are as tight as they can possibly be. The mast clamps are the Kurt-K7NV specially modified clamps to provide for more mast grip. 

I am considering buying a "Slipp Nott" from Teledyne: http://www.tennadyne.com/slipp_nott.htm

Has anyone else had this problem and found a good solution for mast slippage?

I want to avoid pining the clamps through the mast as that voids the rotator warranty.

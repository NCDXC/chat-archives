As I noted earlier, it's a little crazy to think about a Single QSO DXpedition Theme.....but how better to send a strong message about your top priorities for a DXpedition which is not likely to be repeated for a very long time?

Heard Island requires the exact opposite of traditional thinking:  The key is to set LOW Band-Mode expectations long before the DXpedition begins and then manage the pileups.  That translates to very strong operators -- skilled in operating, but also skilled in carrying out the mission.

Optimizing unique calls:
1.  Single QSOs for the first week or so on one or more dedicated bands (e.g., 20, 17, or 15)
2.  Daily uploads to ClubLog
3.  ClubLog Leaderboard stats turned off

If managed properly, the dollars will flow -- although most likely primarily from North America -- from many grateful operators, who not only will have ATNO in the log, but will respect the DXpedition's focus on the masses, not just the superstations.

On May 30, 2013, at 10:30 AM, Tom Berson wrote:

Great comment, Chris.
 
The ability to detect dupes rests on the assumption that all DXp stations are networked, which may not be the case in a difficult geography (e.g. BS7H). Noticing and responding to a dupe breaks the run operator’s flow and reduces rate. The reaction of the calling op who is turned away as a dupe may range from just going away, to arguing (which is why most contesters will simply work a dupe), to DQRM from the jilted, his friends, his compatriots, and general low-lifes.
 
IF I wanted ATNOs only I would advertise ahead of time what I was doing and then spend a long time on a single band-mode. This might let me get QRV faster, avoid operator exhaustion early in the DXp, and do a better job of installing antennas for other bands.  I would expect to be unpopular with band-mode chasers until l opened the floodgates after ATNO rates fell. The white-knuckle aspect would be that some DXps are cut short for political, WX, or other reasons.
 
Such a non-traditional strategy would have economic impacts on the DXp. I have not yet thought them through.

Paul N6PSE, who is on travel, has kindly consented to allow me to pose the Question of the Week. Since I am engaged in finding the right structure for the 2015 Heard Island DXpedition, I would like to ask the following:
 
Heard Island is shaping up to be a very difficult challenge, far more difficult than when we did it as VK0IR in 1997. The main problem is the cost of the vessel. After a year of trying to negotiate a favorable arrangement with the preferred vessel, I reluctantly concluded that the projected cost ($1.5M) was simply too much. So the challenge is how to restructure the operation to enable it to happen at all. One way is to use a much smaller team and therefore a smaller vessel. A small team (4-6 ops) might imply that we would have to change from a goal of logging the most QSOs to logging the most All-Time-New-Ones (ATNOs) or the most Uniques. Most likely this would mean a return to an average of 2-3 QSOs per DXer, which was the norm a decade ago, instead of the 4-5 QSOs/DXer, which has been experienced by recent DXpeditions (e.g., TX5K), stimulated in part by the ARRL Challenge.
 
So here is the question (in several parts): Considering the limitations of the operation, would you favor an initial period of ATNOs-only, say a week or two weeks? If you already have VK0/H confirmed would you willingly stand by and allow others to get their ATNOs? Would you still financially support the DXpedition, even though you may not be able to log 10+ band-modes?
 
The questions above are therefore somewhat equivalent to the following:
 
What should be the goal of the next Heard Island DXpedition?
 
Thanks, Paul, for this opportunity, and I’ll certainly be very interested in the opinions and suggestions of the members. This is the chance to help design the HI DXpedition, so all suggestions will be welcome.

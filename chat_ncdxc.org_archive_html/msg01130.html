Hi Jim, I got a chuckle out of your post!
 
Truth be told, there are guys, some DXers and some Contesters that are doing just that. There are guys that use remotes to gain advantage and work areas that their home station cannot provide. There are also guys who cheat and use remotes in EU to give themselves an unfair advantage in contests.
 
My understanding of DXCC rules is that you can use a remote in your own Country as part of your DXCC count. In other-words, I could use a East Coast remote station to work that rare EU station (like SV2ASP/A) where it is near impossible to work him from a modest station in California. (although I have done it)
 
My interest in remoting is to have a high level remote within 45-60 minutes drive of my home. That way it is not a major trek if I have to re-boot or change something that has failed. I am looking for a repeater type of site that has room for a small two element SteppIR yagi on a mast with rotor. Ground mounting is fine. Until I find a suitable site, I will test and host the remote from my home and let others enjoy it while I find a better place to put it for DXing. I don't think that will be in NH or Florida but the beauty of the remotehams.com network is that you can join and share access to remotes in those areas!  How C O O L is that!
 
Anyway, some old timers are really against this remote stuff, but then again, they could buy a house for under $20K and did not have to deal with CC&Rs!   They also had sunspots to enjoy and we don't seem to be doing well in that department these days.
 
Any others have opinions or ideas on remoting?  Fire away,
 
73,
 
Paul N6PSE

I have been following the discussion about a club remote station with interest.
Kidding aside and for the record I am green with envy of any fellow DX-er with the ability to operate his station remote or have a remote site----we who sit and shout salute you from our height restricted, socially correct, zoned to death city lots-go for it!.

The one-man solution to the ATNO aside, then, let's move on to the core reason why a club remote is needed---the white noise curtain of W1 and W2. I, for one, am dismayed by their endless chatter about DXCC on 160 after a few contest weekends and #1 Honor Roll in less than a decade--good grief it's a chip shot to every postage stamp factory in Africa, Theocratic regime on the Continent and 100 watt New Country. I propose that the NCDXC install the first remote site in the woods of New Hampshire. Further, to add to the whipping we are about to impose, I propose we run a maximum of 200 watts. A moments thought will disclose the W1 and W2 crowd no longer have our pile-up busting, static filtering skills after 6 decades of coasting (sorry about that) across that litle pond so our 200 watts and sharper skills will add to their ignominy.

Which brings me to the reason for site number two, the Everglades. Where do all the W1 and W2 ops retire? Why, W4 and W5 of course. This bunch of fellows-in-arms view the South Atlantic as an extension of their back yard pool, (to say nothing of 80 meters). How many times have 6's and 8's and 0's had to listen to something like this "...hi again Bob, its Harry (no call)---your expedition to South Georgia is a clear 5 and 8 today whereas yesterday it was only 5 and 7.2 on my HP whoozit meter. Today I'm on the 200 foot tower--can you tell? In fact, let me play you back a recording of each of our 20 meter contacts since you got there...etc. " REALLY? We have to accept this? So, for site two, given their now extreme age/limited physical ability I propose the Everglades where if the terrain doesn't get 'em the gators will. Why so cautious? Because this station will run not fractions of legal but multiples---say a pair of 3CX3000s. THAT'll show 'em! It should
clear 80 up for DX, too.

And so we come to site number three--here on the coast. A case can be made for Oregon with its great paths both long and short....but these fellows never really impede us and more than a few have by any standard been allies. Perhaps down south, in that other country of 6's where the propogation or power levels are higher---probably both? No, site three we shall ask the club to resolve so we can get on to the easy part --who gets to use Sites One and Two it and in what order?

Well, first, the members for whom the DX is an ATNO. Surely MM can assemble a Want List for each of us and a quick email (2 minutes to respond or we move on!) will start the process. We can all see our slot and be ready. But, you say, so many of us need Athos, for example--now what? Easy---the newest hams first, for if we old dudes haven't worked it after all these years with a license, well, nature is cruel---and efficient. We aren't proposing you can't work it---just that you go later into the process because by and large the older guys have the skills, stations and timing such that when you go shouldn't matter. And, what better way to get those kids off 440 and into the real world of ham radio? How else to keep DXing alive? When we all have it all we can tear down the remotes. By that time the land we built on will be ready for condo development and the next generation can deal with how to work the expedition to Mars. With just a bit of tongue in
cheek, de JIM K8JRK

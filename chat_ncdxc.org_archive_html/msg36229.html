Dave,
If the old Orion lasted this long, the new revision is likely to last as least as long.  They upgraded the top bearing designs for sealing against moisture to improve longevity.
I have a 2-year old new rev OR2800 for my DB36.  It 'ground to a halt'  a few months ago but the reasons are not known until I get it down which is a chore that I have procrastinated.  As I have been procrastinating, I have looked at the DXE.  In conversation with M2, they seemed bitter that DXE copied their design.  Maybe so, but they have improved one important aspect: made it easy to change the reed switch without removing the rotor from the tower.  As I have looked at the specs, the M2 is rated higher/similar but it it like antenna gain?  One glaring difference is in the weight.  The Orion is much heavier and I do not yet know why but one needs to take out the clamps if M2 includes them in their weight to get a fair comparison.  Heavier does not necessarily mean better, either.
A year ago when I talked to M2 about my initial indication of something going on, I was told they had not properly specified the reed switch to their vendor.  The vendor was surprised when they learned it was going to be switching 'like crazy' and not just once in a while.  The vendor did not want to guarantee its longevity in the Orion's application.  So, M2 found a different switch for their new production and offered it to me for free if I found that the mis specified switch had failed.  How generous.  All I have to do it take it down at great expense and effort.
I may buy  the DXE just on reputation so I have it to use while the M2 gets evaluated.  M2 was pretty vague about numbers in service and warranty claims or repairs done.  It may be due to an installed base that is in its infancy and means nothing.  So, their general rep is all I have.  As you know, the OR2800 has been a go to rotor for years and as good as it gets short of a prop pitch solution which I did not need nor wanted as it is not well supported.  DXE protuction may be spotty as the last time I asked a month ago, they were just out of stock and had no idea when more would come in.
My 2 cents.
73,
Al  N6TA

IHS is currently looking for more volunteers for their February 2019 (February 15 to March 1) mission.

Since 1982 the International Health Service of Minnesota, or IHS, has organized one or two brigadas medicas each year to Honduras. IHS brings together teams of doctors, nurses, pharmacists, and support staff who volunteer to deliver desperately needed health and dental care to remote areas throughout Honduras. For many of the people in these areas, these visits are the only health and dental care the patients ever get. For the 2018 Feb trip, a medical, dental, vision or surgical team went to 9 different locations in Honduras. Each team included the medical staff: the doctors, dentists, nurses, pharmacists, and the support staff: an interpreter, a radio operator, a team leader, and a general helper or two.

All teams use HF with pactor for message traffic. A few teams are able to use VHF for voice. Last year the conditions were so bad that most of days 40M was unusable for local voice as there is alot of noise due to generators and solar inverters. Do not worry if you do not have all the gear as we will find a way to get you supplied it you wish to. No pactor or digital experience, not a problem as all hams go through 10 task training program each year before leaving for Honduras. The tasks are well written and easy to follow and I am happy to help bring you up to speed.

For the 2018 trip I was assigned to Rus Rus as radio operator.  Over 10 days we able to serve 1000 people with medical needs and 350 with dental needs. The people are great to work with. It is just mind blogging think about the amount of work it takes to put 100 people into field in 9 different locations with all the gear they need. My team had everything we need it was just load up and go. This includes three surgical teams with all the people and gear they need, just need a theater to setup in. It was really amazing to be part of. I am sadden that I not able to go this year due to work commitments and hope to go next year.

As far as work, most message traffic is about inquiry sending someone for surgery and scheduling time. There is also messages requests for resupply, health and well fair messages for team members, travel arrangements for side trips. There is some down time while the rest of the team is working where you can get be the DX (you will see me in the video below running FT8). Of course there is the... can you go get such and such or get so and so along with opportunities help out in the clinic ether part time or full time.

For more information
Detail team reports: http://www.ihsmn.org/newsandinfo/application/
A short video of the Rus Rus team: https://vimeo.com/259270506

On 11/11/2013 11:03 PM, Richard Baldwinson wrote:
It really is a marvelous high performance antenna and I have absolutely no regrets putting it up. I would do it again if I was considering an antenna today..
I strongly agree. Dick's antenna was fairly early production of a VERY new and revolutionary product concept. ANY new product with so much that is different from what has gone before will have growing pains in the early years of production.
Between this club and NCCC, a lot of us have helped each other install a lot of SteppIRs, so we have seen the progressive improvements in the mechanical design over the years. Mine has been up 4 years, I think, and I've recommended it to others. NO antenna is perfect, ALL antennas fall prey to the elements -- wind, rain, snow -- and all have some one or more weak spots that can cause failure. This antenna has held up quite well -- 9 years before a problem is pretty damn good for such a new product. 

73, Jim K9YC.

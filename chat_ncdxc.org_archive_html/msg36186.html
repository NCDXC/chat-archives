I congratulate the organizers of the IDXC for driving what I expect is a gargantuan effort to organize and produce a highly respected event.

I've only been to the last two IDXC and signed up for Contest Academy and DX Academy both times.  As a relatively new ham, I'm still exploring the hobby and although I have gravitated towards DX'ing and Contesting, (SSB, CW and most recently diig) I still need a bit more of the basics under my belt to take advantage of the more advanced topics.

Some of my questions and concerns are addressed in software and hardware User Manuals but they are not always written well.  I often have trouble with manuals because I am a hands-on, experiential learner. I'm going to assume there are others like me in the Ham community.  So, I'd like to see some sessions with this in mind.

If offered, I'd like to take advantage of the following topics with 30-45 minute breakout sessions

1) Understanding WSJT-X
    Breakout Sessions (perhaps following the User Manual supplied by the attendee) -
    Level 1 - Basic setup and optimizing of software and calling CQ
    Level 2 - How to chase that rare DX efficiently and not be a Lid
    Level 3 - Making sense of "He's even, He's odd", Fox-Hound, and other relevant jargon

3) Understanding Fldigi
    Breakout Sessions (perhaps following the User Manual supplied by the attendee) -
    Level 1 - Basic Setup and optimization
    Level 2 - Making contact using various modes like Olivia, etc.
    Level 3 - Etc.

4) RTTY Contesting
    Why is it dying? I realize it is old tech now but folks still like to contest with it, perhaps others like me might be well served with a good comprehensive explanation, tutorial and discussion.

5) In 2023 someone gave a good talk about radio wave propagation.  I understood some of it and now that I'm a little more experienced, I'm sure I could get more out of it.  I'd like to see an updated, similar talk,and  perhaps others would too.

6) LoTW is a ubiquitous database among Hams.  This point was driven home when it was shut down due to a malicious attack. In my opinion it also contains a convoluted method of applying for awards.  The instructions are hard to follow.  Perhaps a session illustrating a step-by-step approach to applying for a specific award might be useful.  This could be a breakout session of sorts too.

On a side note, I noticed that many of those NCDXC members attending the event dressed well and proffessionaly.  I bring this to the group's attention because I also notice those foreign attendees always dress to make a good impression too.  I think this is a small effort we make to continue to respect our foreign friends and attendees. Kudos to all.

These are only my opinions and I realize that I am still very new to the hobby so I don't know the history of the event very well nor do I completely understand the time limitations of an event like this. But I'm sure they are significant.  So, take these opinions to heart or with a grain of salt.  My skin is fairly thick.

73 de Rich WN6A

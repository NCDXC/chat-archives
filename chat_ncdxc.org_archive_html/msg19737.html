On Fri, May 1, 2020 at 5:07 PM Charles K. Epps <w6oat@sbcglobal.net> wrote:
Hi Kevin and Paul,

Are you guys aware that it was NCDXC who got P5 officially added to the DXCC list? 

Back in the day when Don Search, W3AZD, was commanding the DXCC Desk, the DXCC entity "Korea" appeared on the list.  We asked Don whether that meant just South Korea, because there were lots of active stations from there.  Would it also include North Korea as part of that entity, or would North Korea be a new one?  Don refused to answer our question.  He said we just wouldn't know until somebody actually went there!  That created a problem because at the time several folks were trying to figure out how to get a DXpedition on the air from North Korea, and if it were going to count the same as South Korea, why bother spending all the time and money it would take.  So that's when NCDXC decided to petition ARRL to actually add North Korea specifically as an entity.  In its petition, NCDXC submitted proof that North Korea had its own government, its own currency, its own postage stamps, its own military, and well defined borders.  We quoted the then existing DXCC rules which said all these things were attributes of what qualified as an entity based upon being an independent nation.  Don did not like NCDXC's petition, but grudgingly agreed to add North Korea as a new entity once somebody operated from there.  That first activation did not come until quite a few years later.

73,
Rusty, W6OAT

FWIW, I support Paul N6PSE 100% for the following reasons.
 
On the proxy issue, of course Paul, Garry, and others are right.  Their passion and intelligence burst through in virtually everything they post generally, and this issue has been no exception. 
On the bylaws issue, yes, I want to make sure that our internal procedures are followed,  But if the bylaws are lacking in logic, as Ted and Craig have deftly and carefully pointed out, we need to change them.
 
On the substantive leadership issue, I have no hesitancy in throwing my support behind Paul.  He's not afraid to shake things up.  There was a time not too long ago that the NCDXC was much more powerful than the NCCC.  Now the roles are reversed.  Paul is the kind of guy who can bring new status and prestige to the NCDXC.  He's one of the world's most celebrated DXers.  He's risked his life on numerous occasions to travel to very dangerous places just so you guys out there can claim a "new one".  Some of you don't seem to appreciate that for some reason. 
 
Just my 2 cents.

On 11/21/2013 5:49 AM, Craig-AE6RR wrote:
NCDXC Members,
 
I believe that this situation can be resolved in the following manner:
 
Under Article IV, Section 5 of the NCDXC Bylaws, vacancies of officer positions are filled in the following manner.
Sec. 5 Vacancies
5.3 Officer - A vacancy in any office shall be filled by the board of directors and ratified by the membership at the next regular meeting.
 
I propose amending the section to read:
 
Sec. 5 Vacancies
5.3 Officer - A vacancy in any office shall be filled by a nominee of the board of directors and ratified (or elected) by the membership at the next regular meeting.  The board must announce the fact that the office needs to be filled and who their nominee is at least 10 days before that meeting, if this is not possible then the election/ratification shall be held at the following regular meeting.  Nominations from the floor for the office will also be accepted at the meeting prior to a vote.  The same rules that govern the normal annual elections shall apply.  In the interim, one of the remaining officers as selected by the board shall serve as the acting officer to be replaced.
 
 
According to the Bylaws ARTICLE VII AMENDMENTS
 
Sec. 2 Bylaw amendments may also be proposed by the written petition of a least five (5) voting members in good standing and submitted to the board of directors. The directors shall either adopt the proposed amendment, or refer the matter to the membership at the next regular meeting, for a vote as in Article VII, Sec. 1.
I am submitting this as a petition for an amendment to the Bylaws of the Northern California DX Club.  If 4 other members in good standing also submit (sign) this petition, I propose that the amendment be voted on at the next meeting on November 21, 2013
 
If this amendment passes, then is should be possible to hold an election at the January, 2014 meeting (there is no regular meeting in December due to the Holliday Luncheon).
 
73,
- Craig, AE6RR

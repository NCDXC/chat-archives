That is another sticky wicket. DXCC rules state that all contacts for DXCC credit must be made by the same op to get credit. That is not rigorously enforced, nor can it be. Many DXers strongly believe that credit should be given to DXpedition ops for the counters they activate, and it has been said that the DXCC Desk looks the other way at Q's inserted in the log. I don't know. To me there is a big difference between "lending" one's callsign to a friend back home to work you--at least there is a QSO--and merely logging a bogus QSO.  As always, it is left to the moral judgement of the individual or the trip leaders, but some individuals do not necessarily deserve that much freedom.

RSGB solves the problem neatly in the IOTA program---if you activate the counter with a minimum number of contacts, you get credit for it. IOTA lists over 1100 island groups. The rationale is that not getting credit disincentivizes ops from going on trips. OTOH, many DXpeditioners and IOTA expeditioners don't give a hoot about personal credit. 

If I am wrong about any of this, I am sure I will be corrected.

Garry, NI6T

My first two 2-wire Bevs were 200' and 300' to stay on my property. Later I got permission from a neighbor, crossed the road and the 200' Bev became 400'. Then the neighbor sold and the new neighbor told me to take it down. Snip--back to 200'. Then she relented--wanted some advice for an SWL antenna--and I patched it back to 400'. Then she decided she did not want it--again--- and I doglegged it to her neighbor. Advice---don't ask flakey neighbors. I got a lot of exercise--the Bev runs down a long hill. But I worked plenty on 160 with all versions.

The 300' unit got shorter when another neighbor dumped dirt illegally and without consulting me, in the gully separating us, changing the course of a winter drainage stream, after the 1989 earthquake. The change in water direction undermined my side of the gully and I lost many cubic yards of dirt--and the end of the Beverage--after a large storm. So I had to move the entire antenna. It is still 300'.

Not your ideal situation, but it taught me that what Bob, KN6J--an early W6 160 DXCC achiever---told me, which was "Any Beverage is better than no Beverage."

Garry, NI6T

On 10/28/2013 1:30 PM, Rich Holoch wrote:
This morning I worked E51NOU - South Cooks for a new one on 160M. At first I couldn't hear him at all on my K9AY Loops, but when I switched to my transmitting antenna - which is a Cushcraft MA-160V mounted on top of a 30' Rohn pushup mast - (meaning its about 60' tall) and with 76 buried radials (mostly 50' long), I heard him loud and clear. Not sure why - the K9AY loops are almost always better . . . . 

I thought my K9AY Loops might have suffered a problem with the remote switch - but when testing it with some SSB ops on 1.875, I heard them and saw that the switch and directionality as well as the preamp were all still working fine. 

In the past I have built EWE's and a Flag. They are pretty much the same thing as the K9AY Loops - so I am not interested in Flag's or EWE's since the K9AY "footprint" is much better. 

However, I do have 200' along a creek that defines the hypotenuse of my small triangular lot. 

Anyone ever try a short beverage antenna? Seems like 200' isn't long enough, eh?

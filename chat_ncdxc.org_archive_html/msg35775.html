On 6/8/2024 9:36 AM, Rick Bates, nk7i via Chat wrote:
  • What defines a 'little pistol' station?
  • How does a little pistol compare to a QRP station?
  • Where is the threshold to "Big Gun" status?
  • How does 'Big Gun' compare to a 'Super Station'?

I don't have a definitive answer while 'super station' seems fairly clear (multiples of EVERYTHING on site being the start, mostly used for contesting).

While clearly the 100 watt max into a low random wire antenna would be accepted as 'little pistol' status; where is the line drawn?

Is QRP into an extensive high gain antenna, a 'little pistol'?  (Is ERP a defining point?)  [I'd suggest it isn't, power output is a choice.]

It's foggier than the CA delta can be at times (denser than a politicians mind); there IS no definition but it is a moral choice.  That's a problem, morality is always a slippery slope (oiled too!); it is not a constant.

[I define my station as 'simple', a few antennas, one atop a 55' tower and ability to have full legal power on all HF bands.  I do not define it as 'big gun' when it is the low noise floor that gives me most of the edge; it's certainly NOT the propagation this far north.  It has several single points of failure, but as I age (not the only thing moving too fast these days), simple absolutely ROCKS!]

Phone is the WORST mode for 'little pistols' to get through because it demands the most signal (and bandwidth) compared to other modes; which by definition is beyond a smaller station ability.  It is the most susceptible to interference.  But as the DX operator is not conversant in CW and FT8 is automatic; it's the only reasonable choice.

In this case, it would have been best to say "ATNO only", which most logging programs would show previous status immediately but require more (noob) operator attention to check, slowing the rate.  A slow rate means prop windows timing is more critical, some are 20 minutes wide (here!) and the (noob) operator would have to KNOW this (he won't).

Given recent behavior and the west coast short path is over them, I have ZERO expectation of EU hams being cooperative at all (having shut down phone operations ENTIRELY at least twice with Glorioso in my hearing).  (See morality comment above!)  Calling certain zones only (NA NA) has not been effective, because of impatient EU ops.  [The same happens when the DX is a chip shot from NA.]

There is no clear, simple answer and that is magnified by having only one op on site; amplified by his inexperience (but he's THERE and trying).

I understand the reasoning, agree with the need (been there!); but for most who seek Glorioso in their logbook (ATNO or slots), this choice will be difficult at best for ANYONE to get through (with cops, DQRM, world class whiners and idiots), let alone pop gun stations.

In other words, good for intent, near impossible to manage well, poorly requested.  Other TEAMS have tried with varied success; none well (not a solo noob op).

From ATNO, I have eked out 30-12 FT8 and one (VERY lucky) 15 phone with ft4gl (6 slots), so I'll not attend this time period (Elvira is a nice distraction too).  I would have added 20 phone, except the EU hordes, simply put, lacked ANY manners, caused enough disturbance and frustration to the op that phone was ended shortly (not the only time either).  

Not casting blame, it should have been more slots here, but this one time, I won't be greedy (and THAT may be shocking to some 😉).  I can 'live' with what I've gotten this time (near antipodal, timing of the year/cycle, the worst place location from here, an abundance of CME caused prop closures etc., I'm content).

Pilot/s:  PLEASE rephrase 'little gun' to ATNO ONLY, accept that the rate will be low (to check the logbook).  That will provide the best possible (totally hopeful) response for anyone (any station ability) to get through.  The only other alternative is filtering out calls already worked (within that time period) after the fact, tedium at best and prone to issues, complaints and other negative 'reactions'.

Overall, considering he isn't well versed in so many things, he's done pretty well (dropped into a blazing fire).  While his lack has frustrated many; who don't consider the DX side of things, amplified by limited time, required shut down periods, sleep needs, understanding or education of the op.

A solid 7 Olympic score (while the East Germans give a 3, lol) for the effort.  And I'm thankful for the ATNO.

73 es DX joyfully!
Rick nk7i

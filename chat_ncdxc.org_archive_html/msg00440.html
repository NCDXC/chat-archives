Congratulations Aaron! FWIW for many years like most people I missed a lot of DX as my professional work responsibilities precluded working DX during most weekdays and many weekends. It gets much better and easier IMHO if/when one is lucky enough to finally become a happily retired old fart as I have finally become with the luxury of being able to spend more time with ham radio!  J. A number of times over the last 52 years of my chasing DX I have had the good sense to take vacation days when I could to work some of the “rare” DXpeditions to a number of countries that had little to no ham radio activity.

 

For most of my ham radio experience I have had only mediocre antennas in noisy locations  for 80M (and none at all for 160M) and as a result I have always treasured any 160M and 80M DX contacts when I am able to make them. It is only in the last couple of years that I have been able to put up fairly decent 160M and 80M antennas up at our “summer” Sierra cabin and I still do not have a 160M antenna up yet here in Saratoga.

 

A week ago with the help of a couple of friends we put up a modified 32 foot Butternut HF-2V vertical antenna for 80M here at our Saratoga QTH. We have the base of the Butternut vertical antenna elevated 24 feet above ground on its own Rohn 25G tower and we also have its 64 foot +/- radials elevated 24 feet and running from the bottom of the Vertical at 24 feet and sloping down to about ten feet above ground.

 

So far I am delighted with the DX performance of the elevated Butternut 32 foot vertical on 80M. The noise level on 80M has dropped 5 dB as compared to my prior compromise 80M antennas here in Saratoga (a low loop antenna and then a ground mounted vertical). With the new vertical antenna I was able to easily work the PT0S on 80M and at the time I worked them my receive noise level was an S1 and the PT0S was an S7 on the FT-2000 S meter. Wow!  I have also worked seven new countries on 80M with the new antenna in just the last week. Most of the 80M DX I have been recently able to work would probably have been buried in the noise had I been using my prior “compromise” 80M antennas down here.

 

Bottom line, congratulations on working the PT0S on 80M with 100 KV transmission lines and in the context of devoting quality time to work and family! Treasure the accomplishment!

 

Jerry, if you haven’t elevated the base of your Butternut vertical and its radials you may want to try it if you can and see if you notice a marked improvement in performance as I have with mine. There is a downside risk though……you may find yourself up at 1:39 in the morning with a cup of gourmet coffee working 80M DX with your vertical antenna and really loving it! J

 

73 guys, Ted, K6XN  I love this hobby!

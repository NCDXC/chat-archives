Hi All,

Pooyan shared with me with some pictures of his station.  You will be amazed at the simplicity of his station.  And everything is home brew (well, except the LCD monitor, hihi).  To top it off he doesn't have an EE degree.  He's one of few people on this planet that could pull this one off :-)

I have asked him to send a picture of his antenna (fan dipole inverted V at 20m high).

DSCN2961 To the right is his 300W solid state home brew linear amplifier.  The knob is the band selector.  The LCD display is the power level readout.  You could see the large heat sink on the back of it.

DSCN2964 The white box is the home brew SDR.

The remaining three pics show previous home-brew transceivers.

There have been some discussions on the NCDXC chat room regarding the morality of using skeds to get a 'rare one' confirmed.  I'm encouraged by the many positive comments I read in the past few days.  I got acquainted with Pooyan after reading Rich KY6R's amazing story on his sked.  Through Rich's assistance (thank you Rich!) I was able to connect with Pooyan and made a successful contact on 20m SSB on the third attempt.  Prior to the contact I have never heard EP let alone try to compete in the pileups from Colorado.  The friendship I developed with Pooyan is far more important than the QSL card.  Sadly some people think a confirmation is the end all be all in DX'ing....Rich and I would disagree wholeheartedly.  I hope you have an open mind and have enjoyed reading this.  73.
 
Best Regards,

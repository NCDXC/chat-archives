I often cringe when I see a DXpedition team comprised mostly of one nationality. 

I remember some years ago, I needed XT2 Burkina Faso for an ATNO. A small team of Japanese went there and worked everyone in Japan
and went home. I heard them but could not work them. 

I feel that the best DXpeditions have members representing most of the continents and part of their role is to ensure that their countrymen back home
are satisfied with the effort and contacts that were made. 

Then there are the teams like the Italian DXpedition Team, All Italian except for one Japanese and they take great delight in following the propagation
and working everyone everywhere. This is the best kind of team in my view. 

Paul N6PSE

Bob,

I agree, if we can find a meeting location large enough, hi.  It IS
frustrating because of their obvious bias; not just at the US but
anything not EU or JA.  It's wrong.

This morning (and now on 10.145) they were working all callers, still
preferring EU if you watch trends.  I was able to bust the FT8 pileup
this morning by doing two things in particular:

1)  The K3 in data mode has a bandpass of (up to) 4 kHz; setting the
center to 2 KHz.  This chops the bottom end of the audio spectrum (a
LOT).  Reset that to a center of 1500 Hz and you hear what you need to
hear.  In F/H mode, this is critical as the Fox (DX) tends to be in the
200-400 Hz range.

2)  Randomly STOP transmitting to make sure someone isn't using the same
tones as you.  MOST operators don't bother or care and it may not have
an effect on the DX ears but it avoids an obvious conflict, a slight edge.

They were running 1 to 5 streams, I copied them all no problem (even
with the other signals from non-hams).  The noise floor is -127 dBm on
the P3 (some 20 dB better than my CA location).

I don't think I'll bother losing more sleep over this team but I really
feel sorry for those wanting the ATNO.  TI9A also won't be getting any
donation via OQRS from me unless they change their habits.  I won't
reward poor behavior (but WILL take that QSL, hi).

Good hunting!

Rick NK7I

I moved to the Bay Area in mid 1977 and met Ted on 15m in early 1978. 

He invited me to attend an  NCDXC meeting and eventually introduced me to some of the big guns of the day: Merle Parten K6DC, Bob Thompson K6SSJ, Ron Panton W6VG, Charlie Kump K6ZYC,  the Colvins, Hal Godfrey N6AN, Howard Hale W6SC, and of course Ron Rasmussen K6OP.  At my first DX convention my banquet seat was with Don Wallace W6AM.  Sitting at the foot of the masters was an interesting entry to the world of DXing.

About the time I met Ted I think he was the the club's president, or president elect.  Two years later he and K6SSJ asked me to take on the club treasurer job in 1980, which I held until I moved to the east coast in 1981.

He was a true ambassador to ham radio and to the NCDXC, always recruiting new club members..

In later years we would meet at the DX conventions and chat about family, work and DX. Ted was a real gentlemen and a great mentor. The last time I saw him at a DX convention both of us were a bit older, (a lot older).

 I miss all the NCDXC old guys, but then - I'm now an old guy too.

Cheers,
Gene K5GS

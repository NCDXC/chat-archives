On 1/5/2013 11:24 PM, Bob Lanning wrote:

I have been through several logging applications and have settled on DX

I'm with you, Bob. I can't say that I've tried them all -- I found DXKeeper in 2003 when I got back on the air, and have been a very happy user ever since. It keeps track of all awards, has an easy entry window for logging non-contest contacts, lots of other features, does LOTW and eQSL almost automatically.
I do my contest logging in N1MM, export as ADIF to DKKeeper, and let DXKeeper deal with LOTW. There are many other parts to the suite. I use DXView a little, also Commander a little (it reads frequency and mode from the radio and enters it for each QSO).
It is FREE, VERY well supported with an email reflector by the author who is there all the time, it is regularly updated, and for features, not bugs. For example, when the new DXCC submittal rules were announced, DXKeeper supported it almost immediately.

This is not to AL but to all of us.

 

If we go backwards, I believe it was more thrilling when you were tuning the bands looking for a faint signal. Does anybody do this anymore???

Today we all look at skimmers, Paul’s emails, do reverse beacon network to see if bands are operable. Let’s not even get into scheduled QSOs! (?)

 

Internet has brought a new life to the Hobby and some challenges to old ways of doing things.

Heck! I operated remote yesterday from the comforts of my home using the Internet as a giant remote control and audio conduit!

However, it’s interesting to note that WSJT-X does not use the internet. My XYL sometimes comes to the shack and warns me that the kids need to reset the Modem if that would screw me up.

I say, no go ahead. I’m not using it! 😊  She looks at me clueless…

 

I do have my own personal opinions on internet enabled links (DMR/DSTAR, etc…) but was not shy to try them. In fact I found that for people who love rag chewing (Couldn’t care less about technology) it’s awesome. You always have a robotic 59 signal and can have a meaningful conversation anywhere in the world with somebody you don’t know. I enjoy it sometimes to keep my foreign languages in check. Cannot do that with a faint DX signal, sorry…

 

Also note that I never personally experienced a anything above a low sun cycle. Maybe one day I will… maybe I will…

Sorry… adrenaline rushing down from yesterday.

 

Roberto

Hi AL,

 

I understand the feeling and thrill of being able to copy a faint signal with your own head. Having said that and given the current conditions, making a QSO (connection between two stations) is what matters.

There is no better mode. There are many modes. Each one decides what they want to do, that’s the beauty of this hobby. I do personally try every mode, including DMR (???).

I’m on QRP SOTA now. Talking about faint signals… don’t even need DX. And add noisy towers next to you. Of course, I could fix it by bringing a Raspberry PI and do FT8 there… 😊 One day I might try and see what I get…

My two cents… (recovering from NAQP SSB). I might be rambling…(?)

Thank you for your thoughts, Paul.  Good propagation on digital does not translate to CW, unfortunately.  I still enjoy the hunt for weak signals that I can copy by careful receiver adjustments, leaning forward n my chair, and somehow making out a callsign.  Getting a 2-way QSO under those conditions makes for a very satisfying event as I practice my hobby.  I wish there were more, but I take what is there.  My 80 M totals are 198 worked and 195 confirmed.  I would love to add to those totals and am not sure how I will feel if I add to that with FT8.  Maybe I should add FT8 to my modes and then decide.

Meanwhile, I will keep working DX and enjoying it as always.

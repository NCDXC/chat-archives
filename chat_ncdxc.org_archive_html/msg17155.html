On May 14, 2019, at 5:36 PM, RICH HOLOCH <rholoch@comcast.net> wrote:

I've had one up over a year on a small ALM-31 crank up by US Towers. Its a great antenna for restricted spaces and even covers 40M. Since mine is up only 35-ish feet, obviously its a cloud burner on 40M - but I used a DXE DV-40-P set of phased verts on 40M so that' a moot point. On 30 - 6M its quite good for a _compromise_ antenna (small footprint). It does feel like it performs in a very similar way to the 2 element STeppIR I vaguely remember from 10 years back that I had.

Take your time - read the instructions first. Be careful when you use the bit they give you to "chamfer" out where the fiberglass meets the black "corner" tubes. Also measure twice and cut once when you cut the few fiberglass tubes that you have to.

A heat gun is a MUST.

I love mine - its no DXU-32 that I used to have - but it also wont ruin my roof if it falls . . . .

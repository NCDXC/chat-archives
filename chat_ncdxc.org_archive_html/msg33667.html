Hello everyone,

Last night at the club meeting I discussed an idea to help our members and 
others solve problems that they do not have the necessary knowledge or
experience to navigate through.  In our club we have guys and gals with 
tons of operating, DXing, technical and antenna building experience. I would
like to connect our experienced hams with people needing help by placing
the list of topics below with an elmer associated with each topic on the
NCDXC website. The person who needs help can contact the appropriate
elmer by email or he/she can contact a club board member who can put 
him/her in touch with the correct elmer.

To make this work I need you to sign up for one or more of the slots in the
list. Just email me (jeisenb558@aol.com) to sign up for one or more slots.
I will get the list posted on the club website with your name and email.
You will get the satisfaction of helping a deserving member through a problem.
It is a win-win!

Here is the list of topics:

1. Ham Radio Basics
2. Setting up an HF station     Tony Dowler K6BV and Bob Lanning W6OPO have volunteered
3. HF operating skills
4. 160 meter operating skills   Bob Chortek AA6BV has volunteered
5. HF DXing skills
6. VHF Dxing skills                Russ Bentson K6KLY and Chuck Leader have volunteered
7. CW operation
8. SSB operation
9. FT8 operation
10. RTTY station setup and operation
11. Simple antennas
12. Antennas for DXing
13. Effective antennas for small lots
14. Propagation
15. Logging and logging programs
16. QSLing techniques                        John Eisenberg K6YP has volunteered
17. Ham equipment repair
18. What is important in a receiver     John Eisenberg K6YP has volunteered
19. Circuit design assistance               John Eisenberg K6YP has volunteered
20. Basic electronics help                   John Eisenberg K6YP has volunteered
21. Grounding, bonding and lightening protection
22. Safety
23. Local interference and noise abatement
24. Solving neighborhood RFI problems

Please pick your favorite topics. It is great to have a few people covering one topic
and send me your topics. I'll take it from there.

Thank you and 73,

John Eisenberg K6YP

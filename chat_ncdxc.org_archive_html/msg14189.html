I ran across an email an old from Pooyan, ex EP2PK, that thanked us for donating funds to help he and his family move to Australia.  I sent an email last night and below is his reply.  It sounds like he is doing well.

Bill
WB6JJJ
-------------

Hi Bill,

Nice to hear from you! and hope you're doing well.

I was delighted by your - and all the friends' - kind gift. The fund was spent purchasing a couple of radios (a second hand FT-920, and Icom 706, which was swapped with an FT450 later) and a power supply for the new VK shack.

I still have the FT-920 as my main radio. Did a few things to it, including adding a high quality studio grade microphone, and a panadapter using and RTL-SDR. I had some thoughts about the 7300, but it really doesn't add much to the current setup I have, with a large-screen panadapter.

We have quite settled down now after four years. I and my wife both have full-time job; I work in ABC (the national Aussie broadcaster) as a broadcast technologist, and my wife loves her work as childcare educator.
Our daughter is 6 now, and barely remembers Persian. Her main language has well altered to be English. She is finishing transition in December, and will be in year 1 next January.

That's pretty much the news from my end.

Yes, I do get on air from time to time, probably a few hours every week. Mainly on 20 and 15m bands, and onto FT8 these days, as propagation is not great for solid SSB QSOs (we all know!)

I hope you and family are "going" well (yes the down under accent is definitely there!)

"All good mate! good to catchup... ava good one!"

Looking forward,
Pooyan
VK8PKH

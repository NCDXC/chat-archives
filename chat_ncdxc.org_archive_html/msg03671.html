I had waited for a response to Paul's question to Garry and never saw one.
The two scenarios are different but both give a QSO that some would consider
'cheaper' than a 'real' QSO.
Some could say that a QSO found by the computer that announced DX you need
while you are watching that TV sitcom is also  'cheap'.  We all have to
decide what constitutes a QSO he is personally proud to have logged.  I make
all kinds.
I made a lot of Africa phone contacts in the mid 70's using W2PPG/WA6BKS on
the Afrikaners Net on 21.355.  Then, I got a bunch of Asia using W7PHO on
14.225.  I have since replaced all those list type QSOs and feel better for
having done so.  They were 'too cheap' by my current standards.  Back then,
I considered each as likely to be the one and only in my life, not knowing
what was really rare.
And last night, I struggled to work C82DX on 40 CW with S9 noise.  After
making the QSO, I had to listen another 15 minutes to hear his all of his
call sign and fully know who I worked and not assume the spot was correct.
More satisfying for me.
73,
Al-N6TA

Will,

I am NOT close to being an expert, don't quote me please.

When a CME happens (the sun, spits, and that has been a LOT the past week), two blasts are sent.  One is an energy pulse; we see that on earth 8.5 minutes later (normally as an attenuation to the bands on the parts of the planet that is in daylight; that are facing the sun).  That dissipates in some hours, usually a few.

The second part is the particle blast, moving on the 'solar wind' (500 Km/s nominal).  That wind, like air on earth, moves at different rates, OFTEN.  And it's much slower than the energy pulse, so the plasma/particles (the physical element) take more time to arrive (IF directed our way).  That window is typically 18-36 hours.

The effects of a plasma hit, takes a couple days to shake off (and why we pay attention to CME for propagation).  This series of CME (and those like it) tend to dampen the bands for days because the hits keep coming.  [Nearing the peak of the solar cycle, but muting it at the same time.]

In the past couple days, we now have SEVEN CME events aimed at us.  One is a 'cannibal' event [large AND 950 Km/s ++] which will eat up other events in the path (and it's much faster, it will do this).  So ALL of that energy will arrive (or is now) in a denser impact [that .22 bullet is now a 44 mag].

[To the purists, yes, grossly over simplified but] Does that make sense now?  And it explains why most bands this week are 'meh' (in part); the shields are holding but hot shields don't give us good prop.

Shortest version:  We rely on the sun for everything (including life) and for ham radio needs sometimes we get storms, sometimes we get drought.  As it stands right now the solar cycle is the wet season and a pineapple express is punching in now.  Too much of a good thing, causes flooding.

For good prop, like crops, we need just the right mix.  Dis ain't dat.  But we get aurora instead, a pretty trade off (for some).

I don't have 2M SSB gear, but it could be kickin too...

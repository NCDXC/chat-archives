Congrats on the 80m contact.  I'm curious how the 80m dipole works on your antenna.  Is the dipole basically the boom plus some extensions?  If yes how long is the dipole?

The DB36 has a optional 80M dipole that works with the SDA100 controller for effortless tuning 6M through the entire 80M band.  The dipole runs parallel to the boom and uses the end elements as capacity hats, creating zero interaction with the rest of the antenna.

On Wednesday, November 12, 2014 8:56 AM, Paul N6PSE via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:


Bob, I worked FT4TA on 80 meters, it was ESP copy for the most part. It was
by far my most difficult contact with them.

With deep QSB fading, their signals were up and down. I would call them when
I could hear them and wait when I could not hear them.

A few W6 guys told me that their signals were coming in from a skewed path
over Japan and once I turned my elevated rotatable dipole (steppIR DB36) in
that direction, their signal improved and I worked them with less
difficultly than when my dipole was broadside to them at 189 degrees.

Paul N6PSE

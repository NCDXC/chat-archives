SuperFox comes at a cost of 9 dB less usable signal; wider equals less signal to the decode.  Great in concept, less so in actual use.

It would not play well on a QRP station (anything under 1500 watts), the pop guns wouldn't stand a chance, only the bigger stations could work well.

While it seems counterproductive, there are times that 1 or maybe 2 streams is the better choice; in particular on the QRPp bands (60/30).  A lower rate, certainly but everyone would be given a more level playing field.

One of the issues for the KP5 team is the clouds, common in equatorial waters; they're not getting a full dose of sun, all day.  Doubling the charging/storage system (at least) to provide overhead would (should) be the next upgrade, which also increases the chance of RFI if not done (and filtered) really well.

Running an amp, while HIGHLY desired by callers; with no one on site or able to quickly respond would involve some risks and require a collection of switchable band filters for two rig operations.  And a MUCH more generous power sourcing.  As a rule, figure 50% efficiency of any amp, THEN provide overhead in the supply for stormy days (too much is just enough).  Base that on using FT8/4 (43% ish duty cycle); then do the math (double that supply or more!).

The odds of mutual interference increase with the power increase but more gain is (always) wanted; which falls on antennas.

Being limited to vertical antennas to meet the permission to land denies antenna gain on most bands, but an electrically switched array would help to take better advantage of prop (or zones unreached).  Or (on the higher bands mostly) a 1/2 wave J-pole design (with a LOT of choke at the feed) could be deployed.

For a first effort, not bad performance BUT take to heart the lessons learned and apply update accordingly (MORE power supply, gain antennas when possible).

Now; will it open the doors to Kure, Johnston and other US possessions for a similar effort?  Maybe; but it's still to easy (lazy) for those in positions of authority, to take the approach of "NO" without really looking at the situations.  THAT is not a simple problem.

73,
Rick

With my only experience being that of a residential user, my understanding is that it’s a linear relationship. The PV panels are rated for an ideal maximum production. That’s based on full sunlight at a 90 degree incidence to the panel surface. There is further inefficiency in the inverter conversion of the usual 50v DC to 110/220VAC. Although there are panels that track the sun, energy conversion efficiency at the panel varies throughout the day based on sun angle and most importantly, cloud cover. More panels and more batteries would be expensive. 
Last night on 80 FT8, KP5/NP3VI was running three streams that were intermittently at my threshold for decoding. I got a -02… at three streams, I think SF would have been a more readable choice of modes. 

SuperFox *might* be about the same (perhaps a wee bit better) than 3 streams of ordinary FT8, but unfortunately they are unable to run SuperFox because the RHR (Remote Ham Radio) system they are using uses MSHV rather than WSJT-X software. MSHV doesn’t support SuperFox mode. Of course, if they were running WSJT I would have issued them a SuperFox key.

SuperFox isn’t great when running low power/QRP (as they are doing on KP5), and with low/no-gain antennas (they are running a 43’ vertical). I doubt they would generate a sufficient pile-up to make SuperFox a better choice than what they are doing.

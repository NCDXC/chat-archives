Wow -- some old memories triggered.

Did not get licensed until 1962 in El Cajon, CA but tinkered before. Live on Yerba Buena Island from 1959 to 1961 while my Dad was Brig Warden on Treasure Island. Spent lots of time drooling in the surplus stores and at Zack Electronics around the 1400 block of Market Street. The public works dispatcher on TI was Morris Holman, W6STV. I spent may a weekend at his place in San Leandro and weeks at their cabin at Tahoe listening to lots of radio. Went to junior high school at Garfield in Berkeley with Wendy Teller, daughter of the nuclear scientist.

Thanks for the heads up Bob!

Ah yes, those were the days. My first visit to Red Johnson’s was in 1957, Sputnik had fired up my interest in radio after hearing the beacon on Dad’s SX-100!

Of course Zacks, but Red Johnson’s had all those CalRad parts imported from Japan to build radios with.  I still have my Dad’s 1957 CALRAD pocket radio (those old germanium transistors don’t last for ever but there are new ones available).

The Palo Alto ARC still holds meetings at the same location as my first visit in 1957.  In 2007 I made a 50th anniversary visit where Wayne introduced the KX3 and we learned how to make a vertical from Coffee cans soldered together!  A good time was had by all.

A major industrial electronics distributor (dang - the name escapes me) on Middlefield had Johnson Viking Rangers in both assembled and kit form in the front of the store.  Just next door was a company building commercial and some VHF Ham Radio gear Kaar Communications I think.

After moving from Palo Alto to Sunnyvale then Sunnyvale Electronics became the hangout, and I had every issue of Popular Electronics.

Quement moved from their downtown location to 1000 So Bascom, where Manny watched out over the big new Ham Radio department where Dad (W6LEH) brought home his Collins S-Line in 1959. What a station!

HRO of course was at the old Burlingame train stop.  I would visit my high school buddy George (then WB6DSV) to see the latest gear and Bob would roll in - in his CHP car at noon to see how things were going.

Didn’t we experience an unforgettable time in what would become Silicon Valley.  

Time to get back to writing my book.
Dave WI6R ex- KD6AZ ex- WB6BDA

After 40 years, as many of you may already know, HSC Electronic Supply (formerly Hal-Ted Specialties Company) is finally going to close its doors for good on January 12th.  They currently have almost everything in the shop marked way down in price, so now is a good time to stop by for one last visit, or your very first visit, if you've never been.


They are located at


3051 Corvin Dr, Santa Clara, CA 95051

https://goo.gl/maps/CGcaxobyn6z


The alternatives now for local electronic parts are Excess Solutions (similar surplus shop, limited customer service), and Anchor Electronics (mostly new stuff, with a very helpful staff).

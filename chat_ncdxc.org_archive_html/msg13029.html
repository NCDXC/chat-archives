I would love to have a Steppir.  But I live in Colorado at 6,000' ASL and we get 100mph winds once in a couple years and 70mph+ several times a year.  I don't think a Steppir would survive 100mph.  On Jan. 9th I lost my Bencher Skyhawk in a 109mph wind storm.  My standard (100mph) Force 12 Delta 230/240 survived.  I ordered a custom heavy duty Optibeam OB9-5 to replace the Skyhawk.  I don't know the wind survival rating on my new Optibeam however I do know the 20m element starts off at 35mm or 1-3/8" OD.  Attached is a picture of my destroyed Skyhawk.  You can also see the slight bend in the 2" boom on the Skyhawk.  The elements were pointed into the wind.  The undamaged Force 12 sits just below it.

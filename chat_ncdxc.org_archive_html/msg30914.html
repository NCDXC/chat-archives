Don't forget Al, that you're looking in ONE direction.  On 40M he is on a vertical (not the Spiderbeam) so he hears ALL directions; including skipping like a rock on water, into EU.  Soon JA will appear too.  He works what he hears, not what you hear.  And he hears it ALL.

-=-=-=-=-=-=-

We have a sudden (delicious) glut of DX the past couple weeks and it's been FUN.  But here is a minor frustration rant: 

Signs of not understanding the mode of FTx: 
#1 Calling on the WRONG cycle.  If you can't HEAR the DX, don't CALL the DX.  Basic stuff.  (DOUBLE CLICK on their printed call, the program helps you out by setting the correct cycle for you but keep reading please.  If in doubt, do NOT start off as Hound.)

#2 NEVER (except in F/H response to the DX report back to you) NEVER call on the DX tones (plural when more than one stream).  Choose one that appears to be unused.  (If everyone called on their tones, the DX would never hear any of them.)

#3 (And this is happening a LOT lately), #1 and #2 at the same time; calling ON TOP of the DX tones, ON their cycle (no one hears the DX).

#4 When asked to QSY, simply change your tone; you don't have to vacate the band head held low; be courteous and shift off.

#5 Just because the DX is on the even cycle, doesn't mean or imply they're using F/H mode; they have only TWO choices, ODD or EVEN!   If they opt for F/H they will be EVEN, every time.  We have no way to KNOW if F/H is being used.  Lack of understanding here, circles RIGHT back to #1.  Watch them work a few stations, see who is closing the QSO and follow their lead.

Yes, stop transmitting every XX cycles to make sure someone didn't start using your tones.  If so, move to a valley in the horde wall and start again.

It's frustrating (and depressing) just how many out there, can't grasp this very simple (nearly automatic) mode (just like the ID machines on CW, sending while NEVER listening, creating QRM).

Can we do better now?  (whew!  While fun these last couple weeks, these issues KEEP popping up, slowing the rate or progress for everyone.)

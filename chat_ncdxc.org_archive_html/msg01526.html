I agree; one of the best teams I've heard.  It was a superior effort.  They were better on CW (no accents to decypher or wrong TX settings or flaky mic cord).  The scariest part was the code speeds, a couple were in the mid to high 30s and I'm not a CW op (I know my call, the report and the entire QSO is reduced to three K3 memory buttons with one more set aside for contests, which I don't enter). 

And they tended to jump around some to not be as predictable as most, the downside is that things tended to expand to very wide (easier on the team but a few CW spreads were 10K of wall on the scope; I'd try later).  Mostly the randomness worked since everyone uses a scope, multiple receivers and some voodoo.  ;-)  Up 5-10 means just that, not up 3.8 or 10.7 which they allowed. 

I know we're spoiled but some real time updates (on air) would be nice like "hey fellas, we're getting slammed by QRN tonight, sorry but we're doing what we can; hang in there."

The EU only for hours on end with 1 Q every 1-3 minutes has GOT to go.  If you're not getting a decent rate, the propagation just ain't there, move ON already. 

From memory (on the road again): 160-10 CW sweep; a near SSB sweep (needed 160 and 1-2 others) and three RTTY Q's. 

And for once, it was easy.  Not bragging, everything lined up this time. 

73,

On 1/31/2014 12:19 PM, Rich Holoch wrote:

How about you? What's your experience?
I use every trick I can think of. The P3 can be a big help in finding holes in the pileup, often showing the guy he's working, showing the shape and limits of the pileup.
When I don't need the 2nd RX for diversity to copy the DX, I use it to listen to my TX frequency and slide around to avoid zero beat QRM. Obviously, this works better when propagation lets you hear your QRM.
I tend to the edges of the pile, but I also move around the pile, looking for areas that are less dense, moving as the swarm moves.
On CW, I send as fast as I think the op can copy, the intent being to squeeze my call into the shortest possible time so that I'm less likely to get stepped on before the DX has it all. On both CW and SSB, I call twice, listen, call once, listen, call once listen,(only the first time twice)until the DX calls someone (or a partial that isn't me). On SSB, I also try to dump my call in as fast as possible "K9YankeeCharlie," with lots of attention to timing with whatever else is happening on my TX frequency.
On RTTY, I'll send my call 6 times, listen, then 4, listen, then 2, listen, then 2, listen, until the DX responds to someone. And on RTTY, I try to stay away from other signals in the pile.
When the DX is not working split, I try to call a bit off frequency following the same general techniques.
And always, my rule is to TX in short enough bursts, and listen in long enough bursts, that I'm not QRMing the station the DX is calling.

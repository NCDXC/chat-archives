Hi Paul,

You might be able to manage an RBOG antenna (reversible beverage on ground), some do quite well within 100' of length.  It can be fence mounted (stapled) or actually under the top layer of dirt (and generally amplified).  That all depends on your lot and your neighbors.  I plan to install two of them (SE/NW and SW/NE).  Bonus:  They are FAR simpler to maintain than a Beverage and can outperform them.

The 80M balun (control) box has been replaced, the one that came with (estate purchase), passed no RF (not the relays, tested and cleaned) and the amount of bolt corrosion (board into box) made it simpler to just replace it entirely instead of repair (it had open relays too, a really poor design decision).  Time matters, it'll snow for real soon so replacing was expedient.

It won't resonate below 7.01 MHz and altering element length doesn't lower SWR but does make the V dip (analyzer) into a shallow dish.  This is at 25', rotating it has zero effect so it isn't locally influenced (shop and wood shed roofs are metal).  This may be integrated with the 60/80 issue... unclear.

I'm working with SteppIR folks (and SteppIR reflector) to resolve these issues, but in the worst case I can use it this winter as is (just not ideal) and worry about it after the melt in Spring.  Move a couple more antenna leads into the temp testing area (garage) and it'll work.

Now if I can just get the tilt plate hook to latch every time (a matter of adjustment, trial and error), some coax standoffs installed (neighbor is making them), I'll have the temp station roughed out until the shack room is built this winter (working it remotely, the garage is COLD; I've also routed the SteppIR coax into the current [warm] shack, using the 7300 and a laptop to manage the antenna; but it's easy to make a big mistake).

I'm trying out PSTRotatorAz to manage the SteppIR remotely.  It works with HRD but it's... lacking.  I'm encouraging the HRD folks to incorporate SteppIR into Rig Control or Rotor control (or as a new included app), the popularity of the brand makes it a good choice for them.

Where it does resonate:  STUNNING performance!  It's like taking ear plugs out AND ear muffs off at the same time the heavy metal/grunge band quits blasting.  I can HEAR again!

After 40+ years of being licensed, I'll finally have a station to be proud of and a noise floor worth envy.  Those were goals for moving out of CA.

73,
Rick NK7I

Thanks for sharing your experiences on top band. Yes, the receive antenna is very important. I don't have room for a long Beverage on the Ground (BOG) or a K9AY flag, but I find that my SteppIR DB36 in 80 meter mode works very well for receiving on top band.

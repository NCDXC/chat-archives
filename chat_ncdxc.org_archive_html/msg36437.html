Hi Mike,

 

It will be exiting and frustrating to upgrade. 

 

While the rest of the world seems to welcome or at least tolerate regular updates, hams seem to prefer a stable platform that, once proven to play well with various radio drivers and amateur applications, remains unchanged.

 

Once you find your PC vendor you might consider discussing the “unique” needs of ham operators when planning your initial setup, as well as security strategies that avoid, or at least minimize, the number of automated updates of the operating system and every software application you use try to force.

 

One set of tools you and they might find useful in setting up your new system is Chris Titus Tech Windows Utility

https://github.com/ChrisTitusTech/winutil

Here is a YouTube video that is a very detailed review.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pldFPTnOCGM&t=1038s

 

One point to emphasize with Windows 11; stay well clear of Microsoft OneDrive and avoid using the predefined My Document, My Pictures etc folders. Microsoft practically forces new users to use one OneDrive and, without using tools like the Chris Titus utility, it can take days to untangle the convoluted registrations to avoid it.  This is a “necessity” in my book if you plan any offline computer enabled radio operation like field day, etc.

 

Once you set up and de-bloat your new system, I recommend a conservative update strategy in which you choose to specifically permit or deny each update attempted by the OS, drivers, or apps. Do this in conjunction with regular full system imaging and save several generations of system images on network storage of some sort. (There are a number of good imaging tools, including Windows built is version.)

 

System imaging allows you restore you system rapidly if an update - forced or otherwise - breaks any of your amateur application. It also serves as a backup to virus and other threats, in that you can have minimum protection to minimize the intrusion of “security” and antivirus programs that often themselves interfere with applications and can be system resource hogs. This also allow you to recover when an OS or other update cannot be rolled back.

 

If something - update, virus infection, whatever - breaks your system, restore your system using you last working image and get back to DXing in minutes rather than days and figure out what broke your system later when there is no DX.

Hello Fellow DXers,

 

After 12 years, it’s time for me to upgrade my shack desktop (floor, really) PC.   I’ve kept my current PC running by upgrading the HDD to an SDD, upgrading the graphics card to support dual monitors, and stuffing the motherboard with as much RAM as possible.  And it’s working OK - for now.

 

I want to move on to Windows 11 and with Microsoft sunsetting Windows 10 in 2025, I feel this is the time for a new PC.

 

I’m looking for a PC vendor who will work with me to spec out a new desktop PC.   I have some hardware requirements for my station beyond what comes in an off the shelf PC, and it’s been a long time since I spec’d and built a PC myself.

 

If you know of a good PC vendor who provides this level of service, please let me know.   Guidance and suggestions are much appreciated.

 

Thanks!

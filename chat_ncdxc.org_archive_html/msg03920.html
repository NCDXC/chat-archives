I tried a short beverage of only 100 feet, and my conclusion – it’s way too short. It did however perform about as well as the K9AY Loop (un-amplified) that was pointed in that direction, but was actually a bit noisier. This all makes perfect sense since the K9AY Loops are 85 feet each.

 

However, I decided to see if there was a new and improved rotatable coaxial – or other similar loop – that I can build myself. And voila! Rick, N6RK does have what seems like a big improvement over the ARRL Antenna Book 160M coaxial loop – in a 2009 NCJ article, and posted on his web site:

 

http://www.n6rk.com/

 

Since I can only transmit with 500 watts between about 1.815 and 1.830 with really decent results (for a top loaded 63’ vertical with 76 radials), I have decided to build the circuit as fixed and instead of varactors – just use a doorknob cap of either 300 or 500 pf in parallel with a 365 pf AM radio air variable capacitor. I have already wound the toroidal transformer on an Amidon FT-82A-61 core.

 

I will put this bugger up tomorrow and then have three antennas to use for 160M RX:

 

1)      The TX vertical (which yes – sometimes is better than the K9AY Loops

2)      The K9AY Loops

3)      This new and improved N6RK RX Loop

 

There are many possible 160M activations this month, and with the higher bands in great shape, this could be a really fun month of DX! I am hoping to get to 60 or 70 on 160M this winter season. 60 seems “in the bag”, 70 would be a minor miracle, but full steam ahead!

 

GL to you – I hope you work all your ATNO’s and band slots.

 

73,

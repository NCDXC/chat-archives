Steve, just realized this went to chat when I just answered you.  First, I doubt I was the only one.  This is not a particularly hard stretch for 160.  In fact until you emailed I wasn't entirely sure (nor did I check or care that much) that I did work him.  Copy was very minimal.  Maximum power helps.  It's easier to send than receive.  But I have this weird antenna.  It's an end-fed sloper, up on the mountain at 1400ft. hanging from my tower.  I have to physically move the end to point to the direction I need.  I don't know how it works and only tunes within few KHz wide range without adjusting wire length.  Funny story is that I lent it to Al, N6TA for a couple of weeks, and he could never get it to work right or tune up from his tower.  We never could figure out why.  It works great for me and have worked a number of west African stations on it....during the winter months.  I use the same antenna design for 80m.  Both of these I bought at my one and only Dayton trip, carrying back in my bags.  I don't remember the manufacturer, it's not in front of me at the moment, but I did find out it's no longer made but I have directions if any are interested.  80m is much more fun imho.  Maybe it's just luck that it works or I have just the right situation.  But I believe it transmits better than it receives if that makes sense.

Its funny - last year I had just the MA160V - which is 34 - 36' tall, and top loaded and hatted. When it was up by itself, the K9AY loops always seemed to win. 

Now that it is on top of the 30' Rohn telescoping mast - it seems that the 60 (+) feet of capture area makes a noticeable difference. 

I have had no way to do any serious A - B tests, but capture area seems to be key. 

Yea…receivers need a threshold S/N to be able to work.  You can boost the Signal, or lower the Noise.  Putting a pre-amp on the antenna only increases the S and the N at the same time, not improving the S/N.

The majority of the noise in the HF bands, especially below 30m is either man-made, or lightning discharge.  The only way an antenna can help with that is reduce the noise it captures, thru directivity away from the noise source - the noise sources are always point and don't arrive at your antenna from all directions.

With directivity, you can point the antenna in the direction of the signal, raising the S portions, and if you're lucky, away from the noise source, thereby reducing the N.


On my pan adapter, the noise characteristic looked the same on both antennas, but it seems to me that the extra capture area - and the fact that there is at least 30' high and above everything in the clear - that it provides that much more signal to pop above the noise. 

Noise has to be considered in the bandwidth of the receiver.  panadators generally are high BW, and don't have as good as an effective receiver as the main signal receiver.  The noise has to be pretty constant and greater than 2 S units before it will show consistently on a pan adaptor.

The spectrum analyzer guys rate this as resolution BW, and Video BW.

On Oct 28, 2013, at 2:00 PM, Kevin Rowett <kevin@rowett.org> wrote:

On Oct 28, 2013, at 1:30 PM, Rich Holoch <rholoch@comcast.net> wrote:

This morning I worked E51NOU - South Cooks for a new one on 160M. At first I couldn't hear him at all on my K9AY Loops, but when I switched to my transmitting antenna - which is a Cushcraft MA-160V mounted on top of a 30' Rohn pushup mast - (meaning its about 60' tall) and with 76 buried radials (mostly 50' long), I heard him loud and clear. Not sure why - the K9AY loops are almost always better . . .

Receive loops provide less signal performance. They win by reducing the amount of noise.  In effect, they improve the S/N ratio, by reducing the noise received injected into your receiver.  They don't hear better, per capture, or apecture area of the antenna.

Different designs reduce the noise in different ways. It's usually by making the antenna sharply directional, without having to have a full yagi implementation.

Your vertical is a much better antenna than your receive antenna, from the description you gave.  If the noise is low enough, you'll be bale to hear him well enough to work him.

Hi Bob,

I'm doing well here.  Thanks for asking.  Regarding KH1, I've noticed that signal reports vary widely from stations in the same geographical area.  I'm seeing this across the stations in Colorado.  When I see a spot from a CO station and I don't hear anything, I get extremely depressed, hihi.  But with some patience I do end up working them.  Conditions have been very marginal.  So it's likely that minor differences in QTHs are magnified under marginal conditions.  It's still way too early to throw in the towel.  So keep chasing them on the bands you still need them on.

Regarding the two element shorty-forty antenna, they are somewhat difficult to tune due to their high Q nature.  But I can give you some tips.  The provided factory dimensions are just a starting point.  You should find a local station to TX a test signal and tune your antenna for maximum F/B.  The max. gain might not coincide with max. F/B, but since you can't measure gain you can only measure F/B.  I did that and I was able to achieve a peak F/B of 18db.  When I had the factory dimensions I had no noticeable F/B.  After I was done tuning my reflector element is actually several inches shorter than the driven element!  But I'm not worried.  A small variation in the inductance of the loading coils could be the explanation.  Also whenever the antenna is down at the ground level, check for continuity across the load coils.  I had a loading coil connection break off and so the antennas wasn't resonating on the right freq.  If this had happened on the reflector element it might be difficult to notice it while the antenna is in the air.  Lastly don't worry too much about getting the best F/B.  The gain pattern will remain intact despite a non-optimal F/B.  So despite a lower F/B the antenna will still perform well in a pileup since gain is what you need in a pileup.  Once you've got a F/B you could elect to call it a day and know your antenna will perform well in a pileup.  I hope this help.

Best Regards,

Yeah - all this "Big Table" stuff. I avoided NoSQL like the plague. Asking someone to write a Map and Reduce program so some analyst can get their data is like going back to COBOL on mainframes. Sorry - been there (at Lockheed in Sunnyvale in 1982-83), and then high tailed it up to Marsh Road - near SLAC for Oracle at the time. 

So now I am on "NewSQL" - using slight variants of the old 3rd Normal OLTP and OLAP models. OLTP designs with ALL type-2 tables and the STAR schema is now replaced with an event log framework that I call "SQL Big Table". I used my silly ham log to design that "SQL Big Table" . . . . nothing more than the renormalized reporting tables we designed at Oracle in the 80's - just now horizontally scalable on the cheap.

We burn a little more disk - but hey - disk is very, very cheap - unlike back in 1982 . . .

I have loaded my logbook onto a massively scalable database that my company makes - which is called Splice Machine. 

Splice Machine runs on top of Hadoop and HBase and uses the Derby SQL Interface and JDBC drivers. 

Just for laughs, I loaded my logbook into Splice Machine into a 4 node AWS Cloudera Hadoop cluster, and used Pentaho PDI to load the data in milliseconds. 

I just wanted to see if LOTW or Clublog could take advantage of such a platform, and the answer is, Yes. I also used Tableau to do advanced data visualization - and its really cool - and really fun.

This is much too big for even the largest DX-pedition, its in the LOTW class of logbooks!

So far, the only explanation would be that gray line effect was more potent than typical, while trans equatorial for us was minimal.  Whatever the reason, it was just odd to not hear him well during our dark (yeah he's been wimpy on all bands from the start for some reason).  A short time later, he went to 10M to work more EU.

I got him on 20 for the ATNO (straining my ears, between the lids and bozos, skewed path).  He can hear and gave me a +1 for that effort (while he was in the dust, I started with the report, -18).  I had 1300 watts output into 4 elements at 60' (20M).  Maybe he doesn't have his amp online yet?

As I recall, there is a volcano on the island and my guess is that he's shielded on the south side.  EU has the advantage, most of that path is over water; we're a little further over his horizon too with plenty of SA (and SE US) to work through.  But previously working the S Atlantic islands, was MUCH simpler than now (in the cycle doldrums too).  I hope he can improve things over the month that he's there (and doesn't get island fever like me, too confining).

Good luck all, seems we're all going to need it this time unless things really change for the better.

Hi NCDXC fellas, it was a good morning for the a West Coast!

I started out on 17SSB just before our sunrise. I worked all across the US however the band gave out and after about 45 minutes I could not hear any N. America. I announced I would QSY to 28.460. By the time I was tuned up and said QRZ, I had an S9 pileup all across the USA.

Signals were strong but faded rapidly an hour after sunrise and soon I could not hear NA again.  I then went to 15 meters and enjoyed more contacts with NA West of the Rockies.

It was really fun to work the following club members, some on multiple bands: N6SJ, K6SQL, K6KLY, W6PK, NE5EE, KY6R, N6TU, WD0FAA, K6YP, K6LM, K6TA and others.

We are having a great time in Myanmar!   The band conditions are quite good. I'm glad Sweepstakes has concluded and we are constantly having overlapping pileups with other DXpeditions.

Tonight, I'll try 10/12 meters long path to NA, that was very productive last night. Also, we are QRV on 160 meters now.

73,

Paul N6PSE

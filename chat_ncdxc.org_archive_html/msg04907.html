On 1/15/2014 10:11 AM, Stuart Phillips wrote:
There are two operating sites on Amsterdam. One on the north facing slope of the mountain, the other on the cliffs near the base of the East side of the island.
Propagation to the West Coast should be better from the more Easterly located station. Ralph and his team are aware of this.
After studying your predictions for us and comparing to those for EU, the morning path (1700) to us over water looks pretty good, especially if there's dirt between them and EU. We and EU are also very different beam headings, which will matter on bands where they're using antennas with directivity. I expect to be able work them on 40 and 20 if they're there from the east side site.
Of course, as Paul has noted, real world propagation has a way of differing from predictions. :)

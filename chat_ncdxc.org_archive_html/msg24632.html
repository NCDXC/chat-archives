PSKReporter isn't the main resource for prop, but is useful.  Simply calling CQ still works; some places just don't update to spotting networks and PSKRporter is near useless for phone; the frequency of update is clamped down, so some spots appear too late, if at all.  It's server limited, crunching a LOT of data (even more in the background, hidden).

10 was mostly closed on Sat, a scan of the panadapter proved that, calling CQ produced nothing.  There was a moderate opening Sun and I worked phone stations, gleaning from the contest some DX band mode slots for my collection.

10 was also spotlighting, a LOT over the weekend (I was in contact with hams in NV and SoCal and we discussed prop in general terms to not violate the contest rules, prop varied in each location).

One tool that has benefit is HamAlert (on the web and as an app).  I have a (one per mode) 'trigger' for any DX I don't have in my collection (it draws from Clublog).  It gathers from RBN, spotting networks and PSKReporter.  Some of the alarms need a little mental filtering (no it's unlikely that Somalia is spotted on 80M at noon local); but it's paid off well too.  (And you can temporarily turn them off, during a contest, limit the alarms per minute, keep domestic peace in your home.)

A large tool box (and amount of time) is all to your benefit when used correctly.  So get to to work!  ;-)

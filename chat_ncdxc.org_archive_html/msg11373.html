Rick,

	The reason for the escalation in the cost of mega-DXpeditions, such
as the ongoing VP8 operation and the upcoming Heard Island operation, is
that there are very few reliable transport options available to DXpedition
organizers--and the owners of these ships have figured that out.  I'm sure
you've heard of the Braveheart, the ship used by the VP8 team and many other
DXpeditions.  The owner of the Braveheart, Nigel Jolly, and his crew are
reliable and have tons of experience with DXpeditions.  But Nigel has
figured out that his services are in great demand and that the DXpedition
organizers don't have many options that are reliable, safe and experienced.
As a result, the price he quotes has gone up big time.  

	I was involved in arranging the charter for the 2009 DXpedition to
Ducie Island, VP8DX, and the team hired the Braveheart.  I can't reveal the
amount involved, but Nigel's price in 2009 was substantially less than his
price for the VP8 and Heard Island operations.  Currency fluctuations may
explain some of it (Nigel is based in New Zealand) but most is explained by
Nigel's realization that he has monopoly pricing power.

	73 Kip W6SZN

I keep reading about how much dxpedition costs have gone up.  I have no
specific knowledge about this, but I do read the Wall Street Journal, and I
don't understand how this "inflation" can be explained, in light of general
economic conditions.
The commodity bubble of the past decade has run its course and most
commodities are back to
2002 levels, most importantly oil.  (The only exception is gold, which is
not just another commodity, and in any event is only 30% above the 1980
price).  Wages aren't going up much; I know my last significant raise was
around 2003.  Central banks are worried about deflation, not inflation.  The
US dollar is at a 10+ year high, so prices denominated in dollars are
especially constrained.  Airline tickets don't cost more, and there is a
glut of world shipping capacity.
Yet somehow, we are being told that charter boats and helicopter services
can command premium prices?  What is special about them?  Not arguing, just
trying to understand.

Rick N6RK

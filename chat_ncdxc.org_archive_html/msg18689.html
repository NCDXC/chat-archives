I offered this to the group, had some requests, so I'll post this to the group to save us all time.

I'm sending two files, the same thing in different formats (Open Office .ODT and MS Word .DOC).  One of these should open for you.

When teams are active, I print this out and leave it in plain sight (a magnet board next to the desk).  Then I find a (working, rats!) pen.

In the tiny box, upper left, I put in the call of the DX station.  Sideways on the left margin, I write their location (because I will NOT remember it).  If the call is loooooong, I write it on the margin too.

An "X" in the box means that I already have the slot filled AND confirmed; don't bother trying unless you're that bored.

A check mark in a box means that I have worked (THAT) DX station.  If they aren't using Clublog or other online log, get an insurance dupe (if rare) and add a second check mark.

A cross on the check means that it's 'confirmed' in Clublog or online log; no dupe required.  That band mode slot is DONE.

After a period of time, it's easy to see which boxes are empty when you compare against the spotting lists (which I use DX Watch filtered to the specific DX station; one station per tab, currently 4 tabs open for me in the browser).

I hope this makes sense to you, this system has worked well for me.  I like simple and old school.


FYI, DXpedition tracking functionality is BUILT-IN to MacLoggerDX, the widely-used Macintosh logging software. (And FWIW, the feature was added specifically at my request, as I am fairly close with the developer and also wanted a way to see what band/modes I had worked with a given DXpedition.)

The feature is called “DXpedition Tracker” and shows up as a movable window unto itself. You enter the callsigns of the DXpeditions you want to track (I haven’t found any limit to simultaneous DXpeditions). It shows you, for each callsign:

* Which bands and modes you have worked them on
* Which bands and modes they are currently spotted on, with any needed slots highlighted in RED.

You can click on the red/highlighted spot indications, and it will tune your radio instantly to the correct band/mode/frequency to work that slot.

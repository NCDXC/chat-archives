Most of the locals I know here in EL09 have them on zero, one or two slots. As you head north towards Michigan my buddy with a pistol station worked them on CW and SSB the other night on 20m which is the money band for sure. Took him three days to hear them loud enough but all it takes sometimes in this hobby is patience, seat time and a good opening. 

Many are trying FT8 and some are wondering if they are still doing it right, like me. I decided to set it up and see if I can get it to work on 20m and 6m. I am now just letting it run on two different rigs and check in on it from time to time.  Seen a few decodes of them on 20m but nothing on 6m. 

A friend in Germany with a modest station, smaller optibeam, 7610 and 1kw amp worked them on 20m CW early morning near daybreak his time on day three with good signal. I know he is super stoked.

I think the smaller pileups is due to signals petering out due to poor prop as we go east. 20m is still money for most folks.

Al,

 

I also think pileups seem to not be a big as we have seen before.  This morning at 4AM I chased them on 40CW for about 30 minutes and made it through  yay!   The pileup was not that big. They were spread up  from about 2KHZ to 10KHZ.  We've seen much bigger pileups as you said with the 3C and 3B7 operations.  Katy bar the doors !  hihi.      With the panadapter I was able to track his contacts as he moved up the band.  For awhile I would call on the same freq as his last contact and was not making it.  Then tried moving my transmit freq a tad higher each time because he was slowly moving up. (duh!)   That did the trick.   

 

You would think that the piles would be bigger but looking at the Voacap charts for various places across the US most of them have poor predictions compared to us on the west coast.  They aren't hearing them very well.   Plus a lot of old codgers like myself are trying to learn the Fox/Hound system.  I still havent got the hang of it hihi.

On 2/16/2014 5:10 AM, Rich Holoch wrote:
> I did this because I had been reading the efficiency of what the 
> MA160V would be (in the ON4UN Lowband DX-ing Book) - and I think it 
> was maybe 25%. I figured adding more metal in the vertical would 
> increase efficiency and also add more "capture area" for RX. This does 
> seem to be the case. It's a surprisingly good RX antenna as well as TX.

I don't know the height of the MA160V, but there's a graph in the talk that
I did at Pacificon two years ago, and at a recent NCDXC meeting that clearly
shows the benefit of making that vertical taller. Short antennas have low
radiation resistance (that's the "good" resistance that accounts for
radiated power), and it's in series with the ground resistance. That causes
two bad things to happen.

First, because the radiation resistance is lower, the current in the antenna
must be greater to radiate the same power.

Second, those two resistors form a voltage divider, and the efficiency is
the ratio of those two resistors. The graph of radiation resistance is
lifted from the ARRL Handbook or Antenna Book, and your experience is
exactly what that graph predicts.

When we make a short vertical taller, or when we add radials, or both, we
are directly improving that voltage divider ratio.

The slides for my talk are at k9yc.com/publish.htm as a pdf file.

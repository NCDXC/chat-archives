On Mon, Apr 14, 2025 at 4:41 PM Bill Kendrick <kendrick.w@ca.rr.com> wrote:

I had a great time. With my mobility issues the elevator outages were a problem. The food was convention food but we go to see people we haven’t seen in some time and the best part for those who have seen hundreds of presentations is chewing the fat with oldies… of which I am now included. I met many people face to face for the first time that I have worked in or competed against in contests, and pile ups. That was really my sole purpose in going… and winning prizes… to give away! (Darn! I need to win a grand prize so I can stop buying all those tickets!)

 

I have to say that when I was younger I was awe struck and intimidated by the giants of the hobby. Nowadays, I feel like part of a bigger congenial community... which include many of those giants and new giants. It is my perception that has changed.

 

As far as the future, it is a sad fact that this hobby is now one of many others that competes for the newbies. If you note most new members are those who were interested when they were young but too busy until they retired. Hopefully a resurgence in STEM will garner some new blood. Then again, even in my time ham radio in general was a niche activity.

 

Next year is after the holidays except the Orthodox Easter which is 10 days after Easter.

 

Good on all of you for pulling off another successful convention.

 

See you next year

Bill N6RV

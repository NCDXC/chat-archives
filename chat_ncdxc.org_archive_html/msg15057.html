I notice that the LOTW QSL from 3B7A includes
"<APP_LoTW_GRIDSQUARE_Invalid:6>LH93SE_//_user-specified_value"

This online map page http://www.levinecentral.com/ham/grid_square.php
maps 3B7A to LH93tk, which is north of St. Brandon, in open water.

That map page shows LH93se to be the correct position, as reported by
3B7A. Perhaps DXCC has a wrong position for 3B7.

In other LOTW QSLs received in 2018, I have seen:
"<CQZ:2>04 <APP_LoTW_CQZ_UserInvalid:1>5"
"<ITUZ:2>06 <APP_LoTW_ITUZ_UserInvalid:1>7"
"<ITUZ:2>08 <APP_LoTW_ITUZ_UserInvalid:1>2"

In an older LOTW QSL received in 2012, "<CQZ:2>34 <ITUZ:2>19
<GRIDSQUARE:6>QO59jn", it appears that the CQZ and ITUZ were
incorrectly reversed by the UA0, but the fields were not flagged as
invalid. Perhaps this additional checking was implemented after
Feb-2012. I noticed this because the WAZ award correctly showed that I
was still missing CQZ 34, and I was surprised to see it in one of the
QSL records.

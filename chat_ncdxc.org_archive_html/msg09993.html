The folks that go on dx expeditions, with StepIR's, just remove & install the fiberglass arms on site--and leave the EMU units on the boom--   all the time. Its a 10 minute operation to either put it together or take it apart. Even less if you don't do anything with the arms themselves.
Its a very easy job with one and two EL antennas. Talk to one of them.
The trombones make the arms very unwieldy and will likely require taking the arms and sweeps apart.  Now that is a big deal fraught with many potential problems. IMO only the very brave will do that.

Hey Jim;

I'm a 185 pound weakling, and maybe I'm also part of a wimpy FD group.  hi hi
 
Still, a 37 pound Steppir (2-element with the 30/40 option) is not something I'd want to have to put onto and remove from a tower multiple times a week.  Hence my suggestion of the folding hex, which is roughly 13 pounds and about the same gain (within a dB or so) on 20-6M.
 
So I guess the bottom line comes down to whether or not having a rotatable dipole on 30/40 is worth having to hoist the extra 24-ish pounds on a regular basis.  If 30 and/or 40 are favorite bands for you, then the answer may well be yes.

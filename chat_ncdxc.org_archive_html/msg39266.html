I went through far too long a period where the HAM III ring gear would break. Every three years or so it had to be replaced. It got to the point where I'd keep a spare HAM III or a T2X on hand so only one trip up the tower was necessary. And there was minimal downtime with the mast stuck in one direction. The broken rotator was then sent off for repair and put back in reserve.

After just too many of these cycles to recall I installed an M2 Orion rotator with a Green Heron controller. More expensive in the short run. That was 18+ years ago without any need for replacement since. Long run savings in not paying someone to remove and install, then sending the broken unit out for repair yet again. DX Engineering now offers an equivalent rotator.

Whatever you install, be sure to bench check the it first.

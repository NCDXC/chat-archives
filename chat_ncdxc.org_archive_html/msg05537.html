Steve,

B/S on "100 guys"--you are thinking of some other expedition. The opening was on 24 January 1997. The path was grayline. The first guy to get through was in the Sierra foothills--real QRS, real old. I forget the call.

 I was second. The Q's marched NNE along the grayline ---landfall was Monterey Bay---and ended near the antipode in VE5. I remember a total of 11 or 13 successful candidates. The log can verify or dispute that.

The OTHER opening--for the east coast--put many in the log. But we had almost zero mutual darkness.

Most exciting topband QSO I ever had, or certainly up to that point. K4UEE told me he was in the shack with N6EK operating. Bob. A (UEE) heard my call on the speaker, but Bob F. did not. Bob A. took the seat and worked me--and it was off to the races.

The zone of death started east of the grayline--no mutual darkness. Our friends in AZ, NV, UT were locked out but a few were successful on 80, with its longer prime time.

That is my story and I am sticking to it.

Garry, NI6T

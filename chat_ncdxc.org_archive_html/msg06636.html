Have a look - it is good reading.
Yes, and much of it well done. It fails, however, to address what many of us consider the most serious weaknesses of our club -- it completely fails to give even the slightest possibility of participation in club activities by those FULL members who live outside the Bay Area. A quorum is defined as 20% of the membership, and for some actions, only 10%, and no provision is made for any form of participation in club business online.
Thus, no matter what we choose to call this club, in reality, it remains the Silicon Valley DX Club -- if you don't attend meetings in person, you have no voice in club business. Thus, a member who lives outside of Silicon Valley is a "Full Member" in name only.
NCCC, a club with a similarly wide geographical spread of membership, and also a high concentration in the Bay Area, works a lot harder at making members living outside Silicon Valley feel welcome. For several years, most meetings are available to any member via WebEx. That process has its technical challenges, most notably the availability of sufficient internet bandwidth at our various meeting locations, so some meetings have been more successful in this regard than others. Officers for the year were nominated and elected at our last meeting, and those members attending on line were permitted to nominate and vote. I also just finished a one-year term on the BoD. We met monthly via WebEx, usually the Monday before the regular membership meeting, and for an hour. This allowed full participation BoD by W6SRZ (near Petaluma), K6DGW (Auburn), N6DE and AD6E (San Jose), W1SRD (San Mateo) NS6T (Livermore), and me, near Santa Cruz. A few years ago, W6SX (Mammoth Lake) took a turn on the board, quite successfully so, until he had to resign for health reasons. Hank remains one of our more valuable and active members, and never misses a WebEx.
I urge those responsible for this re-write to give it another shot, with these considerations in mind.

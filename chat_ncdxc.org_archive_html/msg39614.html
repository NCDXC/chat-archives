Before WSJT-X was modified years ago to handle compound callsigns like KH7AL/KH9, whenever you double-clicked on a callsign like that, the software would call using "KH7AL", because it wasn't able to add the "/KH9". Since there wasn't a reason not to, and to provide backward compatibility, they left in the ability to call KH7AL/KH9 with either "KH7AL" or "KH7AL/KH9". So, your calling him with his complete callsign wasn't an issue for you, Brian. You just experienced the same bad luck I had.

73, Greg N6PM

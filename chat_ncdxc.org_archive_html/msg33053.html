Hi Bill,

I posted to motivate DXing activity only.  I really only notice Marathon points starting around Nov, it's not a focus for me.

Rusty had a GREAT year.  Clublog shows him at 277 DXCC entities (which DO matter to me) for 2023, plus whatever CQ Marathon allows.  That's higher than some folks lifetime counts, impressive.

IF I were a NCDXC member, I'd be #3 (257 DXCC as of last night) for the year (motivating me to do more, as if I needed more motivation?) on Clublog.  No, I have a life too and do not spend every waking moment in the shack hi hi.  I expect others have lives too.

I don't expect to win anything (the competition is REALLY stiff and better qualified) and that's not my motivation either (really, don't care about winning wallpaper)... I compete against me, but the DXCC 'scores' do act as a reference point for my progress (now at 320).

It has been a pretty darn good year.  The only places I don't have logged for the year are random (like Bolivia, Guyana, Ivory Coast) and some islands that haven't been activated in a generation and a couple that are highly improbable or never now (Afghanistan, DPRK).

Rusty and I are roughly at the same latitude in the Pacific north wet, which is not any advantage to say San Diego (except it's possibly quieter).  The prop is 'different' here, seasonal changes (sun angle on the planet) are a large part of that too.  We're either earlier, later, louder or not at all compared to the south (fun/frustrating).

There are other advantages to being up here, none of which have anything to do with radio but quality of life.

Happy New Year all, may 2024 bring the rarest and plentiful DX yet!

Rick nk7i

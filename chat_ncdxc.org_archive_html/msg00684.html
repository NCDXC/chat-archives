Yes, we are all spoiled and we sound it. The service is precarious just now. We cannot keep hammering on it and expect it to recover under load.
 
My idea is to back off for a while to let queues clear. And to set lower expectations for the current instantiation of LoTW. This could be done administratively and in a heartbeat.
 
Rich: Your are correct as far as you go. In fact, the raw input could be sorted into queues and kept at ARRL until their appointed day. The idea is to smooth out the arrival rate at the LoTW server and also to prevent busy-waiting.
 
We sometimes wait years for a confirmation. I can go back to waiting a month or two.
 
It doesn't solve the underlying problems of architecture, schema, query plan, provisioning, backup, recovery, .... But it does keep something usable available while we complain and pray that ARRL creates a better LoTW 2.0.

From: Tom Berson

 

As the server appears to be a scarce resource, one constructive approach would be to ration its use. Think of a gas pump during a gasoline shortage.

 

For example, assign days of the month to letters of the alphabet. On any day, allow uploads only from stations whose first letter of the suffix matches that day's letter. The extra days of each month allow no uploads.

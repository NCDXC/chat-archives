Not necessarily.  There would be other factors involved such as:

-the efficiency of the linear amp

-the QSO efficiency would be improved due to more power, less repeating of callsigns since their signal was weak

-you would need to have the linear run directly off of batteries and eliminate a power supply inbetween;  looks like the batteries they have are 50v or so batteries which would be sufficient.

 

But putting all that aside, if we make assumptions for a 500w amp:

-runs on 50vdc directly

-70% efficient, meaning to get 500w out you need power of approx. 715 watts

-on FT8, worst case 50% duty cycle (which is not really accurate, it would be less just like SSB is not steady state)

-then you need 368watts full time for one radio (not including receiver power and remote uplink)

-obviously you would need the battery storage capacity but putting that aside you still need to generate 368watts of power for 24 hours a day which would be approx 8800 watts per day.  So if you can rely on 8 hours of sunlight a day (and of course this varies too) you would need an 1100watt solar array.

 

Lots of generalizations and approximations I know, but 1100 watts solar array is certainly not unreasonable.  Thoughts?  Mistakes?

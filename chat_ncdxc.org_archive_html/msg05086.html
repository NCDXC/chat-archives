FT5XO was a lot like this current DX-pedition, except the pileups were
no-where near as thick thorny as FT5ZM. I remember bagging 80 through 17M
with the FT5XO DX-pedition with no problem - one after the other - and with
only short hatted vertical dipoles. DX-ing has become much more competitive
and "unruly" since even 2005!

Now - with a MUCH better antenna farm - and more than twice the power - it
will take me many days to do  with FT5ZM what I did with FT5XO in hours. 

Geographically and propagation speaking, VK0IR was a huge success, but it
was rough going for the West Coast. 

We have some very cool plans to make sure that VK0EK will do the West Coast
(very) proud. 

You can blame 1997 and any West Coast difficulties on the mountain "Big
Ben". 

For VK0EK - Big Ben won't be "in the way"  . . . .  News at 11 . . .

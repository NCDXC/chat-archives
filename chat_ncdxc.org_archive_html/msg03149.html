Jim,

I guess I qualify as a DXer as I have recently achieved honor roll and as an OF because
I soon will turn 68. I am a member of NCDXC. From this I conclude I must be one of the
dudes you are addressing in your diatribe. Therefore I feel compelled to respond.

I find that I, along with many others I have gotten a great deal out of my association with
NCDXC. In 1998 I was re-licensed after a long period of inactivity due to work and family
priorities. I always liked to design and build my own equipment but I was never much of
an operator. When I joined NCDXC, there were 6-8 other OF's (I was a YF back in 2000)
that met at Harry's Hofbrau in Redwood City. There was not much in the way of programs
or focus in the club at that time but there was a strong interest in DX. I learned a lot about
how to operate, the value of listening over transmitting and wonder of wonders the need for
cw. I begin to get my code speed back and soon got an extra class ticket to be able to operate
in the phone and cw segments of the DX bands. As YF's, Steve Jones N6SJ and I felt that NCDXC
could again take wing and grow again. All that was required  to rapidly grow our membership
was good programs at each meeting, better publicity to attract people to come, eliminating
the DXCC requirement for full membership and adding a raffle to spark interest. OF's and YF's
alike seemed to enjoy the club meetings and I guess they still do based on membership surveys
taken every now and then say so. In the process of growing the club Steve and I held every just
about office and put in many hours at board meetings. I found that the OFs had many great
ideas and were great sources of club history and tradition and pride. Mixing old and new ideas
was a good recipe for a proud old club. IDXC is another key part of what makes NCDXC great. Every
other year  members put in countless hours putting on the convention in Visalia. Guys like
W6YD, N6SJ, K6YP, K6TD and K6MM have chaired the event since 2003. These guys have each
recruited great teams and put on a convention that has been well received by its attendees.
The convention has grown to over 800 folks and revenue from the DX convention makes ever
growing support for NCDXF and individual DXpeditions possible. Each new group of club
officers, board members and IDXC chairmen have taken the club in new directions and expanded its
focus and horizons. Each, I believe has listened to members requests and ideas, and worked hard
implement them as well as to attract new members. The DXer and the club web site are well served
by folks like W6PK and K6MM. W6OOL has done a super job of updating W6TI, the club repeater.
NCDXC is a far better club today as a result of the efforts of dedicated members each contributing
their time and effort.

Jim, there is a point in the message above. In any club you have to give in order to receive. I
remember each of your excellent talks on ameliorating RFI and how to best use of ferrite materials.
These were of great interest and value to members. I also remember that you decided never to return to
meetings at Holder's because you were miffed that the restaurant needed to close and send its employees home
at 9:30 pm thus cutting your presentation a bit short. I do not recall that you have ever stepped up to serve the
club as an officer or director. Nor do I recall your participation in making IDXC happen. An indeed you have not attend
many meetings at Holder's.

Jim, you are a very bright guy, and and excellent engineer. You have much to contribute to NCDXC.
Please if you have things to say, come to meetings and say them. If you want to change the direction
of the club run for office. These are the ways to build a better club. Of course your ideas may conflict
at times with those of other members but that is how a democracy works. Hurling rocks at NCDXC
and its members simply identifies you as a complainer and not a doer. I invite you to to start doing
and cease being a grumpy OF.

Best 73,

john k6yp

Good luck in getting someone to help out.  I have been fighting with them for 30 years on and off.  My last big push was 10 years ago and I still have 10 cell numbers of the techs and their supervisors.  Closest I got to real help was a guy that drove 35 miles to get here, heard my noise in my shack on my receiver, connected his analyzer to my antenna and confirmed what I called about.  He made one attempt to call in work on a pole and then left he district and that was the end.

Call the 800 number and they take the report, ask you to create a log and say they will investigate in 5 days.  I started a new case two weeks ago and am still waiting for their report.  I need to call and complain and ask for the super for the tech to call and make sure he knows it is serious.  As Rich said, they may call out the work but it gets done when they have time.  Get s storm to do some damage to your area and that goes first.  No telling how long but likely a couple months.  You may or may not see a change so the cycle starts again and can go on forever.

I have not tried the PUC yet.  Once when there was a wide area PGE outage, I hooked up my rig to the generator and the noise floor was the best I’d ever heard.  Never heard it that way since.  If it were killing me a lot of the time, I would be all over this and be calling every few days.

Al

PS: You can try to hit a suspect pole with a good sledge hammer and it may shake things up and confirm it has an issue.  It could also make it improve but will not fix the problem.

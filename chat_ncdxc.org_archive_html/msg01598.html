My view of a "good" DXpedition is:
 
1) Did they activate a truly rare entity, or was this a common entity such as Market Reef, Aland Island, Ogasawara etc.
2) Did they coordinate their QRV so as to provide fair and equitable contacts for all geographic regions, ie did they fill the need or did they leave with an unfulfilled need?
3) Did they operate on all bands and modes?
4) Did they provide a means to check my contacts in the log?
5) Did they share photos, details of their challenges and hardships?
6) Did they make an effort to work Zone 3?
7) Were the operators able and competent?  Did they include any first-timers?
8) What were the challenges, participation costs of the Operators?  Were tremendous sacrifices made to bring this DXpedition?
9) Did the Operators make an extra effort to work me?  If they got a partial call, did they stick with it and pull me out of the pileup or did they just move on?
 
These are just some of the factors I use when thinking about a "good" DXpedition.

A very appropriate question for this week....thanks Paul  

From my perspective, confirming that you're in an online log prevents dupes and makes sure that your call was received correctly.  For me, it's a good feeling that I know I've made the sweep or I got 'em on 160m or whatever.  

Secondly, this business of OP's calling out EU only or JA's working only JA's or avoiding the west coast when propagation is favoring a good path.   Huge frustration with the Clipperton OP calling "UP EU" with no break for anyone else.  I mentioned that to our Pilot and he tried to send word to the crew to allow breaks for the rest of us, but it didn't change anything.  I was watching comments from hams in other parts of the world not getting a chance to work the Clipperton guys and it wasn't kind.

Finally, with the increasingly high cost of international postage, mail theft and the overwhelming task for any QSL manager to reply to thousands of card requests, I think LOTW makes sense.  I would prefer to see and OQRS/LOTW where you can make a donation to the DXpedition and then get the LOTW credit within a few days.  That would not only support the DXpedition with a donation, but get your LOTW credit at the same time.  

I like to read the daily blog and see photos to get a personal feel for what the crew is doing (Clipperton was a good example of this). 

Bill, N6BM

Good question.  I'm pretty concerned about not only working DX, but just ham radio in general.


As the baby boomers continue to retire, ham radio should continue to see growth.  But once that ends, say around 2035, I expect the hobby to drop like a rock.  Compound that, with ever-growing commercial interests, and it doesn't paint a good picture for amateur radio.  Young folks today have little-to-no interest in the hobby.  I know, there's always exceptions.  And hopefully, when those exceptions arise, we can provide as warm a welcome to them, as has been afforded to me.


I love the idea of a remote station for the club.  In fact, at the last San Mateo Amateur Radio Club ( http://www.w6uq.org/ ) meeting that I attended awhile back, the idea was floating around of purchasing a small lot up on Skyline Road to serve as such a station.  It seems to me to be quite an investment for a small club to handle, but if for example, the NCDXC were to partner with some other clubs, a station as such might work out.  (Then we'd just have to handle how to divvy up operating time! :)


Over 50% of new residential construction is under some form of HOAs or CC&Rs, so it would certainly be well worth it to those of our members who have to contend with that.  Even for folks like myself, what I can do with my antennas is still highly limited by city ordinances.  So at least for this one operator, I'd love it.


73 All!

On Jan 27, 2013, at 3:02 PM, chat-request@ncdxc.org wrote:

Here is your NCDXC Question of the week:
 
 
What do you think DXing will be like in 5-10 years:
 
 
 
 
 
My response: I have to admit that I am worried about the future of DXing in the future. A lot of us are not getting any younger and the young guys are just not diving into amateur radio. I also worry about the limitations/impact placed upon future DXers by Home Owner's associations, deed restrictions, CC&R's and the trend towards apartment living for our younger folks.
 
It is not all doom and gloom however. Radio, antenna and accessory technology is better than it has ever been. I am very intrigued by remote access technologies via the Internet. Some radio clubs are setting up Internet remote HF transceivers for their club members to use and I definitely think that is the way of the future.
 
I think the DXCC program will evolve and we will see new countries on the horizon. I predict that entities such as P5, EZ and YK will see major DXpeditions within the next five years. I hope I am right!
 
73,

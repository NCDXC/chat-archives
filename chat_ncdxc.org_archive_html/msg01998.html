Thank you John and Kevin for having the guts to move the convention to such a wonderful venue.  The location was great in many respects... Downtown was within walking distance, the facility was large and clean and had numerous large restrooms.  Parking was not a problem...   Even with all of the foot traffic between rooms, it didn't seem crowded - the flow problems of the Holiday Inn did not materialize. All of the people that I talked to at the Marriott Hotel and at the convention facility seems very pleasant and helpful - much appreciated.  And the food was much better than previous years - Thank You Again!!!

The only complaint I heard was that the vendor space was too far away.  Swapping the banquet location with the vendor location would have put them in the stream of traffic.

Like others, I was also put off by the statements about the SCDXC's need for $$$ over and above the paying public's needs and comfort.  He didn't seem to care what was said about keeping the current location - just $$$.  I believe there should be some profit motive for the clubs and I am willing to support that with additional funds, but please don't make it the only reason to host the convention.

This year's was a very high quality convention - the best that I have attended, and I have been to a bunch.  The bar has been set quite high, I hope the SCDXC will meet and try to beat this year's successful convention.

A big Thank You to all that helped make this convention fun and entertaining.

Bill
WB6JJJ



On Apr 22, 2013, at 2:38 PM, "Bob Lanning" <Bob.Lanning@verizon.net> wrote:

I want to add to Kevin’s remarks about the viability of IDXC and the VCC.

 

In NCDXC’s manual called the Procedures Manual, it is clearly stated that regarding the finances of the IDXC, the goal is to break even.  No mention of profit, just break even.  The IDXC is for the DX’er – members of NCDXC or not.

 

The club has enjoyed a nice net profit in recent years but not always.  As a member of the board, we knew there was a risk moving to the VCC but in our judgment it was worth the experiment.  This was especially true because of the proven talent of the Co-Chairs (John Miller K6MM and Kevin Rowett K6TD).

 

Sure – it is nice to have a net profit but the goal of the IDXC is to provide the best benefit to the DX Community, not the club’s bank account.

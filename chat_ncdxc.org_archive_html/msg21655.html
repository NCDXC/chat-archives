Isn't the hockey stick on the solar flux amazing? We went from 70's to 110 in less than a month.

Not sure how well this will bode for FT8 though. 20/30/40m greyline activity shows an almost solid wall of stateside traces on the 2.4KHz-wide waterfall. Trying to pull out your < -20db DX signal is getting harder and harder it seems. Then I'll look at the spectrum scope and see a massive line on the FT8 frequency and next to no signals on the rest of the first 100KHz of the band.

I feel the debates about FT8 are really not practical as both sides have very valid points. But as it seems to have a black-hole effect, including on yours truly (the "if everyone else wants to use an aluminum bat then by God so will I" thought process) we'll need to be able to use more of the band to effectively handle the usage load. Not sure how as it would take years for say new gear with broader filters to replace the old equipment out there still limited to 2.4KHz.

Yes this contest should be really fun!

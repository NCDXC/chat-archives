On 8/29/2014 9:12 AM, Rick Bates, WA6NHC via Chat wrote:
Paul et al,

It occurs to me again that perhaps the current entity definition is nonsensical.  What qualifies a location as an entity should come under review.  It may not satisfy all DXers with a potentially smaller number of entities, but let's be realistic.

If for example, an island is uninhabited by humans, less than XX square miles and there is nothing on it but plants and critters (birds, crabs, fish); should it really qualify as an entity?  It should also be accessible regardless of tides.

In my mind, habitation by humans should be one of the qualifiers.  We know there is no place on earth that we can't access with a radio, so if there are flea specks of uninhabited islands in the South Pacific, is it really important to have worked them ALL?

One of the risks realized by allowing radio activity on some of the 'sensitive' areas is the fear of opening the flood gates to more visits by other groups/agencies.  That risk isn't trivial.  Reducing the number of entities to a more realistic view also resolves the lack of access issue, regardless of the cause (political, environmental etc.).

Rick, WA6NHC

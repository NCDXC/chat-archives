My 10 year old OpenSpot for DMR (original version) died, so it was relocated (trash) and replaced.  The new hotpot has more digital modes, but is lacking a feature that was very handy; translating any talk group to 'local 9' on demand; so I'm updating to a new rig to compensate (more channels per zone).

As a result, I have 2 DMR portable radios and a Baofeng HT for sale.  The Baofeng is not DMR capable but is also not the more common model.

The Baofeng UV82X HT (charger, spare battery and programming cable) is VHF and 220 (not 440).  $50 plus shipping to this group.  There is no 220 use here in N Idaho and I've sold off my other 220 and 900 gear.

Also one each VHF and UHF TYT MD380 HTs (each with charger, cable, spare batteries); FM, NFM and DMR on ham or business bands. $100 each plus shipping to this group.  

These are nice radios.  Good for DMR hotspot or repeater (and analog FM, 6.25, 12.5 or 25 KHz width); I used them on repeaters also when I lived in Mordor (formerly known as CA).

DMR is digital voice, via a repeater and/or network and used throughout the world.  You can DX or chat with locals, or both with an HT.

One REALLY nice feature of the TYT is that several 'zones' can be (computer or tediously manually) pre-programmed, so the user can setup for multiple locations and switch zones on demand (home, cabin, in-laws).  They also cover out of ham bands (legally) so could be used on GMRS/FRS (UHF) or business.

If one buys both of the TYT, (my preference of course, simpler) a total of 5 batteries (two are brand new with 50% more capacity).

Programming software is available online (free).

All are in great to excellent condition (I MIGHT have the original boxes somewhere) rarely used out of the house.  Photo/s on request.  Shipping labels will be made via PirateShip.com (which is about half the cost).

Funds via PayPal 'friends and family' preferred.   Responses off list please.

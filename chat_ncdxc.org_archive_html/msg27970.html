I can confirm that NOT changing tones can still get you the RR73 that you want.  Which is what I did in WSJT-X in normal mode last night (Wed).

The apps don’t appear to ‘expect’ or demand you on their tones, they listen to the spectrum (but do limit callers to above 1 KHz).  The main (original) point of keeping callers above 1 KHz was space that the weak signals could exchange the report with less interference (under 1 KHz); then move on faster.  Apparently my -10 was enough to beat down the hordes for the RR73.

Others have success calling BELOW the DX tones, if there is enough spectrum space (if the DX uses tone/s above 300 Hz.).  Since the variants came out, they all pretty much broke the Taylor team intent and standards.

Should you change tones; it’s up to you and your ability to make a loud signal.  You have only THREE calls for your report, then they move on.  Choose wisely.

The simplest is to use JTDX (which doesn’t always decode as well as WSJT-X) in F/H mode, tracking the DX cycle... confusing.

So ya takes yer chances.  We’re all waiting for the log of course to prove this out.  It’s an issue that should have been made clearer at the start.

From this point on; may I submit that the DX should announce “We will transmit on EVEN only for FTx modes”, is more accurate since so many options/variables come into play.  After that, dealers choice.

CW hasn’t been any simpler or cleaner with the up cops, tuners and general PITA lids (worst I’ve seen in decades).  The DX knows this (ALL too well) and are patient.

I almost dread phone when they get there (with no amps there, VERY challenging for everyone).

3Y0J is not using MSHV. They are using fox and Hound, as they have said for days. Unfortunately they are transmitting on the wrong sequence (odd). There are two ways you can call them on even.

1 use JTDX software in Hound mode and click on their call. JTDX allows you to be the Hound in either sequence. So you can call them even.

2 you can use WSJT but do not turn on Fox and Hound. You must be calling them above 1000 hz. If they come back to you then you need to quickly change your transmit frequency below 1000 hz but must be above their transmit frequencies. It’s tricky and you have to be quick but it can be done.

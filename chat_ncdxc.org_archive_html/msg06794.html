For what it's worth, I don't believe there is any 'kick-back' at play here with the booking of rooms for IDXC.

I recently retired as a lobbyist and consultant for some California trade associations.  I've been involved in the planning of some decent sized statewide conventions and conferences.  Hotels and convention centers have a "rack rate" for their meeting rooms and event halls, just as they do for the hotel sleeping rooms.  Convention business is the single largest source of profit for large hotels, just behind the profit generated by their bar service.  

Hotels charge very, very high prices for meeting rooms and trade show space, as well as for mundane things like coffee service ($25.00 per gallon of coffee isn't unusual.) And all support services  such as moving and setup of trade show floors, booth dividers, electrical, meal service, bussing dishes, etc. is all extra - prevailing union scale plus a minimum base fee.  

Hotels will reduce these fees for groups IF the convention organizer can guarantee a certain minimum number of "room nights" (number of rooms x number of nights paid for), and these must be booked in advance of the event through the organization sponsoring the event.  

Failure to meet the minimum room night count in your contract results in a penalty being assessed to the convention sponsor.  In many cases, this penalty can be based on the number of missed room nights times the prevailing hotel "rack rate" - sometimes more.  Penalty charges can also be assessed against fees for meeting room rental.  So, a group can get a discount on their convention space, as long as they are willing to risk STEEP penalties if they fail to "perform" per the terms of their contract.  

Smaller conventions (IDXC is small to mid-sized) have much less negotiating leverage than larger ones do.  Discounts are smaller, fees and penalties higher.  The fact that IDXC is able to get the space and rooms at the rates they do is a testament to their hard work.  

I don't think this constitutes a "kickback" in any sense, unless I'm missing something. It's all about a hotel generating the highest possible profit, plain and simple.

If that fee was required for the recent TX5K venture where I got as many band modes as I could (because it was easy, ending up with ~20) the QSL charges could seriously impact my coffee fund.  We can’t have that!  ;o)

 

Since I’m here, I don’t see any reasonable or acceptable way to limit dupes or band/mode QSO’s without impacting the rate/hour or manually limiting the QSL, no matter how sent, after the fact.  That puts a heavy burden on someone (team member or a volunteer) to keep it clean and clear and slows the QSL return down (impacting the monetary return).  It also opens the door for a MAJOR whine festival.

 

You can ask but frankly not expect worldwide acceptable behavior.   I hope for the best but am often disappointed.  Not everyone follows the same ethical standards.

 

The disadvantage of the current solar cycle is that there are many bands open to somewhere at any time.  The number of open bands could easily outnumber available team operators so geographical choices must be made.

 

If you generically limit the bands, there will be whining (“You didn’t do any 6 meter EME JT9”).  This makes it difficult for the team to deal with too and will impact covering the expenses.  The only answer to that is to clearly, frequently and strongly set narrow goals, before and during the venture.  Then repeat often, again.

 

The team has some hard decisions to make.  One is to decide if the signal:noise ratio is matched to their ability to grow a thick hide.  ;o)

But you’re forgetting “fake-it”. I just clicked a transmit tone of 4000 Hz and hit “tune”. WSJT moved the base transmit frequency of my radio up 1500 Hz. That resulted in an audio transmit tone of 2500 Hz, well within any radio’s transmit capability, but a received tone of 4000Hz. As long as a radio is capable of broad selectivity on receive, an increase of 50% in the number of callers can be accommodated. I’m enjoying the academic give-and-take…. OK Brain Trust, what’s wrong with that reasoning?

The pass band on XMIT is usually fixed and rolls off unless you are part of the ESSB crowd and have modded the radio.

On 5/27/2024 3:13 PM, eagleyemd@comcast.net wrote:

Set correctly. most radios, and probably all modern radios not stuck with a single 2.4-2.7 KHz filter do just fine with a broad passband, with use of an AM or FM filter. That brings up a question I’ve had since first operating FT8. In the initial publication introducing F/H mode in May, 2018, Joe Taylor wrote “Hounds make initial calls anywhere in the range 1000-4000 Hz”. Almost all of all have radios that can be set for a broader passband than we are currently using. That would open up an additional full KHz for calling. Does anyone have any idea why this never happened?

73, Bruce K3NQ

I tried Krylon clear as well and it broke down within a few year. If you really want to protect long term you need to use marine paint designed for fiberlass. This is the general consensus on the SteppIR board.  I used Interlux Brightside Polyurethane. Great stuff, but not cheap. A quart and a half easily covered the 28 SteppIR tubes with 2 coats of paint. Sea Foam green matches the SteppIR green pretty close if you care.

I suggest you join the SteppIR google group and search the archives for
"paint".  A lot of info should come up, since every steppir antenna has this
problem.  There is no single type of paint that is "optimum".  There are
tradeoffs with cost, difficulty of application, toxicity, etc.

It is not entirely clear if you can repair exposed fiberglass with paint, as
opposed to resin.

Discussion groups dealing with fiberglass boats may also be a source of
info.

On Sun, Jul 21, 2024 at 2:41 PM Richard (Rick) Karlquist via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
A helically wound vertical is based on wishful thinking.  It's just
a very long, small diameter coil.  Coils of this form factor are
VERY low Q.  The wishful thinking is the idea that somehow they
are less lossy than a lumped inductance IOW a conventional coil.
They only seem less lossy because they spread out the dissipation
over a long length and don't get hot.  Many builders of helically
wound verticals are tempted to use PVC pipe, because they can't
use aluminum tubing.  PVC pipe is a terrible structural material.
At least use fiberglass tubing.  Finally, it is an immense amount
of work to wind all the wire.  Good luck

Rick N6RK

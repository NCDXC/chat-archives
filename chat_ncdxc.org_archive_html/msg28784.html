Hi Rick,

I agree that the spotting networks and RBN (and HamAlert) are not always helpful.  I'm part of the reason why, I post to the email lists first, then to the spotting networks only when I feel like it.  If no one posts, no one else can win.  If everyone posts, instant overload, of the servers and the recipients.    (I also filter what spots I get, against what I've confirmed, so there isn't a lot of overload, just the slots I need).

There are three popular FT8 app versions; the original is wsjt-x, written by Joe Taylor K1JT with a team; the other two are branched variants.

JTDX_improved is the most current version of the more popular variant (Jan 23 latest issue).
MSHV is another (less developed) variant.

There are reasons to use a variant, but it's not important for the noob.

I suggest that you start with WSJT-X, which is now located on SourceForge.  Not all of the documents have been moved from the original site (no long available because Joe Taylor, retired).  It is the best, most documented version and receives updates more often, but you'll have to Google for the documents.

It isn't difficult to set up, presuming you have USB audio to/from your rig and a form of computer control of the rig (serial, USB or IP), just wander through the settings and most of them will be correct or obvious.  You enter your call and gridsquare on the General page.  The next part is the radio control page, then the audio page (looking at the USB drivers to the rig).

Get that far, try it to monitor and we can discuss it further.  The program(s) already know the dedicated frequencies for each band, you can use the drop down on the main panel (page) to get where you want to listen.

Once you have your rig and computer decoding JT modes (there are several) you can watch the activity and gain a sense of what the band is doing (turn your beam too, hi hi).  It proves, beyond a shadow of a doubt that VOA Cap (was a government project and) is hopeful, at best and often just wrong.  It's based on the hour of the day and two locations and reads from tables not the current values fast enough.

It IS important to have your computer time accurate; a discussion for another time (short version; I use Meinburg NTP on my system and a GPS based NTP server in the shack for all my devices, via my network).

For Singapore on 10M, mid afternoon to after our dusk has provided a few openings.  I don't have it on 10M but my 12M was right around midnight UTC.  This far north (my place) that window is less than an hour, on a really good day for the high bands.

Gl,
Rick nk7i

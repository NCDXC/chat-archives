Hello NCDXC friends, 

I am just back from Dayton Hamvention. 

It was really great this year!  Attendance and vendor participation were huge!

The Weather was great during the day, warm and humid on Friday with thunderstorms in the evening. Saturday was nice and clear with a good breeze. 

The Flea market was on overflow with lots of great stuff. 

The DX Dinner was on Friday night with Hal W8HC sharing his Alaska adventures. 
Robert S53R and Nodir EY8MM were inducted into the DX Hall of Fame. 
Marek FT4GL was awarded DXpediotioner of the Year for his Glorioso activation. N5J Jarvis Island Team won DXpedition of the Year. 

Our Tony K6BV won the grand prize of an ICOM IC-7610. 

There was a big party at the Hope Hotel afterwards with beer, pizza and other libations enjoyed. 

Saturday night was the Contest Dinner, very well attended with over 700 attendees including many Youth. DX Engineering and ICOM donated many great prizes. 

Flex Radio revealed the new Aurora series of radios. This will be a real game changer for DXpeditions. It is an 18lb box that contains a radio (two receivers) a 500 watt amplifier and a tuner. It is an 18lb RIB. They took many orders from among the DXpeditioner crowd. 

Kenwood had a new DM-705A Tri band D-Star mobile Radio. 

ICOM had the new 7760 and the PW2 Amplifier on display. 

Yaesu displayed the new FTX-1 Series of radios (HF/VHF/UHF)  They would not reveal if there was going to be a separation kit between the control head and the body of the radio. 

Everyone seemed to be having a great time!

73, 
Paul N6PSE

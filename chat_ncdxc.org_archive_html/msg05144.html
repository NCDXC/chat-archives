Paul,

That "1830" posting was followed shortly thereafter by another from the same K6. It said "freq.correction" and listed 18130. I think the conclusion is obvious--he mistyped the frequency; this is not a rare error. The only signal I heard (or saw on the P3) on 160 this morning other than my own was Rich, K7ZV up in Grants Pass. He worked the same DU.

I know it is only day 3 and I am not complaining. Two points motivate me here:
  • I fear the team might think that their well-attended pre-sunrise lowband activity covers all of NA; it does not, as we have no mutual darkness then. This presents the risk that our absence in the pile will not be noted.
  • Our opening would be in the hour and a quarter preceding our sunrise, short path---between their sunset and our sunrise. Basically, they need to work the Pacific time zone separately. Now is good because the K index is zero. Also, over the remainder of the operation, that period of darkness will shorten slightly.

As for your last point, according to chat on the  Lowband Chat--a popular hangout frequented by serious lowbanders-- no one heard them. That says more than just you and I not hearing them. Surely there would be a posting, even if only from a well-situated lowbgand station.

Garry

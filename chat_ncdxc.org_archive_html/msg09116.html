Guys,

Thanks for the info.  I was trying to sneak a check in from work and missed the QSO dates that were right in front of me on Clublog.

 

Will check later in the day or tonight. 

 

80 was a chore.  Using a BigIR I was running about 600W.  I noticed my SWR unstable which did not give me a good feeling.  The SWR meter finally cut the amp out when the reflected power got above 2.5:1.  Found a flat 10:1 across the band using the analyzer.  Went out and found the ground lead pressed, well melted, against the RF line between the loading coils and the antenna.  Split them apart and put the coil on the grass.  Went back to the shack and pretty sure I logged them about 20min later.  Seems the antenna got twisted when mowing the lawn.  I will probably try to repair it this weekend using  a new lead covered with high voltage heatshrink from work.

 

It would have been interesting to see the arcing under the antenna before the SWR got too high.

 

73s

-Aaron

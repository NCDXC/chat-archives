You say I don’t respect the member’s opinions by expressing my concern about the process and results of the 2012 Dxpedition of the Year. Not allowing the membership a choice in their President in November and eliminating an award without membership input are examples of the board’s lack of respect for the membership.

That is because you are new to the board and as such, you have not seen my previous email messages to the board (on the board reflector) suggesting ways to improve the selection process of the Dxpedition of the Year. The board refused to discuss this topic in our board meetings and it was bounced from agenda to agenda month to month.

You write that the board has been listening but I’ve seen no sign of it. That is why I resigned from the board in November. They would not work in a collaborative or productive fashion and my views, ideas and suggestions were generally rejected. I had joined the board 18 months earlier, full of ideas and enthusiasm to make the club better. The acrimony and dysfunction on the board stripped that away.

I agree with you that there is much apathy in the NCDXC but the membership does care about the actions of the Board. Many of the members share their concerns with me privately. They don’t feel that there is a safe & engaging environment for which they can share their concerns.

Many of these same members are encouraging me to run for Club President. I have to disappoint them. I would only want to lead this club if I could bring about positive and constructive improvements to the club. Keeping things the same is not of interest to me. To bring positive changes, there has to be good will and collaboration on the board. While there are some great individuals on the board, collectively good work is not getting done. I hope that good people can bring about positive & constructive changes.

While I’ve expressed various concerns to the board, I’ve avoided engaging with specific personalities or getting personal. I’m disappointed that you feel the need to do so with your comments.

Andreas, I had high hopes for you when I learned that you would be joining the board as my replacement. We can all be a part of the solution or a part of the problem. You’ve just shown your hand.

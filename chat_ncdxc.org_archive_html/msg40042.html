So, I changed the Subject of this discussion to ARRL-LoTW.

Firstly, I hope no one reading my comments thinks that I'm advocating for the ARRL, until 2 weeks ago I wasn't an ARRL member.
 
What happened 2 weeks ago?
Some unknown person or persons processed an ARRL membership application in my name / call sign.
ARRL can (will) not tell me who paid for the membership, all they will say is it was processed at Friedrichshafen.
Well, that narrows it to 2 or 3 people who participated in a discussion, but that's just a guess.
Maybe they'll pay my car lease payment due next week?

No question about it, hams have opinions about the ARRL, and unfortunately ARRL bashing tends to dominate any discussion.

Thanks for your comments. I still think a user fee structure is one way to deal with LoTW.   User fees / subscriptions are infiltrating our way of life and seem to be gaining traction. If you've bought or leased a new car in the past few years you'll understand that comment. I'm familiar with Ford, General Motors, and Toyota, each hawk subscription services for their brand. Software as a Service is more prevalent, annual fees for software plugins are the norm for WordPress and Joomla.

DXpedition confirmation fees aren't going down, most likely they'll be going up. Fifteen bucks for a glass of wine is becoming the norm, ten+ bucks for a beer isn't unusual. LoTW is really the only game in town, if it goes away something else will replace it, and there will be fees.

Hams are an interesting lot. They'll spend tens of thousands on a station and complain about 5 bucks for a confirmation.

Personally, I have no confidence in the ARRL's ability to manage the issues.

I tend to lean slightly in NK7I's direction (without the drama), although I would argue his use of the words"bounty for a DXpedition card".
Rick, look at it as a "fun fee" for your having "fun" chasing the DXped across the bands / modes.
Sometimes we forget that someone else is subsidizing our "fun".

Cheers,
GS K5GS

I see the benefit in thinning the pileup to make a higher rate but early in a DXpedition or later on?  What seemed to me the random nature of jumping around served to keep everyone sitting there listening and not knowing when their turn would come up.  I do not have the time so left and may have missed my chance.  Just wondered if it was effective in other’s minds.

 

I’ve never worked the business end of a pileup and hope to be given a chance someday.  I suppose the experience of the op may make a difference in pileup management and picking out a piece of a callsign and making it into a QSO.  He might want to stick with his experience and let things fly.

 

Thanks for the feedback.

I was listening yesterday around 01something and was delighted in hearing good signals from South Georgia.  The rate was pretty slow and not sure if he was keeping an intentionally narrow window, 7175 to ‘just under 7185’ (to avoid interfering with a net on 185.  It must have been hard to copy but what I heard was not a massive pileup.

He then went to ‘by the numbers’: 4x fours, then on to 4x 3s.  I left the shack figuring it would be a while before the sixes came up.  When I came back, he was on 8s going to 9s.  I was confused about the pattern or lack of one. Then, he opened it up for anybody and I managed a contact at 0211.

 

I wonder if it is better to do the numbers early or later on in such a DXpedition.  I do not recall any recent operations doing it this way.  What y’all think?

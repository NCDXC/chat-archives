Just to be clear, NCDXC “chat” is the only forum where NCDXC members can communicate with each other.
 
NCDXC “announce” is restricted to a few board members who may make announcements to the membership. While only members may subscribe to announce, at the behest of the membership, the board decided a few years ago to allow anyone to subscribe to NCDXC “chat”. While we unusually discuss DX, DXpeditions, DX Spots occasionally there is some “laundry” as there is simply no other place to discuss it. That is what we are doing now.
 
I think that Rick WA6NHC makes some valid points and as a person who had considered joining the NCDXC,  the board ought to pay attention to his points. No one should just be summarily dismissed whether they are a member or not.
 
As a member of this club, who was once passionate and excited about the Club and who served on the board for 18 months, I too find myself questioning my future involvement and role within the club. I feel that the club has changed and what is important and passionate for me is not important to the current leadership of the club. While I served on the board, I found myself making suggestions and passionate arguments for improvements. I was frustrated and felt dismissed by this board which is why I stepped down.
 
Now then, if one of the most excited and passionate members such as myself can become so disenchanted with the club, is that solely my problem or perhaps is there a bigger problem going on?
 
I feel that at the very least, the leadership is tone deaf and really ought to listen to the members and the potential members. The board recently surveyed the membership and sought their opinions about providing input to the DXAC. That was a good thing to do. Why not ask the members about eliminating an award?
 
Where will it stop?    Would the board eliminate further awards, or eliminate the Holiday luncheon or Summer BBQ without input?   I would certainly hope not but at this point its anyone’s guess. This club could be so much better and could offer so many more features and services to its members. It should start by listening to everyone.
 
Sincerely,
 
Paul N6PSE

On Feb 4, 2014, at 5:18 PM, Rick Bates <happymoosephoto@gmail.com> wrote:

A fine attitude.  If you don’t want public comments, leave it out of the public forum.  The last I heard, this email reflector was for DX posting and related commentary, not linen cleansing.  There is a club forum for things specifically club related; this isn’t it.
 
I thought about joining NCDXC.  I debated it for quite a while because the club HAS done a lot of good things for DXpeditions, education and more.  Through donations and similar a lot of good has been done and our hobby has benefited.  While it wouldn’t and shouldn’t be a driving force for any DXpedition, being named “Of the Year” for almost anything is at least recognition of ones effort by ones peers.  I’ve appreciated all this and want(ed) to be a part of it.  Then I watched the club itself, how it treats membership, how it thinks and how it responds.
 
One of my PRIMARY reasons for not joining is that the Northern California in NCDXC apparently means south bay and a few surrounding areas, NOT the entirety of Northern CA.  That’s a misleading name and a misrepresentation for the rest of Northern CA.  Yes, this is an old argument which you neatly put to bed in much the same manner as the DXpedition of the year commentary; instead of improving things you killed it.
 
Along with that is the insistence that meetings are evening only in the south bay (rarely east bay) making it really difficult for someone else from outside the bay area to attend without a hotel reservation.  It’s a 3 hour drive for me each way and I’m not willing to do a 6 hour drive at night, in particular on any Friday night.  But since most of the members are south bay or peninsula, having a meeting away from the bay area isn’t convenient for the rest of the group, which <big surprise> limits membership to only the south bay area.  I asked for a meeting elsewhere; it was briefly discussed here; lethargy prevailed.
 
I’ve suggested (here) social gatherings such as picnics (family events), weekend or full day seminars (small lot antennas, how to DX more effectively, whatever), RV gatherings and similar in the past (that would be family related, bringing up new hams).  I’ve suggested that the (meager for NorCA) repeater coverage be enhanced by the addition of IRLP (a cheap computer plus internet) and a reflector channel (free or small donation), which could allow a LOT more listeners (perhaps even discretely while at work) and DX spotting.  These things would garner new members and make the club more accessible to the rest of Northern CA.  I’ve received NO response from those ideas, nor have I seen anything that indicates any reaction at all; null response.  The status quo is apparently, enough for the board.
 
I’ve already been part of ham clubs including board membership and when they get in this rigid mind set of “we’re always right; we’ll do it only this way”, it’s time to be somewhere else.  Inflexibility kills; you can’t fix stupid.
 
Thanks for the fish, um, email reflector though; it’s been very helpful in my search for the next ATNO.  If I could hear the repeater (I’m near Placerville), that would be helpful too.
 
I’ll go back to monitoring (for the most part).
 
73,
Rick nhc

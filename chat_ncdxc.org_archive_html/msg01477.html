On 3/6/2013 10:48 PM, Jim Abraham W6EB wrote:
Wish I could say the same for TX5K, two hour listening on 40 cw tonight, rate of 30 estimate with lots of people calling, but operator only wanted EU but didn't say so.. Some good folks in the group, but definitely some poor attitude EU ops.
I've heard a weak op at TX5K only once or twice, and not for long. Most are VERY good. They are favoring DX when propagation favors it. Remember -- TX5 is only 2,000 miles from us, and within about 3,000 miles of most of the US. The times to get them on the lower HF bands is after the band has closed to EU (EU sunrise on 160, 1 hour later on 80, and another hour later on 40), and early mornings when they're working Asia. I've played the clock, and they're in my log 16 times QRP. Six of those Qs were SSB. Late this afternoon, i got them on 15 SSB as a JA run was winding down, and on 80 as things were slowing down to EU about a half hour ago.I did the same thing last night on 160, and N6XI was right behind me.

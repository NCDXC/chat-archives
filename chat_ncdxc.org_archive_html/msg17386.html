On Jul 9, 2019, at 10:09 PM, AL_N6TA via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

9K2HN is a pretty frequently heard signal on the bands and I have managed six QSO with Hamad, both phone and CW.  He is a good operator on CW.  He confirms via LoTW and my most recent QSO was on 80M for a new band for 9K.  Since he has confirmed all my QSOs already, I was surprised to received two cards in the mail today re-confirming all of my contacts.  Postmarked from Santa Ana, CA.  Nice card showing why he is pretty easy to hear and work: Yagis on towers on top of a building.  No idea who sent the cards!
 
Next, I have been casually working on WAZ.  I have worked all Zones but have wanted to confirm all 40 on all 8 bands, phone and CW.  One that I thought would be pretty easy is Zone 18.  18 is west of the most eastern part of Russia.  After looking at all my old cards from Box 88 in days past, I had a ton of UAs on 80 and 40 but no Zone 18.  One noted DXer said I should just get on in a contest and work both bands like it should be easy.  Tried twice on CW major contests but failed in that way.  However, I did manage to work Nick, RW0UM on 80 in 2000 and then twice on 40 CW early this year.  There was hope!  I’d been trying to confirm the 2000 QSO for quite a while but got no answers to emails or letters.  Today, I got Nick’s card for all three QSOs filling my Zone 18 vacancies!  Really happy about that.
 
My next project is to figure out why my card to HZ1HN was returned as undeliverable with the Zip code crossed out.  I researched Saudi Arabia postal system and how they Zip code and it was inconclusive.  So, I need help of some sort.  I sorted out all the spots from the time frame around my 10 M SSB QSO in 2012.  I then looked for who was most active and then used QRZ.com to get addresses and their zip codes.  I managed to find another HZ in the same city and will ask for his help.  Will see how that one goes.  I am not expecting to have another 10 M opening to HZ in the near future…  It was interesting to find that most all of the email addresses are callsign@hotmail.com.
 
Lastly, I thought I was doing well to finally get four 8R1 QSOs confirmed by Esmond so I figured I should try  some other 8R needs.  I still need 15 SSB confirmed but have not been able to work anyone that will QSL.  I gave up on my 1972 8R1Z QSO ever being confirmed so started working on 8R1Z.  That was a solid contact in 2013 where I started with the bureau.  I did not bother checking if there was even one at that time.  So, I started sending emails that were not answered.  I then sent my card and SASE and GS.  No joy so I sent a home made 8R1Z card with the SAE and USD again.  It just came back as undeliverable.  The USD was missing…
 
So it goes as I keep on trying to confirm those ‘rare’ ones.
 
CU all later es 73

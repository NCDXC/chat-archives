Most antennas have served their purpose, but the one antenna that didn’t work well for me was a Butternut HF2V (40M/80M) vertical. NOT because it isn’t a good antenna (it is), but the soil here isn't very good. It's hard adobe and in the summer it’s like concrete. I currently use a doublet for 30M-160M. It’s very versatile, low loss with the ladder line, and the tuner is remote out to the feedline. I have a large shop on my property that’s made out of metal. I’m thinking of putting up an inverted-L (or more likely a “t-configuration”) for 160M on the top of the shop and use the building as a counterpoise. Probably use a 50’ pushup fiberglass mast for the vertical section. Still researching it (ideas welcome!) and will get going on that project soon.

-Greg NY6C

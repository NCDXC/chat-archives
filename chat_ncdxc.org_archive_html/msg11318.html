The op (team) also recognizes that some areas are innately more challenging than others.  40 is somewhat unique in that it covers a VAST amount of the globe at once, unlike 20 (for example) which goes long, then shrinks again, self limiting in coverage.  40 is also a time of the day when most (even those employed) are home for the evening, more accessible is more crowded.

They stopped a few times for AF, OC, Alaska and VK/ZL then asked for 7's then 6's.

While they're weaker here than an hour ago, I jumped in a few ago during that pause and got slot 10 for South Georgia (on my long dipole and 600 watts).  (Yes, I'm a slot slut.)

I think that taking breaks like this is great (when it benefits me  :-D) and evens the playing field for us west coasters when the other areas have an advantage.  It's considerate, just like working band openings or gray line.  It also allows the others being 'ignored' to stretch, use the head, get a drink... which we tend to forget to do periodically anyway (good for our longevity).   And I'd bet that it's a pressure break on the DX end too, just work the loudest on the stated frequency.

Just an ESP hint of a whisper on 160 here though (see some calling) and no one from AK called in during the break, the limits of physics.

Good hunting,
Rick wa6nhc

He then went to ‘by the numbers’: 4x fours, then on to 4x 3s.  I left the shack figuring it would be a while before the sixes came up.  When I came back, he was on 8s going to 9s.  I was confused about the pattern or lack of one. Then, he opened it up for anybody and I managed a contact at 0211.

Paul,

That's unfortunate.

This is a large part of why I asked what's the reason to report the offset and even/odd recently and, particularl,y how were people using the info.    

It is not terribly useful on its own -- you are either decoding the DX or you're not.  If you are, you have the offset and even/odd.  If you aren't, you should hold short.

FT8 is no different than other modes  -- good practice says to listen for the DX before you call.   

I know Paul has far superior antennas that I have.   When he makes an FT8 spot, I only look at the band (and frequency but only for F/H).

I switch to the band.    I listen.  If I don't decode the DX, I do not call.  Besides being proper practice, what's the point anyway of calling?  

If I do decode the DX, I click and even/odd is taken care of.   (And if you like to Tx on the Rx, that's taken care of too.   I never.)

Paul, I have a suggestion that you are free to take or leave:   perhaps you still call the spot, but not report the offset and even/odd?   

-christopher

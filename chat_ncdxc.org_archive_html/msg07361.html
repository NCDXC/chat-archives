Larry, nice to see you at the NCDXC meeting on Thursday night.
 
It sounds like your 4 element SteppIR had a mix of the Fernco and “Brand X” boots. I understand that the Fernco are all they use now and they hold up well to UV.
 
When I took down my 3 element SteppIR late last year, my boots were in great shape as they had been taped, but the tape itself was a sticky mess. The green gel coat on the fiberglass elements was very thin/gone on the tops and the white fiberglass was exposed, but the elements were still quite strong. Overall I was impressed with the condition of my antenna.
 
When I put up my DB-36, I sprayed all of the fiberglass element tubes with Krylon UV clear paint. I did not tape the boots because they were the UV resistant type and they are cheap and easy to replace.
 
Overall, I’m quite happy with my SteppIR antennas.

Two of the "boots were completely demolished by UV. When I rotated the boom, so elements were vertical, both actually fell out. Both SS clamps were holding rubber under them just fine.
The other boots looked almost like new. I had not taped over any of the boots.  People, in areas of heavy icing, safety wire the two SS clamps to avoid this problem. I had always considered this overkill ---for sunny CA.  Not now. I have the wire/tools from my aviation days--takes 1 minute---so why not.
Lots of glass showing on the top side. UV resistant deck paint is on the schedule. Very inconsistent weathering element to element. SteppIR supplied cables are in good shape.  Yaesu G-2800 cable not so good.

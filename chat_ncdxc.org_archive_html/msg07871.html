Paul, there is a huge difference in interacting with the "man on the street" and the folks in power. I would guess that none of the guards at Abu Grabe were advocates of torture, but they were ordered to "subdue" their captives. And the same kind of thing happens in lots of countries where the folks at the top go nuts. As an extreme example consider Nazi Germany.
I would bet the average "man on the street" would be loath to criticize his government many of the countries you have listed. That is not true in the US, you can find severe critics of the government on both sides of the political spectrum. And you too, above. Yet you do not fear that the US will throw you in prison for your statements about its government.
It is hard to know just how bad N Korea is, but 60 Minutes, which is charged with making money for CBS (and little else), opined that many North Korean FAMILIES were imprisoned without charges or trial for decades. If that is so, that is a pretty sad state of affairs.

My belief is that all substances of any kind should be legal without prescription, and with appropriate warnings about the consequences of misuse.

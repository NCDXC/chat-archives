Thanks everybody for all your advice, encouragement and kudos on my DXCC.  All of this has been great, and has reinforced my decision to join NCDXC.  This is a great group, and is one of the most engaged and active of the ham clubs I have been part of.  

I enjoyed my first IDXC - especially the presentations by different DX-pedition participants.  I've always known that even a "minor" DX-pedition is a substantial undertaking.  But I had no idea just HOW substantial until I saw N0TG's presentation on Navassa Island.  All-in-all, a great weekend and one I hope to repeat. My compliments to the NC/SCDXC members who shouldered the planning and coordination of this event.

Thanks again & 73

Peter Gambee
K6TTD

Good morning NCDXC fellas,

How was your IDXC 2014?



I enjoyed catching up with friends and meeting new ones. The DX Forum was really good, they focused on Q & A with the DXAC members. Topics such as remotes, Kingman Reef, Kosovo were discussed. You can tell that the DXAC guys really care about the issues and want to do the right thing. A lot of thought goes into what is considered.

It was nice to see the NCDXC honor K4UEE with an honorary membership. It's good to see the club extend honors outside of the club.

There was some confusion as to whether their was going to be a NCDXC award for DXpedition of the Year, but apparently there was not.

The forum presentations were good but somewhat limited. There were not as many presentations as last year. I really enjoyed W4VKU's presentation on VU7AG.

It seemed that the raffle prizes were much less this year. Very few big ticket items. Last year's prizes were astounding. I could do without the Sunday morning game show "What's my Call" apparently I'm not the only one.

I learned of two different DXpeditions to very rare and exciting places being planned. Get your stations tuned up as you will really need these two!

73 for now,

Paul N6PSE

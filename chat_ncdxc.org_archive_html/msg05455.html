the repeater issue is far from being neglected or dropped. One specific proposed solution - the splitter idea - was dropped.  The board has decided to continue to investigate. The wording of the minutes is unfortunate, but I just gave you the intent of what happened. 

It was voted to not spend $923 on the proposed splitter upgrade and to keep investigating.

"The W6TI repeater was again discussed in light of the proposed additional equipment needed and potential reduction of signal strength for existing users. Russ, K6KLY, reported an estimated $923 needed for additional repeater coverage. A number of Board members reported on the very low usage of the W6TI repeater. The Board decided to continue to explore ways to increase repeater coverage but decided to drop the splitter idea because of the high noise floor and issues around the loss of capture . Russ, K6KLY, made a motion to drop consideration of upgrades to the W6TI repeater at
this time. Tony, K6BV, seconded the motion and the Board passed the motion with a unanimous vote. "

I just read in the minutes of the Jan BoD meeting that plans to solve
the problems of the W6TI repeater system receive coverage have been
dropped. The excuse was that it doesn't get much use. Of course it
doesn't -- many of us who want to use it can't access it because it's
DEAF. My 2M FM base station runs 160W through 1/2-in hard line to a 5dBi
antenna at 70 ft, the system is nearly always 30dB or better quieting,
but more than half the time I can't even bring the system up, and when I
can, I'm noisy into the system.

If there was ever any doubt that this is the South Silcon Valley DX
Tired Old Mens DX Club, this action is yet another confirmation of it.

73, Jim K9YC

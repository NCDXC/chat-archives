>> 2. MOCK DX expeditions :Planning Deploy and return. Like a field day I suppose but to some interstng locale.

There is an opportunity EVERY weekend of EVERY year but most call them a contest.  😜

Since much of any station involves a LOT of time and effort (while capability diminishes every year):
One station, member or not, offers up the time and space.  If the station needs prep work (any type, like new/more coax, antenna work, improved layout) that would qualify as planning and deployment.  The previous week/s would be used for the planning and improving, if needed; then in the day/s prior to the event, ATTACK!

The donated (for the weekend) station wins (workers put in time and effort), the workers win (teamwork, planning) and the team has a shot at winning the contest too.  Costs could be as simple as a few cases of beverage and some hot dogs or as elegant as the budget allowed.

Shall we supersize you antenna party?

Other options would include a YOTA/SOTA/IOTA effort, even if it’s not a ‘rare’ site (like Diablo, Tam or a few sites in the Bay Area).  It’s about learning; what works, what fails and teamwork.

One more would include a gathering of the EMCOM vans/trailers scattered around, to compare designs and plans, then operate as a team (instead of sitting idle for months collecting spider webs).

A WalMart parking lot (Costco?) would make it a public event (WITH WRITTEN PERMISSION), to draw in interested parties, perhaps to make new hams.

Given enough advance notice (for the articles to announced in various publications, then present for MUCH needed club letters or articles) the club/s would gain in fame/notoriety too for more added members.

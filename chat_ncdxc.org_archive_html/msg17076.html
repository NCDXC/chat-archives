Hi Chris,

I actually don’t think FT4 is applicable to a DXpedition.

FT8 has much superior weak signal work, and the fox/hound mode handles guys that “keep calling” even though they can’t hear atet DXpedition.

Working RTTY as a DX is very slow, because most callers overlap tones with other callers (this is the “split tones” problem).  RTTY decoders can’t deal with energy in-between the mark and space tones, despite the best efforts of twin peaking filters.  The result is - you can only work callers at the far ends of each passband, and the tailers.

The same is the case for FT4 and FT8.  The decoder won’t work so well with two callers on the same frequency.  Fox/Huond mode solves this by using frequency separation between the callers answering a CQ, and those the DXpedition are making a QSO with.

With Multi-stream, an AMP, fox/hound mode, and a decent antenna, the QSO rate for FT8 on a DXp is fast enough.  It would be a poor trade-off to give up weak signal performance.

Note - on KH1, we didn’t have agnate AMP, and the antennas weren’t all that great.  Yet, we managed to achieve a pretty high rate, and many QSOs.  There were 8000 callsigns that only worked KH1 on FT8.  I suspect many of those would not have been in the log without the weak signal abilities of the FT8 decoder.

I wouldn’t like to see a DXp use FT4.

FT4 is perfect for contesting, which is why it came about.  Let the games begin!


73, K6TD




On May 1, 2019, at 10:47 AM, Chris Tate - N6WM via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

All I have been doing some heavy testing of FT4, as you can imagine as a contester and someone involved in Dxpeditioning, this mode has gotten the attention of a number of folks myself included. 

I can share that this mode is quite fun to operate, the pace is such that I have been able to interleave qso's from multiple callers, sort of a natural call stacking capability, and the quick turnaround turns this into something that is quite appealing..   rate.  I am also trying to implement it on multiple bands simultaneously, first using my flex that has only 1 transmitter but multiple radio/antennas, then I will move on to attempting some kind of interlock.

The net net here is this is much more fast paced than any JT mode in prior implementations, along the speed of RTTY but with much less bandwidth and better decode capability and the ability to be productive with less than superstation/hilltop antennas.

Its all so new, but I think this will inevitably allow expeditioneers to increase the # of weak sig ATNO stations in the log, as well as providing a relatively engaging contesting capability.

Im pretty excited... I hope others are also.

Hello all, it's Truman N6TRU. I wanted to tell the members of the NCDXC club about something intriguing. Perhaps, some of you may have heard of BOINC (Berkeley Open Infrastructure for Network Computing), but for those of you who don't know: it's a system for volunteer computing which essentially acts like a screensaver when you're not using your computer. Anywho, I signed up for one of the ongoing projects through the system, called Amicable Numbers, and garnered enough credit to start an Amateur Radio team. I'm currently the only member, but if any of you want to join, it'd be much appreciated. You first have to install the BOINC software on your computer, and you set it up from there, joining the team within initial setup. The team I started is simply called "Amateur/Ham Radio Operators", and it appears to be the only general one as I can tell. Anywho, if any of you have good computers, and want to sign up for the first time or update team preferences (if you already use BOINC), then I think it would be much fun.

Link to the team itself: https://sech.me/boinc/Amicable/team_display.php?teamid=2264
Explanation of BOINC: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berkeley_Open_Infrastructure_for_Network_Computing
Amicable Numbers Homepage: https://sech.me/boinc/Amicable/

If you prefer not to use the links, a Google search will do. If you want to join the team, but need some help setting it up, I'll gladly help you. Contact me separately at n6truhamradio at gmail.com for that, please.

Thanks and 73,
Truman N6TRU

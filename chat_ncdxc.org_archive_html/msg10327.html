I have a SB-400, the companion transmitter for the SB-330, in excellent condition. My father used it up to about 1981 when he replaced it with an IC-730. Very clean, no known modifications, and has the crystals that were an option. Also have the line cord and I think that I have the manual. It has been stored in my home for the last 30 years. Jim Brown wrote:
I've been helping Pat sell his ham gear in preparation for a move out of their home (they plan to rent it) to live on a sail boat that they have bought.
Among the stuff is a very clean Heathkit SB300 that Pat said didn't work. I listed it that way and got no responses, so during quiet periods of the VHF contest this weekend, I fired it up. It wasn't easy -- it requires an older style line cord, and, as it turns out, the switch contacts were so dirty they wouldn't even pass 120VAC!
Once I got it running, I let it cook overnight to reform lytics, and this morning, hooked an antenna to it. It was mid-morning, so there were several informal nets on 40M. Other switches were quite oxidized, so operation was erratic until I hit them with Deoxit and gave them some exercise. Ditto for the pots, which I also exercised.
So, I pulled out the Windex, sprayed it on some paper towels, and proceeded to clean the dust from exposed surfaces. The radio doesn't hear much on the higher bands, so there's probably a weak tube or more, and some caps that should be replaced. I made no attempt at alignment -- life has taught me that it if ain't broke, don't fix it -- and I don't have the manual for it. The underside of the chassis reveals nice clean construction, so this is no disaster that needs to be fixed. All the tubes light up.
All the crystals are in place, and the filters are the "larger" ones, which are considered "better" -- 404-M201 (AM), 404-200 (SSB), and 404-M202P23 (400 Hz CW).
Bottom line - this is a nice clean vintage RX. Ham bands only, pre-WARC, so no 30, 17, or 12, and no 160M. It needs work, but it is working. Six SB-300s in less than "good" condition have sold on EBay for price + shipping ranging between $153 and $220.
This RX has to be worth $175 if you want it, and I'd like to get Pat the money for it. I'd also like to keep it local -- I don't know where I would find a box to put it in. :) 

73, Jim K9YC

As an early adopter of remote station operation, and as one that is tasked with defining contest rules I fully agree that a remote station only be defined as within the radius defined by the rules.  the operator should be able to be on the ISS and still work his home station for dxcc credit.  Heck we can call it the astronaut freedom rule.

A rule requiring an operator to be in the same entity supports only 1 thing, malice and intentional intervention with no gain to anyone involved, and further supports the lack of understanding of what internet based control of the "cat tail" is.  people tend to reject what they do not understand.  perhaps I should take the K6LRG remote lecture national.

Stu, again time will wash this out as more technologically savvy operators permeate the ranks of DXing, and costs of fuel and travel continue to rise and more flexible opinions make their way  up the ranks of the senior leadership of the organizations involved..

You are right on the money on this one IMHO.

No remote = N6WM qrt.  Im just 20 miles from my station, but I may want to be sipping mai tai's some day in between band openings and my struggle of DXCC from W6 ;-).

~C.

Pooyan and I are good friends. It all started a few years ago – at the end of 2011 and when I was closing in on DXCC HR. I was 6 or 7 entities away, and did some research on Iran. The biggest two issues were – who could I work, and then who might QSL. It’s one of the absolute most difficult polar paths from Orinda. The equinoxes are by far and away the best times to try.

 

Pooyan and I set s schedule for 3 months starting January 2012, and on March 14 – 2 days after my 53rd birthday – we had a nice clear 20M CW QSO. Prior to that – we had a lot of “near misses”. We both wanted it to count. The vernal equinox delivered!

 

Along the way – we started talking on Skype quite a bit, and all I can say is – he is a wonderful young person who has built all of his own gear and is someone I hope to meet in person some day. To me, this is the real “pinnacle” of DX-ing. These are the kinds of stories I will tell in my Pacificon presentation this coming Friday – in the antenna forum. I hope to see you there.

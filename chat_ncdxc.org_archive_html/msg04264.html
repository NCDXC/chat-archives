Hold on a minute!

 

On Sunday the board looked at a few candidates to replace Ross.  As I recall they were Jim VP, John Miller Director and Paul Ewing Director.  The result was for Jim K8JRK VP to move into that slot to fill out Ross’s term.  John Mill moves from Director to VP to help overloaded Jim.  Mike K6MKF takes on Secretarial duties replacing Bill Smith N6BM who is moving to Florida.  There is a director position available.

 

ALL of this goes to the membership for approval at this Thursday’s meeting.  This is the bylaw process to the letter.  At that meeting as with ALL board appointments or recommendations the MEMBERSHIP has the opportunity to approve that proposal or change it.  And you have changed it only a year or two ago.  If you disagree with what the board recommends, say so AND step up and suggest a change.

 

Again, at the June general election the board after asking for nominations (starts in May), will present a slate of candidates to the membership at the June membership meeting.  If you don’t like what you see, say so and make a change.

 

All seats on the board are up for grabs.  Anyone can be replaced by your vote.  Each of you can run for a board position or officer given your membership meets the bylaw qualifications.  Step up and contribute.

 

There is nothing secret about the board’s actions.  This board is transparent in all its actions.  If it wasn’t I wouldn’t be part of it.

 

Transparency…

Team

 

I have intentionally avoided participating in NCDXC politics for at least 10 years. I am not and have never aspired to be a politician. I have selfishly  rather preferred to just take care of my own business and my wife and children and grandchildren and enjoy chasing  DX. J  Notwithstanding the above as a younger guy over the years I have nevertheless willingly and proudly served  as an NCDXC President, Vice President, Treasurer, Director and gofer….on some occasions multiple times because I loved the club and what it stands for and frankly…on occasion nobody else would step up to the job.

 

It strikes me that all traditional notions of fair play and substantial justice are violated in a rush election being held without knowing far in advance who we are being asked to consider electing as an NCDXC president without knowing their views, what they stand for and we have ample opportunity to consider their positions and all alternative candidates willing to serve.

 

Whats the rush?

 

Cant this wait until Paul, N6PSE, returns and we know all of the candidates willing to serve in the position of President of the NCDXC and his or her position on issues and why they are running?

 

Why the secrecy and why the rush?   Why is a rush judgment in the best interest of the NCDXC moving forward?

 

There may be some very good reasons for stealth candidates (or candidate) and a rush to judgment to the exclusion of the consideration of others who are qualified and willing and able to serve….but maybe not…I believe the club membership is entitled to advance notice, multiple options, and the time to consider alternatives. A two day rush election for stealth candidates (or candidate) IMHO appears to be beneath the integrity and dignity of what many of us would expect of the NCDXC. But please remember…I am not a politician…and I don’t know the logic or the merit for a rush to judgment by a closed meeting by a board for a rush to judgment for stealth candidates (or candidate). There may be a good reason…I say…lets hear it and consider as a club if the reason justifies the stealth process and the exclusion of consideration of other worthy candidates.

 

I for one respectfully request the NCDXC to consider postponing the election for a month until all candidates have an opportunity to articulate their positions on various club issues, communicate their positions to the club membership and we the club members have an opportunity to fully consider and make an informed decision among all the candidates willing to serve the NCDXC.

 

Call me radical.

 

Respectfully, Ted, K6XN, tired cranky old fart

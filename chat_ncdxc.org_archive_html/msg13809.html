I recently got the low-band DXing bug. Why?  Because it is really challenging!  DXing on the low bands tests your ability to operate with high noise, weak signals and antennas that you largely built yourself. 

It is not point and shoot like the higher bands during good solar conditions. 

CQ Contest Hall of Famer-Frank Donovan-K3LPL recently gave a great talk before the Frankfurt Radio/Contest Club about his experiences and knowledge of Low Band antennas. It is well worth watching at this link:

http://qrznow.com/improving-160-and-80-meter-antenna-performance-by-w3lpl/

I am planning a new 68' vertical for 160 meters transmit and a set of receiving antennas for 80/160. See you on the low bands!

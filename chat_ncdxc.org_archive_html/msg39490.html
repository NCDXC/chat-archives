Thank you Rick and Annie.

I did some limited spot checking of my electronic cards last night and of the small sample size (3 &1/3%) all had confirmed on QRZ (which is my primary award source) and LotW.

I like your statement Rick that the "ARRL is unencumbered by progress" which is definitely true.  From what I've read a large part of why they were so easily hacked and why it took so long to restore services this past year was because they were running ancient hardware and software (Windows ME if memory serves).  Definitely unencumbered by progress, security, and common sense.

That's why I suggested the verification code, like the security validation code utility companies bury in your online account so they can verify you are who you claim to be.  ARRL does allow you to stamp or write your initials on the printed card though.

I'm just looking for a practical method here.  I've been actively chasing since May of 2022 and I have over 22,000 confirmed QSOs, and this year alone I have 3,500 QSOs.  If I went back to manually generating QSL cards for all of them... there's not enough hours in the day.

Cards for individual operations are much more doable, as I only made 12-13 contacts per park and usually all CONUS, before heading on to my next stop.  I log into my primary, QRZ, as well as eQSL, HRDLog, and ClubLog.  I'm back to using LotW as well.  I had stopped due to my dislike for ARRL's policies and practices, but not logging there only hurt my fellow hams and not the ARRL.

Since I log, the cards aren't of much value to others, aside from the courtesy and fun nature of receiving them, which I do enjoy as well especially those from events, DXpeditions, or foreign countries like Russia with their cool stamps.

Ryan, KI6BTY

On Friday, May 2nd, 2025 at 11:22 PM, Rick Bates, NK7I via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

A printed code is equally as valueless as the home made card could be.

I make my own cards to send, but each one is hand filled out and signed, which has been enough to be accepted.

ANYONE with a few simple skills can print out a card, from any entity, how can such a card be verified? Electronic (emailed) cards are just as utterly worthless and go in the trash bin; anyone can fake them faster than printing one.

The station initiating that email, proved they have internet access and has enough savvy to create an emailed QSL, so they have enough skills to use LOTW (THE accepted standard of the day) which is accepted everywhere (even though it can be a PITA to set up for some places).

Still, some refuse LOTW for their own reasons BUT send a real QSL card; which is accepted AFTER verification with a card checker (another type of PITA here, the nearest is an 8 hour drive, each way).

Expecting the ARRL to change, won't happen as they are completely unencumbered by progress.

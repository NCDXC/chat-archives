Thanks for the input.

As my ham station is now 200 miles away, my RFI problems are gone so this
shouldn't be an issue.  I can also hear signals at the remote station!

From what I hear, it sounds like Uverse is not for people with stealth
antennas.  Before I had a remote station, I had problems with the DSL
dropping out when transmitting with power on the low bands (DSL is around
1.6 MHz).  A special filter helped a lot there but probably wouldn't work
for 160m.

I would suspect the phone wiring in the house being the culprit for the VoIP
phone system.  Maybe a fully wireless phone system would be a good solution
and disconnect all of that wire in the walls.  I have found that the DECT
6.0 phones work very well in an RF environment.  My older wireless phones
really picked up RF (in the handsets).

Personal experience found that ham radio and Uverse don't mix at my QTH:
Over the last 10 years I have slowly gotten back into Ham Radio.  When we
first moved into our current residence we had Uverse and I loved it.
It worked flawlessly until I purchased an SB-200.  I quickly found that
transmitting more than 200W on 80M & 40M would lock up the TV.  In other
words I am find but the XYL and teen harmonic were not.  I quickly found
that the Uverse tech support written script has no way of dealing with this.
After searching the internet I found many others with the same issue.  I was
never able to get Uverse to send a tech to diagnose or correct the problem.
My last call to tech support was the proverbial straw that broke the camel's
back when I was told I needed to hire an electrician to fix what appears to
be their design problem.

We transitioned over to the local cable provider and have had no problem
since.

If Uverse were willing or able to fix my problem I would go back to them but
right now I am not willing to invest more time as we have a working
solution.

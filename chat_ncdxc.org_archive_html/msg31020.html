Thanks, John.  I worked all of those but have only one card!  Since I know that not everyone used ClubLog (a wonderful tool!), I am copying some info on KH8S for them to see the history.  There were two different KH8SI operations and only one counted.  Here you go.
Al  N6TA

Swain's Island qualified as the first separation entity from American Samoa, now a Political Entity.

QSOs made with Swain's Island, OC-200, on or after 0001z, 2006-07-22, count for DXCC credit.

KH8SI, ops F6EXV-JA1BK-JH1JGX-JR2KDN-K1ER-KB6NAN-N9TK-W9IXX-W0MY was QRV from Swains Island in March 2005, BEFORE the entity start date of 2006-07-22, so counts for American Samoa.

KH8SI, ops F6EXV (WH7S)-K8YSE-K1ER-KS6FO-JA1BK (KH6BK)-JH1JGX, was QRV from Swains Island again, in July/August 2006, this time counting as Swains Island.
Club Log's Log Search tool reports:
16,392 QSOs logged between 2006-07-28 22:19Z and 2006-08-02 16:00Z.

That 40M K5P operator was Mike, K9NW  (see attached snapshot from the K5P log).
 
I don’t think you’ll have any trouble working W8S:
- Cycle 25 will help 
- They should have 4-5 stations running most of the time.
- Each station has an SPE 1.3K amp
- They have a great team of 10 operators
- Their band plan is simple and straightforward:  https://swains2020.lldxt.eu/operating-plan/
- For FT8, they’ll always operate Fox/Hound using WSJT-X
- You have a great station, and they’re only 4,730 miles from your QTH in Oregon : >)

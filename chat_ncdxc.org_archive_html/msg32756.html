Paul,
I submit FT8WW Crozet.  It was relatively low budget, low key and mostly low power with meager antennas (often the DXped norm), but over the time he spent there gave out a LOT of ATNO (including mine); then offered other bands as conditions allowed.
Yes, he had transportation, housing, food and power provided, the conditions (cold and windy) not as bad as some places but he slogged away within the time constraints placed on him (mandatory quiet hours) and became an absolute rock star.
And he, with pr0t, proved that if one can hitch a ride with the military (who KNOW logistics), good things can happen.
It's been a GOOD year for DX; the solar cycle is picking up still and the end of the COVID restrictions provided a welcome burst of new DX for us (and a LOT of band mode slots if not new). 

73,
Rick nk7i

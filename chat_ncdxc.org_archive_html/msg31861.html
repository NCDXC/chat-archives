I come across this almost daily.  Even some 'loud' signals cannot be decoded consistently.  I am no scientist, but I'm trying to understand the issue.

I attribute it to phase distortion along the path; FTx modes are phase modulated and I expect susceptible to this kind of path harm (FEC or not).  And I've also seen one stream (only Vasilie) of multiple from a station, distort, which I attribute to 'selective fading'.  Either or both are when multiple paths from the sending station, arrive at slightly different times (enough delayed to bugger things up when mixing the audio to decode).

It you watch the panadapter in narrow bandwidth (I use one external to the K4 so it's larger and sharper); you can see the typical distinct lines (streams) being received.  There have been many times that I could HEAR the tones (by ear) but the distortion was high enough for no decoding.  (FRUSTRATING!)  Unlike RTTY or CW, the operator can't put it back together; the computer won't 'guess' (all or nothing).

It's not specifically a polar event but it does seem to correlate with solar storms (we're having a minor hissy fit now).  So pending more data, we'll call this an atmospheric issue.  Paths in this case, are like standards with "so many to choose from" but it's beyond our control.

And just like the paths to-from the other station; they may copy you fine (different path), which only increases the frustration (one way paths are real!).

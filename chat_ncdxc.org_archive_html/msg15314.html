A Quick Guide to FT8 DXpedetion mode for KH1/KH7Z

Follow the directions on the web site <http://baker2018.net/pages/plan.html>. Set up our wsjt-s with the new bands using the "transmit" frequencies specified on the web site. What is missing from any of the documentation I have seen is an idea of how a QSO proceeds. Here's my take:

The audio spectrum above the "transmit" frequency is divided into sections. They will be transmitting between 300 and 600 Hz. The pileup is anything between 1000 and 4000 Hz. The section between 600 and 1000 Hz is used for transmitting parts of the QSOs.

Start by picking an open frequency between 1000 and 4000 Hz to make your call. When you double click on their signal you will start calling on that frequency.

You will notice that they are transmitting on up to 5 frequencies. They can send a RR73 to one station at the same time they are giving a signal report to another on each frequency, so they may be responding to up to 10 stations in one cycle.

When they respond to you, you will see their message highlighted in red and the software will automatically change frequencies for your response.

When you get the RR73 message, they have logged the QSO and you should also log it.

When they are using many frequencies at the same time, the effective power on each frequency is reduced. The manual says, "If you are using N Slots signals, your average transmitted power will be P0/(N Slots) and the power in each signal will be P0/(N Slots)2. Thus, for N Slots = 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 the average power per signal will be down from P0 by about 0, 6, 9.5, 12, and 14 dB, respectively." If they are using a 1500 watt amp, and 5 slots, they will be transmitting effectively 60 watts in each slot. If they are using a KPA500, the effective power will be closer to 20 watts.


Things that can go wrong:

They transmit in the even slots (times that end in :00 or :30). If you transmit during these times, they won't hear you and you may cause harmful interference.

Calling outside the 1000Hz to 4000Hz range will result in your call being ignored and possible harmful interference.

They will send a message a maximum of 3 times. After you send the same message 4 times or so, I suggest giving up and starting over.

Many years ago when I became aware that there might be an “authorized” operation from North Korea I took an entire week of unpaid vacation from my work responsibilities to  work this operation because I thought at the time that in my lifetime it might be my only opportunity to work North Korea and obtain a “valid” QSL. It was 2002 and the operation was P5/4L4FN which is so far the only authorized operation from North Korea ultimately recognized by the ARRL  in the 53 years I have been a licensed amateur operator and chasing DX. (If there has been another one I am unaware of it.) I was able to work P5 on two bands and two modes and it took five days.

I would do it again in a heartbeat.

The cost to me at the time in lost earnings was several thousand dollars and neither my fellow professionals at work nor my wife could understand why I would do such a thing.

The “cost” to earn those QSLs was significant in both dollars and in effort. The expense in getting the actual QSL cards was negligible.

I normally donate modest sums to major DXpeditions (and some minor ones) and I normally  *always* include a few extra dollars when requesting QSL cards from DXpeditions.

In effort and expense to *earn* the QSL I am willing to pay a lot in expense and effort. After “earning” the QSL I would probably resent paying more than ten times the cost of the card plus postage although I might be willing to “voluntarily” donate more.

FWIW, 73, Ted, K6XN

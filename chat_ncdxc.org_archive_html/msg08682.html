Down here next to Los Gatos/Campbell it is calm with light rain.  But that is not what it has been.

 

The near-by weather station reported at the peak between 7-9AM 32mph sustained and peak gusts of 48mph out of the SSE.  We are in what is known as the Los Gatos Gulch.

 

The tower was lowered from 40 to 25 feet with elements pointing into the wind.  But now with the calm and radar picture looks like the peak has past I’m considering raising it back to its 40 foot level very soon.  Looks like plenty of rain still to come but hopefully the winds are over.

 

The gutters were flowing like crazy.  The drain out to the street put it in the street’s gutter not around the house.

 

I now know my neighbors palm tree needs a frond trimmed or removed.  Normally not a problem but in the wind it was slapping the dipole sending the SWR from 1.xx to over 2:1 at times.

 

We have had much worse rain and wind but still this one did need my respect.

 

73,

Bob – W6OPO

Down in the folds of Orinda we hit 35 mph winds this morning, but the average wind was not bad at all. Keeping fingers crossed.

 

I lowered by tower from 55’ down to 40’, and while watching the trees – I could tell the wind was much higher up at the 55 – 70’ level, and the elements of my yagi (pointed into the wind coming in almost always from the SW) barely moved.

 

The rain did come down like crazy though, and there is a creek right next to my house that went from a trickle to a torrent – right while I was raking out several storm drains on my street. The creek is very deep – and takes all of the water away from my house. The raking really helped a lot – it’s amazing how just a small clump of leaves can act as a dam and re-route rushing water.

 

Glad I stayed home today.

 

73 and best of luck at your QTH – this is one time I am very happy to be down in the bowel instead up on a hill top.

 

Rich

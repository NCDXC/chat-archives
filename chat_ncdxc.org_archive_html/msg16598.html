I’ve been a volunteer in the Cal Fire VIP (Volunteers In Prevention) program for over 30 years, and I have been manning the Copernicus Lookout Tower off and on for a number of those years. The tower is indeed an interesting place. And on a clear day, the views are spectacular!  But you need to be fit enough to climb a reasonably steep hill, and then scale a VERY steep set of steps. And of course, you can’t be afraid of heights. 😀

Ham radio has been a part of the program from the beginning... and manning the lookout is only one of several activities volunteers can take part in as part of the overall VIP program.  Volunteers also man phones during major fires, staff the communications van at fire camps, and assist with fire safety inspections. Some volunteer activities require a ham ticket, but many do not. 

As for ham radio in specific, we use ham VHF and UHF frequencies as auxiliary communications channels for Cal Fire traffic during major incidents, have ham radio in the communications van, and we maintain a full ham station at the Morgan Hill HQ, including an HF station. 

All that being said, there isn’t really any time for casual HF operations while you’re up at the tower.  You’re there to keep a lookout for smoke, and you need to keep focused on that rather than getting distracted by ham radio, or anything else. 

Don’t get me wrong... time in the tower can be pretty much like flying a commercial airliner; hours and hours of boredom interspersed with brief moments of stark panic. And you get to know your fellow tower volunteers pretty well because you have lots of time to chat! hi hi  But it’s important work, a pleasant way to spend a day, and something I enjoy. I heartily encourage anyone who thinks they might be interested to volunteer. 

I am of course happy to answer any questions about the Cal Fire VIP program in general, and working in the tower in specific. 

73,
  Frank N6OI

Copernicus Peak Fire Lookout Volunteers Wanted!

CAL FIRE Santa Clara Unit and the Forest Fire Lookout Association are currently recruiting fire lookout volunteers to staff our Copernicus Peak fire lookout.

The Copernicus Peak fire lookout is located east of the City of San Jose, CA in the mountains above Lick Observatory. Classes are forming now and include one day of classroom training and hands on training on location at the lookout. No qualifications are necessary, only a desire to serve the residents and communities of the CAL FIRE Santa Clara Unit.

The Copernicus Peak lookout is located at an elevation of 4,360 feet and was first constructed in 1929 and has been refurbished several times over the years the most recent in 2007 by CAL FIRE Volunteers in Prevention members.

Our next orientation class is scheduled for Saturday March 16, 2019 at the CAL FIRE Santa Clara Unit Headquarters located at 15670 Monterey St., Morgan Hill, CA 95037. The class will begin at 11:00 a.m. in our Fire Prevention office at the rear of the compound to the right of our Emergency Command Center. Please park out front near the flag pole as there is no parking at the rear of the compound.

If you are interested in becoming a fire lookout volunteer or would like more information, please contact Pam Temmermand, Fire Prevention Specialist II at

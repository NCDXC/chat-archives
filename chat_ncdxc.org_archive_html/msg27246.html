Hi Paul- 

Take a listen to one of the primary GHFS (Global HF Sys)  US/NATO freqs on 11.175 MHz usb.. Park there for 15mins, and you'll likely here a 37,57, or 87 character EMAS message.
8992 MHz is the other primary.

One site with info is found here:

http://www.swld.com.au/pages/usa_hfgcs.htm

Also, for HF aircraft- click on the Aviation tab and then select Civil Aviation Index HF. Scroll down a little for freq list.
 Aircraft that are well out of tower vhf range use HF channels by region..

For MW DX- try this quick & easy search list:

MWLIST quick and easy - to know what is on your AM dial!

MWLIST quick and easy - to know what is on your AM dial!

Mediumwave radio stations in North America, Central America, Caribbean



Look at other lists for LongWave.. During the evenings I listen for: "MOG" on 403.55 KHz LSB out of Montague( Yreka), CA and "RPX" 362.4 KHz USB in Montana.. These are AM cw id'ing stns- but I zero beat them a little. I use a ground mounted R7 and a Carolina Windom. Tracy Airport and Lemoore AFB are audible too.

For UHF Mil Air freqs: See MilAirComms.com
During Fleet Week I have my TH-F6A programmed around 232 MHz AM.. T-Birds, Blue Angels etc.

73, Eric n6spp (USArmy O5 Bravo Radio Op- NATO & Stateside, hi)

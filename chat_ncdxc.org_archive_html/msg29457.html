Three key points for pirate detection on FT8:

  • The DT will be the same (or at most off by 0.1, processing latency) on all streams; that's hard to fake.
  • The tones will be EXACTLY 60 Hz apart in a multistream transmission.
  • The signal strengths, won't match for a pirate in the mix.

So what is one to do?  WFWL applies, we keep trying and hope, sometimes (Bouvet) there are causes for issues.

I have 4 confirmations in the DX0NE online log; each one was a struggle when it should have been fish in a barrel.  Each one took significant time and wattage (pure raw QRO ERP) since the operator/s don't worry about things like propagation (and many other things).  The only other DXer in my county, has 3 slots; so it's not just us (it appears intentional but they're also severely deaf per expected versus sent signal reports).

https://www.mdxc.support/dx0ne/log/

It's not conditions, DX0NE was 5 over S9 on 17 CW here, MANY calling and the op just walked away (nothing said) from that to show up on 40 FT8 (couldn't stay on 17 for digital?  Didn't take the hint the band was wide open here?).

Was DX0NE sponsored by Icom to work every station in JA?  It certainly appears so.

My opinion:  Do it right (and fairly), or don't bother.  You provide nothing otherwise, except to your homies.  It is not generating 'good will'.

Disappointed is an under statement.  Bouvet was easier (4 slots confirmed there also).

What's sadder, the current Niger and Uganda stations are behaving no better (ignoring openings, no pileup management et al); which means if the ATNO is needed, the odds are highly against it.  There seems to be a trend of late, ET phone home.

On the flip side, the HK0/PYxxx guys are doing great and LOTW too, with no donation.  While not rare, THAT generates a lot of good will.

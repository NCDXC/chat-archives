I have no knowledge of what is going on at the ARRL, however as a recently retired Cybersecurity expert, I can think of a few situations where they may not be free to disclose the nature of their issues. 

 

1) Some Cybercrime insurers insist on no external disclosures or risk having your insurance coverage cancelled. Most corporations and nonprofits have some level of cyber insurance nowadays. The Insurers dictate the response and the disclosure of the cyber event. Different industries have different disclosure requirements. Publicly traded companies almost always have to report a breach of data. Nonprofits rarely disclose a data breach. 

 

2) Many Cyber criminals when taking data as a hostage or encrypting/implementing ransomware will insist that no law enforcement or media involvement/disclosures be made, at risk of the Cyber criminals escalating their levels of attack and disclosure to further levels.  It is often beneficial for the victim of a cyber event to contain the nature of the event.

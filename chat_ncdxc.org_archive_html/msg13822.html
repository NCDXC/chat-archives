For future reference, when a new digital mode appears, you can configure TQSL to automatically "map" that mode to DATA. This will
enable LoTW to immediately accept your QSOs; any confirmations generated are immediately valid for DXCC Digital, VUCC, WAS Digital,
and WPX Digital awards. 

After the new mode has been added to ADIF, which takes about 2 weeks unless there's already a new version of ADIF under development
(in which case it can take much longer!) you can then delete the mode mapping you added to TQSL, and resubmit your QSOs to LoTW. By
resubmitting your QSOs with the new mode, you and your QSO partners can obtain confirmations with an "exact mode match", which is a
requirement for a mode-specific WAS endorsement. 

Instructions for adding and deleting mode mappings in TQSL are here:

<https://lotw.arrl.org/lotw-help/pref-adi/>

When you resubmit your QSOs after LoTW directly accepts the new mode, new QSOs are created in LoTW. The original QSOs you submitted
- the ones with their modes set to DATA - remain harmlessly in place. Any confirmations previously created from those original
DATA-mode QSOs are automatically updated to reflect the mode contained in the newly submitted QSOs. 

If you have questions or need help with this, let me know.

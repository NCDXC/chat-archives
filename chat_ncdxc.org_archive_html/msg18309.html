What a fun new law this is indeed...

If the business does less than $500,000 in total yearly sales in CA, and they are in CA, they charge tax at their local rate. If HRO is shipping from a place with a higher rate than yours and their CA sales are less than 500k, you would see their higher rate.

If the business does less than $500,000 in total sales in CA, and they are NOT in CA, they should charge tax at the 7.5% base rate. 

If the business does MORE than $500,000 in total sales in CA, wherever they are, they charge tax at the rate at the destination, meaning the base rate plus any additional "district" taxes. That would be your local rate.

An interesting twist is that if a business overcharges your tax, they are okay as long as they remit the full amount collected back to the state. An out of state business that doesn't want to deal with the myriad rates across CA may be simply charging the highest rate. 

Sorting this stuff out is non-trivial and often involves paying a lot of money to compliance services to have correct tax rates applied at the time of order based on shipping address (not just zip code) and to make a correct filing every quarter. This would quite likely require raising prices. 

At least we are better off than Colorado, where you also have to collect the district taxes above a yearly sales threshold. However in CA you make one filing and send it to one place. In CO you have file and pay every single district; there are hundreds of them.

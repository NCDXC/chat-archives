It's a brilliant question and when you have the answer please share! :-)
All kidding aside, my view is early in the expedition FT8WW was transmitting during the odd cycle (for no apparent reason). I think folks copied FT8WW in normal mode and then put WSJT-X into hound mode on the assumption he was running as a Fox. The problem is WSJT-X forces the hound to be on the odd cycle. No QSO was ever going to happen and confusion and frustration reigned across the globe.
A few days later FT8WW, based on feedback, changed to even's. Why people are still calling on the wrong cycle is the mystery.
Slowly word is getting out you don't need to use hound mode and people are getting on the correct cycle. I know I am seeing fewer out of cycle callers.
It should be noted if you are still configured as a hound, it will now work since he is on evens, but there is no advantage to doing so.
The major problem now is if someone calls right on top of FT8WW as I see happening this very moment. Less likely to happen if using WSJT-X in Fox/Hound.

On 12/30/22, 4:13 PM, Bruce H. Bern via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:


Well said, Steve. MSHV is as good a choice as F/H in terms of throughput, and allows the waterfall below 1 KHz to be used by callers. That said though, MSHV does open the door to caller interference with the DX station, especially if they’re on the wrong cycle. I noticed this happening early on in the FT8WW activation. FT4 has about a 3 dB SNR disadvantage to FT8, and twice the throughput, but much less efficiency of spectrum utilization. 

MSHV is probably the best choice IF callers understand where and when to call (big IF), and if the received individual streams are strong enough to be decodable, and prevent too many repeats. At some point I recall Thierry was using 250w to his dipole… the additional 6 dB a Kw would have provided would have been helpful for those without a directional array.

The adventure continues.

73, Bruce K3NQ

F/H would not double his throughput...
He is running two streams with MSHV. He would run two streams with WSJT-X in F/H. The overall quantity of LIDS would remain constant.
The only difference would be how each program handles queuing and sequencing which would not make a lot of difference (apparent bugs in MSHV aside).

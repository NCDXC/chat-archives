On Jan 31, 2014, at 12:49 AM, PETER GAMBEE <paramountsa@mac.com> wrote:

Wow.  I have only been serious about DX'ing for the past couple of years, though I have been licensed since the 1970's and have certainly had my virgin ears burned by some pretty insane "intentional interference" (Some may remember the "Freethinkers' Net on 75 Meters back in the day).  

But what I've witnessed during the past few months, largely during DX-peditions has re-set the bar for me in a number of ways.  

I've never seen hard-core, multi-op, coordinated jamming on CW.  I've also never been sworn at on CW before.  At least not until the XZ1J DXP last year.  After having my ear drums repeatedly blown out of the solar system by one op who kept calling on the DX OP's transmit frequency, I lost my cool and transmitted "UP UP PSE", just once, on low power.  OOOOPS. Dumb, dumb, REAL DUMB!

My loss of cool opened the flood gates for a solid 10 minute stream of invective, castigating my lineage, my physiology, my psyche and a few things I can't quite bring myself to mention. All in perfectly formed, tight CW at about 30 WPM.  

Just seemed to roll off his fingertips.

I have since noticed that the pattern of multi-op, coordinated jamming seems to be rampant at times. Not just one or two guys keying down at high power, but two or more stations running fake QSOs & exchanges, repeatedly, all zero beat to the DX OPs frequency.  

This seems to intensify as the number of stations posting spots of the DXP increases on the major clusters.  The jamming then draws other ops into the mix, posting increasingly ugly comments on the clusters (using the clusters like a personal Twitter account to express their outrage). This, in turn draws even nastier on-line responses from the jammers, and intensifies the jammers' on-air insanity. 

And out of control it all spirals.  But the nut-farm seems to have produced a bumper crop for the Amsterdam DXP.  

Why do people post MULTIPLE spots on these DXP's (many without new or useful information)?  It just seems to alert and fire up the piranha.  Why do OPs engage the nuts - either on the clusters or on the air? Engaging them just gives them the food they are looking for. Posting their antics on international cluster sites just amps their ego rush.

Larger Question:  Has anybody done a formal psychological profile, or even a decent psych case study, of a typical jammer?  How does the psycho-profile of a jammer compare with that of a typical violent or criminal sociopath?  Were jammers the sort that got kicks out of torturing small animals, roasting the neighbor's cat in a microwave, etc, as kids?  

Understanding more about what goes on under their hoods could help us develop strategies to "deal" with them, and possibly identify them for FCC action.

It would seem to me that there is more than enough material for a Psychology Masters or Doctoral Thesis here. There has to be a forensic psychologist in the ham community with the ability to put something like this together.

Okay. I guess someone might as well say it ... "welcome to the NFL, rookie."   

End of rant.  We now return your list-serve to its normal operation ... 

73

- Peter Gambee / K6TTD

Team

Which is better SP or LP? Here is my limited on the air experience FWIW
today from Saratoga.

I was fortunate to be able to work FT5ZM on four bands today. I am using a
four element SteppIR antenna system which provides a dipole for 40M and 30M
operation and a four element beam for 10M through 20M and a 6 element beam
for 6M. I use a modest 32 foot elevated vertical antenna with elevated
radials and an antenna tuner for 80M and 160M.

I have a good LP antenna view toward FT5ZM and a not so good SP antenna view
toward FT5ZM as I have a ridge line that the antenna sees in the SP FT5ZM
direction.

I used the SteppIR dipole to work FT5ZM on 30M CW and 40M CW. I have no idea
if they were SP or LP. FT5ZM was stronger early this morning at my QTH on
both 30M and 40M but I worked them on 40M this morning and on 30M this
evening. They were much weaker on 30M here in Saratoga this evening but I
was able to work them anyway.

On 20M CW FT5ZM was several S units stronger on LP.
On 17M SSB FT5ZM was several S units stronger on SP. 

I hope this helpful. There are a lot of variables and we may all just have
to listen both SP and LP until a clear consistent pattern emerges. Good luck
tomorrow! 

73, Ted, K6XN

Team

Which is better SP or LP? Here is my limited on the air experience FWIW
today from Saratoga.

I was fortunate to be able to work FT5ZM on four bands today. I am using a
four element SteppIR antenna system which provides a dipole for 40M and 30M
operation and a four element beam for 10M through 20M and a 6 element beam
for 6M. I use a modest 32 foot elevated vertical antenna with elevated
radials and an antenna tuner for 80M and 160M.

I have a good LP antenna view toward FT5ZM and a not so good SP antenna view
toward FT5ZM as I have a ridge line that the antenna sees in the SP FT5ZM
direction.

I used the SteppIR dipole to work FT5ZM on 30M CW and 40M CW. I have no idea
if they were SP or LP. FT5ZM was stronger early this morning at my QTH on
both 30M and 40M but I worked them on 40M this morning and on 30M this
evening. They were much weaker on 30M here in Saratoga this evening but I
was able to work them anyway.

On 20M CW FT5ZM was several S units stronger on LP.
On 17M SSB FT5ZM was several S units stronger on SP. 

I hope this helpful. There are a lot of variables and we may all just have
to listen both SP and LP until a clear consistent pattern emerges. Good luck
tomorrow! 

73, Ted, K6XN

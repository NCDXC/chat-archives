Hi Vijay,
You’re going to find as many opinions as there are members. Amp purchases are always big ticket items, and like cars and houses, infrequent and colored by sometimes rare experiences. I bought a Collins 30S1 in 1988, and did not buy another amp until 2019 when I acquired my current Elecraft KPA1500. Whatever you choose will probably be with you for years. 
First of all, I love tube amps. They’re relatively quiet compared to SS amps, and they have an emotional gravitas you just don’t find with tiny solid state amps. Alphas were the best, but the company is defunct and parts scarce. Service is available, but will become less so. Tube prices have gone berserk as US production has disappeared, and the quality of Russian and Chinese tubes can be problematic. 
At this point, since you’re making an investment for the future, I’d think ahead to the repair and replacement climate that will exist in 5-10 years. In my opinion, that’s solid state. 
Tube amps were pretty much user servicable, but solid state repair for all but the most experienced is probably left to the factory. Everyone I know with a solid state amp has required some service. I got my 1500 in late 2019, and it’s been back twice since then for minor tweaks, updates,  and repairs. Since Elecraft is in Watsonville, and since Bob K6XX was one of the designers and is an NCDXC member, I have confidence that service will be accessible and competent. 
I both cases I drove the unit back to its birthplace and then drove back and picked it up a few weeks later. The first repair bill was about $180 and the second maybe $100 more. The convenience and nearby support was easily worth the acquisition cost to me. 
The SPE amps are attractive, and if you were thinking of one I’d only consider the 1.5 or 2 because of significant ongoing design improvements (and retrofits are not available or possible). They are manufactured in Italy and there is a single American importer. That said, they’re the lightest of those available, and the choice of many DXps. 
The KPA1500s, as well as the Flex Genius XL have been very popular in the club. Lucas W6AER has a Flex, and Paul N6PSE has a Flex and an Elecraft. There are many KPAs. 
At least in my opinion, the KPA is the choice. The tuner in built in unlike the Flex, does a good job, and the rig will run 1500w output in FT8 all day long, year after year.
The very first thing you’ll need to do is put in a 220 line. Trying to run pretty much any amp on 110 is not a good idea… the current draw is just too high, the voltage drop bothersome, and expecting your residential wiring to tolerate it is risky. 
I’m home from work today and if you wanted to talk just PM me and I’ll call. 
73, Bruce K3NQ

Really?

Are you PHYSICALLY at your radio?

Unless your radio is a physical implant with neural ties to your cortex your are not.  Simple laws of physics says that you are CONNECTED to your radio by a piece of wire – microphone cable, paddles, morse key or heavens forbid, by a computer.

Your actions cause electrons to be coerced into motion that ELECTRICALLY connect you to your radio…

So – how long is a mic cable and why does that make a difference?

Your mic cable is probably 6 feet long…  mine is over 400 feet because I operate a radio that is not located in my house.  I operate it from my office, the electrons travel over a longer wire and now you tell me it doesn't "count" because I'm not physically at my station but at a PC linked to the "station"?

Why is this any different than you on your microphone cable?

Now I extend my microphone cable over the Internet – as Einstein said…

You see, wire telegraph is a kind of a very, very long cat. You pull his tail in New York and his head is meowing in Los Angeles. Do you understand this? And radio operates exactly the same way: you send signals here, they receive them there. The only difference is that there is no cat.

There may be no cat but there are still electrons.

If I operate my station from my office, the electrons still cause the virtual cat to pull its muscles and extend over the ether to the remote cat to cause it to meow.

If I operate my station from India (which I have), the cat is still local.

Please give over this BS about remote operation…  I MAY be remote, the radio is still where it is physically located.  Its irrelevant where I am – its only relevant where the station is.

You can vote by mail regardless of where you are physically located… your vote still counts where you are registered…
You can write a check regardless of where your bank is located but the IRS says you are where your bank account is…

At least the Founding Fathers catered for a world which they could not remotely comprehend.

Perhaps we should do likewise?
Yours and QRT on this matter.
Stu K6TU

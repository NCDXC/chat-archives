Good question. Right now the verification uses a valuable slot on each Fox transmission cycle. Is it worth it? In almost every case I can think of no. Maybe for a top ten operation.

Re: Throughput. SF appears to me to be no better than the *bug fixed* version of classic F/H we used on Clipperton.

Perhaps the WSJT/Jarvis team will provide data to prove SF actually provides a rate benefit over the fixed version. I doubt it does.

For those interested, the Super Fox verification code scheme was broken well before the Jarvis team even left port.

As is norm for reporting such things by white hat hackers, info how to break it was held back to give time to the development team to fix it (they can't and didn't).

Also recognizing the importance of Jarvis as an expedition, the break was not released until after the expedition was complete.

For your reading: https://sprocketfox.io/xssfox/2024/07/24/superflawed/ and https://sprocketfox.io/xssfox/2024/08/21/superflawed-pt2/

I haven't bothered to look if the WSJT-X dev team has acknowledged the issue, but it is NOT "fixable" with it's current design. An entirely new scheme is required not using symmetric keys.

My take away is the senior leadership in the WSJT-x dev team is quite unaware of the current environment of hacking for fun globally.

It is highly likely Dom's key works.

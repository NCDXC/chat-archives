So far I don’t regret any antennas. I just didn’t know how to use them right for a while. 

I bought a couple of endfeds and strung them up about 20 feet in a spruce tree. Worked well. 

My Elmer gave me a G5RV JR which I still use all year long as it fits well on my residential lot. 

I also bought a BuddiHex which I really like for running in contests. My only regret with that is I didn’t lower it when I heard rain storms were coming to Visalia in late Winter 2025. The water soaked ground and intense winds allowed the stakes to pull out. My kids tried to keep it from falling while I was at work and I told them to let it fall. I didn’t want them hurt. All the arms are broken and I just need to save up the money to buy new arms. So my regret is not lowering it like I have times before. 


Will Shattuck

I don't think I've ever had a bad antenna.  Some were better than others.

I WAS and DXCC with my Cushcraft R7 from a city lot and 100 watts CW and Phone.  I'm sure I could have done both achievements sooner with a beam, but I worked with what I had.

I still like my Buddipole a lot.  I have used that antenna many times doing portable work.  I've used it several times at home setting it up on the roof of my house with guys and a counterpoise wire for 80 meters CW and SSB when contesting.

My most reliable antenna is my Alpha Delta 80,/40m dipole stretching from the Ash tree in the front to the fence in the back up over the house.

Jerry Olive KD6WKY

On Mon, Dec 30, 2024 at 11:19 AM Rick NK7I via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
Failing that, those are molex pins, pretty simple to work with.  

An extraction tool is handy (or GENTLY use a sewing pin); don’t bend the stopper ‘wings’; crimp and solder (heat shrink what extends on the wire end) and push in until it clicks (first making sure it’s in the right position on the connector).

That’s presuming you didn’t bugger up the connector itself.  That can likely be replaced without much pains other than finding a source.

Sadly, where the parts used to be readily available in most areas, now you’ll have to order online.  JameCo, JameCo; where have you gone?  Gone the same way as HALted, alas.  Even Radio shack had a worthy collection.  lol.

Make sure you order the correct pin size for the connector; this is a size matters case.

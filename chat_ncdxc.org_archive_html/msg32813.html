> An extraordinarily awesome contribution to Ham society has been the real time propagation information from FT modes. It’s the beacon of the hobby.
> When i operate SOTA and suspect my antena system might no be working properly, all i have to do is tune to the FT8 watering hole and voila.

> Roberto

There’s nothing new here. You can also send CQ on CW, and watch all of the skimmer spots come in. You will get a table of exactly how strong you are, and where.

Also, for years (decades?) the NCDXF has operated a network of Beacon stations, on every band, and every continent (except perhaps Antarctica; not sure). Just listen to the Beacon frequencies, and you can tell if signals are propagating from that part of the world on the band you are listening to.

Real-time propagation information is NOT limited to FT-modes.

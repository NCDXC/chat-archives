Yes, you should.

In addition to being able to tune around to find the DX pattern (everyone
has one unless they actively avoid it, not common) you can SEE the pattern
(the non-DX transmitting in response) and perhaps avoid the more congested
portion of the RX window.  It's MUCH easier to see the DX move up/down x KHz
every move, then position yourself in the right place.  If they stay in the
same place, you can also SEE the pileup (both sad and amusing).

It is as useful as having a second receiver (which lets you HEAR both sides
of the QSO so you can time your 'attack').  Together, a wonderful combo.

With only one receiver, you pick a spot in the supposed window, then hope.

The P3 is a SDR receiver, it just happens to be tuned to the IF of the K3.

I've had the station for about 3 years and I travel about (spent 4 months in
Alaska last year for example) so I'm gone almost half the time.  In that
time period I've made over 200 ATNO entity Q's (as of today, 213).  Compared
to the old days, these tools make DXing MUCH easier (but I've climbed to the
first plateau, it'll get harder from here on out).

Yes, you really should.

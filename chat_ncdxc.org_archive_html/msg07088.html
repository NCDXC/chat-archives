On 7/8/2014 2:21 PM, Hare, Ed W1RFI wrote:

Hi, Garry,

At this point, we don't have full information on all of them, but for the most part, they are switching devices, so they exhibit essentially the same noise as any switcher. That makes them hard to identify. 

There are two traits that seem to point at the current spate of grow-light controllers.  The first is that they are almost always on a timer.  If being use to grow pot, it is common to have them on for 16 hours, off for 8 during the growing season, then switching to a 12-hour cycle when the crop is nearly ready to harvest.

The other giveaway is that these devices are operating at about 40 to 60 dB over the conducted limits, so they are either exceptionally strong locally, or heard for as much as a mile away.

If you have a device that is on a timer, and very loud or heard for a great distance, it is probably a grow light.

Knowing what it is may be important to a ham who may not want to approach a stranger about what could be an illegal growing operation.  Most of the time, it is much more important to know WHERE a device is than what it is, because the circuit-breaker test is usually much easier than trying to guess ahead of time what a source is, then going to find its actual location.  Finding the actual location is the first step.

Ed Hare, W1RFI
ARRL Lab

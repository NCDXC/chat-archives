On Wed, Nov 8, 2023 at 9:07 AM John Litz, NZ6Q via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

ViJay,

I agree with the other assessments. 

I'd start back under the tree - all good?  then the coax and connector you added is the most likely suspect since you have two segments, i'd add one - check your SWR if good, add the 2nd and test again, if not good swap and see if it's good. eventually you should isolate the problem and plan accordingly.  

Other thoughts - how far is your run of coax? if the coax is near 1/4 wave or 1/2 wave of the frequencies it could create issues.  My initial thought is the balun on the antenna is fine since it worked with your first try and unless you changed that i'd focus on the 2 lungths of coax added

Does a neighbor ham or a club elmer have a single length of coax to get yo to the shack? might swap that in line and see what happens. I'd also encourage you to invest in a single length of coax from antenna to shack as each connector introduces loss and the potential for water intrusion further muching things up. 3M makes some great waterproofing tape to minimize that prospect.

Hope this helps -

On 2023-11-08 8:57 am, Frank via Chat wrote:

If the connectors all appear to be OK, the next thing to consider is the balun at the antenna. RF on the shield of the coax will impact SWR, and especially if the total length of coax happens to be close to a 1/4 or 1/2 wave on the frequency you're trying to use. The proper balun will prevent RF on the coax. So if you don't have a balun, get one. If you do, check to make sure it's the right balun, and that it's working properly.

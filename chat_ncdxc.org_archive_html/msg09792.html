Frank K via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

I received my NA4RR Hexbeam a couple days earlier than expected -- it arrived on Friday.  I don't have the mast or rotor that I plan to install it on yet, and even if I did it's going to be some time before I have everything ready for the installation.  So... as an interim solution -- and because I'm not patient enough to wait (hi hi) -- I decided to throw it together and put it on the 20' fiberglass mast that I've been using for my TH3JR.

Getting the TH3JR down went well (I've installed/removed it multiple times in that location), and assembling the Hex was no problem.  However, manhandling the Hex up over my head and dropping it onto the mast was a bit of a challenge.  Well OK, more of a PITA, actually.  But after a bit of groaning and a few adult magic words, it was in place.  And raising the mast back to 20' went smoothly since the hex is about the same weight as the TH3JR.

The next thing I did was force myself to at least throw it onto the AIM-4170C analyzer before plugging it into the rig.  Happily, the SWR looked good on all 6 bands.  A little higher than I'd like on 6M and 12M (both averaged about 1.6-1.7), but that could well be the fact that the beam is still very close to the house roof (which looks like tile but is in fact sheet metal).  And SWR in that range is certainly acceptable.

So I plugged it into the IC-7600, fired it up, and then started up VE7CC to see if any DX might be workable.  I saw that 9Q0HQ was on 20M CW, so I set my split and gave them a call.

They came back to me on the first call!  The op first responded with N6MI, but I resent my call and he picked it right up.

I've been trying to work them on the TH3JR since they started, and haven't even come close.  I know it might just be conditions and not the Hexbeam, but it's definitely cool that my first QSO on the new antenna is an ATNO.


73,
  Frank N6OI

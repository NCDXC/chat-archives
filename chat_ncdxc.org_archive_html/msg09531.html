I (mis)spent an hour or two in the E30 arena wrestling with the lions on 40 CW and have no good news to report.

The op on 7009 worked quite a few NA, but as far as I could tell, all or almost all were way east of here. The pile was solid from 7010 to 7017 or higher, but SH/DX suggested that nearly all brag postings indicated up 3 for the most part. The P3 was useless for finding the sweet spot to call, as there were no gaps where the op was listening and working, and too many continuous callers to enable finding the lucky callers.

The E3 was never loud and clear on my wire antenna and faded out after 0400 as the Horn of Africa lit up. K=3 was no big help, either.

In summary, a polar weak, watery signal, and an overabundance of callers, wrong VFO's--including me--and cops.

I subjected myself to this low-probability aural abuse because E3 remans one of the few counters I have not worked on 40m; the bands above 14 MHz  are all in the shoeboxes from before.

Clearly not the best time of year for the W6 to Horn path, but they have plenty of Euros to keep them busy.

Having said that, miracles do happen, which is why we keep coming back.

Garry, NI6T

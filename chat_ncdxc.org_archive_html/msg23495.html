The problem is not so much WHEN to move the pileup, or to change modes (e.g., to F/H), but how to communicate this change to the pileup. On CW or SSB, you can tell people what you are doing. For example, one might start out running simplex, and then once a pileup is observed, switch to split (by either sending “UP” on CW, or saying “UP” on SSB). Anna and I do this all the time on our holiday DXpeditions; sometimes there is no big pileup, and sometimes (if conditions are good) it can be overwhelming. My CW macros (I use N1MM for DXpeditioning) allow both simplex and split operation, with different macros for each.


But there is no easy way to do this with FT8. The station *could* simply move to a non-standard frequency and HOPE someone finds them and others see what is happening, but that has the risk that once you are gone (from where you were), everyone thinks you are QRT.


If you have a reliable Internet connection, you could post your intention on the cluster, or perhaps on your own website, but again, that assumes Internet availability.


Major DXpeditions will generally give instructions on their website, typically which non-standard frequencies they plan to use for FT8, and that they are using F/H. For a station anticipating regular pileups (rare DX), there is no reason NOT to use F/H, but you MUST communicate this to the world since all callers need to be in Hound mode.


For the more casual DXpedition, it becomes an issue, as noted earlier. When I was operating FT8 from Bora Bora (late summer 2019), I was operating only sporadically (there are much better things to do on Bora Bora than work FT8!), and running QRP, so I didn’t anticipate any major pileups. I ran on the standard frequencies, using normal mode, and made a few hundred QSOs.

Yes agreed with my esteemed station colleague...  as stated in my original response, the 64 bit software will help you system address additional memory and take better advantage of the newer processor power.  Also that the software issue has become less and less a problem as 64 bit friendly stuff becomes more ubiquitous.. There are working if not better alternatives to the software that does not run on 64 bit.  Some hams are very fickle, and want to keep using some software for years after end of life.

In my encounters, the biggest gotcha about Windows 8 is the unsigned driver issue. And how the operating system treats them.  It's a PITA, has potential for causing problems, and  Its enough for me personally to hold at Win7 (64), but I have an experimental win 8 system operational here at the home shack as well.  I also have issue with the Metro tile interface, one of the first things I did is put is classic shell, as N6ML mentioned.  This basically solved most of the metro tile stuff and restored functionality.  Most IT departments in the corporate world have completely turned their back on Win 8.  Many hardware manufacturers have offered downgrades to Win 8 to Win 7....  red flag.

It was just my preference to park the Win 8 system for ham use for now, in hopes that the release of Win9 within the next 12 months will address many of the UI complaints.  I fear the unsigned driver issue will stay with us so we will probably do well to understand how to apply the hacks that allow them, and hope that they don't hard lock them out in Win 9.    

GL Jim.

And now back to the show :-).

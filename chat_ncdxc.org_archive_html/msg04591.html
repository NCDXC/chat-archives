I've used a number of Lacrosse clocks and all have worked well.  For the
past 10 or so years, Ive had a digital model similar to this one set on 24
hours UTC.  I have a second (analog) one set to local time.  Both are on the
wall above the window at one of the operating positions in my home shack.
The only maintenance is a battery replacement every few years and it may
take the better part of a day to sync up with WWVx.  DST hasn't been a
problem either.  I note that sometimes they are a few seconds off from each
other and also the recently sync'd clocks in my shack PCs, but they're
plenty close enough for what they're used for.  The convenience of
auto-syncing is the best feature.

For logging, the time is coming from the PCs' clocks, so these wall clocks
are really auxiliary time pieces.

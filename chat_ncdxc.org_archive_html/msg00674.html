Here is your NCDXC question of the week:

 

How was your experience working the ZL9HR Campbell Island DXpedition?

 

 

Did you have fun working them?     What were your challenges?

 

 

Thanks,

 

 

Paul N6PSE

 

 

 

Here is my response:

 

I had a blast working ZL9HR 10-40 meters!  Their signals were much stronger than I anticipated and I worked them on the higher bands with relative ease. It was a bit more difficult on 40 meters and I never heard them well enough on 30/80 meters. I was very happy for them that they were able to

remain on the island at night time. I feared that would greatly impact their operations and their ability to satisfy the need. I thought their pile up management was good. It was evident to me that several of their operators were possibly new/inexperienced DXpeditioners or DXPP's as our friend Martti Laine would call them, however they did well and maintained a decent rate.

 

The communications from this team was superb. Their pilots, including Rich KY6R really kept the DX Community informed as to the challenges and features of this DXpedition. One of those challenges was uploading their logs to Club Log. That can be a real chore from a SatFone and it appears that they are still missing a few days of activity at the tail end. Club Log currently shows 35,697 contacts and I estimate they made many more than that. Overall, a fun DXpedition to work and a much needed country for many of us!

 

Thank you!

 

Paul N6PSE

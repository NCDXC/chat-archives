                  DXCC News in the ARRL BOD Minutes
______________________________________________________________________

The American Radio Relay League (ARRL) has published the minutes of
the second BOD meeting, which was held on July 19 and 20.  Of
interest to DXers will be minute number 35 notes it “has been at
least 10 years (Editor’s note” “15 years”) since the DXCC rules were
comprehensively reviewed by the DX Advisory Committee (DXAC) and/or
another committee and ratified by the ARRL Board of Directors”.  The
ARRL Board of Directors is asking “the Program and Services Committee
to request the DX Advisory Committee (DXAC) study and, if warranted,
recommend changes to the DXCC Rules for the ARRL Board of Directors
to discuss”.  The BOD is requesting the report be given to the
“Programs and Services Committee prior to the January 2014 Board
Meeting with a final report and recommendations forwarded to the
Programs and Services Committee prior to the July 2014 Board
Meeting”.  You can see the complete minutes on the ARRL Website at
http://www.arrl.org/files/file/About%20ARRL/Board%20Meetings/2013%20Second%20Meeting%20Minutes.pdf

                              On The WWW
______________________________________________________________________

NASA Scientists are predicting the southern pole will soon be
switching polarity.  The northern pole has already happened and it’s
counter pole is expected to go through the change, possibly in the
next three to four months.  This traditionally has marked the
midpoint of a Solar Cycle.  Check out NASA’s “The Sun's Magnetic
Field is about to Flip” article at
http://science.nasa.gov/science-news/science-at-nasa/2013/05aug_fieldflip/
Make sure you also see the “Is Solar Max Double Peaked” video at the
bottom of the article.

                              Silent Key
______________________________________________________________________

K7ZZ (ex KI7DM), Thomas C. Meier, of Turner, Oregon became a Silent
Key on Saturday August 3rd.  He was 74.  Tom had be suffering “from a
rare form of Wegener's Disease” said KI7Y, Jim Cassidy.  Tom did some
DXpeditions as J75Z, KL7/K7ZZ (NA-019), T30T, T88ZX, V25Z, V63ZT,
V73ZZ, ZD8ZZ and was a member of V73E (OC-087) and K3J (KH3).

W8GS (ex KN8HBN, K8HBN, WPE8AIK), G. Scott Henninger, of Maineville,
OH passed away on Friday August 2nd.  He was 70.  Scott was a charter
member of the South West Ohio DX Association and past president of
the Greater Cincinnati Amateur Radio Association.  He also gave back
to the hobby through the VE program.  “Scott will be remembered as
capable of fixing almost anything” and “was never too busy to help a
friend”.  W8GS had 335/354 (current/total) confirmed in the ARRL DXCC
Mixed standings.  Our condolences to N8GTW, Lenore, and the Henninger
family.  The Memorial Service will be held at 11 AM on Saturday
August 10 at Sycamore Presbyterian Church, 11800 Mason Montgomery
Rodd, Cincinnati, Ohio.
http://www.legacy.com/obituaries/cincinnati/obituary.aspx?n=G-Henninger&pid=166273287

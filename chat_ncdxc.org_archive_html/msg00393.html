You're welcome to bring your computer here, but you ought to to be able to do this yourself.

To get the DX Lab logger (DXKeeper) going, go to this page and click on the Download link. This installs a program management application called DXLab Launcher, from which you can then choose the DX Lab applications you want to use. From there, things go pretty much automatically. 

Some of the other DX Lab applications are more useful than others. I use DXView (provides beam headings and distance for a call that you enter, also shows it to you on a map of the world, with sunrise and sunset); and I use Commander (reads frequency and mode from my radio and plugs that into DXKeeper when I log a QSO).  Commander and DXView do other things, but I don't use them.

Once you have DXKeeper installed we can talk about LOTW.  DXKeeper is all you need to work with LOTW (once you are fully signed up with LOTW and can access LOTW through the ARRL webpage).

73, Jim K9YC

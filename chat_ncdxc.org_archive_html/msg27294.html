Well said, Steve. MSHV is as good a choice as F/H in terms of throughput, and allows the waterfall below 1 KHz to be used by callers. That said though, MSHV does open the door to caller interference with the DX station, especially if they’re on the wrong cycle. I noticed this happening early on in the FT8WW activation. FT4 has about a 3 dB SNR disadvantage to FT8, and twice the throughput, but much less efficiency of spectrum utilization.

MSHV is probably the best choice IF callers understand where and when to call (big IF), and if the received individual streams are strong enough to be decodable, and prevent too many repeats. At some point I recall Thierry was using 250w to his dipole… the additional 6 dB a Kw would have provided would have been helpful for those without a directional array.

The adventure continues.

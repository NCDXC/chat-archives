While I agree that the accuracy of these RF Safety calculators is
questionable, it is nice to have a printout that shows that I have
performed the calculation.

This is the calculator that I prefer, because it displays the results
in a table, formatted like a spreadsheet.
http://vernon.mauery.com/radio/rfe/rfe_calc.html

Use the Transmitters, Antennas, and Connections tabs to enter the
details of your station, and then view the Results tab.

Warning: When entering Band, use the drop down menu, e.g. "40m". If
instead, one types in "40", it seems to do the calculation for 40 Hz,
which yields tiny safe distances!

Here are the abbreviated results for my Field Day antenna, which I
will be sitting rather close to:
'K3_FD' to 'OCF_Vertical' via 8 ft of 'B8237'
40m 100W ... 0.6676' controlled   2.111' uncontrolled
20m 100W ... 1.321' controlled   4.178' uncontrolled
15m 100W ... 1.975' controlled   6.246' uncontrolled
10m 100W ... 2.681' controlled   8.479' uncontrolled

If I recall correctly, a BS7H DXpedition to Scarborough Reef was the
cover story of an issue of QST. It showed the operator, rig, and
vertical antenna perched on the small rock. In a subsequent April
issue of QST, someone wrote that because the setup did not meet the RF
Safety requirements, DXCC credit was being rescinded. Many Old Timers
did not realize that it was an April Fool's joke and became outraged.

-John AC6SL

My P3 helps me study the DX op’s methods – is he listening on just one frequency, moving up or down, or just bouncing around?  I use what I think I learn to decide where to call.

 

With my 500W and not-very-high antennas, I find that the frequency of the last station worked is usually immediately jumped on by the big guns and my signal is not competitive there. 

 

My experiences as a SSB op on Conway Reef in 2012 taught me a lot about what the DXpedition op is facing.  On SSB, the pileup is frequently just a roar, a wall of noise from which you are trying to pick out a callsign.   I would work stations calling on the lower and higher edges of the pileup where I could discern individual callsigns.   The main pileup would soon move to this new frequency and I would start listening higher or lower again to keep the Q rate up.  I imagine that CW ops have the same challenges.

 

As a result, I’ve had most success on the margins of the main pileup.  I figure the DX ops will find me eventually, and they usually do.

I have the full Elecraft K-Line and have used FT5ZM to try to see how effective using the P3 is in slipping my call in a "hole" in the pileup / split.

 

I have found that it isn't nearly as effective as my old method - which is to simply tune and hear the last station worked and jump in there - ESPECIALLY when the DX station makes an "excursion".

 

HOWEVER, the P3 does tell me where the MONSTER DX-ers (you guys with the full power and massive antennas) are - and to stay away from trying to compete there. Some of you guys have the power to "duke it out". I don't.

 

I have found that the ops I have worked at FT5ZM are jumping around rather than going up and down by small increments. I think every time I worked them - I tracked the "swing" out and away from the "pack".

 

How about you? What's your experience?

Just noticed that 3DA0RU gets encoded as a hash, not a full callsign in most exchanges via FT8. That means it decodes as <…> until you receive a RR73 message, where the full callsign is sent. Afterwards, WSJT-X remembers the hash and it assigns the corresponding hash, displayed as <CALLSIGN>.

 

So, if there are stations in Fox mode decoding as <…>, wait a couple of minutes to identify it. (I nearly missed 3DA0RU running on top of J5HT on 10.131 MHz now because it was showing up as <..>.)

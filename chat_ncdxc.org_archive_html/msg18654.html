I'm going to try lower power, but my suspicion is a little simpler on a different track.  If you watch who they're working the most, a few things pop up.  Here's how I am noticing things...

They use beams on the upper bands.  They want to work (the most they can) EU, so they point those beams at that heading.  This creates a pattern null to the west coast, aggravated by the lash up they're most likely using.  Those in the e/se/s US manage to get heard but with much lower than expected reports, much lower than EU too.  Those on the west coast have had to struggle mightily on high bands (and get near insulting reports)

On low bands, they're using directional antennas (4 square etc), lean those to EU (duh) and must have significant noise (at least at high angles).  Where that noise comes from, who knows?

They've clearly had some issues; if they choose to share events after, it would make good notes in the 'lessons learned' notebook.

In any case, the temperature rose to almost 30F here and by moving the elements between the bands a few times, the stuck element broke loose and the SteppIR is back in operation!  I'm watching their reports (on 20M at the moment); hearing ALL their streams on FT8 (0- to -3 dB) and KNOW I have propagation to them...  (lessee, 530 watts into 13 dBi gain = yeah baby...)  They've stopped sending between responses, sumpin goin' on there, long breaks.  First guess is that they're just not hearing now, with EU, US, SA all calling (at least what I can copy here now), second is that they're attempting to resolve some other issue.

With the antenna not tuning yesterday, I took the day off (snowed, so I'm caught up on the DVR, mostly).   Ignoring 80/160 (even email) allowed me to catch up on sleep.  I'm energized now.

TI9A has given me 5 band slots, so far; 2 on CW for the FT8 haters.  ;-)

The 'battle cry' at the Fire Department I used to work at:  "Get some!"  (not entirely socially proper without the context)  :-D  But ya'll get the point.

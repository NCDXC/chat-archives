My own variations:

I tin the braid before screwing on the shell.  This keeps it from wrapping
sideways inside the shell and becoming hidden behind the shell wall.  When
tinning, it is really easy to overheat the braid and then you get the center
conductor insulation oozing out through the braid fibers.  But if you're
quick, it comes out clean.  I also file the edge of the tinned braid just a
little, so the now rigid edge won't catch on the lip inside the shell.  The
pre-tinned braid is then very easy to solder through the holes in the shell
with minimal heat and solder.

I put a little soap on the threads of the shell to make it easier to screw
it over the outer cable sheath.

With lightly tinning the braid and soaping the threads, Pl-259s go together
pretty easy.

> Soldering PL259 connectors requires the correct tool.  The problem is, 
> that tool costs $500 on ebay.  Get a Met-cal soldering iron with a 
> chisel tip.  If you've ever done it this way, you'll see how easy it 
> is.  :-).

I've installed a lot of PL259s without that $500 tool, and gradually learned
how to do it better. I use ONLY Amphenol 83-1SP connectors. My
tools:

For solid copper center conductor coax, #12 or larger, a retractable blade
knife to first cut through to the center, then cut the outer jacket to the
right length.

Good small dikes to trim any ragged edges of the braid.

Vise grip wrench to twist the connector onto the coax.

A heavy vise to hold the cable while mounting the connector and while
soldering.

Yes, a good temperature-controlled iron with a large blade. I still use the
Hakko that I bought about 10 years ago. Not a Metcal, but it does the job
with the right blade.

Liquid soldering flux is a BIG help, especially with the braid.

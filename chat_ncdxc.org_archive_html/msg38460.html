On Jan 23, 2025, at 7:54 AM, Rick Bates, nk7i via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Jerry,

That term has been applied to me, by me, for some years now and most on this list, know that.  The word is not sexist (in any use) but implies equally to anyone that wants all slots (and my record online shows that).  There is no one word 'polite' term for behavior like that, which covers both sexes (a lack in the language).  The other words that could be used, applies to those who sell themselves; which is not the case here.

What would you call, in simple to understand terms; someone seeking to have all slots on all bands, every entity, all the time?  Even the question takes more words than the two I used.  At club meetings, the term brings a smile because I've used it, only on myself (and long timers here on lists have seen that).  Others can describe their behavior in their own terms.

It appears there will now be some recognition for other operators, like me that seek more than DXCC (digital was earned here the first week of this new year)?  DXCC (mixed) alone, is not a significant challenge in the peak of a solar cycle.  (I'm stuck at 323 because for political and bureaucratic reasons; both of which deny any access to the sites needed.)  It also provides some small income to the ARRL who charge a lot, for a simple piece of laser printed paper with tiny stickers (many of which I've 'earned' but I have zero use for wallpaper).

All entities, all bands, all three modes (or more if offered); is my goal.  Physics (and time) will limit us (all), but one needs to have a goal or it's just wandering.  I seek to take in all available slots.

So please lighten up, it's meant in a positive, fun but self deprecating way and everyone else, seems to have gotten that.

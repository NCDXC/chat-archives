If you have the room and the time to lay out a radial field, the HF9V is an incredibly effective and space efficient antenna. I worked 80m DXCC with mine over the last year, with only 6 entities prior to installing the antenna. On 40m it works as well as my center loaded 46’ rotatable dipole at 42’. 
It work well on 30 as well… another DXCC in less than a year after never having worked the band. On 20 and up, it’s adequate but almost 10 dB down on my 2 el multiband Yagi.
I’ve been quite favorably impressed by the bang for the size.

Hi, Bob,
IMO, definitely a hex beam. Full size elements, no traps or loading, no controllers, handles 2 KW, light weight (25 lbs), and very broadband flat SWR. 11 ft turning radius. Wind load is about 5 sq ft. Most hex beams also include a 6m element as standard. 40m elements can be added on, but increase the weight and wind load.
Myself and Rich WB6EWM are using the KIO hex beam with great success.
They are great antennas.
I recommend the KIO hex beam.
https://www.k4kio.com/

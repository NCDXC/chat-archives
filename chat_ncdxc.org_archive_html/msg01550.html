I agree with many of the points made by others. I would like to add a few points that may not have been mentioned as to what makes a “Good DXpedition” IMHO.

 

1.)    I believe it is important that all the operators are able to make it back home after the DXpedition alive and well. I can remember DXpeditions in the past where operators were killed (remember the early Spratly DXpedition where their boat was sunk and the survivors were machine gunned from the island?) or the husband and wife team of Doctors in Uganda who during a DXpedition were held captive in their hotel room as  others were killed in the courtyard outside their hotel during their DXpedition? Also we need to remember the recent death of a great guy who was helping to arrange a Dxpedition in just the last year or so (I apologize Paul in that I have forgotten his name and call sign).

2.)    I believe it is important that the DX location will actually count as an “ARRL country” after the DXpedition is over and that the Team has been able to obtain the necessary permissions and documentation from the host country.

3.)    I believe that for a “Good DXpedition” we in the DX community should remember to show our appreciation for those who went in harms way to conduct these operations and that we also support those who may not actually go on the DXpedition but who nevertheless help out in so many ways  to plan and support the DXpedition…the “unsung heros”.

4.)    I believe a “ Good DXpedition” is one which is supported if possible by donations from those of us in the Dxer community who are able to donate to help defray at least a portion of the debts and costs incurred by those who make the DXpeditions happen even if it is only a few dollars. Many are able to do this through their membership dues to  DX clubs e.g. NCDXC, NCDXF etc  or in adding a few dollars to QSL requests.

FWIW.  73, Ted, K6XN

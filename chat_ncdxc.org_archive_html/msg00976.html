If you use XMLog, you can find the QSOs that you have submitted and are not confirmed by going to the “QSO/QSL Info” page, click select and then put a != in the LoTW Send box (not blank) and a = in the LoTW Rcvd box (blank).  Click search and they will be selected.  You could also put a >11/1/11 (or some other date) in the Date box to limit this to only QSOs after a certain date (I got over 2100 with out a date and 91 with the above day).  Then you can export the selected QSOs to LoTW again.

 

XMLog have very powerful search features for filtering your log.

On 1/13/2013 8:59 AM, Bob Lanning wrote:

LoTW is processing logs within an hour of being received!


Yes. NOW, it's time to look through your log for QSOs with known LOTW users that are not confirmed and try to figure out if any of your logs were lost in the software bug they talked about a month or so ago.  If you don't have a QSL by now, it's worth reloading ONLY those QSOs that might have been lost. 

DXKeeper keeps a record of which QSOs have been acknowledged by LOTW and which have not.  The ones that HAVE been acknowledged have a Y in the LOTW sent box, and the ones that have been uploaded but NOT acknowledged have a U in that box.  Using the Advanced button, it's possible to write a script to select QSOs that have the U, then edit that list to change the U to R (requested). Then go to the QSL LOTW page, tell DXKeeper to Add Requested to the LOTW queue, and then upload that queue.  The entire process takes about five minutes, and solves the "lost log" bug on your end.  But it does not solve it if the DX station's log is lost. :)  They will also have to re-submit their missing QSOs (or, to be safe, entire logs). 

I have already recovered a half dozen missing new band-country QSLs by doing only my part of it.  If we're still missing QSLs from DX stations who say they have uploaded, we should probably contact them and suggest that they re-upload the logs in question. We all knew this was going to be needed when the bug was announced, but when the server was badly overloaded was the wrong time to do it. :)

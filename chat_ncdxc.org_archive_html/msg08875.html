Team
 

We have been very successful soldering many PL259 connectors using the  DX Engineering Coax Cable Stripping Tool DXE-UT-8213.

It only cost us approximately $50 and multiple people have been successful in flawlessly preparing and soldering PL-259 connectors for years using it…even the rookies… up at our cabin QTH in the Sierra. We have 11 different antennas and feed lines at that location and very severe winter conditions at 5000 feet elevation. There are ample opportunities every year to repair and replace PL-259 and other coaxial connectors. J We *love* the DX Engineering Tool DXE-UT-8213.   More information can be found here  http://www.dxengineering.com/parts/dxe-ut-8213

We have two sets..one we keep up at the cabin and one set we keep down here. We have not had very good long term success with “crimped” connectors at 5000 feet elevation surviving winter conditions but better success with “soldered” connections using the DX Engineering tool. YMMV.  GL and 73, Ted, K6XN

On Jan 9, 2015, at 10:54 AM, SPWoo via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
 
Hi Keith,
 
I could never learn how to solder a PL-259 properly.  My work looks awful, with melted cable jacket on every connector.  After much frustration I'm now using crimp-on connectors.  There are two kinds of crimp-on connectors, one requiring soldering on the center pin only and the other one does not.  I have used both types with great success.  I purchased a crimper kit from DXE.  With it life is good and with much less frustration.  If you look at the pre-cut coax assemblies from HRO and DXE they are also the crimp-on type.  GL.

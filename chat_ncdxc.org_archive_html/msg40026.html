On Jul 15, 2025, at 7:45 AM, Greg De Hoogh via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Gene wrote:
"My opinion:  ARRL should implement an annual non member user fee, $15 a year. There is no free lunch, LoTW provides a worthwhile service, 15 bucks today isn't going to break anyone's ham radio budget. Although, the usual suspects will loudly complain."


Why use LoTW, whether for a fee or not, when Clublog provides (essentially) the same service, at NO CHARGE, and with NO OBSTACLES to “join” the system.


Well, the usual suspects seem to complain whenever anything is changed and then complain when nothing is changed, so we can safely ignore their howls. My concern in charging non-members to upload to LoTW is about the quiet folks who are DX for us in the US. The little guys who give us FT8 contacts for one of the rarer countries, and for whom $15 might not be an insurmountable obstacle, but perhaps a large enough one that they'll just quit using LoTW - those are the ones I'd worry about.

I have spoken with dozens of DX folks who don’t use LoTW. The main reason is that ARRL makes it extremely difficult for non US/VE hams to get a “LoTW certificate”, because they don’t trust these hams (their own “brethren”) to provide valid license, name, and address information. The US and Canada make their Amateur License databases public, so ARRL (and anyone else) can easily determine whether someone is a licensed ham, that they live where they say they do, etc. Some other countries follow suit, some do not. (And some prohibit Amateur Radio altogether, making contact with them even MORE desirable!) ARRL justifies these roadblocks in the name of “security and integrity” of the system, along with the requirement to use a digital signature to upload QSOs to LoTW (that’s the function of tQSL). It’s almost laughable how they wave the banner of security and integrity when there own system has been shown to be insecure and easily infiltrated.

And to be honest, many DX stations use QSLs as a source of personal revenue, charging $5, $10, or more to confirm a contact. But they are simply emulating ARRL, which charges fees to get “credit” for those supposedly “free LoTW uploads” so you can get your name on a list of thousands of people in 6 point font, in a magazine that you have to pay to get a real copy.


To me, living here in the very non-rare US, getting an LoTW confirmation from them is far more valuable than their getting one from me. There are folks out there who we'd like to see use LoTW, but who aren't doing so right now for whatever reason (e.g., a while back I worked a guy in Tajikistan who doesn't use LoTW, and I can't get a card from him because the USPS won't accept mail for Tajikistan).

But he can send you an email to confirm the contact. Or upload it to Clublog. Or upload it to eQSL. There is no prohibition on any of that, and no cost. Now, if you make a contact, and get an email response confirming that contact, and you still aren’t sure that that contact was “real”, I guess that’s your problem. I don’t need ARRL to tell me what is real and what isn’t.


Rich KE1B

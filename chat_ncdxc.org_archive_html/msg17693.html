Some really great progress was made today on the tower project. I hired the son of some friends to help (cheap too!).  He is a REALLY great worker, accepts and follows direction well, worked hard with no complaints. Someone should hire him in a career job.

The electric power for the tower is roughed in, ready for the electrician (trench filled); the in trench ground rods (every 16' apart) are mostly done (need the machine to finish sinking them or a fresh worker with great sledge hammer aim). The house safety ground is now tied to the tower and the shop safety ground. (I only had to make one urgent run to the parts place; closed Sunday, Monday is a holiday)... One more wire run out into a field is next, ground rods in place.

The 55 A 240 V (was for a spa) is being reduced to two 120 V 20 A circuits.  One I roughed in today, the other needs new wires pulled, the original wires were simply cut, not even long enough to re-use or extend).  That circuit will power the meat smoker (mmm, smoked elk).  The electrician will manage that and connect my dots. The tower power is for the up/down winch, a web cam and whatever power tool I might need in the future.

The circuit placed today is overkill, #10 that was used in a well (direct bury), in 1" conduit (over sized), down 24" per code (if used as direct bury, so more overkill)... for 20 A (#12 is required).  It won't have any IR^2 loss to consider; the tower will not be sluggish in raising (sir yes SIR, how HIGH SIR?).

Once two trench ground rods are completely sunk (add a little dirt over them too for spacing), I can place the two other conduits for coax (3") and rotor/SteppIR cables (1 1/2") with pull ropes in place for both and secure them to the tower base.

In addition to reducing lightning effects; all this grounding (uh, 15 rods in place? I lost count) should reduce ambient electrical noise (quieter radio, hear more of what I want to hear).  Rain/snow/wind static should be a thing of the past (almost).

Add an 'entrance box' (with a second set of lightning diversion hardware) for a graceful cable/wire transition into the garage (soon divided into a 'radio room') and this effort will all be a memory (add grass seed).  One will see the tower, by itself, not an unsightly mass of wires leading into the house (XYL approved!).

Now, the everlasting question? Why does the dirt you dig out, never fill the same space again? (I'll have to relocate dirt from other places to fill the trenches, they're inches lower now).

Ibuprofen already on board, rehydrating and chillaxing are the plans for the evening.

I also need to pick up the pace on the reassembly of the SteppIR DB36 and test it.  Every time I look at it, I think "That sucker is honkin' HUGE".  Once the EHU are proven, I'll attach the mast to the tower, put the boom on the mast, then reassemble there (to avoid the dreaded four person antenna hoist later, the antenna exceeds 200 lbs).

If all goes REALLY well with the antenna, it should be up playing before the end of Sept (then the rainy season starts, followed by snow and frozen ground Nov-ish).  Then when the building contractors are in their winter doldrums hungry for work mode, I'll have them build the radio room (and save money).  That's easy, a weekend; two walls, a window and a door, insulate the concrete floor, add flooring.  Then I'll build/buy an operating desk (with wheels to easily move it away from the wall), a couple work benches (one electronics, one mechanical)... THEN I can AT LAST organize tools, open the last of the boxes and finish getting settled in.

And I found a way to put the shack on the generator panel too (but I'm still going with a LiFePo4 battery bank and inverter).  That means any UPS used, only needs to last about a minute.

Progress at last!  I love it!

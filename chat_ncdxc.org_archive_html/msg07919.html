Unless it's been changed, which SERIOUSLY doubt, Logbook has a tolerance of +/- 30 minutes, not 15. It was also designed to correct for the dates around 0000Z. If you submit a record with 2355 on the 17th and the other party submits a record with 0003 on the 18th, LoTW will produce a match.

On Fri, 17 Oct 2014 16:20:49 -0400
 William Smith via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

I use Amateur Contact Log v. 4.8 for general logging and logged a QSO with
VK9DLX at 00:16 UTC.  I noticed that ACLog stamped "THURSDAY 10/16 " (after
00:00 UTC) - the day prior to 00:00, not Friday 10/17.  I went back into my
log and manually changed the date to represent Friday not Thursday.  I
talked with Dan at the LOTW desk today and he said the LOTW software had a
window +/- 15 minutes but any day outside of that would not verify the
QSO.  I sent an email to the developer of ACLog and mentioned this to Scott
and said I use Dimension 4 to keep my computer on time with NIST.  Scott
said:  "Please be sure to use AC Log 4.8 if you aren't already.  The 4X
versions of AC Log read your date and time direct from your PCs UTC clock,
so as long as your clock is set properly, the AC Log date / time will be
too."  My computer did sync with UTC so I used the second clock in Windows
making the time on one clock to represent the clocks in the house with the
second clock to set to UTC at Greenwitch.  I'll be verifying my settings as
the UTC marches past 00:00 to verify the correct day on my logger.

I've probably missed LOTW confirmations in the past because I never paid
much attention to the correct DAY/UTC stamp on QSO's that are within the 1
hour DST.

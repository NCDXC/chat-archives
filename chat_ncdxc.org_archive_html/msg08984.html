Fellow NCDXC’ers,

Here is my two cents on this discussion.

I am in the camp that thinks DXCC is a personal achievement and not competitive.
I don’t see how it could be competitive anyway.  Their are too many variables 
to have a level playing field.  One example is our individual qth’s.  One DXer could 
live in a low area with hills blocking some directions, another could be on a mountain top 
with down sloping terrain.  One could be right next to the ocean…………  you get my drift ?
I don’t care how others achieve their DXCC entities.  I enjoy doing the best I can with 
my own station, such that it is.  Its very rewarding to me to improve my antennas on the low bands,
160 for example, from my little city lot and see my signal improving and contacts increasing.

DX is fun !

73,
Bob

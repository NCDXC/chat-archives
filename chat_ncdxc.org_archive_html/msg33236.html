The DXpedition seems to be going well.  I worked them on several bands and modes yesterday on 2nd radio while playing in the VHF contest on another. It brought back memories of the FO0XX expedition there back in 1985 or 86.     Bob W6RGG was one of the ops.  He had asked me to help wit the preparations for the trip.  I ended up making long “extension cords”  out of 12/3 Romex and packing them in sealed 5 gallon buckets.  Thanks for letting me help Bob!

I do have to say that I was very disappointed with all the QRM on the 10mtr SSB freq yesterday.  The noise, foul language, DQRM, hords of “policemen” which were much worse than the inadvertent callers on the wrong VFO was awful.   I will say that the excellent TX5S Op, Andreas N6NU,  just powered thru and kept making contacts

 I was thoroughly disgusted with those antics. I ended up leaving the freq and went back to CW & FT8 where it was much more civilized, like ham radio was when I grew up around it as a kid.   What happened to the Gentlemen & Women of ham radio? ( I did not hear any YL Cops!)  I worry for the future of our hobby.  I was glad my grandkids were not listening. 

Dave ka6bim

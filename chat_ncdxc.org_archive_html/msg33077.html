For a T top vertical, EACH of the legs needs to be 84 feet long in
order to be resonant.  IOW, you would need 168 total feet of wire.
The trouble with an inverted L is that you waste a lot of power
making horizontally polarized radiation that doesn't propagate.
The T top doesn't have that problem.  At 43 feet, your radiation
resistance is something like 4 ohms, so put out lots of radials.
All of this is easily modeled with EZNEC, which is now free.

I once threw up an emergency 160m vertical made of 60 feet of
irrigation pipe, with 2 "umbrella wires".  Like a T top, except
the wires sloped down to a few feet off the ground.  It was
easy to tune to frequency by adjusting the ends like on
an inverted vee.  Then by trial and error I adjusted the
shunt matching coil to get 50 ohms at resonance.  The bandwidth
was only a dozen or two kHz over my good ground.  Wide
bandwidth indicates high ground losses.  I used it in a contest
and it actually got out pretty well.

Good luck.

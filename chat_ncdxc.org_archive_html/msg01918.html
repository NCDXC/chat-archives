On 4/15/2013 7:37 AM, paul@n6pse.com wrote:
What is your all time favorite rig?

Some perspective. My first real "rig" that wasn't WWII surplus was a Heathkit Apache that I built and an SX101 that a friend who couldn't pass his 13 WPM code test loaned me. Since then, I've owned and used a Drake 4-line, an HQ129X that I used with an HT37, a Ten Tec Omni A, an Omni V+, Kenwood TS850, Elecraft K2, Yaesu FT1000MP, and now K3s.  The only ones I bought new were the Omni A and the K3s.

Of those, I really loved the Apache/SX101, which were awfully good for 1959, the HQ129X, the TS850, and the MP, all of which were top line rigs for their time. No question though -- my all time favorite is the K3/P3, which I have loaded with the second RX. 

Why do I love the K3/P3?  It's the most bulletproof RX, cleanest TX, has the most features built-in (including lots of stuff that we used to have to add outboard, like equalizers,RS232 level converters for CW and PTT) and is by far the most flexible.  It's easy to make the TX audio really aggressive, and still sound very good and clean, with any decent mic. It has built-in RTTY and PSK decoding that's as good or better than any software I've used, and it can send RTTY and PSK from the paddle that I plug into it, and from CW memories. So I can chase RTTY DX in split mode without a computer attached!   And my neighbor a quarter mile up the hill says I don't take up any more of the band than another neighbor with an ICOM who's 7.5 miles from us.

73, Jim K9YC

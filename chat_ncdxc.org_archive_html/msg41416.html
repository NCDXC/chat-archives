Two in one; read down

On Dec 10, 2025, at 8:19 PM, Lucas Ford via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

ERP depends on the type of antenna (vertical here) and line loss calculations need to be done to do the math, I figure when 15w out from the radio, likely half reaches anyway, 150ft run.

Specifically the ERP depends on the antenna GAIN, (not the type or polarity) compared to a half wave dipole; so you have to MODEL the antenna (or depend on the manufacturer to be honest, few are, their goal is SALES) then use that gain/loss value.

ERP is watts X gain AND then that total =< 9.15 watts (or 100 watts for the channels) to reach the allowed value.

Have gain, use less power; have loss (less than a dipole gain), use more.   I’m using the SteppIR DB36 rotating dipole, it has less gain than a full sized half wave dipole so 100 watts into it, is safe (on the channels, else 10 watts).

Then you have to measure the loss for EVERY connector, device (static arrestor) and line loss (depends on many factors) if you want the maximum legal output to the 1/10 watt.  If you’re using old coax (because we’re cheap); you still use the values of the manufacturer (which ARE pretty close for coax).

What the rules DON’T say, is: free space dipole or one affected by ground losses (a real mounted antenna); it matters.

Like I said, ERP is not a common test question, so it’s an area that many won’t grasp without guidance.  It may be common to NTIA, but for hams it’s relatively new (not just blasting 200/1500 watts into a feed line).


On Dec 10, 2025, at 8:38 PM, Bill - WB6JJJ via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

I presume that the new frequencies are now authorized for use since the document is dated 12/09/2025.


No.  As it says within the document (and with EVERY previous rule change; it becomes codified (law), 30 days AFTER being posted in the Federal Register (which hasn’t happened yet).  That 30 day window allows for changes (oops, misprint, corrections) and bringing it to Congressional review (rarely needed) BEFORE the change takes effect.

So once it’s printed, wait 30 days, THEN it’s lawful (figure mid-January, MAYBE a little sooner, but the gummint nevah moves fast, they wanna be home for the holidays too).

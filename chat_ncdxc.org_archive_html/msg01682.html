Alan, thanks it is interesting to have your perspective. It would be interesting to hear from Robert-KC6AWX too.
 
Among DXpedition organizers the Bureau System is often discussed with some disdain. Not to knock the fine work that you are doing, but DXpedition leaders dislike the many bureau cards that they get from EU/JA stations.
 
They don't want to deal with responding to many thousands of cards that come to them sans donation and take time, resources and some expense to return. They obviously prefer that OQRS or a direct card with a small donation is used, however many still send a card through the bureau. This does nothing to help the bottom line of the DXpedition.
 
There will be a time when there is going to be an ultra rare DXpedition that is going to say "Direct or OQRS cards only", no bureau cards. That time is coming and is very much discussed.

Paul, IMHO the Bureau will be around for years to come.  Amateurs around the world still like to receive
the hard copy QSL for a contact.  LoTW is great because it allows fast confirmation and no fuss.  The
cost is almost nothing compared to sending and receiving a hard copy QSL card.  I think being able
to use the OQRS system is another means to receive your cards without sending one.  However,
since almost everything is rather costly these days, the Bureau is a very inexpensive way to send and
receive cards although the time for return can be months to years.  Our CA bureau has about 26
(give or take) volunteers and cards are mailed every 3-4 months.  We are all managed by Robert
Devine, KC6AWX.  He is also a member of the NCDXC so perhaps he may have a word or two on
the subject.  Hope that helps Paul.
 
73 Alan

Russ,

 

I saw Ted’s Silent Key listing as well.  Ted ran our Customer Service Dept. at Farinon Electric when I started there in 1973.  He was a master at talking customers through tricky troubleshooting procedures, always keeping them happy.  He told great stories at lunch time in the cafeteria about his years as a ship’s operator in the merchant marine.  I saw him about 5 years ago at Visalia and I think at that time he had just turned 90 and was spry as ever.  He was a long distance runner and in his running outfit his small frame gave him the look of Mighty Mouse.  At one point he had a 4-element cubical quad on that 70’ tower and was a real DX Hawg!  His phonetics were “Whiskey Six Big Juicy Hamburger”.

 

Ted will be sorely missed here.

 

73,

Steve

I see in QST that Ted  W6BJH has passed away.  Ted was a long time member of NCDXC,  and my neighbor here in Belmont.    Ted was a avid DXer who liked the low bands  80 & 160 meters.   He had a 70 foot tower in the hills of Belmont  with a 80 meter Delta loop and a 160 meter inverted L.   He was a great guy who memtored me when I first moved to Belmont.  He will be missed.

Dear Friends and Colleagues,

I usually think I'm pretty wary about online scams. But apparently not today.

I was searching for a spare antenna for my MFJ-1789 rotating dipole, to replace it when it eventually corrodes or fails.

As you know, MFJ Enterprises closed last year, and although they are selling off their inventory, they no longer stock most of their former items.

Imagine my pleasant surprise when my search revealed that the supposed  "new" MFJ, at newmfjenterpr.com, has my antenna at a deeply discounted price!

I entered my address, phone, and credit card info, but mysteriously the SMS verification text didn't arrive. I was prompted to enter a different card. The same thing happened again.

After the third time, I got suspicious, and found that the "New MFJ" is blocked by Norton and has been reported by others to be a scam site, billing people's credit cards from a non-existent address in China.

So - avoid this so-called "New MFJ". I cancelled my credit cards, and hopefully that will be the end of it.

When it seems too good to be true, it probably is.

73,
Andy Calman K6ZP

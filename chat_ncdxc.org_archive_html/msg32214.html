Well, I thought I had a bug in my SW since I was fully uploaded to CLubLog but it did not show my UX FT8 contacts.  They were even LOTW confirmed.  After some digging, I learned that no operation was permitted until June 2023 and any prior ops were invalid.  LoTW revoked the cert, too.  So, my several XU from February are now 'dust in the wind' and time to root for ';another operation.  That will need a local or someone employed there, not visitors per this info from DX-News:
A LoTW certificate was issued for the XU7GNY callsign, but was subsequently revoked. The operation by XU7GNY in 2023 is unacceptable for DXCC, in spite of statements to the contrary by some DX Bulletins. (Update on 2023-11-01 by the DXCC Desk (tnx K9EL) – source

NOTE II:

More info on XU7GNY.   Ham radio was not allowed in Cambodia until June 2023.  XU7GNY was operating in Feb/Mar.  In addition, the license that was provided to the DXCC desk was expired.  Therefore, his approval was revoked. If XU7GNY was able to renew his license, then the current operation will be OK.  We will have to wait and see if and when he sends documentation to the DXCC desk. 73 John K9EL

Please be informed that the Radio Frequency License (issued by TRC) can’t be issued to tourists for short visits. The license can only be issued to foreigners in the condition as follows:

1) Having long-term/permanent working visa in Cambodia. 2) They are living or working in Cambodia. 3) They have job/workplace ID in Cambodia –

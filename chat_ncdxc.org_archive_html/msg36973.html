As a Novice 62/63 always looking for good practice as I copied a UPI News report from the Belgian Congo in CW from what appeared to be a battery operated radio! at about 20 WPM.  I copied the entire news report.  

Anxious to read the story the next day in the San Jose Mercury News, I found his report however it was not the report he sent at all, rather a watered down version of what was going on at this rubber plantation in the Belgian Congo.  

 My dad (W6LEH) commented, we would not be publishing a story that spoke poorly of one of our allies, as bad as it was.  An interesting lesson at 14.

I also started my radio journey as an SWL but after the Heathkit crystal radio that put me to sleep listening to LA Dodger games.
i built a Heath GR54 and used it to tune all the international bands, including some that were way off the usual path, like below 3.5 MHz.  I am not sure if I earned DXCC as an SWL but still have a pile of QSL cards from my listening reports sent in.  Got lots of paperwork and pendants, etc over the early days.  It was tne neighbor working 10 M AM that got into our stereo system that ended up being my elmer to Novice in 1970.
I put in a LOT of hours listening all over and reading the World Radio/TV book.  I dreamed of someday owning a Drake SPR4 Receiver that had the 1 kc dial.  Man, if I had one of those....

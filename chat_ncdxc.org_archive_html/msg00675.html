What they are suggesting is really just basic common sense operating. I don't see anything controversial there. All DXpeditions would benefit from the adoption of these guidelines as they are written.
 
What I don't like to see is that DXpedition funding may be tied to the adoption/implementation of these guidelines. Most clubs/funding sources already impose certain requirements that must be met or agreed upon before a DXpedition receives funding.
 
Many clubs require QSL service for their members, an article, a copy of the DVD/Video if there is one. Almost all funding sources require processing Bureau Cards.  Some require the Code of Contact logo on the DXpedition website before they will even consider funding the DXpedition. Almost all EU clubs/funding sources do not make their donation until weeks or months after the DXpedition. This requires the DXpedition leaders to finance the operation themselves with the hope to recoup some of their expenses when the clubs make their donations.
 
Earlier this month, OH2BH suggested in his NCDXF newsletter article that DXpedition funding should consider how many DXPP's or inexperienced DXpeditioners are on a team. If there are too many rookie operators, that should impact the amount of funding available to the DXpedition.
 
That would have really hurt the CY9M operation, which was a pretty good DXpedition comprised of mostly newer DXpedition operators. Why do anything to discourage new people from doing DXpeditions?    Why do anything to discourage DXpedition leaders to bring newer/inexperienced operators on a DXpedition?
 
The current DXpedition funding situation is seriously broken and the implementation of these rules or guidelines for DXpedition operators as another means to apply funding is going in the wrong direction.
 
It would be interesting to see what is being said about them on the DX-news reflector.
 
My two cents....
 
 
Paul N6PSE

Hello,
We believe that much of the chaos on the bands during DXpeditions is caused by the operating tactics of the DXpedition operator – this leads to poor operating by DX-Chasers, and therefore to chaos. At a meeting of RSGB, CDXC and GM-DX on October 13th we agreed to strengthen the ‘DX Code of Conduct’ and to prioritise the DXpedition side. This has now been completed, thanks to Wayne N7NG and ‘DX University’, and has been renamed ‘Best Practices for DXpedition Operating’. Please see:
 
http://www.dxuniversity.com/showpage.php?id=20&title=Best_Practices_for_DXpedition_Operating
 
or go to www.dxuniversity.com click on DXpeditioning Tools, then Basic Practices. Each of the 13 points is hyper-linked to some explanatory text.
 
We would hope that DX organisations will adopt it as a  condition of funding. This would hopefully spread to become the normal requirement for all DXpedition funding: DXpeditions would not only ‘support’ it, but would also implement it on-air. In this way we expect that chaos on DX bands can be reduced somewhat whenever a DXpedition is under way.
 
I look forward to seeing your comments,
73 de Roger/G3SXW.

I'm looking forward to hearing from our more technically erudite members. It's always been my understanding that because dielectric grease is non-conducting, it should only be used to protect the external parts of a connection to avoid corrosion. It is commonly supplied with spark plug wire replacement sets and meant to be used on the inside of the boot to avoid arcing--- but emphatically not applied between metal-to-metal electrical connections.  Although conductive grease (moisture proof grease, similar to marine grease, "salted" with ground conductive material such as copper or aluminum) does exist, and I've used it for antenna construction to ensure continued electrical continuity between element parts, I've never used it at coax connectors for fear of creating an electrical short. I use the self-bonding silicone tape to weatherproof coax connections at the antenna or balun. On the mating portions of a barrel connector silicone dielectric grease would function as an insulator, which would seem to be counterintuitive to the idea of making a connection.

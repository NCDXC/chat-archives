I believe that the ARRL does have a well known process.

First you must show evidence of being in the country. For P5, an entry stamp in your passport would be sufficient.

Then one must show evidence of having a 'license'. That gets tricky with P5, which doesn't issue ham radio licenses. Dom must have had someone's permission or he would have been in lots of trouble operating a transmitter in P5. The EA group that was (is?) planning a P5 operation has complained that he didn't obtain permission from the correct authorities.

How does ARRL determine who in P5 has the authority to permit amateur radio operation?

Also, LotW is not the same as DXCC. One can (and has) get a LotW account for an operation that does not receive DXCC credit. If you attempt to use one of those LotW QSLs for DXCC credit, it will be rejected.

Steve, K6UM

ADDING MY 2-CENTS HERE. 

First Amplifier I ever had was a homebrew 4x811A built by Cliff Kurtz, N6ZU (SK). got it back in 1975. It was a tank that I wish I had today but stupidly sold before a cross-country move.  The amp is still in service today, and the ham who is enjoying it promises its return when he's a silent key (hihi). 

This should speak to the long-lasting tolerance of the Collins 30L1 which was Amp #2 purchased off ebay in 2003 for $300.00 + $150 to a good-hearted ham in San Francisco who added new PS caps, Harbach soft-switch mod, etc.  Island Amplifier in San Diego replaced a parasitic resister and the 811As when I let the smoke out a few years ago.  Even tube amps have a limited tolerance for long FT-8 key downs and/or high SWR so backing off the drive and an external tuner is a must!  

I have 2 LDG 600w auto-tuners on the shelf here if you're interested in one for a good price - email me. 

First amp recommends:

When Annie N6ACL got her Extra in 2016 she also used and tuned the 30L1. When our son Shane K5SML, got his General, we decided on the KPA-500 + KAT500 tuner. Once again, simple and very easy to use.  Highly recommended as a first amplifier.  One side note:  If you're using the KPA500 with the IC-7300, it plugs right into the back of the radio. MAKE SURE YOU SET THE RADIO AT NO MORE THAN 25W DRIVE!  10-12 Watts on FT-8 will give you 200+ watts out - on FT8 no more than about 8watts drive for 100w out.  Yes, I learned this one the hard way on CW when I was trying to work an ATNO and sent 100W into the KPA-500. It did not like it and was a $300 repair bill :) 

If you have the budget, KPA500 is a great amp solid state and is easily keyed by the IC-7300 (remember the drive power limitation) 

OTHER Tube Amp consideration: 

Yaesu FL-2100B is another nice tube amp in the shack. Easy to tune and puts out a solid 600 - 800 w with about 60w drive.  Mine is interfaced with an Ameritron ARB-704 to our IC-7600 since older tube amps cannot be directly keyed by most solid-state rigs. By the way Collins 30L1 is switched from the FTDX10 through a hamgadgets "opticoupler relay" kit that I built for about $25 

Steve brings up a good point about DBs and s-units that you should consider 

100w - 200 w = 3db or 1 s-unit bump
200w - 400 w = 3db or another 1 s-unit bump (so 500w amp puts you 2 s-units above the competition) 
400w - 800 w = 3db or another 1 s-unit bump 
800w - 1600 w = 3db or another 1 s-unit bump (so "full-legal limit"  gets you 2-3 s-units above the 500w amp and 3 - 5 s-units above the 100w competition) 

all things to consider.  Also make sure your audio stream is operating to its fullest (but not over driven) potential - good full properly shaped audio will make your 400w sound like 800w ....

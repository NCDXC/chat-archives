I had a HyGain 2 element 40 meter mono bander and turned it into a 30 meter two element beam.  Don't remember the dimensions, its still up on the tower.  But I did shorten the boom quite a bit and used 300 ohm ribbon line in a "ZL" design to the shortened elements.  In the "ZL" design both elements are the same length and the 300 ohm line criss crosses along the boom and is attached at each element.  No balun is used, a center fed dipole connector with coax connector is attached at one of the boom ends to the 300 ohm line and the RG213 tie wrapped along the boom to the center.  I agree with Mike, I used dipole dimensions for the elements.  The boom was only 11 feet.  Since you have no driven element with the "ZL" design you will hear signals quite well off both ends.  Also much less wind load (which was a big help here in SE Michigan.  The ZL is the antenna on the right.  The other is the good ole TH6DXX.  As you can also see, I have very good take off from the shack on the hill.  Maybe not every ones cup of tea, but sure works for me.

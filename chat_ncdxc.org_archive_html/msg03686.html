Some of you attended my Pacificon 2013 Antenna Forum presentation – and I thank you for that, I hope you enjoyed it. After watching Dean Straw’s presentation – where he showed the use of an “antenna.13” file that was generated from K6TU’s HFTASWEEP program, I had to try this myself.

 

With thanks to Stu, K6TU for his help – I have generated my own azimuthal plot based on one degree HFTA data for all 360 degrees (there is some data missing at roughly 357 – 359, hence that weird blip), but, check out the ridge that wipes me out from about 0 – 90 degrees!

 

Now you know why at times I have been an “alligator” and have had really rough “ESP” QSO’s. It clearly answers the question “Why is some guy in Berkeley – near the bay – hear rare Zone 21 DX S9, and I barely hear them at all?”. And – since you can see that my LP direction to Zone 21 and other nearby zones actually look pretty good – you can see why I couldn’t even hear stations on the SP – but where they were S9 on the LP – during our morning SR.

 

Illuminating for sure.

 

73,

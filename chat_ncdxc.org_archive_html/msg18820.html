> On Feb 15, 2020, at 2:35 PM, Rick Bates, NK7I <rick.nk7i@gmail.com> wrote:
> 
> Hi Bob,
> 
> It has been interesting to watch the different propagations between the areas.  When I lived in Cameron Park (or Sonoma County), what was heard in the bay area was pretty much what I could hear/work.  But you got me curious about how far apart we really are, I was using the SWAG method.
> 
> Using Google Earth (because CA isn't straight north),  I drew a line exactly south of my place.  I'm 330 miles east of SF and ~770 miles north of that line; 805 miles direct (not far from my guess) .  I'm a little further from the coast than I thought too (it's about a 6 hour drive normally, but 380 miles, I guessed about 280).  I used SF specifically as a base line measurement.
> 
> One wouldn't think that the difference wouldn't matter a lot on HF, but clearly it does (Top Band in particular).  Sometimes I hear things first, sometimes it's reversed (or not at all).
> 
> My point of posting is simply to share, to help others as they have helped me over the years.  If they (or I) hear it or not from what is posted, I can't change because that gets into local conditions like noise floor, antenna and radio types; just too much to figure.  Then there is the spotlight effect that happens on some bands...  all part of the magic of radio.  Posting is not bragging or anything else, I'm grateful for FINALLY being able to hear; I want to share what I hear (and others seem to appreciate it).
> 
> I lock DXWatch tabs (at the same time) for each DXpedition of interest (and PSKReporter so I can see where I'm spotted).  That lets me pay attention to the overall band conditions and check where each team is operating based on reports.  Then I have an app (Ham Alert) that filters the spots posted by US listeners (east coast first, almost always) to give me a heads up if I'm not actively watching.  That saves me from having to stare at all the clusters, all day.
> 
> I'm not quite a big gun station; won't ever consider myself to be one, even with the tower and monster antenna and amp.  I prefer simple (it ROCKS) and when done, this will be a simple station, two antennas for HF and 6M, up to legal limit.  Then add 2M and mostly done.  I know that most of us simply do the best we can, money, space and other needs affect that too.  (I was truly BLESSED to have escaped the loony bin known as CA.  There is LIFE away from 'that place' hi hi.)
> 
> I also have a 2M repeater (IRLP node), UHF DMR node and other toys to play with, hi.  I'm not bored with radio yet; there's too many ways to put things together and explore and have fun!
> 
> Let me know if I'm posting too often, too much or beyond the necessary please.  Together, we can all work all the DX and enjoy ourselves.  I'm considering loaning out the use of the station for contesting, it'll soon be SO2R ready (and I have a guest room now).
> 
> 73,
> Rick NK7I

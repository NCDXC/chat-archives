Former NCDXC Secretary and now an Associate member Bill, N6BM,  has organized a proficient group of Florida operators who will operate during Field Day with the special event call "N4A"  .  We will operate from the shores of the Gulf of Mexico in a small fishing town called Cedar Key.  It is IOTA NA-076 and the rare Florida county of LEVY.  https://www.qrz.com/db/n4a

We will have nine CW and SSB operators working on generator power.  
One of our CW operators works at Apple Computer in San Jose and he'll fly out for the weekend to join us.

I'll be uploading our logs to LOTW at the close of Field Day  

ANTENNAS:  
       Cushcraft R8 vertical (6 - 80 meters)
       Cushcraft A3S beam (10/15/20 + 40m kit) on a mobile 33' tower with rotor       
       6 meter beam (on the mobile tower)      
       Alpha Delta DX-CC dipole at 52' above ground   
       Carolina Windom 10 - 80 meters

N4A is listed on the IOTA website for this weekend.  I was contacted by Bernie of the Daily DX that N4A be listed as well. 

I look forward to working and hearing friends of the NCDXC

73 de Bill, N6BM

On Wed, Jun 22, 2016 at 10:41 PM, K6XN via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

K6ON, K6XN, K6YN and K8MP with guest operators K6III, KB8NEW and Theresa (studying to earn her license) will operate this weekend for Field Day as W6DOJ from K6XN’s radio site and cabin located in the Tahoe National Forest at 5000 feet elevation in the California Sierra using solar power, batteries and as needed Honda EU2000i generators. We will be running low power (150 Watts) and using beloved antique equipment e.g. four Icom IC-751A transceivers. We will be using four element KLM beams on 40,20,15 and 10M, a 546 foot horizontal loop antenna on 160M and an inverted Vee antenna on 80M. K8MP, Joe, is travelling to our FD site from Ohio and KB8NEW, Brian is travelling to our FD site from Utah.

See you in the pileups! 73, Ted, K6XN for the W6DOJ Team

Jon, 

do I represent the leadership? I am one of the directors, so I represent a fraction of the decision making process. I am probably the most vocal of the board members and I am sticking to what I said. I agree with you that the tone makes the music. However it is Paul who chose the venue and tone. 

I am not moody. It is Paul’s hypocrisy that bothers me. It was him who called the voting of PT0S as NCDXC Expedition of the year embarrassing. He threw a tantrum on this list as to how wrong this was. The members had spoken.  Reaction? 

We agreed with Paul that it is not easy to pick a winner.  We decided to suspended the award until we had a better way to come up a winner. This has been on the agenda of every club meeting and board meeting for the last few months. We kept the membership in the loop as to where we were in the process. It’s documented in the minutes. 

How can you say that we lack openness?

What’s not creative about using the DX-world poll? 

I am just tired of being accused of going around the membership with every move the board does. 

I am sure Paul is doing this for the right reason. I just think he is missing the mark on the delivery part. 

Exhibit A:  

"BTW, I am not aware of a single club member besides you who has contracted the President, the VP, Secretary or the Board and voiced his/her disappointment about not having the DXer of the year award. You are the only one.”


This is not shining a light on this director alone but to those who sit by quietly allowing the flogging of one who wishes to question the norm and just maybe incite creativity and potency into our ranks; in my opinion the opposite of oppression, which surely is the "old way," 


The leadership does not and should not work in a vacuum. All input from the members is and will be taken into account at all times. We have been elected to by the membership to do business on their behalf. We do not have to run everything through a vote. We do constantly check everything against the Bylaws and the procedures manual. The decision process is documented and published during club meeting and on the web site.

Nobody will be yelled at for suggesting something to the board. However, if one starts attacking and bullying me in a public forum I will respond.  

The fact that there is no input from the membership on this matter can mean that they do not really care or that they silently agree. I believe it’s the later.

Where is the system to handle the hard questions?  Where is the intelligent approach to maintaining world class status?  What do we do with real talent and energy that may seem misguided?  Assuming we are wanting to evolve a vision of our own.

Reaction?  :-)


Jim has laid it out:

Sponsor the DX-Word DXpedition of the year poll and award the trophies at the Visalia convention. The NCDXC is front and center on the web site and front and center at the ceremony. That’s good PR. With over 4000 votes from 127 different countries it’s probably a good representation of perception of what was the “Best” expedition of the year is. 

The second part is to sponsor expeditions and tie the money to the promise to put emphasis on working the West Coast. We encourage the team leaders to consult with Stu, K6T,U to get the propagation analyzed to maximize the outcome for NCDXC member. 
 
73, Andreas, N6NU

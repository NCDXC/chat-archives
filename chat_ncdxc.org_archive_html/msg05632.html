Last summer, I took my Cushcraft MA160V vertical (76 buried radials) and put it on top of a Channel Master push up mast. Yes, I did the cardinal sin and have aluminum on top of steel – but made sure all connections were doubled up and that there is very good conductance all the way up the new 63’ verticals length.

 

I did this because I had been reading the efficiency of what the MA160V would be (in the ON4UN Lowband DX-ing Book) – and I think it was maybe 25%. I figured adding more metal in the vertical would increase efficiency and also add more “capture area” for RX. This does seem to be the case. It’s a surprisingly good RX antenna as well as TX.

 

I have no way of A – B testing, but with my 500 watts on 160M, I have been working much farther distance DX and I also do not have to resend my call (many) times as I used to with the “stock” MA160V. (And no – I could NOT do an inverted L – I would have had to route the horizontal part way too close to my AB-952 and high band antenna.

 

I apologize for this being all anecdotal – and yes, all of this could just be that 160M is in excellent shape this winter, but I am now up to 60 entities and have worked about 15 new ones on 160M just this winter – and it includes a very easy to work Namibia, and not too hard to work FT5ZM.

 

On RX – phasing the N6RK and K9AY through the MFJ-1025 gives great results (thanks Wayne, N7NG for the idea, and Jim, K9YC for the current choke design!).

 

My all-time favorite band is still 40M, but 160M is really threatening that. Of course, I still respect 20M for being the all-time “money band.

 

Ain’t radio fun?

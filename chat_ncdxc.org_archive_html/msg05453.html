Hmm... 

 

I have both the K2 w/dsp and K3 with multiple 8-pole filters, and always find that the K2 seems quieter and more "easy listening."  Have never been able to do decent side-by-side comparisons, so don't know if is just that the K3 is just a few dB hotter, and therefore more sensitive to band noise.  Also haven't worked with the NR and NB settings, not to mention the Rx equalizer, enough to really master it all.  

 

Still have to say that the K2 seems to be less fatiguing to listen to for extended periods and seems to do a better job with band noise. Just glad to hear that others have had the same experience.

What is it that you're trying to accomplish with the preamp and/or
filter? Is it that you're trying to differentiate signals from noise,
separate signals that are close in frequency, or...?

I've found that an outboard box is not necessary. Between the DSP in
the K3, judicious use of RF gain (and sometimes turning off AGC or
messing with the AGC settings), and various NB/NR schemes I can
usually get what I need. From my puny brain's standpoint, the most
effective thing would be within the signal path of the radio itself
rather than an outboard box, where all you can then control is the AF.

In the "old" days (read before the K3 came along) the K2 had a DSP
option - and that DSP board was *amazing* for pulling signals out of
noise. Without the DSP, a signal might be ESP, with the DSP, often it
went from ESP to Q5. Of course the devil's in the details, and the
many adjustments possible on the DSP board made it tough to learn but
once mastered, it was a FB option. The K3 has similar DSP options
available via the NR settings, and they are much easier to manipulate,
but I have yet to reproduce the same level of amazement from the K3 in
that regard as I was in the K2 with the DSP option. Still, the K3,
IMHO, does what I need it to do as-is.

That said, having a 2nd RX with diversity (and associated diverse
antennas) would make an improvement in your RX capability when there
is QSB to deal with. Similarly, in contests, being able to listen to 2
beverages at once for example, is very nice (2 discrete directions).

I'll be curious to hear what others have to say in terms of add-on
filters/preamps...

73 de N6GQ

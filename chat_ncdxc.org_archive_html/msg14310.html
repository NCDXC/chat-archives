Bob,

There is still one troubling section in EC-8 about re-calling directors.

If a Director is elected in a contested ballot, it takes signatures of 10% of the votes cast to start a re-call ballot. But, if a Director was elected without a membership vote (applies to over half of the current Directors), it takes signatures of 10% of the division members.

For instance, there were about 2500 ballots cast in the previous Northwest Division election. Thus a re-call petition would require 250 signatures. If the Director had no opponent or his opponent was declared ineligible, it would take 1200 signatures (10% of roughly 12000) for a re-call petition. That's almost 50% of the votes cast in the election. A very high threshold to attain.

How about making the re-call threshold 10% of the votes cast in the last contested election?

73,
Steve, K6UM

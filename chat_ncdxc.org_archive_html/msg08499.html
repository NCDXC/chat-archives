A year and a half ago, four CQ Field hunters--W6OAT, K9YC, W1CU and NI6T--stumbled upon a "target of opportunity" on (I think) the Seafarers Net on 14313--a ham-piloted boat cruising through empty field BM--understandably rare.  We happily worked WH7XH/mm, which was an ATNO for all.

WH7XH is neither a contester nor a DXer; his interest is sailing. Two of us QSLed immediately to his postal drop in South Dakota; two of us QSLed a bit later---and a bit late, as it turned out. The early birds got the worm, and the other two are still looking for a route that works. The SD address and a  Hawaii address in qrz.com are cold trails.

I know where the boat is now--off Mazatlan--from Winlink. I just staggered through registration of my callsign on Winlink, so I now have an account but I have stalled, i.e. I do not know how to proceed.

Anyone in NCCC/NCDXC have competence with using Winlink? I would like to email the ham/boat owner and put the arm on him for a QSL. If you can help, please email me directly.

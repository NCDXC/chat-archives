Answers woven within, HOWEVER, he has ASKED for tones to be > 1 KHz and NOT use F/H mode for less confusion (see his QRZ page)

On 12/28/2022 3:00 PM, Richard Jackson via Chat wrote:
   If I'm doing my math right, in your original example FT8WW was transmitting FT8 tones at 10,131,400 Hz (10.131 MHz + 400 Hz). You tuned your rig to 1000 Hz below 10.131 (10,130,000 Hz), and then transmitted your FT8 tones at 700 Hz, which would be 10,130,700 Hz. This would be 300 Hz below FT8WW's rig tuning, or "-300 Hz" on his receive waterfall? 

   My rig here consists of: Rx audio out (analog) to a PC sound capture "card" (SignalLink USB) and into my PC. Assuming my receiver has an SSB audio bandpass of 3 KHz, my normal FT8 waterfall displays (roughly) 0 - 3000 Hz, relative to my Receiver's tuned frequency. So if my Rx is tuned to 10.131 MHz, I think I am "seeing" 10,131,000 - 10,134,000 Hz on my waterfall. So it seems like your trick wouldn't work if you called me - your tones at 10,130,700 Hz would look like a "negative frequency" (relative to my Rx tuned frequency) that wouldn't be within my Rx audio passband. Granted, if I were to also detune my Rx frequency I could bring your 10,130,700 Hz FT8 tones into my received audio passband, but since my audio passband is fixed width (3 KHz), that would be at the expense of losing callers at the higher end of my original waterfall.

Your rig MAY limit you to 200-2700 Hz (Icom default for example) with an actual bandwidth of 2500 Hz RX.  Turning the bandwidth wider, does not always allow wider range of reception (at least in phone modes).  He's running a K3, we don't know his settings; it CAN listen higher if he wishes it to (pointless).

Your example would put you ~500 Hz too low to be heard; his cutoff is display ~f.200, you're below that point.  So you would use a tone 1200-1350 to NOT be on his tone (so rude when callers do that, no one wins) yet within his rig's ability to hear.

A simpler means, should you want to call below his tone, is simply use a low calling tone; the rig and SignaLink is able to manage this.  Stay above 200 (WSJT-X enforces this).

So my questions are:

   a) do you know something about FT8WW's receiver that tells you he is capable of "seeing" (decoding) FT8 frequencies below his base Rx frequency? If so:

He is using a K3, which will allow ~200 Hz to whatever upper limit he sets (3,000 Hz nominal).  Below 200, there is a sharp cutoff (by design).  So he can use 200 Hz to his chosen upper limit for callers (see note above).  Should you want to call below his tone, keep this limitation in mind (more so for little pistols with weak signals; you must be LOUD to overcome the dropoff).

That cutoff loss CAN be compensated, a little, by adjusting your shift downward, to a point.  A little, maybe.


   b) does that mean he can't "hear" (decode) up to a full 3000 Hz above his (announced) base Rx frequency, because he has traded it off to be able to hear "negative" FT8 frequencies. Or does his rig have a wider audio passband? (and how would you know?)

Him being on the actual dial displayed frequency, is a guess.  He could just as easily be down 1 KHz and using a higher tone; there is no way to tell.  However it's likely as reported on the spot lists.


Or, (c) do I just not understand how this works?  I am pretty new to FT8, so this is entirely possible  ;-)    Not doubting your results - just wanting to learn why!

Then this is a good learning point:  Do what the DX TELLS YOU TO DO (see note at the top here) for maximum chances.  ALWAYS.  EXACTLY what the DX says.

What we can HOPE for, is that he figures out how to work more than one station per stream for higher rates...

At my job on my company enterprise laptop, I am still running Windows 10 but Chrome has become unworkable with the enterprise security suite. 
It was constantly asking me to verify my identity every time I open up a
browser. The way the security is supposed to work (and always used to) is that it had a "single sign on" feature where I logged in once at the 
beginning of the day and was good all day after that.

The company IT guru said that Chrome has reached a dead end
because the version I have will not work with the security, but OTOH,
it can also not be updated to a version that might work.  Uninstalling
and reinstalling Chrome and clearing various caches didn't work.

However, he told me that Microsoft has basically given up the battle
against Chrome and incorporated Chrome into Edge.  Therefore, according
to him, there is no long any reason to use Chrome, because Edge has
the look and feel of Chrome.  Therefore, I just use Edge in place of
Chrome, and the security software is happy.  I as the user am also
happy because I can't tell the difference between the two browsers.

I was also told that it is getting increasingly difficult for the
company to remain on Windows 10.  We will eventually have to go to
Windows 11, and simply deal with any issues that come up with the
"upgrade" such as it is as they arise.

I remember hanging on to Windows 7 until the last minute before
upgrading to Windows 10 on all my personal computers and
everything just worked.  Maybe that will be the story with Win 11.

There seem to be some similarities between my experience and what
you are describing with LoTW.

I hope that may be helpful.

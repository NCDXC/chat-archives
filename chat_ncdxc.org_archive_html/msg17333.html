I always log F/H QSOs when I have gotten a signal report from the DX. When he sends the RR73, he has logged the QSO and will not send the RR73 again, even if you miss it. The worst that happens is you have a NIL from him, and if you go through OQRS, you'll find out then.
Should you find that you are in the DX's log, but he is not in yours, you can reconstruct the QSO using the data in ALL.TXT file in the WSJT-X folder (in Library/Application Support on a Mac). It has basic information about all your QSOs, back to the beginning of wsjt-x use, unless you delete the file. Mine is now at 39.8 MB, but I have lots of space on my computer.

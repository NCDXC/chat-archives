I prefer the "K3LR PL259" method for soldering PL259s.  It avoids all the
typical problems with soldering PL259s and produces a much stronger, highly
reliable connector installation.  It is easy to do with a Weller 260-watt
soldering gun.  Shorts, or opens, are eliminated.  Adding a piece of heat
shrink (ideally with the internal adhesive) makes a very nice-looking cable,
too.

http://www.k3lr.com/engineering/pl259/

Crimping is great, but many hams have problems like wrong die set for the
particular connector, wrong connector for the coax (LMR-400 and RG-213 use
different connectors), bad crimps, etc.  Often the cable will work fine at
first, and then fail later.

I've had so many pre-made cable assemblies fail that I'm leery of them.
They are usually crimped incorrectly.  Connectors have literally fallen off
the coax jumper.  Inspection reveals the problems above.

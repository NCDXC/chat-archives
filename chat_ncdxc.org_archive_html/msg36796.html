"They refused to even post their proposed location and dates of operation prior to arriving there. They also refused to post their operating frequencies (kinda necessary for SuperFox operation, since it doesn’t work on the normal FT8 frequencies.) Rich KE1B

Hi Rich,

It doesn’t seem odd at all, especially since the verification scheme has been cracked.

Mounting a DXpedition is a time consuming and usually complex project, with countless moving parts. Over the past several years it seems “claim jumpers” have jumped out in front of an announced DXpedition.

The process of getting a landing permit and arranging transportation only to have a claim jumper disrupt the project is not only frustrating, but you may have already paid nonrefundable transportation expenses. Remember what they did with claim jumpers during the gold rush days? Today we seem to put them on a pedestal.

Now you have a decision to make:  if the claim jumper drains the queue and leaves you dust instead of nuggets, can you recover even half your costs.

No Rich, it’s not odd, it’s a sign of the times.

73,
GS K5GS

Al,

Responding to your last comment. As a life long CW op I resisted digital modes for years. In fact until 2 years ago. My favorite band, 6M, essentially went completely digital ~ 5 years ago. Bottom line for me was get on digital or abandon 6M forever. 

Now 2+ years later these are a few of my observations. Some are relevant only to 6M:

1. On 6, digital modes with their sensitivity and everyone being in the same watering hole allowed us to discover propagation paths we didn’t know existed before. A huge plus. 

2. Again maybe 6M centric, leaving JTDX or WSJT running at night after time to go to bed led to further prop discovery. Alerts wake you up or you see what you missed in the morning. 

3. Operating skill shifts from integrating hearing and action to visual and action. Maintaining or finding a clear spot just uses a different one of our senses. 

4. Skill and experience on when and where to look for a path or DX station is about the same. 

5. Only really weak ones, big pileups having visual confirmation of a QSO is more satisfying to me than “I think I heard my call and report”. 

6. Living in an RF noisy environment it is my opinion that FT8 hears better in the presence of QRM/N. 

All that being said many others have similar thoughts to you. My thoughts only, your mileage may vary!

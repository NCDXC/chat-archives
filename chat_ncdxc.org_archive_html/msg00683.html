On Dec 11, 2012, at 11:00 AM, Tom Berson wrote:

> As the server appears to be a scarce resource, one constructive approach would be to ration its use. Think of a gas pump during a gasoline shortage.
>  
> For example, assign days of the month to letters of the alphabet. On any day, allow uploads only from stations whose first letter of the suffix matches that day's letter. The extra days of each month allow no uploads.
>  

That doesn't really improve performance. All it does is move the queue from ARRL to the individual computers of the LoTW users. Instead of a log waiting at the ARRL, it would be waiting in your machine. The waiting time will be the same.

The reason it helps for gasoline is twofold: First, there is a physical problem with managing queues of dozens/hundreds of cars spilling out from the gas station (they clog up the streets nearby), and second, there is a real cost associated with traveling to the gas station (fuel), sitting in line (idling, or inching forward), and the wasted time of the person behind the wheel. None of these costs exist in moving or storing logs waiting for LoTW processing.

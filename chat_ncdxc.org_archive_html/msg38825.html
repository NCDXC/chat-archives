Mike,

Welcome.  My terse question was not to create friction or belittle, but to get a rapid answer (and encourage a fuller post).  The more focused I am, the more terse I get (“Just the facts, ma’am”).

I suggest (and use) an email header of 
“DX Spot: <call> <entity> <freq> <mode> <option up xxx> <UTC time>” 
as the format.  That’s all the basic info required, in one line.  And as a header, it sits on top of the arrived email (UTC time because it may be seen/read hours later).

So in the heat of the hunt (more than one DX location worth spotting DOES happen); one can scan the email headers (only) to save time instead of having to glean the inside content and actually reading each email (and helpful when one has > 1 email list; same rule for the rest).

Further content within the email i.e. “Calling NA”, “LOUD”, “logged up xxx”, “Wow really slow/fast CW” etc. can be helpful.  Brevity in spotting, if complete in detail, awesome; talk later (the opening may not last).

Many of the folks here are long timers at DXing so WITHIN REASON they can just scan the headers if formatted as above and ignore what they already have (or don’t want).   With that, post away.

HOWEVER in short time you’ll figure out what is/not rare (the bulk of EU, not but a few are) and equally important to rare places are new bands (the ‘magic’ of radio) so you’ll get a sense of where/when.  This list is also not JUST CA (I’m in N Idaho, the list casts a VERY wide net; some readers are in other countries).

For example:  Italy, NOT rare; Vatican IS rare; Greece NOT rare; Athos IS rare; JA is never rare, hi hi (and there are more).  Some entities (few EU) are not often heard on the west coast because they are output power restricted (a few, severely); so while not rare, they are not common.

So you see there are shades of rareness (the main determination for being ‘spot worthy”) within (rare, not rare but uncommon, post; JA any afternoon, hold off); there is no simple definition (and it varies on the logbook of the DXer).

I track many of the DXpedition teams (when I have the time) because I want EVERY band mode slot they offer; so my spotting is based on that AND if a truly rare one pops up (North Korea, Afghanistan and other places you may never see again).  Whether you spot before/after you log, is entirely up to you.

Jump in.  Mistakes create learning opportunities and learning is fun (but the mistakes, maybe not fun) so dive in.  You’ll get there and it won’t take long.  We all started somewhere.

Hint:  The uncommon/rare/rare opening often generates comments (replies); so that is feedback into how you’re doing with posting.

73,
Rick nk7i

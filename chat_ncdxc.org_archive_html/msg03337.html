"9. All stations must be contacted from the same DXCC entity. The location of any station shall be defined as the location of the transmitter. For the purposes of this award, remote operating points must be located within the same DXCC entity as the transmitter and receiver."

 

 

This rule could benefit from a revisit.   I actually think this rule is somewhat overly accommodating for award validity.  In fact it could be made more strict.  K9YC made the choice on his own.  To Re-start his dxcc from w6 after relocating from Chicago. Perhaps a switch back to the 150 mile radius make sense. That would be reasonable, with the following condition..   a modification that would basically allow a remote site to become a “source QTH” and a new award could be started from that source QTH, remote, or in person.  What would not be cool is to allow one to jump from transceiver to transceiver at different worldwide qth’s to compete for a valid dxcc.. Unless they introduced something like an “Extreme mode” dxcc that supported experimentation and anything goes..  In the contesting world, the CQWW contest has acknowledged this and added a category for it.

 

Wouldn’t it be interesting to start a new DXCC award from the UK, or France and see what its like on the other side?  The one you were working on locally could and would still be valid.

 

The integrity of the program is preserved.  Embracing new technology is preserved.  Everybody wins.   There is more than one way to skin that cat!

 

Ok enough on this from me..

 

Have a great weekend everyone!!

 

Chris
N6WM

All.  Lets keep an open mind here.  Hams Innovate.  We always have, and we always will.  Ham radio will WILL change.  It always has, and always will.  Awards may even need to flex and change to support the changing landscape of amateur radio.

 

So my station is 19 miles from my house.  I have a K-Line (btw, the K/3 has a microprocessor or two in it and it would not be a stretch to call my rig a computer with an rf deck) and were I not able to remote my station I would be qrt.

 

Are you going to discount the qso’s I have made remote and say they are not good for DXCC?  I drive up there and operate my radio..  its good .. I operate it through the K3/0 that I bought from the innovative Elecraft amateur radio company that is an extention of my K/3 and its no good?  I don’t think so.

 

Seriously.  Remote operation is INOVATION and based on good HAM SPIRIT.  It  represents an advancement in amateur technology. (and that is what is great about HAMS and this hobby!) In my opinion this advancement and innovation is what amateur radio is all about.  I’m convinced it already has and will continue to breath new life into the hobby and further increase its relevance.   As folks age and some will have to go to retirement homes, they will still be able to participate in this hobby(and hopefully the dxcc program) because of this technology.   I was forced to move from my medium pistol qth and I would be qrt without this technology.  I hope this technology will enable qso’s  with a rare entity in a dangerous political or environmental situation to go on the air without risk to the LIVES of my friends.  Before you slam this part of amateur radio.. think about how it may benefit YOU now.. or in the future! 

 

I applaud all the trend setters and innovators that have call signs, that I have worked with and learned from to make it possible for me to be once again on the air, making regular QSO’s from the station I took 6 years to build with my friends and my hard earned $$ using this remoting technology.   It’s a pretty nice ham shack.. and I intend on continuing to operate it for DXCC credits, whether I am sitting there or here in Fremont.  I challenge you to tell the difference. (because there really isn’t one).

 

Keep in mind here.. the radio portion of the circuit is NO DIFFERENT than if you are sitting in front of the radio.  My K3/0 is DIRECTLY controlling my K line and displays the exact data that is on my rig on the hill.  When I hit the split button, the radio goes split.  I still listen to a vfo in each ear.  I send my cw with a kent paddle. I AM in direct control of the station.  Complete control.

 

THE BOTTOM LINE IS..  if I make a qso from K6LRG whether I am physically at my operating position or remoting from Fremont, I am still making a QSO from K6LRG Livermore.. to whoever and where ever. From CM97EQ.  Same circuit.  Same deal.  Can anyone dispute that fact?  Probably not.  Because it simply is fact.

 

I will support any initiative that ensures that qso’s made via remote are supported by DXCC.  I will aggressively oppose any initiative that discourages it.

 

For the love of the hobby!

 

Chris

N6WM

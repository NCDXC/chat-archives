Steve, my perception of 5X8C is essentially the same. They had very poor signals and very low audio. I thought I had worked them but I am not in their Club Log. I noticed that they were on the low-bands during times when they could have been working North America.
 
While the solar numbers were less than ideal, TZ6BB and 9U4U had decent signals and were working all across the USA at the same time. My experience with Fernando TS6BB has been superb and my experience working 9U4U has been good. These other two operations are making an effort to work and satisfy the need.  
 
I hope that the upcoming operations from Burkina Faso make an effort to do the same. I suspect the Italians will, however the last JA group that went there worked JA's and the US east-coast and then went home leaving quite an unfilled need.
 
Good luck to all!
 
Paul N6PSE

No success with 5X8C at all. Fortunately, I only need 5X on RTTY. But, my RTTY experience says much about this operation (& others by this group in the past). RTTY was only operated on the last day. Best signal on 20m I had heard from them. Spent most of the time working French stations with an occasional US station. I did hear them work W1SRD.

They clearly knew that Zone 3 was missing from their logs, but made no attempt to rectify the situation.

On the other hand 9U4U has been easy to work, all bands/modes. I've not heard them louder than S4, but that seems to be enough.

Hi Rick,
Nice video, some useful ideas, but you're mostly putting lipstick on the pig, and have somehow overlooked a simple solution that already exists. LOTW records the IOTA designator when it is uploaded by the IOTA station, and the IOTA designator will be part of the information QSL screen when the other station clicks on the QSL button. The same is true of grid designators, which are used for the CQ Field award.
I recently applied for and received credit for 175 fields, which qualified me for the Honor Roll. CQ accepts eQSL in the form of a screen print of the QSL, and when I showed the CQ Field administrator that an LOTW confirmation included the Field, he agreed to accept a screen print of the LOTW confirmation. eQSL and LOTW screen prints accounted for roughly half of my Fields. I then went through my boxes of received cards and found QSLs for all but about 15 Fields that I'd worked, so my total QSL cost to confirm 175 grids was about $60 (figuring an outbound stamp plus green stamps averaging $3.50 per QSL, plus the card and envelopes).
Not only that, but the CQ Field administrator agreed that I could have the cards checked by a local card checker, and even by W6OAT, who has something like 225 Fields confirmed. I gave Rusty my stack of cards and screen prints, he spent an hour or two, found a couple of my errors (wrong/missing cards, or wrong Field), I corrected them (finding correct/missing cards, finding another Q for the wrong field). and we were done.
I have no patience for the RSGB crying poor about this, nor do I have any patience for their refusal to accept hand printing of information on a card from a non-expeditioner. If a guy happens to live on an IOTA and sends me a QSL with an IOTA identifier, or with an address that's on an IOTA, that QSL ought to count. Their problems are of their own creation -- two card checkers for the entire USA? No LOTW, and no system of their own.
RSGB's problems with low participation are entirely self-inflicted. Paper QSLs in today's world are a complete anachronism, and as you have noted, are bloody expensive. ARRL has put together a system that is robust with respect to preventing fraud, and its nearly free for users. So what if RSGB can't develop their own. Why should they -- one already exists at ARRL. All they have to do is make the decision to accept screen prints, and all the DXpeditioner has to do is get certified with LOTW and upload the log. And once that's in place, the DXpeditioner can say "LOTW only, no paper QSLs." No need for a QSL manager, no QSL printing costs, no mailing costs, no time to prepare and send cards. And everyone who needs a QSL saves $4 per IOTA, that can then be sent to the expeditioner to support the trip. Win, win, win. 

73, Jim K9YC

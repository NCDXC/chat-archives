Just one last comment. I appreciate getting the initial grid report. Otherwise, I can’t be completely sure I know where you’re transmitting from. If you’re portable in a different grid zone or worse state or entity from your normal QTH I have no way of knowing that and potentially incorrectly logging it for a grid or WAS award. It may not be an issue with an DXpedition or some station you intend to verify via QSL cards, but as a standard FT8/4 practice it seems a bit problematic for the other station.

Just a thought…

- Robert, AI6P

On Feb 8, 2026, at 14:00, Al N6TA <al.n6ta@gmail.com> wrote:

Well, three differing responses!
Dave: I have never had someone try to speed up a rate by just going RR73.  It would be a manual thing since he would have to be in control to bypass normal operation, at least in WSJT-X.  I hope this does not catch on.  I am not logging it but will look to see if he does.
Robert: It is quite common for someone to make an initial call on TX2 and i do it when the QSB is bad and I fear losing the path.  This was not the case here since I sent TX1 and he came back with the TX4 or RR73.  This is not a case of hurrying up a contact, IMO.
Ryan: I run FT4 and FT8 and have never used the RRR.  The RRR is a setting you must make and it sticks AFAIK.  I do not believe this is a FT4 vs FT8 thing.

I will assume Dave is right or my guess is that he was telling me to get lost.  I had worked him before so it was a dupe his log would have found, if he cared.

One of those things!

Mike,

I think this is a healthy discussion and we need to have it. New technologies create new challenges and distort the fabric of the universe. Life will never be the same.  Some members will always be on the other side of any issue. We are more or less a cross section of society and there are differences. Nobody is completely right and nobody is completely wrong. Some members are more vocal than others, but it does not make everything they say right or wrong. It’s just another data point and we should welcome it. Take is as it is meant to be  - an opinion and not the absolute truth.

Just imagine the time when SSB was introduced. "Heresy! What’s wrong with them Donald Duck sounding signals. For real hams, CW is the way - all the way! Pry the key of my dead cold fingers …"

Packet clusters? Cheating! Find your own road kill, don’t rely on others to do your homework. We, the group got over it. Now we are all using it.  

Trust issues? Well, it’s all based on trust. How do I know that a #1 Honor Roll member was not running more than 1.5KW. How about 30M? I am sure there are plenty of 200W+++ signals on the air from the Bay Area. The same is true for contesting with assisted/unassisted. I really need to trust what the operator claims he/she did. 

Why not trust that the remote station is where it tells us it is?

If somebody cheats in our hobby, they cheat themselves. 

Just my $0.02 of MY opinion. 

73, 

Andreas, N6NU

Respectfully, shouldn't the onus be on those who want to change the rules to make a conclusive, strong case that a) there is a problem; and b) their solution will solve it?  The current rules have stood since before I was born, and DXCC looks in good shape to me.  Making that case that our current rules are broken ought to be a high standard to meet.  I haven't seen anything yet that convinces me there's a real problem, let alone that anyone has a good solution.

Also, as a relative newcomer to the hobby, it's really dispiriting to read all the comments (here and elsewhere) clamoring to take away other people's harmless privileges.  It does not reflect well on us, in my opinion.

On Aug 30, 2022, at 3:34 PM, Bill - WB6JJJ via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

That would create havoc with attaining DXCC, Honor Roll and #1 Honor Roll.  With the numbers needed for these awards changing you may lose your Honor Roll standing if one was added.  If you had 329 and Honor Roll then when one was added requiring 330, would you turn in your plaque?

No, it wouldn’t create any serious problems. Remember back on October 10, 2010 (10/10/10), when a bunch of DXCC entities were deleted (Bonaire-Curacao, etc.) and four new ones were added (all in the former Netherlands Antilles). On midnight of that day, anyone who was “#1 Honor Roll” was now short by four entities. Of course, there was a wild week when a bunch of DXpeditions were active in these four entities (it’s easy to get folks to go to Sint Maarten, Bonaire, etc. for a week; it ain’t Bouvet!), and everyone scrambled to get them in the log. Now they are all fairly commonplace, and the DXCC list is back to normal.

Entities come and go. Think South Sudan, Kosovo, etc. Each of these “knocked down” everyone on the Honor Roll by one peg, which they had to get back. That’s part of the “job” if you care about such things.

I agree that the bigger question is what to do with entities that are ON the list, but either have not, or can not be activated due to governmental or environmental restrictions. Turkemistan comes to mind, along with North Korea and some French-controlled islands. Now their status MAY change at some point, but quien sabe? I would support a temporary “graying out” of these entities (i.e., they exist, but for all intents and purposes are unavailable for amateur radio), but I’m not pushing for it.

In fact (and I have said it before), I don’t give a rat’s pitootie about the ARRL DXCC program. I don’t send them my updated “entities/bands/modes worked” list, and I don’t pay them to send me any certificates or plaques. I have the KE1B DXCC program, in which I know who I have worked, and who I have not. Sure, I like to work “a new one”, or fill a band/mode slot, but it’s purely for personal satisfaction, and I don’t have to “prove” it to anyone else.

Rich KE1B

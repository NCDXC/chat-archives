I have an idea, that I shared with a couple of you already.  For either our upcoming club meeting or our picnic, I think we could have fun for everyone and learning moments.

Questions I have asked years ago and others are asking now, mostly because they are fairly new to DX.  What does a DXpedition operator hear when one tried to call them via CW?


Our meeting event sequence.

First:
    We ask a small number (3 - 5) of our CW operators to be a local DXpedition. All we ask that they have good ears, no external decode devices.  Paper and pencil (log) are okay.  No exchange is required.

Second:
    We ask the remaining members of the audience to send their call, as one would for a real DXpedition, but using a tone generator of some sort and their CW key.  Laptop and other devices are okay to generate morse code.

Third:
    Start calling the DX for a period of one minute.

Fourth:
    At the end of one minute, each DXpedition operator indicates how many call signs they managed to correctly copy.

Five:
    Feedback from our DXpeditors.
    Verify their list, winner with the most confirmed call signs receives an award.

Six:
    Conduct at least two more sessions.


Just an idea to have fun and potentially learn something.

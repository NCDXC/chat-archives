While most of you have been chasing the ATNO (successfully) on 20m and above, I have been tracking 30 and 40m. Here is today's report from my station.

40m, As far as I can tell, no E3 40m appearance today.

30m. Activity reported this morning in the 1500 to 1700 slot, LP?. I was not there. I have no idea if any locals were successful. I am blocked LP.

Sunset here was 0207Z. First VE7CC postings this evening were at 0402Z by a YO. K7SP posted at 0418--I presume he got through. 5 minutes later, he reported "W6-W7 up 1 to 2", followed by some Euro whining and W4QN at 0430Zand K5UR (big station in AR) at 0439. That appears to terminate the postings for west coast--at the very end of the likely opening for us. So the first 2 hours + must have been a free-for-all for the midwest and east coast. That is not breaking news. 

Where was I? Doing chores, and when I finally got to the radio, I heard nothing at all on 10123 from the E30 but noted a huge split pileup from the rest of NA etc, which continues as I write this at 0510, presumably with EU. How many are running >200W? Take a WEG! Seems too late here. The lowband openings are short in duration.

The ops are apparently still feeling their way--the call for west coast came too late for most.

I shudder to think of what the 80m pileup will be if 30 and 40 are zoos. Right now zero Q's in the log may be my fate and that of others unless the operators look for zone 3 starting at our sunset---not 2 hours later when the opening such as it is has already peaked. My E3 summary for today? No shots fired. 30mm antenna here a linear-loaded rotary dipole at 130 ft.

Garry, NI6T

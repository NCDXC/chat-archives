On Dec 26, 2014, at 7:13 PM, SPWoo via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Here's my stack configuration.  Two-element shorty 40 at the bottom, a tribander 7' above the shorty 40, and a five-element 6m beam 4' above the tribander, turned 90 degrees.  I figured I won't be spending a lot of time on 6m hence the 90 degree offset doesn't bother me.  Just subtract 90 degrees from the indicated beam heading to get the true heading (I turned the 6m beam counter-clockwise from the tribander).  For example to work the Caribbeans your indicated heading should read 180 as opposed to 90.  I didn't want to take a chance with pattern degradation on other bands.

On Friday, December 26, 2014 1:34 PM, Ak6zz via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:


At my old QTH I had a six element Innovantenna OP DES (4.8m boom) 8' above my Mosley S33 (40,20,17) 3 element yagi with no issues. Both played nicely together.

I never sandwiched it between another yagi but on another occasion I had a hex beam 2' above a seven element M2 6m antenna and I didn't see to have any issues on any band.

I am sure the purists would advise against this but in my experiences it worked fine.  You could always model this configuration using yagi software but that would be something I haven't learned yet but have always wanted to learn.

Here at my inlaws I have two 6m Yagis (second one going up tomorrow) and it is a really fun band.

Phone patches remind me of one of my favorite ham radio stories.
It was sometime in the late 1970s, and we were having a party at our house in Los Gatos for the local caving club. There were about 50 people in the house when the telephone rang. Someone picked it up, looked at my wife and asked, "Peri, will you accept a collect call from the South Pole?" Peri immediately said, "Oh that must be Ed. Of course we'll accept the call."
Ed was a caving friend, and it turned out that about half the people in the house knew him. The telephone went from person to person and about 1/2 hour later, everyone had talked to Ed and we said goodby.
I was thinking in trepidation about how much this call was going to cost, but it wasn't all that bad. The call was actually from a ham in the Central Valley. He had Ed on a phone patch from a radio station set up at the South Pole Research Station. 

Now, that's what phone patches are for.

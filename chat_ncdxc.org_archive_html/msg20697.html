As I understand it, a DXCC Challenge band-point is equivalent to a DXCC band entity.

CW, Phone and Digital modes count for Challenge, but multiple modes do not increase the Challenge score.  DXCC Mode awards are available but they are not Challenge awards.

So working Mt Athos in CW, Phone or Digital mode seems to be your only path.

73, Phil W6PK



On Tue, Aug 25, 2020 at 5:20 PM Al N6TA via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
I thought I would ask on this chat group instead of a bigger group since I am likely to embarrass myself!  (not that this is a small group...)But, here goes.

My DXCC account as seen in LoTW site shows progress towards the Challenge.
2237 ' DXCC Credits Awarded'.
The ARRL webpage for the Challenge says this:
' The DXCC Challenge Award is earned by working and confirming at least 1,000 DXCC band-points on any Amateur bands, 160 through 6 meters (except 60 meters).'
So, I wanted to know what is a 'band-point'.  I am a bit surprised that they do not clearly define a band-point.  If it is there, I could not find it.  So, I looked more.
I found this statement:
'The DXCC Challenge Award is available for working and confirming at least 1,000 DXCC band-entities on any Amateur bands....'

So now they talk of 'band-entities'.  I assume it is the same as a band-point.

What role does mode play?   It seems that CW and SSB are counted since those modes are the only ones that are in my totals as seen in the  LoTW awards view.

So, the question is simple this: If I have worked an entity on a band on both CW and SSB and then work it on a digital mode, does my challenge total increase?  If so, I could quickly increase my Challenge totals quite a bit by adding FT8 contacts.

I ask since this could be my motivation(not to get on a new mode.  The other motivation would be to try to work my final ATNO, SV-Mt Athos.

Thanks for any comments!

> On Apr 13, 2017, at 8:30 PM, Richard (Rick) Karlquist via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
> 
> The issue with both the K7NV clamps and the Tennadyne
> clamps is that they are machined for a specific mast
> diameter.  They don't say what that diameter is.
> It might be 2.000 inches, but it might have an allowance
> for galvanizing.  The K7NV clamps appear to be machined
> for 2.000 as even a lightly galvanized mast is nearly
> an interference fit.  That's what I have on my MonstIR.
> I have to count on the clamps deforming to follow the
> larger than planned for diameter, but still be stiff
> enough to get a good grip.  I used the K7NV rotor
> to mast clamp that came with my K7NV prop pitch.
> 
> I had problems with the MonstIR slipping on the mast
> and I added a Slipp Nott.  Same problem with mast
> diameter.  The Slipp Nott still has slipped.  IMHO,
> it only wraps around 180 degrees, vs 270 degrees for
> K7NV, thus it cannot really hug the mast except by
> luck.
> 
> Asking for people's experience is problematical.
> Slipping depends on the vagaries of mast diameter,
> fitting, and friction depending on the surface
> condition.  Not to mention the random effects of
> wind and different characteristics of antennas.
> 
> Then there is the argument that slipping is not
> a bug, its a feature, acting as a safety valve.
> Which the manufacturer requires in your case.
> 
> I live with occasional minor slipping and just
> reset the Green Heron.  I put in
> 3 wraps in the rotor loop so that
> I won't get into trouble for reasonable slips.
> If it slips so much as to increase this to 4 turns
> or decrease it to 2 turns, I just do a 360 degree
> turn and reset the GH to fix it.  As long as any
> one storm doesn't go more than a turn, I should
> be OK.
> 
> The bulletproof solution is to use a big enough
> rotator so you don't need slip as insurance, and
> then pin the mast using an interference fit bolt
> as one of the other posters suggested.  The
> initial shipments of MonstIR's suffered from
> the elements slipping on the boom.  SteppIR sent
> me a kit with a special drill bit and a special
> bolt.  I drilled one continuous hole all the
> way through both sides of the boom and the element
> clamp.  I then force hammered the special bolt
> through the perfectly drilled holes.  I just
> followed their instructions and 10+ years later
> the bolts are still very tight and the holes
> have not ovalized.  I don't know if this would
> work the same on a chrome moly mast with very
> little "give" vs the aluminum boom on the MonstIR.
>

I second Rick's motion ... I also got up at sunrise (not the first time this week :), saw no 30M, waited, then 30M but the JA monopoly took over.    It would help if they looked for 6's on 30M at dawn and 20M in evening and even the wee hours...

On 3/28 I was hearing them (building from ESP level to mumbles, or the CW equivalent :) from about 0900z to 1000z (2-3 am here).  Things were slow enough that the op was even calling CQ, but he did not hear my 500W calls (he did work a few other 6's, more VKs) and he eventually went QRT (signal was still building here).  

With the poor conditions, many little pistols/small rifles are struggling for that ATNO, even on CW ... I hear the pileup much more often than their signal, and when I hear them they are very weak and/or DQRMed.   (And I missed the best 20M CW opening the night that DXA seemed to indicate no 20M operation.)   Frustrating.    Working conditions here are: hexbeam at 35' for 20 and up and inverted V with apex at 32' for 30/40/80.    The inverted V runs approx 30/210 degrees (from one end of house to the other), almost exactly at right angles to the short/long paths... I'm thinking about how to rotate the house by 90 degrees :).    And, according to HFTA (thanks to K6TU for the data), my elevation profile towards 210 degrees on this end (pointing into the Saratoga hills/Santa Cruz mtns) goes up almost as fast on this end of the short path as Atlas Cove does into Big Ben.  

Our prop windows seem to have a fair amount in common with JA and the VK0EK antennas seem pointed to JA (based on watching DXA patterns) during many of these windows.    (Maybe they figure that 6's will be addressed later from Spit Head?)   I don't think they have seriously tried to use 17M towards us (they are either not on, or aimed at Europe, during our windows).   20M is not quite as bad, but still underutilized towards us in our windows.    I can't complain about 30M ... I just need to get a few more dB out of that V ... looks like a roof climb in my future.

I know that we don't have much common daylight, but more hours (and aiming to 6 land in prop windows) of CW on bands like 30/20/17M would help.    That's where the ATNOs will happen (not on SSB or on 80 or 160 :)

Also, what's the word on Spit Head?  Sounds like some wavering on whether they will go there at all?   I hope they can pull that off soon.

Not sure venting helped, but I'm ready to go back to the grind ... still two weeks left.

Mike

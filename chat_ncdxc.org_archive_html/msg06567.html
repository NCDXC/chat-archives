On Apr 24, 2014, at 5:24 PM, "Paul Ewing" <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:

Christian, I understand your confusion. It confuses me too as to why any Amateur Radio Operators would create a phony radio club so that they can hold three 1X2 or 2X1 call signs. It appears that N6RO was not a good example for me to cite as a winning contester that does not hold multiple Extra Class call signs. You can look up any call sign on QRZ.com, then click on detail. Scroll down and you can see which calls that they are the Trustee for. You can click on the other calls listed and you can see if they are held by a Club of some sort.
 
In the days since this conversation began, I have gone through the roster of a large Call Area Six Contest Club and examined the amount of club call signs being held by the membership. It is astonishing the amount of guys that think they need multiple 1X2 and 2X1 call signs for their contesting purposes. I think the prevalence of this indicates that the club call process is likely being abused.
 
I am not in any way against contesting but what is going on with the hogging of these call signs is shameful.
 
I don’t want to abuse the privilege of using this reflector, so I am moving the conversation to my Blog where I will continue with this topic.
 
I am also going to write to the ARRL and make them aware of the problem. I am going to ask them to task the Official Observers with auditing the use of club calls and to validate that the rules are being followed.
 
I’m also going to continue to ask from the Heart for those contesters that find that they truly don’t need these multiple Extra Class call signs to give them back. There are others that have upgraded and deserve to be able to obtain a 1X2 or 2X1 call for their personal use.
 
You can follow this topic on my Blog at http://n6pse.wordpress.com
 
The conversation continues!
 
Paul N6PSE

Eighteen months ago I decided to replace my TS-940 (a great CW rig).  The TS-590 was seriously considered here at one point but since I knew I was going to get an amp, the spiking problem made it an absolute no sale.  Firmware has improved it but has not eliminated it.  One spike and goodbye $4K amp. 

Ten Tec was another logical option but operating one had a steeper learning curve from the Kenwoods I was used to (think Reverse Polish Notation compared to an adding machine).  TT support is near flawless.  TT weight is much higher (they're larger too) and I wanted small and 12 V for the power source (RV or FD ops).

Most new receivers are in the same class now so attention must be paid to ease of operation, customer support and system integration.  I also want cleaner transmitters to be a good neighbor, hoping others feel the same. 

Size and weight are huge considerations for transportation (flying or RV) but not everyone is concerned about ever moving the station. 

I didn't get to have the Collins S line of yore (real knobs and dials) but I'm quite satisfied with the K line and Elecraft support (if needed) is superb.  KIY support essentially doesn't exist.  For once in my ham life, I have an integrated and matching station. :-D

Some folks complain of Elecraft rigs being too small or light, but show me a radio that is made for fat fingers. ;-)  Non-skid shelf liner or a table cloth solves the movement issue; it packs well, doesn't require extra baggage fees and its rugged enough for DXpeditions or RV in BFE.  

Everyone has compromises in building a station (antenna arrays are harder) but at least there are some viable options these days. Budget is likely the biggest compromise and controlling factor.  

If one will never have an amp and budget limitations are tight, the TS-590 is a great choice (though it needs more roofing options).

73,
Rick wa6nhc

Craig makes some pretty good points about the Kenwood TS-590 vs the Elecraft K3. The TS-590 in my opinion is the "best bang for the buck" currently within medium sized & priced radios. While I still prefer the K3's (I own two of them) I used the TS-590 at 7O6T. It was a good rig but my audio preference is for the K3. The TS-590 has a harsh digital sound whereas I like the warm analog sound of the K3.
 
For DXpedition purposes, more and more DXpeditions will be giving the TS-590 a serious look because not only is it a performer, but with the cost significantly less than the K3 it is a really attractive option for a DXpedition looking to bring 5-7 identical radios. I'm not in the market for a new radio, but if I was, I would have to give the TS-590 serious consideration.

From the web:

Day Zero as it has been dubbed is the day that Cape Town’s water supply will be switched off, and residents must go to one of over 200 sites across the city in order to receive a rationed water supply.  

Should Day Zero occur, some 4 million residents would be left without water and forced to queue for their quota. 

South Africa’s second largest city has asked residents to limit their water use, requiring them to use just 50 litres of water per person per day, a further decrease on the previous restriction of 87 litres per day.

Many of you may not know about this, but when I began exploring reasons why we hadn’t hear from K0IR since early on the 1st, I began checking Bouvet weather sites and came across the fact that there was on January 28th a 6.5 earthquake SW of Africa and it was officially named the “Bouvet Earthquake!”  Talk about coincidence, but fortunately not bad luck!  The tsunami was mild and within 3 hours was already north of the tip of South Africa and the ship likely cut through those waves moving westward into their path which likely have contributed to the rocking and rolling they experienced three days west of Bouvet.  In their posts they attributed the rough seas only to a south Atlantic low pressure storm, but it was likely that both events contributed to what they experienced.

 

I suspect that they will hear about it when they get to Cape Town unless they already found the same information I did. With time on their hands they probably did.

 

At least, many if not most of the team will be able to say “I have been to South Africa,” hardly enough to offset the immense disappointment, time, and expense, however.  Cape Town has always been on my ucket list, but I am going to wait until they gewt their water problems fixed first!

 

Best 73’s from Tucson to all my DX friends!

 

Dick

On 1/23/2013 8:12 AM, Andreas Junge wrote:

The Youkits FG-01 Antenna analyzer. It beats the pants off the MFJ by a wide margin. It has a color TFT display, is the size of a pack of cigarettes. It plots SWR and Z.
Ah -- that reminds me of another REALLY cool accessory that I bought this spring. It's the DG8SAQ-designed VNWA, built and distributed by a ham in the UK. $750 delivered to my driveway. This unit is the "cat's meow" of antenna analyzers, AND it does measurements THROUGH a 2-port device (like a filter or a preamp, or the isolation between antennas or through an antenna switch box). You can measure your antenna in your shack, use the TDR function to measure the length of your feedline (and check for any problems in the feedline), then, knowing the length of the line, plug the data (as a Touchstone file) into Sim Smith, really nice Smith Chart software, to compute the complex impedance at the antenna. Sim Smith is FREE, and written by a Los Gatos ham, AE6TY.
I've used my VNWA to measure my antennas, find a bad feedline (on my 10M Yagi), design matching stubs for many of my antennas, and eventually end up not needing the six Ten Tec tuners that used to sit on my desk. I've also used the VNWA to tune the TXBPF filter sets that I built, and to evaluate a lot of other bandpass filters. It's self-powered from the USB port, so it's easy to drag it to the base of your tower if you want to. You can also "calibrate" it to a long length of coax that reaches to the top of your tower, then pass tht coax up to a climber and get an good antenna measurement that way.
Both of these products are "techie" tools for techie guys, but if you're in that category, you will LOVE both of them. If you're not a techie, stick with the MFJ or this YourKits unit.
http://sdr-kits.net/VNWA3_Description.html If you're buying one, you want the 3E version with the calibration kit. Skip the case. 

http://www.ae6ty.com/Smith_Charts.html

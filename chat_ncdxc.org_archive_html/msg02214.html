On 5/9/2013 6:35 PM, paul@n6pse.com wrote:
Jim, are you suggesting that the NCDXC try meeting in Santa Cruz County?

Of course not. My concern is for those living in the North Bay, the East Bay, the Sierra, and we heard from some of those members last week when I raised the subject. In fact, as a member of the NCCC BoD I've rejected that suggestion, and have worked to coordinate meeting locations for members in more outlying areas to the north and east.  I'm only an hour from much of Silicon Valley, so almost any meeting location S of SFO is fine for me.

 
Are you suggesting that additional repeaters be installed in remote regions?
 

In an earlier post, I suggested the use of multiple voted RX sites and perhaps one or more additional TX sites. That combination is a well known solution for topography like ours. In a voted system, a "voter" gets audio from multiple receivers and picks the best signal -- it fills in ghe gaps when a station who wants to use the system is shadowed to one RX site but can see another site.  That happens a lot with topography like ours. 

Pave Paws makes 440 MHz unusable around the northern part of our membership area, but 440 could work down this way.  Extending the system requires hardware, and gaining access to suitable sites can be difficult.  One of my colleagues owns an AT&T Long Lines site with a 150 ft tower at 3,000 ft 40 miles due west of the Sacramento airport that covers much of the northern part of our membership, well into the Sierra (and probably the wine country), It's likely that we could use it on any band but 440 MHz (due to Pave Paws). That doesn't help me in Santa Cruz, but it helps a lot of the rest of our outlying members.

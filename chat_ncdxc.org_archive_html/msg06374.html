On 3/31/2014 8:55 AM, Rich Holoch wrote:
I have an HT that I never use - but was wondering if anyone uses UHF / VHF in the mobile to chat on the way to Visalia? If so - what frequencies are used these days?
Here's an excellent system, mostly 440 MHz, but also 2M, that covers much of California. 

http://www.carlaradio.net/
There are three dozen repeaters in this system, all linked. I have several of them programmed into my mobile rig. The 146.85 repeater (162.2 Hz PL) covers much of the Bay Area from Oakland. Repeaters on 443.475 cover San Jose and Santa Cruz/Monterey (173.8 PL) from Mt. Umunhum (S of San Jose), and the central Sacramento Valley from about 4,000 ft near Sonora (151.4 Hz PL). A repeater on 440.75 (162.2 Hz PL) may have talkie coverage in Visalia.
Note that each of these repeaters has a "local" PL and a "link" PL. When you use the local PL, you're talking only to that repeater. When you use the link PL, you're talking to the entire system. I have my mobile rig programmed for both. Do NOT try to use PL receive with the linked PL -- these repeaters transmit only the local PL tone.
I'll be monitoring this system while I drive from Santa Cruz on Thursday, and maybe with my talkie at Visalia.
I have some personal history with several of these sites -- a few years before I moved to CA from Chicago, a pro audio colleague who is a ham interested me in jointly buying one or more decommissioned AT&T Long Lines sites, and we toured a dozen or so of them. A large investor beat us to them. Ten years later, the Sonora, Cisco Grove, Gualala, Topaz Lake, Hawthorne, McCloud, house repeaters on this system. 

73, Jim K9YC

Kevin,

When I had a reasonably decent station back in San Diego my goal was generally considered one contact (SSB, CW) any band for DXpeditions, so two contacts per DXpedition.  I didn't worry about digital because it didn't interest me too much. Unless it was the only way I could make contact.

I have generally stuck to that but then got into going after DXCC on 80m (for five band DXCC) so I tried to make that one contact mode focused if possible on that band. Even at my old place that was tough.

Even though these greedy hams that want every band / mode exist, I don't generally worry about them. These are usually the more experienced better operators who know what they are doing. I am more upset with the DQRMers who attempt to make the hobby less enjoyable.  I also have seen some  notable big guns want to make daily contacts same band and mode with DXpeditions just to show off so that is a little aggravating also.

Kevin,

To answer your question, I think a number of DX'ers today look at a dxpedition as a mini-contest.  With the popularity of Clublog one can look up anyone's logged QSOs with or without the leaderboard.  For those who are competitive they feel the desire to work the dxpedition on as many bands/modes as possible as each log upload provides an informal 'scoreboard'.  You could randomly ask a few competitive DX'ers about this and perhaps they will admit to you that they do check the logged QSO's of their peers.  The purpose of these log checks is to see how one is doing against another.  If the logs are made private as in LoTW I think there will be less competition to work them as fast as possible.  Recently I proposed to another planned dxpedition to see if they could implement an online log check similar to LoTW.  You could type in your callsign, date, time, band and mode and the log check program will tell you if you've worked them or not.  I believe making the logs private will reduce the occurrences of folks working them on every band/mode combinations (9x3=27).  Unfortunately their database programming was already done and no new functionality could be added without additional cost.  I really would like to see another dxpedition that recognizes the problem and decides to implement the private log check feature.  My $0.02.

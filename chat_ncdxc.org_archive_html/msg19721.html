We all know that the timing is more critical in FT8.  This may make it difficult to identify a station if the limits are exceeded.

If there is no phone operation in parallel (or email) to advise them, on the presumption that the EX8 is able to copy anyone at all, one could send "EX8*** fixclock" in lieu of your call or grid (if your call is short).  That 'lights up' in color on their end (because it has their call embedded), but that may also blend in with the herd.

Send it a few times.  If more than one does this, chances of improvement are better.  It also presumes they have an accurate local source to adjust their clock.

Then send one transmission with your call as an identifier to meet the legal needs.  (It's certainly ham friendly, unlike some of the comments or cursing sometimes noted using this method.)

Since I use a driver level app (Meinburg) to keep the clock within a few hundredths of a second, manually changing it means the app would correct it quickly making my change moot. 

(On that note, do NOT rely on an MS operating system to be accurate; they broke their NTP spec long ago and can be off by several MINUTES.)

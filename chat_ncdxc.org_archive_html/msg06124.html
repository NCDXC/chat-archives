Most of you know I like building my own antennas. Actually – a lot more than chasing DXCC awards. Why a 20M Monobander?

 

One thing I have done recently is to completely drop out of DXCC Challenge – I have no aspirations to get to 3000+, and have gone back to where I started – just get one CW and one SSB Q for any ATNO. After 160M DXCC (I am at 60 entities), I will stop at 9BDXCC as far as “5BDXCC” is concerned. This will leave me with working toward DXCC HR #1 – but even there – if it happens, fine, if not no big deal. I will stay on the HR list anyway – hi hi. I’m actually doing something I used to do between 1973 – 1977 – rag chew, and even rag chew in CW! Oh – and QRP with the KX3.

 

The award bit is one part of the answer, and the fact that as soon as you multi-band, you make compromises. Having said that – I am thinking about adding 2 elements for 15M – and am modeling that in EZNec now. I would add a 15M driver ahead of the 20M driver and a 15M driven element ahead of the 20M driver – and get rid of the hairpin match – it would then be a “open sleeve coupled - cell driver” arrangement. But not sure if I really want to bother . . . . it’s easy enough to crank the antenna down – and as you will see on the blog – I have a very nice antenna work area above the trellis I built above the deck. And yes – I built it with working on antennas in mind! The slats swing away for quick access to the antenna!

This was posted on the Contest reflector…

Glenn Johnson, WØGJ has written a detailed article regarding all aspects of planning and executing the recent k1N Navassa Island DXpedition. You can download a full color copy from the Twin Cities DX Association (TCDXA) website. Select the March 2015 issue of the /*Gray Line Report*/ from here:

http://www.tcdxa.org/newsletter.html

Glenn's article is going to give you the complete story, showing that you don't simply pack up a bunch of equipment and set sail to a distant DX location. DXpeditions of this scale take professional levels of planning, negotiations, and execution during every step of the way.


The article is excellent and I was flattered to see K6TU referenced.  I’m still not sure how best to use this quote that Ralph Fedor K0IR gave me…  this is verbatim.

From Ralph, K0IR - Here's a little, all true, testimonial.  You may use it as you wish.

Once again the K6TU propagation tools proved their worth.  On the K1N, Navassa DXpedition they showed us the major openings, but more importantly they showed us key, short openings to difficult parts of the world.

For example, the 15 meter predictions made us aware of an opening to deep Asia at approximately 1200 to 1300 UTC.  Signals we not strong, and had we not been aware of the path, we may have missed working these stations.  But when we called for Asia in this time window,  and the rest of the world stood by, the deep Asian stations were there and we were able to work them.

The K6TU prediction tools proved their worth on Navassa as they did on Amsterdam and as they do at my home QTH.


Its been my pleasure to now help 22 DXpeditions over the last 28 months including the upcoming expeditions to VP8/S, E44 and one yet-to-be-annouced.  Working with these Dxpedition teams has given me great respect for the effort (and money!) it takes to put on a successful expedition and a deeper appreciation of the challenges they face.

It’s made me much more empathetic to the challenges when I’m struggling to work a station myself especially considering that so much of the cost is borne directly by the operators themselves.  I just hope that the ops on the DXpeditions don’t read the awful comments and insults posted on the DX reflectors both cluster and otherwise.  Otherwise it could only instill feelings of “why do I bother”.

The DXpeditions show be the ones getting the standing ovations at Visalia!

One man’s view…
Respectfully.
Stu K6TU

On 1/13/2014 5:49 AM, Rich Holoch wrote:
Jim, K9YC brought up a good point about the antenna not covering the lowest angles – that will be where the best signals are for FT5ZM. When I designed this antenna, I knew what HFTA said about the hills (aka “signal shunts”) . .
Yes, terrain will modify what your NEC model predicts about the vertical pattern, and HFTA seems to be quite effective in predicting that effect. My reaction to that for my antenna system was to model VERY extensively to find the optimum height(s) to various DX locations, to try to choose the best compromise. How extensive? I ran every band five degrees and every 5 ft of mounting height, and I did it from two different locations on my property. K6XX went far beyond that (he has a lot more property). :)

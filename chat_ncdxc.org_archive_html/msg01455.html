I also missed the larger DXpedition about five years ago. In addition  I had lost my QSL cards for contacts made over the years with Burkina Faso. As a result  I decided to put in a good effort working them and thanks to them I now have the following  QSOs confirmed in their online log:

 

12M SSB

12M CW

15M SSB

15M CW

17M SSB

17M CW

20M SSB

20MCW

20M RTTY

30M CW

40M CW

80M CW

 

I never heard them on 10M (but I am still periodically checking 10M J for them).  I am very impressed with how well they heard me and with the quality of their operators especially since I was usually running only 100 Watts of power (although I did crank up the Alpha 77SX amplifier on occasion when needed.)  I am very pleased with the XT2TT operation and I am grateful in obtaining a number of ATNO band-modes as a result of  their efforts.

 

Thank you to the XT2TT team!  73, Ted, K6XN

It's Monday and time for your NCDXC Question of the Week:

 

The XT2TT DXpedition is in their final days. How's your experience been working them?

 

 

 

 

My response:  I needed XT2 for an all-time new one. I missed the large DXpedition that was there about five years ago. Last year, there was a group of JA's that was active for about a week. They worked JA's and the East Coast and then went home. Never got them.

 

I was surprised and shocked to hear XT2TT calling for the West Coast during their first day of activity!

 

I've worked them on:

 

20 SSB

20 RTTY

20 CW

30 CW

15 CW

17 SSB

15 SSB

 

These seven contacts greatly exceed my expectations. Solar conditions have been quite poor, yet they continue to work hard. I'm really looking forward to meeting the Team Leader, Silvano-I2YSB at Visalia and congratulating them on an outstanding effort!

 

Paul N6PSE

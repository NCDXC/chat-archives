My opinion is favorable and I agree with John K6YP’s and John K6MM’s observations, opinions and comments.

 

I thought the PT0S DXpedition  was a difficult operation to work on several bands but so what? It was a lot more difficult for those four wonderful guys out on the rocks.

 

My unrealistic aggressive goal was to work them on all bands 10M through 80M and on CW, SSB and RTTY and to pick up 11 needed band-mode contacts. Realistically though I considered I would be lucky to pick up one or two needed band-mode contacts. Currently I am in their on-line log for 8 needed band-mode contacts and contacts 9, 10 and 11were made the last 2 days of the operation and contacts for that time frame are not yet posted on their on-line log but I expect all my 11 needed band-mode QSOs will ultimately be in their log (call me optimistic).

 

As many of you have been on DXpeditions I have also been on a number of DXpeditions when I was younger and I know what its like to push yourself to make as many contacts as possible for the deserving without sleep, with equipment problems, weather problems and endless abuse from rude operators, jammers, band cops, know-it alls who have never been on a DXpedition or supported one etc.

 

If I ever meet any of those four operators I don’t intend to criticize them but rather I would like to just thank them and buy them an adult beverage or two and/or a dinner. I will try to restrain giving them a hug!  J

 

Yes they could have been luckier and they could have planned better and they might have been able to control the pileups better. I don’t care….I would just like to thank them for their effort and for the contacts and show my appreciation.

 

Call me radical.  73, Ted, K6XN

Paul,

I think that the St Peter and Paul Rocks DXpedition was amazing.
Remember that these guys had one tent (that leaked) on a hunk
of plywood less than a meter out of the surf breaking over the
rocks. They lost a radio and a PA. They kept awake by chewing
instant coffee when their coke and chocolate ran out. The finally
got a little extra wet sleep after they strung up a hammock under
the scientific station. An yet they still were very good ops thruout
the entire DXpedition. Gimme a break. These guys are DX animals.

Even though SPSP was not a new one for me it was a very fun
DXpedition to work. Assuming my 10 meter cw Q is in the final
log I got them on 10 band modes.

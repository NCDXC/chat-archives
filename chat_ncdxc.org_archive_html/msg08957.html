2 separate issues.  New DXCC rules facilitate my shipping a flex to my aunts house in CE3 and experiencing starting a new DXCC from there.  (if they finish their remote software : -) )

 

If you read the entire article.. there is a big section on ethics.. and what your DXCC means to YOU.  The bottom line is the ARRL took the high road.. enabling more options. 

 

My personal view is that DXCC source locations should emanate from (at a minimum) ITU zones..  And my personal rules for my station are much more stringent.. and are likely more lax than some others.

 

I would never remote into a NY remote ham station and call that a valid contact for my mixed nearly 8 band DXCC all worked from the bay area.. although most from a hilltop station I played a large part in constructing.  That’s my DXCC.. yours is yours.

 

but this rule was timely and on target and opens up a lot of possibilities.. any may save Dxpeditioneers lives and ham $$ in the future, while offering an otherwise impossible entity, or enable a someone to experience the other side of the pile up without selling their car.

 

JM2C..

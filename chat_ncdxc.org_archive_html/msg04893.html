Hi Guys,

There is a lot of excitement brewing with the FT5ZM team departing Australia on its way to Amsterdam Island.  Yes, lots of us need Amsterdam for a new one or at least to fill in some band/mode slots.  But there is another aspect of this trip likely to be overlooked.  Anyone who is working on the CQ DX Field Award knows that the Braveheart (the vessel transporting the FT5 team) will pass through what for us on the west coast is one of the rarest grid Fields on the face of the earth.  It is Field NF, a rectangle defined as the area between latitudes -30 to -40 degrees south, and longitudes 80 to 100 degrees east.  What makes this Field so rare is that it is on the opposite side of the planet and there is nothing in it but water -- no land, nada -- and it is not even on normal shipping routes.  The FT5ZM operators are aware of the rareness of Field NF and are going to make a special effort to operate as a maritime mobile station while they pass through this Field.  I don't know myself what callsign these guys will be using for their /mm operation, but if you hear them, you probably should try to work them.  Even if you don't chase grid Fields, work them anyway, because who knows ... maybe someday you'll change your mind and start chasing Fields and there is a high probablity that Field NF will not be on again during your lifetime! :-)  Oh, and if you do hear them in Field NF, please spot them.

On 4/15/2013 1:30 PM, Dave Gomberg wrote:

I have a question for Elecraft fans, how do the filters available for
the Kx's compare with the electronic filters in an SDR?

I have become totally spoiled by the filtering in PSDR, I am not sure I
would ever be happy giving it up.  But I am open minded, fill me in.
The K3 and KX3 are SDR radios, but built with front panel controls. The IF filters are in DSP, and can be adjusted in 50 Hz increments. Two front panel knobs can set either bandwidth and shift, OR the LOW and HIGH audio frequencies corresponding to the filter skirts. To switch from one method to another you simply push the knob (like you would a button).
The HARDWARE filters that you buy for the K3 are ROOFING filters that protect the DSP IF from overload by strong signals outside their passband, and you can select from a dozen different ones depending on your own preferences. The 400 Hz or 250 Hz 8-pole filters are all you need consider for CW, RTTY, and PSK. The K3 comes with either a 5-pole 2.7 kHz or 2.8 kHz filter that is also used for transmitting. Serious SSB contesters typically add 1.8 kHz (most popular) or 2.1 kHz roofers.
If you don't do contesting, I'd suggest ordering a K3 or KX3 with no roofing filter, and add one only if you've got a close-in neighbor who blows your RX away in pileups. It's VERY easy to add one later. The exception to that is is your K3 has the second RX installed, which must be removed to insert a roofing filter into either it or the main RX. So if you're buying the second RX, I'd buy the roofers and install them then.

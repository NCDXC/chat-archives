A comment I made about DXLabs software (the look is so ‘90s) was not intended as a slam.  It was an observation.  If it was taken in the wrong vein, my apologies.

Because it IS from the 90’s and updated regularly (often) when things change.  Updating it for looks would take MONTHS (of time better spent DXing); just to make it pretty or tie some applets together.  Pretty comes at a cost.

As a function before form kind of guy, I get it.  DXLabs just works though it’s a little tedious to set settings dialed into what the user wants; because it’s precise (unlike other logbooks).

That allows Keeper to be picky; which is to the benefit of the user; details matter.

Again, I’m migrating FROM a long time (20 plus years) use of HRD for a variety of reasons (mostly lack of support and not being current in things like rig control).  The latest version of logbook still has issues.  Their support, I’d be impolite in my full description; underwhelming.

DXLabs once dialed in, with the learning curve managed, will do a better job (it already has).  Soon I won’t be running HRD in parallel and the migration will be complete.

Sorry Dave (I really thought the comment went to a couple of guys, not the list; oops).

Here is the setup:
K3, SignaLink USB sound card (I know, not great but I'm waiting for a K4) and WSJT-X.  The 'normal' audio level on receive is set to ~60 dB while hearing signals (per Jim K9YC months ago).  The K3 LIN OUT, is 5.
The DX (C92RU) chose to use fairly low base tones (291 Hz up). In case there was rolloff at that tone, I moved the "SHIFT" from the default of 1500 Hz to 600 (ignore high tones, they don't matter for this DX; it appears to help).  A WSPR signal (it appeared modulated) was present at about 650 Hz, which caused me to wonder if it was affecting my ability to copy the DX (came and went, seemed to match the WSPR carrier pattern).  (Yes, it could have been a tuner upper, I'm being generous.)
So I set the notch to 700, which blocked the WSPR, but also made the DX MUCH better copy (and it went away again, each time I turned the notch off).  This was repeated (without the WSPR) many times on other bands; same result, the low level signal became better copy.
The downside is the because the notch falsely lowers the RF noise floor, the signal reports are silly high (-19 becomes +00).  I can live with that, the report is inconsequential compared to completing the DX QSO (compared to 15 retries, then oh you're 59).
I've tried lowering the input levels into the computer (device management, the Windows driver); the RX report values stay the same, as expected and uber weak signals are not printed, until I turn the notch back on.  I tried driving it harder (LIN OUT 27); no real effect, notch was a super pre-amp.
1)  Is this because the (admittedly poor) sound card is in ALC and blocking the lower level signals?
2)  Does this mean my sound card driver level, K3 combination could be better (i.e. I'm not giving the sound card enough to work with)?
I'd prefer to give accurate reports, that is a strong favorite feature in a 59 world.  That value has meaning (except when I warp the spectrum with a notch).  I'd also like to hear low level signals better (a rousing 'duh' is heard here). 

Comments?

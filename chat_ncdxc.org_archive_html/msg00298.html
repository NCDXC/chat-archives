I look at propagation predictions much the way Fijian boat captains look at the calendar. It does not matter what day it is, either it is there or not!

 

Seriously, when we plan a DXpedition we look at the predictions, mostly to plan our dates of activity. Dates around the fall or spring equinox are best. Mid summer is about the worst.

 

As for propagation predictions in my own shack, I cannot count the number of times where the solar numbers looked terrible and the Solar-Terestrial data on the DX Summit page was bleak, yet I was able to work some truly spectacular DX!

 

Of course, there is no substitution for truly great propagation and that is really enjoyable!

I look upon propagation predictions much the way Italian drivers look upon traffic laws – as general guidelines.

 

What I have found most useful when stalking specific DX is the Club Log propagation tool which uses actual QSO data from its database to provide actual UTC times of contacts made between entities.   You can specify entities, month, SFI, etc.  I’ve found it generally pretty accurate and helpful.

 

Check it out at:

 

https://secure.clublog.org/propagation.php

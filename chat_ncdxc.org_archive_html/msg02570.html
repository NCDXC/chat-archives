I currently have 2 elements on 20 and 15M up 50' and 2 elements on 17 and 12M up 35'. 

I have a 50' hatted vertical for 160 - 30M with 76 buried radials and a 2 vertical phased array for 40M (40M is by far my favorite band, so the 3 dB gain with direction is a great thing). Loss on 160M, unity on 80M - and I'm fine with this - its "good enough". 

On 30M, the 50' hatted vertical is essentially a half wave vertical, and with the 76 radials I am getting (maybe) 1.5 dB gain. But I keep thinking if I could become competitive on 30M . . . .. I'm thinking about changing things a bit where I end up with 2 elements on 30 and 17M up 60' and the 2 element 20 - 15M beam dropping down from 50' to about 40'. Id have to end up with 12 and 10M being essentially rotatable dipoles up high - which I think for this cycle - that's not much of a sacrifice  . . . if at all. 

Its always a compromise, but I'm thinking that 30M is going to become very important - very soon in this cycle. Especially for FT5ZM and anything else I need starting in a couple of months. I'm mostly a CW guy - so 30M for me is almost as much a fave band as 40M.

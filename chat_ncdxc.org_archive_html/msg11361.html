On Fri,2/5/2016 8:27 AM, Richard (Rick) Karlquist via Chat wrote:

Yet somehow, we are being told that charter boats
and helicopter services can command premium
prices?  What is special about them?  Not
arguing, just trying to understand.
Some factors you learn about when you run your own business. There's the cost of buying the boat or copter and equipping it, of maintaining it, of maintaining a crew, of naval and flying regulations, and of marketing it. And, of course, supply and demand.
We typically need one of these resources to get us to rather remote parts of the world, drop us off, then pick us up and take us back a week or two later. It takes these resources time (days, weeks) to get to where we want to be picked up, and to wherever the next job is. And what can these resources (boat, copter) do while we're on the island, which is nearly always very remote?
As NI6T so aptly stated it, these countries are rare because they are very remote, hard to get to, hard to get on and off of (or in and out of, in the case of land-based entities), or dangerous, either by virtue of geography or political conditions.
Equipment must be acquired. Yes, some stuff gets donated, but some does not, and everything must be carefully packed for hazardous transport 
And then there are "duties" and "fees" (bribes?).

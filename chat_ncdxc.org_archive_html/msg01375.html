For me, it's the challenge. Topband is a lot like 6M. You can have the best transceiver, maximum legal power and it's still a challenge. It's building better transmitting antennas, building better receiving antennas, and being at the right place at the right time. It's the opportunity to listen carefully and work something -- maybe.
Topband operators might be a bit more understanding, but mostly it's the lower operator density -- less competition -- that makes it seem more civilized.
It's way different out in the West compared to the East. Two-hundred watts and an Inverted-L in a tall tree, and you're all set in New England, at least set for lots of easy countries. DXing on 160M to so many countries over the pole is a real challenge. 

Wayne, N7NG

It was a LONG battle (several hours), but the Q is again safely in the logbook for band mode 6, shortly after slot 7 came in on 12 phone (already have CW).  Lest you think I'm bragging, this is on a low altitude dipole with 600 watts... so yes, I am.  :-)

I note two oddities:  The CW spread on 10 meter CW was up 2-20 KHz wide which I don't see often.  The offset on 12 phone was 20 KHz, fixed, no spread whatsoever.  Neither of these traits would be easy on the DX op.  Too much territory (and almost no way to track where they are) on 10 CW and hearing only one frequency CREATES the pileup (loudest mutt in the dog pile wins) on phone.  Brutal.

Not common behavior at all, but I muddled through, it's their circus.  Now for something on 15, 60, 80 and 160... nothing yet on those bands.  Or RTTY, haven't heard a single diddle yet.

I suspect that the daily grind is beginning to wear on them, the style changed radically from last weekend.  Or the pilots are steering them.

Rick nhc (liking retirement even more with things like to to play with)

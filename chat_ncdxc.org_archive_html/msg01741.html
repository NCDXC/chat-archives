On Apr 1, 2013, at 10:09 AM, Steve Jones wrote:

> Paul-
>  
> How about a “DXpedition Camp” to train future DXpedition ops?  I’d love to go on an expedition myself.  But I’d like to first develop the skills needed to be a full contributing member of a team, so I’d be up to speed at the outset.  I’d rather not go on my first DXpedition simply hoping I can get up to speed with only OJT.  Perhaps a field day type local outing during a DX contest weekend would allow exposure to the various facets of antennas, power, logging, shelters, transport, and pileup operating skills.  A key ingredient would be a hot callsign that would ensure pileups on the operator trainees, maybe a special event call like “N6C”…?
>  

Sounds like you want to join one of the county expeditions in the California QSO Party. As you state, it has:

-Field day style short-term setup and teardown
-Big contest weekend
-Portable operating, power, shelters, etc.
-Big pileups, especially if it's a rare county
-Special event callsign

Plus, no airfare, no spending weeks getting seasick on an Antarctic ice-breaker, no leeches/mosquitoes/malaria/etc., and no repercussions of politically-dangerous locations (unless you plan to operate from Berkeley). 

Go for it!

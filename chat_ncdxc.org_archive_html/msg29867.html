That section of the presentation boils down to

1. identify when and where a needed DX station has been QRV

2. identify the propagation openings between your QTH and that of the needed DX station

3. patrol the overlaps between #1 and #2

Responding to spots of needed stations certainly works, but is more reactive, and often makes success a function of your ability to break a pileup. As I learned on the NCDXC packet cluster all those years ago, it's much more satisfying to spot the rare DX you just found and worked than to battle the cluster hordes for the rare DX that someone else just spotted.

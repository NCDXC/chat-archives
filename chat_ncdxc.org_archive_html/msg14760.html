For the past year I was in a very poor qth, and FT8 along with CW when conditions warranted, kept me going with a hobby that I love.  For very small stations with mediocre antennas it is modes like these that keep people engaged, enjoying themselves, and supporting the hobby.

During the past year, I also got totally into learning about SDR platforms and getting deeper into SDR through the Anan and HPSDR projects. I plan to get another brand of SDR this year (probably the Flex 6600M) because life is too short to have only one brand of SDR radio.

Now that I am back in a more FB QTH, I can broaden my horizons into more traditional modes and what I would call more standard DXing. 

Some of my old timer 6m Meteor Scatter friends call me a Digicrat and i get great humor out of that because I am actually a Digitarian (lol). I am a big supporter of the modes and the great thing Joe Taylor and his crew have done for our hobby through this innovation.

FT8 is really a fun mode. It propagates extremely well. 

As it is a new mode, we are all learning it and watching at is evolves. 

Ian-N6ML gave me a great tip on working split and it has really increased my rate.

You merely "shift click" and put your transmit marker in a clear spot. Then you have less competition with the other
stations calling the DX on his frequency. The software will find you and decode you with less competition.

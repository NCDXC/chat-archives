They are paying attention to my messages.   In my role as West Coast Pilot, I feed suggestions to Chief Pilot Col (MM0NDX) who passes on the info via Satellite Phone to the team on Tromelin.   I can see some positive changes taking place already.  

I've asked them to take advantage of the Long Path along the Grey Line at their Sunset - our Sunrise.  This morning they got on 40M CW early with a great signal, and put a lot of West Coast QSOs in the log. 

I've asked them to call out W6-W7 stations more frequently.  This morning they spent over one hour targeting W6-W7 only on 17M SSB.  Several NCDXC members got through.

Areas for improvement:  
(1) Better propagation management to the West Coast, especially on the low bands.  Start earlier and stay on longer.
(2) Less RTTY time....more CW and SSB to optimize the chance for an ATNO
(3) Shrink the pileup bandwidth on all bands, especially RTTY.
(4) Stay on the money-band (20M) every day and for longer time periods.

This DXpedition will likely be compared to FT5GA on Glorioso.  Here's the data thus far:
Final Stats:  FT5GA = 50,000 QSOs.  EU = 70%.  NA = 15%.   Almost no QSOs on the West Coast.  157 QSOs on 160M (15 of them for NA).
Interim Stats:  FT4TA = 28,000 QSOs thus far.  EU = 57%.  NA = 27%.    Many QSOs on the West Coast.  Over 1,000 QSOs on160M (71 of them for NA).
The numbers are encouraging.

A Tromelin DXpedition really requires 14-16 operators and 6 stations -- similar to the FT5 DXpedition to Amsterdam.  What we have instead are 7 operators and 4-5 stations who have to operate, eat, sleep, and take care of non-amateur radio duties.  They are holding their own at this point.

If you already have them in the log.....Congrats.  You have no complaints.   If not, and you'd like to pass on helpful suggestions to FT4TA, send them to me.

BTW - Because Col (MM0NDX, Author of DX-World.net) is paying for all of the Satellite phone charges for this DXpedition, he has the most "first hand" information about this DXpedition in real-time.  It's great to be working with him.  See his website for the latest news:  http://www.dx-world.net/

Also......keep your eye on your LOTW account.  : >)

Richard,
I was really surprised the first time my radio returned to life and completed an FT8 QSO after I had given up calling. 
In the usual F/H mode, the Fox adds up to ten callers to his queue. The software then sends reports to those callers in order. If the Fox is using 5 streams, and the callers are able to decode the reports, things can move quickly. There is no maximum delay limit, because the period of time between the Fox adding a call to the queue, and the report itself being sent to the caller is dependent on the callers position in the queue, the number of streams the Fox has selected, and whether the reports are all being acknowledged by the Hounds. The Fox will send a given caller a total of three reports, but “listen” for the callers response an additional two cycles before dropping the caller. The caller will have to start over at that point. 
If there are fewer streams selected, or if SNRs are borderline, the delay between adding a caller to the queue and the caller actually getting a report can be lengthy. 
If you, as a caller, remain on the frequency with your radio under the control of your WSJT program, your radio will reanimate itself in Zombie Mode and reply. (I just made that up… it’s not official terminology). 
Others may know more, but you’ll need to exit WSJT one way or the other to prevent this behavior. Your email did answer a question I had as to whether SuperFox behaved the same way.
73, Bruce K3NQ

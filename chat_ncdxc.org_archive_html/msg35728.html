﻿I understand.  I was noting their clear priority doesn’t align with mine (admittedly somewhat single minded), restore LOTW.  Loss of income and costs of hired out recovery services (and more) drives their prioritizing; make money, offset costs.  I get it, they are a business primarily.

And yes I’m also a long time user of Clublog which has a simpler method of uploading logs, allows editing for corrections, detailed stats, tracking and far more.  In general terms, Clublog has more usefulness than LOTW (Livestream ROCKS but fast Leaderboard response, lags often on a busy DXpedition).

But while it can also draw from LOTW, Clublog is not a replacement for the “official” records for DXCC, WAS etc. and offers no awards (for those wanting such things).  They’ve (wisely) stayed out of the awards programs entirely (time consuming and expensive to run and fraught with drama).

Clublog total offline time in the past ten years, can be measured at under one week, total time (a very few days, because their server, puked and died, some maintenance time too).

Both are managed through volunteer efforts and accept donations.

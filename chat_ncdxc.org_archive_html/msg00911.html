Paul and Team

 

I think the short answer is yes to both questions but it should be qualified in that “hams” and “organizations” that do lobby should be tactful, on point and make considered, rspectful and reasoned requests for the policy changes. Hopefully someone (or some groups) with *success* in “persuading” government policy makers in changing policies should be allowed and requested to provide guidance to the rest of us. There are many successful DXers and Contesters (they know who they are) who have successfully dealt with these types of issues and there is of course the ARRL.

 

I don’t need to say this but some of us should exercise extreme restraint in providing any policy remarks to government policy makers…especially in the US Fish and Wildlife Service and the EPA among others. …that are critical of their sanity, ancestry, attitudes, legitimacy, and whether they are all a bunch of buttheads….. these types of comments are usually not persuasive… we know who we are…… and I recommend we listen to those who have been *successful* in persuading changes in policy and that we listen to the recommendations of the successful….*before* some of us share what we may or may not really think……

 

Back to my short answer..yes to both questions but with care, respect and thoughtfulness…..the tone of our remarks will be extremely important in addition to our well reasoned and persuasive arguments and respectful requests. The ultimate decision may however balance ultimately on whether they *trust* us with what they are trying to protect and whether they *believe* we will take the care we promise to take….. if they grant our requests.

 

FWIW.  73, Ted, K6XN

Here is your NCDXC Question of the Week (QoTW)

 

If you look at the latest most wanted DXCC list, you will see that US Islands managed by the US Fish & Wildlife Service are rapidly rising to the top of the list.

 

For some time now, the US F&W Service has made it virtually impossible for DXpeditions to activate these remote and rare islands.

 

Question: Should hams actively lobby their politicians and government representatives to open access to these islands?

 

Should organizations such as NCDXF/INDEXA expand their role to include lobbying on behalf of the DX Community?

 

 

What are your thoughts?

 

 

Paul N6PSE

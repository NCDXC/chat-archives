My antenna (Bencher Skyhawk) is a trapless design.  It has ten elements, 3 on 20m, 3 on 15m, and 4 on 10m.  The tuner has an L network with very low loss.  Yes it's weird that modeling results vary vastly from the real world.  I have thought about adding a single element full length rotatable dipole for 17m but never got to it.  But now that space on the mast has been taken up by a 6m beam.  It would be interesting to compare the tribander to a classic dipole on 17m.  By the way the rotatable dipole would make an excellent antenna when it's 75' high.

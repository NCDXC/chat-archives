ALERT: This email is from an external source.

I purchased this antenna from Tom Schiller, N6BT in the mid-90s. Its performance was outstanding. I brought it, and my MA-40 mast to our new home and erected them in 2000, where they continued to work beautifully. In 2017, I performed a complete disassembly and renovation on the antenna. It worked so well I thought of it as my “forever antenna”. I disassembled all of the tubing, polished the tubing junctions, and re-rivetted the elements together with aluminum rivets. I replaced all of the hardware with high-quality US-made stainless at a cost of about $500.

I became more serious about DX in early 2019, when a shoulder surgery temporarily took me out of the gym and off the road. In early 2020, as the Pandemic gifted me with a 2 month “vacation”, I decided to purchase a Force 12 Navassa 5 only for the additional WARC capability. The C-3 has 2 full-sized and wide-spaced elements on 20 and 15, and 4 on 10 (with the installed F-12 conversion kit). It provides directionality and probably about 3 dB of gain on 12 and 17 with a 3:1 SWR, but it was not ideal for WARC without a tuner.

I disassembled the antenna into “bite-sized” pieces in the Spring of 2020, purchased a new 5 Kw balun for it, and gave it to Andy K6ZP to consider using at his new QTH in Montara. It is completely renewed and ready to use. Assembly should take a few hours, and only require some hand tools,  Noalox, rivets, and a rivet gun.

Andy simply doesn’t have the space at his new home to use the antenna, without destroying his neighbors’ coastal views.

I’m offering the beam free to an NCDXC member who can use it.

It fit easily in Andy’s van. He also has a copy of the manual.

Please contact Andy, K6ZP at the above email if you’re interested.

73, Bruce K3NQ

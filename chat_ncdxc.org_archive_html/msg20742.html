On Aug 31, 2020, at 1:15 PM, Paul N6PSE via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

It has been only the last few years that I have discovered the allure of the low bands. Sure 30/40 meters are awesome. 80 and 160 meters are quite challenging. Most 80/160 antennas are quite compromised in some fashion and propagation on these bands primarily happens in the Winter months and even then, it can be quite fickle. This past Friday night, 80 meters was open world-wide and it was really fun. Nights like that will become more common as winter approaches. These past 2-3 years, I focused on 160 meters but recently have been giving 80 meters some attention as well.  DXCC on 80 takes way more effort than it does on the higher bands. It is fun nevertheless.

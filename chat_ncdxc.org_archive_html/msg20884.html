A possibly dumb question from a non-digital mode user.
It would seem to be trivial to get within a few 100
milliseconds using standard time stations like WWV, etc,
without Internet, GPS, etc.  What possible excuse can
there be to have more than a half second timing error?

I also suspect that the NCDXF beacons have pretty accurate
timing.  Every station on earth should be able to copy one
of those as long as they have propagation on 20 meters to
somewhere.

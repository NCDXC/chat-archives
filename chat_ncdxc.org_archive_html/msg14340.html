On Sun, Jan 21, 2018 at 5:28 PM, Aaron McClure via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Hi Everyone,

I am trying to decide what antenna to put a US Tower TX-455 that I am planning on installing this summer.  A great deal of my troubles Is how to determine the correct wind load for the tower.  Web searches uncovered 12.3 sq. ft. @ 70 mph but the Structural  analysis from US Towers puts it at 7.4Ft round and 4.2ft square (90mph 3Sec gust 76 mph fastest mile)  with a maximum allowable wind load weight of 123lbs and antenna weight of 125lbs.  
 
I would like to install a SteppIR beam.  The ultimate would be the DB18 so that I have 30/40M in a directional antenna but 3ele Yagi with 30/40M dipole option is tolerable.  The 3ele Yagi has a wind load listed at 8.1 sq ft with the 30/40M dipole (100mph), just greater than the Structural Analysis and I would like to install a small 2M/440 beam and vertical at top.  The DB18 Manual lists the wind load at 10.1sq ft. (100mph) which is within what I found on the internet but well above the Structural Analysis.  Both antennas are below the maximum weight.
 
I searched TX-455 and DB18 photos and found a few so I know people are mounting.  
 
Hopefully some enlightenment from the group.

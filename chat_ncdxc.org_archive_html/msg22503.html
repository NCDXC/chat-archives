Well, it's interesting of course.  HF always is... well 5% of the time?
20 CW (14.025.33) was building nicely to peak on the west coast, the distortion (sounded polar but more likely multipath) cleared up and they were only calling NA stations.  It doesn't get much better.  Except that far too many answered during an exchange, when a single station was called out... pretty much anytime the DX wasn't transmitting.  That's pretty easy to see on the waterfall. It was proceeding 'normally' for a DXpedition.
UNTIL a group of contesters landed right in the middle (and everywhere else) of the pileup.  One station set up about 400 Hz below the DX; with horrid key clicks, heard even when they moved down further.  The contest lasted about an hour but by then, A25RU had shut down for other spaces; the west coast loses out (worked a bunch of east coast).  Perhaps tomorrow, at least they were on CW (FTx does get boring, but you can do a few things at once while working stations).
If you haven't gotten them on 30M, they're being heard on the east coast now (10.131 F/H) and 40M elsewhere in the world.  So in a couple hours, I'd expect much the same propagation as the past couple days.  Last night 30/40 opened pretty much at the same time (about 1600 local PDT) peaking over the next hour, with a slow fade.
All the prop tools I've used, shows zero chance for below 40M contacts (I'd keep watching anyway, magic happens).  Plus they haven't appeared to make any real effort for the west coast, arriving on the upper bands, as that path closes...
So keep on a huntin' and knock the rust off of those skills (I'm talking to me here).  Thar's DX to be had!

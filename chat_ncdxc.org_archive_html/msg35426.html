On May 27, 2024, at 4:22 PM, John Owens NN6U <john@nn6u.net> wrote:

If you’re using unmodified wsjt-x, you made at least one click per QSO, either selecting a call in S&P, or clicking to send CQ and letting it pick one (and only one) response. To send CQ again or reply to another caller, you needed to click “enable tx” or manually select another caller. The “at least one click per QSO” counts as “contemporaneous direct initiation”. (Even in “hound” mode, the “contemporaneous” part is when you “initiated” sending the calls, even if the completion of the QSO might be many minutes later.)

 


Except the issue is about the DXpeditioner, not the DXer. The DXpeditioner (in this case) is using MSHV (not WSJT-X), is running multiple streams, and the software is queuing up callers according to its algorithm. Most such software can run unattended, but of course there is no way to determine ANY of that, either from your QTH or ARRL HQ. 

By the way, Marek and the rest of the pilots are aware that there is a problem with many stations not receiving “RR73”, and not showing up in the log. Marek has tried to alleviate this by reducing the number of streams (which also improves his signal level), but the problem is not fully resolved at this time.

Clublog upload is also spotty, for two reasons: (1) Marek only has Internet access for about 2 hours each day, and (2) F8CRS (QSL and Log Manager for FT4GL) is working his regular job, and gets around to Clublog upload “when he can”. 

By now, he should have the second Hexbeam up. One is pointed at NA, the other at JA/Asia. There is a vertical for 40-10, and 5 elements on 6m (not likely to be heard on the West Coast).

SSB seems to be OK, as he is using N1MM for logging, and no “FT8 RR73” issues. However, Marek is a fairly new ham, and is just getting his feet wet with working a massive SSB pileup, so his rate is not the best. He has sometimes gone to “by the numbers” to thin out the herd. Please be patient as we work out the issues. He still has weeks left on his assignment to Glorioso.

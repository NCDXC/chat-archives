Rex--good points. Back in the early 60's when I first began to fly fish Michigans rivers I did so with an elderly client who elmered me. Each season and sometimes each trip during the season I'd show up with my latest rod---bamboo, graphite whatever---and he'd chuckle. Now, Michigan Brown and Rainbow trout are wild---and wiley---these are not planted fish and in fact are fished hard by folks from all through the east coast and midwest for that reason. This, of course, makes the fish very wary and clearly smarter than the guys chasing them. I caught darn few and my client released multiples of what I did. He fished with a fiberglass rod dubbed "The Ugly Stick" by the rest of us. He chuckled a lot. Best JIM---and tnx for the very prompt qsl.

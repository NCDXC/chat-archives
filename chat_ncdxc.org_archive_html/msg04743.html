I want to sell this fine rig that I bought to do VHF/UHF contesting when I lived in Chicago, and later at a mountaintop QTH where I had access before I moved here. The TS-790A is the same vintage as the TS-850, resembles it physically, same size/shape, and same AUX connectors and control interface. PL Encode is standard, PL Decode is optional, and is not present. There's space for an optional 1.2 GHz module that is NOT present. Power out is 45W on 2M, 40W on 430-450 MHz. Modes are USB, LSB, CW, and FM. Both bands can operate simultaneously for satellite modes, and there is Doppler effect frequency correction.
This radio is in very nice shape, and has only been used on an operating desk. Unfortunately, I live on the wrong side of the ridge and have too many trees to do anything useful above 50 MHz, so I want to turn it into dollars that can help me buy a KX3.
The going price for this radio seems to be in the range of $800 - $900. I'll include a custom serial interface cable that I made myself. Please email me directly if you're interested.

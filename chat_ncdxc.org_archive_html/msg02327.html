Fellow DXers - Many years ago, the Motion Picture Academy changed their presentation from "And the winner is .. " to "And the Oscar goes to .. " to dispel the notion that there were losers.

At the risk of hi-jacking the topic, I'd like to know what the criteria is for selecting the banquet presentation for Visalia. For me, 7O6T was a no brainer for 2013. Or it could have been presented at the Sunday breakfast with PTØS on Saturday night. That is, an opportunity for all to see presentations from two major and very different operations without convention activity conflicts. HKØNA was another no brainer for the 2012 Saturday banquet but that didn't happen in favor of a non-DX presentation, if I recall correctly. At least it got the Sunday breakfast slot.

Rather just read a "That's nice," headline about who won a plaque, I'd rather hear and see a presentation on what it took to make an operation happen. That's where real recognition takes place from the audience. Presentations about the travels of retired military hams or Noble Laureates, while interesting, would be more appropriate for a Saturday afternoon slot.

It was unfortunate that many missed the 7O6T presentation on Saturday afternoon because of schedule conflicts, missing Dave's, K3LP, insightful response about how someone might best prepare to go on a DXpedition: "Contest and get your rate up."

73 - Steve WB6RSE

On Feb 15, 2023, at 5:14 PM, Chortek, Robert L. via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

We could focus on what they actually accomplished instead of what “should, could, might, etc.” have happened.

18,883 Q’s from the most difficult place on earth in 8 days of operation.  

There have been many failed operations over the years (1968, 1981, 1988, 2018 and possibly others) but these guys actually managed to activate Bouvet and put an ATNO in many of our logs.  Without this operation many of us would still be wishing someone would get on the island and give us an ATNO.

Moreover, how many of us who managed to work them and put an ATNO in our logs would rather they had skipped the operation altogether and left us with another needed entity? No a single one of us - but Im just guessing.

Thierry has been doing an incredible job from Crozet.  Many have compared the Bouvet operation to the Crozet operation and found the former lacking by comparison.  There is NO COMPARISON between the difficulties faced by 3Y0J and FT8WW.   I would even begin to list them.  

Here is some food for thought:  FT8WW had about 19,000 Qs by a single operator in about 21 days with a 500 watt amplifier. Slightly less than 7,000 Qs per week. 3Y0J had 18,883 Qs in 8 days by two stations running BAREFOOT.  If you prorate the performance you get 9,416 Q’s per station in just 8 days sitting in a tent on a cold floor.   

No one is criticizing the FT8WW operation and none should.  Thierry is doing an incredible job.

I’m sure there will be lessons learned from the  3Y0J operation and a list of things that might, in hindsight, have been done differently.  

Part of the problem, as I see it, is that human nature causes us to compare what we “expected” to what “actually” happened.  If the reality falls short of the expectation, we are unhappy even if we would have been absolutely thrilled with the actual result if we had had no expectation or a lower expectation.

Let’s give these guys the credit they deserve and be happy if we managed to get just one contact for an ATNO.

Just saying….

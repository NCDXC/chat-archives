..worth noting the Green Heron does not like the low and mid price HyGain rotors --it works but almost impossible to make accurate---Hygains pot for direction feedback is "off"...it is not always  wound linear and the Green Heron can be dialed to a tenth of a degree (not thay it matters with MY antennas)..but I found up to 20 degrees off, especially at mid-point because of that uneven wiper resistor construction---also HyGain is as I recall ac and the Yeasu is dc...motors are better as are the mechanicals in Yeasu  and last, to me the controller picks the rotor---I like to be able to reach up, dial in a heading and let go---it ramps up to speed, eases down as it approachs your selected setting and hits the brake automatically...one touch---less if you are tied to your rig.   The cogs in the Yeasu are so closely spaced there is virtually no beam "swing" in high winds so all in all a good combo for the smaller/mid size setups  JIM K8JRK

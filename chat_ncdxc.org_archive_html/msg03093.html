On Sep 10, 2013, at 9:22 AM, Paul Ewing <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:

Hello Northern California DX Club fellas,
 
Well, yesterday after many months (okay years) of planning, we completed the project to replace my 55’ tubular tower with three element SteppIR Yagi with a US Tower TX-472MDP 72’ tower with a SteppIR DB36.  Wow, what a big project.
 
I am very thankful for the help that I received from Jake-K6MP who prepared the hole and the concrete foundation for the TX-472. The hole was 5’ X 5’ by 7 1/2 feet deep. It consumed 7 3/4 yards of concrete!
 
I'm thankful to Dennis AG6HE and Mike K6MKF for helping me take down the MA-550 and the three element Yagi. George N6NKT helped me prepare the rotor cable.
 
I am thankful that I was able to hire Kurt K7NV and Glenn K6NA of “Tower Busters” to manage the crane operations and to coordinate the lifting and removal of the MA550 and placing the TX-472 in place.
 
Kurt and Glenn did a masterful job of rigging and installing my rotor and mast and placing the DB36 on the tower. I am very grateful that I could hire Kurt and Glenn to do the heavy lifting that ensured this projects success. These guys are true professionals and masters of their craft.
 
Unfortunately, Murphy visited us several times. Bob-W6OPO discovered that the second hand tower’s motor and switch were dead and in his true fashion he figured out a work around so that we could still manage to raise the tower. We also discovered bad N connectors on a important cable just as it was being lifted by the crane. Fortunately we had a spare cable on hand and was able to work through the issue. Larry-W6OD provided valuable insight and was a pleasure to meet.
 
Dennis from A/C Crane Service was very professional and had a great attitude. During a point where the crane had some downtime, we used the crane to lift my kids old kiddie play house over the fence and into the neighbors yard where their kids will now enjoy it.
 
During this project, I received excellent support from Adam-K7EDX and Jim-K4DLI at SteppIR antennas. What a great bunch of guys on this project!
 
I've uploaded yesterdays photos to my Flicker.com page at this link:
 
http://www.flickr.com/photos/26802917@N05/sets/72157635463111611/
 
Please let me know if you have any questions,
 
Paul N6PSE

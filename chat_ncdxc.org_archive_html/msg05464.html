On 2/6/2014 7:26 AM, Rich Holoch wrote:
> I figured I'd ask to see if anyone is using a headphone preamp / > filter of any sort between the headphone and K3.
Rich,

As I retired pro audio engineer, I'd say the definitive answer to that is no. The gain distribution in the K3 is entirely adequate to drive decent phones unless the user has serious hearing issues. The K3 has a menu setting for AF Gain that you may want to set for high if you don't have enough. The K3 also has excellent TX EQ to shape the TX audio, and even RX EQ to shape what you hear in your cans (or speaker). FWIS, I like to keep the RX EQ flat and do what I need to shape response on the fly with the IF settings.

I do have the DXE preamp on my Beverages, but bought it when I was running MPs, which needed the preamp. The K3s probably have enough gain of their own.

The DSP noise reduction seems most useful in situations when a more traditional impulse-type Noise Blanker gets pumped by strong signals outside the IF passband. It's worth the time it takes for find the "right" settings.

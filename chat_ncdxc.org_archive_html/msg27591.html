I would run reduced power with my KPA1500 soon after I purchased it in a misguided effort to increase its longevity, but noticed that absolute heat dissipation was considerably higher at lower power levels than it was at full power. Like swept wing performance at near supersonic speeds, solid state amps have a relatively narrow operating envelope. Gotta keep them in that narrow band between stall and overspeed!

It isn't just that the efficiency drops, but that is drops so rapidly that the power dissipation actually increases at lower power levels.

In the 1970's, the government issued a rule that if a manufacturer advertised an audio amplifier as having a particular output power rating, they had to show that it wouldn't overheat when running a sine wave at 1/3 of the rated power, because that was the worst case for heat generation.

This basic audio principle also applies generally to RF amps.  The exact details of it are a function of the music properties for audio, or the modulation mode for RF.  But it is a good guideline.

The efficiency of the devices improves as the power rises. I keep the efficiency screen up and have the fans set quite conservatively. I have noticed a more rapid increase in heat sink temp at lower powers, where the efficiency drops from 50-55% to as low as 30%. It's important to keep it tuned, watch the temp, and don't inadvertently run more than 1.5 Kw under any circumstances. Be sure your wattmeter is calibrated and GL!

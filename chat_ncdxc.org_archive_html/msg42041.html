On Feb 23, 2026, at 7:43 AM, Paul N6PSE via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

The 3Y0K team has posted their "DQRM strategy"  . What do you think?

  1. DQRM strategy
This strategy may only be implemented if the DQRM is very bad and slows down our QSO rate significantly.
Normal CW/SSB pileup handling is listening UP 1 kHZ on CW and 5 kHz on SSB.
DQRM strategy is to work simplex on all bands within a 20 kHz bandwidth for CW and 30 kHz for SSB.
We will work simplex or “contest mode” within the 20 kHz window in CW. For example, operate SIMPLEX between 14010 and 14030 kHz.
In DQRM mode - we do S&P within a specified window like in any contest.
All stations are calling SIMPLEX. 3YØK tunes across the simplex window and works stations SIMPLEX.
 

Not the worst idea in the world. It’s sort of a “Don’t call us, we’ll call you,” the idea being that the DX is not sitting on a constant frequency that can be easily jammed. It definitely gets rid of the frequency police (“Up, up!”). 

It does makes things harder for the DXp operator, because for this to really work, he/she has to actively move around the calling window, preferably in a quasi-random manner. (Spin the dial after each QSO.) If he/she sits on one frequency, the ploy is negated, and even if the op simply works gradually up and down in the window, everyone will pick up on this and the whole pileup will move with him, including the DQRM.

And of course, it doesn’t work at all for FT modes, especially Superfox (which requires the hounds to know the fox’s transmitting frequency EXACTLY (within a few tens of Hz).

Rich KE1B

This is exactly the sort of scenario that convinced me to put up my Optibeam. When I was evaluating antennas, I looked at just about everything on the market, from Steppir to Force-12, Hygain, etc. There were clearly a LOT of folks who like the Steppir; it almost seemed like a cult following. However, the idea of having a whole bunch of moving parts and a complicated mechanical assembly up at the top of the tower is what turned me off. In addition, the *necessity* of an antenna controller just added more complexity, particularly since I want logger integration and run on a Mac platform.

The Optibeam has no moving parts, no traps, no loading coils, no capacity hats, and no adjustments AT ALL. (Seriously, there aren't even instructions during assembly about "for the CW portion, set this element to such-and-such a length, etc.") Like the Force 12, it is an interlaced Yagi; essentially five monobanders mounted on the same boom, with a single feedline. The antenna was designed and manufactured to millimeter precision, and everything went together exactly per the drawings; everything clearly labeled, no off-length pieces, no holes to drill. There are no measurements (everything is pre-drilled) and NO ADJUSTMENTS, not even at setup/assembly. I put it together per the drawings, and the antenna met all specs on all bands, 20/17/15/12/10m. It has not been touched since going up in the air.

A little more expensive than some others, but to me, not having to rebuild the antenna after a few years is more than worth it.

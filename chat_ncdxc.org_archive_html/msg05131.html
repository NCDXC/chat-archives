Apparently no joy on-island re satellite connection for uploading logs. So I will seek another 17 CW Q when the opportunity arises. 17m was new for me and I suspect that with my high horizons anything higher will be problematic.

All,

 

               An email from John, 9M6XRO, had the following paragraph that I thought the club members might like to know about, in the event that any one worked FT5ZM on 17 CW yesterday:

 

BTW I heard you working FT5ZM last night - it was either on 40 or 30m - I was trying both bands and got him a bit later on 30m. I could also hear two station using "FT5ZM" on 17m CW almost on the same frequency. There was not much to choose between them in signal strength and my Quad was not sharp enough to let me figure out who was the pirate. They were both working EU stations at a similar rate so half of them are going to be NIL. It is really sad when this happens to a DXpedition with a $450,000 budget.... Last year on Spratly we had the same situation with someone using our call 9M4SLL virtually zero beat on us. We strongly suspect it was a pirate in China as they kept trying to jam us especially with Chinese music on our SSB freqs. Spratly is a big issue for them...they kept shouting "China, China...no Malaysia" on our freq.

 

            73 Kip W6SZN

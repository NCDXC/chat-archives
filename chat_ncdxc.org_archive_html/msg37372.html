Will, it is a balance as to what you're willing to spend and what you think you need.  Back in the 70's I bought a grounded grid pair of 811's amp, 500w.  Later I upgraded to a solid state 500w which is all I used to work everything on a Mosley TA33jr.  The sunspot cycle was probably better back then too.  But I think you need at least 500w.  Which means an Elecraft KPA500 would do and there are a number available used that come on the market as people upgrade to 1500w.  There are other brands out there including tube amps that you might find for $1000 and some 1000w solid state amps for under $2000.  I would recommend a solid state amp.  If you go with a tube amp, I would stay away from those that have hard to find and pricey tubes that you might have to replace.  Also you have to consider how to key the tube amps from the newer transceivers.  If you work CW you should consider whether the amp is capable of break-in keying.

There are three choices:
A.    WSJT-X FT8 F/H (MS)
B.    MSHV FT8 F/H (MS)
C.    WSJT-X SF (MS)

They all work but they all have drawbacks.

#A Requires you to select F/H in order for it to work.  About 15% of users appear to not do this - partly because you never know for sure what the DX is doing (F/H or MSHV) since there really isn't a good way to tell quickly.  What is printed in the Dxpedition's instructions is only sometimes followed.  It also sacrifices the first 1000 Hz.  It is, of course, apparently the best system for them to hear us.

#B is generally good but people who need to have a "73" are often disappointed.  I think that is not a practical requirement but others do...It avoids the other problems with #A.

#C is pretty awesome - it keeps the DX signal at maximum power, solves the "73" issue (most of the time), allows you to work them anywhere your radio will hear (like 5KHz), doesn't sacrifice any bandwidth but...
It is not ready for prime time.  The KEY thing appears to be an issue if you care, since it is not really past Beta there are many versions and the one that you have may not be the one the DX has and it may not work, if the DX is not loud you may not be able to notice their transmission.

So, If it is between A, B and C for today I would pick B; if it is between A and C today, I would pick A; but give it six more months and I would probably go with #C.

David W6DR

When we moved to Texas I needed a temporary antenna while I waited for my tower.  The Greyline OCFV "flagpole vertical" made sense so I went that route (OCFD is a proven, solid concept IMO). Sadly the 4 sections relied on solid  fiberglass insulators which were required to hold short screws. The antenna lasted one season before it began ejecting screws . I replaced the fiberglass with 1/4 inch wall aluminum tube stock and made the " flagpole" antenna into... a flagpole. As the joints between the sections are now made of thicker wall sections than the sections themselves the whole assembly flexes beautifully in the occasional very high winds we experience. On the other hand Wendy did add to her first DXCC on it...I have heard the current  version is better engineered.  Jim K8JRK and Wendy WF8R

On Sun, Jan 18, 2026 at 11:32 AM Dave Bottom via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
Certainly the antenna I put up getting back on the air in 1980 at our
new house, in Mountain View.

I picked up a HyGain 18AVQ?  80-10M Trap Vertical. I buried 4 radials
for each band in the ground (before I knew they should be shorter when
buried), and even with my new FT-101ZD and a Clipperton-L it took just
over 6 months to work my first 100 countries.  Yes, with the radials too
long my feed point was underground.  Then the traps...  Oh my...

However, with this same poor setup, I ran a regular Phone Patch for a
fellow in Finland to his daughter in Marin County every week at Midnight
on 10M.  When was the last time 10M was that open at midnight?

Fast forward to a temporary location in 2014, the ARRL CW DX Contest in
just 6 hours worked 105 countries.  Antenna was a ladder line feed full
size 80M Dipole at about 34 feet. Used on all Bands 80-10M.

We often learn the most from our mistakes.

Dave WI6R

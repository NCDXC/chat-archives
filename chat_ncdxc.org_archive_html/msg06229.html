Jim regardless of the processing power you are looking for (and for most ham applications going for the fastest proc is not necessarily the best bang for the buck)... but just a Win8  ham world collision note..

There are some intricacies with Windows 8.X I have discovered that can be problematic in the ham world.  This has casued me to freeze upgrades at N6WM and for our team to do the same at K6LRG at Windows 7.

Ham hardware and their drivers are sometimes done on the cheap. In certain circumstances, they develop drivers, but do not submit them to Microsoft for qualification.  In the past this resulted in a pop up box that could simply be acknowledged, and then the drivers would load.

In Windows 8, by default I have discovered this not to be the case by default.  Windows 8 will not load the unsigned drivers in fact in 8.0 where I stopped my experiment, it didn't even notify the drivers wouldn't be loaded.

There are a series of "hacks" to get this to work, but the stability of the drivers could be in question.  The transition to the 8.X UI will take a learning curve as well.

Its not that it cant be done, but there could be some challenges involved that may or may not be worth your time.

Again just some casual observation...  Your mileage and decisions may vary.

Crew,

I started looking for a new laptop back in the fall, and the best advice (I think) was to wait for a Thinkpad with one of their new "Haswell" 
processors. Well, they're due to hit the street in late April, and I see very attractive units at equally attractive prices. Specifically I'm looking at a G710 with 8GB RAM and a 1 TB hard drive, for $600 (this is a special price from Lenovo -- they say the list price is $849). I see two issues, though, that give me pause.

First, they only come with Windoze 8, and I've seen suggestions to wait for 8.1. At least for the shack computer, I need a computer that doesn't fight with my ham software. I'm mostly thinking audio here.

Second, they have only a touchpad, not a red mouse button. I hate touchpads, and have mine turned off.

One "gotta have" is a VGA port so that I can feed a projector to do presentations at meetings.

I don't mind backing up a generations if I can get something that better meets my needs, but I will probably buy more than one (the XYL needs one, and I need something on which to do modeling, etc.), and I really like the idea of being able to use common accessories (power supplies, batteries, etc.)

Comments and suggestions appreciated.

73, Jim K9YC

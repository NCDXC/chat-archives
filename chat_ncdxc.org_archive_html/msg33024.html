There were times this past year that focus on DX, was difficult to maintain.  Life sometimes, just gets the way. 

While DXing as much as I could, I was able to work in a vacation, whole house exterior painting, complete SteppIR DB36/80 rehab (and the usual home chores) into the timing making it a particularly hectic summer and fall.  Who ever said retirement meant nothing to do?  I need employment, if just to rest!

I am a firm believer in early hard pressed attack on any team or individual that offers new slot opportunities; because ‘stuff’ happens (Bouvet left early, while I was constrained to only 500 watts; amps of teams failing, wars starting and some teams, frankly didn’t try very hard for more than calling homeboys, working elsewhere was incidental).  As a result, I find that it’s best to get as much done as rapidly as they offer the band mode slots and conditions allow.  That approach, pays off.

Most teams quickly settle into a routine unless really motivated by pilots or other ‘prods’ (more bands on different modes!).  Others, made rookie mistakes (completely ignoring loud zones, in favor of home; RTTY in the middle of the FT4 window, only a few nearby stations were heard but a lot of frustration even animosity appeared) or don’t pay close attention to DAILY propagation or gray line timing.

Some teams go out for 2-3 weeks, but for the truly great teams, I’m often done in less than one (but still have to watch in case something new appears).  They are great in part, because they DO pay attention to the details (and pilots, requests via email) and offer all modes on all bands while doing this.  Paying attention results in more contacts.

Post COVID meant an explosion of teams heading out.  Many, at the same time.  FREEDOM!!!

The downside of that many at once, is that tracking as many as 6 targets (or was it 8?) at once is hectic, takes a lot of mental discipline, with lost sleep since the times zones never sync up; keeping track of what slots were still needed for each team and constant reviews of several threads of spotting lists.  Strong coffee and random naps, were staples.   (It’s a contest mode that just doesn’t end.)

Long path and gray line were both strong friends this year; some windows opened for as long as 10 minutes only.  Short term spotlights added to the excitement.  All of the bands provided something this year (I’m on 160-6M).  Slowly, ever so slowly, the band mode slots are filled.

It was like running a large wildfire or rescue (my former career); lots of activity to keep track of, plan for, all the time, non-stop; invigorating and exhausting at the same time with adrenaline peaks and valleys.  Would that I was still as capable now, as then, my multitasking of 15+ things at once, is down to about 5, maybe 7 on a good day.  But it is still just as challenging and honestly, fun as it was then.

When the team site time is over and the teams head home,  the excitement is quickly missed and DXing life, becomes mundane once again.  Fortunately, most of the year it was near continuous team activity, somewhere with a short breather between them (perfect for an adrenaline junkie!).

All while we’ve enjoyed some of the best conditions seen in many years (no, still not as good as prior solar cycles, but we’ll take it; we adapt and overcome; our rigs are hearing more than then, antennas and amps are better too, which helps to compensate).

In the end, this has been a satisfying year.   Some ATNO were achieved (I lost count, maybe 7 for me?), there were successes and failures, happiness, opportunities lost (family time, life events or simply not in the right place at THE moment), glee and frustration, pretty much the full range of emotions.  

Sometimes, the highly improbable happened, radio magic raised its head as well while what the sun gives us (propagation) it also takes away (long series of CME and solar storm blackouts).

I hope it continues in this vein a bit longer, so I can get my last 20 entities while others get their final wanted entity too.  Then what?  We’ll see, ham radio provides for LOTS of things to accomplish.  There is always something new and exciting over that horizon.

And sadly we note that for some, the final CQ has been sent as they QSY to another band.  We acknowledge them and the loss to our community as well.  It’s another kind of cycle, at the frequency of life.  #CancerSucks!

I look forward to the next year with high anticipation, because I still believe that the best, is yet to come.

C’mon 2024, BRING IT ON!

HNY ES 73,
Rick nk7i

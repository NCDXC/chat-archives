The continuing saga of trying to update my shack computer, and finding that I cannot get my Icom IC-7600 to connect reliably via USB to my MacOS logging program:

* I have determined that MULTIPLE IC-7600’s behave the same way (i.e., don’t work, with the same symptoms), so I don’t believe the USB port is broken on my 7600. I strongly suspect that the USB *implementation* on the 7600 is somehow incompatible with the Icom driver supplied for the MacOS.

* I have verified that it is not the USB port on the Mac, the USB cable, or RFI.

* My K3S works perfectly via USB connection to the MacOS logger, so it’s not the computer itself, or the OS, or the USB cable. (And no, I don’t want to use the K3 as my main radio, nor do I want to start an Icom vs. Elecraft flame war here.)

I am now trying to determine if an IC-7610 will work properly under MacOS. To that end, does ANYONE in the area have an IC-7610 that I can try to connect to my new Mac? I would be willing to bring my Mac to your location (no need to physically remove or borrow the radio). The ONLY thing I would do is connect the USB port on the radio to a USB port on my Mac. I can determine within seconds/minutes whether the configuration works properly or not.

If that configuration works, I’m willing (actually, would really like) to upgrade to the 7610. They are not available anywhere at the moment, but that will change at some point, and my current setup (7600 with older Mac) works fine.

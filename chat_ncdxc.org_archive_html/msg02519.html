I assume you have a 3 el since there are two mast to plate U-bolts, probably 1.75".  The 4 el uses four, 2" U-bolts.  Perhaps you could fab a plate with more and get the result you need?

If the antenna is pinned to the mast in some way, the winds will apply force somewhere else.  Rotor?

Some of you may know the very strong winds we have up here in the Altamont Pass.  Paul suggested that since the SteppIR U mast clamps my not do the job at my QTH, I had my neighbors help me lower my tower today with a tractor (the tower is 40' aluminum and hinged at the base).  A photo shows a year of damage where tightening the clamps didn't help.  Paul / N6PSE told me about the Slipp-Nott product.  The sales guy didn't tell me I needed two sets so I'm ordering a second set in the morning to replace the other U clamp.  I can then re-route the wiring and re-aim to North and we'll see how it goes.  The photo shows a hole that was drilled months ago to bolt the mast in place but the bolt was sheered off from these winds.

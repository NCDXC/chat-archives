Paul and Team

 

Lloyd Colvin, W6KG and Iris Colvin, W6QL.

 

My DX heroes also include those who put together DXpeditions and the DXpedition operators plus those who are not necessarily on DXpeditions but nevertheless operate under extreme conditions from their own countries and or have provided the DX community with  serious activity over their lifetime. There are two however who are at the top of my list. Lloyd Colvin, W6KG and his beautiful wife Iris, W6QL. They provided thousands and thousands of DXers with new country QSOs and QSLs and they put together and participated in *hundreds* of DXpeditions over their lifetime from 1965 until 1992. Wow!  A brief overview of their lifetime accomplishments  can be found at  http://www.ncdxc.org/newsletter/1994/dxer0194.pdf .

 

My first serious DX was a CW contact with Iris when she was VR1Z many years ago and I had no idea at the time what or where VR1Z was. Iris wasn’t rude or impatient with me as she understood I was a rookie Dxer and she took the time and care to tell me where she was operating from…and she sent me a VR1Z QSL card signed by her which I still treasure. I have *many* QSL cards from all time new ones thanks to Lloyd and Iris.

 

I have been blessed with having met and conversed many times with both Lloyd and Iris over the years in person. I consider them as friends and DX models to aspire to and I still miss them. They were really nice people and inspirational to many of us for many years…and are still inspirational to me.

 

Respectfully, Ted, K6XN

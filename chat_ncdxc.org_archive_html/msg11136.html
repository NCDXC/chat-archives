I heard John/K6MM working NA on 14250 KHz SSB this morning.  He was regularly announcing “K5P, listening five to fifteen up”

 

Sure enough, my P3 display showed a solid mass of signals 5-15 KHz above his signal.  But then I noticed spikes popping up a bit higher in frequency, in exact sync with each of John’s QSOs.

 

I tuned there and found John was working a relatively quiet “sweet spot” at 14273 KHz, up 23 rather than up 5-15.

 

I worked him after about four calls on this quiet spot, and listened while he worked quite a few more at 14273.

 

When I left, about 95% of the pileup was still calling 5-15 up between 14255 and 14265.  Nice way to thin the pile!

 

You might want to listen beyond what the DX operator tells you…

Hi all,

I am not a member in part because I'm frequently on the road (like now) and a trip to an evening Bay Area meeting would be an overnight trip (I dislike night driving these days).  Overnight means a motel or RV park, neither are cheap in the bay (and I'm cheap, hi).  

An afternoon meeting simplifies this, but usually negatively impacts member schedules. 

However, I'm noting from what's been said here that perhaps a rotating venue with or without a meal might be valid options.  A school cafeteria or gym, hotel meeting rooms or similar options come to mind.  Most will be reasonably priced or fees might be waived.  Projectors and screens are often provided or available (and are of good quality) with imbedded sound systems so everyone can hear. 

Restaurants are typically fine for 10-15 attendees, dodgy beyond that.  Their business is to serve food to the masses, not be a meeting place (feed em, ship em, bring the next bunch in; quantity based).  They don't make money if space is taken up for hours (even if they charge a fee) instead of shoveling customers through.  Those that eat elsewhere (before or after) increase the restaurant losses. 

Any food based meeting should have acceptable quality chow to the majority of the attendees.  Not everyone likes Denny's.  ;-) (Personally, I find consistently good strong but not bitter or burnt coffee hard to find.)

A review or member survey of current members home and/or comfortable travel areas shouldn't tax the board; which then tells you how far the meetings should range.  i.e.  Is an hour too far a drive?  How far is too far?  (No) food?  How long is too long for a meeting?

Now if it means that South Bay folks have to occasionally travel to say Sacramento (with gripes, duh), consider how many more members you could gain from say Reno to Redding since it increases your 'local' membership range.   They'd have to (optionally) travel just as far for a South Bay meeting.  Sauce for the goose argument. 

Since Paul opened the door, one more thing I'd like to see the group do is connect IRLP to the repeater, make arrangements for an IRLP reflector channel (usually free) so those of us outside the local repeater coverage area could still hear the spotting reports or activity on the repeater.  If there is Internet at the site, it's a trivial task and not expensive.   Hint: the repeater coverage is really thin in the foothills, at least at my place with an HOA hidden 10db antenna. 

It would also allow an enhanced coverage area for members over a wider area as repeaters and nodes connected to the reflector.  That can be a dual edged sword, but done well not risky (can be made listen only for example).

I have two nodes and can offer coverage around one of them if wanted.  Since they're not on hilltops, it's local only coverage. 

73 y'all,
Rick wa6nhc

On Apr 29, 2013, at 5:14 PM, <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:

 
I’d like to see the NCDXC, possibly in partnership with another club or association sponsor a teacher/ham and ultimately a school based station, such as what is being done in Southern California with the Dorothy Grant Elementary School Club Station (K6DGE) http://k3lp.com/dges_field_day_2.htm
This group has gotten a teacher licensed and now has a group of young kids on the air with donated equipment and antennas.   How cool is that!
 
I’d like to see us have some joint meetings, perhaps with PAARA or NCCC.  There is a lot of passion and excitement in both of those clubs that could add to our member’s excitement.  
I’d like to see our club develop and sponsor several remote HF stations for our club members to use and enjoy. The technology is rather simple and the cost is not a lot. This would be a nice benefit, particularly for our HOA impacted members.
I’d like to see our club be more involved in supporting DXpeditions in and above the support that we provide to the NCDXF. We have a very sound treasury; we just need to budget accordingly. The ultra-rare DXpeditions like Heard and Amsterdam cannot happen unless we all pitch in.
I think we should work closer with the Pacificon organizers. Last year’s event was fantastic and I think there is potential to add more DX related topics.

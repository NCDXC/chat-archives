I’m not a joiner, so I won’t take the poll, but here is an opinion or two. 

 

When I operate remotely, it’s mostly to keep in touch with the folks at home (morning nets and repeaters).  Latency and rig control don’t allow what is easy at home.

 

I DX because it appeals to me, even though it is a struggle at times (part of the charm).  I’ve been offered the use of big gun stations to gather more Q’s (Pooyan for example, I still don’t have that contact), but while grateful for the offers I’ve turned them all down.  My reason is simple:  the countries in my log are from what *I* built and own.  To do otherwise violates my moral code as much as being intentionally out of band or running over legal limit power.  It’s cheating to do otherwise.

 

If I can’t get all the way to the top of the list, I won’t get there.  But every rung on the ladder is an honest climb.

 

I think that removing the 150 mile station limit from the original starting point for awards was a VAST mistake.  I could claim WAS because I’ve been in most of the states but I haven’t; it just isn’t proper.

 

I have no chance in repealing that rule change, so I follow my own code and conscience.  Other stations must do the same thing.

 

These are the reasons that I don’t bother with wallpaper; it just isn’t my style.  While it is interesting to compare the standings, DXing is a lot like golf; you ultimately only compete with yourself.  To be any good at that, you have to live with yourself too.

 

73 and Merry Christmas,

Rick wa6nhc

On 1/29/2014 8:57 PM, Paul@n6pse.com wrote:

NCDXC fellas, here are my notes on FT5ZM so far
I subscribe to your preliminary assessment of this operation as first class.
Exhibit 1 -- They have a really good handle on propagation. Their windows to work Zone 3 are fairly narrow on most bands, and they have made a point of being there at those times. There's an hour or so of prop on 160M to us, and they were there this morning. At their corresponding opening to the east coast, I saw lots of spots for them from Zone 5. They have taken great advantage of 40M openings to both Zones 3 and 5, and they have exploited 30M openings.
Exhibit 2 -- they seem to be working bands that allow the greatest number of uniques.
Exhibit 3 -- they put out good signals, and as you have noted, they aim their antennas where they want to work. And this operation is close to our antipode -- 11K miles short path, 13K miles long path!
Exhibit 4 -- every operator seems to be first rate, and their QSO rates are high.
Exhibit 5 -- working under difficult conditions, they have managed to get their log on ClubLog, which will greatly reduce the number of "insurance" QSOs.
Bottom line -- this trip looks poised to generate a lot of uniques, AND a lot of band-countries. 

73, Jim K9YC

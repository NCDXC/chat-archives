I am in full support of including members of the Sothern California DX Club and eliminating the boundary in the bylaws allowing anyone anywhere to be a full member.  I am not in favor of a name change for the following reasons.

 

If we become the “California DX Club” would some of the monthly meetings be held in Southern California? I think not but it would only be fair if we are the California DX club. If a southern California DXer becomes President would the meetings shift to Southern California? What about the summer picnic or holiday luncheon? Do they move around California? If the monthly meetings move around California say rotate between San Diego, Los Angels, Bay Area, and Sacramento I would be in favor of a name change. If the meetings stay in the Bay Area I would say no name change.

 

Matt K6EME

I’m indifferent about changing the name.  

 

I can see a number of pluses and minuses. 

 

I think it’s premature to consider a name change because we don’t know how many new members are likely to join without a name change, and how many members would only join if we did change the name.  

 

At the risk of using extreme and silly examples, I wouldn’t change the name for 3 new members. On the other hand, if it meant, we would have 500 new members I would change the name. 

 

Obviously, the actual result will be somewhere between those two extremes.   I want to see the results of the survey before making any decision with regard to a name change.

 

That said, I fully support welcoming new members from Southern California or the northwest region and including them as full members. 

 

Adding to our membership can only strengthen our organization and make it even more vibrant.

 

73,

 

Bob/AA6VB

Robert L. Chortek

Some of us have worked closely with the SCDXC folks on the IDXC committee and have formed friendships. We have come to know, admire and respect them just as we do our fellow members. 

 

Last night's board meeting was very interesting. What could have been quite contentious was handled with a lot of tact and diplomacy. Several SCDXC folks attended and made their views known. There was a lot of passion and respect for the hobby displayed last night. I came away from that meeting feeling very proud to be a part of the NCDXC. 

 

Prior to the meeting, several stalwart NCDXC members expressed strong opposition to the possibility of renaming the NCDXC to reflect a broader, more inclusive club. As the club ended, I was pleased to hear that one of those stalwart members had changed their mind during the course of the meeting and after hearing from the SCDXC members in attendance. 

 

Currently, our by laws cannot be changed until we have an IRS determination on our non profit application. Once that is resolved, the members will have an opportunity to voice opinions and vote on any changes to the club. Nothing is being done in a vacuum. Art N6MFT is providing us with calm and steady leadership. 

 

I for one would like to see the NCDXC be more inclusive, to grow, evolve and to expand our membership to be absolutely inclusive of the SCDXC folks for the benefit of all of us. I would support a name change to reflect those changes. 

 

Best 73, 

 

Paul N6PSE

After 77 years of existence, the SCDXC has begun its dissolution,
with no board member nominees for VP or president.  An email was
sent out to members.  The NCDXC offered to merge, to form what will
be called the California DX Club.  This will take about a year as
they work out their 501-C-3 application and implement any changes.

They note only 12 people show up for meetings and "almost all in
[using] walkers," and three on Zoom.  The law requires a board to
maintain the corporate status.  "It has been a long time coming."
Said another way, "Last night (Thursday) the Southern California DX
Club was dissolved.  Dissolution resulted from the declining
membership and from the lack of members who are willing to serve in
the major offices."  NCDXC is encouraging SCDXC members to join the
"California DX Club."

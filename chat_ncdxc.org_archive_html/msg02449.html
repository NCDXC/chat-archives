I have to agree with Paul, the KPA500 is an innovative, and useful tool.  It has made QRO remoting of K6LRG possible and easy.  A similar full legal limit offering, along with a KAT1500 would be awesome. (probably a bit expensive however).  Its this price point, and specific boutique customer that probably would make this a tough sell to move the volume needed for it to make sense to elecraft, but it would be sweet if they could pull it off.
 
Elecraft seems to be setting the bar for practical remote control.  I think for a combination of reasons, including a working full time ham community, CC&R's, financial constraints, etc. etc. that alot of the future of our fine hobby is going to include remote control operation of shared stations, or remote properties and this is a huge market.  the fact that they are investing more and more in tools like the K3/0 and twin mode, mini K3/0, partnerships with companies like remote rig give us as operators a hint of the direction things are going, and I look forward to more products that support this type of operation across the board.
 
Any products that support this type of operation, that, in effect put you in front of a great rig in a great location, without having to slide in the mud with your 4W drive, keeping neighbors and XYL's happy you dont have a large antenna farm in your garden,  and all of their supporting equipment, Amps, wattmeters, rotor controllers, logging support and the like.
 
I am hopeful the cost and time investment of these technologies makes this a possibility for more and more operators. 
 
Maybe someone will develop the technology and methedology to drop a parachute with a remoteable drone rig on a needed reef somewhere so it can be operated without latencey by a team of DXpeditioneers that never need to leave their shacks. ;-). Latency will always be an issue, so I guess we can be thankful physics will allow this from going too far.. and keep the adventure going hi hi.
 
Happy Monday everyone!
~Chris

Paul,

From my experience I would agree that Livestream does minimize dupes, I think KK6EK's DXE also helped reduce dupes. 
However, the internet connection must be rock solid for internet dependent tools to be effective.

If I recall correctly, H40WA had an unstable internet connection because vegetation continually disrupted Starlink's view of the sky. Rob called me using WiFi calling and he'd get knocked off the internet every few minutes, this went on over several days.

A bigger problem, especially with MSHV, is the lost or missing RR73. What I've seen and experienced myself, most recently with ZS8W:  I didn't get an RR73 so I worked him again a few hours later. The second time I did get the RR73, and I'm now in the ZS8W log twice on 40m.

We've seen this missing RR73 problem many times with another famous DXpeditioner who doesn't think dupes are a problem.

The dupe rate for TX5S was 2.62% which is the lowest I've ever seen. We had 2 Starlink Terminals running 7 x 24, both with unobstructed views of the sky and rock solid connections.

Missing the received RR73 can be caused by QRM on the caller's end, propagation, QRN, etc.

Cheers,
GS K5GS

The Clublog Livestream feature requires a very robust internet connection between the DX Station and the Clublog servers in the EU. Livestream was created to reduce dupe contacts but with its inherent problems, it actually increases the number of duplicates. 

If the connection falters in any way, the Livestream feature will fail to show the current status of the DX Station and the stations being worked. 

If you get an RR73 from the DX Station, you should be satisfied that you are in the log.

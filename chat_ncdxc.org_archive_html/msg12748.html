I never would have expected this, and while EZNec does show this, I don't always believe EZNec, especially with verticals.

But the phased vertical array, with two 34.4' verticals spaced 35' apart, and using a DXE phasing box with two quarter wave RG-11 lines is within 1 dB of the N7JW 60' vertical that has two 60' sloping top hats.

I'm not complaining, in fact I'm elated but don't want to fool myself either. I'm guessing that the vertical on 40m looks like a half wave on 40m, and that the hats compress the lobes and also reduce high angle noise.

I have 96 radials on the N7JW and 44 on the phased verticals.

I swear the Christman phasing I did a few years back was better than this DXE box but I digress.

Any comments / ideas?

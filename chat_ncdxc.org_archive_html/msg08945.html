Well, that little lovefest on 20 SSB was deeply satisfying, no? Yet we keep coming back for more!

Nice to see a short path opening to us, and congrats to those who got through, but cramming hundreds of SSB callers into 5 kHz would not seem to be an efficient means of keeping up the rate. Just where one should transmit seems a moot point. It does efficiently remind me why I detest SSB pileups.

I am deeply grateful---that I do not need EP for an ATNO. As for the IOTA group, I need that, but this has to be the hardest IOTA I have encountered, excepting those never heard at all, which is a common occurrence in the IOTA program--where half the expeditioners are clueless. And a RTTY Q would seem to be as likely to occur as winning the lottery, at least for a west coast little pistol trying to make the RTTY Honor Roll.

Let's hear it for our Euro friends with 9 bands in the log.

Garry, NI6T

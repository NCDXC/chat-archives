On Oct 30, 2012, at 4:27 PM, <<mailto:paul@n6pse.com>paul@n6pse.com> <<mailto:paul@n6pse.com>paul@n6pse.com> wrote: >My friend David-K3LP tells me that you can expect the Addis Ababa University Club station-ET3AA to be quite active in the coming >days and weeks!
Looking at DX Summit spots, they are mostly on 15 & 20m, with the following timings (Z=Zulu, P=Pacific, E=Ethiopian): 

ET3AA   20m 14-15Z   17m 08-12Z  15m 06-15Z   10m 07-14Z
ET3AA   20m 07-08P   17m 01-04P  15m 23-08P   10m 00-07P
ET3AA   20m 17-18E   17m 11-16E  15m 09-19E   10m 10-18E

Note that Paul's spot didn't stand out on DX Summit.
The propagation at the times they are on (09-19E, good for kids) is nearly hopeless, especially on the bands they are using. Any suggestions how to bag this one, short of a DXpedition to ET to activate the station during the local night?

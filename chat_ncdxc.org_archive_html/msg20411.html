Hi All,

I have a related question.

When on FT8 I will split my RX and TX audio frequencies and JTDX behaves as one would expect, however if I switch to FT4 set up a split and call CQ, within a few seconds (fraction of a cycle) JTDX aligns my RX to my TX frequency.  If I split again, it does it again.

Is there a difference in best practices or operation between FT8 and FT4 in regard to split?  
Is there a setting I missed or could it be a JTDX bug?
I don’t recall what it does once a QSO' starts but I think it keep the frequencies together.

I agree. The FT8 stations listening even and those listening odd remain in parallel dimensions: Neither aware of the other unless they sit out a cycle and listen. I suspect that ambient noise is so high in some third world DX locations that DX can easily miss callers after listening and start a new CQ cycle at a different even/odd point. I’ve heard quite a few stations alternating even and odd, while callers who responded to an earlier CQ on a different time sync continue to blindly call out of sync with the DX. I’ve done that myself. It’s easy to lose situational awareness in FT8.
Thanks to all for the enjoyable discussion. de Bruce K3NQ

Mike,

I recently asked the same question on the NCCC reflector and got some pretty good inputs. I'll forward the ones that influenced my purchases to you. Here are some of the things I thought were important along with some things I learned and my thoughts on the monitor(s) I ultimately purchased. 

Ultimately I decided a 27" diagonal display was the largest I could accommodate in my shack. I was planning to get two of them, adding to the existing 24" display which would remain in the center position. The 24" display resolution is 1920x1200, which provides a little more vertical real estate than Full HD (1920x1080) - something I find very useful. I decided I wanted a 27" display with the same aspect ratio, which is known as QHD (2560 x 1440 in the screen sizes I was looking at). I decided against an ultrawide display which gives you more horizontal real estate with less vertical. You have to figure out what works best for you.

Something I didn't know about was the current state of display technology and terminology. LCD displays come in two basic flavors these days, TN and IPS (plus a couple of variants). TN is less expensive, and may have faster response time with lower color accuracy, while IPS (and variants) is more expensive, has a little slower response time, and has very good color accuracy. Here's a link to a brief description of the technologies if you're interested: https://www.pchardwarehelp.com/guides/lcd-panel-types.php . If you search on each display type you'll find more in-depth articles. I decided I wanted an IPS (or variant) QHD display. 

And then there's connectivity. Most of the monitors I looked at have a combination of HDMI and DisplayPort (DP) connections. I thought DP must be new because I hadn't encountered it before. But I looked at the back of my 6+ year old computer and its graphics card has a DP socket, so it's been around for a while.  

After a LOT of research I chose the HP 27Q. It is actually made by Samsung. It is a great looking display that doesn't seem to produce ANY RFI and doesn't seem to be affected by legal limit HF transmissions (so far, anyway). I recommend this monitor highly, with a couple of caveats. The control buttons are on the back side of the lower edge of the display which makes them just about useless in my opinion. Fortunately, I am perfectly satisfied with the factory settings and haven't found a need to mess with the buttons. And the stand only adjusts for tilt - no up/down or rotation about the vertical axis. For now this is OK, but I plan to mount it on an arm for full positional adjustability.

After I set the display up I immediately decided I wanted a bigger screen! And more measuring showed I could actually fit a 32" display in the space. More research and I purchased an HP Pavilion 32 QHD display. It just arrived a couple of days ago and I haven't had a chance to set it up yet. I'll let you know if it has any bad habits once I've set it up. I just looked this monitor up to verify one of it's specs and see the price is dropping fast! I ordered it about a week and half ago - it's available at Best Buy now for $249 ($40 less than I paid for it). Guess I should have waited!

Brian NT6F

I think John, K6MM has done a great job reminding the team to call for West Coast and also to try to stay on 20M to wring out as many uniques / ATNO’s as possible.

 

I’m very pleased that I got in the log that first night – I’d be more than happy with just this one QSO (#339 / 334). I was able to fully test my home brewed 3L mono band 20M yagi and new ACOM-1500. Both helped a lot – that was a blast. I used to have to wait days for a Q with the really rare ones.

 

This operation is better than FT5GA was from my POV, and the operators are on so many bands and modes – it’s amazing for such a small team.

 

I would love to see them continue to wring out ATNO’s for everyone on 20M, and hope they show up on the West Coast morning gray line on 40 and 30M – as TO4E did so well back in 2003. But TO4E had at first ignored the West Coast until the pilot alerted them to pay attention, and then it was great after that – when they responded in kind to the pilots request. My favorite bands and path are 40 and 30M LP during our morning gray line.

 

73 and I hope all get at least one Q / ATNO.

 

Rich

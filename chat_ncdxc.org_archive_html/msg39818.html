Hi Mike,

I don't have experience with that particular radio, but looking online I've seen conflicting information as to whether or not the KX3's sound card is usable for this.  Most people recommend a digirig, but some have stated that the "KX3USB" cable that comes with the radio is all you need, just like with Icom and newer Yaesu radios.

I'd plug the radio in via the cable and keep an eye on the Device Manager screen to see if a new USB controller gets detected as well as a new "USB Audio Codec" in the "Sound, video, and game controllers" section of the Device Manager.  If both of those get established, you should be able to run FT8 directly from the radio and computer without the need of a third party soundcard.  If you do not get the USB audio codec device, you'll need something like a DigiRig to provide that.

My friend with a KX3 is out of town, when he's back I can ask him how he does FT8 on it (if he does).

I would be careful about using Windows 11, apparently a recent update killed the USB Audio Codec for some people I know, and this is not the first time that's happened.  They had to reinstall it to get their radios to do digital again.

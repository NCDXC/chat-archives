Some essential DXpedition elements for me:

 

1.       A team of excellent, experienced DX operators.  If it’s a huge team (eg. VP6DX) then new learner DX ops are cool, but the learners shouldn’t detract from the overall rate of QSOs.

2.       Time the operation for Fall-Winter-Spring.  Avoid summer doldrums.

3.       Give out as many ATNOs as possible.  Additional band-modes are great, but shouldn’t detract from high rates on SSB and CW on open bands.

4.       Well-planned antennas which can be heard with minimum TX power (eg. MicroLites on the South Shetlands?)

5.       Efforts to work difficult propagation paths to the antipodes areas should be in parallel with separate high-rate stations working ATNOs on open bands.  Should always try to have at least one 20M station running high rates.

6.       Digital modes, 6M, 160M are all great, as long as they don’t slow down the ATNO rate.

7.       On line log is nice to avoid DX ops wasting time on dupes, but not critical.

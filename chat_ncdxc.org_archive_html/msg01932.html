Paul; My all time favorite is the rig I have currently. In January of 2008, I bought a new Yaesu FT-450 from HRO is Oakland. A vast improvement over my previous ICOM IC-730 and IC-707. I bought Yaesu because of the unfortunate experience of the dial lights going out on the IC-707, which I bought new from HRO in March of 1999, right after moving here to the San Joaquin Valley. The dial lights went out 1 month after the warranty expired and ICOM up in Seattle was going to charge me half the price of the rig to replace the lamps, which they assured me I would be unable to replace myself without special tools to removed the face plate board from the face plate. I spent the next 7 years using the rig with no dial lights and decided I would not ever buy another ICOM radio. I liked the FT-450 so much, I bought a second one in October 2008. I like to think of them as "a matched pair" even though the first one has a green LED back light and the second one is blue. I keep one set up for CW, and the other set up for phone. I originally thought I would eventually try to develop an SO2R set up, but have since decided that idea is probably a bit more than I can do. I have found that the range of the filters and the DSP in the FT-450 has been a great help both in contests and for DXing. I would like to own one of the bigger Yaesu rigs or better yet a K3, but until I can do that I think the FT-450 is the best I could get for the money I had available. Bert, K6CSL

My response: My all time favorite rig is my Yeasu 1000MP. I bought it new circa 1991 and I still have it. This was my first "big box" rig replacing a Kenwood TS-690. I never really liked or enjoyed the TS-690. The 1000MP is a joy to operate and a very fine radio. My primary pile up busters are a pair of K3's that I use on CW/SSB/RTTY but for casual listening I still enjoy flipping the antenna switch over and listening from the 1000MP.

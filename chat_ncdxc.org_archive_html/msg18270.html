David,

In WSJT-X, go to Settings and click on the Radio tab. In the right column, near the bottom, do you have Rig selected for Split Operation? This is recommended, and assuming you do the following applies.

WSJT-X will adjust your transmit frequency to keep your transmitted audio between 1500 and 2000 Hz to minimize any filtering introduced by your transmitter. So if you select 20m, 14.074 MHz on the WSJT-x main display page, and you enter 645 Hz into the Transmit frequency box on the same page, WSJT-x will adjust your transceiver Tx frequency to 14.073. If has reduced your transmit frequency by 1 KHz and added 1 KHz to your audio. The result being that you are actually transmitting tones based at 1645 Hz, but transmitting them one KHz lower in RF frequency. Everyone else tuned to 14.074 will decode your transmission at 645 Hz.

Now, if you have NOT selected Rig for Split Operation, the above does not apply and you should always have your Transciever Tx=Rx. If you are transmitting at 14.073 with an audio Tx frequency below about 1200 Hz no one tuned to 14.074 will decode your signals because they will be below their passband. Above 1200 Hz you will be decoded.

And of course there's always the possibility your signals just aren't getting to Europe.

Hope this helps.

Brian NT6F

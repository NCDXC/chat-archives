I have mostly inactive on all but 80 M due to broken rotators.  That is a long story I will tell only if someone is concerned about theirs.  My brand new $2K rotor that had been up for two years stopped responding so I was stuck in one direction or another, but I could operate.  That condition existed for over a year.  It sat that way due to my inability to remove it from the tower.  That is also a long story.
Once I figured out how to (sort of safely) climb a partially nested crank up tower and get the mast out of the non-responding rotator, I was able to replace the rotator.  That happened in November of 2024.  On December 14, 2024, the new heavy duty rotator failed.  It failed such that it took out (read: destroyed) my coax and control cable ($5/foot) to the DB-36.  The antenna did NOT slip on the mast, if you wondered.  I got a replacement rotator up with the same effort needed for the first replacement.  Tower had to be tilted over for cable changes and antenna maintenance. Tower tilted back up and on the air.
The replacement rotator broke the same way as the other one in a few days, just after I got my needed 5N9DTG contacts.  The failure took out my coax and control cables again in the ugliest way possible.  $$$$
I had my original rotator repaired and again got it swapped in with significant effort.  Got the tower tilted over again and changed out the cables.  I got the tower back up and everything works.  Very FB.  But, the WX has been awful and I will not be able raise the tower above its low setting of 22' until mid tomorrow.
I made a contact on Thursday afternoon on 12 M after a 5 hour work session as the sun sank.  It was mostly dry but did hail.  I worked new slot, a PJ7.  I have worked 9 new slots since back on the air with the antenna fixed South at 22' working in the 180 deg mode.  I will take them.
The image is my DB-36 pointed South with its boom tethered to a tree.  I cannot rotate until I release it tomorrow, I hope.  
https://photos.app.goo.gl/xoGFfMnnu9pBiGbHA
If luck will finally be on my side for the first time in 18 months, I might be able to rotate my antenna and use it at 71'.  WX permitting.  It has been tough to miss 3 months of the best of Cycle 25, but I survived the tower climbing and hope that is the end of that.
73, es Cu in the pileups.  I hope!
Al  N6TA

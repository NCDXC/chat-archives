From the Radio Oakley team --
The N6RO team mourns the loss of our team member Dean N6BV, who passed away last july.  We got a word from his wife Rayma last week. Dean was a great colleague, and longtime friend of Ken, N6RO and was a primary contest operator at the station.  He was my primary SSB and CW elmer and nominated me into CWops during my meteoric entry into contesting.  I eventually took over as the 15m primary operator at the station filling a role he had for decades.  Dean was an instrumental design engineer and operator at Radio Oakley joining fellow antenna experts like N6BT, K6NA and K7NV in building this west coast power house and its amazing antenna systems with Ken.  Learn more about this journey at http://n6ro.com
As Vice president of the NCCC at the time, I,and the entire NCCC Bod worked hard to get him nominated and subsequently inducted into the CQ contest hall of fame. I was present on behalf of the NCCC in Dayton for his induction.  We are glad he was able to enjoy this exclusive honor.
Dean joins former N6RO top level operators Mike WA6O and Steve K6AW as a silent key member of the N6RO family and the NCCC family.  far too many.. far too quickly.  the memory is so vivid, it seems like yesterday all of us were sitting together in the station operating.. enjoying radiosport and winning contests together.
in his later years, I set up a remote for him to try and contest from his san francisco home and keep him qrv but eventually it was too much, and he did for a while join us on our weekly zoom calls, that started during the coronavirus pandemic and is now a regular event weekly.
I ask you all to join me in reflecting on the great contributions Dean made to our avocation,  to the NCCC,  to the N6RO superstation and to Radiosport. 

73  N6BV(SK)

For the N6RO team

Chris N6WM

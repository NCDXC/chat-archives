> If 3Y0K were in SuperFox mode, those people would then be QRMing 3Y0K, since the SuperFox uses a wide bandwidth.

Correct. (Now, if radios only had a remote “Self Destruct” code, we could eliminate all of the DQRM, and most of the stupid.)

> At least with regular Fox mode or MSHV, the odds of the yahoos accidentally QRMing 3Y0K are much smaller.

Sure, but the QSO run rate would be much lower. The only purpose of SuperFox is to increase QSO rates, typically for DXps with huge pileups. (It has also been used to maximize the very narrow window openings for 6M EME. Once.)

> Also, I'm currently seeing N7BUD calling CQ and IW4CIL calling TF3VE during the even cycle, both of which would lead to QRM if 3Y0K were in SuperFox mode.

DXps almost always advertise their operating frequencies. This is especially important (and necessary) if SuperFox is in use, since the software on the SuperFox end will NOT TRANSMIT on the normal FT8 operating frequencies while in SF mode. If some Italian is trying to work an Icelandic station on the same frequency as a major DXpedition, once again you can’t fix stupid.

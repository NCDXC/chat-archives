On Tue,1/13/2015 11:59 AM, Dave Gomberg via Chat wrote:
What I DON'T understand is why after all this time there are no competitively priced noise cancelling headsets. Seems like Heil and Sennheiser are the only games in town. Come on guys, this isn't rocket science, you put a small mic near the ear cup and feed the amplified signal back into the earphone speaker. Why has no one else stepped up to the plate????
Because it's NOT easy to do it right. It requires very precise matching of electroacoustic components in amplitude and phase over a wide bandwidth, which in turn means excellent quality control. It also requires that the mic and earphone be VERY closely spaced, which in turn makes acoustic feedback trickier to control. And it requires excellent consistency of the physical construction of the headset.

On Sat, Jul 12, 2025 at 6:57 AM Paul N6PSE <pauln6pse@gmail.com> wrote:
Hi Will,  thank you for your nice words. The most important thing about chasing DX is that it should be fun. Challenging yes, frustrating at times, yes indeed.  And life events will get in the way. But if you enjoy it and you stick with it, it is rewarding in various ways. 

Chasing DX and sometimes being the DX has enhanced my life in various ways. I have travelled to far away and strange countries that I might otherwise not have visited. I have made friends all around the world and they have added to my life. I pay more attention to world events and how they might impact the hobby. 

Just remember, DXing is fun. If it is not fun, something is out of alignment and needs a tweak. I have been DXing for almost 50 years and I still enjoy it very much. 

Best 73

Paul N6PSE

On Fri, Jul 11, 2025 at 11:11 PM Will Shattuck via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
Hi Paul,

Thanks for the Deserving/Underserving section.  While i have learned a lot since I joined the NCDXC I didn't really understand what it meant to be a DX Operator.  This helped me refocus a lot.  I checked in ACLog how many country's I've worked and was stunned to see it sitting at 99 worked.  Confirmed is a different story, but I'll work on that later.

So I started trying to find DX, but realized I hadn't made sure that "my station was optimized", my station is "ready and prepared", and that really "100 watts and a wire" really isn't enough.

I also realized I didn't have a way to easily identify DX that I haven't worked yet.  I started looking into JTAlert, but couldn't figure it out until I found this video on the NCDXC YouTube channel.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=55as0kIucJg

It really helped me understand how to search for the DX I want to work.

So my next action items are to:
  • Optimize my two antennas.  My G5RV JR and my BuddiHex (which was damaged in 2025 winter winds)
  • Get the "new to me" Heathkit SB-1000 (Thanks Bill N6ZFO) amp up and working with a new 220v line installed and an appropriate tuner that can handle that many watts.
My life has been crazy these last 5 years with my wife's health issues and it's been over a year since she passed.  Now I get to start checking off the "Deserving" section items to make sure I'm good.

The other good thing about this "Deserving" list is that it's teaching me some thought process I didn't have due to how I grew myself up these last 55 years.  I'm a bit sad that these "new to me" habits haven't been in place for the last 30+years, but late is better than never.

Thanks to you all who have helped me start and continue this journey of ham radio and DX hunting.  I really appreciate the help, mentoring and understanding you all have shown.  

Sincerely and 73,
Will KN6TZK

On 2/4/2014 8:04 AM, Rich Holoch wrote:
If you do, what do you have?


Wire antennas.

A delta/delta bisquare on 80 and 40--designed by me and KA6W 20 years ago, and recently compuiter-optimized by me using SimSmith. It's an apex-down delta loop on 80 and a delta bisquare on 40. Height 100 ft., which is too low for 80 and too high for 40. Matching section is two quarter wave transformers in series. After recent optimization I don't even have a tuner in-line. SimSmith is great!

The basic delta loop is "squashed" to 45 degree corner angles. Flat top is about 116' long.

40m pattern is a stretched figure 8, typical of what amounts to a collinear array. About 3 or 4 dB over a dipole and no F/B and requires a separate solution in the quadrature directions, provided by a dipole at 70'. 80m pattern is that of a delta loop, of course. 295 worked on 80, 336 on 40 (including deletes).

When I was between beams last year, it also served well on 30 and 17m and I used a tuner for the other bands.

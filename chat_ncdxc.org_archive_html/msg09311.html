I did manage to work them last night on 40M SSB.  It was a little after 1:00 AM, and I was running 100W into a multi-band inverted-V.  I followed the op as he responded to stations calling him (love the bandscope on the IC-7600), and then called him when he got to the top end of the listening range he had announced.  He caught the suffix on my first call, and came right back to me when I repeated it.

I could hear them on 80 as well, but couldn't get through.  They weren't as strong on that band, so it's no surprise.  I might have had a better chance of working them on 80 if I stayed up a couple more hours, but I had to go to work this morning and wanted to get a little sleep.

So I've managed to work them twice; once on 15 and once on 40, both QSOs are SSB.  I was really hoping to get at least one CW and one RTTY contact into the log, but most of the CW and RTTY success stories seem to happen during the work day.  So I guess I'll have to be satisfied with a couple voice QSOs.  

This whole "day job" thing is really a distraction.  Maybe I need to buy a lottery ticket or two.  hi hi

Frank  N6OI

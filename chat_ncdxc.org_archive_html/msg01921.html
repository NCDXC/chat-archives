I really liked my TS830S back in the day, I saved up a bunch of $$
working all summer long to pay for it, and that was after a few years
of lusting after it and looking closely at every QST ad I could find,
reading as much as I could get my hands on, etc.

When I finally got it, it was a joy to use.

Then, around 2006 I build an Elecraft K2. The fun was back again!
Great little radio and I had a blast building it. The receiver was
great and my old TS830S went on the shelf. After using the K2 for a
year or so, the K3 came out and I got one of the first ones. It was
everything I wanted - great Rx, great filters, and lightweight so I
could put it in my backpack on the way to wherever I was going. Gone
are the days of carrying 75+ pound radios on the airplane
(thankfully), and since having the K3, I rarely struggle to defeat
QRM.

Doing a lot of travel for work, I got one of the first KX3's to take
with me, and have been loving it as well. I prefer the K3 still, but
then again its hard to beat such a small package for taking on a trip
for fun when raw horsepower isn't the main objective.

In fact, I'm leaving this week for another trip, see you on the bands
(with KX3) :)

73 de N6GQ, YN2AA, etc., etc.

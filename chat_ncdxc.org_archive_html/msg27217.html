All of the comparisons of the SNR advantages of digimodes in relation to CW and SSB assume that CW will be decoded through a narrow filter, reducing the noise and elevating the SNR. In real life, the advantage of CW over SSB frequently cannot be fully realized because of the limitations of our receivers. Most of the stuff I’ve read describes the FT8 signal bandwidth as 6.25 Hz, and CW as closer to 25 Hz. With ideal filtering, this would imply a 6 dB advantage of FT8 over CW. In real life the “filtering” of the FT8 signal is achieved by the DSP software, which may be more effective than traditional filtering in (my) “old-tech” receivers. The numbers I’ve seen for reliable decoding are -20 dB for FT8, and -15 dB for CW. That would seem to be consistent with theory, although I suspect a 6 or 7 dB advantage may be more realistic..

There’s an additional positive point in regards to CW… we DXers can recognize our own call, and the ubiquitous “599” even slightly below the noise. That’s our own built-in signal processing and error correction based, just like WSJT, on a predictive algorithm!  For a stereotypical DX contact, that could even narrow the advantage of CW versus FT8, although I’ve had my share of human a3? decodes on CW … especially during the ARRL DX test.

So far, the best triple-stream SNRs I’ve seen from FT8WW have been between -12 dB and -22 dB. Allowing for significant signal strength degradation caused by multistreaming, CW should be completely feasible, and maybe even SSB with a minimum of +5 dB signal strength. Thierry was on 30m CW a few hours ago, so we can hope we’ll have a chance for CW, and maybe SSB over the next 2 ½ weeks.

Disclaimer: I’m not a tech guy, so if I missed something, let me know, but gently… not like the poor guys that are calling off-cycle!

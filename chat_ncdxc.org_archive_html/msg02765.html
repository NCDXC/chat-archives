Great topic!!!

My last thought

If a DXpedition wants to fully self fund on their own they should not be at all discouraged and more power to them heck they can do or go wherever and take whoever.  It is indeed a hobby. In fact folks do this all the time. 

HOWEVER what I do think it that certain organizations offering big support money, in particular the NCDX FOUNDATION should consider operator experience and pre-qualification prior to offering funding. e.g. if not X qualified ops then 0 funds.  when it comes to tens of thousands of dollars, even hundreds, hobby ends (for the team accepting money to follow through on a commitment) and responsibility as well as follow through begins.

If I (we) donate my(our) sheckles to NCDXF or other dx supporting organization, my expectation is it will be properly stewarded..  team evaluation for effectiveness would be a part of that.

Team

As long as there is at least *one or more skilled experienced operators* on a major DXpedition team I don’t object to there also being lesser experienced DXPP  operators on a major DXpedition team. If for example a Medical Doctor or a PhD environmentalist is needed (or even a good cook) to support the effort and he or she is not a top notch operator or if someone’s contribution in money or equipment or political influence is needed for the operation to move forward then I would not object to anyone or any organization  offering additional support.  FWIW many of us routinely contribute as a matter of course to many “casual” mini DXpeditions by adding a few dollars when requesting QSLs from less than optimal operators who went to the personal expense and effort to travel to  locations comprising new “band modes” needed by us. They were there (when nobody else was there or operating) and I would not recommend DX Foundations (or individuals) deciding not to support a DXpedition simply because “DXPPs” are part of the DXpedition team.  73, Ted, K6XN

PS Why would we want to encourage *not* supporting a DXpedition effort that is otherwise credible and has skilled experienced operators simply because there are also  DXPPs as part of the Team or because the  “credibility” of an operation might be  “damaged” in the minds of some or “chaos” might exist as a result of an error by a “DXPP”?  I don’t mean to be insensitive or uncaring but so what!  If there are experienced operators managing the Team effort I say support it.   IMHO the experienced team operators will usually know how to manage these issues and apply appropriate KITA motivation and training as needed to manage DXPPs before and during the operation.

Martti Laine-OH2BH has written several recent articles about DXPP’s or “DX Paying Passengers”. Martti describes a DXPP as an operator with only a minimal degree of operating expertise, but who contributes to the Dxpedition financing to help the Dxpedition.
Martti writes that DXPPs can seriously damage the credibility of an operation and create chaos on the bands. He is critical of the K5D Desecheo Dxpedition where a strong operator was relieved by a lesser skilled operator and problems resulted. Martti also criticizes the TX5K Clipperton Dxpedition, writing that “a seat was available for anyone who wanted to pay”. Martti suggests that the number of TX5K DXPPs added to the stress level of the Dxpedition team members and the Dxpedition audience.
Martti suggests that DX Foundations consider the number of DXPPs included in a Dxpedition when funding them. He writes that a Dxpedition may not qualify for support if the percentage of DXPPs is too high.

Here is your Question of the week: As members of the DX Community, are you concerned about DXPPs?   Do you feel that a Dxpedition suffers if there are a few lesser experienced operators on the team?   Should the DX Foundations offer less support for a Dxpedition to a rare place even if they have some lesser skilled operators on the team?
Give us your thoughts-

Paul N6PSE

This is exactly how I do it too:

On CW, I send as fast as I think the op can copy, the intent being to squeeze my call into the shortest possible time so that I'm less likely to get stepped on before the DX has it all. On both CW and SSB, I call twice, listen, call once, listen, call once listen,(only the first time twice)until the DX calls someone (or a partial that isn't me). 

I usually end up at 24 wpm - slower sometimes on 160M - like 21 wpm. I especially like 160M - because that band reminds me of the "old way" of DX-ing - where people seem to take turns and are quite polite. Plus - the loud signals are close - so you know everyone calling. On SSB I call with phonetics the first time and just read the letters the second. 

I have been working a ton on calling a LOT less than I used to - and working more on "zinger" timing. That has been the best improvement I have ever made. Timing trumps calling "too much" every time.

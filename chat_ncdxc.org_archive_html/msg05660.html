Paul,

These statistics only tell part of the story. The propagation statistics data shows how bad it really was from zone 3.

Of the 8117 NA QSOs, less than 1100 were with zone 3. Only 1 on 160m and 22 on 80m and 20 on 40m - and this was a low band emphasis??? FT5ZM was easy to work on 160/80m around 15z - these guys were working their buddies back in France on the higher bands at that hour. I only need FR on 160/80m and I didn't work them on either band.

On the other bands, their signal averaged about 25 dB lower than FT5ZM. That is certainly not a good omen for Tromelin.

So, I give them a D-; better than FT5GA (there's a low bar), but way below FT5ZM.

Steve, K6UM


On Tue, Feb 18, 2014 at 9:58 AM, Paul Ewing <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:
TO7CC wrapped up this weekend. They made a total of 49,367 contacts. Initially they were tough to work. Not because they were hard to hear, but they were employing techniques such as “French only” and “UK only”. Towards the end they had pretty much worked EU and they were much easier. I made four contacts with them but could have made more, but found that there was a certain level of aggravation in working them vs. the pure excitement and enjoyment of working FT5ZM. These were drastically different kinds of operations using much different techniques.

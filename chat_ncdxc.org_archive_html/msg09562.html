If someone knows the pilot for E30FB (or has a direct email line) please
condense and pass along;

As noted by many now posting their weekend contest scores (another list),
conditions are rather miserable this week.  Ok, they suck. ;o) Solar
activity has not been in our favor this week.

Eritrea is about as far away from the West Coast as one can get (not quite,
but close).  It was always going to be a roll of the dice for small
stations.  (No need for antipodal discussions.)

The stations worked on 15 meters today is hit and miss in CA.  Even stations
a few miles apart range from S9 to ESP (below my noise floor as I type).
The opening has a hopscotch pattern, not a lot of sense.  Not even
variations in antennas can't explain it. it's like a shotgun with an open
choke... hits scattered everywhere.

The opening on ten meters (peak S3 forecasted) is about an hour (thanks
K6TU, I printed it out).  I could see the pileup, but not even a glimmer of
the station.  Many of the 6 calls are SoCal or in 7 land.

I have a simple station (cursed HOA).  But I note (via VOAProp and
HamCap) that 20 meters should be open from there to somewhere 24/7. 20 has
always been 'the money band'.

**One DX station should be dedicated to 20 M 24/7, preferably CW since CW
maximizes any stations ability.  That would provide max ATNO to ease the
pressure for many (me too) and likely the pressure on other bands as
well.**

I appreciated the 20 minute W6 window on 20 CW yesterday; but with the
robocallers, DQRM and basically really crappy operating by far too many, I
still wasn't able to punch through (with 600 watts plus they were fairly
loud).  I'm hopeful those lids are gainfully employed and at that location
this week so I still have a chance while they're away.

In closing, I submit that the DX CW stations on site vary their TX frequency
(30 Hz or so) from time to time to help defeat the DQRMers.  A few were spot
on the same exact frequency...  zero beat is zero beat... 
nuff said.

It's not sour grapes, I get that I can't work the world from a dipole in
this meager cycle.  But those working in less than optimal conditions (like
my station and most DXpeditions) need every advantage to make it work he
best for all.

73 es gud hunting...

Rick wa6nhc

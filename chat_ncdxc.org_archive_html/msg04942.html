Sounds like a bad idea. Sounds like you could easily create a short between the center pin and the shield connector. 

Even if there was a clean way to apply the no-alox, heating and cooling (especially summer heat) could cause expansion - and shorting.

I only use no-alox between each aluminum element in my home made (designed and built) vertical arrays and yagi's. And I use 4 pop rivets on each joint as well, with a 4" overlap. Some go even further, but I have found this approach to work great. Of course - we do not have the extreme WX they do in other parts of the country.

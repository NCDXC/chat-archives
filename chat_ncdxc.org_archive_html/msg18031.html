On Sat, Oct 19, 2019 at 4:26 PM eddrad--- via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
Andy and Dennis were magnificent in the work they did last Sunday and today in helping me get my refurbished self-supporting Wilson MT-61 tower erected, with
my refurbished Force 12 C4 antenna on top, at its rightful place 63 feet above ground.

I couldn't have done this work without Andy and Dennis.

On 15 meters and 20 meters, the 1.5:1 bandwidth more than covers the entire band, phone and CW.  This is indicative of the fine engineering work
done by Tom N6BT when he designed this antenna many years ago.

On 10 meters, the 1.5:1 bandwidth stretches from 28.0 MHz to 29.25 MHz.

On 40 meters, the resonant frequency is evidently just below the low edge of the band.  The SWR at 7.01 MHz is 1.2:1, and the 2:1 bandwidth takes me
up to 7.063 MHz.  With my MFJ antenna tuner, I can get the SWR below 1.3:1 across the entire band, phone and CW.  The resonant frequency of the 40 meter element
didn't rise as much as I thought it would when I took the antenna from being ladder mounted at 5 feet above ground to where it is now at 63 feet above ground.

The rotor (which mounts at the bottom of the tower base) still needs a little work, in that I need to drill a couple of holes through a steel plate to get 
everything to fit together.  That work should be completed on Monday.

CU in the fall contests!

73,  Ed  AJ6V

Some think this discussion is about me and what I want for me. That could not be further from the truth.
 
I care about the NCDXC and our image and  reputation. I think we look silly when we handed our award to a DXpedition that was undeserved and we overlooked several DXpeditions which were much more worthy. These are just my opinions of course.
 
Those that know me know that I don't care or need a plaque on my wall. Heck, even though I have 312 entities confirmed, I've only got DXCC with 105 entities listed with the ARRL.  I've given many points in contests but I have yet to submit a contest log.
 
I would have been so proud to see our award go home with K4UEE the Leader of HK0NA or K3LP the Leader of 7O6T who both sat there and watched us award PT0S. Even ZL9HR and NH8S were more deserving. We got it wrong in 2013 and I feel embarrassed for the club.
 
This discussion is not about what I want. It is about the NCDXC and our image as seen externally to the club. I think we look goofy when we give an award to a DXpedition that had some poor operating by part of the team.  I'm not afraid to say that. We can learn from this and be better. Only if we cannot get consensus and improve our process should we eliminate this award. We haven't tried hard enough to kick the can to the curb.
 
As for DXpeditions, they will happen whether you award them or not. Our award should be prestigious and well earned. SWODXA usually gets it right in their annual Hamvention presentation. They have a lot more practice than we do.
 
I would like to see our award be coveted by DXpeditions and that some would actually make an effort to satisfy our members needs. That is the benefit to offering these rewards.  Sometimes a mere donation accomplishes that but sometimes as we have seen even that does not always occur.
 
Cancelling this annual award as we are just getting started is the easy path to avoid further controversy. But then we are left again with each club member fending for himself and we have no sway or influence with the DXpeditions.
 
Lets revise and improve how we give this annual reward. Lets make it more meaningful and sought after and the result will be an annual award that will be prestigious and that we can all be proud to bestow on the best DXpedition of each year.
 
Paul N6PSE

Yes. Why do you care? What does it mean to be "DXpedition of the Year"? What are the criteria?

Knowing you, I seriously doubt that you put in the enormous effort that you do in order to receive an award, a pat on the back, or a global "Atta Boy". I sure don't see you cutting back on future DXpedition plans as a result of some arbitrary vote.

You know better than anyone in this club the level of effort expended by the various DXpeditions, the personal discomfort and expense borne by the participants, and whether the outcome (QSOs, ATNOs, targeted bands and regions, etc.) met or exceeded expectations. The rest of us have an *idea* what it takes, and a few have gone out to join a team, but other than being able to say "I needed this one and got it", we are not generally qualified to vote for "the best" on anything other than subjective grounds.

I say, do what you enjoy doing, as long as you can do it. Achievement is its own reward. If you would do it again, it was a successful DXpedition...for YOU, the best judge.

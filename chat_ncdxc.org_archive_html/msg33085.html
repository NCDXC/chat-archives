Hi All,

I wasn't sure about submitting an entry to the DX Marathon (my score would only be about 266), but decided to do so when I learned that there is a QRP category called "Formula 5".

2023 was the year I discovered the joys of QRP - entirely by accident. Back in February on a day of fantastic propagation, I logged many DX stations before discovering that my amp was turned off! That was the start of a year-long adventure, mostly on digital modes but also CW. When propagation is really good, there seems to be almost no lower limit to how much power you need to get through on these modes. Many QSO's were accomplished with 2 or 3 watts. Highlights included working Mayotte, Uganda, Israel, and most of the southern African entities, as well as most of Europe and many Pacific islands.

My QRP total for the Marathon was 114 entities and 31 zones, for a score of 145 - not nearly enough to be a top finisher, but respectable for a new QRPer. I would have had a couple more zones in Canada, Australia, and Russia if I had paid closer attention to the zone boundaries. And I do still turn on the amp when I need it. But it's fun to try QRP first.

I'm curious whether any other club members are entering the Marathon in the QRP (Formula 5) class, or have their own QRP adventures to share!

73 and good DX,
Andy K6ZP
Montara, CA

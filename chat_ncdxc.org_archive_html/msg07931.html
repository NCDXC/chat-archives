Dear Northern California DX Club friends,
 
If you have been following my Blog, then you know that I have been having a blast playing with the remote HF stations of the RemoteHamRadio.com service. One of the stations that I have been enjoying is W7RN located near Reno. This station has amazing capabilities.
 
Now you have a chance to try operating W7RN through RemoteHamRadio.com at no cost. It has been specially configured to act as W1AW/7 and sign ups for local and remote operators are being taken. (See Below)
 
If you want to operate as W1AW/7 and try RemoteHamRadio.com for free, send an email to service@remotehamradio.com
 
Give it a try!  Its fun!   Many thanks to Tom-K5RC and the guys at RHR for making this opportunity possible!
 
Paul N6PSE
 
 
Service Bulletin October 20, 2014
Ops needed for W1AW/7 Operation


 


 

WELCOME TO THE W1AW/7 NV SECOND OPERATION!

 

WHEN:                    0000Z October 22, 2014 to 2359Z October 28

 

WHERE:                  LOCAL - The Comstock Memorial Station, Virginia City NV

 

                                  REMOTE - Through the RemoteHamRadio.com Network

 

BANDS:                   160, 80, 40, 30, 20, 17, 15, 12, 10, 6  CW  SSB RTTY 

PSK31(30,17,12 ONLY)

 

Thanks to the great folks at Remote Ham Radio http://www.remotehamradio.com/ , we invite you to participate in the week-long operation of W1AW/7 NV either in person or logged into the W7RN station remotely. RHR is setting up a unique console where you can logon from any computer, anywhere, connected to the internet. No special equipment required.

 

TECHNICAL

  • WebDX Operating Tutorial (Must watch if willing to participate) http://youtu.be/0Xlc4juU53o
  • The Remote DX Console will give you complete radio control using a mouse and keyboard
  • Must use Google Chrome browser
  • You will need a microphone connected to your computer
  • CW is operated through your computer keyboard (in RHR console)
  • Those who have full Premium DX (K3 or K3-0) can operate other modes than CW and SSB
  • You will be provided a login and password for the duration of the event
  • You must keep a computerized log in a separate window

 

RULES OF ENGAGEMENT
 

  • If you operate W1AW/7 NV, you must agree to follow the following protocol. This is a massive logistical operation at ARRL and your following the guidelines is an imperative
    • CQ: CQ W1AW/7 NV - UP
    • Exchange 59(9) NV
    • Stay away from nets, SSTV, etc, frequencies
  • Logs must be kept via any logging program that creates an ADIF file
    • Logs must be saved as Call, County, Grid. - Example K5RC_STO_DM09.adi
    • Logs must be sent to tom@k5rc.com no later than October 31.
  • There is NO CHARGE to participate in the W1AW/7 + RHR event.

 

SCHEDULING
 

  • The Remote Operating Schedule can be found at http://comstockpride.com/?page_id=155
  • It will be updated regularly starting Monday evening, Pacific Time
  • Operating will be in 1 hour minimum blocks, 2 hours maximum at a time
  • Multiple operating slot requests will be honored, especially during the nighttime hours, Pacific Time
  • We will make every attempt to honor your requested times and bands, but we can have only one station per band at a time. There will be three operating positions at W7RN and we will do our best to resolve operating time/band conflicts.
  • The Local operating schedule is also on the URL. A call in a time slot only means that the person is on-site, not that they are on the air. Remember, with RHR, we can be on 4 bands at a time.
  • SEND ALL OPERATING REQUESTS TO tom@k5rc.com
    . No phone calls, please
  • You will receive login instructions with your request. Please familiarize yourself with the console before your operating time.

 

How to Signup:


  1. Simply email service@remotehamradio.com with your request to operate the event.

 

73 and have fun!

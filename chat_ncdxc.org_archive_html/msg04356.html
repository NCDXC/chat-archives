Proxy voting and other bylaws issues:

 

I’m passing this along not as a board member but what I saw and heard at last night’s membership meeting.  Those that couldn’t attend need to hear this and sense the spirit of the meeting.

 

Wow what a great turnout.  My count was almost 50!  Jim K8JRK did good job going through the process step by step.  Every comment or point from attendees was responded to and accepted by those attending.  The slides were great support and to the point, well done!  Truly professional.  And so were the attendees.

 

Great discussions occurred and from my perspective everyone went away understanding how and why things happened.  But not everyone was satisfied with the bylaws, the rules the board had to follow. 

 

How to correct the issues with the bylaws…

 

To get started realigning the bylaws with current thought, Craig AE6RR has taken on the task of getting input from members regarding changes to the bylaws and submitting the results to the board and membership.  At the meeting Craig asked that everyone send their thoughts and language via email to him.  The idea I heard was not to ram-rod changes from any one person but to get input from a broad base of members.  Then produce recommended changes.

 

I am pleased with the outcome of the meeting and how professionally it was conducted and how the attendees were respectful and yet targeted salient points in an intelligent and articulate manner. 

 

Almost 50 members, all agreeing with the actions taken, approving them (ratify) and then on to fixing some bylaw problems.  This will make the club even better.

 

Good job everyone!

 

Now from my role on the board – Holiday Luncheon

 

Remember the Holiday Luncheon is December 7th.  Be sure to register via the club’s website www.ncdxc.org.  PayPal is an excellent means of registering, selecting your entre and paying for it.  While on the webpage look over the great raffle prizes.  Already I have received via PayPal notification 23 attending.  Last night Mike W6WZ said he had received 3 checks.

 

This is a social event like the picnic – no meeting business.  It is a great time to see each other and catchup on things.  Then of course the event’s program on DXpeditions by Dr. Robert Schmieder, KK6EK. 

 

The club’s next meeting is in January.

On 11/14/2013 2:45 PM, Jim Brown wrote:
With it, you can make a measurement in the shack, measure the line length with its TDR function, then transform the measurement to the antenna terminals.
Another measurement method that's even simpler, requiring no TDR and translation, is to do the VNA calibration with a 50 ohm cable long enough to reach from a point on the ground where the computer is, up the tower to the antenna. W6GJB and I have used it this way, with him on the tower and me on the ground. The impedance you're measuring is what's at the far end of that cable.
The AIM family of analyzers are popular, but the VNWA 3e is far more bang for the buck. See my Antenna Matching With Stubs Power Point for a rundown of these analyzers. http://k9yc.com/publish.htm

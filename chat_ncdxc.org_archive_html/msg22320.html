Truman,

I have the Butternut HF9V on a small lot with  limited space for radials. The antenna is near a corner of the lot due to space and view issues, so most of the radials are short. With this antenna, I’ve worked 205 entities in 16 months. This includes DXCC on 20, 30, and 40 meters, and getting close on 17 and 80 meters. A lot of these were FT8 contacts, but I’ve worked plenty of DX on CW and SSB with this antenna. 

The Butternut is a true 9-band antenna, and you can even work 60m by jumpering the 80m coil.  And you can fudge 160m, but the SWR is atrocious on 160 and it’s hard to get out beyond the Western  states (I did work Hawaii once). There is a 160m coil for the Butternut, but it is no longer manufactured. 

You’ll always do better on the higher bands with a beam, but the Butternut is terrific on the low bands and does surprisingly well on 20m. Even after I put up a beam (hopefully next year), the vertical will be great on 30 through 80
meters.

I would recommend the ground mount, as long as you can lay down at least 20 radials. They certainly don’t all have to be 60’ long. More radials is better, but there are diminishing returns after 40 or so. It’s important to take the time to tune the antenna on all bands; an antenna analyzer is very helpful for this. 

I researched other vertical antennas, and talked
to the elmers at DX Engineering, and I think I made the right choice. The SteppIR vertical would also be a good choice, but more expensive.

I still dream of a beam, but I have been extremely happy with my Butternut. Just don’t go over 800 watts on 80 meters. 

Feel free to email me if you have questions. 

73 and good DX,
Andy Calman K6ZP

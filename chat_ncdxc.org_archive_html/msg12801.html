Uploading "correct" QSO data after uploading "incorrect" QSO data simply
adds another QSO to LotW.  There is no "edit" in LotW.  There are also no
deletions, only uploading of additional QSOs.  But, once you get a correct
QSO uploaded (regardless of any uploaded incorrect QSOs preceding it), you
will get a QSL if your QSO partner has also uploaded a correct QSO.  The
rogue (incorrect) QSOs are just taking up storage space, but serve no
purpose.

Yes, Vlad won't do LotW because he disagrees with the policy that he cannot
edit or delete his own QSO data once it has been entered.  That's his choice
and our choice is to work him or not.  I agree that LotW is much, much less
work on both ends, not only to get the QSL but also the DXCC credit.  Like,
Vlad, I'm frustrated when I make a LotW upload mistake and cannot delete it,
but I don't boycott LotW because of that.  In fact, I love LotW as anyone
might after handling thousands of paper cards for years.

And, yes, Vlad wants $3 per card, but that covers multiple band-mode QSOs
with a given call sign.  And, it is only $1.50 for each additional call
sign.  He typically goes through several countries, about a week or so each,
on each trip, so when you QSL multiple entities afterward, it really isn't
that bad.  Using PayPal is easier, faster and less risky than sending a
request via postal mail.  In my case, I'm picking up lots of WARC band
credits so I don't mind.

He recommends against Bureau cards and direct mail to him, preferring a
simple PayPal transaction.  That's simpler for us, too.  No envelope to
address, cards to fill out, etc.

The extra QSL work for Vlad's operations is offset by more and more
DXpeditions that upload to LotW daily during their trip.  Sometimes I
receive new Challenge QSLs the same day I work the station.  Whatever.

It's a good question and a good point. I would much rather have it activated
than deleted. I also don't expect anything will happen quickly with its
status. I have compiled a "Visual History of the DXCC Program" here:

http://dxccsleuth.wordpress.com/2013/02/09/special-edition-the-visual-histor
y-of-the-arrl-dxcc-program/

But I do hope the ARRL and DXAC at least look into it. To answer your
question, I think if I were one or two away from Honor Roll - or one or two
away from HR #1 it would be more front and center in my mind than it is.
Kingman Reef hasn't been activated since 2000, and the clear trend since
then is that it has become "more awash" each year than the previous. In fact
- you can go to Flickr and look at photographs taken by people who visited
the reef since 2000 and actually see how it has eroded to its present state
which the US F&W says "mostly awash". Back in 2011, DX World ran a question
asking "Is Kingman Reef still above water". I did some research and found
that indeed it was - but that there was very little of it left. That
probably would have been the last best time to activate it. It's very
expensive to go there, and I would be shocked if the US F&W were to grant
access to anyone.

On another note - and all kidding aside (from yesterday). I think the DXAC
did a good job. 

They did what they were tasked to do. Through my research of the DXCC
program, the one thing that I find most curious is the group that always
wants to add new ones - and will try to bend the rules to do so, yet, block
any effort to delete something. I just made Honor Roll a little over a year
ago - and I would love to see more DX-ers reach that pinnacle, not less.
It's a matter of making the DXCC program something that is yes, not easy to
achieve, but also not so hard that people lose incentive. Wayne, N7NG has a
really great article on this topic:

http://www.dxuniversity.com/dxing/weeklydx.php?id=51

As I got closer to Honor Roll, I started doing research that led to that
info-graphic on the program. I was stuck at about 325 and had a "dark night
of the soul" and asked myself "Why in the world am I doing this? What in the
world is the point?" I felt like a masochist and it felt like torture (sorry
I'm being pretty dramatic there . . . ). I looked at the numbers of what it
took to make Honor Roll "way back when" and was thinking along the same
lines that Wayne was. I also realized that if I wanted to make it - I just
had to keep at it. And the only thing that was deleted were entities that I
already had (R1M and the PJ's). Several had been added since I started my
quest in July 2001 - Swains, South Sudan, Ducie, 4 new PJ's, Macedonia? 

Anyway - as you can see - the "lust" to add new one's made it harder and
harder as I got closer to HR. It really felt like a conspiracy (just
kidding). But I did see a strong bias in the program that seemed to favor
those that had "worked them all" vs. the "newer" DX-er trying really hard to
make it to the top. I'm not one to have anything "dumbed down", but I am
also not for discouraging new DX-er from joining us on the Honor Roll list.
And the HR is harder to make than ever. Wayne joked that since I made it in
11 1/2 years - maybe its "too easy?" . . . . 

Don't get me wrong - as long as the program exists - I will be chipping away
at the stone. No entity was Deleted that I did not already have on my way to
HR- so I never got about a "freebie". Deleting the one's that were Deleted
cost me a year or two in the chase, but I'm young enough where that didn't
matter. If I were a lot older, it would matter a lot more.

Could Kingman rise above? Maybe? Could Maria Teresa Reef rise above? Time
will tell.

73,

Rich
KY6R

Food for thought:    Why is there such a push to delete Kingman Reef today?
During the last operation there, they operated from a debris spit of broken
coral and shells.  That sounds like it was debris washed up on top of the
reef.   Last years storm could have washed that away.  Next years storm may
deposit it back, leaving more above the high tide line than before. Such is
the nature of sand bars and small islands.  Wouldn't it be better to have a
wait and see attitude? We could end up in a situation where it is deleted
and then have to add it again.   Now if there was an underwater landslide
and the island completely collapsed that would be a different story.  

Dave KA6BIM

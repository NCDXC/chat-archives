On Dec 18, 2014, at 2:19 PM, Dave Gomberg via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

I really wonder why the board does not refer more decisions of this sort to the members.  It would make it more OUR club, not THEIR club.

Dave,

The answer to your question is for members to volunteer for duties supporting the club and to submit their names for consideration for election by the membership to board and officer positions.

As you know, this is called a democracy and is the form of governance cooked into the bylaws of NCDXC and many other clubs.

Clubs fail when apathy sets in or those who volunteer for office are criticized via personal attacks and conclude that being a volunteer simply isn’t worth it.  When the volunteer pool dries up, clubs wither and often die.

The membership elected the BOARD to provide stewardship of the club - recognizing that few are willing to serve and those who do so, are willing to bear a burden most others will not.

Public, non personal criticism is fair game - for those who feel strongly enough about issues of governance, they can always choose to run for office come the next election cycle and reverse prior decisions if that is the consensus of the newly elected board.

But having a membership referendum on multiple issues is NOT the answer.  It places an undue burden on the board/officers since it defeats the purpose of their election or demonstrates that the board lacks the convictions of leadership which should have been the criteria for their election in the first place.

WE the MEMBERS elected the board to represent US.

It is OUR club because WE elected the board.

There is no THEIR there.
Stu K6TU


PS: I grumble (and sometimes whine loudly) about the decisions made by our Government.  But I respect that they were duly elected by (we) the people until replaced by different representatives we choose to elect.  If the will of the people elects mediocrity, we (the people) have only ourselves to blame.

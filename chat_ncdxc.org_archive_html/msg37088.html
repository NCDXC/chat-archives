On Wed, Oct 2, 2024 at 7:15 PM Paul N6PSE <pauln6pse@gmail.com> wrote:
These kinds of problems are very common when trying to do radio in the Third World.   

We have experienced similar issues in Iraq, South Sudan, Ethiopia and Eritrea among others. 

I suspect that they may have missed some subtle hints that some consideration needed to be paid. Call it an expedite fee for customs clearance or a license fee.  In Iraq, a Ministry we never heard of demanded $2200 in cash. We were able to negotiate that down to $50. 

 Even in the modern world, there are significant problems with customs and shipping agents.  From our trip recently to the Solomon Islands, our crooked shipping agent is trying to misappropriate a $12,000 Customs Bond that they collected from us.  It's a tug of war to get back what is ours. 

DXpeditioning in these places has hidden risks and costs. 

Paul N6PSE

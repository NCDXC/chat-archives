It was not long ago where I thought someone remoting into a contest station
was "cheating" in regards to DXCC. I now think that that is the farthest
thing from the truth. Why?

When I started DX-ing in 2001, I lived on the ridge right between Burton
Valley in Lafayette and Rossmoor (Walnut Creek). It was a ridge that ran
down from Las Trampas peak - which was a GREAT dx location. At one time I
had a 5 element 40M Bruce Array (a curtain of sorts) and it was just
amazing. I even just put up a 55' Force-12 LPT-1252 tower with Force -12
multiband yagi. I had a "propagational advantage" back then - over what I
have now!

 Then - following a divorce in 2005, I ended up in the "cheap" neighborhood
in Orinda - down in a horrible bowl - literally at the bottom of an old
volcano (Sibley Preserve - Glorietta and Moraga Way). . Great cycling
location, terrible DX location (except) it has NO HOA, covenant or
restrictions. My old QTH didn't either - I simply won't live in such
oppressive neighborhoods . . . 

This made me think, and concluded my thinking that there is NO competition
between one DXCC enthusiast and another. Absolutely NONE. There are just way
too many variables. Some can afford to live at the top of the hill - some
the bottom . . . 

There is no way - and never has been a way to "level the playing field".
Anyone who suggests this is dreaming. 

So - we are left competing against ourselves. It simply becomes our personal
quest, our "Holy Grail".

And being an IT Guy since 1981, the Remote technology - especially the way
Elecraft shows it - is totally "Advancing the Art of the Radio Hobby". I
might be jealous of someone who remotes in, but being the German that I am -
I want to do everything the tough way - from my own backyard. Not because
its easy - precisely because it's hard. That's my own personal problem - not
anyone elses . . . . 

I totally support the guys that create remote contest stations that they
remote into. Remoting into a NY State contest super station from Orinda to
bag FT5GA would be cheating, but would that person that did that cheapen my
experience? No - not in the least. If it's legal per the DXCC rules, then
fine. Not the way I choose to do it (although I missed FT5GA), but hey -
that's my problem and no one elses . . . 

Sorry - long rant.

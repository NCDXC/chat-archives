I'm very sad to hear the news of Jack Troster's passing.

 

I had the priviledge of knowing Jack personally for almost 40 years after I moved to San Francisco from the East Coast. In fact, Jack was one of the first NCCCers to welcome me to San Francisco. However, even before moving to the West Coast, I was an avid fan of the down-home, gentle wit in his writings in QST. Jack truly was the master of the ellipsis!

 

Jack was also one of the kindest people I ever met. He encouraged everyone lucky enough to cross his path.

 

As a graduate of Dartmouth College, he loved gently tweaking me about my own school back East. And he and Marguerite loved the San Francisco Opera. Jack knew that Rayma and I also love opera, and Jack would clip out the opera reviews from the San Francisco Chronicle and mail them to me, just in case I had managed to miss the review myself.

 

If there's one word that characterizes Jack Troster I would say it would be "Class." Jack was a class act and he'll be sorely missed.

 

RIP, dear friend,

Dean, N6BV

As impressive as were all his radio achievements, even more so was Jack himself.  He was the consummate gentleman.  He was warm and welcoming to everyone he met, and many amateur radio operators today enjoy the hobby as a direct result of his nurturing, encouragement and enthusiasm.  Jack was known for his quick wit and encyclopedic knowledge of amateur radio history.  His passing leaves a huge void.  Amateur radio has lost one of its giants.

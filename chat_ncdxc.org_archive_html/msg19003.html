In my opinion, ARRL should wise up and take the lead of the CQ 160 m contest, with the entirely reasonable rule of allowing a remote receiver within a reasonable distance (100 km, I believe).   Otherwise, city dwellers in noisy environments will eventually give up on DXCC... or they'll just ignore the out-of-touch ARRL rule.  

This doesn't mean that people will not prefer to work stations with their home RX if they can.  They will still try to put up low-noise receive antennas and otherwise mitigate their noise.  

But it does mean that they will more likely stay active as amateur radio operators, rather than getting so discouraged that give up and find another hobby/passion.

I speak from personal experience... From 1998-2010, I worked over 250 from my home station and DXCC on many bands.  Then the world became a noisier place and it became (and still is) nearly impossible to work anything but the loudest station.  My log got sparser and sparser.

I should add that it *does* make perfect sense to have a "reasonable distance" limit... otherwise, one could work DL on 160m with 100 milliwatts to a dipole in the basement by connecting to a remote receiver in Germany, while he does the same to one in California... that is clearly not in the spirit of DX'ing.

If allowing remote antennas offends the purists, then have a different flavor of the awards (this is analogous to the fact that if you use a remote RX in the 160m contest, you are automatically in the "assisted" category.)

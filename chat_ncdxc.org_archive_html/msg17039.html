The way I look at it, we are all just grown men (and Women) playing with radios and having fun, experiencing the "magic".

I don't begrudge anyone if they want to participate in nets, list operations, FT8, Remotely located HF stations or any other form of two way communications. 

Actually, I do take a dim view of HamSphere, CQ100 and other VOIP programs where no radio is involved in any fashion. 

But to each his own however they want to enjoy radio is fine by me. 

Paul N6PSE


On Wed, Apr 24, 2019 at 8:01 AM AL_N6TA via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

So, I’m tuning 40 SSB to get a feel o f the band and at 0250Z. I come along to the net control of the ‘Danie Net’.  What’s that, you ask?  Well, Danie, ZS3D,  and some NA folk have set things up to make it easy to work South Africa.  Get on the list and work Danie.  Get on the list?  Sure.  Go to 7156List.com and fill in your info.

http://7156list.com/

With Bill, ZS6CCY and several others active on 40 SSB in our morning, I thought it was already easy to make a contact with ZS.

Back when I was a new little pistol, I used the Afrikaner net on 21.355 MHz with Cal, W2PPG and Herbie, WA6BJS to make several nice ATNOs.  All have been worked many times since but it was a bonanza for me at the time.  There were others like W6PHO on 14.222 MHz and getting a QSO was just dropping in your call when the control asked if anyone needed a contact.  I have not favored these types of operations since.

 

Do you think that  this more automated list type of operation is needed?  Is this DXing?

 

To each his own!

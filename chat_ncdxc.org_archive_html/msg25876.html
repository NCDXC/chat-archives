I was saddened to wake up to this morning's news that Franz had passed away.
 
I first met him in Yangon, Myanmar, where we joined Zorro and others and travelled inland for the 2013 XZ1J DXpedition. Myanmar had not been active for quite some time and I marvelled at the way in which Franz handled the SSB pileups. To say he was masterful would be an understatement.  Franz was a quiet man. He did not express his views or opinions but when it was time to erect antennas or work the pileups, he brought his "A" game 100%. A few years later, we met again with Zorro and the team for the E30FB Eritrea 2015 DXpedition. We enjoyed that time together and became friends. Franz published many books and occasionally, he would send me one of his books with a nice personal note in it. Just a few days ago, I received a AP2TN QSL card from Franz with a personal note as he was the QSL Manager. 
 
Franz was a very fine gentleman, like Zorro, he was willing to help others and to make a difference. One aspect of this hobby that I dislike is that we all too frequently have to say goodbye and Rest in Peace to fine gentlemen such as Franz and Zorro. 
 
You can see Franz working the XZ1J pileups here:   https://vimeo.com/manage/videos/86383125
 
Paul N6PSE

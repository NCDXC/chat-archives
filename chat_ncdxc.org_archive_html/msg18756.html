I agree with Rick about the documentation.  I’m in the middle of building the DB36 with the 80 Meter dipole and 6 Meter elements option.  

DO check every part against all of the parts lists.  I actually used a Sharpie to mark the part number on ‘each’ and every part.  Then verify the part number with the description of the part in the text.  Dry fit to make sure that it is correct.  Take your time and read and understand the manual.  Then reread the manual a couple more times to make sure that YOU understand what needs to be done and why.

I also went with the upgraded controller.  When adding most of the accessories to the standard controller, the cost is only a bit more since the 2000 has those built in.

I am going through the DB36 manuals making a lot of corrections that will be sent to Roee at SteppIR.  He said that he will send a corrected manual when they rewrite the manuals this Spring.

Good luck choosing the antenna of your dreams...

Historically, SteppIR goes dark in April and May.  There
may be a sneak preview of Dayton at Visalia.  If you
wait in order to get the convention price, what happens
is that they collect convention orders and then make
a big manufacturing run after they get back.  Nothing
ships for months after Dayton.  I don't know how they
prioritize shipments after that.

40 meters is a crucial band.  The DB-18 is well worth
the extra money to get a unidirectional pattern.

SteppIR's have many desirable features, but they are
not easy to construct and the documentation isn't great.
Don't be in a hurry. Double or triple check everything
ON THE GROUND.  Point the completed antenna straight
up with the reflector on saw horses and check VSWR on each band

BTW, I am the owner of 3 SteppIR antennas.

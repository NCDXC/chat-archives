Craig, I could not even sell my LPF’s at the local flea market.  Here’s my take on it.  Any modern day ham equipment has to meet FCC Class A or B, and CE regulations.  Those determine harmonic content and level above 30MHz.  By design and test, and in order to sell the equipment needs to meet this.  I used to have lpfs installed in the days of TV’s not connected to cable, where they could be overloaded but today with satellite and cable, I don’t see those problems anymore.  More likely the TVs interfere with our radio receivers.  But clearly should RFI problems occur then a number of solutions may be needed from grounding, bonding, common mode filters and LPFs;  whatever works.

 

73s, Paul, W6IBU

I have always had a low pass filter on each of my stations. Having sold my second station, I decided to sell my Bencher YA-1 which is the top rated 5 KW filter for which I paid $150 plus CA tax.

 

When doing some research on eBay, I was surprised to see that few of any brand were selling for much over $20.  I have one posted now at $50, and no one is even watching it.

 

Even with a well tuned rig or amp, you can still get spurs that cause RFI. Back in the day, if the FCC received a formal interference complaint, (I got two for TVI) the first question they would ask you was if you were using a filter.

 

My question is…don’t most hams still use a LP filter between their rig/amp and the antenna?

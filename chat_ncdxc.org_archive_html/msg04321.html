Congrats back at ya! 

Doing the 'DX chores' like sitting around for the paint to dry is one way that you get the contact while others complain and wring their hands.  That must have felt awesome to just have him pop up!

When I heard him, some other DX just started calling CQ right on top.  Down in strength but worried the heck out of me that it would kill my chance.  I was AMAZED that there was not one single cop trying to deal with it.  No nothing to get in the way. I was also surprised to hear the op responding to a partial call he was trying pick out of the pile:  "The three Norway.  Please make your call".  Seemed a bit 'novice' to me.  Did it several times.

 

Anyway, fun to try and more fun to succeed, huh?

Congrats!  I heard your QSO.  I enjoy reading stories like yours.

This is my story.  Earlier today I was in two pileups however I wasn't able to break either one.  I heard two Denver guys whom I knew work him back to back.  I zero-beated however so did everyone else.  Later another local DX friend said the DX did come back to me while the loud recorded music was also playing on top of the DX.  I never heard my call possibly due to the music.  I was bummed.

This evening I predicted that he'll be on 20m looking for NA.  I had my VFO parked on 14.160.  I emailed my friend telling him that waiting for VU7 to come on is like watching paint dry.  I had my beam and amp. all ready to go for VU7.  I was doing some computer work when all of a sudden someone just picked up the mic. and started calling CQ without even checking if the freq. is clear.  It was VU7AG.  I called and he came right back to me.  Immediately he recognized me from the eham DX forum and we chatted for a few minutes on simplex.  Krish was very excited to work me and he wanted a true signal report.  DX'ing is like a roller coaster, one moment you are on the top of the hill, the next moment you could be stuck in the slum!

Have fun and GL!

On Wednesday, November 20, 2013 10:50 PM, Six2Go <six2go@comcast.net> wrote:
With encouraging words from Steve, Ken, Garry, Jonathon and Rich, I drove home wondering if it would be too late for an opening to VU7, assuming he would be on.  Rich noted his QSOs on 40 M at 0130 in January 2007 so I thought I had a chance, especially seeing spots on 20 M a few hours earlier. 
 
In thru the door and straight to the shack.  Went to 20 M and heard a pileup at the low end of the phone band.  DX was on 14.150 and nice copy.  Only problem was that it was XR0ZR.  Rats.  My beam was pointing at 80 deg from a 30 M attempt at something last night.
I tuned up the band and heard another commotion.  A little listening and I heard someone at 14.160 saying 'Spread out'.  Did not take long to figure out who it was: VU7AG, back on since going QRT earlier in the afternoon.  On with the amp for that never ending filament warm-up.  When the light stopped flashing, I made a call where I thought I had a chance and that is when I got my early present!  One call and in the log for an ATNO!  I let out a shout and gave a fright to the XYL!
 
I will never get tired of that feeling!
 
So, you need not worry about me clogging up the pileup for this one!

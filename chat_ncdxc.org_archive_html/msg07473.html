On Sep 5, 2014, at 9:59 PM, Garry Shapiro via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Dennis,

I have an EU2000i and have not used the Generac competition, but I do have an opinion about generators.

IIRC, Generac offers a vertically differentiated line of generators, with each quality level associated with a paint color---green, red, yellow, blue. Some applications are uncritical about waveshape quality or noise level while others are critical about such factors. A building contractor wants to run Skil saws, table saws, cement mixers, routers and other power tools, heaters, etc. and is likely to treat generators roughly on a job site, maybe pushing/overloading them. (Ever rent a beaten-up generator from a tool outfit?) When the genny burns out he junks it. Such a buyer wants a low replacement price and is not picky about the waveform, its frequency or its harmonic content. He might pick a cheaper Generac. I suspect most Generacs  purchased are of the basic entry class.

OTOH, generators chosen to run telecom equipment usually have near-sinusoidal waveforms from an inverter, good frequency stability, etc. For a Field Day or CQP, quiet operation may be sought. For these attributes, Japanese generators with their high-quality small engines dominate that market segment---not just the ubiquitous Hondas that are top dogs but Suzukis and other JA brands.

The EU2000i is popular with serious hams due to its waveform quality and stability, quiet operation, light weight and the ability to be paralleled with a second unit. That is why our group buys have been Hondas. I would not bring my Honda to a construction site!

If both price and quality are important, consider one of Honda's JA competitors, likely to be priced below Honda. Remember that you get what you pay for---or less.

As for DXE's specific offering, figure out where in Generac's lineup it is.

Garry, NI6T

Hello David,

I'm ex WA2QHN from Newton, NJ, but my family moved to Northern California when I was in college in PA and forgot to tell me!

The PJ split meant they became Deleted as they used to be and new entities moving forward. So old QSOs before the split don't count for DXCC  current list but do add to your Deleted count, which adds to your overall count. The ARRL DXCC section on the web site has a list with hieroglyphic like footnotes that give the dates when the changes took place.

I have the same hill and noise issues over the hill in Orinda.

I use phased Wellbrook ALA1530LNP loops through a DX Engineering NCC-2 for RX on Top Band. Amazing noise cancellation, but not a cheap solution!

I'm going to replace my Inverted L with an 80M phased vertical array using Spiderbeam 60' fiberglass poles with wire inside and the DXE DV-80-P. I have the DV-40P and love it.

So I'm going to cash in my 160M chips for a band where there's a lot more DX to work from the West Coast.

I also have a small lot, so pretty much have to take one antenna down to put another up.

73,

Rich

On Feb 25, 2018 8:13 PM, David Jaffe via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
Hi folks.  I just joined this list.  I've got a rather trivial question, and wanted to respond to Rich's comment, but first I thought I'd introduce myself. . .  

I'm David--K6DAJ (aka "Dave" on CW/RTTY) in Berkeley, formerly WA2BHJ/WN2BHJ in N.J. in the 1960s.

I've got a relatively modest station on a small 100' x 40' lot (KT34A tri-bander on the roof at only about 30 feet due to neighbor issues, a recently erected HF2V for 80/40, and a very low half-sloper wire for attempts at 160, the WARC bands and 6).  I'm in the hills with downhill facing SW, which means most of the DX is straight into the hill. I have bad noise problems, especially the last few years and have recently gotten a Wellbrook magnetic loop and a rotator to see if that helps, but have just started experimenting with it.

I was licensed in 1967 at age 12 and was an avid ham for 3 years, esp. CW, then went totally inactive. In 1998 I got back into it, doing a lot of DXing and some contesting; also did CW in my car.  I was very active from 1998-2006, then slowed down to a trickle, especially the last few years, due to local noise and competing priorities.  But this year, I've gotten the bug again. I put up the 80m antenna, am trying for 80m DXCC and have done a few contests. 

Which brings me to Rich's comment about awards. I got DXCC around 1969 and was at around 160 entities. Since then, I've worked/confirmed all but 15, but never had the time or inclination to send the cards in. But I do keep lists of what I've worked and confirmed on which bands and such.  Lately, 80 has been my focus and FT8 has helped, esp. given my increased noise situation here.  (160 is out of the question for me.... even hitting the east coast is hard.)

But I'm also an avid birdwatcher, and birdwatchers keep lists as well.  They keep lists of birds seen in their life, a particular year, particular state, particular county, etc.  And just like DXCC, species are split, are merged, etc.  It drives bird watchers nuts!  One guy I met was working on seeing 100 species in every county of California (what is it with the number 100?)   But the interesting thing is that birds don't QSL. It's *ALL* the honor system. And people are honest.  Because if you're not, you're just cheating yourself.  So that's pretty much my feeling about DXing.  

That being said, it's nice to get QSLs and I've just recently been dragged kicking and screaming to start using LOTW and have managed to confirm a lot of QSOs quickly and easily, except for the fact that--except for contests--I am still logging on paper... another thing I probably should change if I want to be a card-carrying member of the 21st century. 


OK, so finally, in spite of all the above, I have a trivial "what counts?" type of question about DXCC so I can answer questions like "how many have you worked?" as opposed to "how many have you not worked?"   I'm sure this was covered on this list or elsewhere on the Internet, but I couldn't locate it...    

My question is (drum roll)...   What's the story with PJ2-4-9 and PJ5-8 versus the new entities?  If I have QSOs before the split, do they still count as entities?  And, in addition, the split entities count (if after the split)?  So, that means that (e.g.) Curacao counts twice??  Or do I lose the old PJ2,4,9 band slots and have to work them again after the split?  Finally, exactly how many new entities are there and what are they? 

Thanks & 73,

K6DAJ

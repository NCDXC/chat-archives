Over the years in business, I found the most effective way to influence organizational change was to work with those who had the most authority to make decisions --- preferably at the top of the organization.

Here's your target audience:  Daniel Ashe, USFWS Director.

http://www.fws.gov/offices/biodanielash.html

You'll have to find a way to convince Daniel to support our need to activate those rare US Islands we all covet.  

But your winning arguments will have little to do with our hobby and more to do with helping him in his role.  He will resonate with terms like:

science-driven business model
landscape conservation
sanctuaries
resource management
wildlife refuge
endangered species
biodiversity conservation
coastal resource protection
habitats
policy and program support
Code of Scientific Conduct
migratory patterns
wetlands
forward vision

If Daniel happened to be traveling through the Bay Area this year, and by chance could attend our monthly NCDXC meeting -- what would we say to him about DXCC, US Islands, the Honor Roll, Rare Entities, DXpeditions, Awards, etc. --- that would "win him over" ?

Relating our language to his language is a daunting task indeed.

73,
John K6MM

On Jan 7, 2013, at 7:50 AM, <paul@n6pse.com> <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:

Here is your NCDXC Question of the Week (QoTW)
 
If you look at the latest most wanted DXCC list, you will see that US Islands managed by the US Fish & Wildlife Service are rapidly rising to the top of the list.
 
For some time now, the US F&W Service has made it virtually impossible for DXpeditions to activate these remote and rare islands.
 
Question: Should hams actively lobby their politicians and government representatives to open access to these islands?
 
Should organizations such as NCDXF/INDEXA expand their role to include lobbying on behalf of the DX Community?
 
 
What are your thoughts?

What I have done, rightly or not, is to double click on the DX CQ, which gives me their grid.  [Or click on their exchange since I don't seek grids, even on 6M.]  I then click on the button for the report, then enable the transmit (for the next cycle).  That initiates the call, with the report, saving 15/30 seconds (FT4/FT8). 

At all times, my call is from the home station (even if I'm not).  When on the road as portable, I sign and log that way (and report my current grid).  This means that anyone can look up my grid when it's my straight call, if they want/need it; if it's not from home, I'll show it.

It seems the simplest means to get the exchange and to allow their Q rate to rise.

As we're talking etiquette, I'll add it's NEVER proper to call on their tone (a pet peeve); always offset by at least 30 Hz (half 'their bandwidth').  If everyone called on their tones, fewer would get through (JT modes are amazing but why test the limits?).

Rick NK7I

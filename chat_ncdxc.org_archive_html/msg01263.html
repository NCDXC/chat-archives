Craig makes some pretty good points about the Kenwood TS-590 vs the Elecraft K3. The TS-590 in my opinion is the "best bang for the buck" currently within medium sized & priced radios. While I still prefer the K3's (I own two of them) I used the TS-590 at 7O6T. It was a good rig but my audio preference is for the K3. The TS-590 has a harsh digital sound whereas I like the warm analog sound of the K3.
 
For DXpedition purposes, more and more DXpeditions will be giving the TS-590 a serious look because not only is it a performer, but with the cost significantly less than the K3 it is a really attractive option for a DXpedition looking to bring 5-7 identical radios. I'm not in the market for a new radio, but if I was, I would have to give the TS-590 serious consideration.
 
Paul N6PSE

On 2/11/2013 1:38 PM, Craig-AE6RR wrote:
If you compare a Kenwood TS-590 with an equivalent K3 (spec’d out below) you are looking at $1,529.95

That's an BMW and Honda comparison. To approach the quality and performance of the K3 you've got to be talking about a TS7800 or FT5000, which are much more expensive.
 
[Craig] I am not sure if I would equate the K3 to BWM as they are very expensive to maintain, besides it should be an American car vs. Japanese J
The K3 is #4 on the http://www.sherweng.com/table.html list write behind the FTdx-5000D.
The TS-590S is #11 and the IC-7800 is #21.
Check out the QST review of the TS-590S in the May 2011 issue (available on line), the 20KHz Blocking Gain Compression is 141 dB vs. the K3 at 139 dB (who cares about 2 dB at these numbers though).

But I was suggesting a KX3, tricked out with the yet to be delivered 100W amp and tuner would likely come in around the price of theTS590, the KX3 is a vastly superior radio, and has many advantages of form factor and versatility.
 
[Craig] I agree on performance, SDRs are the wave of the future.  However, a small portable radio may not be everyone’s idea of the best rig for the shack.

Besides -- if you want to consider the K3, anyone with even the slightest of assembly skills can buy the K3 as a kit  for a saving of about 15%.  You've got stuff on your bill of materials that are not needed, like the mic and KBPF.  Virtually any decent mic works with a K3 or KX3, and the KBPF makes it a general coverage RX.  And the roofing filters you put their are extra options that are most likely not on the Kenwood. These are things a serious contester needs, but that are not all that necessary for a DXer.
 
[Craig] I was trying to compare apples with apples as the TS-590S only comes factory assembled, comes with a microphone in the box and is a general coverage receiver.  It’s true that everyone may not want these items.  The TS-590S has the roofing filters built in, nothing extra to buy.
 
Another "by the way" -- both the K3 and KX3 can decode CW, RTTY, and PSK31 directly on their display and send RTTY and PSK from a paddle or from a memory. Think about the convenience of that when you're trying to quickly work a DXpedition on RTTY!  Program one memory with your call, another with 599TU, and  you're ready to rol!  During WPX RTTY this weekend, I found that my K3 consistently decodes stuff that MMTTY with a very good sound card misses, and W6GJB tells me that the decoders in the KX3 are even better.
 
[Craig] Yes, the decoding in the K3 is nice, I used mine in the WPX RTTY also this last weekend and I found the dual band pass filtering and the RTTY tuning indicator to be particularly useful.  I still want to use MMTTY though as the display scrolls by too fast in the limited space on the K3’s LCD.
 
This price for the TS-990 has been announced, HRO is offering it a $7999 which is way too high for my taste.  If I was going to spend that much, I too would opt for the Flex 6000.  SDR radios are going to make all of the current high end radios obsolete soon.  How about 8 receivers running at the same time, you could monitor every frequency that a DXpedition is on at the same time and see which one has the best SNR.  The software could also track the frequencies of the stations being worked and predict a pattern, it could probably also automatically place your call for you at the right frequency at the right time.  But what fun would that be?
 
So will the K4 be an SDR????

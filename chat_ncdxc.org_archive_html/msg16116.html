A few days ago, wire antennas were mentioned in the vein that they were 'unworthy'.


When I lived in CA (land of the never ending HOA and mommy-may-I), I used a double extended Zepp (340' dipole) hung too low (in stealth).  That wire was used on all bands and earned me over 240 countries.


Now that I live in North Idaho, still waiting to get a contractor to build the tower foundation, I'm using an Inverted L (cut for 160M) and an 80M dipole.  Both are set up field day fashion (no real counterpoise, not the best of locations).  I have an R7 but the noise (too close to the house) makes it mostly unusable.  So the next 50 or so new countries I've worked from here, on all bands, were also on wire antennas; some with pretty stinky native matches (6:1) with a tuner to keep the finals happy.  All with 600 watts or less (I haven't put the new KPA1500 online yet).

And don't forget that 6M offers some openings too (fast, rare but great fun).  Tiny antennas and beams are simple to build, of wire.  I worked a bunch, including some DX on a simple 6M loop antenna, at 10'.


The point is; do not sell yourself short or belittle the common wire.  It may demand more chair time, a closer attention with the ear but it's certainly possible to work plenty of stations, DX included, on the simple wire.


Case in point, the recent VP6D Ducie effort here (5100 miles away).

CW 160-12M
FT8 160-15M
SSB 40/20/27/15
RTTY 20/17


Total, 22 contacts.  ALL those were done on the above L and 80M dipole; not ideal antennas at all; but I did what I could.  I'm too far north for 10M propagation long enough to be successful with this team.  This isn't bragging, it's showing that marginal stations can have moderate or better success.


One more thing that running wires does (besides teach humility) is that you'll learn and appreciate more of the nuances of propagation.


Yes, a large beam is easier, focus your energies in one direction and I look forward to the SteppIR DB-36 being in the air (AND using the KPA1500).  But, if all you can muster is wire, even with a crummy match; go for it.  Even with the lousy propagation we have now at the start of the new cycle, you can do well.  Honestly, finding lower noise floors are a bigger issue (yours AND that of the DX stations).

Oh, Zimbabwe was added this week for me; ATNO, and on 17M CW.  On a wire antenna.  How cool is that?


Rick wa6nhc

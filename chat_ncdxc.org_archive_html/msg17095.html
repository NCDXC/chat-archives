On 5/3/2019 7:02 AM, Bill Frantz via Chat wrote:
I'm quite happy with my Yaesu FT-60, which is, of course, no longer in new production.
I, too, have been very happy with my FT-60 for EmComm work. Built like a tank, and easy to program from the keyboard. It doesn't have dual receive, but it can operate at it's full 5 watts on AA batteries. I don't know about the production status, but it is still readily available (I just checked HRO and Amazon).
The only other major lack is some kind of battery status indicator. The radio will show you the battery voltage when you turn it on, but there are no warnings in continuous operation until it refuses to produce full output during transmit.
What I've done is to set the "P1" key to display the voltage (I don't remember the settings option, off hand -- check the manual). To see the voltage, press "F/W" followed by "P1" (above the "7" key). Not as convenient as glancing at the display, but better than power-cycling the rig. 

-Don, KE6DM

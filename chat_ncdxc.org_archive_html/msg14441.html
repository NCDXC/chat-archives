On Feb 8, 2018, at 12:09 PM, Rich Holoch via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

I just completed an interesting experiment on 40M.

I have been using the DXE DV-40P with 2 phased 33' verts, spaced 35' and with 44 interconnected buried radials. It's been awesome.

I also had a 2 element 20M yagi up 45', also excellent.

I took down the yagi to test a Cushcraft D40. I wanted to A - B test the dipole vs. phased vertical array. I use ZS6CCY, Bill as my "beacon".

At night on the SP to ZS, the vertical array is 1 - 3 S Units better. On the morning LP, the D40 dipole is better.

This leads me to the SteppIR UrbanBeam, which would be perfect. Because it's pricey, I needed to see if a rotatable dipole was better on 40 than the array.

So, I'm thinking of selling the D40 and purchasing the UrbanBeam.

What do you think? Know anyone who has one? I saw it at Dayton last year and love it's "footprint"

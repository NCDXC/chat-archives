I've learned to view DX contests from the west coast as a non-competitive exercise because of the lopsided scoring rules that strongly favor stations in the Atlantic basin. When I do enter them, it's with another objective -- lately I've been collecting QRP band-countries.
This time around, I had planned to combine QRP with building my countries count on 10M at full power. I started out on 10M at full power, and things were going so well that I never turned off the amp! The JAs never stopped on Friday evening, until the band closed around dinner time, and after dinner I went down to 80M to see how the new wire Yagi was working. I worked what I could hear, which was not much, saw a 40M spot for 3V8BB and worked him, and went to bed.
That let me hit 10M at 6 am, to discover the band open to EU on a skew path over AF, and it stayed open to EU for quite a while. By dinnertime Saturday, I had > 500 Qs and 95 countries in the log on 10M. After dinner, I hit 20M looking for the opening to northern EU that often occurs then. After a few S&P Qs, I settled on 14032 to run, andby the time the band closed, had about 160 Qs and about 55 countries in the log!
Sunday morning was a completely different story -- 10M was not nearly as good, but 15 was decent, and I was able to both S&P and run effectively into EU. Beginning around 6 am, I worked 80 and 40 for a half hour or so, enough to grab the available AS mults on both bands, then headed to 15M. Also unusual, at least for me, was that I was able to work EU into the late afternoon on 20M -- signals were weak, and it was mostly S&P, but they were coming back to me.
The final result was 7 new countries on 10M including 3V8, 3V8 on 15 and 40, also new band countries, and TO7CC on 80M. And in W6, that's all a DX contest is good for. :) 

73, Jim K9YC

A reasonable signal (QSB but peaks nicely), working US calls and EU up 1-2 BUT that spectrum is NOT within the US allocations.  It falls in between our channels 2 and 3.

60M doesn't count for DXCC but there are a LOT of Extra class calls being worked that aren't within the law; at least the way I'm reading it.

Their band plan states they'll listen on channel 3 (5357) which is heavily used for FT8 (meaning they won't likely listen or hear).

The following is CHANNEL CENTER and the US is allowed +/- 1.4 KHz from these frequencies (make room for bandwidth too, ~100-150 Hz for CW).

(You can go no lower than 5357.1 for CW on channel 3; channel 2 highest is 5349.3 for CW, below j38r.)

Do the math, it ain't worth the risk.

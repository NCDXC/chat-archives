MS has been working on solving the Plug-N-Play features for a very long time now.  It's better, but far from ideal for any application connection.  Opening an app that wants to connect to a device that was last in use by another app, and not plugging in a new audio device, is a hard one.  The choice seems to have been to attempt to have Plug-N-Play Devices work as smoothly as possible, and keep the last used app connection to a device as the default.  

I think this is why FlexRadio's DAX works pretty reliably when several different apps connect to several different Audio Devices on both Win 10 Pro and Win 11 Pro.  I haven't had to clean out extra audio devices or connections in a while. 

Supporting free apps is no small (or free) feat.

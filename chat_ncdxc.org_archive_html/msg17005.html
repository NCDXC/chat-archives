Congratulations to the NCDXC's convention committee and their volunteers for another wonderful event.

I attended my first DX convention in 1978 at the then Holiday Inn, Visalia and at least one in Fresno. The move to the Marriott was a breath of fresh air, moving back to the former Holiday Inn is a huge disappointment.

We especially appreciated the convention committee allocating the South Orkney Island DX-pedition a fundraising table. We exceeded our goal thanks to the generosity of those who stopped at the table and took a chance on winning a radio, but we still have a long hill to climb. The winner of the Icom 7300 was Rick WA7BNG, from Loveland, Colorado.

Having now been the treasurer for 4 major DX-peditions there's no doubt it's the foundations, clubs and individual DXers that make these projects possible. Project costs aren't going down anytime soon, and there's a limit to what the team members can or will pay to give you an ATNO, band fills or just the thrill of busting the pile-up.  We made the first non refundable payment to the Braveheart last week, and are now in the process of sourcing the cold weather tents needed for the project.

Thanks again for another great Visalia weekend, we hope to hear you all in the pileup from VP8/O.

73,
Gene K5GS

My only regret was selling my IC-756 Pro II to buy an IC-756 Pro III. 

The Pro 2 was like a rock and the Pro 3 kept blowing driver transistors.   

Once it was fixed for the last time, I sold it and bought my Elecraft K3, which has turned out to be the best rig I’ve ever had.  

If this is the extent of my rig regret, I think I’m doing pretty well!

Aloha from Maui!!

> How about Rig regrets?   I have a few.   I bought the first version of the Yaesu FT100 mobile VHF/UHF/HF radio. This is the rig that aspired to be the later FT857D or the FT-891 of today.

> This rig was awful. It was very deaf and just very poor quality. I used it for about six months mobile and then tried to off load it but the word was out that these were very poor radios and I still have it to this day. I thought of giving it to a kid but what is the point with such a poor radio?

> I once bought a used K3 and found it to be absolutely deaf. I sent it to Elecraft and they determined that all of the filters were in the opposite sequence of how they should be. Elecraft fixed the radio and I have taken that trusty K3 on many DXpeditions. No regrets there.

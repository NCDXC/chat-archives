That's exactly in line with their activity last night.  When they were
'done' working EU for 7 straight hours (still ignoring NA ops on 80M the
entire time), they went to 40M and did exactly the same thing with EU,
only allowing a few VK and seeking JA (beFORE the band was really open
to Asia, still daylight there).

The only (4-5) US they worked on 40, were in the same path as EU (read
that as deep south and east coast).  It was not for lack of trying from
the US (I saw several signals) and with the signal levels TI9A were
presenting; I simply don't believe the US was not heard.  It wasn't
their lack of listening ability, they worked the arctic EU long into
daylight there, some were clearly struggles. I gave up after 1:30 am.

I'm glad they're not ATNO for me, but they're the leader on my personal
'Worst DXpedition of the year" so far (and it's not sour grapes), this
kind of blatant bias has no place in ham radio. Work what is presented
by propagation to meet your goals BUT be fair to all (it wasn't just NA
they ignored, no room was left for anything in AF, SA as well).  I know
from my previous contacts in that region that I can present plenty of
signal, yet they clearly work NA only when nothing else is heard.

I'm glad you got through at last Al,  I'll check on them now and then
but they're not worth losing more sleep for me.

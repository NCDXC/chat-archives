On Tue, Jun 28, 2016 at 2:44 PM, Paul N6PSE <pauln6pse@gmail.com> wrote:
Sometimes an operators rate may be slow due to shifting propagation or other reasons. If you want the VP8SGI video, there is a scene where I am operating SSB with my GoPro camera strapped to my head. I was trying to
catch a shot of the tent collapsing in on us which really startled us when that happened. My rate was pretty low when a big gust of wind hit the outter wall of the tent. The impact was so hard
that my table and I was pushed backwards and I flipped over with the table and the radio gear coming down on top of me. Needless to say my rate was really impacted and my pileup was
probably wondering what was going on. 

At other times, antennas were blowing over and it would be necessary to quickly assemble a team of 3-4 guys to go make a quick repair and put it back up. So slow rate is not always related to the Operator's ability
to manage his pileup or propagation. 

Paul N6PSE

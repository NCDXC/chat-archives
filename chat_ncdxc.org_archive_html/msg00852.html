Here is your NCDXC Question of the Week (QoTW)
We all know that there are some DXCC entities that have not been activated for a number of years. Some entities appear to be impossible to activate for various reasons. Question: Should the ARRL consider de-listing any entity that has not been activated for a prescribed number of years, perhaps 15 years of no activity? Would this enable some to reach the Honor Roll, or would this be a step backwards and make it too easy to reach HR? 
 What are your thoughts?   HNY!   Paul N6PSE


Consider the following strange scenario:
Suppose I get myself appointed the new FCC honcho of a new country (maybe a split of an existing country, maybe I buy my way into the office). This country has UN and ITU recognition and a prefix, maybe even a commercial HF station. And my amateur license policy is: no licenses, ever, to anyone. If that would keep me off the ARRL list, imagine it is only a de facto policy, not an announced one. 

Now NO ONE is #1 HR and no one ever will be.   How silly is that?
#1 HR should be a distinguished award, but not something that no one can ever achieve. 

Some sanity needs to be applied to the process.   Comments please?

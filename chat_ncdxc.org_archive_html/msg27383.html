Don’t expect LOGIC from ARRL. Their rules are totally arbitrary, and as you note, at times illogical or contradictory. *I* can’t understand why they distinguish a QSO from a boat MOORED and tied to a dock on a rare island from a person sitting at a table on that same dock. The former is “Maritime Mobile” and the latter is a “real” QSO. Why?

That’s why I completely ignore the ARRL when considering what entities I have worked. For example, I “counted” Kosovo long before ARRL “recognized” them. I’m sure the thousands (millions?) of Kosovars “recognized” who they were, and where they lived! The fun part is working difficult, rare, and even not-so-rare DX locations around the world. Once you realize that the “official” award programs are simply a GAME with totally arbitrary rules, you can choose to opt out of the ARRL’s game rules, and apply your own (or join a club with its own contests and rules, in a private “game”.) There’s no “law” regarding DX, it’s a hobby, and you can play it however you want.

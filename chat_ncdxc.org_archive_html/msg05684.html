Please note that I said “for the low bands”.

 

They will be on a very sandy island with unobstructed beach heads all around the island. They will have the same problem on 30 and 40M that I do here – since they won’t be able to get a horizontal antenna up ½ wavelength, phased verticals are the way to go.

 

I don’t understand all of the talk of why phased verticals right at the (salt) waters edge on ANY band is a bad thing. Maybe because several DX-peditions did not set them up correctly?

 

VU7AG used them – and they did it right. I won’t name the two or three DX-peditions that did not set them up correctly. TX5K was almost all phased verticals – and they made 113K Q’s.

 

Team Vertical proved that they work – and their DX-peditions and contest results are legendary.

 

The guys at Tromelin are using 4 squares on 30 and 40M. If they set them up right – they will be superb.

Phased verticals near the water for the low bands are the _only_ option. In
fact, I am glad this is what they will have on 40 and 30M (4 squares
especially!). Since I need it for an ATNO - I would be more than happy to
work them on one band and one mode - during our morning grey line. CW and
SSB would be better, but with the competition we will have from both EU and
East Coast - one Q is all I want. I had 2 bands and modes with TO4E, but
that was when I only wanted 1 Q, 2 modes and an "insurance band" since there
was no log updates during the DX-pedition.

Funny how this has changed since only 2001. . . . (damned DXCC Challenge and
the Marathons!) . . . I've got to wean myself off the DXCC Challenge . . .
hi hi

20M is the money band - and I would bet they could even have two 20M
stations running and still not satisfy the demand for this one for the short
time that they will be there. They could have one 20M mode with antennas on
the NW side of the island and the other on the SE side, and with enough F/B
- and really good band pass filters . . .

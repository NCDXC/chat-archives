Chris, I too have been appealing to members of Board (the 2018-2019 Board) trying to garner support for the So Orkney team. 

The individual board members tell me there was interest in supporting the So Orkney Team and they invited me to come to the April meeting to make a motion from the floor as a Club Member. Unfortunately, I was in Japan and unable to come to the April NCDXC meeting. 

During my time as Club Secretary and later as a Board member, it felt like to me that it was part of our Club purpose to support DXpeditions that were to entities needed by our members. It certainly seems to me that we as a Club would want to carry on the tradition of supporting DXpeditions, particularly those involving our members. 

I can tell you that in the last decade, the NCDXC has been very generous in supporting DXpeditions that I was involved with. So I am not 
understanding what has changed. I don't think it has anything to do with lack of sunspots. 

To compound the problem, In the last few years, two other large DX Clubs on the West Coast have stopped making direct donations to DXpeditions. The Southern California DX Club and the San Diego DX Club will no longer donate directly to a DXpedition. 

The US West Coast was once a significant supporter of DXpeditions, but that has diminished. Also, several major West Coast donors including Kip Edwards W6SZN have become silent keys. Kip was one the largest donor to major DXpeditions for many years. 

The failures of the last two attempts to activate Bouvet have given all of us pause to consider the risks involved in making these activation's. 
All of the DXpedition organizers that I know are trying to figure out how to manage the financial risk and carry out safe and effective activations of rare and remote entities. 

When the clubs that used to give generously keep their hands in their pockets it makes it that much more difficult to go forward and accept the risks. They hey day of many bold and well funded DXpeditions may be behind us. Time will tell. 

I hope that the NCDXC will reconsider supporting the So Orkney team. 

Best wishes and 73, 

Paul N6PSE

On Wed, Jul 3, 2019 at 3:46 PM Chris Tate - N6WM via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

I have heard that the NCDXC declined to provide any financial support for the upcoming VP8-O South Orkney expedition.  This one hits near and dear to me, and gives me concern.  First, most of my fellow Dxpeditioneer colleagues from VP6D, a wonderful and skilled international group of top amateurs are making this adventure. Second, I actually need VP8-O as an ATNO to move up the DX ladder.  (PS how many of you have VP8-O in FT8?)  As you can imagine, this is extremely disappointing to me as a member of the NCDXC and also a team member with this groups last and quite successful expedition to Ducie Island.  I think it was quite evident the level of professionalism involved.   So I would like to understand the BoD’s rationale for not supporting this expedition, and encourage the the club leadership to vote to contribute in support of the South Orkney expedition.

 

Whats the deal here?  Can someone loop me into how we as a premier DX club can move forward and support this expedition team?  Much smaller DX clubs seem to be pulling it off.. I am sure we can do it.  What say we use our club dollars smart?

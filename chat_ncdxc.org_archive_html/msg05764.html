Several DXAC reps have made additional or revised their previous comments concerning the 
remote operations. Requiring that the control point shall be in the same entity as the transmitter 
(and receiver) is still the majority opinion. The requirement for a maximum distance between 
the control point and the transmitter in the 200km to 200 mi distance has been mentioned so as 
not to gain a propagation advantage. 

Luddite thinking!  

Remote operation should be defined consistently across contesting and Dxing.  
  • Define the “station” - 500m circle and no remote receiver – DONE.
  • Create a circle within which all contacts must be made by one or more stations – 175 miles, just like ARRL clubs – DONE
  • The DXCC award is allocated based on its geographic center of the circle – DONE
Who cares where the control operator is?  Oh, and by the way, how do you KNOW the control operator is either within the entity OR within 200km/miles of the transmitter.

I’ve operated my station from China and India…  the station is located in Woodside, CA – that’s where my DXCC certificate is issued.  How does me being in India gain a propagation advantage?

Einstein said…
You see, wire telegraph is a kind of a very, very long cat.  You pull his tail in New York and his head is meowing in Los Angeles.  Do you understand this? And radio operates exactly the same way: you send signals here, they receive them there.  The only difference is that there is no cat.

My remote station just reinserts the cat…  I pull the cat’s tail from India and the cat meows loudly in Woodside.

Suggestion:  The DXAC should define the problem they are trying to solve by attempting to restrict remote operation.  Being able to operate from physical stations in different areas of the entity is the REAL PROBLEM – how can that possibly be equitable?

Please, start by defining the problem before designing yet another camel.

Stu K6TU

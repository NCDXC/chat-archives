I agree that it's challenging and addictive.  I'm hooked.  Period.  If I can work 27 countries in 18 months without a serious antenna or effort; this could be great fun!  I'm amazed that a low non-resonant antenna has done so well but most of that credit must go to the DX teams, their excellence is helping me.  Plus it sure helps pass the winter nights.

Once I move to ID, I absolutely have plans to put up top band antennas (raised counterpoise vertical is high on that list, pending more education; a Beverage for hearing better)...  The new place will have property to play and enough vegetable towers to make it interesting.  The trick will be in making things sturdy enough to handle the conditions (snow, ice and wind)... but you're familiar with most of that. ;-)  Another learning curve, cool!

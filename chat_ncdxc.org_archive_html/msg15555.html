Those that know me know I like to work most modes. I have made digital (FT8), CW, and voice (FM and SSB) QSOs in the last week. (Note that my CW isn't really up to rag chewing except at glacial paces. Fixing that is a continuing project.)
There are also people who specifically don't want to chat. One of the younger members of our club said he liked contesting because he didn't have to sit and listen to old men talking about their medical problems.
When I first tried one of the JT modes, I think it was JT9, I thought that is was a bit like watching paint dry. It was nice to make the QSO, but the rate was too slow. FT8 seems to work fast enough for most hams.
We mostly gave up on FT8 during field day and went back to PSK31 and RTTY. The ad hoc techniques people were using to send their exchanges was too much, and it took too many round trips. (I will note that, during field day, some stations like to get your exchange but not send theirs. Log checking in real contests stops this kind of nonsense.)
As always, the RTTY people tended to know how to operate in a contest. The PSK31 people tended to send, "Please copy 3A SCV", sometimes in all upper case which is slower that all lower case.
I always like to say, "Ham radio is a very big tent. Do the stuff you enjoy."

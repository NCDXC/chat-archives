On Jul 8, 2014, at 4:58 PM, Garry Shapiro via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

PG&E is one of the most dysfunctional corporations around. Good luck waiting for them to solve your problem. My last three complaints have never been addressed, despite promises to contact me directly after an investigation (and the stated but incorrect belief held by some PG&E employes that their employer actually gives a crap).

One suggestion is to call them back and say that you saw arcing on the primary wires. That will get them to respond. If they hassle you, claim that you thought that was what you saw, and BTW how about checking out this noise like you promised? If there are two liars, at least one cannot be blamed.

I have been jousting with PG&E about noise problems since about 1990. There have been PG&E workers who actually did give a crap (recall Wally Hanifin, KE6FYW now retired fishing in Florida) and believed the company owed its customers some respect. But, mostly, the people responsible for solving these problems could care less and often are actively hostile to hams.

I suggest that if you want results, start pestering them daily. Demand to talk to supervisors, write letters to the corporate fat cats at the top, harass the FCC. No one is going to do this for you.


I have had a number of noise investigations with PG&E, and have gotten quite good response, including having a Comm Tech come up *the same day* as the problem report. The issue (for me) has not been getting them to determine what/where the problem was, but getting the work crews to actually fix it once the work order has been placed.

What I found works *wonders* is to file a complaint with the PUC. You can do this on their website:

http://www.cpuc.ca.gov/puc/cec/e_complaint/

In a few days, you will get a letter from the PUC acknowledging the complaint, and soon after, a phone call from PG&E. It may take a few days/weeks for the complaint to filter down to the responsible department, but it does happen. I have had them repair/rebuild three poles in my area, one as recently as a few months ago.

My power-line noise levels are S-zero; i.e., I am limited only by atmospherics. (I don’t know who to call about that one.)

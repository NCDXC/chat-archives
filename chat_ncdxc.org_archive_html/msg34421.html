Some thoughts on this matter....

While it's clear that recouping the travel expenses is part of the reason for the high(er) costs of OQRS, some of the costs are heavily influenced by local considerations.  Some are blatant grabs for money (pay or get nothing).

One should not presume that mailing costs are similar in every location on the planet; even if bins of cards are shipped to another country for 'local' mailing (not done as much these days).  The mailing/shipping expenses are MUCH higher in non-NA countries (look at the stamps!).

Card collectors now expect (demand) a first class piece of paper, some folded multipage with high resolution images on a glossy high density cardstock.  And some truly are artwork, worthy for a shack wall presentation.  That, costs.

Others want only rapid LOTW confirmation; so they pay to save time and the monies go into the team fund pool.  Or they wait some period of time.

The DXpedition travel and access (local fees, permits, monies used to 'assist' the process) costs are (MUCH) higher.  The card materials supply costs are higher.  The production costs are higher.  The mailing costs are also, higher.  It all adds up.  (The reasons go beyond COVID and that discussion is for another time and place.)

If a DXer wants the card for any reason (box storage, display or needed to prove confirmation when LOTW is not used), it's expected that those costs are passed along (while a few solo stations expand that to ANY card sent, exceeding my value:worth threshold).

Hams are strange creatures; having (often or over time) spent many thousands to build up a station then quibble at the 'operating costs' of that station.  QSL is one such operating cost; be it electronic, to a bureau or manager, direct to an individual, it's a part of being on the air just like the energy used to power the station.  The worst case, the station is a black hole after the exchange (and it's real too).

MOST of the time for teams, I add a small donation, sometimes only $5, rarely is nothing added.  I base that amount on the efforts I've seen (varies a LOT by single nation teams vs multinational), the 'fairness' of the team to reach ALL zones (many only care about their homies and yes need/demand varies too by locale but that should NOT be a major consideration that unbalances the effort); the difficulty to make an exchange (flea power on a wire in the Indian Ocean or did the team really TRY) and what contacts I was able to glean.

i.e. the recent team to Grenada, moderately loud on most bands across NA/AS; ~64% EU, 25% NA (can't hear next door?  Almost zero for SA?) earned the minimal donation only; clearly uninterested in working more than EU.  I watched (and called) for hours and not once did they show any effort anywhere non-EU, except when there was no path to EU (or a few hours on the last day for AS).  Their blatant bias earned a minimal donation and only that much because I was able to be patient enough and wait for EU to close to gain every slot they offered (except 60M because it was out of US band the way they ran it).  (They are VERY high on my 'worst team of the year' list but not alone either.)

Acknowledge the need/demand but at the same time, be balanced to ALL needs or expect nothing.  Effort out equals monies in return; pretty simple math.

On the other hand, another recent team DID balance the callers by using pileup management and watching the stats; so they got a much higher donation (and yes, I got all of the slots offered there as well).  Communication FROM the team ABOUT their goals should be made clear; we are supposed to be communicators, not elitist snobs.  (Grenada scored an F- because I'm not in EU.)

For those running small stations on a restricted budget, waiting (up to a year in some cases) for LOTW is the answer if they don't want to deal with the fumbling DX bureaus.  The risk is that it could be forgotten, lost or the individual passes over that time period (rare, but possible).

DXing is a long term marathon, not a sprint and the current noob ham has literally no first hand knowledge of long delays (Box 88 Moscow, years, if ever to get a response) when instant gratification (Livestream) is often available now.  Newer hams expect instant, it's a reality slap to wait.

MY discussion lead up question would be:  Why do many DX stations (not just teams) NOT use LOTW (besides the ARRL making it complicated for non-US to set up?) and what can be done about it that WE can help to make it more available?  That discussion would benefit all DXers casual to intensive, if a solution was to appear.

The cost (once set up) is essentially free.  Even the smallest of remote villages have SOME internet (nearby) or mail, almost all stations have a web site or QRZ page (proving internet access) and it's fast to confirm since there is no physical transfer.  Local (time, political or legal) limitations apply or we wouldn't have international QSL managers (punt the log to a neutral country to disseminate).

Second question:  WHO will run LOTW if the (fumbling) ARRL ends, should they continue to falter into failure.  (What is 'plan B' for confirming contacts?)  What then becomes of DXCC since the ARRL provides that (with documentation, for a fee)?  While my dislike of the ARRL is well known, they are doing an exceptional job of self-destruction the past ten years (except crying poor and begging for funds, while reducing services and dubious decisions and spending).

We need a plan B (that is part of my DNA).  Clublog (doesn't want the burden) and QRZ are options and eQSL makes things more difficult than the ARRL...  What happens if the ARRL closes up?  It needs to be fast, secure and available world wide at a minimal expense to the individual ham.  That, is a humongous hill to climb.

THAT would make for a lively discussion Paul.  And it affects ALL DXers and those who casually operate.  Just reduce it to:  "QSL, where do we go next?"

I'll supply some popcorn for that one...

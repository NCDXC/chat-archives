Here is your NCDXC question of the week:
 
What makes a good DXpedition?     What criteria do you use to decide if a DXpedition was good or not?

How good are they at working the TOUGH ones -- both propagation and bands.  Can they be heard in places where propagation is tough? Can they hear weak signals?  Much of this comes down to technical competence. Are they trying to work everyone at least once, or going for easy QSOs to band-slots? Are they smart about propagation or dumb? Beyond that, it's their control of the pileups and RATE, RATE, RATE.

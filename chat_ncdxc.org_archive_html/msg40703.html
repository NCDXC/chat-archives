Hi Rick,

You will want to download the WSJT-X_IMPROVED app.  It currently has the best decoder for FT8.  Within that menu, download the latest CTY.dat file (it’s not automated to update).

MSHV has known issues and flaws; but the EU folks adore it in part because it has a robot mode that hangs, hangs, hangs, hangs often until the op kicks it.  They don’t take the time to properly set the audio output, so each stream will have a different strength (and VERY often it can look like 5 streams when it’s really 3, because there are ‘shoulders’ on the tones; that is wasted energy and spectrum).  It’s also not updated often to take advantages of the base code (WSJT-X) updates.

The ONLY advantage of them using MSHV is that you can call BELOW their tone (and below 1000 Hz) so there is more useful spectrum for callers.  With WSJT-X (any flavor) that is not allowed in Fox/Hound mode (not really used much).

WSJT-X also has SuperFox mode with a potential of 10 stations at once being worked; but there is a cost; a LOT more signal is required to decode (a 10 dB hit).  Running multiple streams also causes a lower signal per stream, but the callers can move SHIFT to their tones and narrow the bandwidth (less noise to sift, louder apparent signal); but SuperFox requires more bandwidth (the WHY there is a signal hi).  Going narrow for SuperFox, can’t be done.

PLEASE take the time to correctly set the transmit audio and make SURE that ONLY the computer sends FT8 tones (NOT OS sounds) into the radio.  If you listen to others, you’ll be able to tell who hasn’t bothered (TV, kids, OS noises, hot mic…).

NEVER use the rig as a default device for the OS and NEVER leave a hot mic; the two largest oops.

Since you have a K4, it’s super simple, but DO make sure you only have (JUST BARELY) 5 showing on the ALC during transmit; less and the rig may struggle to reach the output power (allowing random noise mixed in) and too much, you may splatter.

The K4 also strives to keep the audio clean; so you MAY see a little less output power (90 watts instead of 100).

I would practice soon, get a feel for the mode.  It’s not a bad mode, still requires a little understanding and technique but it’s BORING and lazy (phone, CW, RTTY each requires an actual skill).  But it also gives a popgun station a shot at working DX.

You MAY hear that it’s a low power mode; that is FALSE, it’s a low receive mode, meant for weak signals.  Use the transmit power required to make the contact; up to the band (and operator class) limit.  For the rare ones, legal limit is common (and I prefer to get in, get logged, move out quickly).

My personal rules; TRY to set your TX tone in a visual gap on the audio waterfall (it’s only based on what YOU hear, not the DX); NEVER call any station ON their tone (others do and that may only cause a mix with no decode) and IF they use Fox/Hound, you’re interfering.

My personal gripe:  If I’m calling DX that may be rare or a Marathon point; PLEASE do NOT keep calling me if you’re not that station.  Call once, then wait; I’ll get back to you; repeat after 5 minutes if needed (ONCE). (Italians then French then the Russians excel at this; it’s ANNOYING.)

73,
Rick nk7i

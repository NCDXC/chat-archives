Greg,

Look at the shadow line on say, PSK Reporter.  You'll see that who goes dark on the west coast, varies a LOT over the year (right now, I get earlier sunrise and sunset than AZ).  Those times will be greatly offset, twice a year.

Then considering the path, it's mostly antipodal (literally the other side of the planet) AND near antarctic so the path will vary (A LOT).

Yes, RX and TX paths are not always the same.  I've proven this to my satisfaction but it's not documented here.

Your vertical ignores path, beams/arrays don't.  Your vertical has a LOW angle of radiation, most beams don't (or are at least higher).  Lower angles are more useful as the band opens/closes; high angle may work better mid-opening.  It's apples and bananas.

I got in on 40 CW as soon as I got an alert; which brings up a critical point:  USE the tools that are available (HamAlert for one) so you can get in on the action SOONER and BEFORE it's well known (spotted).

I live alone, spent most of the time at home lately; so stopping what I'm doing to get the DX is easier (and it meets my goals).  I got in fast several times, a few of those paid off; some it was already spotted and I didn't get through (or hear, polar effect clamps down here).

Remember that you only hear YOUR side of the spectrum; so competing with another location (same tones) is constant.  Then there is path distortion, someone ALMOST on your tone; even FT8 can be a disaster zone (and WHY your report may be more meager than you'd expect; it's the spectrum noise floor [LOUD] used as the measuring baseline).  The rest is up to the settings in the DX app (MSHV in this case); which can be set to hear/ignore zones, favor by distance and so much more...

What happened even 10 minutes later on 40 (when he actually favored NA/SA) was simply embarrassing, disgusting and shameful.  AND it was generally US created (signal strength showed that).  It's also what EU does on a regular basis (pot calling the kettle black?) and EU is generally FIRST CLASS WHINERS; some countries better at it than others (even excel or more consistent at least).

EU had the lions share of Marion; yet the whining NEVER stopped (MORE MORE MORE GIVE US MORE!).

That is a truly sad state of affairs.

Has no one read the DX Code of Conduct?  It certainly doesn't seem so.

I'm not happy that Yuris didn't share the wealth with NA (west coast in particular); he can work EU all  day/night for WEEKS but the windows to the west coast; he ignored or didn't understand.  He went (paid) to do work and ham radio was an after though, with his gear.

Overall, he did well, but a little more thought and attention to prop would have been helpful to us.  In the end, I got 7 band mode slots (from ATNO) so I have no reason to whine, but those are my thoughts and comments.

73,
Rick nk7i


On 5/13/2025 8:57 AM, Greg De Hoogh via Chat wrote:
I'd be interested in hearing from folks with more experience than me, as well as from Yuris, about the possibility that asymmetrical propagation played a part in the apparent slighting of West Coast stations by ZS8W. The night I worked him on 40m FT8 with my 43' vertical, a number of other folks here in SoCal who have 40m beams failed to get him. Is it possible that the primary propagation path from here to Marion Island was in a different direction than the path from there to here, thus resulting in his not hearing them even though they heard him just fine?

I worked him on 20m FT8 about 2-1/2 hours after he first went QRV, and later got him on 40 and 80m FT8. Considering that my only HF antenna is the 43' vertical, I think I did just fine considering it was a one-man DXpedition. I failed to get him on 40m CW, but that was because of QRM on his frequency, not because of anything he did/didn't do. (I'm sure I heard the "PM", but couldn't tell if he got the prefix right because of the QRM - apparently he didn't.)

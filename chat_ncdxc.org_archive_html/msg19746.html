After tending to someone recovery from surgery for a few weeks, knocking out some other projects and necessaries then having to wait for dry weather to get wood (without having to dry it); today I was able to make some progress on the new station shack.
I cut then installed a 'backer board' on the new wall for mounting mobile radios (dual band for now but room for the 220, 900 and 6M FM as needed as things progress).  This will reduce the damage to the dry wall and make a firmer mounting point.  In the space under it and above the baseboard, I will place a copper pipe for a common ground bus which will be attached to the tower/safety/etc ground (rod in place, a few feet outside). Everything radio will be tied to that pipe.
I installed the dual band radio (the first in the shack), control head on the desk (local repeater and the IRLP node).  A quarter wave spike antenna is sufficient, for now. 

Once I install the pipe, I can move the HF station down.
I hung a shelf for things I need to see but don't need to touch often (computer controlled) and for speakers; to keep the desk 'less' cluttered (like that'll help?).  I will add one small shelf on the right for another K3 speaker and some books (and snacks shelf).
I hung the monitor (22" TV) for the P3 panadapter display, next to the 50" computer monitor.  Then I set the new UPS in place (to keep the station alive until the house generator transfers over; the KPA1500 won't be on the generator, but I can move the KPA500 into place if needed; the KAT500 will be idle; all resonant antennas).
I moved the new table that I built (the legs are a short rack mount, the Asron 60A p/s is in place), set up the p/s for the KPA1500 and placed the amp too.  The top is maple butcher block, stained with 5 coats of poly.  While there is rack space set aside for the IRLP node repeater (half duplex mode), there won't be space for a duplexor when I get one so that will stay on the other side if this wall (where the antenna coax enters the garage then passes into the shack).
It's slow, tedious work but most of the wires will be hidden (less visual clutter); the space next to the panadapter will be where the cables enter the shack (patch board optional, maybe later).  For now they're passing through the dog door. ;-) 

But it's progress and slowly coming together.  And time for lunch!

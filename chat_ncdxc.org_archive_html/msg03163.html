More input. Since posting my comments, I've been contacted by several relatively young hams who I know, by virtue of their activity in NCCC, as real balls of fire. This morning, walking around the DeAnza Swap, I was approached by one of them, K6YL, who has become very active in NCCC, both as an operator and helping out with various club awards. Among other things, she coordinated awards for CQP and annual NCCC contest competitions. This summer, she and N6DQ were married.
Joanne told me that when she attended an NCDXC meeting, she was ignored. At NCCC, she got a warm welcome. Joanne is not shy, she's very bright, outgoing, and very receptive to input.
When I inquired two weeks ago on the list about the need for increased involvement of DX hams w ith eQSL and LOTW, Chris, N6WM immediately jumped in and reinforced my ideas. Chris is a dynamic middle age guy with background in computers and RF. He and several of his colleagues have built a very nice mountaintop contesting station that they can run remotely. Iain, N6ML, another very sharp younger guy, and superb operator, has used that station to great effect to build impressive totals of countries and band countries in a very short time. Iain is shy, and does not like to take an active role in club activities, but he's very good at piping up to help folks solve problems with software and computers. N6WM is a past president of NCCC, and is currently chairing CQP.
At Visalia this spring, when the MC of the awards event was looking the the youngest ham, it seemed from my chair that the number of hams under 25 could have been counted on the fingers of one hand. Anyone who thinks we're doing things right is seriously out of touch!
Why am I a member? Because I like a lot of the people, I like hanging out, I like contributing, and I appreciate learning from other members. And because I strongly believe in "giving back." And one of my contributions is stirring the sctix. :) 

73, Jim K9YC

This message is intended for NCDXC members. This is not an official news release.

Hello NCDXC friends,

We are now just past the halfway point in our DXpedition. Yesterday, several operators had to return to Japan and Hong Kong, so we are now QRV with six team members.

On Saturday we will begin RTTY operations instead of CW during the contest. We will run RTTY until the very end.

The pileups are diminishing, but we always have JA and EU callers. The best times for NA seem to be on 20 meters around 1600 UTC and on 10/12/15 meters at our sunrise.  We sometimes have a brief long path opening to the east coast at our sunset but it has not been consistent. 17 meters has not been a productive band thus far.

Overall conditions are quite good, although we briefly lost high band propagation yesterday due to an X-class solar flare.  Our location on a hilltop is just superb. We can't walk too far however as we are surrounded by jungles which have Burmese Pythons in them, so we are told. We have a police/military outpost next to our hotel and the officers are quite amused at the goings on at XZ1J.

We will start tearing down on Nov 25th, leaving just two stations QRV until the very end on the morning of Nov 26th.

Our Leader, Zorro JA1JHT has been very busy meeting with Myanmar Government officials to bolster the future of amateur radio in Myanmar. Yesterday, Zorro met with the President of Myanmar.

Myanmar is a very beautiful and special place. I hope you can all visit this amazing place.

73 for now,

Paul N6PSE

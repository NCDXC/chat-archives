Nice summary, Paul.

I agree that the system is not working well for big operations.  Up front funding should be more common and accepted by the world of DXers.  But, it seems that getting many DXers to part with a ‘ham sandwich and cola’ is quite difficult.  Donate?  Me?  What if I don’t work them?  Well, what if they never get on the air because you were unwilling to forego that extra beer and piece of pie?  Many of the same DXers will spend like crazy on their station so they can enjoy ham radio and want to be DXers, but do not seem to realize that equipment is not the only part of the hobby to which they need to spend if they want to enjoy all facets, especially DXing.

I wish The Rebels luck and hope those that did not yet help with the GoFundMe effort review the reasoning behind their lack of action.  Link below for convenience.

https://www.gofundme.com/f/3y0i-bouvet-island-expedition

(The above link works but something is temporarily wrong with that page.  Hope if gets fixed…)

73,

Al

I think the Bouvet guys have softened their approach as N0UN seems to no longer be their spokesman.

 

I really want to see this Bouvet thing happen.  I have donated $50 and will donate more upon activation. 

 

Heck, with 24,000 regular DXers if everyone donated $10 there would be plenty of money for major DXpeditions like this. 

 

With this many regular DXers, money for an ultra rare entity should not be the limiting factor. 

 

Problem is, a few give a lot and many give little or nothing. 

 

Most don't know this but almost all EU Foundations and Clubs pledge a donation up front but only fund the pledged donation after

the DXpedition is successfully completed and the QSL cards are in the mail. 

 

The current model of fundraising is broken and puts too much financial risk on the DXpedition organizers. 

 

Paul N6PSE

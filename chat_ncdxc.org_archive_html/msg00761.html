I like the idea of the Pledge Drive method, but have a question about the "cost of administration" of a pledge drive. The question of how much money would it take to raise the necessary funding. We might contact our local NPR or PBS station and find out how they do it. Of course, in the case of NPR or PBS, they have the built in medium to solicit funds. Would we have to do it by a mail, or e-mail outreach to seek potential donors? In past experience, pledge drives also get into problems of "pledge drive fatigue" when multiple groups begin competing with each other in the pool of potential donors. I personally am virtually overwhelmed with the number of charities that are forever "begging" for donations. Bert, K6CSL

There were a lot of great responses to yesterday's question of the week. Many of these topics have been broached before, either in email discussions such as this, or in chatter during or after our regular NCDXC meetings.

 

Jim (below) suggests a shared pool of equipment. That makes sense, but do you really trust a bunch of equipment used by another group on previous trips? You would have to do extensive set up and testing to make sure everything works as it should. We normally do that anyway, but greater care would have to be applied since another group has used some of the gear. Getting equipment really isn't our main challenge when planning to do go a rare island entity. It is money for fuel and boat costs that is our most significant challenge when considering these ultra rare destinations.

 

Boats cost a lot of money. Our 3D2C DXpedition boat costs were approximately $90,000 for the trip. That breaks down to a little over $8000 per day for the charter. That is on the lower end of boat costs. For a trip to a rare and much needed Southern Ocean/Indian Ocean trip you could easily have boat costs of 3X or 4X that figure. While the 3D2C operators covered the cost of their boat passage themselves, that just would not be possible on a trip where the boat costs are significantly more.

 

John-K6YP suggests a way to economize by hitting several islands during one trip. While I think that might make the DXpedition more popular by providing multiple entities instead of just one, I see it also adding to the costs of the trip by adding fuel consumption and extra dates for the boat booking. It is hard enough getting the DX Community to support a DXpedition to one rare entity, by adding a second entity and increasing your costs, I have doubts that the DX Community would step up and do their part.

 

Dave-NE5EE suggests that he would donate $10 to a rare entity that he needs for an ATNO. While that would normally be considered a generous donation for an entity such as 3D2C where our cost per QSO was probably $2 per contact, a DXpedition to Bouvet/Crozet could easily cost the team $5 to $7 to provide each QSO!  So a $10 donation would probably not cover the costs to provide a few contacts.

 

Jim-K9YC likes the DXpeditions done by George-AA7JV. George just finished activating PT0S with a group of three other guys. Transportation was provided by the Brazilian Navy and I tend to think their costs were minimal. Not much of a chance that our US Navy or the French Navy will take us where we want to go.  

 

 

Anyway, a lot of great responses this week and a lot of food for thought.

 

73's for now,

 

Paul N6PSE

On Dec 17, 2012, at 8:30 AM, <paul@n6pse.com> <paul@n6pse.com> wrote:



Here is the NCDXC question of the week:

 

We all know that DXpedition funding is a big challenge these days. Is it time for a complete paradigm shift in how DXpeditions are funded?

 

For example, there are entities such as Bouvet/Crozet that could be activated relatively easily assuming that proper funding was available. Those that are currently leading DXpeditions to rare destinations are reluctant to launch such an endeavor because of the uncertainty of funding.

 

What if a group were to determine a likely budget figure such as $500,000 and then announce that once they have raised the funding, they will set a date for their DXpedition and make it a reality.  Perhaps the organizers could specify that all funds are collected by the NCDXF and then dispensed once the goal is met.

 

How would this approach be accepted by the DX Community?

 

The current model of making a donation with your OQRS or a few bucks in the envelope does not work for this kind of DXpedition and requires the organizers to carry too much financial risk.


What are your thoughts?

 

Paul N6PSE

Last night he was off by +0.4 seconds.  Not horrible and still workable.  When I've run a Pi, a cheap UBlox GPS/GLONAS usb receiver ($12 on Amazon https://www.amazon.com/dp/B01MTU9KTF) along with configuring the Pi to accept GPS has helped me keep my time even when I was out of cell service range.

For windows, I use BKtTimeSync which allows you to set your clock off of an NTP server of your choosing or a GPS dongle, like the $12 one above.  It's free, simple, and easy to setup and not only runs at boot but can be set to run at a regular interval.  Mine is set to check the time every 30 minutes.  Not that my drift is ever more than 0.002 seconds, but I have the ability, so why not!  It's created by a ham IZ2BKT.

On Sun, May 11, 2025 at 11:50 AM Rick Bates, nk7i via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Which BEGS the question: Why is the clock off? His clock has been consistent; +1.2 DT. He's not the only one with this issue. (His DT drifted WIDELY his first night on 40 FT8, he clearly has an issue but he caught it and resolved it.)

I have a Raspberry Pi 3B+ (chosen because it has wifi, in part) with a GPS HAT mounted to it. The HAT is a GPS receiver and it's sole purpose is to sent a 'tick' to the Pi; when then acts as an NTP server.

All computers in my house, are pointed to that Pi; all are typically within ~0.2 ms accurate. Those computers using Windows, run the Meinburg client (to replace the flawed MS NTP client). The others are linux and are already good clients (iOS devices don't allow me to manage them).

The total cost for this tiny NTP server was well under $200 but I'll have to print a case or mount it on my shack rack. In the meantime, it sits on a wood shelf and just works.

Add one power supply (5V, couple amps) and if in a poor sky location, the external GPS antenna. One short cable into the router and/or use the wifi path; Meinburg factors path delays. No noise, no fan, no RFI, it just works.

It takes up almost no space and it isn't expensive. So; WHY is it not part of the list of gear taken to activate any entity or site?

Is it that after spending thousands in gear; that $200 is too much to make sure all clocks are accurate?

For logs and modes like JT modes; it just seems wise to have a known accurate clock on site.

On Fri,1/9/2015 11:05 AM, Kevin Rowett via Chat wrote:
Soldering PL259 connectors requires the correct tool. The problem is, that tool costs $500 on ebay. Get a Met-cal soldering iron with a chisel tip. If you’ve ever done it this way, you’ll see how easy it is. :-).
I've installed a lot of PL259s without that $500 tool, and gradually learned how to do it better. I use ONLY Amphenol 83-1SP connectors. My tools:
For solid copper center conductor coax, #12 or larger, a retractable blade knife to first cut through to the center, then cut the outer jacket to the right length. 

Good small dikes to trim any ragged edges of the braid.

Vise grip wrench to twist the connector onto the coax.
A heavy vise to hold the cable while mounting the connector and while soldering.
Yes, a good temperature-controlled iron with a large blade. I still use the Hakko that I bought about 10 years ago. Not a Metcal, but it does the job with the right blade. 

Liquid soldering flux is a BIG help, especially with the braid.

While preparing to get price quotes, I learned that Fair-Rite has a new, and VERY useful #31 clamp-on. It's 1.4-in i.d., 2.5-in long, so it's the equivalent of five of the 1.4-in i.d., 2.4-in o.d. toroids. That means we can very easily get 6-7 turns of RG8/RG213 through it with connectors installed. Even better news -- I've got a quote of $10.34 in box quantities (before tax and shipping). The last time we bought toroids we paid just under $4 each. Not only that, this new part costs less than a buck more than the 1-in i.d. part we've been buying. 

So -- this time around, we're buying two parts. They are:

1 - The new 1.4-in i.d. clamp-on, approximate cost $11
2 - The 0.75-in i.d. clamp-on that W6EB bought several years ago that is great for chokes to kill RFI to/from computer systems, home entertainment systems, switching power supplies, etc. Approximate cost $2.75 each
To pull this off, we need to boost our quantity a bit, because we're buying in full box quantities to get these prices (and also to avoid breakage during shipment). It's unlikely that we'll do this again anytime soon, so think about all the things that can go wrong, your FD and CQP setups, and future DX trips, and order now. The last time we bought clamp-ons was 4-5 years ago! Bought 2-5 pieces at a time, costs are typically 2-4x what we'll be paying.

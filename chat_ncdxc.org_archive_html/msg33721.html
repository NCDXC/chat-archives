Hello everyone,

I just got this notice today from the California Historical Radio Society in Alameda that they are getting rid of old radio/amateur radio publications and books. If you are interested please contact them.

CQ CQ CQ DE CHRS

 

RE QSTS, ETC

 

The California Historical Radio Society in Alameda is now making available to interested amateurs and ham radio clubs duplicate copies of QST (1920s to 1970s), CQs, vintage ham radio handbooks, Popular Communications, Monitoring Times, Old Timers Bulletins, and many hard-to-find books on amateur and commercial radio history. We have many boxes of these volumes, including, for example, complete sets of 1940s QSTs. These are going out on a first come, first serve basis.

 

We are a non-profit dedicated to preserving wireless communication history. Donations are appreciated, but not required. Those interested in taking this opportunity to expand their collections should contact CHRS archivist Bob Rydzewski at brydzewski(at)sbcglobal.net. Or just show up at our "all you can eat surplus sale" (not food, but old radios, ham equipment, etc.) from 9AM to 11:30 AM on Saturday, March 2nd at CHRS, 2152 Central Avenue in Alameda.

 

73

 

KJ6SBR

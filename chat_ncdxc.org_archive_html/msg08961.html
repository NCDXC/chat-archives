Rex,

AND THAT IS THE WHOLE POINT..................

If you want do your DXCC from your own QTH as Rex does and as I do
then you play the game your way and feel proud when you hit Honor
Roll or reach 340 entities.

If you don't care where in the USA the transmitter is that is fine as well.;.
If you move from New York to California, you get to keep your DXCC
standing or start over. It is up to you.

DXCC is a game YOU play yourself by your own rules. It is only YOU
that YOU must answer to. If you want to work em from your own QTH
do so. If you do not have a QTH conducive to a reasonable antenna farm
work em with a remote if that floats your boat. If YOU feel good about what
you have done then that is what counts. The important thing is that you are on
the air and having fun and that is what ham radio is all about.

Your own QTH, a remote QTH mixed US QTHs it does not matter. Run legal
limit into a stack of 6 el monobanders at 6000' ASL, or run 5 watts into a wire
dipole. Every situation is different and can not be compared against others.
Your score should only matter to YOU as YOU are the only competition that
matters

It is certainly OK to see how the other guy is doing. I often look at the results
on Club Log on certain DXpeditions to see how a great op like W6OAT did. But
even this does not matter because if Rusty already has the DX worked on all
the West Coast available band slots he is likely to be in the pileups. It is really
about YOU improving your own operating skills, knowledge of propagation, and
improving your station in whatever way YOU choose that will help you work your
way up the DXCC ladder.

Have fun, learn work DX. That is my 2 cents.

Best 73,

john k6yp

On Wed,1/21/2015 4:18 PM, Mike Flowers via Chat wrote:
> “Now the location of the operator doesn’t matter; the operator could be on the far side of the Moon if he or she could figure out how to remotely control a station on land back on Earth from there.” Transmitter location continues to define a station’s location, and, for DXCC purposes, all transmitters and receivers must be located within a 500 meter diameter circle, excluding antennas.

All that's fine. But what is NOT fine is that there's no distance limit
on the TX/RX locations from which QSOs can be counted for DXCC. QSOs
from a rent-a-shack or motel in Maine, or even a previous home QTH in
Maine, can be combined with those from QSOs from the Bay area for DXCC
credit. And that stinks -- it cheapens the DXCC program, making it a
longevity contest rather than one of operating or technical skill.

73, Jim K9YC

If you have trouble working them from your home station I would recommend using remotehamradio.com. Still not too late to sign up, pay the annual $99 fee and learn how to operate. If you account value for your time and equipment for your station it is a bargain.

I worked them from big gun station in east coast and probably spent 5 minutes ($1.25/min or so) on it plus popping in few other times checking other bands. So I probably spent total of $20 getting them to the log. Just the drive time to some better station would have cost me more for bridge tolls and car charging. Plus that time of being in pileups for hours/days...

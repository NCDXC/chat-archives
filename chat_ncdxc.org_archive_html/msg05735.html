Hi Peter,

I would not worry about those that have not confirmed lotw.  Many of those never will because they believe it is too much of a hassle or it really is hard to set up.  I was annoyed at the effort needed to set it up here and DX has a different process.  Going to the trouble to write to them will not change their mind.  A direct QSL is much more likely to get a reply but this is not guaranteed, either.  I have received lotw QSLs 20+ years after the QSO, some for new band/modes!  A pleasant surprise, for sure.
If you plan to be active, I suggest that you keep working them until someone does a QSL via lotw or via the bureau.  Patience will reward you for many that you need to confirm.  If you want a direct reply, I use QRZ.com to get a station's preferred method, some of which are strange.  It usually costs you to do so but if it is one you really want and feel will be hard to work again, go for the direct.

As Bob mentioned, DXKeeper, a part of DXLab Suite, is a good logging and award tracking program.  But, it has a learning curve that I have yet to finish. (Y vs. V vs. R, etc?? and how to get LoTW credits and the log to sync).  It can be tied inmto the Pathfinder app that simplifies the lookup process.  It can be downloaded without committing to their DXKeeper log.
I'd have a local card checker look at your cards which will make getting credit pretty quick and remove the doubt of getting LoTW confirmations.

Bottom line: work multiple hams in the same country and someone will get you satisfied!  Takes being patient, though.

Most people have never heard of this, but I used to use an earlier model of this receiver back when I was working as an EMI engineer at Digital Equipment. The Rhode & Schwarz ESW-44 is probably the most sophisticated, sensitive receiver ever made. It covers from ONE HERTZ (that’s not a typo) to 44 GHz. Bandwidth can be set anywhere from 1 Hz to 10 MHz, and the sensitivity is -169 dbm (that’s .00079 microvolts, or 795 femtovolts in 50 ohms). The noise floor is below that.

Of course, it sells for about $200K. It is primarily used for laboratory measurement, but it would make one hell of a general-coverage receiver!

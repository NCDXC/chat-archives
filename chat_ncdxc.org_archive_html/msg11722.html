On Mar 25, 2016, at 9:31 AM, John Miller wrote:

Rich:

Got them last evening twice on CW within an hour:  Both Short Path pointed at about 210 degrees.

30M  0655 UTC  just above the noise level but clear enough.  Pileup was small.  200 honest watts with a SteppIR at 30 ft. Op was N6TQ - gave me a nice 73 and sent email note this morning.

40M  0745 UTC  peaked at about 559.  800 watts w/ SetppIR.  Very good CW op but in my opinion sending a bit too fast for conditions -- although I understand how the adrenaline flows from the other side.

Also heard or saw them work several local stations (K6MKF, W6OAT, K6YP, N6ML, W6GJB, K9YC, etc.) + many other NW and Southern California station over a period of 3-4 hours last evening.  Even though they are not yet operating in the best location on Heard for the West Coast, they're doing a great job thus far.

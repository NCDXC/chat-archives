This morning I worked E51NOU - South Cooks for a new one on 160M. At first I couldn't hear him at all on my K9AY Loops, but when I switched to my transmitting antenna - which is a Cushcraft MA-160V mounted on top of a 30' Rohn pushup mast - (meaning its about 60' tall) and with 76 buried radials (mostly 50' long), I heard him loud and clear. Not sure why - the K9AY loops are almost always better . . . . 

I thought my K9AY Loops might have suffered a problem with the remote switch - but when testing it with some SSB ops on 1.875, I heard them and saw that the switch and directionality as well as the preamp were all still working fine. 

In the past I have built EWE's and a Flag. They are pretty much the same thing as the K9AY Loops - so I am not interested in Flag's or EWE's since the K9AY "footprint" is much better. 

However, I do have 200' along a creek that defines the hypotenuse of my small triangular lot. 

Anyone ever try a short beverage antenna? Seems like 200' isn't long enough, eh?

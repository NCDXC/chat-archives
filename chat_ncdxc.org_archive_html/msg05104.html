Being a little pistol, I usually try for a little while and then come to my senses and realize I can't compete with the big guns on the first day - sometimes even the second or third. 

 

But FT5XO (Kerguelin) taught me that on 40 and 30M I am competitive - even though I don't have a 70' tower with 40M yagi - I do understand the band like its no ones business. I also am a morning grey line junky. 

 

So I was hoping that FT5ZM (AMSTERDAM) would have similar characteristics propagation wise as FT5XO (KERGUELIN) especially since they are in the same general vicinity. I did not like VOACAP's predictions - I did not trust them. 

 

Sure enough, FT5ZM hit 40 and 30M like FT5XO did back then. 

 

I always work band slots later in the DX-pedition - I actually do not like huge pileups - I like some competition, but I understand my limitations - which I chose to have - and strategize around them. 

 

I also know that a pair of phased verticals work very well to that part of the world from my QTH down in the ravine. I don't know why - but they do. I would expect the low angles to get chopped off by the surrounding hills. 

 

Regarding "DX Hoggery", I have recently been asking myself why I am even bothering with DXCC Challenge and asking myself "How much is enough?". So your email was quite ironic. 

 

DXCC Honor Roll and DXCC HR #1 followed by 10BDXCC seem like good goals - for me - anything more is excess. I don't care for these "Marathons" and do not like how all of these awards overlap each other - because I think it adds to the "mayhem" of these ever growing and nasty pileups. 

 

But its not my job to foist my "rules" on any one else. 

 

Its a hobby, and the person with the most fun wins.

 

73,

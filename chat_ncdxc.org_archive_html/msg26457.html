I run an Arrow with a Kenwood HT @ 5 watts, full duplex.  I have been into the ISS repeater before with it and made a contact with another ground station, did not catch the astronauts on though.  I rigged up a cheapo UHF preamp that runs on AAs and velcros to the side, just have to be careful not to key up in to it - ask me how I know :-).  Luckily the preamp units are very cheap on amazon.  I have used the preamp to make it easier to aim/listen on some of the weaker satellites, its not necessary for the ISS though.

  A headset is also very useful, operating the satellite pass is busy and takes some practice.

I would recommend using a rig that can record the audio or otherwise putting your cellphone with a recording app in your shirt pocket, too much going on between aiming, tracking, operating w/doppler shift to also log.  You can then listen back and log later, and make manual entries into LoTW to post as satellite mode and show in the clublog sat standings if you like.

ISS passes at sunrise and sunset are incredible because it's remarkably easy to see with the naked eye. 

I use an app for android called W1ANT Pro that actually has an aim mode - you can place teh cellphone in the same plane as the antenna and it will get you there, and updates live with expected doppler shift freqs.

Good luck!  I keep my setup in my work vehicle and pull it out sometimes when there is doentime, station setup/teardown is about 5-10 minutes including assembly and loading the recording and sat app.

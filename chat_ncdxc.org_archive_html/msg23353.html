These won't necessarily make the DXpedition standout, but in my opinion they are good practices:

1) Don't use the leaderboard. It just creates a competition for addition slots/Qs that have no meaning in the world of counting for DXCC. 9 bands, 3 modes are enough. The only reason to work a band again is for a new mode.

2) Stick with a call to complete a Q. Work dupes. You have no idea what the chaser's rx problems may be.

3) Keep stations running on the money bands: 20m +/-

4) Work the digi modes from the start. Do not delay until late in the operation as so many other DXpeditions have done with RTTY. This is really important if you have to end activity earlier than planned or in the case of a solar event.

5) The ops for FT8 Fox should be well practiced. Bring equipment to maintain clock sync.

GL to the team.

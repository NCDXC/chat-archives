On Oct 27, 2017, at 5:28 PM, Christopher Hoover via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:

Your 107' doublet is not resonant on any band. Thus it will have a very poor SWR
on all ham bands either with or with a 9:1 balun

It sounds like Michael has an end-fed long wire and not a doublet.    If so, a 9:1 "balun" (really an unun) is typical.  If it is really a doublet, yeah, that's the wrong balun.

An end-fed long wire needs a counterpoise of some sort.  If you don't have a dedicated wire, the shield of your feedline (and perhaps your radio and even you) will become the counterpoise.  You can make that work, but it can be  finicky, depending on how the long the coax is and how it runs.  It can also give you RF in the shack.   A dedicated counterpoise wire and choking the coax (see K9YC choke cookbook) is a better idea.

I got good service out of an end-fed long wire strung along my roof line.  I used no center support.  I had  a wire counterpoise -- I forget the length -- attached at the "ground" lug of the 9:1 unun, and the wire was laid directly upon the roof.  Adding an external tuner (LDG in my case) at the balun feedpoint help a ton, as the tuner took the feedline variable out of the antenna equation.

Michael, do you have the antenna isolated from the support?   

73 de AI6KG.




On Fri, Oct 27, 2017 at 11:47 AM, John Eisenberg - K6YP via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
Hi Michael,

I would suggest an Alpha Delta DX-CC Multiband Dipole available from HRO
and DX Engineering at $179. It is 82 feet long and is resonant on 80, 40, 20,
15 and 10 meters. It is rated at 1 kW max power.

Your 107' doublet is not resonant on any band. Thus it will have a very poor SWR
on all ham bands either with or with a 9:1 balun. A 1:1 balun might have been a
little better choice. but without a WIDE RANGE antenna tuner the antenna will not 
work well. Likely the tuner in your radio can only handle a 3:1 SWR antenna. Your
wire will be much worse than that on some bands. The Alpha Delta DX-CC is matched
on 80, 40, 20, 15 and 10 meters to better than a 2:1 SWR. The tuner in your rig can
easily handle this SWR or it is probably OK with no tuner at all.

Get it up as high as possible. Rig it as a flat top dipole if possible. I use one on 80 and
40 as an inverted Vee with the center at ~ 40' and the ends at ~15'. I work DX all over
the world with it. It is the version for ONLY 80 and 40 meters. The 20, 15 and 10 meter
wires are not present. It is also 82 feet long.

Just my two cents.

Good luck and 73,

John K6YP

I need some help and advice regarding antennas.  Currently I am operating with a long wire (107') in an inverted V configuration along my fence line.  It is held aloft with a 35' Channel Master telescoping mast at the center and with a 10' mast at each end.  The telescoping mast is not guyed except parallel to the antenna along the fence line.

The antenna doesn't work worth squat (it may be the 9:1 balum I use to attach it to the RG8x feed line).

I need a new and better antenna.  Unfortunately my wife, who acts as my HOA, has decreed, "Thou shat not turn my back yard into an antenna farm".  This rules out most guying options and vertical antennas that require ground radials.

Ideally I'd like one antenna that covers 160-6 meters, but wouldn't we all?  I'd be satisfied with something that covers 80 to 10 meters or 40 to 10 meters plus perhaps a dipole for 80 meters.

Two alternatives I've been considering are:
  • a terminated folded dipole (http://www.b-squareengineering.com/TerminatedFoldedDipole.htm)
  • a vertical dipole without ground radials that can be deployed without guy wires or with minimal guy wires (http://gapantenna.com/shop/antennas/titan-dx/)
I'd love some advice from more experienced hams.
Michael Wheatley

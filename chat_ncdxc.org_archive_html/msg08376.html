Ed, I think location is a major factor.  On many of the mornings when they were calling West Coast via long path, they were not workable here in Souther Oregon.  They picked up here a bit later than the Bay area,  Unfortunately they would then switch to short path just as they were picking up here, so opportunity was limited.  I could hear many Nor Cal stations making contacts.

> A key success factor is often timing relative to propagation.  Sometimes the
> window of copy is very short and, of course, at a very specific time.  Copy
> can be great one day and non-existant the next.

> horizontal dipole at 85', broadside at 70 degrees, on a ridge top.

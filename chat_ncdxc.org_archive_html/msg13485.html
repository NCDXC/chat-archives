Putting all of the torque on the rotator is OK as long as the peak
torque is withing the safe limits of the rotator.  The 2800 is a
strong rotator.  When I bought my K7NV clamp I asked Kurt if he has
seen any rotator failures due to the mast now effectively 'pinned' to
the rotator by using his product.  He said 'no'.  Then I went to the
M2 booth and asked the same question, and the M2 representative said
it's not recommended to have a clamp that doesn't slip.  I honestly
think the true answer lies somewhere in between.  For a small
installation I think the 2800 is strong enough to take the torque.
I've seen the internal parts of the 2800...they are massive.

I can understand Paul's frustration and his desire to fix the problem.
I know because I had the same problem until I installed the K7NV
clamp.

> Hi Paul,
>
>
>
> I also have the K7NV clamp.  I used to have a lot of issues with
> 'windmilling' but after switching over to the K7NV clamp I haven't had any
> slipping issues.  The clamp bolts have to be pretty tight, to the point
> where you could see the steel clamp deform.  I have spoken to Kurt and he
> told me to tighten the clamp so that there's 1/8" gap between the clamps.
>
>
>
> I have no experience with the Slipp Nott clamp.
>
>
>
> You should never pin the mast.  Unless the you use a tapered bolt that will
> match the diameter of the hole, a regular straight bolt will always be
> smaller than the hole, which over time will ovalize the hole and defeat the
> purpose of pinning the mast.
>
>
>
> In the worst case and as a last resort, you could spot braze the K7NV clamp
> to your mast.  The spot braze will prevent any movement at all and it can be
> grinded off in the future to uncouple the clamp from the mast.  But doing so
> will put more stress on the rotator.  The slipping mast is currently acting
> as a 'fuse' and therefore your rotator is being protected from damage.
>
>
>
> If you have a Green Heron controller, it can be used to realign the mast to
> true north.  I do this as long as the slippage is less than ten degrees.
> This is done by lining up the mast to true north and use the 'calibrate'
> function to reset the controller to 0 degrees.

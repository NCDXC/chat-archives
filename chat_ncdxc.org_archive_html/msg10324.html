Rich

The Anan had a fat client. Like drinking from a fire hose. Also Apache doesn't do software but rely on the HPSDR software Crew. They are very active and releases updates every couple of weeks. It's a great experimenters radio but not in the same class as the Flex in terms of architecture and support. 

Good example of you get what you pay for I guess.

Also the Anan hardware has had heat problems where it's receiver performance drops as the unit heats up. 

I ran the Fat Client model with my original Flex 5000. It worked fine as long as I carefully managed all software updates to the PC especially device driver changes. The fat client model is very sensitive to Windows delayed procedure calls. 

The Flex 6000 client needs a decent graphics card - not a gamer class unit - just not the integrated graphics that are on most motherboards. 

I run my 6700 remotely from all over the U.S.  Works great. It wouldn't be possible to run the Anan remotely in the same way. 

FlexRadio started the 6009 software from a clean sheet if paper with a modern design. The software is now very robust and pretty much at the same functionality as PowerSDR which was open sourced and is the basis of HPSDR. 

I have beaten every contest score I made on the 5000. The radio is amazing and very low fatigue level to operate. 

Diversity is only possible on the 6700 and has amazing benefit as pile ups spacially spread out in your head. Amazing advantage when running in a contest as you can get full calls first time most times and so reduce the need for repeats or fills. 

Stu K6TU

Rich if you are not interested in band preselectors, (this would move you to a 6500) than the 6300 is a great rig.  As a competitive contester, I am still using K3’s but am starting to migrate to the Flex series at the home QTH, and soon testing one at N6RO.  Their receive performance is outstanding, and MAJOR software improvements are being released on a regular basis.  Its remote control capabilities are amazing and it has the ability to display a full spectrum pan adapter remotely..  I truly suspect that radios like the flex 6000 represent the future of amateur radio..  (computing power built into the rig itself.. essentially a server). 

 

The Anand is another well performing SDR but offloads its processing to a computer interfaces to it.. leaving it in the earlier gen SDR bracket.  more along the lines of the earlier flex radios.  But is less expensive.  Not sure about its remote capabilities, but its performance is apparently quite good, but would require a very decent PC as well adding to cost.

 

Bottom line..  Great rig.   The 6300 won’t match your K3’s performance(read keep the k3 around for a bit from this) in a lot of areas and to get into that arena you should consider the 6500.  But the 6300 will open up the future.  Make sure you have a more contemporary PC available to work with it as well. (doesn’t need to be a supercomputer, just something decent.. since much of the work is done by the rig itself).

 

Just my opinion and experience so far..  GL.  Happy to chat with you in detail if you would like.


Chris

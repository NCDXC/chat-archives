On 4/24/2014 7:25 PM, Paul Ewing wrote:

I would be glad to discuss this or any other issue.
I made a somewhat detailed post in response to one of yours, in which I asked a rhetorical question that I believe deserves a response. Chris and I both gave you the courtesy of an reasoned response to YOUR rhetorical questions, which you chose to deny the validity of.
Also in this thread, noted that I'd seen quite a few QSLs, mostly, but not all, from JA hams, with their US call listed. I took a few hours today to go through my DX cards, and found 45 US calls printed on them. I then went to qrz.com and confirmed that all of those calls were licensed to those foreign hams. 28 of them are either 2x1 or 1x2. The QSL instructions all point to the DX address, or to a QSL manager in that DX country usually saying that's where they live.
You stated that top contesters do not need or use club calls. I just did a survey of top contesters, and, in general, a strong majority of top contesters west of the Mississippi whose call is not a 2x1, have a 2x1 available to them. That's because WPX is a contest that gives west coast hams a fighting chance at winning, whereas nearly all other DX contests are tilted to the east coast by a 20:1 ratio. Likewise, many serious west coast contesters have access (or own) contesting stations in other countries -- for example, the Caribbean, ZD8, HC8, and so on. Those guys generally do not have domestic contest calls. An exception is W0YK, who holds virtually all world records for RTTY contesting from P4. Ed has a 2x1, a 1x2, and is trustee for a bunch of NCCC 1x3 CQP calls. K5ZD is trustee of N1LI. N2IC, who has won Sweepstakes from NM and competed at WRTC, is trustee of WK5T. K5RC, who hosted N6TV's WINNING CW Sweepstakes performance this year, is trustee of W7RN and K7SB. Bay Area contesters WX5S and ND2T either operate themselves at W6YX and K5RC, or they loan their 2x1 calls to those stations. K6MM, VP of this club, is trustee of AK6M. K6XX and K6XI, who competed at WRTC last time around, are respectively trustees of WF6C and NF6A.
Executive summary -- the top contesters who care about WPX (those west of the Mississippi and who contest from home) have a 2x1 call. Likewise, many members of PVRC and NCCC, who have a very active competition for Sweepstakes, have at least one addition call available for their use to facilitate station swapping.
As Ted has laid out quite well, the clubs for which I am a trustee have bylaws, members, officers, and other required documents of organization. And our meetings are about planning for and operating contests.
Another point. When I came into this hobby, in 1955, age 14, you had a shot at a 1x2 call (2x1 didn't exist) when you had an Extra class ticket and and been licensed for 25 years. The Extra ticket in those days carried no privileges beyond the General. In other words, a 1x2 meant that not only that you were an Extra, but that you were an OT. I had my Extra, which required 20 wpm cw in 1959, and was waiting out the 25 years when incentive licensing came along, and the FCC gave away the farm. It still rankles me to see new hams with 1 year old licenses and can't copy their own call at 5 wpm, with a 1x2.
As Chris said, this issue was debated and a resolution was reached in the form of a change to FCC Rules governing club licenses. It's a done deal. Give it up, and move on to something that matters. 

73, Jim K9YC

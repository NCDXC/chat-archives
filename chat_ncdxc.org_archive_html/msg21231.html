When a DX station is operating simplex, if one makes a contact on CW, SSB, RTTY, PSK31, etc. when that contact is finished, the DX still "owns" the frequency and the protocol is to change frequencies before transmitting again. 

I don't know why people treat FTx differently from these other modes.
One thought is that the FTx modes let you make QSOs with two stations on the same frequency because when the first is decoded, it's transmission is regenerated and subtracted from the incoming signal and then decoding is retried. Some people may think that frequency separation doesn't matter on FTx.

On 10/25/20 at 3:15 PM, chat@ncdxc.org (Paul N6PSE via Chat) wrote:


Like many of us, I enjoy operating on FT8 and FT4.

Frequently, I see the following scenario played out.

A DXer calls the DX and works him in the same frequency on the waterfall.
(Not Split) The DXer then proceeds to call CQ CQ or Call other DX without
moving away from the first DX station on the waterfall. This causes great
QRM to the DX and anyone trying to work him.

I see this happen a lot, yet this entire scenario is easily avoided by
working Split. In WSJT-X all you have to do is check the Box for
TX-Hold Freq. This makes you transmit only where you have placed your red
cursor. It will not automatically move.  I have great success
transmitting below 500 Hz or about 2500 Hz.

I rarely work DX on his frequency, but if I do, I carefully move my red
cursor away after making the contact.

This avoids causing needless QRM or causing me to be a LID.

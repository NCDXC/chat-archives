My 160M TX vertical is a 63’ tall vertical – top loaded and hatted. The coil at the top is from an MA-160V. I mounted it on top of a 30’ push up mast. 76 buried radials. It actually works very, very well on several other bands (30, 17, and more). It works very well on 80 – but only the 75M SSB portion of that band.

 

At the base, I have a 1:1 current choke followed by a Ten Tec 30 – 400 pf variable capacitor, and have the center frequency at 1.821. Its weather proof.

 

I can use the antenna on all bands from 160 – 10M using the KAT-500 in the shack, but I think remotely tuning at the base would be a much better way to go. To do this – I have two Jennings vacuum variable capacitors – 30 – 400 pf and a ? to 1000 pf (UCS 400 and UCS 1000). I also have a really killer high voltage variable inductor – and all of these can be remotely driven using servo motors if need be. Stepper motors seem too expensive.

 

Anyway, I’m wondering what circuit I might use for such an application – an “L”, a “T”, or ??????

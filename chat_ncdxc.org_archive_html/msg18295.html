On 12/2/2019 12:20, John Miller via Chat wrote:
There really is no clearing house for DXpeditions, although major foundations like NCDXF and INDEXA become aware of most DXpeditions in their early planning stages.  NG3K’s excellent database keeps track of expired, active and upcoming DXpeditions, which is helpful.

https://www.his.com/~wfeidt/Misc/adxo.html

But perhaps another way to look at it:  Any well-planned DXpedition to a desirable DXCC entity is not a “wasted effort” — simply because there is always a demand around the world for a Top 50 country.  Take a look at ClubLog’s Most Wanted list:

https://clublog.org/mostwanted.php

Just focusing on US territories, you’ll see even recent DXpeditions to these rare locations didn’t drive down the demand dramatically.

Current Ranking:
KH7K Kure Island = #7
KH3 Johnston Island = #8
KH4 Midway = #14
KP5 Desecheo Island = #19
KH5 Palmyra Atoll = #23
KH1 Baker Island = #32
KH8/S Swains Island = #34
KH9 Wake Island = #37
KP1 Navassa Island = #46

Similarly, high global rankings for 
VP8O South Orkney Islands #16
VP8S South Sandwich Island #21
VK0H Heard Island #29
VP8G South Georgia Island #36

I think it’s fair to say, that even under the best of circumstances, no one DXpedition, operating for a 10-14 day period, can put enough ATNO QSOs in the log to satisfy the overwhelming demand for DXCC credits from the Top 50 Most Wanted.  This is especially true during this current down Cycle, with unpredictable propagation and ever-chainging weather patterns.  I applaud and fully support those well-planned DXpeditions to the Top 50 — especially the ones who might be competing with each other for financial resources.  We should all do what we can to make sure that every DXpedition is not a wasted effort.

73,
John, K6MM

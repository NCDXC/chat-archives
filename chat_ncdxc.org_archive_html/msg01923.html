My K-Line, K3-P3-KPA500-KAT500, is my all-time favorite rig.   The receiver performance is impressive.   The operating convenience is outstanding.  I am still exploring and learning about its capabilities, in effect, still learning how to operate the rig.   It’s a whole lot of fun!

 

My sentimental favorite has to be my first station’s receiver I had back in 1968.  It was a Navy Surplus Hammarlund SP-600JX.   It must have weighed 50 lbs, and the power supply another 50 lbs.   It was a beast!   It had pretty good selectivity and sensitivity for its day and its stability was unmatched.   It was a great Novice receiver.   I paid $75 for it.

 

- 73 de Mike, K6MKF, W6NAG, NCDXC, Conway Reef 2012, K3-P3 Addict, Maui

Here is your NCDXC Question of the Week:

 

What is your all time favorite rig?

 

 

 

 

 

My response: My all time favorite rig is my Yeasu 1000MP. I bought it new circa 1991 and I still have it. This was my first "big box" rig replacing a Kenwood TS-690. I never really liked or enjoyed the TS-690. The 1000MP is a joy to operate and a very fine radio. My primary pile up busters are a pair of K3's that I use on CW/SSB/RTTY but for casual listening I still enjoy flipping the antenna switch over and listening from the 1000MP.

 

 

Paul N6PSE

On 8/15/2014 9:36 AM, Rich Holoch via Chat wrote:

Then the thought "must have more power on top of that" . . . . and oh well -
it's an affliction I guess . . a costly one at that
You're not alone, Rich. I think most guys who are serious about DXing or contesting and who are also at least somewhat technically competent are continuously tweaking their systems -- antennas, rigs, feedline, etc. to squeeze out every half dB, simply because those half dB add up, and because when things are marginal (especially close to the other guy's noise), a dB or two can be the difference between in the log or not.
Something like six years ago, I replaced the RG8X that was feeding my high 80/40 dipoles with Belden's 8213 (their best RG11) and picked up more than a dB (a couple of dB at the band limits on 80). As I was able to find hard line, I ran it all the way to the top of my tower (a 350 ft run), picking up another dB on the higher bands, half a dB on the lower ones. Going from a very good 3-el to a very good 4-el is not much more than a dB, and if I could turn the 4-el SteppIR in my trees I would. :)

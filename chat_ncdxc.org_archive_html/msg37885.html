Hello Andreas,
A name change is really not in consideration at this time. If in the future that subject should come up it would require a 20% in favor vote of the Membership.There have been no motions made to change the Club name. There would not be without involving the membership. The Board is in favor of removing the current geographic restriction. We would like to wait on that until our 501c3 has been approved. The removal of the Geographical restriction would mean a change in the Bylaws which can only be done with a 20% in favor vote by the membership. So, we are looking at around a year or less before that could happen,.

I think there is much the NCDXC can do to promote inclusivity without changing the name of the Club.
 
If the Club Members so choose:
 
  • Eliminate the geographic restrictions on full membership
  • Offer full membership to our Associate Members 
  • Eliminate the Associate Member as a membership class
  • Reach out to DXers who may be interested in joining our Club
 
Our Club is widely recognized by name within the DX community for our support of DXing.  
 
The NCDXC just celebrated the 78th anniversary of its founding.   I am convinced that our Charter Members would want the name of the Club to remain the Northern California DX Club, as they intended.
 
However, it is up to the Club’s Board of Directors to approve any changes to our Bylaws, and for 20% of the voting members to approve those changes.
 
That’s my story, and I’m sticking to it.

in my opinion, the NCDXC should remain the NCDXC. It’s our name, it’s been our name for years, and it’s who we are. NCDXC forever!

After 77 years of existence, the SCDXC has begun its dissolution,
with no board member nominees for VP or president.  An email was
sent out to members.  The NCDXC offered to merge, to form what will
be called the California DX Club.  This will take about a year as
they work out their 501-C-3 application and implement any changes.

They note only 12 people show up for meetings and "almost all in
[using] walkers," and three on Zoom.  The law requires a board to
maintain the corporate status.  "It has been a long time coming."
Said another way, "Last night (Thursday) the Southern California DX
Club was dissolved.  Dissolution resulted from the declining
membership and from the lack of members who are willing to serve in
the major offices."  NCDXC is encouraging SCDXC members to join the
"California DX Club."

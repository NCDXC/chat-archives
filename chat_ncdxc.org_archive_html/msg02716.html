John, a very informative response.
 
Yes I was surprised that the IARU Region President was there helping the Kosovar hams get established with their new self-assigned prefix.  It almost seems by the presence of the various VIPs that they are somewhat condoning the use of an unassigned prefix. That is sending a mixed message about conforming to the rules and I am not sure how wise that is.
 
Interestingly, I learned in November during my visit to Kosovo that the Kosovars do not plan to seek UN admission as a member state any time soon. Kosovo is essentially a "welfare state" heavily dependent on the UN and neighboring countries. The Kosovars told me that they know the assistance will be diminished if they truly become independent.
 
I think the South Sudanese did it the right way, where they truly declared their independence upon a vote of secession and sought agreement and alignment that enabled their successful UN admission. They are truly an independent country. They also worked with the ITU and submitted a proper prefix application and ultimately received one.
 
Should the ARRL change the criteria to add an entity?  My opinion is no, the current process works just fine.

Some facts:

Kosovo is not recognized by the UN yet -- currently a requirement for DXCC approval.  Russia and China have vetoed their membership.
http://www.dxcoffee.com/eng/2012/09/26/kosovo-dxcc-entity/

The ITU has not assigned the Z6 prefix.  It was self-assigned by the Kosovo telecommunications authority.

http://www.itu.int/online/mms/glad/cga_callsign.sh

http://dx-world.net/2012/the-republic-of-kosovo-soon-on-air/

So....reactivating amateur radio in Kosovo seems like a good idea.  But transmitting from Kosovo using an illegal prefix probably isn't OK.

Unfortunately, the ITU -- and not the ARRL or IARU -- is the authoritative organization and the key here.

For ARRL to change their entity admission process requires rolling up their sleeves and squaring off with the ITU.  Not likely to happen.

Politics and amateur radio:  Not an easy road to travel.

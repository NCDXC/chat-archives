Rick,

 

Here’s the VOACAP prediction for my QTH in CM87. Remember that prop forecasts are educated guesses, so like the weather forecast, are only a place to start. The hotter (shorter wavelength) colors are good, like yellow, and indicate a higher probability of a successful path.

Keep HamSpots running on your phone, and when a new band or mode is spotted, jump in quickly. If you’re using a directional antenna, try both LP and SP headings, although as a rule-of-thumb, LP has been predominating in our morning, and SP in our evening.

3Y0K, in my opinion, has been favoring Europe when the path is open from Bouvet. In addition, azimuthal bearing from Bouvet to Europe are almost exactly 90 degrees away from their bearing to CA. If they’re beaming Europe, we will be at a disadvantage.

I worked them on SP 40 SSB in our evening, and on LP 20m SSB in our morning. They have some good ops with keen ears, so hang in there. Every day presents a new opportunity.

73, Bruce K3NQ

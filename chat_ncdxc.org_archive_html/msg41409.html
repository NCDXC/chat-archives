60M is a surprising band, at times amazing, even stunning.

This was a perfect opportunity for the FCC to make operations on 60M simpler AND more aligned with the (nearly worldwide) spectrum limits.  

But once again Newington got in the way (yeah, I filed a comment directly to the FCC and the ARRL).  Instead of 100 watts ERP over the entire spectrum (easy, what I had suggested); it’s now more complicated.  Thanks for nothing ARRL; another strike out ignoring the membership wants.

With local noise often very high at the DX end, 100 watts is marginal much of the time (in a tight crowded space for the rare ones, because it’s a channel, no options).  Reducing that to 9.15 watts ERP (relative to a half wave dipole) for the rest of that spectrum, makes the added spectrum near useless for DXing.  Plus many licensed hams didn’t see that question in the test pool so they have zero idea how to measure ERP (a mentoring opportunity for those that do get it).

Even this same FCC report states that 100 watts ERP has not caused interference; it can’t be stated plainer.  But here we are, 9.15 watts ERP if outside the channels.

The ONLY DXing advantage in this change is that the US can now work an EU centric team (most of them out there this year) on 60M legally; if they can measure 9.15 watts ERP and get through the EU hordes.  The ALL-but-US filter is removed.

I don’t see the ARRL blessing 60M DXCC because some regions (i.e. SEA, SoPAC) don’t allow it.

As for DXCC, I’m already past that, I don’t require a Newington blessing to prove what I already know I’ve accomplished already.  They can keep their purchased ‘award’ for my effort; I’d rather hang QSL cards on the wall instead of a phony “award” I had to buy. (Oh right, pay for the STICKER to put on that wallpaper).

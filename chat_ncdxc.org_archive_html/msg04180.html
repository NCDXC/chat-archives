Hi,

It's Saturday evening on American Samoa and we're approaching 10,000 QSOs.
 Lots of club members
are already in the logs.  I said "Hi" to those I recognized on phone and a
few times on CW but the
pileup have been huge.  Discipline has seemed to be worse than the last
time I was on the other end and
I think our signals have not always been strong enough to completely
control the pile ups--but we continue to
get calls in the logs.

While we are doing our best to get each call correct before moving on to
the next QSO, I'm sure we have not
always succeeded.  Jacky, ZL3CW, is uploading the logs to ClubLog each
morning around 8 am local, 1900 UTC.  If you cannot find your QSO on
ClubLog, please work us again.  There are six more days before the
CQWW, so don't worry about a duplicate QSO.

While trying to make skeds is not practicable, let me know if there is a
band that you believe needs more emphasis from our end.  Each morning we
look at the totals for each band and mode and, so far, I think we're
doing a pretty good job of being everywhere, all of the time, given that
we have only two stations on the air
at any given time.

I will be on 12 SSB tomorrow morning starting around sunrise here, 1730
UTC.  It's the one band/mode that has been neglected so far.

It's still a real hoot to work folks you know.

73 Kip W6SZN/W8A

The actual reason is simpler.  MSHV has robot mode; they can start it and walk away (sleep, party, eat or manage another station).  Which often also demonstrates one of the MSHV issues, it can lock up, lock up, lock up, lock up... slap!
If an RR73 was not sent BUT the calling station was logged; MSHV allows a lockout for that call on that band, for up to 24 hours. The callers aren't aware that they are bring ignored, so they call again, and again to try to get the RR73 (Livestream offsets this some, you can see the log).
The concept of F/H is valid, keep a spectrum open so stations can answer the report.  IF folks paid attention AND didn't call on the same tones; but the spectrum cost condenses the callers into 1 KHz less space and under educated hams don't take the time to learn how to use the mode (effectively or often cleanly).
In short, human nature and a willingness to take the easy ways out; which negates some of the benefits of F/H.  While MSHV has other significant flaws that have never been addressed.

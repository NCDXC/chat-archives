After 77 years of existence, the SCDXC has begun its dissolution,
with no board member nominees for VP or president.  An email was
sent out to members.  The NCDXC offered to merge, to form what will
be called the California DX Club.  This will take about a year as
they work out their 501-C-3 application and implement any changes.

They note only 12 people show up for meetings and "almost all in
[using] walkers," and three on Zoom.  The law requires a board to
maintain the corporate status.  "It has been a long time coming."
Said another way, "Last night (Thursday) the Southern California DX
Club was dissolved.  Dissolution resulted from the declining
membership and from the lack of members who are willing to serve in
the major offices."  NCDXC is encouraging SCDXC members to join the
"California DX Club."

Al,

 

Good discussion.  But one thing I really like about FT8 is that it gives a chance to DXers who would otherwise be severely limited by antenna restrictions in apartment houses, condos, or homeowners’ associations. 

 

DXCC on FT8 might be a real accomplishment for those hams who can barely string a short wire off a balcony.  And I’d much prefer to see those hams on HF working DX with FT8, rather than having them hang out on VHF repeaters with their shack on a belt!

Dave and all,

 

It is interesting to hear the various replies to my simple question about using FT8 vs. CW.  Most were thoughtful and positive.  One called my question ridiculous.  Regardless, the comments reveal a number of reasons why the new ‘easy’ modes are used.  I understand all of those reasons though I am sure I do not want to watch TV and work FT8 at the same time.  Not my flavor of amateur radio!  I think one of Dave’s comments reflects my thoughts well:  “I will note however that I do not consider my accomplishment of DXCC with FT-8 anywhere near the feeling of accomplishment I have with the other modes.”

We all work our DX for our own reasons and for me, I want a challenge that requires my skill and experience to get a QSO that I seek.   I will continue that way on my path to the awards I pursue along the way.  I may further develop my CW and SSB skills along the way as I wait for the ‘last one’ I need.

Meanwhile, I continue to enjoy the challenge of working with 10 W to see what I can log.  Up to 122 entities so far and still seeking more.  Maybe I can do a 5BDXCC that way some day!

 

Tnx to all for the feedback on why you use FT8.

 

73 es gud DX de Al-N6TA (that is CW speak!)

Hi Al,  Interesting question.  I have always considered myself as primarily a SSB op, but the log does not really reflect that.  Most of my contacts are from contesting, but I have actually been dong better at CW than SSB, especially in the solar conditions we have been having the last couple of years.  There is also less interference, profanity, “police” etc.  However I also have a large number of RTTY and FT8 contacts in the log as well.  RTTY of course from contests again, but FT8 is a new mode, where I can still find something new.  It is like starting all over with a new call. I am working all bands on FT8 with under 100 watts, And can even run it remotely while traveling, or even from the couch while watching a movie.  I can’t do that on SSB/CW or RTTY.  Long answer, but,  No  it is not because I don’t want to do CW, It is something new to do in addition to CW.  And with only a handful of DXCC entities left to work, it keeps the interest up.

 

I will note however that I do not consider my accomplishment of DXCC with FT-8 anywhere near the feeling of accomplishment I have with the other modes.

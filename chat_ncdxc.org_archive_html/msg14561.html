The convention for specifying RTTY frequencies is to use the RF Mark frequency.   It is irrelavent whether the transmission is AFSK or FSK.  When either method is properly configured and adjusted they are indistinguishable on the air.

The reason some spotted frequencies are wrong is that the operator uses their radio dial frequency which may not be the Mark frequency.  Often it is the suppressed carrier frequency which differs from the Mark frequency by whatever local tone frequency is being used.  Each operator on the two sides of the QSO can use any local audio tone frequencies.  They are independent and can be arbitrarily different. 

When an operator fails to understand these frequency relationships, they are likely to be confused and specify the wrong frequency.  The error could be up to about 2 kHz off since most people use local audio tones at about 2 kHz or lower.

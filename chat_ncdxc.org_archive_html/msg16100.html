Scott –

 

The DXCC rules leave a wide set of options for DXCC station requirements for DXCC to accept your application.  But the old mileage limit is long gone.  When I moved long ago, high school days, I threw away all those logs and cards!  They had some value to me but not for DXCC.  My dad was in the Air Force and household shipments were limited so out went old stuff – cards and logs included.  Yes I should not have done that but at the time it was the thing to do.  Then years later – What! I can count those contacts!!?

 

But for what aspect of the rules you want to use - as someone once told me “it’s your DX”.  I personally don’t go for the remote option in the rules.  I will only submit contacts from my station setup I installed in my home.  But that is “my DX”.

For some reason, I thought that DXCC required operators to work from within X distance (I'd thought 75 mi)  of their home station (in my case, this would be San Jose, CA, USA). Can someone sanity check my new understanding?

 

Reading through the rules posted at http://www.arrl.org/dxcc-rules:

9.  Station Location and Boundary:

 
a) All stations used to make contacts for a specific DXCC award must be located within the same DXCC entity.

[AD6YT: OK, so all of my contacts would need to be made from US (entity 291), but anywhere in US would be valid - remote shack in FL, my FT-991 in CA, kx3 on vacation in WY]


b) All transmitters and receivers comprising a station used for a specific contact must be located within a 500-meter diameter circle.

[AD6YT: for *A* contact, so all contacts don't have to originate within that 500m circle, just no 2km feedlines or using HF emitters cresting all the Bay Area ridges for diversity during a single contact]


c) QSOs made with legally licensed, remotely controlled stations are allowed to be used for DXCC credit.

[AD6YT: suggests I misunderstood, and that remote operation of stations emitting from anywhere in the US would be valid.]

 

So use of services of like remotehamradio.com to borrow someone else's stateside shack and operate it under my own call would be considered ethical and valid for credit?

 

If so, this significantly changes my impression of how reasonable DXCC would be for me to achieve in spite of my HOA.

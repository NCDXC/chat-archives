Congrats. 

Got them on 80M (500 watts to my 63' hatted 160M vertical - 76 buried radials), but I did hear them very well at their peak on 160M. I thought they came back to me - but that was before they were in the clear. At first they were bobbing in and out of the noise - but then had a really nice peak for almost 15 minutes.

The phased K9AY and N6RK loop - with MFJ-1025 noise canceler and K9YC chokes get the credit for being able to hear them so well. Now I can just concentrate on getting a clean 160M Q.

I worked what I think was a SLIM on 15M last night. I say this because the guys fist wasn't as good as FT5ZM's has been. And it was weird - he would work several of us - then seemed to have some problem, come back, then go away for a little bit. He also sent UP FT5ZM instead of FT5ZM UP, he seemed a little "distracted". It wasn't the right "cadence". 

We will see - he certainly came back to me. But a few minutes later - someone with a better fist, lower signal was there working the pileup with the right cadence. That signal wasn't as good as SLIM's . . . . 

Who knows - I'll check the log on the next upload. I think 12 and 10 would be a gift at this point - but the solar numbers seem to almost support it. The "REAL" FT5ZM on 15M last night was not strong - but I still heard him at 7:30PM PST - working JA's and UA0's.

On Feb 5, 2014, at 8:37 AM, KE1B <ke1b@richseifert.com> wrote:


Decided to get up a little early this morning, and see if I could hear them on 80m.

Heard them on 3523, but didn't poke around to see exactly where he was listening. Just set the split to +3 kHz and gave him a call. That's all it took; one call, with a "shot in the dark" offset.

So I figured I'd see if I could hear them on 160. (I don't have much of an antenna on 160; just a loaded inverted vee, with the major lobes oriented NW/SE.) Heard their signal--weak, but copyable--and heard K6MM calling about 2 kHz down. They came back to John, and I called on his tailgate. Again, first call got through! Very unusual for me on 80/160.

Didn't even have time for the amp to warm up.

Here is my response:  I really dislike the term DXPP or DX Paying Passenger. It gives the connotation that the operator is not otherwise qualified to be on the team other than the thickness of his/her wallet.
 
Every DXpedition has operators who bring to the table various skill sets and sets of knowledge. It is a rare DXpedition that can attract and consists solely of world-class operators.
 
My position is that DXpeditioners can best serve the DX Community by helping to develop and hone operating skills at both ends of the pile up. There are times during a DXPedition where the focus is full speed ahead, pedal to the metal and there are times where you slow down, adjust the pace and make it truly fun and enjoyable for operators at both ends of the pileup.
 
As a DXpedition operator, I have grown considerably due to the mentoring and guidance that I have received from world-class DXPeditioners such as K3LP, YT1AD, N2WB, K1LZ and others. These guys took the time and had the interest to give me positive and constructive input that have made me a better operator. What I have received from them during these DXpeditions has been priceless!    This is why I will always make it a point to include several lesser experienced Operators on my DXpedition teams. I believe that we should all develop and mentor who we can, when and where we can. All of my DXpeditions have included first time DXPeditioners on the team and no one has suffered. This is not brain surgery after all.
 
I cringe at the thought of the DX Foundations offering less support or withholding support altogether if they deem there are too many inexperienced operators. Just look at the success of last year’s CY9-St. Paul DXpedition for example. This team had some inexperienced DXpeditioners however they did a fantastic job! I would hate to see a team such as this have their desire dashed because of a lack of funding due to their relative inexperience. We need more of these kinds of DXpeditions not less of them!
 
For me, some of the best moments during a DXpedition are when we expose the locals to amateur radio or spend time with them developing their skills. I’ll never forget during the 2011 Rotuma-3D2R DXpedition, we spent every waking moment when we were not operating at the Rotuma High School, setting up their station and training the kids to operate. During the last hours of the DXpedition we had dozens of kids in our shack and they were making real QSOs and logging them in the DXpedition logs. How cool is that!
 
DXpeditioning should not be an elitist function. We all put our pants on the same way. We need to let our hair down (for those that have hair) and recognize that each DXPedition does the best job that they can with what they have got, whether it is manpower, propagation, radios, amplifiers or antennas.
 
Paul N6PSE

Martti Laine-OH2BH has written several recent articles about DXPP’s or “DX Paying Passengers”. Martti describes a DXPP as an operator with only a minimal degree of operating expertise, but who contributes to the Dxpedition financing to help the Dxpedition.
Martti writes that DXPPs can seriously damage the credibility of an operation and create chaos on the bands. He is critical of the K5D Desecheo Dxpedition where a strong operator was relieved by a lesser skilled operator and problems resulted. Martti also criticizes the TX5K Clipperton Dxpedition, writing that “a seat was available for anyone who wanted to pay”. Martti suggests that the number of TX5K DXPPs added to the stress level of the Dxpedition team members and the Dxpedition audience.
Martti suggests that DX Foundations consider the number of DXPPs included in a Dxpedition when funding them. He writes that a Dxpedition may not qualify for support if the percentage of DXPPs is too high.
 
Here is your Question of the week: As members of the DX Community, are you concerned about DXPPs?   Do you feel that a Dxpedition suffers if there are a few lesser experienced operators on the team?   Should the DX Foundations offer less support for a Dxpedition to a rare place even if they have some lesser skilled operators on the team?
Give us your thoughts-
 
Paul N6PSE

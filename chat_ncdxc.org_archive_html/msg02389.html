A lot can be learned from studying the expeditions of George Wallner, AA7JV.  He has proved himself a master at doing a lot for not a lot of money.  In three seasons, he's put five entities in my log, and he's done it on the most difficult bands, 160-40.  Sometimes he's put me in the log on a lot of band modes because he travels light, sails himself, takes only a few operators, but often stays a long time. 

Goals?  They must be matched to realities.  $1.5Mil is not a reality.  Sure, the paradigm of working a trip on 20 band/modes is fun, but it's not a reality if it takes $1.5 Mil to pull off.  Yes, that paradigm must be changed.  The new goal for these difficult trips must be UNIQUES. And the DX code of conduct must be modified to add

"A TRUE BIG TIME DXER will stand by and let the guys who really need the entity work them first."  

and

"A TRUE BIG TIME DXER won't call in a pileup with a DX station he doesn't need, then tie up the DX station while he brags about how happy he is to work him for the tenth time, and while the band goes out so that those who really need the QSO have lost their chance."

and

"A TRUE BIG TIME DXER who's already worked the DX on that band-mode will be a good sport and try to work him QRP for a real challenge."   

Can you tell I've had it with "big time DXers" with more ego than consideration for their fellow man?  :) 

73, Jim K9YC

Paul and Team

 

I for one believe that the 7O6T DXpedition deserves more appreciation and recognition than it has received for all the reasons stated.

 

I was guilty of not voting at all and I also have been negligent in not making my appreciation sufficiently known for the great work done by those wonderful people who incur expense, do the hard work and go in harms way to create the DX for us to work. I QSL and I make modest donations but I now understand that I should do more……. And that I have not been doing enough vis a vis thanking those who do the heavy lifting for the rest of us or voting when I have an opportunity to do so for outstanding DXpeditions.

 

I for one promise to clean up my act and vote moving forward……

 

I am one of the ones who didn’t get it. Thanks for sensitizing me….and I plan to be less fickle with my public appreciation moving forward.  J

 

Thank you!  73, Ted, K6XN   

 

PS I am good for at least one free beverage (adult or otherwise) and at least one “thank you” for anyone part of the 7O6T  DXpedition Team at any/every  NCDXC meeting  for the next year that I attend (and yes I really plan to  attend some NCDXC meetings this year!)  J  It is not be enough but it cant hurt. … and it’s a start.

Dick, this isn't about just NCDXC's voting. We are talking about a global result.

 

You have to understand, these things are our passion and we put our heart and soul into them. We divert time and finances away from our families to carry out these DXpeditions, so we have every right to ask these questions even if the answers aren't forthcoming.

 

The Clipperton DX Club (France) overlooked 706T, NCDXC voted PT0S. the readers of DX-World.net voted for HK0NA (a well deserved honor) and this weekend, the members of SWODXA voted NH8S as their DXpedition of the Year.

 

7O6T was the rarest entity activated in 2012 and was second in QSOs to HK0NA yet was overlooked by all major awards.

 

The DX Community has no problem making it known what they want and need, but they seem pretty fickle with their thanks.

 

I really have to wonder if the DX Community as a whole "gets it".  I tend to think that they don't.

 

Other comments?

 

 

Paul N6PSE

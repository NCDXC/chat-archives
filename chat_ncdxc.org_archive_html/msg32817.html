FTx listening for prop awareness does have two advantages over beacon tracking or sending a CQ; you SEE who is calling (what you hear) and who they're calling (what they hear) on the display for that band.  That tells you at a mere glance, what the band is offering/allowing, without relying on outside resources (random skimmers) or transmitting.

It also gives you hard data, signal reports (caller/called) in dB, that beacons don't offer for most users at their exact location.

While FTx is one more tool in the prop awareness box, it's potent.  That FTx is so popular (far more transmitters than beacons) and wide spread on any band, increases that value.

It's nice to have a collection of things in the toolbox while we continue to hope for the prop magic events (like gray line).  Every tool has it's purpose and place; they all add to the fun of radio.

There’s nothing new here. You can also send CQ on CW, and watch all of the skimmer spots come in. You will get a table of exactly how strong you are, and where.

Also, for years (decades?) the NCDXF has operated a network of Beacon stations, on every band, and every continent (except perhaps Antarctica; not sure). Just listen to the Beacon frequencies, and you can tell if signals are propagating from that part of the world on the band you are listening to.

Real-time propagation information is NOT limited to FT-modes.

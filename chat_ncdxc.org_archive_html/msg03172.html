On 9/15/2013 3:18 PM, Garry Shapiro wrote:
Actually, if you read my posts, SteppIR did not ship "beta firmware with newly purchased hardware". I purchased the beam one year ago; due to a "supply problem", it arrived just before Thanksgiving. I was traveling after T-giving, until almost Christmas and was not about to get into putting stuff back on the treetop in midwinter. I opened the box in July. The point is that the SDA-100 had the then-current firmware, Pinto and my unit was FUBAR on 6m with way-wrong values stored. Instead of loading Pinto again, old Jim suggested the beta Mustang.

When you bought it, and how long it took you to open the box is irrelevant. The SDA-100 didn't work, and from the description it sure sounds like firmware. What did it not do?  If I understand your descriptions of your problems (and we went through them over and over, and in considerable detail), 1) When you entered changes in element lengths and saved them, the original values were still there  and 2) Your 6M element lengths were WAY off (like half what they should have been).

Clearly, the firmware that you had was bad. Call it beta, call it alpha, it should not have been shipped. Perhaps inadequate (or non-existent) final test is involved. And nearly a year after you bought it, they should have had non-beta (as in WORKING RIGHT and fully documented in the form of a current user manual) to send you.

For the record, I have a bit of skin in this game -- I was the guy that talked you into buying it as the solution to your unique problem, and we've had a lot of conversation about your issues with the antenna.  :)

Whether Mustang was a good idea or not remains to be seen. So far, what it lacks is adequate documentation, and for that point I agree with you. I did miss the September VHF Party, because I was screwing with the controller instead of operating on six.

You didn't miss much. :) 

So it goes. SteppIR is not Elecraft.

Thanks Al.  I've been busy the last couple weeks with 'family sh-tuff' (moving kids and prepping that house for sale) and have recovered (while definitely proving that I'm not 18 anymore) in time for the bands to continue to stink.  When I left, the flare had wiped them out lowering the noise floor here by ~10db (yay) but not allowing propagation (boo).

But I've filled other band slots too this week; Cambodia (XU7) on 20 phone, some Chatham slots (15 RTTY was EASY, one call), Taiwan on 40... nothing exciting but Africa is ALWAYS a challenge, so this was fun.  I had to sneak in between calls since he was allowing tailgaters (a rotten trick but he's in the log).  55/55 QSO against big guns, I had to do something...  At times he came in S9+ (so was everyone else).

I'll collect a few more parts, then I'll replace the dipole with longer lasting components (i.e. multi-strand feedline), plus raise it a bit (the trees have grown, maybe another 10-20' higher on the ends)...  just in time for the summer doldrums.  ;o)  Also, instead of the wire running almost true n/s, I'll be able to reconfigure (using another tree since I'll add another 30' of wire) the pattern by about 20 degrees NE/SW.  That may make Africa easier too.

I've also used the stinky bands down time to reprogram my scanners in preparation for fire season (my former occupation).  There've been a lot of (tedious) changes on them because of more channels created from narrow-banding (and CHP is moving to P25 and 700...).  The new shack is still a work in progress... no lack of things to be occupied with, just the willpower and energy components have to be managed.  ;o)

73,
Rick (still stuck at 254 worked)

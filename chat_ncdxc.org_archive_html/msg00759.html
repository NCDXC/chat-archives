Hi Paul--good job of firing up some solid suggestions! I really hope a composite solution can be found---we need an answer for the problem. If I may I'd like to revisit the equipment pool concept. I ran a 200 radio system with 6 repeaters and a full service bench on race tracks in Europe, Australia, here and in South America so I am in total agreement NOBODY wants to open a crate 5000 miles from a Radio Shack (!)and find anything broken. We had protocols, of course, about packing, testing etc. but sometimes volunteer labor (local hams, usually) didn't quite meet our anality if that's a word ! In any case, the concept of check and check again works. For new groups each time you'd have to both require an insurance policy premium payment and have a field check at its domestic storage site--likely here---when it returned. Storage is easy BUT as somebody noted, you better have a seriously good mechanic/tech in your DX team, nonetheless. We stored generators a
 few times--drain them of all fluids, run them dry, spray everything in sight with GIBBS, pull the carb and plastic bag it with silica jell packs---good for a very long time. I note your group and most others pre-test antennas, radios etc.---that wouldn't change and I, like you would check a brand new anything even if the box was still sealed. I would also not have a radio pool---amps, sure, but no radios--guys should bring their own--after the leader signs off on suitability-or borrow from manufacturers. Tents, antenna of all types, coax,tables laptops generators, shipping containers,medical kit,,,all from a pool. If tightly managed it seems to me a modest "rental fee" would be far less than new purchases and provide a sinking fund for wear and tear, etc. Well, as you can tell, retired guys have WAY too much free time. 73  JIM

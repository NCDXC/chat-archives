I had an IM conversation with one of the Heard operators this morning (he monitors THIS list too).  Sadly the move to Spit will not happen, another (terrestrial) storm will hit them in a day, negating any chance of moving.  They will be forced to hunker down again.  It's a risk vs benefit study; the risks are too high.  (Even on 'nice' days, it's long johns, multiple sweatshirts AND cold weather gear outside; the winds [almost] never stop, 40 MPH is common.)

The winds did die down enough that most were able to get better rested (without the rattling noises caused by the wind).  Ability to sleep under these conditions is a learned item.

They were under an aurora umbrella that denied them much propagation, slowly lifting.

They are WELL aware that the West Coast is begging, including 160.  He emphasized the need to be on at every grey line, opening the pipeline to the West Coast (I have, got one Q).

The activity on 160 this (our) morning (their evening) produced one QSO that he was aware of and it wasn't the US.

They're well aware of the DQRM issues, but there isn't a lot they can do, except wobble the CW TX frequency, which isn't effective (for long).  We'll have to suffer through (same for FT4).

We kept the convo short (he was heading to bed), they're in good spirits and eager to do as much as they can (for everyone).  One week left, they intend to maximize it for all.

Rick nhc

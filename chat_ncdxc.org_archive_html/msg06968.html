OK – linear loaded.
 
Tony – do you put 1500 watts into it on 12 or 17?  The SWR voltages have got to be high especially on 12 meters.  Is the linear loading spacing enough to eliminate flash-over?  I know that is of concern to some owners.
 
The C3S has full sized half-wave elements that are naturally resonate on 10-15-20 meters.  But on the WARC bands 17M has a 3:1 SWR and on 12M a little over 5:1.  At 1500 watts and 5:1 there are some high voltage nodes up there.

In a message dated 6/25/2014 10:09:56 A.M. Pacific Daylight Time, chat@ncdxc.org writes:
Jerry-  Your 3 element steppIR with 30/40 kit really is the best bang for the buck. A KT34A is just as big and heavy. It is a tri-bander so you lose 12/17/30/40 meters, four bands gone with the change of antenna. The KT34A while a stellar antenna in its time is dated technology and has traps that can flood and require maintenance to keep them dry.
 
The only other smaller “decent” antenna is a Force 12 C3SS. This is a shorter boom tri-bander that gives you two elements on 10/15/20 instead of three that you have now. With a tuner, you can use it on 12/17 but I never had much luck with that. I replaced my C3SS with a three element SteppIR.
 
Your US Tower MA-550 is quite robust and should handle your 3 element SteppIR just fine. The DB11 is a smaller antenna with folded elements for 20-6 meters. It has two elements for 20 meters and three elements for 10/12/15/17 meters. It weighs 63 lbs vs the 58 lbs that your three element with 30/40 kit.
 
So in essence by changing to the DB11, you would be losing gain, losing 30/40 meters and adding five pounds of weight. Not a good trade off in my book.
 
Your current set up is pretty hard to beat!
 
Paul N6PSE

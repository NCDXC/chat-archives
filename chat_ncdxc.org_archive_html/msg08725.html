Paul,

 

To clarify –

 

The club’s agreement is to financially support the awards (plaques, mailing, etc.) up to $1000 in a year.  The actual cost will probably be much less.  BTW: this was presented to the membership at a club meeting a few months ago.

 

The second part of your first sentence is true.  The club has no say in the award process, it is solely Col’s.  Historically he has done a good job of it.

 

Save $900 and do it ourselves?  The answer is an emphatic NO.  We tried that and it fail miserably. 

 

DX World’s exposure to the world of DXer’s and its ability to produce a large, (thousands) of responses where the club is lucky to see 100 participants makes it the most credible means to offer such an award.  The club is simply helping defray some of his costs.

 

73,

Bob – W6OPO

On Dec 16, 2014, at 11:20 AM, Paul N6PSE via Chat wrote:




I just got done completing the DX-World, NCDXC sponsored DXpedition of the Year-online survey.

 

I am not an expert in survey methodology. The method in use this year struck me as odd.

 

There is a list of about 15 DXpeditions (I didn’t count them) and you chose five of your favorites.

 

Included in the list were the big grand DXpeditions such as FT5ZM and FT4TA. Also included were a number

of one man shows such as C21CG, UA4WHX and E30FB.

 

Seems like this is much like “ranked choice voting” which gave the City of Oakland their second choice as mayor.

 

Any survey experts in the club that think this is a good selection process?  I guess I am “old school” but I

like the approach of pick the best from a set of given criteria. I am impressed by skill, stats, complexity and rarity.

 

I chose to vote for only one instead of the five votes that I was allotted.

 

What do you think?

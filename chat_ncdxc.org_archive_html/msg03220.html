"Close" means different things to different people.  In this context I'd consider "close" to be within 1/4 wavelength. If more than 1 wavelength, the advantage diminishes greatly due to ground eddy currents that sap power from your signal (TX and RX).  Also .. having the ocean to your west doesn't help at all in those other directions.

If you have the room, and can put up nice "close" verticals, then that's great, but also install high horizontal antennas for those other directions. Beverages really help!

The closer you are to the ocean - the more I would want 4 squares on many bands.  . .

It's important to understand that being near the ocean only helps when you're working in the direction of the ocean, and you've got to be pretty close. That's because the effect of the salt water is to provide a stronger low-angle first reflection.

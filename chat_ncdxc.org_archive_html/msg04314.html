On 11/21/2013 7:12 AM, Rich Holoch wrote:
I use the amp almost daily, and I did have a “twinge” to return to a tube amp and manual tuner after this second failure.
A few weeks ago, Bob, K6XX, made a superb presentation to NCCC about how to generate and maintain clean signals. In doing so, he talked about transceivers, phase noise, clicks, output stages, power amps, tuning the amp, matching the amp to the antenna. I added to the presentation with comments on audio processing, and also made a lot of on-air measurements to illustrate his points. Bob is an engineer at Elecraft, where he has been responsible for putting the KPA500, KAT500, and KX3 into production and running the Beta programs for them. At preliminary stages of design, he looked carefully at many competitive amps to understand their strong and weak points.
One thing he observed is that in general, a good tube amp is 8-10 dB cleaner than the best solid state amplifiers. He also stressed that a poorly tuned tube amp, or a solid state amp working into a mismatch, produced a lot more distortion (splatter and clicks). Some rigs are a lot cleaner than others. We all know about clicks from the FT1000MP and its descendants, and most Icom rigs produce both nasty clicks and a LOT of phase noise.
I have three 80s vintage Ten Tec Titan amps (the original, designed in the late 70s) that I bought used, total cost less than half that for a new Alpha, and a KPA500. They're not automatic anything, but they're clean enough that K6XX and I can work 500 Hz apart on CW, even though we're only 3 miles apart. The KPA is my "through the week" amp -- I love the instant on feature. The Titans get used mostly during contests, but I fire one of them up to break an east coast, EU, or JA wall. And I'm using KAT500s in my SO2R station both with the Titans and the KPA500.
The program committee might want to invite Bob to do this for NCDXC. Several guys who heard his presentation at NCCC said it was the best thing they had ever heard at a meeting. 

73, Jim K9YC

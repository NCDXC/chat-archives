I used a R7000 and Gap Eagle at different times when I lived in a rental home in San Jose. I had them elevated on a MFJ tripod and guyed to trees in the yard. They got me on the bands doing mostly digital modes primarily JT65. The eagle was a decent antenna. The R7000 was not quite as good.

I also had a Traffie hexpak portable hex beam that I used for dxpeditions like the VU4, TI9, etc which was pretty key and wouldn’t have worked with those verticals. It is super lightweight but a super pain to change bands. I had that up on a portable military masts anchored to the house using earthquake straps.  When you want to work dx you got to be creative. The acom 1000 I had at the time also helped a little. 😁

Good luck!

Lately myself and a couple others have noted that the HF path to DX is skewed, by as much as 90 degrees, from the expected direct path.  The paths include Crozet and Bouvet of late.  Most of the times, the shift is on most higher bands, consistently (as they appear on different bands).
For example, direct path to Crozet is the same as S AF from here, but over EU is often much better.  Bouvet was better heard looking at the SoPac (90 deg west of direct) instead of a path through the middle of SA.  Paths were not long path, we checked.
The differences in signal strength, night and day (-15 direct, +3 skewed for FT8 folks).  I believe that in both Crozet and Bouvet, a vertical was used, so there should be no dipole pattern.  (Yes, most of NA was blocked on Bouvet, part of the equation; I don't know about Crozet.)
I could understand maybe a 20 degrees shift to account for local obstacles on either end or a reflection warp, but 90 off direct? Since these reports come from different areas (all PNW, in ID and WA); excluding the reporting point local obstacles is reasonable. Again, it's consistent.
Does anyone have THE answer why a vast azimuth change?  I'm not THAT well versed in propagation and it could be an interesting discussion on the list/s.

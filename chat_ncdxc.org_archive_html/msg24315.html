My story:  Before getting ready to head to the bedroom at 0500 (10 PM), I went to see if anything was on 80.  The signal at 3504 was huge and he was working mostly EU.  I called about one minuet and made a contact at 0520Z.  Since he was strong, I figured I might be, too.  Worked with my borrowed KPA500 at 400 W and vertical with 4 radials.
My experience is that during times when there is a path to EU from SA or PAC, these ops will favor that over the easier to work NA.  I have no problem with that as long as it works in our favor with other ops in other location.  Some are kind to us and others not.
I was looking for this contact to avoid getting rusty during my period between a full station and the new QTH.  I only need HC8 on 160M.

If you're reading this tonight, you're up late.

The window to EU finally closed as the JA window cracks open.  A new operator has taken the seat and things are moving along; FINALLY for the US operators.  I finally got 80 CW on my ground plane antenna; hours of chair time.

My take is that the Beverages they rely on (80/40), are optimized for 'home' (EU) as they are showing significant favoritism in that direction.  It also appears that the NW (me and a neighbor for certain) are in the null of that antenna.  Alaska was worked before here, after much of CA (congrats, I saw several familiar calls on FT8).  Nothing but RAW power got us through.  I also suspect they are on the south side of the island (as were the Russians last year) which also shelters some of the northern path.

HOWEVER, should you have a decent antenna AND some power; you can make
80 CW contact now (although not as loud as a few hours ago). Be patient, they are not looking for high rates; long pauses between contacts is common.  You either have to not be in a null or can power your way through.  Their transmit antenna has been pretty good, considering it's temporary.

Having said this, I've been able to add 13 band (all) mode slots to what I had previously, so it's not a total disaster, hi hi. (17 total for 7p8ru).

I hope each of you are meeting your goals as well,  my dance card for the Galapagos is looking pretty full now (needing 7 more slots).

NOTHING heard on 6M here either, yet.  PLEASE comment if the band opens; the usual N/S path works well in favor for the west coast. After that, a VAST ocean.

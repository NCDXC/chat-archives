I bought two FT-1000Ds over their 10 year plus production.  Both were marked FT-1000.  Both came with factory installed BPF for the sub-receiver and seven filters for the main receiver.  On the last one I ordered the optional 8th filter (250Hz) and TCXO-1.

Since the FT-1000D is a key-click generator (notorious) I took the easy way and bought ready-made plug-n-play fixes from Tom Rauch W8JI including the noise blanker.  Cured the key-clicks (rise and fall times went from 1ms to 5ms) but never knew if the NB did anything.

The digital voice keyer with memories is a separate device that plugs into the DVS DIN jack.  It works fine for impromptu recording and for circumstances like contests.  But one can't playback recordings recorded over the air - only recordings made via the microphone.  I rarely use mine but it is nice to have and play with.

I purchased a FT1000 no suffix and also purchased the optional Bandpass filter and a bunch of other options.  I don't believe the Bandpass filter was standard equipment on the FT1000D, but in any event, my FT1000 (after my upgrades) was identical to an FT1000D except for the digital voice keyer.  I could have installed that and made it a full FT1000D.  It is also possible for someone to have purchased an FT1000D and removed some of these options.

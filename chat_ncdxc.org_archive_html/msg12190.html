On Thu,5/26/2016 9:50 AM, Andreas Junge via Chat wrote:
One thing I am doing right now is to lay “chickenwire” throughout my backyard while doing the landscaping. Antennas are good, but I think the most critical is the ground system on 160M.
Yes, that's a good idea, and one of the counterpoise systems I recommended in the 160M talk I did for NCDXC a few years ago. The original idea was first published in the '70s by NC0B (Rob Sherwood) in Ham Radio. Copper ground screens are widely used AM broadcast stations, typically 40'x40'with radials silver-soldered to the perimeter. During college, I spent a summer doing that for a 4-tower 930 kHz array.
Another decent counterpoise option for limited space is K2AV's Folded Counterpoise. NI6T has built one. The link below is to my 160M talk. 

http://k9yc.com/160MPacificon.pdf

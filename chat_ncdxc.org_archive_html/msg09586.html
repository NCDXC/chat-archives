The 12 meter phone opening (NA only) this morning was the loudest I've heard from the E30FB team all week. It was S9 or better on my dipole. After the poor reception of the last week, I was greatly surprised.
Unfortunately, I went to a weekly breakfast with the local retired ham group, stopped for a couple errands on the way home and got to the shack just in time for the band to start to fade. (about 1800Z) Slow deep QSB gave way to slower and deeper fades.
I struggled for the next couple hours until they went below my 'give it a try' threshold... an hour later they were gone (East coast was still happy). During that time there was an op change and the new op admitted that he didn't know the radio then he wanted to say hi to all the stations worked that he knew. Let's just say that he knows a lot of people and you can draw your own conclusions. I am purposely not saying his call, he WAS putting some in the log.
So if someone is watching, again please suh... I'll be home to listen again and forgo the lumpy oatmeal AKA wall board paste... :)
Again, it was the loudest I'd heard from them, on any band. Amazing, maybe conditions will hang in there a few more days or better yet, improve (?!?). The 20 meter daily peak here should be starting in the next hour, if they go there to try it.
I'm absolutely convinced that the 12M path was viable except that it was also viable for ALL of the US, much of EU, SA and some JA comments were tossed into the DX cluster mix as well. There was no shortage of griping, per human nature. ;o) 

Well done E30, well done.

So close to this ATNO... so bloody close...

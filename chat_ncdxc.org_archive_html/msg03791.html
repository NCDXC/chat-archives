Rich, I too have the K-Line and I love the KPA500, however I also have the Alpha legal limit which I use about half the time.
 
I had a blast on ten meters these last several days. I‘ve easily worked over 100 countries just on ten meters this week. The bands are spectacular at the moment, I hope it holds for our sked tonight with EP3PK.
 
Prior to the contest I find some pileups are tough slogging. I usually start calling either with 100 watts or with 500 from the KPA500 and turn on the Alpha only if needed. The biggest problem is hearing the DX under the kops, lids and tuners.
 
My new SteppIR DB-36 is working very well although suboptimum at about 40 feet. For eight weeks now, I have been trying to get a working motor and up/down control from US Tower. Their customer service is extremely poor and I have been unable to raise my tower. That would make a big difference on this end if I could do that.
 
The new Steppir gives me three elements on 30/40 meters which is very new and exciting to me. I was able to work C82DX on 30 RTTY and 40 CW which I could not have done with my dipole.
 
Have fun in the bands today!
 
Paul N6PSE

I run the full Elecraft K-Line and turn the amp on whenever I am trying to work a DXCC Challenge band slot or an ATNO. Most of the time (>85% of the time) I make the Q, and am "happy enough" with my antenna farm (see QRZ.COM) and power level. I run the KX3 just for goofing around – and just because I like the rig more than any other – even at (maybe because) its 12 watts max. So I guess you can say I only use the amount of power required to make a Q.

 

What I have seen lately - especially this past week, with great conditions and obviously a bunch of top notch contest stations on, my rate busting through the pileups seems to have dropped down a bit. I still have racked up about 20 band slots just this week, and I am getting very close to the 1900 mark, which will be a big milestone for me. I’m no longer in a hurry (which I was when working toward HR), and now just having fun.

 

My rate might be about 70% because I think more people in the pileups are running full power, and so its tougher competition. I’m pretty sure it’s the number of additional people in these pileups AND what sounds like higher power levels that I am running. My favorite band is 40M – and my new phased vertical array is performing wonderfully – I am hearing lots of long haul DX very strong and substantially better (3 dB with 25 dB F/B) than my 60’ vertical that has 76 buried radials. But WOW – 40M SSB seems to be one of the most competitive bands these days. The CW segment is much easier for me to bust through pileups.

 

What say you? What is your experience?

 

73,

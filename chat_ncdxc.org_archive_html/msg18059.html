Interesting.  I've found that running the bandwidth wide open (4 KHz on the K3) and allowing the software to work the spectrum as it was designed often brings in better results.  Besides, you want to find a 'hole' for your calling tones, so there is less competition (at least based on what YOU hear, not the DX).

I don't call until I can decode; figure if I can't hear them, I can't get the report.  ;-)  When they are running multistream, they are significantly quieter too, so any CQ (one slot) will be a more realistic test of signal strength.

I've done it and it works. However, I found that what worked amazingly well was an Avantone mixcube (Auratone knock-off). The restricted frequency range centered on the speech band and extremely accurate phase response is amazing for speech intelligibility.

On Mon, May 7, 2018 at 8:43 PM, David Jaffe via Chat <chat@ncdxc.org> wrote:
I'm sitting in my ham shack and have 2 good audio speakers going through a mixing console that I use for playing music, plus a typical ham radio "communications" speaker connected to my transceiver.  

As I recently got a stereo rig, I'm thinking of getting a second "communications" speaker, but before I do that, it occurs to me to wonder whether people have experimented with using hi fi speakers with ham radio?   

Yes, the frequency response is overkill, but as long as the speakers are "flat" and accurately rendering the signals, it seems like they would likely sound better, no? 

The main reason I haven't tried this before is that my hi fi set up is prone to RFI from the ham radio transmissions, but I could probably attack that with my drawer full of torroids.

Any anecdotes / opinions would be appreciated.

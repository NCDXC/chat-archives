Hi,
Triangulating jammers is difficult because that would require several fox hunters in different locations to triangulate at a moment’s notice.
However, there could be a better way that would work 24/7 with minimal intervention and a relatively low cost:
  • Basic setup:
    • Mount 2 cheap loop antennas oriented at 90 degrees in the horizontal plane
    • Connect them to two software defined radios (preferably phase locked; inexpensive RTL-SDRs should work)
    • Have a Raspberry Pi continuously record the 2m band
    • Mount it on a high point to minimize nearby reflections
    • This would yield a direction to the jammer
  • Mount a few of these setups around the Bay Area to triangulate the sources
Such a system would easily monitor the entire 2m band (for jamming , kerchunking, etc). The directions can be calculated after jamming occurs, no need to hang around the fox hunting antenna all the time, or even to listen on the right frequency.
This is not expensive or difficult to build but requires a few locations and people to install and run.

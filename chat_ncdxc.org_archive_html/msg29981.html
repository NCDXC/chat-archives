Mobile HF can be tricky, but might be fruitful during this time of the sunspot cycle.

To mitigate noise from.your own vehicle, you may need to bond the body panels together using braid straps.  But, you won't know until you try it both ignition-on and ignition-off.

I had decent luck in the LA area with a 102" CB whip on a ball mount, with a tuner.  I don't have experience with them, but if I were going to try again I'd probably go with a tunable screwdriver antenna with the longest practical whip.

The hamsticks from my experience aren't good for much, at least below 20 meters.  

If you can get something that's nearly resonant on 10 and use a tuner, you may find you can get use (and decreasing returns) with a tuner on 12, 15, maybe 17 and 20.  

I worked Japan on SSB more than once with the CB whip/tuner combo, on 12 according to my memory, as 10 wasn't often open when I was running this setup.  CW may have produced better results as well, but I wasn't fluent in it when I ran this setup.

Experimentation is the name of the game, and also calibrate your expectations based on the many compromises that come with mobile ops.

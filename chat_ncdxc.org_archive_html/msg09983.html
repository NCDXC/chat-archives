On Apr 16, 2015, at 3:45 PM, Frank K <kibbish@gmail.com> wrote:

I don't own a Steppir, but I helped assemble the 2-element version for Field Day a few years ago.  It's not what I would call "portable".  It took 2 of us more than an hour to reassemble it (it had been used before) and get it up in the air, and it's heavy enough that it was definitely not a one-man job. And we didn't have the 30/40 option.

That being said, someone who actually owns a Steppir may disagree with my limited experience.

But as an alternative, how about a Hex?  I'm using a hex these days and like it a lot.  It won't cover 30/40, but on the other hand, there is a 6-band version that gives you 20m through 6m.  

The "folding" version of the hexbeam is intended to be portable, and looks like it would be pretty easy to raise/lower on a regular basis.  Here's the manufacturer's website: http://www.foldingantennas.com/gb.html

They are in Europe, but Vibroplex is their  US supplier.
